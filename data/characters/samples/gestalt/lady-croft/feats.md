## Free Feats (from Gunslinger class)

### Gunsmithing

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | General |
| **Source** | Ultimate Combat |
| **Prerequisites** | None |

**Benefit**: You know how to use a gunsmith's kit to craft and maintain firearms and ammunition. Once per day, with an hour of work and a gunsmith's kit, you can add 1 gold piece worth of black powder (or 2 g.p. worth of bullets) to your ammunition supply for no cost. You can also use your gunsmith's kit to create and repair firearms, and to load blackpowder cartridges and pellets. Alchemical cartridges can be made with a successful Craft (alchemy) check.

*Free from Gunslinger at level 1. Lara maintains her own pistols in the field — she does not trust anyone else to do it, except Mistress Dorovan, and Dorovan is retired. Her evening ritual at camp includes a thorough disassembly, cleaning, and reassembly of both primary pistols. The ritual is why her misfire rate is lower than a typical Gunslinger's.*

### Rapid Reload (Pistol)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Combat |
| **Source** | Core Rulebook (modified for firearms by Ultimate Combat) |
| **Prerequisites** | Weapon Proficiency (selected crossbow/firearm) |

**Benefit**: The time required for you to reload your chosen type of weapon is reduced to a free action (for a hand or light crossbow, or alchemical cartridges in a one-handed firearm), a move action (for any single-shot two-handed firearm or heavy crossbow, or alchemical cartridges in a two-handed firearm), or a standard action (for a double-barreled shotgun, musket, or pistol).

**Chosen Weapon**: Pistol

*Free from Gunslinger class feature at level 1. Combined with **alchemical cartridges** (which count as the trigger condition in the benefit text), Lara's pistol reload is reduced to a **free action**. This is the core mechanical pillar that allows dual-pistol full attacks to function at all.*

### Exotic Weapon Proficiency (Firearms)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Combat |
| **Source** | Core Rulebook |
| **Prerequisites** | Base attack bonus +1 |

**Benefit**: You make attack rolls with this weapon normally.

**Chosen Weapon**: Firearms (covers all firearms as a group)

*Free from Gunslinger at level 1. Without this feat, wielding a pistol incurs -4 on attack rolls. Lara has been proficient since childhood — her father taught her to shoot at six, before he taught her Common grammar.*

## Character Feats

### Point-Blank Shot

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Combat |
| **Source** | Core Rulebook |
| **Prerequisites** | None |

**Benefit**: You get a +1 bonus on attack and damage rolls with ranged weapons at ranges of up to 30 feet.

*Human bonus feat at level 1. Prerequisite for [Precise Shot](#precise-shot) and [Rapid Shot](#rapid-shot). The +1 atk/+1 dmg is a flat bonus that applies to every shot Lara takes at typical combat range (her pistols' first range increment is 20 feet).*

### Precise Shot

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Combat |
| **Source** | Core Rulebook |
| **Prerequisites** | Point-Blank Shot |

**Benefit**: You can shoot or throw ranged weapons at an opponent engaged in melee without taking the standard –4 penalty on your attack roll.

*Essential for any ranged build that expects to shoot into the middle of a brawl. Without Precise Shot, Lara would take -4 to hit allies engaged in melee — and since she's a dedicated shooter without meaningful melee options, this would be disabling. With Precise Shot, she can fire through friendlies, past grappling allies, and into dogpile situations without accuracy loss.*

### Two-Weapon Fighting

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Combat |
| **Source** | Core Rulebook |
| **Prerequisites** | Dex 15 |

**Benefit**: Your penalties on attack rolls for fighting with two weapons are reduced. The penalty for your primary hand lessens by 2 and the one for your off hand lessens by 6.

**Effective**: Instead of -6/-10 (primary/off-hand) penalty for TWF, Lara takes **-4/-4**. Further reduced to **-2/-2** because pistols count as light weapons for TWF (per firearm rules).

*The foundation of dual-pistol combat. Without TWF, Lara couldn't attack with both pistols as part of the same full attack action.*

### Deadly Aim

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Combat |
| **Source** | Core Rulebook |
| **Prerequisites** | Dex 13, base attack bonus +1 |

**Benefit**: You can choose to take a -1 penalty on all ranged attack rolls and combat maneuver checks to gain a +2 bonus on all ranged damage rolls. When your base attack bonus reaches +4, and every 4 points thereafter, the penalty increases by -1 and the bonus to damage increases by +2.

**At BAB +16**: **-4 attack / +8 damage**.

*The ranged-weapon analog of Power Attack. Normally the attack loss would hurt, but firearms hit Touch AC within the first range increment, and Lara's attack bonus is so high that -4 means nothing against the low touch ACs of most enemies. The +8 per shot × 8 shots = +64 damage per round. Mandatory.*

### Rapid Shot

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Combat |
| **Source** | Core Rulebook |
| **Prerequisites** | Dex 13, Point-Blank Shot |

**Benefit**: When making a full-attack action with a ranged weapon, you can fire one additional time this round at your highest base attack bonus. All of your attack rolls take a -2 penalty when using Rapid Shot.

**Effective**: +1 extra shot per full attack at main-hand highest BAB. All attacks (both hands) take -2.

*Cheap attack-per-round increase. Combined with Haste (+1 extra main-hand attack), Lara gets two "bonus" shots per full attack on top of her 6 iteratives (3 main + 3 off). Total: 8 shots per round.*

### Improved Two-Weapon Fighting

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Combat |
| **Source** | Core Rulebook |
| **Prerequisites** | Dex 17, Two-Weapon Fighting, base attack bonus +6 |

**Benefit**: In addition to the standard single extra attack you get with an off-hand weapon, you get a second attack with it, albeit at a -5 penalty.

**Effective**: 2nd off-hand attack at +11 (same-as-iterative logic: -5 from highest off-hand).

*The middle of the TWF chain. Prerequisite for Greater TWF. Adds 1 off-hand attack per round to the full attack sequence.*

### Lingering Performance

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | General |
| **Source** | Advanced Player's Guide |
| **Prerequisites** | Bardic performance class feature |

**Benefit**: The effects of your bardic performance continue for 2 rounds after you cease performing. Any round in which you start a new bardic performance or continue a performance already in effect, this bonus does not apply. The bonus rounds from lingering performance do not stack if you use another performance during those 2 rounds.

**Interaction with Archaeologist's Luck**: Per RAW ruling and the Archaeologist archetype's explicit clause ("this ability can be affected by feats and other abilities that affect a bard's bardic performance, such as Lingering Performance, and counts as bardic performance for any prerequisite or effect that requires bardic performance"), Lingering Performance fully applies to Archaeologist's Luck.

**Effective**: Each session of Archaeologist's Luck gains 2 free rounds of continued effect after Lara stops maintaining it. Over a day of 5-7 combats where she activates Luck once per combat, this adds ~10-14 free rounds of Luck — a significant budget extension.

*Essential for the archetype. Without Lingering Performance, Archaeologist's Luck runs out fast. With it, Lara's round-count is effectively doubled in practical play.*

### Greater Two-Weapon Fighting

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Combat |
| **Source** | Core Rulebook |
| **Prerequisites** | Dex 19, Improved Two-Weapon Fighting, Two-Weapon Fighting, base attack bonus +11 |

**Benefit**: You get a third attack with your off-hand weapon, albeit at a -10 penalty.

**Effective**: 3rd off-hand attack at +6 (same-as-3rd-iterative logic: -10 from highest off-hand).

*Full TWF chain completion. At level 13+ Lara has 3 main-hand iteratives and 3 off-hand iteratives, plus Rapid Shot and Haste extras, for the 8-shot full attack.*

### Signature Deed

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Grit |
| **Source** | Ultimate Combat |
| **Prerequisites** | Grit class feature, gunslinger level 11th |

**Benefit**: Choose one deed whose minimum level is at least 4 levels lower than your current gunslinger level. You can use the chosen deed without spending grit. If the chosen deed requires you to spend more than 1 grit point to perform it, you spend 1 fewer grit points to perform that deed (to a minimum of 0). Once chosen, the deed cannot be changed.

**Chosen Deed**: [Up Close and Deadly](features.md#up-close-and-deadly) (Pistolero 1st-level deed)

**Effective**: Every single pistol shot Lara fires gets **+4d6 damage** (Up Close and Deadly at level 16) **for free, with no grit expenditure**. On an 8-shot full attack, that's an average of +112 extra damage per round, sustained indefinitely.

*This is the single most impactful feat in the entire build. Signature Deed turns Up Close and Deadly from "a conditional burst damage option" into "your baseline damage profile every shot." The Pistolero archetype is specifically chosen to enable this combo — a vanilla Gunslinger wouldn't have Up Close and Deadly to Signature Deed in the first place.*

*The math: Without Signature Deed, Up Close and Deadly costs 1 grit per shot. Lara's grit pool is 4, regenerating on crits. Over an 8-shot round, she could use UC&D on maybe 4-5 shots on average. With Signature Deed, she uses it on ALL 8 shots, AND her grit pool is free for other deeds (Gunslinger's Dodge, Dead Shot, Menacing Shot, Lightning Reload buffer). The DPR increase is roughly +60 damage per round, and the quality-of-play increase is even larger (no bookkeeping).*

## Feat Count Verification

| Source | Count | Feats |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Gunslinger free (L1) | 3 | Gunsmithing, Rapid Reload (Pistol), Exotic Weapon Proficiency (Firearms) |
| Human bonus (L1) | 1 | Point-Blank Shot |
| Character feats (L1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15) | 8 | Precise Shot, TWF, Deadly Aim, Rapid Shot, ITWF, Lingering Performance, GTWF, Signature Deed |
| **Total effective feats** | **12** | (9 slot-spent + 3 free) |

Note: Gunslinger has **no regular bonus feat slots** (unlike Fighter's 9 bonus feats over 16 levels). The 3 class-granted feats at level 1 are one-time grants, not a recurring progression. This is the primary mechanical cost of choosing Gunslinger over Fighter for the primary-hand class.
