### Fate's Favored

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Faith (Pharasma) |
| **Source** | Ultimate Campaign |

The fates watch over you. Whenever you are under the effect of a luck bonus of any kind, that bonus increases by 1.

**Effective**: [Archaeologist's Luck](#archaeologists-luck) base bonus of +3 at level 11+ becomes **+4**. Any Divine Favor, Bit of Luck, or other luck-typed buff Lara picks up is similarly amplified.

*Lara doesn't trust gods, but she trusts outcomes, and the outcomes have been consistent enough over twelve years of field work that she has started leaving coins at Pharasma shrines as a form of professional courtesy. Pharasma, for her part, has never given Lara reason to doubt that the relationship is mutually beneficial. This trait is the mechanical acknowledgment of that arrangement.*

### Favored Class Bonus

**Source**: Core Rulebook (Human Bard FCB)

Add +1 to the bard's total number of bardic performance rounds per day.

**Applied to**: Bard (Archaeologist), adding +16 rounds of [Archaeologist's Luck](#archaeologists-luck) per day. With Fate's Favored active: 4 base + 4 CHA + 16 FCB = **24 rounds/day** of Luck, extended effectively by [Lingering Performance](feats.md#lingering-performance).

*This is the hidden MVP of the Human + Bard combination. Archaeologist's Luck scales poorly with level (only +1 per 6 levels to the actual bonus), but the round-count scales with every bard level via performance rounds. Sixteen free rounds over the whole character build is the difference between "Luck is a rationed resource" and "Luck is on all day."*

## Pistolero Gunslinger Features

### Grit

*Ex*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat

A gunslinger makes her mark upon the world with daring deeds. Some gunslingers claim they belong to a mystical tradition, imbuing otherwise mundane bullets with the power of fate and destiny — they call this mysterious power "grit."

At the start of each day, a gunslinger gains a number of grit points equal to her Wisdom modifier (minimum 1). Her grit goes up or down throughout the day, but usually cannot go higher than her Wisdom modifier (minimum 1), though some feats and magic items may affect this maximum. A gunslinger spends grit to accomplish deeds, and regains grit in the following ways:

- **Critical Hit with a Firearm**: Each time the gunslinger confirms a critical hit with a firearm while in the heat of combat, she regains 1 grit point. Confirming a critical hit on a helpless or unaware creature or a creature that has fewer hit dice than half the gunslinger's character level does not restore grit.
- **Killing Blow with a Firearm**: When the gunslinger reduces a creature to 0 or fewer hit points with a firearm attack while in the heat of combat, she regains 1 grit point.

**Lara's Pool**: WIS 12 base + 6 (Headband of Mental Superiority) = 18 total (+4 mod). Enhancement bonuses to ability scores raise the modifier for all purposes, including grit pool calculation. **Grit pool = 4**.

*With 4 grit regenerating on every crit (pistols crit on a 20/×4) and every killing blow, Lara's pool is effectively infinite in any combat that lasts more than 2 rounds. Signature Deed applied to [Up Close and Deadly](#up-close-and-deadly) means her main damage-boost deed costs no grit at all, leaving her pool free for reactive deeds.*

### Deeds

*Various*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat

Gunslingers spend grit points to accomplish deeds. Most deeds grant the gunslinger a benefit for a limited time or a single use of a special action. Some deeds stay active as long as the gunslinger has at least 1 grit point. A gunslinger can only perform deeds of her level or lower. Unless otherwise noted, a deed can be performed multiple successive times, as long as the gunslinger has the grit to perform it.

Lara's deeds, in order of utility:

---

### Up Close and Deadly

*Pistolero 1st-level deed*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat (Pistolero archetype)

At 1st level, when the pistolero makes a successful firearm attack with a one-handed firearm against a target within the first range increment of her firearm, she can spend 1 grit point to deal 1d6 additional points of damage on a successful hit. If she misses with the attack, she grits her teeth and only loses 1 grit point. This is precision damage and is not multiplied if the pistolero scores a critical hit. This precision damage increases by 1d6 for every 4 levels beyond 1st (to a maximum of 5d6 at 17th level).

**At Lara's level 16**: **+4d6** (avg 14) per pistol shot.

**With [Signature Deed](feats.md#signature-deed)**: **Lara spends ZERO grit on this deed.** Every single pistol shot gets +4d6 damage for free, no resource expenditure. On an 8-shot full attack, this is ~112 bonus damage per round.

*This is the cornerstone of Lara's entire damage output. The Pistolero archetype trades a number of general Gunslinger features specifically to get this deed, and Signature Deed (taken at level 15) removes the grit cost entirely. The net effect: Lara has a baseline damage profile that looks like a high-crit sneak attack rogue, but applied to every shot from a ranged weapon that hits touch AC.*

### Quick Clear

*Gunslinger 1st-level deed*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat

At 1st level, as a standard action, the gunslinger can remove the broken condition from a single firearm she is currently wielding, as long as she has at least 1 grit point. In order to use this deed, the gunslinger must have the appropriate tools for repairing the firearm (typically her [Gunsmithing](feats.md#gunsmithing) kit). Alternatively, if the gunslinger spends 1 grit point to perform this deed, she can perform quick clear as a move-equivalent action instead of a standard action.

**At Lara's level**: Fix a misfire as a move action by spending 1 grit, or as a standard action for free as long as she has 1+ grit point.

*Firearms misfire on a natural 1 attack roll. This happens roughly once per 20 shots. Quick Clear is Lara's contingency plan — if a primary pistol jams, she either spends a move action + 1 grit to fix it immediately, or she draws a backup pistol from her bandolier (free action with Quick Draw, or a move action without) and switches. Misfire management is why she carries six backup pistols.*

### Gunslinger's Dodge

*Gunslinger 1st-level deed*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat

At 1st level, the gunslinger gains an uncanny knack for getting out of the way of ranged attacks. When a ranged attack is made against the gunslinger, she can spend 1 grit point to move 5 feet as an immediate action; doing so grants the gunslinger a +2 bonus to AC against the triggering attack. This movement is not a 5-foot step, and provokes attacks of opportunity. Alternatively, the gunslinger can drop prone to gain a +4 bonus to AC against the triggering attack. The gunslinger can only perform this deed while wearing medium or light armor, and while carrying no more than a medium load.

**At Lara's level**: Immediate action, 1 grit, +2 AC vs a single incoming ranged attack (or +4 AC by dropping prone). Can be used once per incoming attack.

*Lara's panic button. When an enemy archer or caster lines up a shot that will hit, she burns 1 grit to step out of the way. At 4 grit base with constant regeneration, she can use this 4-8 times per combat.*

### Gunslinger Initiative

*Gunslinger 1st-level deed*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat

At 1st level, as long as the gunslinger has at least 1 grit point, she gains the following benefits: First, she gains a +2 bonus on initiative checks. Furthermore, if she has the Quick Draw feat, her hands are free and unrestrained, and the firearm is not hidden, she can draw a single firearm as part of the initiative check.

**Effective**: Passive +2 initiative as long as Lara has 1+ grit (she basically always does, since grit regenerates on crits and killing blows). If she has [Quick Draw](feats.md#quick-draw-combat-trick) (which she does via her Combat Trick rogue talent at L4), she can draw her pistol as part of her initiative check with no additional action cost.

**Total initiative**: +8 DEX + 2 Reactionary + 2 Gunslinger Initiative = **+12 baseline**. Enough to go first against essentially any encounter-level threat.

*Lara goes first. She has always gone first. She will always go first. This is a structural feature of her identity — her reflexes are the product of twenty years of drills and a traumatic childhood that taught her never to be the second person to draw.*

### Pistol-Whip

*Gunslinger 1st-level deed*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat

At 1st level, the gunslinger can make a surprise melee attack with the butt or handle of her firearm as a standard action. When she does, she is considered to be proficient with the firearm as a melee weapon and gains a bonus on the attack and damage rolls equal to the enhancement bonus of the firearm. The damage dealt by the pistol-whip is of the bludgeoning type, and is based on the size of the firearm. One-handed firearms deal 1d6 points of damage (1d4 if wielded by Small creatures), and two-handed firearms deal 1d10 points of damage (1d8 if wielded by Small creatures). Regardless of the firearm's size, its critical multiplier is 20/×2. If the attack hits, the gunslinger can make a combat maneuver check to knock the target prone as a free action. Performing this deed costs 1 grit point.

*Rarely used. When someone gets within 5 feet and she doesn't want to waste a shot, she strikes them with the pistol butt. 1d6 bludgeoning plus a free trip attempt. It's a contingency, not a strategy.*

### Dead Shot

*Gunslinger 7th-level deed*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat

At 7th level, as a full-round action, the gunslinger can take careful aim and pool all of her attack potential into a single, deadly shot. When she does this, she shoots the firearm at a single target, but makes as many attack rolls as she can, based on her base attack bonus. She makes the attack rolls in order from highest bonus to lowest, as if she were making a full attack. If any of the attack rolls hit the target, her single attack is considered to have hit. For each additional attack roll beyond the first that hits the target, she increases the damage of the attack by the base damage dice of the firearm. For example, if a 7th-level gunslinger firing a pistol hits with both attack rolls, she does 2d8 points of damage with the shot, plus twice her Dexterity modifier, plus any modifiers from the firearm. Precision damage and extra damage from weapon special abilities (such as flaming) are added with the first hit and are not multiplied for multiple hits. Performing this deed costs 1 grit point.

**At Lara's level 16**: Base attack rolls = +16/+11/+6 = 3 attack rolls. Plus Rapid Shot = 4 rolls. Plus Haste = 5 rolls. She makes 5 attack rolls against one target, and every hit adds 1d8 to the single shot's damage. On a full hit: 5d8 weapon damage + full modifiers + Up Close and Deadly + crit potential. Avg 160-180 damage in **one attack**.

*The boss-killer. Dead Shot is single-target DPR. Instead of 8 shots at 41 damage each (332 total), Lara fires one impossibly precise shot dealing ~180 damage that counts as a single attack for crit purposes (potentially x4 multiplier = 720 damage on crit). Use this against high-HP bosses with heavy DR that would eat normal shot damage.*

### Lightning Reload

*Gunslinger 11th-level deed*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat

At 11th level, as long as the gunslinger has at least 1 grit point, she can reload a single barrel of a one-handed or two-handed firearm as a free action each round. Reloading in this way still provokes attacks of opportunity. Alternatively, the gunslinger can spend 1 grit point to reload a single barrel of any firearm she is wielding as a free action, and this free action does not provoke attacks of opportunity.

**Effective**: Lara gets 1 free-action reload per round (both pistols eligible) as long as she has 1+ grit. She can burn 1 additional grit per reload to avoid provoking AoOs.

**Critical interpretation**: Most tables rule that Lightning Reload bypasses the "free hand" requirement for reloading, because the intent of the deed is clearly to enable dual-wield gunslinger builds. Combined with Rapid Reload + alchemical cartridges (which handle the remaining reloads via the "transient free hand" interpretation during full attacks), Lara can sustain an 8-shot full attack every round.

*Without Lightning Reload, a dual-pistol Gunslinger caps out at ~3 shots per round. With it, she full-attacks indefinitely. This is the feature that unlocks the entire build.*

### Menacing Shot

*Gunslinger 11th-level deed*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat

At 11th level, the gunslinger can spend 1 grit point and shoot a firearm into the air to cause those within 30 feet of her to become shaken for a number of rounds equal to her gunslinger level. This is a mind-affecting fear effect, and a successful Will save (DC 10 + 1/2 her gunslinger level + her Wisdom modifier) negates the effect.

**At Lara's level 16**: DC 10 + 8 + 4 = **DC 22**. Shaken for 16 rounds on failed Will save. Area: 30-ft radius.

*AoE fear + intimidation. Useful when Lara wants to debuff a group before closing with the frontline. Shaken enemies have -2 attack, saves, skill checks, and ability checks.*

### Targeting

*Gunslinger 7th-level deed*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat

At 7th level, as a full-round action, the gunslinger can make a single firearm attack and choose part of the body to target. She gains the following effects depending on the part of the body targeted. If a creature does not have one of the listed body locations, that part cannot be targeted. Creatures that are immune to sneak attacks are immune to these effects. Performing this deed costs 1 grit point.

- **Arms**: On a hit, the target takes no damage but drops one carried item of the gunslinger's choice, even if the item is wielded with two hands.
- **Head**: On a hit, the target is confused for 1 round. This is a mind-affecting effect.
- **Legs**: On a hit, the target is knocked prone. Creatures with four or more legs or that are immune to trip attacks are immune to this effect.
- **Torso**: Targeting the torso threatens a critical on a 19-20.
- **Wings**: On a hit, the target must make a Reflex save (DC 10 + half the gunslinger's level + her Dex modifier) or fall. Creatures immune to difficult terrain effects are immune to this effect.

*Lara's surgical option. Against a flying enemy caster: target wings → they fall. Against a melee brute with a signature weapon: target arms → they drop it. Against a mounted knight: target legs on the horse → knight falls. Each costs 1 grit and a full round action (so no multi-shot that turn), but the debuff can define the fight.*

### Nimble

*Ex*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat

Starting at 2nd level, a gunslinger gains a +1 dodge bonus to AC while wearing light or no armor. Anything that causes the gunslinger to lose her Dexterity bonus to AC also causes the gunslinger to lose this dodge bonus. This bonus increases by +1 for every four levels beyond 2nd (to a maximum of +5 at 18th level).

**At Lara's level 16**: +1 (L2) + 1 (L6) + 1 (L10) + 1 (L14) = **+4 dodge bonus** to AC. Lost when flat-footed or in medium/heavy armor. Lara wears Mithral Chain Shirt (light), so Nimble is always active.

*The Gunslinger's core defensive feature. Lara's "heavy armor" is a dodge bonus baked into her class — she stays light and fast and lets her trained instincts do the work that plate mail would do for someone slower.*

### Pistol Training

*Ex*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat (Pistolero archetype)

Starting at 3rd level, a pistolero becomes more accurate and deadly with one-handed firearms. She gains a +1 bonus on damage rolls, and her critical multiplier when using one-handed firearms increases... wait no. Let me get this right.

At 5th level, a pistolero increases her damage with one-handed firearms. When firing a one-handed firearm, she adds her Dexterity modifier to the damage dealt. She gains a +1 bonus on damage dealt with one-handed firearms at 5th level, plus an additional +1 for every four levels beyond 5th. At 5th level, she can also spend 1 grit point when she misfires with a one-handed firearm to have that firearm only gain the broken condition rather than misfire.

**At Lara's level 16**: **+8 DEX to damage** (DEX mod) + **+3 bonus damage** (base +1 at 5, +1 at 9, +1 at 13) = **+11 flat damage per pistol shot** from Pistol Training alone. Additionally, misfires can be downgraded to "broken condition only" for 1 grit.

**This is the Pistolero's signature class feature.** Vanilla Gunslinger gets Gun Training at level 5, which adds DEX to damage with one firearm group. Pistolero's Pistol Training is similar but stacks +1 damage scaling on top and specifically targets one-handed firearms (pistols).

*Without this feature, Lara's damage per shot would be roughly 2/3 of what it is. Pistol Training is why she invested in Pistolero over vanilla Gunslinger — it's the single biggest damage amplifier in the archetype's kit.*

### Gunsmith

*Ex*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat

At 1st level, a gunslinger gains one of the following firearms of her choice: blunderbuss, musket, or pistol. Her starting weapon is battered, and only she knows how to use it properly. All other creatures treat her starting weapon as if it had the broken condition. If the weapon already has the broken condition, it does not work at all for anyone else. This starting weapon can only be sold for scrap (it's worth 4d10 gp when sold). The gunslinger also gains [Gunsmithing](feats.md#gunsmithing) as a bonus feat.

**Lara's starting weapon**: She chose a pistol. It's long since been replaced by her current +5 pistols, but she keeps the original in a drawer at the Croft estate as a keepsake — the first thing her father taught her to disassemble when she was nine years old.

## Archaeologist Bard Features

### Bardic Knowledge

*Ex*

**Source**: Core Rulebook

A bard adds half his class level (minimum 1) to all Knowledge skill checks and may make all Knowledge skill checks untrained.

**At Lara's level 16**: **+8** to all Knowledge checks.

*The foundation of Lara's identity as an archaeologist. Combined with maxed ranks, INT 19, class skill bonuses, and [Lore Master](#lore-master), her Knowledge totals are in the +30-31 range across all nine Knowledge fields. She can take 10 on any of them. She identifies almost anything she encounters.*

### Archaeologist's Luck

*Su (Bardic Performance variant)*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat (Archaeologist archetype)

The archaeologist does not gain the standard bardic performance ability; instead, she gains the archaeologist's luck ability. The archaeologist picks up tricks and uncanny luck on her travels. Using this ability is a swift action, but maintaining it each round is a free action. While maintaining this performance, the archaeologist gains a +1 luck bonus on attack rolls, weapon damage rolls, saving throws, and skill checks. This bonus increases by +1 at 5th level and every six levels beyond 5th (11th, 17th).

Like a bard, the archaeologist can use this ability for a number of rounds per day equal to 4 + her Charisma modifier. At each level after 1st, an archaeologist can use this ability for 2 additional rounds per day.

Additionally, this ability can be affected by feats and other abilities that affect a bard's bardic performance, such as [Lingering Performance](feats.md#lingering-performance), and counts as bardic performance for any prerequisite or effect that requires bardic performance.

**At Lara's level 16**:
- **Bonus**: +3 luck (base +1, +1 at 5, +1 at 11) → **+4 with Fate's Favored trait**
- **Rounds/day**:
  - Base: 4 + 4 CHA = 8 rounds
  - Level scaling (+2/level after 1st): 2 × 15 = 30 rounds
  - Human FCB (+1/level): 16 rounds
  - **Total base: 54 rounds/day**
- With [Lingering Performance](feats.md#lingering-performance): each activation gains +2 free continuation rounds. Across ~5 activations per day, that's +10 bonus rounds. **Effective total: ~64 rounds/day.**

That's enough for 10+ full combats of continuous Luck. Lara never runs out.

*This is the Bard's contribution to the build, and it's enormous. A +4 bonus to attack, damage, saves, and skills — applying to all 8 of her shots per round, plus Fortitude/Reflex/Will saves, plus every skill check — is a universal multiplier on everything Lara does. At level 16 with Fate's Favored, she essentially adds a permanent +4 to every die she rolls, limited only by round count. And with FCB + scaling rounds + Lingering Performance, the round limit is functionally irrelevant.*

### Clever Explorer

*Ex*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat (Archaeologist archetype)

At 2nd level, an archaeologist becomes a master of traps and locks. She adds 1/2 her level on Disable Device checks and Perception checks (minimum +1). She can disable intricate and complex devices in half the normal time (minimum 1 round) and open a lock as a standard action. At 6th level, she can take 10 on Disable Device checks even while distracted or threatened, and can use Disable Device to disarm magical traps.

**Replaces**: Versatile Performance.

**At Lara's level 16**: **+8** to Disable Device and Perception from this feature alone (on top of ranks, DEX/WIS, class skill, Bardic Knowledge note: Bardic Knowledge is Knowledge-only). Can take 10 on Disable Device while distracted. Can disarm magical traps (this is HUGE — normally a rogue-only ability).

*This is the single most archaeologist-flavored class feature in the game. The ability to disarm magical traps is what elevates Lara from "adventurer with high Perception" to "this is her actual job." The downside is losing Versatile Performance — she can't substitute Perform checks for Acrobatics/Sense Motive/etc., so she has to buy those skill ranks directly. Given her skill density, that's an acceptable trade.*

### Uncanny Dodge

*Ex*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat (Archaeologist archetype) / Rogue class feature

At 2nd level, an archaeologist gains the ability to react to danger before her senses would normally allow her to do so. She cannot be caught flat-footed, nor does she lose her Dex bonus to AC if the attacker is invisible. She still loses her Dexterity bonus to AC if immobilized. An archaeologist with this ability can still lose her Dexterity bonus to AC if an opponent successfully uses the feint action against her.

**Replaces**: Well-Versed.

**Effective**: Lara is never flat-footed. Invisible attackers don't catch her off-guard. She keeps her full +8 DEX and +4 Nimble to AC even in situations that would deny them to other characters.

*Combined with Evasion and Trap Sense, Uncanny Dodge is why Lara walks out of ambushes alive. Her awareness is trained at a level where "surprise" is a word she understands academically but does not experience.*

### Trap Sense

*Ex*

**Source**: Core Rulebook (Rogue class feature, granted to Archaeologist)

At 3rd level, an archaeologist gains an intuitive sense that alerts her to danger from traps, giving her a +1 bonus on Reflex saves made to avoid traps and a +1 dodge bonus to AC against attacks made by traps. These bonuses rise to +2 when the archaeologist reaches 6th level, to +3 when she reaches 9th level, to +4 when she reaches 12th level, and to +5 when she reaches 15th level.

**At Lara's level 16**: **+5 Reflex save vs traps, +5 dodge AC vs traps**.

*The archaeologist's specific-scenario defense. Traps are the signature encounter type for her field, and Trap Sense ensures that the ones she doesn't spot still don't kill her.*

### Lore Master

*Ex*

**Source**: Core Rulebook

At 5th level, a bard becomes a master of lore and can take 10 on any Knowledge skill check that he has ranks in. A bard can choose not to take 10 and can instead roll normally. In addition, once per day, the bard can take 20 on any Knowledge skill check as a standard action. He can use this ability one additional time per day for every six levels he possesses beyond 5th, to a maximum of three times per day at 17th level.

**At Lara's level 16**: Take 10 on any Knowledge check she has ranks in. Take 20 **2/day** (3/day at L17).

*When Lara has ranks in a Knowledge (which she does for all nine varieties), she takes 10 automatically. Her take-10 result on K(History) is 10 + 31 = 41. That's enough to identify any ancient civilization, cult, or lost empire in the Bestiary. Take 20 is reserved for research sessions where she needs to crack a specific artifact's mystery.*

### Rogue Talents

*Varies*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat (Archaeologist archetype)

At 4th level, and every 4 levels thereafter, an archaeologist gains a rogue talent of her choice. This ability replaces inspire courage +1 and versatile performance at 6th level.

Lara gains talents at **4, 8, 12, 16**.

**Lara's Talent Selections**:

- **Level 4 — Combat Trick**: Grants a bonus combat feat. Lara chose **Quick Draw** to expedite pistol management and backup weapon deployment.
- **Level 8 — Ledge Walker**: Allows her to move along narrow surfaces at full speed without needing Acrobatics checks. This is mechanical for the "she climbs and balances across crumbling ruins" visual.
- **Level 12 — Finesse Rogue**: Grants Weapon Finesse as a bonus feat. Unnecessary for her pistol work but useful for pistol-whipping and any melee scenario.
- **Level 16 — Dispelling Attack** (advanced): Allows her sneak attacks to function as a targeted dispel magic on the victim. Lara doesn't have traditional sneak attack dice, but Up Close and Deadly counts as precision damage for this purpose per community ruling. When she lands a shot on a buffed enemy caster, it strips one of their active spells.

*The Rogue Talent tree lets Lara customize the "adventurer skill set" on top of the core Pistolero+Bard chassis. Quick Draw + Ledge Walker are her defining utility picks.*

### Evasion

*Ex*

**Source**: Core Rulebook (Rogue class feature, granted to Archaeologist)

At 6th level, an archaeologist can avoid damage from many area-effect attacks. If she makes a successful Reflex saving throw against an attack that normally deals half damage on a successful save, she instead takes no damage. Evasion can be used only if the archaeologist is wearing light armor or no armor. A helpless archaeologist does not gain the benefit of evasion.

*Combined with her Reflex save of +23, Lara fails only on a natural 1 against most AoE effects. Fireballs, lightning bolts, dragon breath weapons — she walks out of them untouched.*

### Jack of All Trades

*Ex*

**Source**: Core Rulebook

At 10th level, the bard can use any skill, even if the skill normally requires him to be trained. At 16th level, the bard considers all skills to be class skills. At 19th level, the bard can take 10 on any skill check, even if it is not normally allowed.

**At Lara's level 16**: She can use any skill untrained, AND every skill is a class skill (granting her the +3 class skill bonus on top of ranks).

*The ultimate utility ability. At level 16, every skill in the game is a class skill for Lara. Combined with her existing skill ranks, her non-maxed skills all gain +3 retroactively. This is a significant buff to her Climb, Swim, Sense Motive, Diplomacy, etc.*

### Cantrips

*Sp*

**Source**: Core Rulebook (Bard class feature)

Bards learn a number of cantrips, or 0-level spells, as noted on the spells known table. These spells are cast like any other spell, but they do not consume any slots and may be used again.

*Lara knows 6 cantrips: [Detect Magic](spells.md#detect-magic), [Read Magic](spells.md#read-magic), [Prestidigitation](spells.md#prestidigitation), [Mage Hand](spells.md#mage-hand), [Ghost Sound](spells.md#ghost-sound), [Light](spells.md#light). All six are unlimited-use utility spells. Detect Magic and Read Magic are essential for archaeological work; Mage Hand lets her manipulate objects at distance (pressure plates, distant keys); Ghost Sound is distraction; Light is a torch that doesn't burn out.*
