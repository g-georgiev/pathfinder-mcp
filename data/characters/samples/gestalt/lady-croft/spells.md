### Read Magic

| | |
|---|---|
| **School** | Divination |
| **Level** | Bard 0, Cleric 0, Druid 0, Paladin 1, Ranger 1, Sorcerer/Wizard 0 |
| **Casting Time** | 1 standard action |
| **Components** | V, S, F (a clear crystal or mineral prism) |
| **Range** | Personal |
| **Target** | You |
| **Duration** | 10 min./level |
| **Save** | None |
| **SR** | No |

You can decipher magical inscriptions on objects — books, scrolls, weapons, and the like — that would otherwise be unintelligible. This deciphering does not normally invoke the magic contained in the writing, although it may do so in the case of a cursed or trapped scroll.

*Essential for an archaeologist. Every Thassilonian runestone, every Osirian burial inscription, every cryptic warning on a sealed tomb door — Read Magic lets her parse the magical component even when the ancient language itself is beyond her. Paired with [Comprehend Languages](#comprehend-languages) for the linguistic side, she can read any inscription she finds.*

### Grease

| | |
|---|---|
| **School** | Conjuration (creation) |
| **Level** | Bard 1, Sorcerer/Wizard 1 |
| **Casting Time** | 1 standard action |
| **Components** | V, S, M (butter) |
| **Range** | Close (25 ft. + 5 ft./2 levels) |
| **Target/Area** | One object or 10-ft. square |
| **Duration** | 1 min./level (D) |
| **Save** | See text |
| **SR** | No |

A grease spell covers a solid surface with a layer of slippery grease. Any creature in the area when the spell is cast must make a successful Reflex save or fall. This save is repeated on each round that the creature remains within the area. A creature can walk within or through the area of grease at half normal speed with a DC 10 Acrobatics check. Failure means it can't move that round (and must then make a Reflex save or fall), while failure by 5 or more means it falls.

**DC**: 15 (10 + 1 + 4 CHA)

*Area denial. Cast in a doorway to block pursuing enemies, on a ramp to turn it into a slide, or on a specific enemy's weapon grip to disarm them. The spell also greases objects, making grapple checks against a greased Lara much harder.*

### Saving Finale

| | |
|---|---|
| **School** | Evocation [sonic] |
| **Level** | Bard 1 |
| **Casting Time** | 1 immediate action |
| **Components** | V, S |
| **Range** | Close (25 ft. + 5 ft./2 levels) |
| **Target** | One ally currently benefiting from your bardic performance |
| **Duration** | Instantaneous |
| **Save** | Will negates (harmless) |
| **SR** | Yes |

The target ally can immediately reroll a failed saving throw. The ally must take the result of the second save, even if it is lower. Casting this spell does not disrupt your bardic performance. This spell's effect ends your bardic performance and your ally cannot be affected by your bardic performance for 1 round.

**Important interaction**: Lara is her own "ally" for spell-targeting purposes, meaning she can cast this on herself. When she fails a critical save, Saving Finale gives her an immediate reroll.

*The "don't-die" button. Cast on yourself (or an ally) as an immediate action when a failed save is about to kill someone. Costs 1st-level slot and 1 round of Archaeologist's Luck. Well worth it.*

## 2nd-Level Spells (6/day)

### Glitterdust

| | |
|---|---|
| **School** | Conjuration (creation) |
| **Level** | Bard 2, Magus 2, Sorcerer/Wizard 2, Summoner 2, Witch 2 |
| **Casting Time** | 1 standard action |
| **Components** | V, S, M (ground mica) |
| **Range** | Medium (100 ft. + 10 ft./level) |
| **Effect** | Creatures and objects within 10-ft.-radius spread |
| **Duration** | 1 round/level |
| **Save** | Will negates (blinding only) |
| **SR** | No |

A cloud of golden particles covers everyone and everything in the area, causing creatures to become blinded and visibly outlining invisible things for the duration of the spell. All within the area are covered by the dust, which cannot be removed and continues to sparkle until it fades. Any creature covered by the dust takes a –40 penalty on Stealth checks.

Any creature in the area at the time of casting must make a Will save or be blinded for the spell's duration.

**DC**: 16 (10 + 2 + 4 CHA)
**No SR** — works against outsiders and dragons.
**At Lara's CL**: 16 rounds duration.

*The single best anti-invisibility and anti-caster spell in the Bard's toolkit. Glitterdust doesn't care about spell resistance, the save DC is relevant but forgiving, and the blinded condition (on failed saves) is devastating: -2 attack, half speed, 50% miss chance, loses DEX to AC. Lara opens many fights with this.*

## 3rd-Level Spells (5/day + 1 CHA bonus = 6)

### Haste

| | |
|---|---|
| **School** | Transmutation |
| **Level** | Bard 3, Magus 3, Sorcerer/Wizard 3 |
| **Casting Time** | 1 standard action |
| **Components** | V, S, M (a shaving of licorice root) |
| **Range** | Close (25 ft. + 5 ft./2 levels) |
| **Targets** | One creature/level, no two of which can be more than 30 ft. apart |
| **Duration** | 1 round/level |
| **Save** | Fortitude negates (harmless) |
| **SR** | Yes (harmless) |

The transmuted creatures move and act more quickly than normal.

When making a full attack action, a hasted creature may make one extra attack with any weapon it is holding. The attack is made using the creature's full base attack bonus, plus any modifiers appropriate to the situation.

All of the hasted creature's modes of movement increase by 30 feet.

A hasted creature gains a +1 bonus on attack rolls and a +1 dodge bonus to AC and Reflex saves.

**At CL 16**: 16 rounds, up to 16 creatures.

*Mandatory party buff. Lara opens every serious fight with Haste — the extra attack per round applies to her dual-pistol full attack (giving her one more main-hand shot), the +1 AC is a flat defensive upgrade, and the party-wide scope makes it a force multiplier on everyone. Bard 3 is typically auto-include-Haste, and Lara is no exception.*
