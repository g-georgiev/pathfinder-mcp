## Character Traits

### Magical Lineage

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Magic |
| **Source** | Ultimate Campaign |

One of your parents was a gifted spellcaster who not only used metamagic often, but also developed many magical items and perhaps even a new spell or two—and you have inherited a fragment of this greatness. Pick one spell when you choose this trait. When you apply metamagic feats to this spell that add at least 1 level to the spell, treat its actual level as 1 lower for determining the spell's final adjusted level.

**Chosen Spell**: [Shocking Grasp](spells.md#shocking-grasp)

*Yusuf's father, the lamb-seller, was quietly a middling evoker who never opened a spellbook in public but who used to season his stews with the faintest whispered syllables of evocation. The boy inherited the knack — his Shocking Grasp carries the metamagic weight of spells one rung higher than it should. Stacks with [Wayang Spellhunter](#wayang-spellhunter) for a total -2 to metamagic adjustment on this single spell.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Regional (Sorshen's Isle / Jungle) |
| **Source** | Dragon Empires Gazetteer |

You grew up in a region where wayangs were common, and they taught you their practices for enhancing spells against their shadow-touched foes. Choose one spell when you gain this trait. **Chosen Spell**: [Shocking Grasp](spells.md#shocking-grasp)

*Yusuf traveled a year in the Xa Hoi jungle during his College of Mysteries practicum, ostensibly to study wayang metamagic techniques. He brought back the trait, three new cooking recipes, and a permanent immunity to chili peppers most grown men find intolerable. The discount stacks with Magical Lineage.*

## Kensai Magus Features

### Canny Defense

*Su*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat (Kensai archetype)

At 1st level, when a kensai is wielding his chosen weapon, he gains the canny defense ability. This is identical to the duelist prestige class ability of the same name, using his Intelligence modifier and treating his magus level as his duelist level.

**Duelist Canny Defense**: When wearing no armor and not using a shield, a kensai adds 1 point of Intelligence bonus (if any) per magus level as a dodge bonus to his Armor Class while wielding a melee weapon. If a kensai is caught flat-footed or otherwise denied his Dexterity bonus, he also loses this bonus.

**Effective Bonus**: **+10 AC** (INT mod, cap = magus level 16 = 16 max, not reached).

*This is the heart of the build. Canny Defense is a DODGE bonus, which means (a) it applies to touch AC, (b) it stacks with every other dodge bonus, and (c) it is lost when flat-footed — but [Iaijutsu](#iaijutsu) lets Yusuf make AoOs while flat-footed and [Forewarned](#forewarned) makes him act in surprise rounds, so he is almost never flat-footed in practice. In narrative terms: his awareness of the kitchen is the same awareness that lets him step out of a knife's path without appearing to move.*

### Weapon Focus (Scimitar)

**Source**: Ultimate Combat (Kensai archetype)

A kensai must choose a single weapon to be his favored weapon. This can be any melee weapon that can be used with the Weapon Finesse feat (including unarmed strike) or any sword (including those that cannot be used with Weapon Finesse). At 1st level, a kensai gains Weapon Focus with his chosen weapon as a bonus feat, even if he would not normally qualify for the feat.

**Chosen Weapon**: Scimitar (*Aisha*)

*Yusuf has owned exactly one weapon his entire adult life. It was his grandmother's. The Kensai archetype gives you mechanical reward for that kind of singular devotion — Weapon Focus is the smallest of those rewards, but it is the one you notice on every single attack roll for the rest of the character's career.*

### Perfect Strike

*Ex*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat (Kensai archetype)

At 4th level, when the kensai hits an opponent with his chosen weapon, he may spend 1 point from his arcane pool as a swift action to maximize his weapon damage (do not include extra dice from weapon properties such as *flaming* or *shock*, or from precision-based damage like sneak attack). If the kensai confirms a critical hit, he may instead spend 2 points from his arcane pool to increase the weapon's critical hit multiplier by 1 (×2 becomes ×3).

Replaces **Spell Recall**.

*Kensai trades the ability to refund spells for the ability to say "this cut must be perfect" and have the universe agree. For a level 16 character who rarely runs out of slots, this is a good trade. Use it when you absolutely need a specific enemy to die on this specific attack — typically a finishing blow on a wounded boss, or the critical confirmation on a nova round.*

### Fighter Training

*Ex*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat (Kensai archetype)

Starting at 7th level, a kensai counts 1/2 his magus level -3 as his fighter level for the purpose of qualifying for feats (minimum 1). If he has levels in fighter, these levels stack. He only gains this benefit with regard to his chosen weapon.

At magus level 16: counts as fighter level 5 (16/2 -3 = 5).

Replaces **Knowledge Pool**.

*Minor utility at this level. [Critical Perfection](#critical-perfection) does most of the heavy lifting for Critical Focus qualification.*

### Iaijutsu

*Ex*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat (Kensai archetype)

At 7th level, a kensai adds his Intelligence modifier on initiative checks. In addition, he is not considered flat-footed while using his chosen weapon before he has acted in combat. He may also draw his chosen weapon as a free action, even as part of an attack of opportunity.

Replaces **Medium Armor**.

**Effective Bonus**: **+10 to initiative** (INT mod). Not flat-footed before acting in combat. Free-action draw.

*The whole archetype lives in this feature. Initiative is the single most important number in Pathfinder combat — going first means you get to buff, reposition, or kill a threat before it becomes a threat. A +10 stacking initiative bonus on top of Forewarned (+8) is absurd. And the "not flat-footed before acting" clause means your full AC is up from the moment the surprise round starts.*

*Narratively: Yusuf's kitchen awareness. He has been slicing, turning, and seasoning in parallel since he was a child — nothing in combat moves faster than a well-run dinner rush.*

### Critical Perfection

*Ex*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat (Kensai archetype)

At 9th level, a kensai adds his Intelligence bonus (minimum 0) on all critical hit confirmation rolls with his chosen weapon (this bonus stacks with the bonus from Critical Focus). The kensai can use his magus level as his fighter level for the purpose of meeting the prerequisites for Critical Focus and the feats that have Critical Focus as a prerequisite. He cannot use this ability to meet the prerequisites of feats that modify or change the effects of a critical hit of a weapon other than his chosen weapon.

This ability replaces the magus arcana gained at 9th level.

**Effective Bonus**: **+10 to confirm critical hits** with scimitar. Qualifies for Critical Focus and critical feats as if fighter level = magus level (16).

*With a 15-20 threat range on a +22 attack bonus, Yusuf's crits confirm on almost anything. Adding +10 to confirm means even against T-rex-level AC he's confirming on virtually every threat. [Prescience](#prescience) can pre-roll the confirmation die if he wants to be *sure*.*

### Superior Reflexes

*Ex*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat (Kensai archetype)

At 11th level, a kensai gains a number of attacks of opportunity per round equal to his Intelligence modifier (minimum 1). This bonus stacks with the bonus from Combat Reflexes.

Replaces **Improved Spell Recall**.

**Effective**: **10 AoOs/round** from INT, **+4 AoOs** from [Combat Reflexes](feats.md#combat-reflexes) DEX = **14 AoOs total per round**.

*Combined with [Counterstrike](#counterstrike), this turns Yusuf into a walking zone of control. Any enemy caster casting defensively within reach of his scimitar provokes an AoO the moment they finish their spell. And he has 14 of them. The kitchen does not tolerate fumbling.*

### Iaijutsu Focus

*Ex*

**Source**: Ultimate Combat (Kensai archetype)

At 13th level, a kensai is always considered to have acted in a surprise round, even if he fails to notice his enemies and cannot act during the surprise round. He can draw his favored weapon as a swift action at the start of a surprise round, even if he cannot otherwise act. Additionally, the kensai adds his Intelligence modifier on damage rolls against flat-footed opponents.

Replaces **Heavy Armor**.

*Combined with [Forewarned](#forewarned), Yusuf always acts in surprise rounds. Combined with Iaijutsu, he is never flat-footed before acting. The extra INT damage vs flat-footed opponents is a +10 damage bonus on any attack where you've beaten someone's initiative or caught them off guard — effectively +50 per round if you crit on all five attacks.*

## Core Magus Features

### Spell Combat

*Ex*

**Source**: Ultimate Magic

At 1st level, a magus learns to cast spells and wield his weapons at the same time. This functions much like two-weapon fighting, but the off-hand weapon is a spell that is being cast. To use this ability, the magus must have one hand free (even if the spell being cast does not have somatic components), while wielding a light or one-handed melee weapon in the other hand. As a full-round action, he can make all of his attacks with his melee weapon at a –2 penalty and can also cast any spell from the magus spell list with a casting time of 1 standard action (any attack roll made as part of this spell also takes this penalty). If he casts this spell defensively, he can decide to take an additional penalty on his attack rolls, up to his Intelligence bonus, and add the same amount as a circumstance bonus on his concentration check. If the check fails, the spell is wasted, but the attacks still take the penalty. A magus can choose to cast the spell first or make the weapon attacks first, but if he has more than one attack, he cannot cast the spell between weapon attacks.

**Extended to Wizard spells via [Broad Study](#broad-study).**

*This is the entire reason the gestalt exists. Both classes get their full turn in the same full-round action. The "one hand free" requirement is why Yusuf does not two-hand his scimitar — his off hand is always cooking, whether with a spell, a spice pouch, or a piece of flatbread.*

### Spellstrike

*Su*

**Source**: Ultimate Magic

At 2nd level, whenever a magus casts a spell with a range of "touch" from the magus spell list, he can deliver the spell through any weapon he is wielding as part of a melee attack. Instead of the free melee touch attack normally allowed to deliver the spell, a magus can make one free melee attack with his weapon (at his highest base attack bonus) as part of casting this spell. If successful, this melee attack deals its normal damage as well as the effects of the spell. If the magus makes this attack in concert with spell combat, this melee attack takes all the penalties accrued by spell combat melee attacks. This attack uses the weapon's critical range (20, 19–20, or 18–20 and modified by the keen weapon property or similar effects), but the spell effect only deals ×2 damage on a successful critical hit, while the weapon damage uses its own critical modifier.

**Extended to Wizard touch spells via [Broad Study](#broad-study).**

*Crit range matters enormously here. Scimitar's 18-20 + [Improved Critical](feats.md#improved-critical-scimitar) gives 15-20 — 30% chance per attack of crit-doubling the touch spell damage. On a Maximized Intensified Shocking Grasp, that's 120 guaranteed damage on a critical.*

### Arcane Pool

*Su*

**Source**: Ultimate Magic

At 1st level, the magus gains a reservoir of mystical arcane energy that he can draw upon to fuel his powers and enhance his weapon. This arcane pool has a number of points equal to 1/2 his magus level (minimum 1) + his Intelligence modifier. The pool refreshes once per day when the magus prepares his spells.

At 1st level, a magus can expend 1 point from his arcane pool as a swift action to grant any weapon he is holding a +1 enhancement bonus for 1 minute. For every four levels beyond 1st, the weapon gains another +1 enhancement bonus, to a maximum of +5 at 17th level. These bonuses can be added to the weapon, stacking with existing weapon enhancement to a maximum of +5. Multiple uses of this ability do not stack with themselves.

At 5th level, these bonuses can be used to add any of the following weapon properties: **dancing**, **flaming**, **flaming burst**, **frost**, **icy burst**, **keen**, **shock**, **shocking burst**, **speed**, or **vorpal**. Adding these properties consumes an amount of bonus equal to the property's cost. These properties are added to any the weapon already has, but duplicates do not stack.

**Pool Size**: 8 (half magus level) + 10 (INT) + 16 ([Human FCB](#bonus-feat)) = **34 points/day**

**Enhancement at Level 16**: +4 bonus available (+1 base, +3 for levels 5/9/13). Since Aisha already has a +5 enhancement, the pool's enhancement value is repurposed to add **+4 worth of properties** for 1 minute per activation.

**Recommended combat combos**:
- Pre-Haste: **Speed (+3) + Shock (+1)** = extra attack, +1d6 electric per hit
- Haste running: **Flaming Burst (+2) + Frost (+1) + Shock (+1)** = +1d6 fire (+1d10 on crits) + 1d6 cold + 1d6 electric per hit

*The arcane pool is the heat supply of the kitchen. You budget it carefully, spend it decisively, and if it runs out mid-service the dinner rush goes wrong. 34 points is enormous — Yusuf never meaningfully runs dry, especially since he also has [Arcane Accuracy](#arcane-accuracy), [Accurate Strike](#accurate-strike), and [Bane Blade](#bane-blade) to spend pool on.*

### Improved Spell Combat

*Ex*

**Source**: Ultimate Magic

At 8th level, the magus's ability to cast spells and make melee attacks improves. When using the spell combat ability, the magus receives a +2 circumstance bonus on concentration checks, in addition to any bonus granted by taking an additional penalty on the attack roll.

*Concentration checks during Spell Combat: CL 16 + INT 10 + 2 circumstance = +28 before taking the voluntary penalty. Yusuf essentially cannot fail concentration.*

### Greater Spell Combat

*Ex*

**Source**: Ultimate Magic

At 14th level, the magus gains the ability to seamlessly cast spells and make melee attacks. Whenever he uses the spell combat ability, his concentration check bonus equals double the amount of the attack penalty taken.

*Take -5 to attacks, get +10 concentration. This means Yusuf can cast spells defensively in the middle of melee without thinking. At max voluntary penalty his concentration is +36. Irrelevant against normal enemies, but meaningful when casting inside an enemy wizard's Stinking Cloud or through similar conditions that force high-DC concentration.*

### Counterstrike

*Ex*

**Source**: Ultimate Magic

At 16th level, whenever an enemy within reach of the magus successfully casts a spell defensively, that enemy provokes an attack of opportunity from the magus after the spell is complete. This attack of opportunity cannot disrupt the spell.

*The level-16 capstone before True Magus. Combined with [Superior Reflexes](#superior-reflexes) (10 AoOs) and [Combat Reflexes](feats.md#combat-reflexes) (+4 AoOs), Yusuf punishes every enemy caster who stands within 5 feet of him. The spell still resolves, but the caster eats a Spellstrike in return — which often finishes them before they get to cast again.*

## Magus Arcana

### Arcane Accuracy

*Su*

**Source**: Ultimate Magic

The magus can spend 1 point from his arcane pool as a swift action to gain an insight bonus equal to his Intelligence bonus on all attack rolls until the end of his next turn.

**Effective Bonus**: **+10 insight to attack rolls** for 1 round. 1 arcane pool point.

*The nuclear accuracy button. When Yusuf absolutely needs every attack to land — against a high-AC opponent, or on a nova round where a missed Spellstrike means losing a prepared spell — he activates this. It stacks with everything because it's an insight bonus.*

### Broad Study

*Ex*

**Source**: Ultimate Magic

The magus selects another spellcasting class he possesses. The magus can use his spell combat and spellstrike abilities when casting spells from this class's spell list. This arcana only applies to spells the magus learns from that class.

**Chosen Class**: Wizard (level 16)

**Prerequisites**: Magus 6, levels in another spellcasting class.

*This is the arcana without which the entire build doesn't exist. Picking this at magus level 6 unlocks the entire Wizard spell list for Spell Combat and Spellstrike — every touch spell on the Wizard list can now be delivered through Aisha, and every standard-action Wizard spell can be cast inside a full attack. Bestow Curse via scimitar. Calcific Touch via scimitar. Touch of Idiocy via scimitar. This is the multiplier that turns a solid gestalt into the "gold standard" gestalt.*

### Accurate Strike

*Ex*

**Source**: Ultimate Magic

The magus can spend 1 point from his arcane pool as a swift action to resolve all of his melee attacks until the end of his turn as melee touch attacks. The magus must be at least 9th level to select this arcana.

*Against high-AC opponents (CR-appropriate full-plate enemies, solars, pit fiends), this turns every attack into a touch attack. Yusuf's +22/+17/+12 becomes essentially an auto-hit against touch AC, which is typically 10-15 even on the toughest creatures. Stacks with [Arcane Accuracy](#arcane-accuracy) — use both swift actions across two rounds for consecutive alpha-strike turns.*

### Bane Blade

*Su*

**Source**: Ultimate Magic

The magus gains the ability to use the arcane pool to grant the bane weapon property to his weapon. Each round the weapon has this property, it counts as two of the normal arcane pool weapon bonuses. The magus must be at least 12th level before selecting this arcana. The magus must expend a point of his arcane pool to designate a foe for the bane (as the weapon property). He can change this foe as a standard action.

**Effective**: Swift action, 1 pool point. Scimitar becomes *bane* vs a chosen creature type. Bane weapons get +2 enhancement and +2d6 damage vs the chosen type.

*The fight-is-a-boss-fight button. Designate the enemy type, and every hit against them adds +2d6 and an effective +2 enhancement. When combined with [Arcane Pool](#arcane-pool) Shocking Burst setup, the total per-hit damage becomes genuinely absurd. Bane Blade costs 2 of the 4 available pool bonus points, so you can combine it with one +2 property or two +1 properties.*

## Foresight Divination (Wizard)

### Forewarned

*Su*

**Source**: Advanced Player's Guide (Divination school)

At 1st level, you can always act in the surprise round, even if you fail to notice your enemies, but you are still flat-footed until you take an action. In addition, you receive a bonus on initiative checks equal to 1/2 your wizard level (minimum +1). At 20th level, anytime you roll initiative, assume the roll resulted in a natural 20.

**Effective at level 16**: **+8 initiative**. Always act in surprise rounds.

*The Divination school's passive initiative bonus is the entire reason to go Divination. +8 stacks with [Iaijutsu](#iaijutsu)'s +10 INT for a total of +18 before DEX. Combined with DEX (+4) and a normal d20 roll, Yusuf's average initiative is around 32 — he is going first against everything short of an Int-capstoned gestalt rival.*

### Prescience

*Su*

**Source**: Advanced Player's Guide (Foresight subschool)

At the start of each day, you can predict the results of fate. Roll a d20 and note the result. You can substitute this roll for any roll made by you or an adjacent creature. You must choose to make this substitution before the roll is made. You may use this ability once per day at 1st level, plus one additional time per day for every five levels beyond 1st.

**NOTE**: The Foresight subschool version modifies this — the wizard rolls each d20 at the *start of each turn* rather than once per day, using the result for any roll made before next turn. Uses = 3 + INT mod per day.

**Effective**: Free action at start of each turn, pre-roll a d20, spend to replace any d20 made before the start of your next turn. **13 uses/day** (3 + INT mod 10).

*Prescience is the hidden MVP of the Foresight subschool. It synergizes perfectly with Yusuf's 15-20 crit range: he threatens a crit 30% of the time, needs to confirm, and can pre-roll the confirmation die with Prescience before the confirmation is even needed. It also doubles as a save-or-die insurance policy (pre-roll a 19, use it when you fail a Will save).*

### Foretell

*Sp*

**Source**: Advanced Player's Guide (Foresight subschool)

At 8th level, you can emit a 30-foot aura of fortune telling, granting yourself and all allies within this aura a +2 luck bonus on attack rolls, saving throws, ability checks, and skill checks. Alternatively, you may curse all enemies within 30 feet, imposing a –2 penalty on these rolls. You can use this ability for a number of rounds per day equal to your wizard level.

**Effective at level 16**: 30-ft. aura, +2 luck to allies OR -2 to enemies. **16 rounds/day**.

*A free-action area aura that improves the entire party's numbers. This is a significant force multiplier on any extended fight — 16 rounds is more than enough for a normal combat encounter. Yusuf uses it reflexively in boss fights and saves the Prescience uses for critical single-die moments.*

### Arcane Bond (Bonded Item)

*Su*

**Source**: Core Rulebook (Wizard class feature)

At 1st level, wizards form a powerful bond with an object or a creature. This bond can take one of two forms: a familiar or a bonded object. Yusuf has chosen a bonded object — *Aisha*, the scimitar.

A wizard who selects a bonded object begins play with one at no cost. Wizards who select a bonded object must designate it as a masterwork weapon, masterwork suit of armor, shield, amulet, ring, staff, or wand. Once a wizard has selected a bonded object, it cannot be changed.

Starting at 1st level, a wizard can cast any one spell from his spellbook without preparing it ahead of time. He must use his bonded item to cast this spell, and the chosen spell must be of a level the wizard can normally cast. Once cast, the wizard cannot use this ability again until the following day.

If a wizard attempts to cast a spell without his bonded object worn or in hand, he must make a concentration check or lose the spell (DC 20 + spell level).

**Chosen Bonded Object**: *Aisha*, the scimitar. Also the Kensai chosen weapon.

*The narrative justification writes itself. Aisha has been in Yusuf's hand since he was twenty-two — it is literally bonded to him in every sense that matters. The Wizard bonded-object mechanic is a happy coincidence: one free spell per day that doesn't need to be prepared, plus a guarantee that Yusuf can never be truly disarmed. If someone knocks Aisha from his grip, the bond allows him to recall her into his hand as a standard action, and the concentration penalty for casting without her is trivial at his skill level.*
