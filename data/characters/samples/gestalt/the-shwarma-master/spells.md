## Cantrips (shared Magus and Wizard list)

### Prestidigitation

| | |
|---|---|
| **School** | Universal |
| **Level** | Bard 0, Sorcerer/Wizard 0, Magus 0 |
| **Casting Time** | 1 standard action |
| **Components** | V, S |
| **Range** | 10 ft. |
| **Target/Area/Effect** | See text |
| **Duration** | 1 hour |
| **Save** | See text |
| **SR** | No |

Prestidigitations are minor tricks that novice spellcasters use for practice. Once cast, a prestidigitation spell enables you to perform simple magical effects for 1 hour. The effects are minor and have severe limitations. A prestidigitation can slowly lift 1 pound of material. It can color, clean, or soil items in a 1-foot cube each round. It can chill, warm, or flavor 1 pound of nonliving material. It cannot deal damage or affect the concentration of spellcasters. Prestidigitation can create small objects, but they look crude and artificial. The materials created by a prestidigitation are extremely fragile, and they cannot be used as tools, weapons, or spell components. Finally, prestidigitation lacks the power to duplicate any other spell effects. Any actual change to an object (beyond just moving, cleaning, or soiling it) persists only 1 hour.

*Yusuf's most-cast spell, by an order of magnitude. Clean a cutting board between meats. Warm a plate to serving temperature. Flavor the tahini. Color the sumac for presentation. Dry his mustache. Chill a glass of water for a weary customer. The campaign may remember him for the Intensified Shocking Grasp that killed the demon prince, but his regulars remember him for the Prestidigitation that kept the shwarma perfect. Ten thousand uses. No duration tracking — just vibes.*

### Shocking Grasp

| | |
|---|---|
| **School** | Evocation [electricity] |
| **Level** | Magus 1, Sorcerer/Wizard 1 |
| **Casting Time** | 1 standard action |
| **Components** | V, S |
| **Range** | Touch |
| **Target** | Creature or object touched |
| **Duration** | Instantaneous |
| **Save** | None |
| **SR** | Yes |

Your successful melee touch attack deals 1d6 points of electricity damage per caster level (maximum 5d6). The spell gains a +3 bonus on attack rolls against opponents wearing metal armor (or who are carrying metal weapons or are made of metal).

**With [Intensified Spell](feats.md#intensified-spell)**: cap raised to **10d6**.
**At Yusuf's CL 16**: full 10d6 (avg 35 per cast).
**Spellstrike crit on 15-20**: spell deals ×2 = 20d6 (avg 70).
**Maximized** via [Spell Perfection](feats.md#spell-perfection): flat **60** damage, guaranteed.
**Crit + Maximized**: 120 damage, guaranteed.

**Slot costs with [Magical Lineage](features.md#magical-lineage) + [Wayang Spellhunter](features.md#wayang-spellhunter) (-2 total):**

| Metamagic | Normal Level | With Traits |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Intensified | 2nd | **1st** |
| Intensified + Empowered | 4th | **2nd** |
| Intensified + Maximized | 5th | **3rd** |
| Intensified + Quickened | 6th | **4th** |

*The entire build's damage engine. Every combat round sees at least one, usually two, and sometimes three Shocking Grasps delivered through Aisha. The +3 bonus vs metal armor is not a minor rider — against enemies in heavy plate, Yusuf's Spellstrike attack rolls hit a ceiling that puts him into auto-hit territory.*

*Narratively: the flash-sear. The blade meets the meat and in a single instant the surface is crackling, carbonized, transformed. Yusuf has spent more time experimenting with Shocking Grasp than most wizards spend on their entire spellbook — he knows, to the d6, what each variant does to what kind of muscle tissue.*

**Spell Perfection interactions**:
- Free Quicken → swift-action 10d6 + Spell Combat SG = 20d6 per round minimum
- Free Maximize → 60 guaranteed damage per cast, no variance
- Doubled Spell Penetration (+4) and Greater Spell Penetration (+4) = **CL 16 + 8 = d20 + 24 vs SR**
- Doubled Weapon Focus → +2 to Spellstrike attack roll (when delivering SG specifically)

### Frostbite

| | |
|---|---|
| **School** | Transmutation [cold] |
| **Level** | Druid 1, Magus 1, Sorcerer/Wizard 1 |
| **Casting Time** | 1 standard action |
| **Components** | V, S |
| **Range** | Touch |
| **Target** | Creature touched |
| **Duration** | Instantaneous |
| **Save** | None |
| **SR** | Yes |

Your hand crackles with frost, and your successful melee touch attack deals 1d6 points of nonlethal cold damage + 1 point per level (max +15). A creature damaged by this spell is also fatigued. The spell ends if you successfully damage a creature, or when you would otherwise discharge the spell. You cannot use this spell to make multiple attacks in a single round with Spell Combat—it counts as a single spell cast.

Wait — Frostbite actually holds charges. **Corrected text**: Your melee touch attack deals 1d6 + caster level cold damage (nonlethal, max +15) and fatigues the target. The spell allows one touch per caster level or until discharged.

**At CL 16**: 1d6+15 per touch (max 15 levels of bonus), avg 18.5 per hit. **16 separate touches** before the spell ends.

*Yusuf's sustained-damage spell. Cast once, deliver through every iterative attack AND every subsequent turn's full attack for the next 16 rounds. Each hit = weapon damage + 1d6+15 nonlethal cold + fatigue condition on the target. The fatigue debuff is especially valuable — fatigued creatures take -2 STR, -2 DEX, can't charge or run, and most enemies don't have immunity.*

*Cooking metaphor: slow cold-marination, achieved by contact. The meat tenderizes. The beast tires.*

## Magus Spells (2nd Level)

### Mirror Image

| | |
|---|---|
| **School** | Illusion (figment) |
| **Level** | Bard 2, Magus 2, Sorcerer/Wizard 2 |
| **Casting Time** | 1 standard action |
| **Components** | V, S |
| **Range** | Personal |
| **Target** | You |
| **Duration** | 1 min./level (D) |
| **Save** | None |
| **SR** | No |

Several illusory duplicates of you pop into being, making it difficult for enemies to know which target to attack. The duplicates move with you and mimic your actions, pretending to cast spells when you cast a spell, drink potions when you drink potions, levitate when you levitate, and so on.

Enemies trying to attack you or cast spells at you must select from among indistinguishable targets. Attacks that hit an image destroy it. An image is destroyed by a ranged attack that would have hit it even if the miss chance roll indicates the attacker missed. Area spells affect you normally and do not destroy any of your figments. Spells and effects that do not require an attack roll affect you normally and do not destroy any of your figments. Spells that require a touch attack are rolled against the figments normally; if an image is hit, the image is destroyed.

You gain 1d4 images plus 1 image per three caster levels (maximum 8 images).

**At CL 16**: 1d4 + 5 images (max 8). **16 minutes duration.**

*Pre-combat buff alongside Shield and Mage Armor. Against any enemy not equipped with True Seeing or area attacks, each swing has a 6/7 or 7/8 chance of hitting an image instead of Yusuf. Combined with Greater Invisibility for nova turns, Yusuf is practically untouchable.*

### Haste

| | |
|---|---|
| **School** | Transmutation |
| **Level** | Bard 3, Magus 3, Sorcerer/Wizard 3 |
| **Casting Time** | 1 standard action |
| **Components** | V, S, M (a shaving of licorice root) |
| **Range** | Close (25 ft. + 5 ft./2 levels) |
| **Targets** | One creature/level, no two of which can be more than 30 ft. apart |
| **Duration** | 1 round/level |
| **Save** | Fortitude negates (harmless) |
| **SR** | Yes (harmless) |

The transmuted creatures move and act more quickly than normal. This extra speed has several effects.

When making a full attack action, a hasted creature may make one extra attack with any weapon it is holding. The attack is made using the creature's full base attack bonus, plus any modifiers appropriate to the situation. (This effect is not cumulative with similar effects, such as that provided by a *speed* weapon.)

All of the hasted creature's modes of movement (including land movement, burrow, climb, fly, and swim) increase by 30 feet, to a maximum of twice the subject's normal speed using that form of movement.

A hasted creature gains a +1 bonus on attack rolls and a +1 dodge bonus to AC and Reflex saves. Any condition that makes you lose your Dexterity bonus to Armor Class also makes you lose dodge bonuses.

**At CL 16**: 16 rounds, up to 16 creatures (within 30 ft. of each other).

*The fundamental caster-martial buff. +1 attack, +1 dodge AC, +1 Reflex save, +30 movement, and most importantly: an extra attack at highest BAB during full attacks. Yusuf casts this on round 1 of any serious fight via Spell Combat (cast Haste + full attack in the same round) and the whole party benefits for 16 rounds — more than enough for any normal encounter.*

*Cooking metaphor: the rush. The dinner-service tempo applied to combat. Everything happens at once and none of it is rushed.*

### Dispel Magic

| | |
|---|---|
| **School** | Abjuration |
| **Level** | Bard 3, Cleric 3, Druid 4, Magus 3, Paladin 3, Sorcerer/Wizard 3 |
| **Casting Time** | 1 standard action |
| **Components** | V, S |
| **Range** | Medium (100 ft. + 10 ft./level) |
| **Target or Area** | One spellcaster, creature, or object; or 20-ft.-radius burst |
| **Duration** | Instantaneous |
| **Save** | None |
| **SR** | No |

You can use dispel magic to end ongoing spells that have been cast on a creature or object, to temporarily suspend the magical abilities of a magic item, to end ongoing spells cast by a creature, or to counter another spellcaster's spell. A dispelled spell ends as if its duration had expired. Some spells, as detailed in their descriptions, can't be defeated by dispel magic. Dispel magic can dispel (but not counter) spell-like effects just as it does spells. The effect of a spell with an instantaneous duration can't be dispelled, because the magical effect is already over before the dispel magic can take effect.

You make a dispel check (1d20 + your caster level, maximum +10) against the spell or against each ongoing spell currently in effect on the object or creature. The DC for this dispel check is 11 + the spell's caster level. If you succeed on a particular check, that spell is dispelled.

**At CL 16**: d20 + 10 (cap) vs DC 11+CL. Cap hurts.

*Utility tool for stripping buffs off enemies and debuffs off allies. Yusuf's dispel check caps at +10 (Dispel Magic's own cap), which is less effective at level 16 than it is at level 10, but still reliable against CR-appropriate opponents with CL < 15.*

| **Duration** | 1 day/level or until discharged |
| **Save** | See text |
| **SR** | See text |

You can place another spell upon your person so that it comes into effect under some condition you dictate when casting *contingency*. The *contingency* spell and the spell it is to bring into effect are cast at the same time. The 10-minute casting time is the minimum total for both castings; if the companion spell has a casting time longer than 10 minutes, use that instead. You must also pay any costs associated with the companion spell.

The spell to be brought into effect by the *contingency* must be one that affects your person and be of a spell level no higher than one-third your caster level (rounded down, maximum 6th level).

The conditions needed to bring the spell into effect must be clear, although they can be rather elaborate. A contingent spell is cast instantly when the prescribed circumstances occur. If complicated or convoluted conditions are prescribed, the whole spell (*contingency* and the companion magic) may fail when called on.

You can use only one *contingency* spell at a time; if a second is cast, the first one (if still active) is dispelled.

**At CL 16**: 16 days duration. Max triggered spell level = 5th (CL 16/3 = 5).

**Recommended setup**: Contingency ([Dimension Door](#dimension-door)): "If I am reduced below 20 HP, teleport me 500 ft. away to a predetermined safe point."

*The insurance policy. Yusuf burns 1,500 gp per recast to maintain this, but the value is enormous — it's a guaranteed "do not die" button that triggers automatically.*

### Mind Blank

| | |
|---|---|
| **School** | Abjuration |
| **Level** | Sorcerer/Wizard 8 |
| **Casting Time** | 1 standard action |
| **Components** | V, S |
| **Range** | Close (25 ft. + 5 ft./2 levels) |
| **Target** | One creature |
| **Duration** | 24 hours |
| **Save** | Will negates (harmless) |
| **SR** | No |

The subject is protected from all devices and spells that detect, influence, or read emotions or thoughts. This spell protects against all mind-affecting spells and effects as well as information gathering by divination spells or effects. *Mind blank* even foils *limited wish*, *miracle*, *wish*, and *psionics* when they are used in such a way as to affect the subject's mind or to gain information about it. In the case of *scrying* that scans an area the creature is in, such as *arcane eye*, the spell works but the creature simply isn't detected. *Scrying* attempts that are targeted specifically at the subject do not work at all.

**At CL 16**: 24 hours.

*The ultimate anti-mind-control / anti-divination defense. Cast at the start of each day. Makes Yusuf immune to every charm, compulsion, fear effect, and mind-affecting spell for the full day. Also makes him impossible to scry, which matters once you start making enemies who have access to divination magic.*

### Moment of Prescience

| | |
|---|---|
| **School** | Divination |
| **Level** | Sorcerer/Wizard 8 |
| **Casting Time** | 1 standard action |
| **Components** | V, S |
| **Range** | Personal |
| **Target** | You |
| **Duration** | 1 hour/level or until discharged |
| **Save** | None |
| **SR** | No |

This spell grants you a powerful sixth sense in relation to yourself. Once per spell, you may choose to use its effect. This spell grants you an insight bonus equal to your caster level (maximum +25) on any single attack roll, combat maneuver check, opposed ability or skill check, or saving throw. Alternatively, you can apply the insight bonus to your AC against a single attack (even if flat-footed). Activating the effect doesn't take an action; you can even activate it on another character's turn if needed. You must choose to use the *moment of prescience* before you make the roll it is to modify. Once used, the spell ends.

You can't have more than one *moment of prescience* active on you at the same time.

**At CL 16**: +16 insight to any single roll. 16 hours duration.

*The emergency save button. Cast in the morning, held in reserve. When Yusuf fails a critical save against a save-or-die effect, he spends it to add +16 insight — enough to turn almost any natural 1 into a success. Can also be used for critical attacks (guarantee a confirmation on a major boss) or opposed checks (winning an initiative reroll).*

*Not to be confused with [Prescience](features.md#prescience), the Foresight school ability. Yusuf has both. He is cripplingly well-prepared.*
