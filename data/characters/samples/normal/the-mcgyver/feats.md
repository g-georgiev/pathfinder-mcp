### Scribe Scroll

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Item Creation |
| **Source** | Core Rulebook |
| **Prerequisites** | Caster level 1st |

**Benefit**: You can create magic scrolls. Scribing a scroll takes 2 hours if its base price is 250 gp or less, otherwise scribing a scroll takes 1 day for each 1,000 gp in its base price. To scribe a scroll, you must use up raw materials costing half of the base price. See the magic item creation rules in the Core Rulebook for more details.

*McGyver's first crafting feat and a wizard class feature. Scrolls are insurance policies — cheap, portable, and they let him prepare for edge cases without burning daily slots. At CL 10, a scroll of Teleport costs 562 gp to scribe (half the 1,125 gp market price). Always keep backup scrolls of key utility spells.*

### Improved Initiative

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Combat |
| **Source** | Core Rulebook |
| **Prerequisites** | None |

**Benefit**: You get a +4 bonus on initiative checks.

*Going first is everything for a controller wizard. McGyver's total initiative is +8 (DEX +2, feat +4, Reactionary +2). Getting Haste or a control spell down before the enemies act can define the entire fight.*

### Spell Focus (Conjuration)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | General |
| **Source** | Core Rulebook |
| **Prerequisites** | None |

**Benefit**: Add +1 to the Difficulty Class for all saving throws against spells from the school of magic you select. You can gain this feat multiple times. Its effects do not stack. Each time you take the feat, it applies to a new school of magic.

**Chosen School**: Conjuration

*Conjuration is the generalist's best friend — it contains summoning, teleportation, creation spells (pits, clouds, tentacles, walls), and healing subschool spells. The +1 DC applies to Grease, Glitterdust, Create Pit, Stinking Cloud, Black Tentacles, and Web. Also the prerequisite for Augment Summoning.*

**Interactions:**
- Glitterdust DC: 10 + 2 (spell level) + 7 (INT) + 1 (focus) = **DC 20**
- Stinking Cloud DC: 10 + 3 + 7 + 1 = **DC 21**
- Black Tentacles: no save (CMB check), but Spell Focus still matters for other conjuration spells

### Craft Wondrous Item

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Item Creation |
| **Source** | Core Rulebook |
| **Prerequisites** | Caster level 3rd |

**Benefit**: You can create wondrous items, a type of magic item. Crafting a wondrous item takes 1 day for each 1,000 gp in its base price. To create a wondrous item, you must use up raw materials costing half of its base price. You can also mend a broken wondrous item if it is one that you could make. Doing so costs half the raw materials and half the time it would take to craft that item.

*THE McGyver feat. This is how he built his own Headband of Vast Intelligence, his own Cloak of Resistance, and his own Handy Haversack — at half price. A wizard with this feat effectively doubles their WBL on wondrous items during downtime. It also lets McGyver solve problems by building custom solutions: need a specific item the party can't find in a shop? Give him a day and some gold.*

### Augment Summoning

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | General |
| **Source** | Core Rulebook |
| **Prerequisites** | Spell Focus (conjuration) |

**Benefit**: Each creature you conjure with any summon spell gains a +4 enhancement bonus to Strength and Constitution.

*Summoned creatures are expendable allies — they scout, flank, absorb hits, and provide battlefield presence. With Augment Summoning, they're actually dangerous. A Summon Monster V creature with +4 STR/CON hits harder, has more HP, and lasts longer. McGyver summons what the situation demands: earth elementals for brute force, lantern archons for ranged support, dire animals for flanking.*

**Interactions:**
- +4 CON = +2 HP per HD on summoned creatures
- +4 STR = +2 attack and damage on natural attacks
- Stacks with Haste cast on the summoned creature

### Fast Study

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Arcane Discovery |
| **Source** | Ultimate Magic |
| **Prerequisites** | Wizard level 5th |

**Benefit**: Normally, a wizard spends 1 hour preparing all of his spells for the day, or proportionately less if he only prepares some spells, with a minimum of 15 minutes of preparation. Thanks to mental discipline and efficient study habits, you can prepare all of your spells in only 15 minutes, and your minimum preparation time is only 1 minute.

You can also prepare a spell in an open slot by spending 1 minute studying your spellbook.

*This is the single most important feat for the McGyver concept. Fast Study transforms the wizard from "I prepared the wrong spells today" to "give me 60 seconds." Leave spell slots open in the morning, then fill them as needed throughout the day with just 1 minute of study. Entering a dungeon with lots of undead? Swap in anti-undead spells. Need Knock for a locked door? One minute. Need Tongues for a negotiation? One minute. Combined with Spell Study for cross-list access, McGyver can adapt to literally any situation given a brief pause.*

**Tactical usage:**
- Leave 1-2 slots open at each spell level in the morning
- Fill them on-the-fly as situations arise (1 minute each)
- In a 10-minute rest, you can swap your entire prepared list

### Heighten Spell

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Metamagic |
| **Source** | Core Rulebook |
| **Prerequisites** | None |

**Benefit**: A heightened spell has a higher spell level than normal (up to a maximum of 9th level). Unlike other metamagic feats, Heighten Spell actually increases the effective level of the spell that it modifies. All effects dependent on spell level (such as saving throw DCs and ability to penetrate a lesser globe of invulnerability) are calculated according to the heightened level. The heightened spell is as difficult to prepare and cast as a spell of its effective level.

*Flexibility incarnate. Heighten Spell lets McGyver use higher-level slots for lower-level spells with boosted DCs. A Heightened Glitterdust in a 4th-level slot has DC 22 instead of DC 20. More importantly, it interacts with Spell Study: if McGyver needs to borrow a low-level cleric spell but wants a higher DC, he can heighten it. Also a prerequisite for several advanced metamagic feats if the build grows.*

### Spell Penetration

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | General |
| **Source** | Core Rulebook |
| **Prerequisites** | None |

**Benefit**: You get a +2 bonus on caster level checks (1d20 + caster level) made to overcome a creature's spell resistance.

*At level 10, McGyver's CL check to penetrate SR is d20+12 (CL 10 + 2). With Focused Spells active, that becomes d20+16 (CL 14 + 2). Spell Resistance becomes increasingly common at mid-to-high levels (demons, devils, drow, dragons), and failing an SR check means the entire spell is wasted. For a Spell Sage who burns two slots per Spell Study use, wasting a spell on SR is doubly painful.*

### Craft Wand

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Item Creation |
| **Source** | Core Rulebook |
| **Prerequisites** | Caster level 5th |

**Benefit**: You can create magic wands. Crafting a wand takes 1 day for each 1,000 gp in its base price. To create a wand, you must use up raw materials costing half of its base price. A newly created wand has 50 charges. See the magic item creation rules for more details.

*The third leg of McGyver's crafting trifecta (scrolls, wondrous items, wands). Wands hold spells of 4th level or lower with 50 charges, making them ideal for frequently-used utility and buff spells. A wand of Shield (CL 1, 375 gp to craft) gives the party 50 uses of +4 shield AC. A wand of Grease (375 gp) is 50 battlefield control spells. McGyver can build the party's entire consumable arsenal during downtime.*

**Key wands to craft:**
- Wand of Shield: 375 gp craft cost
- Wand of Grease: 375 gp craft cost
- Wand of See Invisibility: 4,500 gp craft cost (CL 3)
- Wand of Glitterdust: 4,500 gp craft cost (CL 3)
