## Spell Sage Archetype Features

### Focused Spells (Su)

**Source**: Advanced Class Guide — Spell Sage Archetype
**Replaces**: Arcane Bond

At 1st level, once per day the spell sage's understanding of spells allows him to increase his caster level by 4 for a single spell cast. He can do this twice per day at 8th level, and three times per day at 16th level.

**Current Uses**: 2/day (8th level progression)

*This is McGyver's "overclock" button. When a spell absolutely must land or a CL check must succeed, he can bump his CL from 10 to 14. At CL 14, Dispel Magic becomes significantly more reliable (+14 vs DC 11+CL), Haste lasts 14 rounds, and Teleport gains a much better chance of on-target arrival. Use this for critical Dispel checks, high-stakes teleportation, or maximizing duration/range on key buffs.*

**Key interactions:**
- Stacks with other CL boosts (e.g., traits, items)
- Applies to the spell's CL-dependent effects: range, duration, damage dice, CL checks
- Declare before casting — cannot retroactively apply

### Spell Study (Su)

**Source**: Advanced Class Guide — Spell Sage Archetype
**Replaces**: Arcane School

At 2nd level, the sage's understanding of the spells of bards, clerics, and druids is so great that he can use his own magic in an inefficient, roundabout way to duplicate those classes' spells. Once per day, a spell sage can spontaneously cast any spell on the bard, cleric, or druid spell list as if it were a wizard spell he knew and had prepared.

**Casting the spell requires:**
1. **Time**: 1 full round per spell level of the desired spell (using the lowest level from among the bard/cleric/druid lists). If the spell's normal casting time is 1 full round or longer, that time is added on top.
2. **Spell Slots**: Expend two prepared wizard spells of that spell level or higher.

At 6th level and every 5 levels thereafter, a spell sage can use this ability one additional time per day.

**Current Uses**: 2/day (gained at 2nd and 6th level)

**Example costs at Level 10:**

| Borrowed Spell | List Level | Casting Time | Slots Expended |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Good Hope](spells.md#good-hope) | Bard 3 | 3 full rounds | 2× 3rd-level+ |
| [Remove Disease](spells.md#remove-disease) | Cleric 3 | 3 full rounds + 1 std action | 2× 3rd-level+ |
| [Restoration](spells.md#restoration) | Cleric 4 | 4 full rounds + 3 rounds | 2× 4th-level+ |
| [Freedom of Movement](spells.md#freedom-of-movement) | Bard/Cleric/Druid 4 | 4 full rounds | 2× 4th-level+ |
| [Raise Dead](spells.md#raise-dead) | Cleric 5 | 5 full rounds + 1 minute | 2× 5th-level+ |
| [Breath of Life](spells.md#breath-of-life) | Cleric 5 | 5 full rounds | 2× 5th-level+ |

*This is the defining ability of the build — the reason McGyver exists. With Spell Study, there is no problem too specialized to solve. Party cleric down? McGyver can raise them. Someone got hit with a disease? He handles it. Need Freedom of Movement for the underwater dungeon? Done. The long casting time means this is NOT for mid-combat emergencies (5 rounds = 30 seconds for a 5th-level spell), but for out-of-combat problem solving, it's unmatched. Combined with [Fast Study](feats.md#fast-study) to rapidly swap prepared spells, McGyver always has an answer.*

**Important limitations:**
- Cannot duplicate spells with costly material components unless you provide the component
- The borrowed spell uses your wizard CL (10, or 14 with Focused Spells)
- Save DCs use your Intelligence modifier as normal
- Subject to arcane spell failure chance from armor (not that McGyver wears any)

### Cantrips

**Source**: Core Rulebook — Wizard Class Feature

A wizard can prepare a number of cantrips, or 0-level spells, each day. These spells are cast like any other spell, but they are not expended when cast and may be used again. A wizard can prepare a cantrip from an opposition school, but it uses up two of his available slots.

*Since Spell Sage has no arcane school, there are no opposition schools — all cantrips are equally accessible.*

### Wizard Bonus Feats

**Source**: Core Rulebook — Wizard Class Feature

At 5th, 10th, 15th, and 20th level, a wizard gains a bonus feat. At each such opportunity, he can choose a metamagic feat, an item creation feat, or Spell Mastery. The wizard must still meet all prerequisites for a bonus feat, including caster level minimums. These bonus feats are in addition to the feat that a character of any class gets from advancing levels. The wizard is not limited to the categories of item creation feats, metamagic feats, or Spell Mastery when choosing those feats.

*McGyver used his bonus feats for [Fast Study](feats.md#fast-study) (5th) and [Craft Wand](feats.md#craft-wand) (10th) — both fitting his "build anything" ethos. Note: Arcane Discoveries (like Fast Study) are available as wizard bonus feat selections per Ultimate Magic.*
