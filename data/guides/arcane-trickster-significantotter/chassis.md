# The Arcane Trickster’s Chassis

## ½ BaB

This matters for an arcane trickster magus, maybe even a bard. It shouldn’t matter that much to you. You’ll be attacking with rays against touch AC. There are, of course, monsters with high touch ACs, but you should have some other tricks to help handle them. Point blank shot + Precise Shot or Weapon Focus (ray) helps to mitigate this, but even that isn’t strictly necessary. For most of the game you’ll likely feel more accurate than any of your full BaB companions

## Saves

You have Will and Reflex as your good saves. Will is most important. It protects you against many save or suck spells. Reflex is just fine. It’s often just damage mitigation. Failing fortitude saves can really suck. Nauseated is awful and ability damage is awful. It definitely is not a bad choice to pick up Great Fortitude or a fortitude trait as an arcane trickster.

## Spellcasting

Full spellcasting progression. That’s why we’re here!

## Ranged legerdemain

This is a fun, useful trick. You can stay out of the range of traps while disarming them. You can pick locks from the shadows, even as the door itself is guarded. You can pick pockets without ever moving next to your target. The +5 DC penalty will be felt, but it isn’t onerous. This is a very cool ability. It will be useful sometimes, but more or less depending on the game you’re playing in. It’s very flavorful. it’s just not as important to the Chassis as other tricks you get.

## Impromptu sneak attack

This is a limited, but extremely useful option for any arcane trickster. Your combat actions will be centered around trying to land sneak attacks on your opponents. A turn where you fail to sneak attack, either through a bad stealth roll, or through an inability to find new cover, is one where you feel far less effective. Impromptu sneak attack shores up those rounds for you. 1/day doesn’t feel like a lot, but it really helps your consistency. Don’t keep this in your pocket. Use it whenever it’s useful!

## Tricky spells

Some GMs will let you hide in the shadows and cast a spell like Aram Zey’s Focus or Keyhole when infiltrating a manor. However, “To provide a verbal component, you must be able to speak in a strong voice.” There’s no stealth check that keeps a strong, clear voice from being noticed by nearby guards. Luckily, somatic components can arguably benefit from concealment and your stealth checks.

Early on, if your GM is strict about spellcasting while stealthing, the moment you cast your first spell is when the alarms go off and you have to hurry to finish whatever it is you’re focusing on. This makes a Lesser Silent Spell Metamagic Rod is a good pick-up for an arcane trickster. So how good is a slotless Greater Combination Silent/Still Spell Metamagic Rod? Really heckin’ good.

Throughout your career it’s very helpful to have instant free silent/still metamagic up your sleeve when dealing with out of combat situations where you want to cast spells, but not attract attention. When filling a rogue’s out of combat role, situations that make great use of this ability are very common.

## Invisible thief

This is an amazing ability and it’s a shame that it comes into play so late. Greater Invisibility as a free action? And you can split up the rounds throughout the day? This is a fantastic “oh shit” button. It’s wonderful for quick escapes. It’s an easy source of sneak attack damage (though by this point you should always have the actual spell greater invisibility active in combat). However, by the time you have Invisible Thief, you’re likely level 14, which means that you won’t have it for the majority of your time as an arcane trickster.

## Surprise spells

What a cool capstone! This certainly isn’t the reason you play an arcane trickster, but it really adds pizzazz to your playstyle. And flexibility! The first thing you’ll think of is sneak attack fireballs. That’s cool and all, and it’s awesome to do AOE sneak attacks instead of attaching them to single target rays, but where this becomes really cool and efficient is with AOE spells that are primarily status effects with marginal amounts of damage. Drop Hungry Darkness on an enemy encampment, 60ft radius spread dealing 2 con damage a round as well as 3d6 force damage and your 7d6 sneak attack to every single enemy within! Sneak attack with wall of fire or with spiked pit! All of those amazing control options with additional damage now get a big damage buff! And when we combine this with the feat Mind Strike…
