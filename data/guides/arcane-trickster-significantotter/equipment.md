# Equipment

In this section, I’ll note some of the standout, most-important items in the Arcane Trickster’s arsenal. For a full evaluation of all items I find thematic or relevant to the arcane trickster after working through every single item on Archives of Nethys, see:

Otter's Arsenal for the Arcane Trickster

Here, I’m going to point out the most important items for you to worry about.

## Spells Known (??? gp)

It’s so important as an arcane trickster to expand your utility and have options for any situation that might be thrown at you! Buy spells. Figure out what will be useful for you. Scribe some scrolls of the spells that you want to have available even if you don’t prepare them.

Copying spells from existing spellbooks in town by paying wizards there is most economical, but you can also purchase scrolls to copy them. In either case, you then have to pay to write the spell into your spellbook.

### Pages of Spell Knowledge (1,000 gp (1st), 4,000 gp (2nd), 9,000 gp (3rd), 16,000 gp (4th), 25,000 gp (5th), 36,000 gp (6th), 49,000 gp (7th), 64,000 gp (8th), 81,000 gp (9th))

As a sorcerer, spells known are just as important if not moreso, but they’re much pricier. It’s still very worthwhile to expand your arsenal. You just have to be more selective due to the much greater cost.

## The Big Six

### Cloak of Resistance +X (Cloak)

Saving throws are the name of the game in pathfinder. You want to make sure that your saving throws keep up with your level. Cloaks of Resistance are essentially required. Once you get a ton of money, you can offload the +X resistance bonus to saves over to ioun stones, and replace it with nice cloaks like the cloak of displacement or the cloak of the mountebank.

### Headband of Vast Intelligence +X (Headband)

Again, +2/4/6 Int is such an incredible bonus for you that each form of this item needs to be obtained fast. It’s not quite as pivotal as it is for a spellcaster who relies more on saves, but it’s still super important. It governs spells per day, concentration checks, and many other spell abilities that scale off your casting stat.

### Belt of Incredible Dexterity +X (Belt)

The +2/4/6 Dex boost is also amazing. It’s a bonus to hit on your rays, it helps your reflex save, your AC, and your stealth checks! So many stats that are important to you. I’d still prioritize the Int headband, but this is close on its tail.

### Bracers of Armor/Haramaki/Silken Ceremonial Armor +X (Wrists/Armor)

Despite the fact that you will be using mage armor for most of your levels, you can still get some valuable abilities from armor with no spell failure chance. Spell storing mirror image armor is great. Shadow armor only has a flat cost to add onto the armor for another +5 stealth bonus. Slick does the same for escape artist. Shadow Blending gives you a good miss chance in your normal shadowed hiding places. Expeditious armor is a flat cost for a 3/day swift action move speed boost.

### Mithril Buckler +X (Offhand)

A shield bonus stacks with your Mage Armor. A mithril buckler won’t mess with your spell failure, or give you any penalty, except the -1 to attack with your other hand. This is a great, economical source of AC bonus.

### Ring of Protection +X (Ring)

The ring of protection competes with your ring slot, and it also costs more. You don’t need piles of AC too bad, you’re a backliner. But you don’t want to ignore it.

### Amulet of Natural Armor +X (Neck)

The amulet of natural armor is similar to the ring of protection.

## Other Items

### Sleeves of Many Garments (wrists) (200 gp)

This is a fantastic, cheap method of disguising yourself, or just fitting to any occasion! Even once I get better wrist slot items in the future, I’ll grab these to throw them on for a fancy party in the city.

### Cracked Dusty Rose Prism Ioun Stone (slotless) (500 gp)

Slotless +1 initiative for 500gp! That’s a steal!

### Quick Runner’s Shirt (chest) (1,000 gp)

This is a great chest slot item early on. It’s wonderful for hurrying to new cover for another stealth check. Or for an extra burst of speed when chasing someone, or running away from him. Very versatile!

### Ring of Spell Knowledge (ring) (1,500 gp (type I), 6,000 gp (type II), 13,500 gp (type III), 24,000 gp (type IV))

This is a very flexible, useful way to gain spells known for a sorcerer. These take up a ring slot and are more expensive than Pages of Spell Knowledge. However, with just a DC 20 spellcraft, you can teach a spell to the ring, and have it provide that spell instead! That’s way cheaper and more flexible than going around selling and buying your pages. You can instead just hold onto scrolls, and switch the ring between the spells on your scrolls depending on your needs.

### Hat of Disguise (head) (1,800 gp / 12,000 gp greater)

The hat of disguise is a cheap way to make the Disguise skill entirely irrelevant, especially in combination with the Sleeves of Many Garments. It’s also fantastic for a face arcane trickster who uses a lot of bluff. Or even a non-face who wants to blend in with guards or manor staff as they sneak through passages towards the more private rooms. Even if you have a better head slot item, you can always pop this on for an out of combat scene, and swap it back afterwards.

The greater hat of disguise gives us some nice potential bonuses by allowing us to transform into other races. Maybe it’s useful if you’re an uncommon race and need alter self to be able to blend in. But the use of a hat of disguise is usually to blend in, not to turn into a race that gives you all the bonuses you want. You can get different senses or speeds from spells.

A really nice part of the Greater Hat of Disguise is the essentially permanent +2 dex size bonus. It’s cheaper to gain that through the hat than the +4 -> +6 belt upgrade. However, it won’t overlap with other polymorph spells and size bonuses if you plan to use those frequently.

### Silent Metamagic Rod (3,000 gp lesser / 11,000 gp normal / 24,500 gp greater)

Silent spell will be a solidly useful pick up for you at all points in the game. Tricky Spells helps somewhat to cover this need, but a rod will never serve you wrong when infiltrating with stealth.

### Shadow Armor Enhancement (+3,750 gp / 15,000 gp improved / 33,750 gp greater)

Bonuses to stealth are always welcome! The initial enchantment is very well priced, but it gets more expensive as the price goes up. +5 additional stealth will never stop being valuable, but it’s hard to justify the last upgrade at 18,000 gp additional from the improved version. Wait to upgrade until that price tag doesn’t come close to breaking the bank.

### Sandals of Quick Reaction (feet) (4,000 gp)

Wow! This is so good for us. A move action and a standard action means that surprise rounds allow us to both cast a spell and stealth! And playing as a rogue-ish character, surprise rounds will happen plenty around you. And a bonus to your speed in surprise rounds as the cherry on top! This is an extremely well-priced action economy.

### Eversmoking Bottle (5,400 gp)

This right here is an excellent way to get consistent total concealment. The only issue is that it’ll likely affect your party just as much as you. A goz mask is great with it, but can you convince all your allies to pick one up as well? Otherwise, you’re stuck slapping on Ashen Path before every combat.

### Fog-Cutting Lenses / Goz Mask (Head)  (8,000 gp)

Immunity to visibility effects from fog-like spells is fantastic. Ashen path does this as a second level spell with 10 min/level duration, but this saves us a spell slot and allows us flexibility to be caught without having prebuffed. Obscuring mist and other fog spells are amazing ways to get sneak attack from concealment without caring about darkvision, see-invisibility, or stealth checks.

The Goz Mask does the same thing as the fog-cutting lenses, for the same slot, for the same price, but better. Weird. Eventually, you can tack on casts of Life Bubble so that you can throw cloudkills or acidic fogs on top of yourself and your party.

### Weapon Enhancement: Training (+1 bonus)

Pop this enhancement onto a set of +1 gauntlets, and enjoy your free feat! Grab point blank shot, precise shot, weapon focus: ray, mind strike, arcane strike, deadly aim, etc! You can even add this on multiple times. The only issue is that the bonus feats from training can’t allow you to qualify for other feats. So if you had Training (point blank shot) that wouldn’t let you then add Training (precise shot). You would need to pick point blank shot as an actual feat.

### Unfettered Shirt (chest) (10,000 gp)

Way cheaper than a ring of freedom of movement, but 10 minutes a day is often enough for the critical fights that end with you getting grappled or dealing with other restricting conditions. And because it’s command word activated, it’s simple to activate when grappled. Just use it before you get paralyzed or stunned!

### Gloves of Shaping (hand) (10,000 gp)

This is one of those open-ended magic items that just breaks aspects of the game. Any wall of stone or a less hard material than that is at your whims. You can just start tearing out pieces of it as if it were clay using simple tools. You can break through any wooden doors or wooden walls. The whole town is your oyster!

### Blessed Book (slotless) (12,500 gp)

Blessed books are a money saver! It’ll hurt your wallet at first, but you’ll thank yourself as you copy higher and higher level spells into your spellbook for what would have been greatly increasing prices. Plus, as an arcane trickster wizard, you don’t actually get 2 new spells known per level. A kind GM will agree that the RAW ruling is pretty dumb and you should get your new spells. But in the case of a more strict GM, this goes up from just being purple to being absolutely essential and almost needing an entirely new color.

### Broom of Flying (slotless) (17,000 gp)

If you’re going to get a flying item, this is the best bang for your buck. 9 hours of flight per day as per overland flight without taking up a slot! Of course, you also get overland flight as a spell, and have other important purchases around this price.

### Mage’s Crossbow (18,335 gp)

This is one of the few ways to get enhancement bonuses to your ray attacks. It also helps with spell pen. Very nice!

### Ring of Invisibility (ring) (20,000 gp)

This is an item that I consider to be core. Even if you swap it on and off with other rings, it lets you live invisibly. In combat, you should have greater invisibility by the time you can afford this. But at-will invisibility when stalking a city or breaking into palaces? Invaluable.

### Sniper Goggles (eyes) (20,000 gp / 50,000 gp greater)

This is the other core item for the early/mid game arcane trickster. As you get reach metamagic, or longer range rays, you realize how restrictive the 30ft range limit on sneak attack is. Now you can sneak attack from any range! And when you’re within 30ft, you do a nice chunk of extra damage! The upgrade is a lot of money for extra damage. But hey, it can be hard to find sources of extra damage for your sneak attacks.

### Ring of X-Ray Vision (ring) (25,000 gp)

Now this is a wonderful ring! It’s fantastic for dungeon crawling, for infiltrating structures, and for scouting. It gives so much versatility, with an effect that isn’t duplicated by any spell. Plus, since it’s mostly useful out of combat, you can just swap it out for your combat ring to use in 1-minute increments while scouting.

### Rod of Metamagic, Quicken (35,000 gp lesser / 75,500 gp normal / 170,000 gp greater)

There is nothing that provides a larger power boost, especially at high levels, than casting two spells on one turn. Unlike a lot of other metamagic rods, Quicken Spell is one that you can easily justify paying for the normal, or even greater versions of. 170,000 gp is a lot, sure. But how would the BBEG like to deal with an opener of quickened waves of ecstasy into sneak attack polar ray? Plenty of nastier options too if you start combing through your spell list.

### Ring of Freedom of Movement (ring) (40,000 gp)

Freedom of Movement is one of the strongest buffs in the game. Complete immunity to anything that would restrain you from moving. A bear trap won’t stop you! Nor will paralysis, stunned, grappled, etc.!

### Star Cinders (neck) (50,000 gp)

If you’re an admixture evocation wizard, you don’t need something like this. If you aren’t, then this is a fantastic way to force through enemy resistances, which will surely be plaguing you by the time you can afford this.

### Otherworldly Kimono (body) (67,000 gp)

This is classically the body slot item for high level casters, and it’s certainly fantastic for you as well. It gives you +4 to CL checks, which is amazing for spell penetration. It’s a +4 robe of resistance, which is fine. And we even get a free maze spell, which doesn’t rely on the item’s DC! Just a fantastic item
