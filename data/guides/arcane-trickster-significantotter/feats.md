# Feats & Traits

## Traits

You get a lot of class skills by leveling through 3 classes. If you want to take a trait that makes a skill a class skill, be sure that one of your other classes doesn’t already do that for you! A vivisectionist alchemist/Wizard doesn’t get bluff as a class skill, but the arcane trickster does! Wait for the arcane trickster level, don’t spend a valuable trait to get it as a class skill right away.

There are tons of awesome traits. I heavily utilize this guide whenever searching for traits, and I recommend you do so as well. https://docs.google.com/document/d/1dVQA-uI740Hh8vq-zsnbHV6UwJg-4QKlpmkxBEmCdhA/edit?pli=1#

The traits that I’ve compiled here are ones that I think are exceptional or very useful for an arcane trickster. I’ve avoided repeating traits that give the same bonus. For example, there are multiple Racial or Region traits that give +2 initiative just like reactionary does. I’ve only listed it once, however. If you want Resilient and Reactionary, check the guide I linked to see other types of traits that give the same bonus.

### Combat

Accelerated Drinker - Drink a potion as a move action. This is fantastic if you’re a vivisectionist alchemist dip who wants to make use of brew potion.

Blade of the Society - +1 sneak attack damage. This is a solid choice if you take your first level as an unchained rogue. +1 damage is especially nice for your low levels.

Reactionary - +2 initiative. Going first is important, especially for getting sneak attacks off.

Resilient - +1 Fortitude. Fortitude is your weak save, it’s good to shore it up!

### Magic

Desperate Focus - +2 bonus on concentration checks. Half of combat casting! Concentration checks are important.

Magical Knack - +2 CL brings you to full caster level despite your dip. Even if you only get 1 CL out of this, it’s so worth it for spell pen checks, for spell durations, and for spell damage dice!

Magical Lineage - Reduces the spell level cost of applying metamagic to a specific spell by 1. This lets your scorching rays, or another ray spell, turn into a monstrous powerhouse with metamagic feats.

### Racial

Glory of Old (Dwarf) - Huge boost to your defenses

### Region

Wayang Spellhunter (Minata) - This does the same thing as magical lineage, and stacks with it!

### Religion

Lessons of Chaldira (Chaldira Zuzaristan) - Rerolls are amazing. This is a fine reason to worship Chaldira.

There’s also a campaign trait called Trap Finder, that you should not take. Campaign traits are extra powerful options that give specific bonuses geared towards the specific campaign. GMs should not allow you to take any campaign trait in the book unless there’s a specific reason to give it to you. Especially not a campaign trait that circumvents a spell option (Aram Zey’s Focus) that you already have in your arsenal.

Trap Finder should be used by GMs when that particular role is not able to be filled by a party member. If you have a hyper-sneaky swashbuckler who invests in disable device, let them take Trap Finder! They have no other way to reasonably get the ability to disable magical traps. We do have a way to get trapfinding, so we shouldn’t get the extra powerful option intended for a specific campaign that we’re not building a character for.

## Feats

As an arcane trickster, you’ll get 10 feats from level progression going from levels 1 to 20. If you’re a wizard, you might get an extra feat at level 5. But you also owe a feat for Accomplished sneak attacker. Your classes might pick up some feats such as scribe scroll or brew potion. That’s not many feats. There are so many options for feats, you need to be very careful and deliberate about what you take and when. For everything I said earlier about spending two feats on Iron Will + Familiar Bond, if you then take improved familiar, that’s almost ⅓ of all the total feats that you get to choose spent on having a familiar! Familiars are powerful, but you need to weigh your options carefully.

### Accomplished Sneak Attacker

This is what makes the whole build possible! Take this as soon as you have a source of sneak attack.

### Mind Strike

This feat is BONKERS. I build almost every Arcane Trickster I make around it. Will save penalty for 1 round equal to your number of sneak attack dice every time you land a sneak attack. And it extends itself every time you land more sneak attack damage? Holy crap! Amazing. Especially if you have Surprise Spells, and can throw out some will save targeting spells that will carry out your sneak attack. But I bet your cleric or bard would also like to target some will saves! You’ll be having fun late game dishing out -9 to will saves on a cold ice strike, following up with a dazing stone call.

Note that a spell always occurs with the order of cast -> save -> effect. As such, Mind Strike will never occur for the spell’s own saving throw, even if it’s something like a rider on a touch attack. For example, sneak attacking dazing Stone Call will not reduce the target’s save for the dazing effect. However, this is very easy to combo with cold ice strike or quickened fireballs. And Mind Strike even applies on a successful save! It triggers as long as you deal sneak attack damage, even if that damage is reduced!

### Additional Traits

This is often useful, but We don’t have all that many feats to spare. Plus, two of the most useful traits for us, Magical Knack and Magical Lineage are the same trait type. The Magic Exemplar trait is a serious tax that we don’t want to have to spend another Additional Traits feat on.

### Improved Initiative

Initiative is amazing for an arcane trickster. We very much want to go first.

### Great Fortitude

We struggle with Fortitude saves. Great fortitude helps shore up our biggest weaknesses. Lots of traps have poison, and you will fail to disarm them sometimes!

### Lightning Reflexes

Not only is this one of our strong saves already, but failing reflex saves is rarely as bad as will or fortitude saves. This isn’t worth a feat for us.

### Boon Companion

Four extra levels of familiar progression isn’t worth it, not when we know that we have 11 total levels outside of our casting class. Don’t waste a feat on this.

### Iron Will + Familiar Bond + Improved Familiar Bond

Iron will is a fine feat. Will is the most important save in the game. However, you already have a good will save. The only reason you’re taking Iron Will is for Familiar Bond, because familiars are fantastic! However, two feats is a lot, and you’re often going to want to go with an improved familiar while skipping over Improved Familiar Bond, which just grants your familiar the wizard familiar special abilities. Of those abilities, a few are useful, delivering touch spells, speaking with you, or scrying on them. Yet, you’ve already spent so many feats on this tree. Is it really worth it to spend another?

### Wasp Familiar + Wasp Familiar (again)

Why spend four feats to get a familiar with full wizard familiar abilities with an improved familiar species? Instead, you can have a wasp! Wasp familiar lacks flexibility over your choice of familiar, but it makes up for that by giving you two of the most powerful options, a flying greensting scorpion, and then an imp. Sadly, the imp gets a useless offensive ability instead of invisibility, but can you really ask for much more with these feats?

If your GM allows this as a flavor option for a different god or power, cool! Otherwise, consider how drawn you feel towards lust and revenge… This is not a bad reason to worship Calistra.

### Improved Familiar

This is an excellent upgrade to your familiar even if you get the default wizard/witch/arcanist familiar and then restore any of its progression. Look for familiars with useful Spell Like Abilities, languages you speak or can learn, and, most importantly, the ability to hold a want. The imp is the classic choice. At will invisibility is phenomenal, especially alongside an arcane trickster. But I love little pseudodragons!

### Shadow’s Shroud

This is a great way to essentially hide in plain sight while in locations with little cover. It’s a swift action, so works nicely with action economy, and you can essentially hide with it a number of times each day equal to your level (as long as you keep up with your knowledge: planes!)

### Skill Focus: Stealth + Hellcat Stealth

Skill Focus: Stealth is useful, sure. But it’s absolutely not what you want to be spending your feats on. You have so few feats, and so many more useful abilities to choose from.

But Hellcat Stealth, on the other hand… That is a worthwhile feat. Hellcat stealth gives you everything you want for your stealth. You can attempt to hide in any bright or dim light even when being observed! This gets past blindsight, blindsense, tremorsense, etc. Being observed doesn’t matter for your stealth! The -10 penalty hurts a bit, sure. But we had to take Skill Focus anyway, so whatever. Now, the language is stupid and can be read that if you’re observed in darkness, Hellcat Stealth doesn’t allow you to hide when observed. I think most reasonable GM’s would say Hellcat Stealth can be used in any light level. But if they don’t, then light yourself up with a spotlight (or just some jewelry with continual flame) for every combat, and hide in that light!

### Skill Focus: Stealth + Dampen Presence

Dampen Presence would be better if Hellcat Stealth weren’t so amazing. The reason that you’re unable to hide from creatures with blindsight or blindsense is because they can observe you through invisibility or concealment. If you just take Hellcat Stealth, being observed doesn’t even matter! There is a niche that Dampen Presence falls in, however. If you’re in an underground campaign where blindsight/sense is common and you don’t want to suffer the -10 penalty for using Hellcat Stealth, then Dampen Presence is a perfectly viable option.

### Skill Focus: Stealth + Eldritch Heritage (Shadow) + Improved Eldritch Heritage (Shadow)

With skill focus: stealth, we also have the option to take the eldritch heritage for the shadow bloodline. The first ability is hot garbage, but if we’re already a sorcerer with a bloodline that has a better ability (or who wants a bloodline familiar from their core bloodline), then we can take this Eldritch Heritage to gain Blood Havoc from this source instead. Then, we can follow up with Improved Eldritch Heritage for a Hide in Plain Sight that’s even stronger than the one from Hellcat Stealth.

### Signature Skill: Stealth

Signature Skill: Stealth gives you awesome abilities at every single level. At level 5, you can start sniping with only a -10 stealth penalty. Expert sniper takes care of the rest. At level 10, you can move faster in stealth with a much lower penalty. At levels 15 and 20, it gets nasty. First they’re flat-footed to you after you sneak attack them. Then, they’re flat-footed to everyone.

### Signature Skill: Disable Device

This is a method of being able to disarm magical traps without either spellcasting or a level in rogue. But you’re a spellcaster. Use Aram Zey’s Focus! Other than that, you get to disarm traps faster. Yay?

### Kobold Sniper or Expert Sniper

These are solid feats if you want to snipe. Either of them removes half the penalty for sniping. You probably don’t need both, though. Signature Skill: Stealth takes care of the other half while providing so much more as well.

### Kobold Ambusher

There’s a nice bonus! No penalty on stealth at all when moving up to full speed, and only a -10 when running. And this time it’s not completely overwritten by Signature Skill: Stealth!

### Friendly Shroud

This is a cool, flavorful feat that lets you hide your allies as long as you take a stealth check. However, it takes a round to do so, and you both have to remain still for the duration. Most group stealth checks happen when the party is moving.

### Deceitful + Conceal Spell + Improved Conceal Spell

Deceitful is worse than Skill Focus: Stealth. The skills are far less useful than stealth. Conceal Spell, however, is a nice way to cast spells while stealthing before getting Tricky Spells. Note the major drawback that you’re still speaking in a loud, clear voice. This hides spells in social situations, not while you’re stealthing.

Yet… a rod of silent spell costs 3000gp. Is it really worth taking 2 feats for this? Maybe if you’re infiltrating a ton of the time. Or if you can convince your GM to let you take Conceal Spell without requiring Deceitful.

Improved conceal spell just gives you a better chance to pull it off. The extra numbers help, but is it worth a feat?

Note! You can get Deceitful by choosing a Familiar with the Decoy archetype. This makes Conceal Spell a much more achievable goal.

### Deceitful + Cunning Caster

This feat was just pointed out to me, so hasn’t made it into other sections of the guides, or my sample builds, but it absolutely would have! If you take a Decoy familiar, you can gain deceitful instead of alertness, and then pull out Cunning Caster as a much more effective way of hiding your casting than Conceal Spell. The best thing about this feat is that, unlike Conceal Spell, it appears to fully cover your verbal components without having to still speak clearly and loudly.

### Extra Impromptu Sneak Attack

Impromptu sneak attack is a surprisingly strong feature. This is a very good boost. Though if you find that you’re never without the ability to sneak attack–with hellcat stealth and invisibility for example–this may not be worth picking up. Furthermore, we’re pretty feat starved already, so a small incremental bonus like this can be hard to justify.

### Point Blank Shot + Precise Shot

Precise shot is very helpful early on. Even when you’re targeting touch AC, -4 is a rough penalty to deal with. Point blank shot is a hard sell for a prerequisite, though. If your GM is using Elephant In the Room feat taxes (which they should be!) then precise shot is way easier to justify picking up. After level 5-7 or so, your attack will be getting high enough against touch AC that the penalty will barely matter anymore. Pick it up early and then retrain it

### Deadly Aim

Deadly aim doesn’t actually work for us! It works on guns, but not any other touch attack, and all of our spells with sneak attack are touch attacks.

### Weapon Finesse

Why? You have cantrips that let you shoot magic beams from your fingers. Sneak attack dice work with those cantrips. Don’t spend a feat on this! … If you have Elephant in the Room, you get this for free, though!

### Weapon Focus (Ray) + Anatomical Savant

Weapon focus is just +1 to hit. Maybe not worth a feat for us since we target touch AC and hit so easily. But, to get to Anatomical Savant…

I want this so bad… But it’s impossible. Even with Fractional BaB, at level 20, you’ll only have +10 BaB when you need 12. Maybe your GM will be nice and let you take this early around the point when a rogue could T_T

If you have a very lenient GM, you can pull off a trick by using Emblem of Greed to gain BaB, and then Paragon Surge or Barroom Brawler/Training gauntlets to gian Anatomical Savant.

### Improved Critical

Improved Critical also needs Weapon Focus (Ray). So it’s more worthwhile if your GM lets you take Anatomical Savant. But! 19-20 crit range is very nice for you if you’re sticking with rays at high levels. However, at high levels you have surprise spells. So do you really want to stick to rays, when you can sneak attack sirocco?

### Critical Focus + Accursed Critical/Blighted Critical + Precision Critical

Unfortunately, you aren’t getting these until BaB +9, which is basically around max level. You can get to it a bit earlier if you take eldritch knight, but it’s still very late game. In either case, it’s awesome stacking another debuff on top of your sneak attacking spells! What’s not awesome is that it costs a swift action, so you can’t quicken spells on the same turn. And if you choose Accursed Critical, that’s an immediate action which eats up the next turn’s swift action too.

### Staggering Critical

If you somehow manage to get to BaB +13 with eldritch knight levels (I think only at level 20 with fractional BaB), staggering critical is your best feat to apply with critical focus

### Arcane Armor Training

You can now wear +3 armor with +6 max dex, congratulations! … Instead of +4 armor and infinite max dex at hours/level for a first level spell. Or an enchanted haramaki.

### Arcane Strike

Arcane strike is maybe on par with Deadly Aim. At level 20, Deadly Aim gives +6 damage for -3 to hit. Arcane strike gives +5 damage for the cost of a swift action. As you get to higher levels, you want quicken spell. Is this really worth it?

### Item Creation Feats

Here’s a secret. At higher levels, for more expensive items, it can be cheaper to retrain a feat to the proper crafting feat, craft that item for half price, and then retrain the feat back to what you had it as before! But no, don’t take crafting feats in place of your normal feats. You’re feat-starved enough already!

### Spell Penetration + Greater Spell Penetration + Elven Spirit (Half-Elf)

Spell Resistance is awful. As you get to high levels, these feats basically become required. Elven Spirit is a nice bonus if you’re a Half-Elf.

### Spell Focus + Greater Spell Focus

Spell Focus and the Greater counterpart actually aren’t quite as important for an Arcane Trickster as they are for your typical wizard. In combat, you’ll often be targeting touch AC rather than a save.

However, as you get surprise spells, it’s likely worth retraining feats like precise strike to spell focus, as you begin to focus more on save-targeting spells that also do damage to trigger your sneak attack damage, these feats become fantastic. Boosting your DC’s is important!

### Spell Mastery

This is bad. It’s only really usable if you get dragged away to jail without your spellbook? But you’ll still have previously prepared spells. It’s really not worth a feat.

### Spell Perfection

This is amazing! Choose a level 5 spell to quicken for free every time you cast it.

### Toughness

You’re a bit squishy. However, even more so than the average wizard, you shouldn’t even be being targeted in the first place. You should be invisible, have mirror images, be stealthing, etc. All of that will help more than some hit points.

### Varisian Tattoo + Flumefire Rage

+1 CL is great as a blaster. But this really isn’t that critical to spend a full feat on.

Flumefire rage is very nice. We love having more damage per dice, since that applies to our sneak attack as well! However, the 15 + spell level fort save vs fatigued is rough when we have such poor progression for our fort saves. This feat will be unreliable, have a serious drawback, but still get us some awesome opening blasts in combats.

### Greater Feint

This is an okay way of ensuring your sneak attacks. But… it comes online just so late. Around levels 12-13 you should already have a consistent source of sneak attack. Maybe this is fine if you’re starting a game at that level.

### Deific Obedience

Deific Obedience gives a plethora of incredible buffs that are fantastic for literally any character. The flexibility of the spells, abilities, and obediences offered is well beyond what is normally packed in a feat. So why is part of the label red? Well… Because the obediences themselves aren’t often worth a feat, and you don’t get the real feat-worthy powers until 12 HD, then 16 HD, then 20 HD. So this feat that becomes green or purple if you take it at level 17. Cool if you reach that point.

### Eldritch Heritage

There are some good abilities that you can get from the eldritch heritage line. But it’s such a feat investment and we have so many other feats we want.

### Extra Hex

Hexes just don’t have much synergy for you…

### Extra Arcanist Exploit

Arcanist Exploits are strong! They’re worth picking up with feats.

## Metamagic Feats

### Intensified Spell

This is a very nice metamagic feat. For +1 level, you get to increase the scaling of that spell by 5-10 levels past where it otherwise would have ended. For a blaster type character, this is great to keep your lower level spells relevant! It becomes better as you get to higher levels where it actually significantly changes your damage. Before that, empower is often more effective. I find in practice, though, that Empower is often easier to prepare and use and more effective in the end due to the math with sneak attack. Intensified works with very specific spells, empower works with any spell. Though intensified battering blast is crazy good.

### Empower Spell

This is solid for improving damage. It goes great on a rod, or can be picked up if you’re more focused on damage than on utility feats. In most games, the math works in Intensified Spell’s favor. However, Empower Spell effects sneak attack damage! That’s a huge swing in its favor

### Maximize Spell

Maximize spell is on par with empower spell.

### Quicken Spell

Quicken metamagic rods are very expensive, and the action economy boost from having quicken spell is incredible. Once you start to have 6th to 7th level slots, you want this feat.

### Dazing Spell

Dazing spell isn’t quite as necessary as quicken, but it provides us some very important utility. Mind Strike naturally synergizes with effects like dazing spell that put will saves on normally save-less spells. The easy combination when you have Surprise Spells is Dazing Stone Call. However, that’s much easier to stick on a rod than quicken. Plus, it won’t be super important until you have surprise spells at levels 15+

### Reach Spell

This is another good spell to have a rod of.There are a lot of good touch spells that can turn into great sneak attack spells with reach metamagic. But there are also a lot of other good options that make you wonder if it’s worth spending one of your few feats towards this.

### Persistent Spell

This is another classic good metamagic. It’ll be good for you, but mostly after the Arcane Trickster capstone. Before that, it will have fewer good targets for you.

### Piercing Spell

This is another great metamagic to have a rod of. It’s hard to justify preparing this unless you know that you’re facing an enemy with SR. It’s better on a sorcerer.

### Selective Spell

This is another classically useful metamagic. But again, why are we making our rays selective, or our utility spells selective? Mostly good after Surprise Spells.

### Silent Spell

If you’re going to do any infiltration, this could be a much easier pickup than the Conceal Spells feat. As a wizard, you can much more easily prepare for an infiltration and set up a spellbook full of silent spells that you plan to cast in nearly empty rooms.

### Solid Shadows

Good if you want to use a bunch of Shadow Spells. However, although realness % is good, pumping the DC is more reliable. Tenebrous Spells is more useful for a shadow caster.

### Tenebrous Spell

A better spell for shadow casters. +1 DC and no increase to spell level!

### Still Spell

This is far less useful. You can duck behind concealment to hide the somatic components. Verbal components were the issue we really needed to solve. We can hide hand movements with more mundane means.

### Elemental Spell

We don’t want to take this, but if you didn’t take the admixture wizard school, then you sure what to get a rod of this! Elemental resistances suck, and it’s important to have a way around them.
