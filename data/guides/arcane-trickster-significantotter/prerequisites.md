# Prerequisites and Entry Classes

## Sneak attack +2d6

One of the main reasons I’m making this guide is that older guides are missing one of the most important options for the arcane trickster, the feat Accomplished Sneak Attacker from the 2015 Dirty Tactics Toolbox. In exchange for one feat, you essentially replace two full levels of non-casting prerequisite class (or a second dip in a martial class). This means that the arcane trickster requires only a single level dip in a non-casting class, which greatly ramps up your effectiveness as a caster. A wizard, for example, will be able to fully progress as an arcane trickster while never falling behind the spell level progression of a sorcerer. And sorcerers won’t have to sacrifice 9th level spells by only reaching 17th level casting as a 20th level character.

Really, there are three options for obtaining the sneak attack dice:

### Variant Multiclass: Rogue or Order of the Blossom Cavalier

Rogue VMC feels like a powerful option at first glance. If you value not losing a spellcasting level so highly with the single level dip + Accomplished Sneak Attacker, why not instead spend 0 levels on a dip while sacrificing more feats?

Five feats is a lot. I would happily spend two feats on 2d6 sneak attack, but Arcane Tricksters are surprisingly feat intensive. You potentially want Mind Strike, Skill Focus: Stealth, Hellcat Stealth, Spell Penetration, and Greater Spell Penetration. That would be all of your feats for the entire game. No flexibility, very few options. You want much more metamagic like Quicken Spell. You want an improved familiar. You might want deific obedience, or improved initiative. There are so many feats that will be more effective for you than gaining trapfinding, an extra 2d6 of sneak attack, evasion, and improved uncanny dodge, or tactician if you choose cavalier.

Furthermore, with the progression of VMC, we don’t get to enter Arcane Trickster until level 8. A VMC Rogue/Cavalier will necessarily want to retrain their level 5 feat to Accomplished Sneak Attacker once they hit level 7, so that they can qualify for Arcane trickster at level 8. Otherwise, you wouldn’t be able to enter Arcane Trickster until level 12. So you’d only ever be able to even gain 8 levels of arcane trickster. Eww. Even with Accomplished Sneak Attacker, you’re entering the class pretty late, and will only hit the arcane trickster capstone at level 18. Very few games go up to the 12-16 level range, let alone 17-20. Especially if you’re starting from level 1, pick an option that lets you feel like a cool arcane trickster for the majority of your levels!

VMC: Rogue/Cavalier will allow you to be a perfectly full caster without dipping, but while gaining some of the signature bells and whistles of these classes. However, for VMC rogues these bells and whistles are some of the weaker rogue abilities. Evasion is just… fine? Uncanny dodge is ‘You shouldn’t even be visible and easily targetable anyway’. I wish you got rogue talents instead. Cavalier gets tactician and greater tactician, which is neat, but not particularly synergistic for you. So this is perfectly playable, but you give up a massive amount of build flexibility. If you choose this option, it might be because you’re playing an arcanist, sorcerer, or another class with spontaneous caster spell progression, and you don’t want to be a full spell level behind the party cleric’s progression.

### Single Level Dip + Accomplished Sneak Attacker

This is the typical choice for entering arcane trickster. You sacrifice a level to dip into a class that gives you sneak attack, and then spend a feat to bring you up to 2d6 sneak attack to qualify for arcane trickster. With this we can qualify for Arcane Trickster at level 5 and only lose 1 level of spellcasting progression and one feat. It’s the best of all worlds!

These are the class options:

#### Unchained Rogue

This is the classic way to enter the arcane trickster. You get trapfinding and finesse training alongside your sneak attack, making you a surprisingly decent melee finesse fighter in the first two or so levels. However, finesse training won’t matter for the rest of your build, and trapfinding is something you can also get from the spell Aram Zey’s Focus. I do love the 8+INT skill points, though. If you’re a sorcerer arcane trickster, you can avoid having to spend a spell known on Aram Zey’s focus, and the skill points will be invaluable for satisfying the Arcane Trickster requirements.

#### Vivisectionist Alchemist

Vivisectionist Alchemist not only grants 1d6 sneak attack on a dip, but also gives you 10 minutes of +4 dex and +2 natural armor every day, a free feat for brewing potions, bonuses to crafting alchemical items, more Int-based first level spells for your repertoire with some outstanding options. and … throw anything? I mean they can’t all be winners. In any case, the Vivisectionist offers so many useful options on a single level dip that it blows all of the other options out of the water.

When looking at alchemist spells, pay attention to some of the exclusives. The spells that only alchemists get, or that they get earlier than other classes. Tears to Wine is one of my favorite low level buff spells out of combat, and it’s a level 1 alchemist spell. Waterproof lasts for 24 hours, and you never need to waste a wizard slot on it. You can help shore up injuries at low levels with Cure Light Wounds. Boost your effectiveness in combat with Reduce Person.

Archetype: Internal Alchemist

Why do you need to throw things? You don’t have bombs! Instead you get to… hold your breath? You’re giving up an ability you’ll basically never use for one that you might use once in a blue moon. This stacks with Vivisectionist and Mindchemist.

Archetype: Mindchemist

Instead of boosting your Dex with a mutagen, you get to boost your Int! But good things come at a cost. It’ll be very annoying to deal with 2 points of strength damage every time you use this at low levels. It’ll be more and more useful though as your divine casters end the day with extra casts of lesser restoration. +4 Int is +2 DC to your spells and +2 to a lot of important stuff for you. You’re exchanging your Dex mutagen for this. Dex helps you hit, but you already target touch AC and will have no problems with accuracy for most of the game. You additionally give up poison use. Because… you were going to poison your scorching ray? This archetype is very worth it!

#### Ninja

Alchemist lets us trade poison use for Cognotogens. 1 level in Ninja gives us nothing but poison use and sneak attack, and there’s not even an archetype to swap poison use out for something more useful!

#### Snakebite Striker Brawler

It’s sad that martial flexibility is what Brawler has to give up for sneak attack. It could have been a really cool option for a dip if we could swap out feats on the fly. Unfortunately, all we’re left with is brawler’s cunning (we’re probably already int based), martial training which lets us qualify for nothing, and Improved Unarmed Strike. We use spells, not fists. At least we get +1 BaB? That’s the only reason this is orange.

#### Assassin

It’s like the Ninja dip, only we need to be level 5 before we can enter it, and a neat death attack thing. The problem is that the death attack save is 10+INT+½ assassin level. So that’s 11+INT for us. Paralyzed/death is for sure a powerful effect, but that’s a hard save for a creature to fail at level 6 and up. And now we aren’t entering Arcane Trickster until level 7 which is pretty annoying.

#### Master Spy

Master of disguise is a cool effect for flavor. I might make this prestige class orange if it didn’t require two feats and 24 skill ranks (with one being at least 7 ranks!) to enter it! At least Iron Will is useful for getting Familiar Bond. But this is way too much investment for some mild flavor abilities alongside the sneak attack dice.

#### Sleepless Detective

This is much better. If we have a familiar, we already have Alertness for free. The required skills are all useful. We can enter this at level 6 and be in arcane trickster at level 7 (after retraining a feat to Accomplished Sneak Attacker). Adding Int to all Perception and Sense motive checks is pretty awesome, and Forensic Thaumaturgy gives a neat extra feature to detect magic by allowing you to detect the duration of an aura. If you start a game at level 8-10, this could be a fun way to enter Arcane Trickster.

#### Inner Sea Pirate

You get nothing. +1 fort save. That’s all you deserve.

#### Red Mantis Assassin

There might be a build here with the Magus due to the saber ability? But this is a guide for full caster arcane tricksters. I guess the extra wizard spell is nice. It’s Charisma casting, though.

#### Field Agent

The only other thing you gain alongside sneak attack are the wayfinder upgrades. The silence one is… decent? But do you really want to delay until 6th level and spend a feat for a 1/day silence when you could have extracts or trapfinding or finesse training?

There is one last option, but it isn’t recommended:

### Two/Three Level Dip

Nope! We want caster levels and spell progression! Two-three levels of dipping is too much!

## Second Level Arcane Spellcasting

### Equipment Trick (Sunrod)

This technically works, but any rational GM will shut you down. It’s cheese of the highest order. Or they’ll at least give you the sideye. Besides, 3-4 levels in our casting class gives us some nice goodies and our feats are hard to come by.

### Class Options

#### Wizard

Wizard arcane tricksters are the bread and butter of the class. You get fast spell progression that is on par with spontaneous casters even after dipping. You have prepared spells, and can pay to purchase more spells known. As an arcane trickster, you really want flexibility in your spell repertoire. You should have a set of spells for infiltration, for investigation, for combat, etc. Wizards also gain some excellent front-loaded abilities. You can get a familiar or bonded item, as well as abilities and extra spell slots from your school.

One important note for wizard arcane tricksters: By rules as written, you don’t get two new spells added to your spellbook with each arcane trickster level. This is because of the specific language “Each time a character attains a new wizard level, he gains two spells of his choice to add to his spellbook.” This is even, apparently, the rules as intended. I think it’s horribly stupid, though. Arcane Trickster is a less powerful, more flavorful, option than progressing more as your base spellcasting class. All casting prestige classes are. Why make it even more expensive and painful to do so? Ask your GM if you can continue to gain two free spells with every arcane trickster level. Otherwise, your levels will grow increasingly expensive…

Familiar

I love familiars, and they’re fantastically useful. The problem here is that levels in Arcane Trickster don’t progress your familiar. So you get 3 levels of familiar advancement, and then nothing. Luckily, you can still get improved familiar due to arcane trickster advancing your wizard Caster Level, allowing you to take good improved familiar options. However, your familiar won’t even be able to Speak with Master until level 17. Pick an improved familiar that can speak! You can also potentially use Iron Will -> Familiar Bond to advance your familiar's progression, but are two feats worth Speak with Master, or Speak with Animals of its kind? I didn’t think so.

This could be a route you take if you choose any of the awesome wizard familiar archetypes. The sage familiar, the decoy familiar, the figment familiar, etc. These usually gain a lot of power with levels, and so they might drive you to invest some feats into your familiar.

Bonded Object

This is essentially a bonus spell of your highest spell level, as well as essentially a free crafting feat for that item. But, be warned, if you die, you lose the money you spent enchanting your bonded object, even if you’re breath of life-ed the round after. Maybe bond a wand, or another consumable so death doesn’t hurt your wallet too bad! Still, this feature is great for prestige class levels as no part of it scales off of your wizard level, just spellcasting level and wealth.

School: Evocation (Admixture)

Evocation (Admixture) provides everything you want in terms of combat capability for an arcane trickster. You get a solid +1 to damage which, though not impressive later, is very important to stack at low levels when you’re relying on cantrip damage. More importantly, it gives you the ability to change the elemental nature of any spell 3+INT times per day. As any other class or school, your biggest frustration in combat next to managing to sneak attack will be elemental resistances and immunities. If a creature is resistant to fire and cold, are you going to be stuck resorting to acid splashes when your snowballs and scorching rays are resisted? Nope! Just change the type of your scorching ray to acid. It’s a very straightforward boost, but it dramatically increases your effectiveness in combat and reduces the variety of elemental ray spells that you need to prepare. Furthermore, it allows you to focus on one spell. You can optimize a spell like scorching ray with metamagic master, wayang spell hunter, and spell perfection and then not see that investment go to waste against the all-too-common fire immunity.

School: Divination (Foresight)

You always act in surprise rounds, which is great. You get a minor +1 initiative bonus for most of the game. The reason to take this, though, is that 3+INT times a day, you can roll a d20 as a free action, and save that result for any roll you want up until your next turn. It’s not as good as a reroll, but it’s still very strong! Especially when you have high stakes stealth checks, disable device checks, etc.

School: Void

Void isn’t particularly synergistic with an arcane trickster, but its abilities are nuts and scale well for you. First off, flat +2 bonus to all saves against spells or spell-like abilities. That’s awesome. On top of that, you can reduce a target’s saves and AC by ½ your caster level as a standard action 3+INT times per day. That’s just bonkers. It’s great against enemies immune to sneak attack, or just ones that are really tough to hit. And because it scales with caster level, it keeps up with your Arcane Trickster progression!

School: Conjuration (Teleportation)

You won’t get beyond 5ft teleports for a long time, but getting 3+INT 5ft teleports as a swift action is awesome! It’s great for disengaging from enemies that might have rushed you.

School: Illusion (Mage of the Veil)

This is a neat little out of combat infiltration ability that gives no save. It’s only one minute per wizard level, but sometimes that can be enough! It’s not a particularly excellent ability, but it’s flavorful and certainly useful for games with lots of infiltration, especially crowd infiltration.

Archetype: Thassilonian Specialist

Thassilonian Specialist has high highs and low lows. You get two extra slots for each level, which is incredible, but your opposition schools are chosen for you based on your specialized school. Plus, because they remove the opposition spells from your list entirely, you can’t even cast scrolls of them or later take Opposition Research to recover those schools. On top of that, the Evocation specialty would require you to give up conjuration and abjuration. Eww. Wizards, especially arcane tricksters, are about flexibility. This sacrifices so much of your flexibility.

Archetype: Exploiter Wizard

Exploiter wizard is just fine. It’s usually a more powerful wizard, but in this case we’re interested in effects that scale without further wizard investment. If you can convince your GM to allow you to take Extra Arcanist Exploit despite not having the “Arcanist Exploits” class feature, that will make this option better, or convince them to allow wizard spells to count as arcanist spells for the purpose of Potent Spells. Only then does this hold up to the combined strength of a lot of the stronger schools plus the bonded object.

Good choices to use your initial exploit for are Quick Study, Bloodline Development (especially with an extra sorcerer dip to cheese full bloodline progression at the expense of an extra spell level. If you do this, I recommend waiting until you’re well into Arcane Trickster progression, though), or School Understanding (Though it’d be better to just start as that school, and retrain your archetype at level 17 or so)

Archetype: Spellslinger

The spellslinger exploiter wizard is a very interesting build. It acquires a lot of power by buffing its already strong ray attacks through the enchantment bonus of its gun, but takes the rough penalty of 4 opposition schools and no main school spell slots. The early game is easier as well, due to the power of an enchanted gun against low level monsters. The big question is if the extra accuracy, DC, and damage from the gun’s enchantment bonus is worth it. It may not be due to the already excellent accuracy of touch spells, but once you finally hit surprise spells, your cones with +5 DC will be devastating. However, that’s extremely late game.

#### Witch

Instead of a school ability and a familiar, you get two hexes, a much worse spell list (for a blaster/rogue) and a… familiar. Are the two hexes worth that cost? Evil Eye + Cackle gives you things to do at low levels. You can always take extra hex for more hexes. Fortune is good, but it’ll never scale for you. Worse still, your hex DC’s stop scaling as you level in arcane trickster.

When we look at the limited spell list, it gets even more painful. No Aram Zey’s Focus, no Scorching Ray…

Also, just like the wizard and arcanist, you don’t get your 2 free spells known each level. See the wizard section for my thoughts on that.

The witch is a debuffer. It’s a cool class that scales well and is fun to play. The arcane trickster is not just a debuffer. Although you can play a witch arcane trickster, you’re losing out on so much of what makes a witch effective. You’re taking a delicious cake and a delicious meatball sandwich, and trying to mush them together without regard for the fact that they don’t really work that well together.

For patrons, there are plenty of useful options. Storms gives you fog spells, there are a few that provide invisibility, mountain gives us the amazing stone call for surprise spells, and moon gives us darkness and darkvision. The infernal contract could give us a free misfortune hex for earlygame, but at the cost of damage that starts out extremely painful but becomes less relevant, as well as the much worse cost of the inability to be resurrected.

Ashiftah Witch

If you’re going to be an arcane trickster witch, this is the standout archetype to help make the witch provide at least some synergistic tools for you. Every ability this witch archetype offers helps with your sneak attack in some way, which is awesome! Unfortunately, they mostly help earlygame, and aren’t tricks that you’ll find yourself frequently using into lategame when you have far more spell choice.

Ghostwalk is fantastic. You get essentially an at-will vanish (albeit at the cost of move+standard action) using cackle. This is very useful out of combat. However, your GM might not be nice about how “cackling madly” interacts with your stealth check. Other options, if cackling creates too much noise, are the Congeal or Water Lung hexes, which have no limit on uses/day and can be used with just the witch herself. Protective Luck, or Ward to a lesser extent, are more useful but require having a buddy around.

Deliver touch spells is also wonderful for the earlygame witch Arcane Trickster. We don’t have the signature snowball at level 1, but we do have Chill Touch and all of the Inflict Wounds spells. Stricken heart will be another great option. It’s a full round action, though, so we might have trouble combining it with stealth and positioning, and it won’t play nicely with our action economy lategame.

Fog of War is also decent, providing us a more limited form of Ashen Path. However, we’ll probably only be getting it around level 19 when we should be geared to the teeth and a goz mask will cost pennies for us. Even if you go with VMC and wait until level 8 for your first AT level, this ability will be helpful, but won’t astound you.

#### Sorcerer

Sorcerer arcane tricksters are perfectly good, but they’re slower and have less utility than their wizard counterparts. They enter the prestige class later, and progress to powerful spells slower as well. However, they really excel on the damage side of things. Note that with Eschew Materials, we can use cheap Alchemical Reagents that are 1gp or less (such as Saltpeter or Brimstone) for free! It’s a subtle, but very noticeable, power boost.

Bloodline Mutation: Blood Havoc

This is the reason that you would play a sorcerer arcane trickster. Sacrifice utility for pure damage. +1 damage per dice is incredible. Sadly, I don’t believe that it applies to sneak attack damage on a spell, because sneak attack isn’t part of the spell’s damage. (This is argued about, and I can’t find a clear answer. It seems that precision damage on a spell modifies spell damage, which would in fact allow blood havoc to provide bonuses from it). Either way, it’s a very powerful option that scales with your arcane trickster spell progression

Bloodline Mutation: Blood Intensity

You’ll reach third level bloodline abilities before qualifying for arcane trickster, so you’ll have the opportunity to take this. Once per day, you basically get a supercharged intensify spell metamagic, only stronger because it works off of charisma. You’ll be throwing out powerful snowballs at mid levels, but then monstrous battering blasts and fireballs at high levels.

Bloodline: Arcane (Sage)

If you were going to be an int caster, why sacrifice a spell level and prepared casting to do so? I guess that this makes entering Arcane Trickster easier because at least you get skill points now. The first level ray attack ability is so bad, you’re practically required to replace it with blood havoc. Metamagic adept is meh. Normal metamagic casting time is good, but you still need the higher spell level to cast it.

Bloodline: Orc

This is again why you play a sorcerer arcane trickster. Take blood havoc as your first mutation, blood intensity as the second, and then jump into arcane trickster with +2 damage per spell dice supercharging your damage.

Bloodline: Draconic or Elemental (Primal)

These bloodlines are nicer than the Orc bloodline in most ways, but with a more restrictive damage per spell dice bonus for their arcana. Your battering blasts won’t be able to pack quite as much of a punch anymore, but you can get a better selection of bloodline spells, feats, and ability choices. … However, we should probably be swapping all of our bloodline abilities out for bloodline mutations anyway. Is the increased restriction on element type (which is very painful against enemy resistances) worth 3 better spells and a more useful feat like Empower or Quicken spell? Not in my opinion.

There are some interesting expanded bloodline options for the Draconic bloodline. The Esoteric Draconic bloodlines could give you psychic casting to enter the Arcane Trickster archetype, the Thought Thief. However, if you’re giving up the +1 damage per dice, I’d just go psychic bloodline. The Primal Draconic bloodlines let you reroll damage dice, but that will almost always end up being far less damage bonus than the simple +1 damage per dice. It has more versatility, though, without a type restriction.

Bloodline: Psychic

The psychic bloodline gets us access to the Thought Thief archetype for the Arcane Trickster. In my opinion, it’s the best way to access that archetype. Psychic casting has pretty good synergy with an arcane trickster, allowing us to cast from beast shape forms, gaseous forms, and hide our casting more effectively.

Bloodline: Empyreal (Celestial)

This bloodline gives you access to wisdom-based casting. There might be races, feats, or other options you’d like to try with it. Wisdom has some useful skills for us like perception and sense motive, and it’s nice for will saves, but I don’t necessarily feel that it’s an upgrade over intelligence for us. And I wouldn’t say that it’s worth the opportunity cost of a damage/dice bloodline, even one restricted to an element.

Archetype: Crossblooded

Crossblooded is great for dips. The classic Orc/Draconic bloodline with Blood Havoc for +3 damage per dice is alluring. However, the -2 will saves hurt, and the one fewer spell known at every level is just brutal. You already sacrificed utility to choose this route. Giving up so many of your precious few spells known is awful. The extra damage per dice is not worth it.

#### Arcanist

Similar to the Sorcerer, the Arcanist also sacrifices a spell level in exchange for class features. However, it doesn’t gain so much pure damage like the Sorcerer does. Instead, it aims to pump up DCs and offer some other nice casting utility. This makes it very good for Shadow Spell builds.

Unfortunately, many arcanist options grow with Arcanist level, not so much just with spell progression. There are excellent exploits such as Potent Magic and Quick Study. Bloodline Development doesn’t give you too much on its own, since you can’t take a mutation without being an actual sorcerer. You can also get a lot of value out of the feat Extra Arcanist Exploit, which is frequently stronger than a feat.

The Familiar exploit is also good, for the same reason as the wizard. If you want full progression for it, take Iron Will + Familiar Bond feats for a familiar that scales with you. Additionally, upgrade it with Improved Familiar. Better, Wasp Familiar is a familiar in a single feat. It’s restricted to Calistra, but see if your DM wants to re-flavor it.

Dimensional Slide is great for mobility. Shadow Veil is flavorful, but it’s only 20% concealment so it doesn’t let us stealth. Counterspell is a nice defensive option.

Arcane Discovery can get you Yuelral's Blessing for +1 CL and +1 DC on certain spells as well as Resilient Illusions for a shadow caster. But most of the good discoveries require at least 9 “Wizard” levels. You’ll never be a 9th level arcanist.

You can do some cool things with an extra dip in crossblooded sorcerer to cheese the wording of Bloodline Development. You essentially get two full bloodline progressions in exchange for a single dip. If you don’t want your GM to be annoyed at you, I would recommend offering to choose between bloodline powers at every level. This trick might be part of the cheesiest arcane trickster build. But it’s not one that I’d recommend pulling on a GM.

Additionally, see the note in the wizard section about the prestige class spell progression not granting the usual two free spells for your spellbook. The arcanist suffers from the same issue. Ask your GM if they’ll let you progress with spells known as usual.

An arcanist makes a good arcane trickster. But it gives up the rapid progression next to a wizard, and I don’t know that it gains enough in return. The arcanist arcane trickster uses Quick Study to have their vast library of spells ready on the turn of a dime, and Potent Magic to pump their caster levels to use brutally effective spell attacks.

#### Psychic

The psychic won’t let you qualify for the standard arcane trickster, but it does let us qualify for the odd little archetype to the arcane trickster, the thought thief. However, the Psychic really doesn’t have much going for it in synergy with the arcane trickster. On the plus side, it’s much easier to hide your casting with thought and emotion components. However, you have a miniscule arsenal of spells that actually work with your sneak attack before you finally get surprise spells. Thank goodness for telekinetic projectile! Worse, the Thought Thief’s abilities work off of charisma, and we’re an intelligence caster. Ugh. Maybe your GM will be nice and let them go off of your casting stat. As such, we want to pick a charisma discipline and make a marginal investment in charisma as well.

Our choices as a psychic will be about making up for these shortfallings. When we’re looking at a discipline, we want one that gives us a way to reliably deliver sneak attacks as an arcane trickster. We want to grab for Reach Spell metamagic very early, since most of our touch attack spells are melee touch, not ranged touch.

One big draw to a psychic is the powerful will save targeting magic, and incredible synergy with Mind Strike. We get to lower will saves by a crazy degree, and then we have the spell toolbox to make those will saves truly hurt.

Rebirth

This is the most reliable, useful discipline for us. Every day, we get to choose a spell from another caster’s list that’s one below the highest level that we can cast. We get to cast that spell spontaneously from our list. This is amazing flexibility out of combat. We get simulacrums, contingencies, clones, demiplanes, and all that jazz without having to invest the spells known. Early on, we get reliable sneak attack spells. With a single, reliable sneak attack spell under our max level, we can rely on metamagic feats to spread it around our spell slots. We could take chill touch, for example, and slap on reach metamagic, or heighten/intensify snowballs, etc.

Shadow

I want to like shadow. It seems like a great option for an arcane trickster to reclaim our flexibility and synergize with the strengths of the Psychic. However, this is a wisdom based discipline, and the only discipline spell that we really want doesn’t come online until level 10. Why does the shadow discipline have only one single mirror magic spell? Ugh. This means that we would have to wait until level 10 to enter Arcane Trickster. And that means that we need VMC to gain our sneak attack if we ever want the Surprise Spells capstone (which we do!). Worse, none of the other abilities in the discipline are really that helpful to the shortcomings that we’re trying to make up with the psychic.

Faith

This is a weird one! The reason we’re drawn to this discipline is the spontaneous cure/inflict spells. We’ll take inflict, and then use reach metamagic for a decent ranged touch attack! Sadly, we can only cast one cure/inflict spell per level per day. We get some other goodies too. Bless and spiritual weapon are very nice early utility spells. Unfortunately, Resilience of the Faithful won’t matter much as we’ll quickly be leaving the Psychic class.

Other Disciplines

We don’t really have any other disciplines that help us with our core shortcomings–getting early sneak attack spells before we jump into arcane trickster levels. However, we can make do and choose disciplines for other reasons. I would recommend looking at a guide on the psychic. There are some nice goodies, like the Abomination which helps us with an extra +1 to our will DCs, synergizing with Mind Strike.
