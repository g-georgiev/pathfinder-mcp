# Races

Race selection highly depends on your entry class. I'm only going to recommend races for the possible witch/wizard/arcanist arcane trickster, as these are the Int-based arcane tricksters. Use any of the sorcerer guides to find good races for a sorcerer arcane trickster.

Primarily, as an arcane trickster, you’re going to want Intelligence and Dexterity. Constitution and Wisdom are also as useful as they are for any character with saves and hp. Charisma could be useful depending on how much you want to leverage face skills, or if you’re an arcanist.

I’m only covering races that I find notable here. There are plenty of other options, both good or bad, but I would mostly judge them on their ability scores, and then they might have some flavorful racial abilities that won’t be too impactful.

## Human

An extra feat is always fantastic, and you love your skill ranks. I only rate this lower than the average guide does as you really want your ASIs, and have two important primary abilities. If there was a feat that gave you +2 dexterity, would you take it? Of course you would. So is it actually better to take a single +2 ability score boost (that will go to Int) and a feat rather than a race that gives +2 Int and +2 dex? Or even +2 Int and +2 con? The feat gives Humans a leg up on other races that just offer a +2 bonus to a stat you need like half-elves, but not over a race like Ratfolk with +Int, +Dex, small size, and stealth-focused abilities. Humans also don’t have great abilities for a stealthy character. Natural Darkvision is super nice, though you can always reserve a casting of the second level Darkvision spell to fill that need.

## Half-elf

Half elves are great arcane tricksters. They have access to the racial bonus spell penetration feat Elven Spirit as well as access to a wide variety of useful traits. They can get the trait Adaptability for Skill Focus: Stealth, or Illustrious Urbanite for a Spell Focus.

The racial spell Blend gives you Hide in Plain Sight as a 10 minute/level first level spell. It has the major drawback of ending as soon as you attack, but it’s still absolutely bonkers, and invaluable to every arcane trickster. This can be a prebuff for you in literally every situation. Even better, the racial spell Paragon Surge provides a flexible free feat.

In PFS, racial spells like Blend straight up cannot be learned by other races. In plenty of other games, racial spells originated with a race, and there’s no reason they can’t be learned by other races. If blend is available to any PC, half-elves don’t really have much of a leg up on humans. You’re trading a bonus feat for a bundle of abilities that aren’t super relevant to an arcane trickster outside of +2 bonuses to certain skills or darkvision which you can find on many more effective races.

## Halfling

Halflings are very nice arcane tricksters due to their alternate racial trait Human Shadow. This gives halflings a constant source of cover for stealth, which makes their combats far easier, especially early on before they have access to greater invisibility. The stats suit a Sorcerer or Arcanist arcane trickster more so than a Wizard, but if you plan to be a face, you can make do with these stats even as a Wizard. It hurts to start with 16 Int instead of 18, though.

## Aasimar

I love Aasimar. They have fantastic stat blocks and great flexibility for ability scores (multiple choices of +2 +2 and no penalty is amazing!) with the alternate heritages. But what I love most is that you can choose any base race for the aasimar stat block! And that base race can even change the aasimar to be small. So if you want to be a kobold, but don’t want the -2 con and -4 strength penalties, you can be a kobold aasimar! This is true for Aasimar, Tiefling, and any of the elemental races.

Azata-blooded aasimar are perfect for a sorcerer arcane trickster. Peri-blooded Aasimar are great for an Arcanist arcane trickster, or even good for a Wizard arcane trickster.

## Tiefling

Tieflings have many of the same benefits of the Aasimar, but in my opinion have slightly weaker racial abilities. Unlike aasimar, they have a weak ability score in conjunction with their two strong ability scores, putting them more on par with the average race. They’re plenty strong still, however, and catch up to the aasimar with further combinations of ability scores through alternate racial heritages. Aasimar don’t have a +Int +Dex option, but Tieflings have the Daemon-Spawn who even get skill bonuses to disable device and sleight of hand! Tieflings are an all-around excellent choice.

## Catfolk

Catfolk are solid sorcerer arcane tricksters with their (admittedly common) +Dex +Cha ability scores. Where they stand out is in the ability to start off with a 20ft climb speed. If you’re playing an urban game, there’s nothing nicer for a low level rogue-ish character than a climb speed! However, any race of arcane trickster will have access to spider climb, or eventually fly/overland flight. Still a good option.

## Kobold

I love these little scaly buddies. You get a bonus to Dex, though the Con penalty certainly hurts. I just wish Kobolds got an Int or Cha bonus to make up for all those penalties. For a short time, kobolds had their time in the sun with these builds using the feat Kobold Sniper, which you could combine with Signature Skill: Stealth for no penalty to stealth while sniping. That’s a great boon for sneak attacking in combat. However, Expert Sniper means that you never need Kobold Sniper to reach -0 sniping penalty by level 5.

## Ratfolk

Ratfolk have excellent ability scores for an int-based arcane trickster, and generally decent abilities. There’s a slight annoyance that comes from choosing between 60ft darkvision and a 20ft speed versus a 30ft speed and low light vision. You can make up for either eventually with the darkvision spell, or expeditious retreat, or just a fly speed. In early levels, 20ft movespeed can really hurt when jumping from cover to cover. Despite that, Ratfolk have an excellent statblock for an arcane trickster, with some useful racial options such as Skulk.

## Sylph

Sylph have the +Int +Dex abilities we want, but unfortunately with a Con penalty. No one likes a con penalty. Other than that, their abilities are fairly unremarkable. Plenty of useful, flavorful stuff, but nothing that stands out as exceptional. They’re still a solid choice, as they buff the main stats for an Int based arcane trickster, but you will probably want to spend a feat on Great Fortitude to shore up your fortitude saves. The Subrace options are also fine for a sorcerer arcane trickster, but still just an okay option.

However, I wanted to include Sylphs to point out another option that lets you choose any base race, not just a human. Yay flavor and interesting races! Be an orc arcane trickster with a djinn ancestor!

## Samsaran

Samsaran are always a good option for any full caster class. There are so many amazing spells locked behind other spellbooks. Even with the constitution penalty, the improved flexibility makes up for it.

This is also an excellent way to make up for the limited spell options on the psychic list for sneak attacking if you choose to go with the Thought Thief.

## Races with an Int/Cha bonus and a Dex bonus

Any race with a bonus to Dex and your main casting stat will be a good choice

## Races with an Int/Cha bonus and no Dex penalty

Any race with a bonus to your main casting stat that doesn’t penalize Dex will be fine!

## Races with a Dex bonus and no Int/Cha penalty

Any race that gives you a Dex boost and doesn’t hurt your main casting stat isn’t as good as one that boosts your main casting stat, but it’s still fine!

## Races with no penalty to Int or Dex, but no bonus to either

You can play an arcane trickster without bonuses to your main stats from your race, but you’re making it harder on yourself.
