# Alchemical Reagents

Alchemical reagents are a little-known piece of content from the Alchemy Manual and Adventurer’s Armory. They let different alchemical items serve as bonus material or focus components to spells to provide different effects. We’re going to use them to make our cantrips ever so slightly more effective at low levels so that our damage isn’t that far behind. I’m only going to point out the ones I think are important for an arcane trickster, but there are tons of effects at a fairly cheap per-cast price!

Acid (10gp/dose) - This adds +1 damage to acid splash as a focus component. We can also expend it to cause Acid Arrow to last an extra round, or grease to deal a tiny amount of damage (giving us sneak attack with surprise spells!)

Alchemist’s Fire (20gp/dose) - +1 to hit for all scorching rays as a focus component. That’s… fine. We’ll rarely miss anyway. The other effects are forgettable.

Darkwood (2gp/dose) - expend it for +1 CL for conjuration creation effects. Snowball is Conjuration creation! Early on, we can add +1d6 damage for 2gp each cast. How worthwhile that is depends on you.

Liquid Ice (40gp/dose) - +1 damage to Ray of Frost as a focus component. Fantastic early on! Ray of Frost will be our backup cantrip for quite a while, and this damage adds up. Unfortunately, this is a pricey component, so I’d only really recommend it for the cone of cold material cost, which adds +1 damage/CL.

Saltpeter (1gp/dose) - 1 gp for 1 damage on any fire spell. This can be worth the cost if you have a good amount of income, but I rarely bother.

Myrrh (.5gp/dose) - Keep like 10 of these on you at all times. +1 CL to dispel magic is awesome. And it’s so cheap, too!
