# Skills

As an arcane trickster, you’ll be pigeon-holed into a few initial skills, and then have a large number of skill points to spread out. …unless you’re a sorcerer, in which case you have to be far more discerning. In either case, when I rate these skills by color, I’m doing while judging the direct usefulness to the average arcane trickster.

You can be an arcane trickster with handle animal and ride, bringing around her trusty steed. That’s perfectly okay! These just aren’t skills that will come up for the average arcane trickster.

## Acrobatics

Acrobatics are often solidly useful out of combat, and have in combat uses as well. It can be very important to have a high enough acrobatics check to tumble away from an enemy in your face. However, as an arcane trickster, you should have plenty of magical options to escape as well. Everything in acrobatics is helpful and useful, but can also be accomplished with spells and magical flight.

## Appraise

Appraise is a fun out of combat skill. But it really doesn’t have much application. The RAW rules for it are a mess and universally ignored. Often, it just comes as arbitrary identify checks where the DM just tells you the value anyway. Plus, you can literally just cast the spell Identify.

## Bluff

Bluff is good out of combat. Cover your tracks, lie your way into a target’s home so you can check out the defenses. Or, in combat, use feints to ensure your sneak attacks. Again, there are spells to cover you if you don’t have great bluff. And the in-combat uses are limited due to the BaB requirements of feint feats.

## Climb

Climb is strength based, and extremely easily replaced by magic. Pick up spider climb with your level 2 spells and never look back! Or better, get fly or overland flight!.

## Craft

Craft can be fun and flavorful for your character. But it really isn’t all too useful. The most useful it will be is with crafting Alchemical Reagents using Craft: Alchemy

## Diplomacy

Diplomacy is a fantastic out of combat skill. It’s not needed for the average arcane trickster, but is great if you want to be the face.

## Disable Device

Disable device is very important. Your GM may not throw traps in front of you often, but when they do, you’ll be glad you have this! You never want the party to stand in front of a locked door and realize that no one has disable device. This is your shtick! You get ranged legerdemain! Put points in this!

## Disguise

Disguise can be neat. But you can cast Disguise Self as a first level spell…

## Escape Artist

Escape artist is also required to enter Arcane Trickster. Now, you absolutely want a way to get free when you’re grappled. But that way is the best level 4 spell in the game, Freedom of Movement. It’s not on your list, but get a wand, get a cleric/bard in the party, or get the ring.

## Fly

Fly gets more and more important throughout the game. At high levels, the whole party is flying all the time. And you don’t want to mess up your flight. This is usually a great target for headband of Int skill ranks.

## Handle Animal

Handle animal just doesn’t come into play for a usual arcane trickster.

## Heal

You get infernal healing if you want to heal. Or wands of cure light wounds. Unless you do one of those weird specific heal skill builds, the heal skill is of very marginal usefulness.

## Intimidate

Intimidate can be fun to use out of combat. There can be builds that use this in comment, but they’re feat intensive and not likely to synergize with an arcane trickster.

## Knowledge

Knowledge skills are great! Arcana is needed for Arcane Trickster. Planes is very common for outsiders. Religion for undead. Local for humanoids. Dungeoneering for aberrations. The others are less useful, but still keep them trained!

## Linguistics

Woo! Lots of languages. Also, forgeries can be fun. Unfortunately, these uses are also accomplished by a number of spells.

## Perception

One of the most important skills in the game. What’s the point of breaking into a lord’s manor if you can’t find his hidden safe?

## Perform

Can be flavorful, but just isn’t useful for the average Arcane trickster.

## Profession

See Perform.

## Ride

See Profession.

## Sense Motive

It’s great to know when an NPC is lying to you! There are some combat uses too, with feints, but those aren’t as important for a stealthy backliner like you.

## Sleight of Hand

As much as I want this to be better, sleight of hand just isn’t that important. It’s neat for pickpocketing, but when’s the last time you really pickpocketed something important? It can be fun for cheating in gambling games, or doing other tricks like that. It’s just not super commonly useful. However, it fits the rogue-ish playstyle, so I’ll upgrade it from red.

## Spellcraft

It’s good to know what’s being cast at you! And this is important if you ever want to dip into magic item crafting. Most importantly, though, whenever you copy a spell to your spellbook, you have to make a 15+spell level check to copy it. Don’t fail that or you’ll waste your money and can’t try again for another week!

Note, of course, that you can take 10 to copy spells because you aren’t in danger. So just make sure you have enough of a bonus to copy the spell levels you want, with maybe a buffer for negative levels or other effects.

## Stealth

Stealth is your most important skill, both in and out of combat. It’s your source of sneak attack damage, and it’s how you scout.

## Survival

This can be more or less useful depending on how wilderness-focused your game is.

## Swim

Learn Touch of the Sea or Free Swim if you need to! Though this can take some points if the campaign has a lot of aquatic content.

## Use Magic Device

UMD gives you tons of versatility. You can cast with wands of cure light wounds, freedom of movement, or give those to your familiar and have them cast these on you.
