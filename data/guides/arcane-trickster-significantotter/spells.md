# Wizard/Sorcerer Spells for the Arcane Trickster

Let’s face it. Most arcane tricksters will be using the Sorcerer/Wizard spell list. A lot of these options are on the witch list too, but I’m not going to go through every witch spell in the game when the class, as I’ve said a dozen times in this guide already, doesn’t synergize well with the Arcane Trickster.

As with the Equipment section, I’ve gone through every Sorcerer Wizard spell in the game to pull out just the ones I think are notable or useful for the average arcane trickster. I may exclude spells like Winter Feathers. It has such a specific, difficult target and Endure Elements is already an option. It’s just not as useful for you to know in the long run. Other otherwise useful spells like Gravity Bow won’t make it in, because your combat role isn’t to buff other individuals. It’s not a spell you look at and would care about. However, I will include options that you might be tempted to take, but aren’t worthwhile. I likewise will include options that are bad to prepare, but good to have as a scroll. Those tend to be most orange options.

For the full list of spells, see the following:

## Cantrips

Acid Splash - While this would usually be better than Ray of Frost due to the lack of SR and the better acid typing, it isn’t a ray. Ray spells carry all of your weapon attack damage buffs. Regular ranged touch attack spells don’t necessarily do so. However, this can still be a fine choice if you don’t have an ability like admixture, an acid elemental rod, or don’t have any weapon damage buffs in your first level or two. Remember that you can use Acid as an Alchemical Reagent focus component to increase the damage by 1 without consuming the Acid!

Detect Magic - if you aren’t preparing this spell, something is wrong. The most valuable items are often magical, and this gives you a beacon that leads right to them. Additionally, it points out any magical traps, and you can use spellcraft to figure out what any lingering spell effects are.

Read Magic - This is another pivotal spell. How are you going to disarm a magical glyph trap that you can’t read? How are you going to decipher scrolls that you can’t read? How are you going to copy spells into your spellbook when you can’t read other spellbooks? You can maybe get away with not preparing this at low levels until you need it. But those scrolls will sit in your pack unread and unusable until you get around to preparing it.

Ray of Frost - This is your ray cantrip. Learn to love it! This will deliver most of your sneak attacks early on. Remember that you can enhance its damage by 1 with the alchemical reagent liquid ice. Since it’s a focus component, it doesn’t get consumed!

Mage Hand - Mage hand is super versatile and useful. 5lbs isn’t a lot, but it’s often enough to trigger pressure traps or lift a key from the corner of a locked cell. Note that it targets 1 object. Is a doorknob a separate object from a door? If so, mage hand could open a door. If not, you need Open/Close.

Message - Message is fantastic. It’s extremely useful to exchange private words while in tense social situations. And this cantrip doesn’t require you to cast it every time you want to exchange those words! It lasts 10 minutes/level, so you can cast it before entering your social situation. Even better, your allies can whisper back a reply! The only downside is the DC 25 perception check for individuals nearby your target to hear as well. You also have to mouth the words and whisper, be careful of those who can read lips!

Prestidigitation - This cantrip is fantastic for roleplay value, but my controversial opinion is that I don’t think it sees as much real, mechanically impactful use as other cantrips. I like the cleaning aspect of it, the chilling, warming, or flavoring of things, and the creation of little items. You can use it like mage hand, but only for ⅕ of the weight that mage hand handles. I’ll always prepare it for the flavor uses, but I just don’t think it has the same actual impact as something like mage hand.

## Level 1

Expeditious Construction 📜 - Create your own cover to stealth behind! This is really nifty.

Glue Seal - Reverse Grease! This is a neat spell. I’d prepare it at first level before I can sneak attack, or as a backup to cast if I can’t sneak attack in a turn at low levels.

Mage Armor - Your everyday hour/level defenses until you get really good enchanted harimakis or silken ceremonial armor.

Obscuring Mist - Obscuring mist is an extremely easy way to get total concealment against enemies. However, you suffer from total concealment against them too. Consider Ashen Path (2nd level transmutation) to let your whole party see through it and gain total concealment from your enemies.

Stone Shield - This is quick, easy cover, and a great immediate action defense against ranged attacks. The problem is that you still need to move to stealth behind cover, and as you go up in levels, the AC boost won’t matter nearly as much as your AC lags behind.

Comprehend Languages 📜 - Remember that this is not Tongues. It doesn’t let you speak the language. A great scroll to have, or spell to prepare when you enter a town where folks don’t speak common.

Ears of The City - This is a surprisingly useful spell. When you’re going into a city, there are frequently pieces of information that you’re hunting for. This spell helps you gather the information with multiple checks and, even better, lets you use perception instead of diplomacy.

Heightened Awareness - This buff is fantastic. You want to be good at perception and knowledges, it lasts for 10 minutes per level which is usually a whole scene or bout of dungeoneering, and you can trade it for a great initiative bonus if you get into a fight!

Identify 📜 - This lets you replace appraise. Either keep scrolls of it, or swap out preparations of this after you’re done with a dungeon or heist to learn about all your magical loot.

Kreighton's Perusal 📜- This is a fantastic wand. You broke into the secretary’s room, but there’s no time to go through all the ledgers? I marked it with a scroll, but it’s way better as a wand so that you don’t need verbal components on it.

Locksight 📜 - A time saver when you’re infiltrating a building! No need to check every door, just know at a glance. It’s hours/level too, so you can throw it on before attending the big ball, and scope out the lock status of every door you get shown past. Don’t prepare this daily, but it’s great when you’re infiltrating some place.

Speechreader's Sight - This could be a cool, situationally useful buff when spying. Now you don’t even need to get in the same building, as long as you can see mouths moving through a window. It lasts hours, but how often will you really need it?

Keep Watch - I like this a good bit more than alarm. I’d rather have my own perception check watching the camp than a 20ft radius detection spell.

Snowball - She ain’t much, but she’s our level 1 ranged touch attack spell that can carry sneak attack. It’s too bad that snowball has the same elemental typing as ray of frost, and that it isn’t a ray so doesn’t get weapon damage buffs.

Blend (elf) - This is like invisibility minus the +20 bonus, but also hide in plain sight. Insane for us as a first level spell. However, it’s an elf racial spell.

Blend With Surroundings - This is another fantastic way to get stealth off at low levels. The +20 completely negates your sniping penalty, and you also get the benefits of hide in plain sight. However, it’s easily countered by enemies walking up to you and punching you in the face, since the spell ends if you move.

Shadowfade - This is very cool! It’s a nice, early way to deal with darkvision. The effect of this is practically equivalent to ashen path + obscuring mist for any creatures without extra senses. The problem is that it’s both very conditional (on dim light/darkness conditions) and doesn’t handle other senses like Blend With Surroundings does. It’s still a good option, especially early on when darkvision is the most powerful extra sense you’ll see frequently.

Vanish - This is a very flexible, useful first level spell for you. It’s a way to get out of a pickle, or to reset your stealth.

Chill Touch - This is the most tempting reach metamagic spell on the level 1 list for us. It carries our sneak attack, and we get to do ability score damage! The problem is there are probably better reach metamagic targets by the time we can afford that.

Ant Haul 📜 - When this is most important at low levels, it won’t last long enough to be great. At higher levels, you’ll have bags of holding. It’s still good to have around, especially to haul away loot you stole.

Coin Shot - The perfect spell for our Vin cosplay! Coin shot doesn’t scale like snowball does, but it’s a fun little flavorful ranged spell that sustains you for a few turns. It’s still better for you than snowball at CL 2, but snowball is quickly better after that.

Expeditious Retreat - This is very useful for getting from cover to cover or infiltrating. However, I’d focus long-term on a wand of longstrider due to the hour/level duration, even if the bonus is smaller.

Feather Fall - This will only come up every once in a while, but when it does, you’ll be so glad for it.

Keyhole 📜 - This is such a cool, flavorful spell for us! Break into any portal, even if it doesn’t have a lock or even a way to open it from your side. It’s also excellent on a scroll, as it only needs a saving throw if you cast it on a magical portal.

Monkey Fish 📜 - The speed is slow and the duration is just minutes/level, but this spell offers so much movement flexibility. It’s super useful. I always pack this with me until I get spider climb or flight.

Reduce Person - This is a solid buff for you

Snapdragon Fireworks - This is classically an amazing spell to mix with Dazing Spell and Heighten spell metamagic. And once you get surprise spells, it can carry sneak attack on the first hit. It’s pretty cheesy, though. Your DM may get frustrated with you shutting down encounters like this. It also very much does not fit your flavor or playstyle.

Windy Escape - This is amazing at high levels when you have first level slots to dedicate. At low levels, it’s not quite as good. Immediate action immunities are always fantastic. This can save your ass against crits or enemy rogue sneak attacks. This gets better to prepare the more slots you have to devote to your bread and butter spells.

## Level 2

Resist Energy 📜 - This is a fantastic pre-buff when you have an idea of what kind of elemental attacks your enemy is going to use. It’s also great to keep a scroll of, in case you see a dragon flying overhead and want to reduce the pain of its breath. Keep a scroll at low levels, prepare it at high levels when you have extra slots.

Acid Arrow - This would be a great spell for our sneak attacks if scorching ray didn’t exist. Unfortunately, although it’s a good elemental typing and has decent damage to carry our sneak attack, it’s not a ray, so it doesn’t get weapon bonuses to damage like inspire courage. Note, however, that this does pierce spell resistance. It’s a good choice if you’re dealing with monsters with difficult SR. As you get to higher levels, keep a few of these against SR.

Glitterdust - This is a solid backup spell for you if you can’t get sneak attacks off. Not only does it reveal invisible enemies who would be immune to your sneak attacks, but it also potentially blinds them which would give you sneak attack against them if they don’t have anything like blindsight.

Stone Call 📜 - This is fantastic battlefield control, and even decent for you. If you pick this as a backup for when you can’t sneak attack, it’ll actually still turn into a great sneak attack spell with Surprise Spells around level 15. At that point, you can get nasty and make this a dazing spell too. This has no save, so it’s also a nice scroll!

Aram Zey's Focus  📜- Ok. So, I made this spell purple because it’s an amazing spell to have in your repertoire. If you didn’t take a level in rogue, this is a must for you. You get a rogue’s trapfinding (based on character level, not CL, great for a scroll), and you can reduce the duration to reroll trap disarm failures where you set the trap off! The only reason I rated it half green is because you really don’t need to be preparing it all the time. It doesn’t have a huge duration, but still fits very nicely on a scroll.

Detect Thoughts - Information is power to you, and this is a reliable way of gathering information… if you have silent and still spell on it. Otherwise you’re standing in front of someone, chanting really loudly, and making magic gestures while you stare at them real hard. Not subtle. It’s green once you have tricky spells. This is a spell that you need to excuse yourself from the ball to go cast in the bathroom.

See Invisibility 📜 - This is a very useful spell to have around, and honestly a solid pre-buff as you go into a dungeon. This is also why you can’t rely solely on invisibility for your sneak attacking.

Investigative Mind 📜 - This is a really great out of combat buff! I love 10 minute/level buffs like this. It affects skills that are important to you, gives a strong effect, and doesn’t overlap with most other skill boosts.

Seducer's Eyes 📜 - I fucking love this spell. Because every time I talk to someone, I get to ask the GM, “Could they find me hot?” It’s actually a solid spell too. A slight boost to all charisma based skills (except intimidation) for 10 minutes/level.

Burst of Radiance - This is a very solid spell with a good effect even on failure. It’s very worth preparing once you have surprise spell, and is even a fine backup spell for when you can’t get a sneak attack off before surprise spells.

Frigid Touch - Much better than elemental touch. This is solid with reach metamagic. No save 1 round of staggered is an awesome rider effect.

Scorching Ray - This is the gold standard for your ray spells that will carry sneak attack.

Invisibility 📜 - Invisibility as an effect is excellent for you. It’s nice to have the duration go up from vanish, especially as you’re doing espionage. But in combat it’s equivalent to vanish. It’s very useful to have this out of combat, though. Within combat, vanish will almost always be just as good and greater invisibility will be way better.

Mirror Image - This right here is your primary defensive spell. Just like every wizard. You will love it and cherish it. That this is minutes per level is just wonderful.

Defending Bone (pharasma) - This is a fantastic defensive spell. Hour/level 5 DR is great. It’s a nice reason to worship someone like pharasma.

False Life - This is a solid defensive buff once you have extra low level slots. It’s not a priority, but that bit of extra health is really nice. At those levels, it really makes a difference to pop this on your familiar too.

Lipstitch - … weirdly, this isn’t bad for you with surprise spells. No verbal component, sneak attack, and silencing the target. Absolutely horrifying to be a wizard hit by this spell from an arcane trickster in the shadows.

Spectral Hand - It’s cool that this lets you make melee touch attacks without being next to your targets. But having to cast a second level spell every time you want to use them? And only 1 minute/level? This cost is easier to stomach as you get to higher levels. But it’s a hard balance to strike. Reach metamagic is cheaper.

Stricken Heart - Almost exactly the same as Frigid Touch, but less resisted.

Ashen Path 📜 - You, and your party, can see through the concealment of all magical or nonmagical airborne effects up to 60ft! This ability is so rare to find on an enemy until much higher levels with blindsight, etc. This, in combination with obscuring mist, is an amazing way to get sneak attacks off. And you’ll make your entire party harder to hit to top that off!

Darkvision 📜 - If you don’t have darkvision, this is obviously important for you. Hours/level is great!

Pyrotechnics - This is a nice, flexible effect. This is also a decent control spell backup for when you can’t blast. Both blinding and smoke are synergistic with you.

Spider Climb - 10 minutes/level 20ft climb speed. This is just a really solid early buff for you. Go on and scale those walls and rooftops!

Tears To Wine - I really love this skill buff. I actually prepare it a lot across all sorts of casters. It’s just a fantastic, broad benefit for the entire team, and it’s fun to reflavor the drink to different alcohols!

## Level 3

Dispel Magic 📜 - This is one of those spells that you always want to have in your arsenal. Whether to remove a magical effect from an ally, an NPC, or magic obscuring a thing you’re investigating.

Ash Storm - I think at this point I’d rather be using obscuring mist first level spell slots to give myself concealment, as battlefield control isn’t our main job. However, Ash storm is a solid option if you want to combine your concealment with some extra battlefield control. However, if you cast it on enemies, you’ll be visible to them as soon as they make it through! If you cast it around yourself, the difficult terrain will hurt you. This is maybe best for your arsenal after your team gets a communal air step or air walk from the cleric? You also have sleet storm at this level which has a better version of difficult terrain than this. (but that mistborn aesthetic calls to me!)

Gloomblind Bolts (fetchling) - This is scorching ray, but with negative energy and a save against a 1 round blinding effect. It’s also not a ray, so doesn’t get weapon damage buffs. This isn’t a bad pickup at third level to add a bit of extra debuffing to your blasting. It’s a preface for when you’ll be dropping sneak attack siroccos.

Grasping Tentacles - This is a precursor to black tentacles, and it’s actually a bit nicer of a control spell for you. You like blinding enemies as a debuff, because it lets you sneak attack them for free. So this is a perfectly fine control spell to grab as a backup when you can’t manage to get sneak attack off on a turn.

Ice Spears - This is a great way to deliver sneak attack with your third level slots once you have surprise spells. It’s especially potent if you throw dazing metamagic on it.

Phase Step 📜 - Budget dimension door! Teleportation is very good. It’s not as flexible since it just targets a single creature, but that’s fine for getting you out of dodge. It’s nice that the range is close instead of touch, so you can have some nice, creative uses of this. It unfortunately has verbal components, so you’ll have to silence it if you want to use it while stealthing.

Sleet Storm - See Ash storm for how to use this. Sleet storm is just straight up better than Ash storm, unless you want to pretend you’re in final empire Scadrial. Same effects, but things that bypass ‘difficult terrain’ don’t stop the movement penalty, and there’s a small chance of falling prone if you have bad athletics (which you don’t). Most wizards would want to cast it over an enemy’s approach or a group of archers. You aren’t most wizards. Still a double edged sword when you cast it around yourself, so take it as you will.

Insect Scouts - If you’re going to use a spell to scout for you, this is the one. However, it’s better for exploring sewers or caves, or other non-urban places to be inconspicuous. Still, it’s great to know the layout of a place before you go in.

Tongues 📜 - Here’s the language spell for the rest of your arcane trickstering career! You can get it permanencied too. Slap this on a scroll and pull it out when you need it.

Heroism - This is a great, long-lasting buff for you.

Battering Blast - Not only does this scale uncapped by CL, but it has a nice rider with the bull rush attempts on your blasts. This is a solid choice to carry your sneak attacks. It unfortunately doesn’t benefit from weapon damage buffs like rays do, though.

Daylight - Less darkness is the last thing you want. … Unless you have hellcat stealth and your GM is strict on its light condition.

Fireball - This spell goes from meh to awesome for your blasts when you get surprise spells.

Heatstroke - This is actually a neat spell for you! You can sneak attack with it and it’s a ray so it carries weapon damage. And it’s super nasty for martial characters that you zap with it, especially since wearing armor will give them a -4 to save against the effect. If you want a nonlethal source of damage for some fight, this isn't bad option for that either.

Sheet Lightning - This isn’t a bad debuff at all to attach surprise spells to.

Silt Sphere 📜 - This is a really solid method of getting total concealment if you’re underwater. In order to disbelieve this, your enemies need to study it and perform a knowledge nature check. Nice!

Fly - Only 1 minute/level, but the flexibility and strength of positioning flight offers you is great!

Haste - This is the quintessential level 3 buff. Unfortunately, all it does for you is +1 attack and AC and +30 move speed. The move speed might make it worth it, but rounds per level is hard to justify for that. Still, your allies will love you if you take this. But the buff just isn’t that amazing for you.

Slow - This is maybe the best debuff for a third level spell, and always a solid backup spell to have even if we aren’t a debuffer.

## Level 4

Aram Zey's Trap Ward - If you’re delving into a dungeon or other situation with tons of traps, then this is fine, if a bit weak for the level. Otherwise I wouldn’t bother.

Globe of Invulnerability, Lesser 📜 - This isn’t a bad spell against an enemy wizard, but I find wizard bosses usually have 1-2 spell levels on you, and are spamming their most powerful spells rather than their low level ones. The very specific situation this shines in is against multiple low level wizards. And that’s a far rarer type of combat to run into. No saving throw or CL involved, though, means that this is a great scroll to keep on you.

Ward Shield - Conditional spell resistance is not bad! Put it on your mithril buckler, but don’t interpose it when an ally casts buffs on you, only when you’re up against an enemy spellcaster. This is actually a nice buff when dealing with enemy casters.

Dimension Door 📜 - This is a fantastic, flexible teleportation effect. You can bring others with you, it has excellent range, and can be used for all sorts of scenarios.

Shadow Invasion - If you want to use a lot of shadow spells, this is an easy way to get +20% reality. Shadow spells become excellent for you once you have surprise spells. On top of that, there’s a chance of a summon, which is just gravy on top.

Scrying - Information is power! This takes a 1 time focus cost, and then you can prepare it in downtime to figure out what the bad guys in your campaign are doing. Or just look up the mayor of the small town across the continent, so you can scry on them and use the scrying to teleport there. Scrying is very versatile, and very helpful.

Confusion - This is an excellent backup control spell for when you can’t get off your sneak attack. Confusion is a phenomenal buff that you get in an AOE.

Overwhelming Grief - You have other spells this level that provide a nearly as strong effect but don’t allow a save at the end of each round. HOWEVER! It causes the target to lose their dex bonus to AC. That means sneak attack! We prefer backup debuff spells that help our playstyle!

Bit of Luck (catfolk) - This is a fantastic, flexible buff. It’s maybe not worth picking up right away as you get 4th level spells. But it’ll serve you well when you’re working with 5th or 6th level spells.

Ice Storm - This is a fine surprise spells candidate. Single instance area damage with a decent followup non-damaging effect. It just doesn’t stand out in any way, though.

Volcanic Storm - A different flavor for your Ice Storm.

Invisibility, Greater 📜 - This is what we’ve been waiting for! Invisibility that doesn’t end with our attacks. This is our usual buff on the first turn of (or right before) combats, so that we can sneak attack with every subsequent turn. If only it were longer than rounds/level.

Shadow Conjuration - Phase step, gloomblind bolts, sleet storm! If you build yourself to be good with shadow spells, this is great. It has some spells that carry your sneak attack, and will also carry many more once you have surprise spells. There are also great utility spells like air bubble, obscuring mist, communal mount, and slipstream. This is much better when you have surprise spells. Check out this guide. It’s outdated, but has some good core options. Also this spreadsheet.

Shadowform - This is a very nice defensive spell. The enemy has to save each time they cause you to take an effect, and if they fail it’s an 80% damage reduction and 80% to negate any effect! That’s very good for a defensive buff. The rounds/level duration is hard though, and it competes against greater invisibility for an in-combat defensive buff. Much better than displacement.

Enervation - Fantastic debuff backup spell. Sure, it doesn’t get sneak attack, but we’re awesome at firing ray spells!

Firefall - Wow, absolutely gigantic range. This is a fine target for selective spells + surprise spell. Fire damage + blind isn’t bad at all for us. Pyrotechnics is kinda better for a real wizard, but it doesn’t let us do our cool sneak attack!

Imbue With Flight - This is a great, cheaper alternative to overland flight. And it allows you to take your party with you! Far cheaper than a magic carpet. It’s maybe a bit harder to be stealthy with, though.

Obsidian Flow - This would be pretty forgettable, but unlike a spell like volcanic storm, it goes through spell resistance! Pretty good once we have surprise spells.

Stone Shape - Soooo much flexibility. Bend caves to your whim. Create tunnel bases. Make stairs. Do that dumb handhold spell earlier, but forever. Create your own cover.

## Level 5

Life Bubble - Necessary if you go to space or travel somewhere you can’t breathe. Also a huge upgrade on Ashen Path. The immunity to cloud spells is great. Now you can use spells like sickening cloud around yourself for concealment without worrying about harming yourself or friendly fire!

Stoneskin, Communal - Woo! I would have loved stoneskin last level if not for the fact that this covers a whole team and costs the same amount. Sure, we aren’t a buffer, but I feel bad about wasting money. And your team will love you for this.

Cloudkill - Just plop this on top of your party that’s buffed with Life Bubble. You get concealment, and enemies suffer from con damage even on a successful save!

Teleport 📜 - This is just an essential spell, no matter what sort of role you’re playing as a wizard. This is, as its namesake suggests, one of the most useful teleportation effects in the game.

Wall of Stone 📜 - This is fantastic. It’s just a permanent wall of stone. There are so many flexible uses for this. It can even be a bridge. It’s cover for you, it shapes the battlefield to be most useful to you, it blocks the enemies chasing you.

Contact Other Plane - We finally get a powerful divination question spell! This is great, but be very careful using it. The chance for a lie or random answer is larger than the chance for the truth. Also, the downside is rare, but horrible.

Dominate Person - Detect thoughts? Charm person? Suggestion? Who needs any of that crap? This is your one stop shop for enchantment spells that give you whatever you want out of the target. Have them tell you the secret you’re trying to parse out, or turn on their allies in combat! Just remember that the target gets an extra save whenever made to do something “against their nature” which is very dependent on your dm.

Mind Fog - This is a brutal debuffing fog. Great with Air Bubble.

Icy Prison - This is a great damaging control spell. Unfortunately, it’s only single target, so it doesn’t resonate as well for us with surprise spells. That doesn’t keep it from being very good, though.

Wall of Light - This is probably your best condition-based wall spell to this point. You love blinded since it gives you sneak attacks, and this blinds without a save! Very useful.

Persistent Image - An illusion following a script with no concentration! A usable illusion, yay!

Shadow Evocation - Fantastic flexibility for sneak attack spells, especially if you’ve invested in shadow magic.

Waves of Fatigue 📜 - This is an incredible debuff spell. We aren’t a debuffer, but it’s certainly not bad to have as backup when you’re staring down a barbarian. And because it doesn’t take a save, you can keep a scroll of it!

Overland Flight - If imbue with flight didn’t exist, this spell would be purple. It’s still very good.

Permanency - Lots of good effects can be made permanent! Keep it in your arsenal.

## Level 6

Chains of Light - One of the classic, incredible control spells. This is a true save or suck. No rolling each round, no spell resistance, no fiddly effects. It doesn’t offer anything particularly synergistic with us, but it’s still an incredibly powerful option.

Getaway - Fantastic escape spell! Not only is it a swift action, but you have a decent range to get out of dodge with your party.

Summon Genie, Greater - This actually adds a bunch of utility to our arsenal. Shaitan have at-will stone shape. For an extra spell level, you can have the shaitan cast your CL number of stone shapes! Already a great deal. Then you add in the options of Stone Tell, Wall of Stone, and the mud to rock/vice versa spells. Sadly, you can’t have it cast stoneskin. If you want to do stone-based utility stuff, carving tunnels or altering terrain, this is your spell.

Battlemind Link - Ooooh, this is a standout buff right here. Roll twice and take the better for initiative? For every ranged touch attack we make? Though we might be switching to AOE save spells soon with surprise spells. Even then, the cleric, druid, or control wizard would love to share -2 on a target’s saving throw with you. It’s minutes/level, so we can prebuff it too.

True Seeing - Never let any illusions get in the way of your heists again! See past mirror images and other annoying effects. The material cost is annoying, but the effects are worth it.

Heroism, Greater - Amazing! Plop this on yourself and enjoy the huge boost to your stats.

Unconscious Agenda - I actually like this more for us than Geas. It feels like such a cool move to break into an enemy fortress to sneak up on the sleeping king, and whisper to them directions that will sabotage their actions. Unlike Geas this has a save, so make sure to buff/debuff accordingly. Ill Omen is recommended. The bit of green rating is for intrigue-heavy campaigns.

Cold Ice Strike - A pre-quickened blast for us! Wonderful. This effectively replaces a quickened scorching ray. With no other bonuses, the scorching ray would be 12d6 + sneak attack. This is up to 15d6 + sneak attack and can hit multiple targets. A nice little upgrade, and all the damage is in one bundle which helps deal with resistances. Unfortunately, the range is extremely limited at a 30ft line, so you have to get closer than you might like to hit anything at all with it. This is a tradeoff.

Contingency - This is just as excellent for you as it is for other wizards. Action economy is difficult for us. We don’t want to waste time buffing ourselves or countering effects. Pop an immediate prebuff the second initiative starts, or react to dangerous conditions with countering spells.

Sirocco - This right here is surprise spells gold. Start your fight with a 4d6 + 8d6 + 15 AOE that automatically fatigues everyone inside, forcing them to leave the storm if they don’t want to be exhausted. (throw down a quickened ice/sleet storm to make that even harder!) This is a lot of bang for your buck!

Permanent Image - Finally! Another good illusion spell. We don’t need to concentrate on the illusion, but can concentrate to move it around the casting range. This is a great tool for your toolbox when you’re doing creative sneaking stuff. Less useful for dungeon crawling.

Shadow Enchantment, Greater - This is a heck of a lot of flexibility! Dominate Person, Mind Fog, Confusion, Overwhelming Grief, all the charm spells, etc.

Disintegrate - If you specialized in ray spells, this is a fantastic addition. It’s a great single target damage spell. It also has okay utility with removing force effects and destroying 10ft cubes

Borrowed Time - You can only cast up to one swift action spell a turn. This lets you do an extra swift action, but that won’t give you more spellcasting. It’s not bad. At this point, You’re unlikely to reach the staggered drawback before the end of the fight. It just requires you to have good non-spell swift actions.

Ether Step - This is a great way to avoid brutal effects like power words or such. The drawback is rough,  but trading your turn for a boss’s turn is almost always a good deal, because your team has 3-4 other turns before the boss goes again. Action economy!

Tar Pool - Very solid non-SR spell that deals your sneak attack in a decent area on cast and has an okay secondary effect. This is nice to have in the surprise spells arsenal.

Undead Anatomy III - This is a very good defensive form for resistances, DR, senses, and other useful abilities. The +8 bonus on saves against mind-affecting effects, disease, poison, sleep, and stunning is insane

## Level 7

Spell Turning - I really like long duration defensive buffs. This is great if you’re planning to go fight an enemy wizard. Make sure to cast it after allied buff spells, though!

Caustic Eruption - Just pretty good AOE non-SR damage as well as a persistent damage effect. I think it sort of occupies the same space for us as tar pool in the previous level. But you might prefer this if you’re focusing on conjuration spells.

Dimensional Bounce - Here’s a spell to use that extra swift action from Borrowed time with! Jump out, blast, and jump back. Though there are probably better ways to use a 7th and 6th level spell, and all your swift actions for a fight. This is very nice for positioning as well, though. It’s great for getting you fresh concealment for stealth.

Mage's Magnificent Mansion - Instead of using a boring lesser demiplane, rest up in opulence! Plus, unlike a demiplane, literally no one else can enter it unless you allow them to. This is a souped up rope trick, but a very nice one that’s well-defended.

Plane Shift 📜 - This is important to have on your list as a backup, but you get it much later than your allies. Prepare it when everyone needs to go to a different plane, obviously, but otherwise you should likely just keep a scroll around.

Signifer's Rally - I think this is actually a super cool spell for an arcane trickster. You infiltrate past the incredible defenses of a magically fortified stronghold where you want to strike at its heart. This spell lets you pull in your entire party to land that finishing blow.

Teleport Object 📜 - Lets you teleport away large loot you find without having to leave yet yourself. I think I’d keep this on a scroll more than I’d ever prepare it, though.

Teleport, Greater - Wow, this is crazy powerful. Your GM will likely want some serious descriptions for target locations, because otherwise you can just teleport right into vaults and treasure rooms and have at them!

Insect Spies, Greater - Very nice! Get knowledge of a location’s layout, without the risk of the insects not returning or you having to piece together what the location is like with a series of questions.

Perceive Betrayal - Very cool spell for an intrigue campaign. The best part about this spell is that there’s not even a save involved. You just get perfect information from it. Unfortunately, it’s still stymied by immunity to mind-effecting effects.

Scrying, Greater - This adds a number of good detection spells to your scrying. You’ll likely be casting this on days where you don’t need to worry as much about combat, It’s an easy one to learn and use in your offtime.

Waves of Ecstasy - This spell is amazing, even if we don’t get our sneak attack. Staggered for a round even if they save, and then stunned then staggered if they fail

Hungry Darkness - If this dealt damage to all creatures on cast, this would be my favorite spell of the level. Sadly, it deals damage on the start of enemy turns, so it’ll apply sneak attack to a single enemy. It’s still nothing to sneeze at. It helps your concealment by providing deeper darkness, and it deals constant con damage in a huge area. Very nice!

Invisibility Bubble, Mass - See mass invisibility, but take this instead if you’re in an aquatic campaign.

Invisibility, Mass - Turning everyone invisible is super good, Use this in stealth or infiltration situations where you need to smuggle your team in. There are plenty of other uses too. I’d rate this higher if it weren’t a 7th level spell. It just feels a little too high costed here for me.

Shadow Conjuration, Greater - There are so many good conjuration spells for all sorts of different scenarios. Even if you aren’t a shadow casting wizard, this can get you some nice buffs or effects. Check out this guide. It’s outdated, but has some good core options. Also this spreadsheet.

Temporal Divergence - This is an expensive spell (by spell levels) to cast, but it’s a great way to improve your consistency in combat. At the price of a quickened third level spell, you can instead choose between the effects of two other spells of any level. For example, if your destruction would be saved against, you could instead throw down a much more reliable sirocco.

Limited Wish 📜 - Any non-wizard spell of 5th level or lower opens up many doors. The 1,500gp cost is very painful, but the flexibility is fantastic. You now get access to all sorts of divination effects from divine lists, resurrections, etc.

## Level 8

Dimensional Lock - This is a great way to prevent foes from teleporting away. It has an insanely long duration, and no save involved. It does have SR, though.

Mind Blank - Note that this is a resistance bonus, it won’t overlap with a cloak of resistance. However, it is also 24 hour immunity to all divination spells, which is pretty damn nice. That includes things like see invisibility and true sight! If you just want the mental save bonus, undead anatomy III can give that to you, but that immunity is great for an additional buff.

Prismatic Wall - This is just an insanely powerful wall. Even though it doesn’t carry our sneak attack more than once, I can’t help but rate this very highly

Create Demiplane - There are tons of cool things you can do with this spell. Professor Q’s guide is right that a whole guide could be written about it. Search online for ways to have fun with this!

Maze - This is just a brutal shutdown for an enemy with low int.

Seamantle - This is a great defensive buff against fire. Combine this with Ice Body for a really nice set of defenses. The cover that seamantle provides doesn’t give you the ability to stealth, but you luckily can still stealth within it.

Discern Location - “Find Plot” the spell

Moment of Prescience - This can be life saving on a pivotal saving throw or ability check. Great buff for yourself.

Prediction of Failure - This is surprisingly decent. Shaken and sickened aren’t the best conditions, but they set up a -4 to the target’s saves, which leaves them very exposed to you. And there’s no real chance of failure. This is the kind of spell I’d throw down on a temporal divergence along with disintegrate. If the disintegrate fails, they get -4 to all saves and you try again next turn. Plus, with a debuff like this, you’re at the point where you can then quicken a nasty lower level effect to hit them immediately with.

Euphoric Tranquility - Damn. This is touch range, but no saving throw and a seriously powerful effect. This is a great spell if you find yourself dealing with intrigue, but also has combat uses.

Irresistible Dance - The target is ensured to dance for at least one round. That makes this a great combo with save or sucks with a reflex save. This + dazing cold ice strike is nasty.

Polar Ray - This is a nice reason to stick with ray spells! No save Dex drain as a rider on some decently good damage.

Stormbolts - This is a fantastic elemental AOE with a stun rider that has a built in selective spell. It’s a great primary spell to use with surprise spell sneak attacks.

Shadow Evocation, Greater - We have some fantastic evocation options, and this lets us pick and choose exactly what’s needed for the situation. Again, check this spreadsheet. This is purple if you’ve taken abilities to make shadow spells more real.

Clone - Make a clone in a demiplane, and cheat death! Plus, even if you die before the clone is created, on completion, you immediately pop back into the clone’s body.

Angelic Aspect, Greater -This is an incredible buff. There’s so much bang for your buck packed into here, with immunities, resistances, special abilities, and stat boosts!

## Level 9

Aroden's Spellbane - This is a hard spell to use. If you know what spells your enemy is preparing, then you can wreck them with this. But it takes serious prep work or divination to determine that. If you’re good at planning and preparation, this can turn you into an unstoppable force.

Echean's Excellent Enclosure 📜 - I’m not trapped in here with you, you’re trapped in here with me. Or rather, my familiar trapped you in with this raging barbarian. This is the ultimate cage spell, though. This is also great on a scroll since it has no save.

Impenetrable Veil 📜 - As much as this spell synergizes with us, we’ve kind of already gotten most of these benefits from other spells. However, if you need some of the aspects of this such as the hide in plain sight, it’s fantastic on you. There’s also the really nice divination and tracking protection. Otherwise, it’s not worth spending a level 9 spell for just +7-10 on your stealth check. It’s fine to keep a scroll in case you need it for some reason, though.

Mage's Disjunction - Greater dispel magic, but you don’t have to make a check and it hits magic items and a whole AOE. This is a fantastic spell. The lack of anything resembling a CL check for spells and effects makes this an extremely reliable way to tilt the playing field in your favor. Additionally, it has out of combat utility.

Clashing Rocks - This is an okay blast spell. It looks a little underwhelming at first, but it deserves its place here for reliability. It works in a 60ft line centered on an enemy target, hitting everyone along that line. You’ll pretty much always hit, so they just get a reflex save for buried vs prone. Buried is nice, they get restrained and it’s a tough condition to escape for anyone who can’t easily make a DC 25 strength check.

Create Demiplane, Greater - You can do silly things with this. You can make timeless planes of existence to prepare however much you want. Look up what you can do with this on the internet.

Maze of Madness And Suffering - Wow, I love this spell, even with its 1000gp cost. It’s a very fun one against PCs, and it’s great for PCs to use as well. Each of the different possible effects is quite nasty. I’d be happy with it for that reason alone, even without the higher int check required to escape! The only downsides are [evil] and [mind-effecting] descriptors opening up potential resistances that the previous version didn't have.

Summon Monster 9 - The Astral Deva is really nice. Holy Aura, dispel magic, heal, condition removal, see invisibility, etc. It’s a great divine toolbox for an arcane caster. Plus, it’s useful out of combat with its discern lies ability. The Ghale is less of a toolbox, but offers some overlapping options, and some other useful choices like wall of force, death ward, globe of invulnerability, and a few others. The others don’t offer us as much except the nalfeshnee with greater dispel magic.

Dominate Monster - I like Professor Q’s suggestion not to think of this as a combat spell. It works as a backup in combat as a save or suck, but the real power lies in out of combat situations due to that great duration. Combine this with a quickened ill omen, temporal divergence, and prediction of failure to make sure it sticks.

Icy Prison, Mass - This is what mass hold monster and overwhelming presence wish they could be. I just wish the damage all happened at once so we could get more than one sneak attack out of this!

Shades - This is excellent. Part of being a wizard is embracing versatility. Pulling out the perfect conjuration effect for the scenario has so much value.

Astral Projection - Conduct all of your business in perfect safety from your demiplane. You have all your spells, abilities, and equipment in your second bodies, so you could even adventure with it! You can safely cast this from your demiplane, and then recover your two permanent negative levels in peace if the body on another plane is killed. It costs 1000gp per cast of this, but as long as your demiplane sustains you, you can keep it going until you’re done or your second body dies.

Scourge of the Horsemen - AOE negative levels are a very nice rider to an AOE damage effect! And acid damage too. I’d rather be dealing conditions with my sneak attack blast spells. I don’t know if I’d even prefer this over stormbolts. But what saves it is that it still deals ½ negative levels even on a successful save. This is a good candidate for greater empower/maximize rods.

Shapechange - God, there’s soooo much bookkeeping involved in this. It’s a cool spell, the flexibility is nice. It’s actually awesome for jumping between different movement types and sizes and lasting for 10 minutes/level. But holy shit, the bookkeeping. I think this is the best 9th level arcane trickster infiltration spell, and that annoys me because of how much homework it involves.

Time Stop - Classic. Lay down buffs, control spells, delayed fireballs, etc. Then let time return and decimate your enemies.

Wish - Pricy, but powerful and flexible. You can do all sorts of crazy things with wish.
