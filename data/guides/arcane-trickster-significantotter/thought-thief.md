# The Thought Thief

This is a weird one. It turns out that the Arcane Trickster has an archetype! I never knew that prestige classes had archetypes. I’m sticking it way down at the bottom here because it’s an interesting choice, but certainly not a core option.

The thought thief requires 2nd level psychic spellcasting instead of arcane spellcasting to enter, and has abilities that scale off of charisma. That makes the psychic bloodline sorcerer the perfect candidate for a Thought Thief. The psychic itself is sadly not quite as effective as a thought thief, but you can push through the early levels with the rebirth discipline, and still have a very fun build to work with. There are other ways to get psychic spellcasting, such as by being a psychic bloodline blood arcanist.

The question is why? The thought thief is an interesting option that opens up arcane trickster playstyles for psychic casting, but there are few reasons to bend over backwards to gain access to it over the standard arcane trickster.

So what do we give up and get in return?

We lose Impromptu Sneak Attack, which is unfortunate. It’s not a pivotal part of the arcane trickster’s loadout, but it helps immensely with improving the arcane trickster’s consistency. In exchange, we get Mental Assault. I would like this ability a whole lot more if the DC weren’t so mediocre, and if we could reach metamagic it. Worse, we don’t have much out of combat utility for it, since the dominate is only rounds/level. We can do the same thing as a typical arcane trickster by just casting heatstroke with Mind Strike and then dominate person! Still, this isn’t a bad way to peel off the enemy fighter that’s up in your space who might not have the best will save.

On the plus side, it’s not subject so spell resistance, and may have a somewhat comparable dc if you’re a charisma caster. It dishes out mind strike in the same action as it forces a will save! And, although it references dominate person, it also doesn’t seem to be type restricted. That’s a major boost to usability

At 5th level, we get Unseen Compulsion instead of Tricky Spells. Hiding psychic spellcasting is much easier than hiding arcane spellcasting, and this is meant to let you consistently use psychic casting in stealthy ways. …unfortunately the ability is worded to make our lives miserable. They get to use their sense motive check to sense our casting, but we don’t get to use our bluff check. We get to use 15 + our ranks in bluff + our charisma score as the DC. We don’t get to improve this in any way except for pure skill ranks and ability score improvements. That’s so unfortunate. Furthermore, it only hides the effects of a spell, and not the casting itself. This is very relevant if you used something like the Esoteric Draconic Bloodline to enter and are still casting spells with verbal components. They’ll notice you casting, they just won’t see the effects.

At least the DC becomes decent when you’re dealing with enemies who haven’t focused on sense motive. However, the second you find someone with a sense motive class skill, maybe some other source of sense motive bonus or two, and ranks near your own, you’re screwed.

The major boon in that ability is that it’s pretty much always active. If you want to do some active psychic spellcasting while wandering around a party or dealing with diplomats, this can be a very effective option.

Another plus for us comes from simply using psychic casting instead of arcane casting. One benefit has already been mentioned with the components being easier to hide, but we have another major benefit in being able to cast in pretty much any form! We can do everything we want to in gaseous form, or polymorphed into a bat, a mouse, etc! That’s a lot of fun intrigue potential.

Even though the psychic bloodline Sorcerer is a great way to enter the Thought Thief, I wouldn’t recommend it over a typical Sorcerer going Arcane Trickster if you want to be a sorcerer. Use the Thought Thief as a neat way to play an actual Psychic. As a psychic bloodline Sorcerer, you’ll play very similarly to a normal arcane trickster. However, the psychic offers a whole new spell list and perspective on the arcane trickster that you can’t get from the normal arcane classes… even if it’s a bit of a struggle to use.
