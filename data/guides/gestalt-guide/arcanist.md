# Arcanist Combinations

Arcanist


This odd little hybrid is one of two casters to require two good mental stats (Int for spells, Cha for arcane reservoir), so it's particularly important to pair it with a class that uses complementary stats. The best choices are Int or Cha-based hybrids or divine casters that improve or diversify a casting role, and add durability. Wis-based casters and martials tend to have issues with MAD, split focus, or armour, though some such concepts are passable.


Blade Adept is also worth mentioning since it does give you a few combat advantages including an arcane bonded black blade and Spellstrike – however the blade's limited progression makes it a mixed blessing, since you can't enhance it on the fly like a magus can with their arcane pool. If your GM bends the rules to let you improve the enchantment (since it is also an arcane bonded weapon) or you're in a low wealth gestalt it's a good archetype, particularly for one of the few martial arcanist gestalts.


Arcanist // Barbarian * (Dreadnought **)
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full casting, physical Int Cha
Little synergy, and inconvenient to cast while raging.
Anonymous says: I think you underestimate barbarian full wizard list casters. An invulnerable Rager barbarian gets 2 HP per level more than a hybrid like Magus or Alchemist and half your level in untyped DR without spending any actions and can run away better. Rage is for when you can't cast making the dreadnought or bloodrager rage casting options false synergy. You rage because you have cast Beast Shape or Antimagic Field or have someone with Disruptive and Step Up in your grill and can't cast anyways.
Weirdo’s response: If you want a martial class that gives you something to do in situations where you’re prevented from casting, there are a variety of options with signature features that don’t lock you into not casting for a while. If you’re playing a Fighter//Wizard and someone is disrupting your casting, you can stab them and then get right back to spells if you want - you can switch round by round if tactically convenient. With rage, you have to eat a fatigue penalty if you decide you want spells back, and if you’re prevented from using spells again you can’t re-enter rage for a bit.


Arcanist // Bard ***/*
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills+, full and partial casting, Int Cha
A solid troubleshooter – lots of skills and spells.
ShadeL0rd says: The Chronicler of Worlds archetype keys Bard features off of Intelligence, removing the MAD component at the cost of Inspire Heroics, Greatness and Versatile Performance. You may have to stray away from exploits based on charisma.


Arcanist // Bloodrager **
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full casting, physical Int Cha
A tough full caster, capable of casting arcanist spells in rage with the Mad Magic feat. However, Sorcerer-Bloodrager is stronger thanks to better stat synergy.


Arcanist // Brawler **/*
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, physical Int Cha
The AC bonus is nice on a caster, and unarmed strike works well for delivering touch spells, especially multi-touch spells like Chill Touch or Frostbite. Otherwise not a lot of overlap in the skill set.


Arcanist // Cavalier **
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full casting, physical Int Cha


Arcanist // Cleric (Ecclesitheurge, Elder Mythos Cultist)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 2 skills, two full casting, Int Wis Cha
Extremely versatile spell lists, but quite MAD for a caster. I'd recommend sticking to support spells on the cleric end to minimize Wis requirements - or pick up Elder Mythos Cultist.


Arcanist // Druid ** (Feyspeaker ***)
Basics:  Poor/Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 6/4 skills, two full casting, Int Wis Cha
Feyspeaker gets rid of one of your stat requirements.


Arcanist // Fighter * (Blade Adept **)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 2 skills, full casting, physical Int Cha
Steep stat requirements and no real synergy. Fighters and full arcane casters are rarely great and the extra stat requirements from the arcanist don't help. Blade adept gives you a little extra combat synergy but it's still not very effective.


Arcanist // Gunslinger (Mysterious Stranger)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting,  Dex Int Wis Cha


Arcanist // Hunter
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills, full and 6-level casting, Int Wis Cha
You don't have the stats to support real weapons combat, but can do pretty well casting from the rear while your pet enters melee, or just using your pet as a mount. Try Empyreal Sorcerer // Hunter.


Arcanist // Inquisitor **/*
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 6 skills+, full and 6-level casting, Int Cha Wis
Not going to do well in martial combat, so might as well take Relic Hunter – or in a smaller party, Sacred Huntmaster as a reach build. Living Grimoire?


Arcanist // Investigator *** (Empiricist, Blade Adept ****)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills+, full and partial casting, Int Cha
A fine pair if you want an arcane caster with a lot of skills, though consider replacing Investigator with Mindchemist for its good Fort save and Cognatagen. Remember you can write spells from your formula book into your spellbook without researching twice. Blade Adept together with Studied Combat gives you a credible melee option so that brings this up a step – as does Empiricist for Int synergy. 


Arcanist // Kineticist **
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, Con Int Cha Dex
You can do this with fewer stats.


Arcanist // Magus ***/* (Kensai *****)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, full and partial casting, physical Int Cha
The extra mental stat is a bit rough when you want to be in combat, but it's still a very potent combo. Take Broad Study at level 6 to use arcanist spells with spellstrike and spell combat. The Kensai gives up armour, which the arcanist doesn't want, and spell slots, of which you have plenty, in exchange for Int to AC and some great tricks with a favoured weapon.


Arcanist//Medium
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Will, 4 skills, full and 6-level casting, Int Cha
Not a whole lot of synergy and having only one good save hurts. Pretty much any partial-arcane caster will give you better defenses, at least equal skills, and comparable versatility to the medium. 


Arcanist//Mesmerist ****
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills, full and 6-level casting, Int Cha
Mesmerist's stare and Mental Potency help Arcanist spells land.


Arcanist // Monk ** (Scaled Fist ***)
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, Int Cha Wis
The scaled fist is definitely the way to go with this one – even then it will be a bit tricky to get high enough physical stats to use your weapons combat features. You can maybe pull together a maneuver build with Sensei or turtle up with Master of Many Styles, but you'd do better pairing Monk with either Wizard or Sorcerer, or taking Kensai or Alchemist if you want an Arcanist with physical prowess.


Arcanist//Occultist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and partial casting, Int Cha
Strongly consider silksworn to get extra mileage out of your Cha – the only thing you really lose is access to panoplies, which are not fantastic for the arcanist. Recommend conjuration implement between resonant power and access to healing.


Arcanist // Oracle ***/*
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Will, 4 skills, two full casting, Int Cha
Great variety of spellcasting options and some stat synergy with your arcane pool (possibly supplemented with the Lore mystery’s Int boost), brought down by mediocre HD, only one good save, and no armour.


Arcanist // (Anti)Paladin **
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will+, 2 skills, full casting, physical Int Cha
The paladin packs a punch and Divine Grace makes up for the lack of a strong reflex save. However, many of your abilities use swift actions, and you've got an armour problem. Paladin//Sorcerer is a better choice.


Arcanist // Psychic **
Basics: Poor BAB, d6, Will, 2 skills+, two full casting, Int Cha
Durability issues, but good stat synergy as long as you pick a Cha-based discipline, and since your spell DCs are both Int based you can make that a primary stat. Psychic spell selection should prioritize staple spells, class-specific spells, and usability with phrenic amplifications – Arcanist fills in the gaps.


Arcanist // Ranger *
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 6 skills, full casting, all stats
Lots of issues here. Extremely MAD. Armour wearing class plus arcane spell failure. No thanks.


Arcanist // Rogue **
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 8 skills, full casting, physical Int Cha


Arcanist // Shaman ***
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Will, 4 skills, two full casting, Int Wis Cha
Arcanist // Skald ***
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 4 skills, full and partial casting, Int Cha


Arcanist // Slayer *** (Blade Adept ****)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills+, full casting, physical Int Cha
Sturdier than arcanist//rogue for minimal cost. Intelligence helps slayer DCs and you can use Studied Target and Sneak Attack with rays and similar spells as long as they deal HP damage. Not fantastic but a decent combat arcanist.


Arcanist // Spiritualist (Fractured Soul)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and partial casting, Int Wis Cha


Arcanist // Sorcerer *
Basics: Poor BAB, d6, Will, 2 skills, two full casting, Int Cha
Very fragile, two casting stats, both classes have the same spell list so not much of a versatility boost... awful idea. 


Arcanist // Summoner **
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Will, 4 skills, full and partial casting, Int Cha
The eidolon and standard action summoning are very useful. The low saves are a big weakness so you'll want a stronger than usual Dex and Con, and maybe even Great Fortitude.


Arcanist // Swashbuckler ** (Blade Adept // Inspired Blade ***)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Ref and Will, 4 skills, casting
Not bad, but runs into the typical action economy problem with non-Magus gish builds – without Spell Combat it's very costly to both cast and attack. Inspired Blade is the most synergistic since you'll get more out of the Arcanist's high Int.


Arcanist // Warpriest *
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 2 skills+, full and partial casting, all stats
Terrible because you need all three mental stats, which leaves you no room for the physical stats you'd need to make warpriest a better option than cleric. Go Arcanist//Cleric for casting, or Empyreal Sorcerer//Warpriest if you want to fight.


Arcanist // Witch ***
Basics: Poor BAB, d6, Will, 2 skills+, two full casting, Int Cha
Super fragile by gestalt standards but hugely magically powerful by combining two forms of int-based full casting with different lists, and an extremely versatile set of goodies between hexes and exploits.


Arcanist // Wizard **
Basics: Poor BAB, d6, Will, 2 skills+, two full casting, Int Cha
Marginally better than Arcanist//Sorcerer because the half-spontaneous casting complements true prepared casting better than true spontaneous casting, you've got faster access to the most powerful spells, you don't need quite as much Cha since it's just for exploits, and you might be able to share spells across your spellbooks. Still a bad idea because you're terribly fragile and even if you've got lots of spells they're all from the same list. Try Arcanist//Witch or Kineticist//Wizard.
