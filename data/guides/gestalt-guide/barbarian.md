# Barbarian Combinations

Barbarian


The barbarian is a strong martial class, tough and capable of massive damage in melee or, with the right build, solid damage at range. Note: spell sunder works at range. It works best with hybrid classes that enhance the barbarian's combat options while also providing some utility options. Pure martials generally don't add a lot of versatility to the barbarian. Pure casters rarely synergize well.


The barbarian's trickiest element is the inability to cast while raging. There are a few ways to manage this. The easiest, if your spell list co-operates, is picking out of combat spells, prebuffs, or occasionally first round buff or control options. Moment of Clarity and Mad Magic give you a limited ability to ragecast (specifically, one round per rage), at the cost of 1-2 rage powers and possibly a feat depending on how badly you care about keeping rage bonuses while you cast. It’s a reasonable route if you want to be able to cast an emergency mid-combat spell - or if you can find an easy way to rage cycle. Alternatively, the Dreadnought suffers no penalties or limitations during a rage, but also gains only half the usual benefits. This archetype makes it possible to build a broader array of gestalt barbarians that actually integrate casting with combat, including reach casters.


Some GMs may interpret the restriction against “abilities that require patience or concentration” more broadly than spellcasting. With respect to psychic casters, it's also important to check whether rage counts as a “hostile emotion effect.” Descriptions note if a combination's effectiveness is subject to this table variation.


Barbarian // Bard ****
Basics: Full BAB, d12, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, physical Cha
Strong melee capability, good support and utility. In early levels bardic abilities compete with your attacks, but at higher levels you can spend a round at most starting a performance and maybe casting a group buff before wading in. Lots of options out of combat, too. Avoid debuffs, charm, and other effects with saves – you probably won't afford a high enough charisma to pull it off. In the event that your GM won't let you use performance while raging (uncommon, but not unheard of) consider Archaeologist – otherwise the combination is green at best.


Barbarian // Bloodrager *
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort, 4 skills, no casting, physical Cha
You get two non-compatible sets of rage abilities and fail to shore up your weaknesses. If your GM allows you to merge your rages and use rage powers and bloodrage abilities at the same time, this could be yellow, but only if you choose archetypes to swap out duplicate abilities (for example, Invulnerable Barbarian and Spelleater Bloodrager). You'll also want Superstition for your saves.


Barbarian // Brawler ***/*
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, no casting, physical
Lots of damage, great durability, and some control options, but shy on utility. The strength bonus is great when you're using TWF with full Str on each attack. Soak damage as well as you deal it with Stalwart and Improved Stalwart – bonus feats and Brawler's Cunning take the edge off the stiff prerequisites and Combat Expertise is great for the Brawler since you can fill in maneuver feats with Martial Flexibility, adding control options. If you're interested in rage cycling, consider Barbarian//Martial Artist Monk.


Barbarian // Cavalier
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort, 4 skills, no casting, physical


Barbarian // Cleric *** (Dreadnought ****)
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full casting, physical Wis
A solid combat combo with support capability, but relatively low on out of combat utility and you'll find it more difficult to balance fighting with casting than the similar Barbarian//Oracle, Barbarian//Druid, or Bloodrager//Cleric gestalts. Dreadnought makes a good reach build – the Quick Reflexes rage power can compensate for a less than stellar Dex.


Barbarian // Druid ****
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full casting, physical Wis
The raging beast! You have plenty of out of combat utility spells and long duration pre-buffs to avoid ragecasting; a reach casting build with Dreadnought is possible, but Moment of Clarity will suffice for the occasional mid-combat spell. Beast Totem, while thematic, is less useful in this gestalt since the natural armour and pounce overlap with wild shape's bonuses. Consider Goliath Druid for massive strength with weapons-based combat.


Barbarian // Fighter ** (Mutation Warrior ***)
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort, 4 skills, no casting, physical
If you want to do something feat-intensive with your barbarian, this works OK. Mutation Warrior takes this up a step because of the stat boost and extra tricks from alchemist discoveries. Martial Master is worth considering for flexibility – as with the Brawler, it works nicely to add
 Combat Expertise-based feats if you want the Stalwart/Improved Stalwart chain.


Barbarian // Gunslinger * (Urban Barbarian **, Savage Technologist ***)
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, no casting, Dex Wis
Normally the barbarian and gunslinger have incompatible combat styles, but the Urban Barbarian and Savage Technologist archetypes offer Dex-based rages that work moderately well with the gunslinger.
However, you're still low on out of combat utility and have a poor will save, so meh.


Barbarian // Hunter ****
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Ref, 6 skills, 6-level casting, physical Wis
Both you and your pet will be strong fighters with this combination; strongly consider the Ferocious Mount line of rage powers, along with Pack Flanking. Like the druid, casting can be used for utility and long-duration prebuffs rather than while raging. Strongly consider Iron Will, especially since the Int 13 requirement for Pack Flanking means your Wis will likely be modest.


Barbarian // Inquisitor ****/*
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Will, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, physical Wis
Bane and either judgement or studied target pile on more hurt to the devastating fury of the barbarian. You have plenty of options to make use of Intimidate, and a good range of other utility potential including scouting, tracking, social and knowledge skills. Sacred Huntmaster gives you access to a mount with Amplified Rage, making it one of the stronger pet gestalts.


Barbarian // Investigator **/**
Basics: Full BAB, d12, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, physical and Int
Depends entirely on one question: will your GM let you use Studied Combat while raging or does that ability require patience and concentration? If they are compatible this is a fantastic combination with full BAB, lots of combat buffs, plenty of durability and good skills and magical utility. I recommend Mutagen and Quick Study as your 3rd and 5th level talents. If the two classes' combat boosts aren't compatible then the usefulness plummets compared to similar full BAB/hybrid gestalts.


Barbarian//Kineticist * (Dreadnought ****)
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, pseudo-casting, physical
Blasts, as spell-like abilities, can't be used while raging, which makes Dreadnought practically mandatory. The high-investment alternative means taking the Moment of Clarity and Perfect Clarity rage powers and the Mad Magic feat, and then becoming immune to fatigue (eg: android with the Empathy feat) so as to use Moment of Clarity every round. Either way, the Con bonus and extra HP makes this combo a heavy hitter with blasts. Raging Vitality is a must-have for another +2 Con. Rage Powers Surprise Accuracy and Reckless Abandon are useful for increasing accuracy on physical blast users. Make sure to patch the low will save.


Barbarian // Magus * (Dreadnought ***)
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Will, 4 skills, 6-level casting, Str Con Int
Rage makes spell combat and spellstrike impossible, unless you take Dreadnought, in which case you're looking at a pretty decent strength based frontliner. The halved bonuses for rage aren't fantastic with a one-handed style, and rage powers requiring activation hamper the magus' action economy. Specific rage power suggestions?


Barbarian//Medium
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Will, 4 skills, partial casting, physical and Cha


Barbarian // Mesmerist **/*
Basics: Full BAB, d12, all saves, 6 skills, 6-level casting, physical and Cha
Check with your GM if the Hypnotizing Stare, which requires “focus” is compatible with rage, which doesn't allow “patience and concentration.” If you can use the two together, proceed to trantrum your way to victory, glaring at your foes to shake their resolve and increase the damage of your assault. Otherwise, the main benefits for the barbarian are the good saves and utility or pre-buffing spells, which isn't bad but you can get it elsewhere. Consider Barbarian//Bard or Bloodrager//Mesmerist.


Barbarian // Monk *****
Basics: Full BAB, d12, all saves+, 4 skills, no casting, physical and Wis
To get around the alignment restriction, I recommend Martial Artist, which gets immunity to fatigue at level 5, and that means rage cycling. That's not all. Offensively you can flurry while “rage pouncing” – consider Dragon Style to increase unarmed strike damage and allow you to pounce through difficult terrain. Defensively you have all good saves, evasion, the option to take Superstition for even better saves, a boatload of immunities and resistances besides fatigue, DR, and loads of HP. You can even manage a pretty good AC between beast totem, the dodge bonus on exploit weakness, and the monk's AC bonus. If your stats aren't great consider Urban Barbarian for a Dex build. If you take Enlightened Warrior, there is also some potentially interesting synergy between the Drunken Monk and the Drunken Brute archetype, which would let you gain drunken ki points and drunken rage points at the same time. Evasion overlaps – consider additional monk archetypes that swap that ability out (such as Monk of the Sacred Mountain, compatible with Drunken Monk). Unchained is meh; you’d rather have the Will save than redundant BAB.


Barbarian // Oracle *****
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full casting, physical and Cha
I'm assuming you're taking the lame curse here, because immunity to fatigue at level 5 allows you both to rage cycle and to drop out of rage whenever you want to cast - or invest in Mad Magic and keep raging while you cast. (Other curses drop this down to green.) Lunar is ideal for a natural weapons build and using Cha for AC reduces MAD.


Barbarian // Occultist ***
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Will, 4 skills, 6-level casting, physical Int
Hard to go really wrong taking a ragemonster and adding self-buffs and utility. On the other hand, the Occultist needs more Int investment than most partial casters and most of its active focus powers are SLAs that can't be used in rage, so this isn't ideal. Essentially, you have two options. You can take Dreadnought and build like a standard battle occultist with rage powers and reduced rage bonuses. Or, you can enjoy full barbarian bonuses but avoid in-combat focus powers (side step, danger sight) in favour of utility and careful prebuffing. Abjuration has a lot of in-combat powers, so it's skippable for the latter ragecultist – Transmutation is still great, aside from sudden speed. Thematically, you can describe implements as talismans if you'd like a consistent tribal feel. The Psychodermist archetype's trophy implements may also be thematic.


Barbarian // (Anti)Paladin **/**
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Will+, 4 skills, 4-level casting, physical Cha
The alignment restriction normally limits you to the Antipaladin, which doesn't add a lot to the barbarian. However if your GM allows it the Barbarian//Paladin is a massively durable combination, truly terrifying against evil, and the fatigue mercy allows you to rage cycle. The lesser celestial totem, in addition to being thematic, lets you add your level to your lay on hands during a rage.


Barbarian // Psychic **
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full casting, physical Int and Wis or Cha
MAD, not much synergy. Unless you're keen on a Wis-based discipline I'd recommend Bloodrager//Psychic for a little more stat synergy and easier access to Mad Magic.


Barbarian // Ranger ***
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Ref, 6 skills, 4-level casting, physical Wis
Makes a pretty good switch-hitter with the archery ranger style and a natural weapons barbarian build (boosted by ranger spells). It's easy to get three primary natural attacks by level 2 with careful race selection (tiefling is a good option). You also get quite a bit of utility between skills and spells. 


Barbarian // Rogue ***
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Ref, 8 skills, no casting, physical
The barbarian shores up the rogue's weaknesses – poor accuracy and fragility – while the rogue gives the barbarian a nice chunk of extra damage, a ref save, and some skills. The Urban Barbarian archetype is thematic and works well with a finesse rogue. The scout archetype gives you a sneak attack on your first attack after moving, which works well with pounce. Nice candidate for Sap Adept and Sap Master with Enforcer to add demoralization. For Strength-based TWF or ranged combat, see barbarian//slayer.


Barbarian // Shaman ***
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full casting, physical & Wis
I want this to be better. It’s not bad - definitely versatile - but light on actual synergy. Pending closer look at the Shaman spell list and other abilities to decide if Dreadnought helps.


Barbarian // Skald
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, casting


Barbarian // Slayer ***/*
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Ref, 6 skills, no casting, physical Int
Strong combat abilities, good utility. Big advantage over barbarian//rogue is access to Str-based TWF, which is pretty devastating with rage and the Double Slice feat. Also less invested in sneak attack since it shares space with the studied target buff – which, together with combat styles, also makes this a good choice for a ranged character or switch hitter.


Barbarian//Spiritualist
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Will, 4 skills, 6-level casting, physical & Wis
 
Barbarian // Sorcerer * (Dreadnought ***)
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full casting, physical & Cha
You’re going to spend a lot of effort working around arcane spell failure and the casting while raging restrictions, and the barbarian doesn't normally use Cha. If you’re a big fan of rage powers you’d do OK with Dreadnought and a combat-focused bloodline. Otherwise look at Bloodrager.


Barbarian // Summoner ***
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Will, 4 skills, 6-level casting, physical Cha
Best as a lightly armoured mounted build, since Ferocious Mount and Greater Ferocious Mount are big boosts to your eidolon. Use pre-buffs and tap Moment of Clarity if you need to drop a control spell (Mad Magic optional). If you're not going mounted, consider Bloodrager//Summoner instead.


Barbarian // Swashbuckler * (Urban Barbarian or Savage Technologist **)
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, no casting, Dex Con Cha
Normally the barbarian's rage does little to help the swashbuckler's combat style, but the variant rages of the Urban Barbarian or Savage Technologist increase Dex. Savage Technologist can act as a switch hitter, or maybe a thrown weapons build thanks to the Str/Dex combo, but its TWF ability doesn't work with Precise Strike or the Dex-to-damage feats. Only moderately strong in its melee niche, and not very versatile. Consider instead Urban Barbarian // Unchained Rogue, which gives you a dexterous barbarian with better skills, or Alchemist // Swashbuckler, for a more versatile swashbuckler enhanced by alchemy instead of rage.


Barbarian // Warpriest ***/* (Dreadnought ****)
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Will, 4 skills, 6-level casting, physical & Wis
Killer combatant with solid self-buffing ability and some support/healing/debuff potential. Warpriest allows more variety in combat styles than your average rager; notably TWF (take Double Slice), throwing (Hurler barbarian + Arsenal Chaplain warpriest for Ricochet Shot), unarmed (Sacred Fist) and even archery (Savage Technologist or Primal Hunter barbarian). You'll need Dreadnought or Moment of Clarity and Mad Magic to get the most use out of fervour's spellcasting action economy. If you aren't interested in Beast Totem you can significantly increase the usefulness of fervour's self-healing using lesser celestial totem.


Barbarian // Witch **/* (Old Scarred Witch Doctor ****)
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full casting, physical & Int
You can use hexes while raging, and the pre-errata scarred witch doctor offers excellent Con synergy. Spells still cause problems between rage and armour. Overall the best true arcane class to pair with Barbarian.


Barbarian // Wizard *
Basics: Full BAB, d12, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full casting, physical & Int
Armour spell failure, needs investment to cast while raging, no stat synergy. Try Bloodrager//Arcanist.
