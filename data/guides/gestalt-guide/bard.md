# Bard Combinations

Bard


The bard is a versatile part-caster with two big draws for gestalt: utility and action types. Swift action spells and move or swift action performance can often be combined with attacks or spells from another gestalt class. As typical for hybrids bard pairs best with charisma-based full casters and with martial characters. When gestalting with a caster, take advantage of neat bard-only spells like Glibness or Saving Finale. For archetypes:
* Geisha is nice for unarmoured gestalts – you lose just weapon proficiency and bardic knowledge in exchange for a bonus on courtly skills, the ability to use performance as a long-duration buff (tea ceremony) in addition to its normal form, and Scribe Scroll. 
* When combining bard with a martial class, consider Arcane Duelist or Chelish Diva for the ability to cast bard spells in medium or even heavy armour. The dervish archetypes and the archaeologist (“selfish bards”) let you activate your main buff more quickly at low levels, and for the Dawnflower Dervish and Archaeologist your personal combat bonus is bigger than Inspire Courage's group buff. That makes them strong options if you're in a small party or someone else has Inspire Courage.
* Chronicler of Worlds turns the bard Int-based, swaps Bardic Knowledge, Inspire Greatness, and Inspire Heroics, for more circumstantial planar-themed abilities, and gives you a Linguistics-based version of Versatile Performance. The archetype pairs well with Int-based casters (particularly full casters) and is also worth considering for any character that expects to see a lot of extraplanar enemies, as long as your second class isn’t Charisma-based. (Thanks to Shade L0rd for flagging this archetype.)
* Dwarven Scholar is useful if your second class relies heavily on Wisdom. It’s particularly nice for reach builds, between bonus feats and getting proficiency with one dwarven weapon (dorn-dergar, longaxe, longhammer, and giant-sticker all have reach).
Ilde Senescence says: While it does lose inspire courage which can be painful, reducing down to a single (primary) casting stat does open up building towards masterpiece DCs (The Waning Bolero is a favorite) and the replacement song allowing for what amounts to party-wide martial flexibility isn't much to sneeze at.
Weirdo replies: The utility of war chant is limited by the combat feats that you know (which is fortunately increased a bit through trading versatile performance for bonus feats). Whether it’s a good trade compared to inspire courage depends on your fighting style and your party’s. I note that several teamwork feats, including Precise Strike and Outflank, are eligible for War Chant.


Bard // Bloodrager ****
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills+, 6- and 4-level casting, physical Cha
Strong melee capability, good support and utility. In early levels bardic abilities compete with your attacks, but at higher levels you can rely more on pre-buffing and swift actions. You should be able to avoid casting bard spells after the first round of combat but Mad Magic is there for you if you need it. Casting stat synergy is nice, though it may not be worth investing enough charisma and feats to have strong offensive spells. 


Bard // Brawler ***/*
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, physical Cha
Good combat base with a light armour preference, support abilities, and skills. Very versatile in and out of combat. Dwarven Scholar’s war chant becomes very interesting when combined with martial flexibility. Good candidate for archaeologist since the Brawler doesn't use many swift actions.


Bard // Cavalier *** (Daring Champion ****)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, physical Cha
The Don Quixote. Good combination of melee skill and support, with the best option being the Daring Champion for a Dex build with extra charisma synergy and a preference for lighter armour.


Bard // Cleric *** (Dwarven Scholar or Elder Mythos Cultist****)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills+, full and 6-level casting, physical Cha Wis
Good pair for a support character. You may also be able to build for a secondary weapon-using role (though your stats make reach style difficult) or offensive spells with either class. With a good charisma consider channelling – or make an effective Undead Lord. The Dirge Bard is thematic for this but doesn't directly enhance the cleric's ability to Command Undead. You can get a bit more synergy by consolidating on one casting stat, either by switching Bard to Wis with Dwarven Scholar, or switching Cleric to Cha with Elder Mythos Cultist. The Cultist is particularly recommended if you want to make others’ minds your plaything. Bokrug grants the Ranseur for reach builds, while Hastur is otherwise thematic and the Rapier isn’t bad as a backup weapon. Mute Musician is thematic with the Elder Mythos and as written the Mute ability lets you use your instrument for verbal components from any class (though check with your GM, and note that your Dulled Horror save bonus is partially wasted). The Dwarven Scholar gives you better deity selection and makes a great reach cleric. If neither of those fits your concept, try Bard//Oracle.


Bard // Druid *** (Dwarven Scholar or Feyspeaker ****/*)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills+, full and 6-level casting, physical Wis Cha
Lots of support, control, and utility, but MAD and short on martial skill. Feyspeaker is thematic and adds Cha synergy, which gives you more room for the stats to support a secondary combat role. Dwarven Scholar also gives primary casting synergy, and thematically fits well with mountain druid concepts (sadly, I do not recommend the mountain druid archetype). Recommend reach or Songbird of Doom (see druid description).


Bard // Fighter ****
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, physical Cha
Good commander with a strong combat base, support, and utility. Note that the bonus skills from Lore Warden would apply in addition to the bard’s 6+Int skill ranks. Dwarven Scholar’s War Chant can become more useful when you have a lot of combat feats.


Bard // Gunslinger *** (Juggler ****)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, Dex Cha Wis
Mostly lends itself to using the gun with a little buffing on the side, but you should also be able to afford a high enough Charisma for offensive or in-combat casting if desired, and since you don't have a lot of Swift actions, you can make good use of Arcane Strike or Quickened spells at higher levels. Mysterious Stranger eats into those swift actions, so it's usually not worth the Cha synergy. Dwarven Scholar might be worth the Wis synergy, but it depends on whether your feat selection as a ‘slinger makes War Chant remotely helpful for the rest of the party. Juggler gets a special mention since juggling your pistols lets you dual-wield, reload, and cast spells at the same time (plus it looks cool).


Bard // Hunter ** (Dwarven Scholar) ***
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, physical Wis Cha
Good support and utility build in a small party - good versatility between the two spell lists and skill sets. The animal companion benefits from the bard’s buffs. There’s a little swift action crunch between animal focus and the bard’s buffs, but the fact that Animal Focus has a duration in minutes means you might be able to trigger it right before bashing in a door. Dwarven Scholar takes this up to green thanks to making the casting SAD, plus the synergy with sharing the Hunter’s free teamwork feats.(Thanks to Jeff for pointing out I’d missed this combo when adding theDwarven Scholar archetype to the Guide - and that this combo can share Outflank using War Chant at level 2). However, it’s still not ideal to have neither full BAB nor full casting on either end, so consider Bard//Druid, Ranger, or Slayer.


Bard // Inquisitor **
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, physical Wis Cha
Too unfocused. You have to spend a lot of actions buffing yourself into a competitive combatant – though bardic music, judgment, bane, and spells all provide good boosts, you'll need two or three at least to catch up to most full BAB gestalts, and three of these abilities are eventually competing for your swift actions. Dwarven Scholar helps a bit by relieving MAD - and makes the combination of war chant with teamwork feats work a bit more smoothly - but it’s not enough to bump this to green.


Bard // Investigator *** 
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, physical Int Cha
A good support/utility gestalt. You get lots of skill points and even more skill-enhancing abilities, plus utility spells/extracts. You're also fairly strong in melee with Studied Combat giving you a hefty accuracy and damage boost which can be combined with Bardic Music. Mutagen is also a useful buff to have in your pocket. A little MAD, and a little fragile for a melee gestalt, but not disastrously so. Chronicler of Worlds helps the MAD by turning this all Int-based, but I’m not sure if that’s enough to kick this into blue territory given the mediocre basic statline.


Bard // Kineticist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level and pseudo-casting, Con Dex Cha


Bard // Magus ** (Dawnflower Dervish or Chronicler of Worlds ***)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, physical Int Cha
Lack of full BAB or full casting and MAD issues make this one a tepid combo. The dawnflower dervish gives you a leg up on the popular dervish dancing magus build, including enough of a self-buff to keep up in combat with medium BAB. The main competing archetype is Chronicler of Worlds to make all your abilities Int-based. You can also reduce MAD with Eldritch scion on the magus side for a Cha focus, but this makes it harder to use key magus features so I’m not confident recommending it.


Bard // Medium
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills+, 6- and 4-level casting, physical Cha
Note that the Marshal's Inspiring Call ability doesn't stack with Inspire Courage. Spirit Dancer is thematic and really lets you get the most out of the versatility of the combination.


Bard // Mesmerist ***
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, physical Cha
This combination enables a lot of mind games with many different ways to mess with your opponents or assist your allies. It doesn't do a lot to diversify your concept, which prevents a high rating, but if you want to double down, have fun!


Bard // Monk **/* (Zen Archer, Sohei*** Unchained Scaled Fist or Dwarven Scholar****)
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, physical Wis Cha
Unchained is the best option with full BAB, and all saves; you’ll want to consolidate your ability scores either by using Scaled Fist on the monk side or Dwarven Scholar on the bard side. Works best as a melee build, though with Cha investment you can debuff pretty well between stunning fist, spells, and intimidation if you’re a Scaled Fist. The Scaled Fist should also consider Geisha, since you’re already dropping armour. If you're looking for a different combat style, you could make something half-decent with Zen Archer, or a Sohei reach or switch-hitter build. Sohei’s weapon training (polearms) should also work with Dwarven Scholar’s weapon proficiency if you pick longaxe or longhammer.


Bard // Occultist ***
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, Int Cha physical
Lots of utility, only passable offence made more difficult due to MAD. When considering implement schools, you'll want to give extra weight to spells not on the bard list. 
* Abjuration has a lot of these: Shield, Resist Energy, Magic Circle Against [], Protection from Energy, Life Bubble, Dimensional Anchor, Stoneskin, Spell Immunity, Antimagic Field, and Globe of Invulnerability.
* Conjuration's got a few goodies across multiple levels: Mage Armor, Mount, Minor Creation, Major Creation, Teleport, Wall of Stone, Heal, and Create Demiplane.
* Divination also has a handful of new spells: Augury, Status, Arcane Sight, Commune, True Seeing, and Prediction of Failure.
* Enchantment mostly features bard spells without the early access the bard gets. There are a handful of additions - Inflict Pain, Mind Swap, Mindwipe, and Antipathy/Sympathy – but overall it's just inferior to bard options.
* Evocation is almost entirely new ground. It's not great ground, but versatility is handy.
* Illusion, like enchantment, is almost entirely shared with the bard, with minor exceptions such as Mask Dweomer or Illusory Wall. On the other hand, the occultist does at least get these spells at the same level as a bard does, and being able to learn them as an occultist frees up bard slots for something else.
* Necromancy has quite a lot of new stuff. Notables include the Inflict spells, Animate Dead, False Life, Bestow Curse, Gentle Repose, Riding Possession, Death Ward, Possession, Major Curse, Object Possession, Suffocation, Harm, and Greater Possession.
* Transmutation of course has the widest variety of spells. Many of the good ones are shared with the bard. Occultist-only options include Enlarge Person, Lead Blades, Gravity Bow, Pass Without Trace, Greater Magic Weapon, Magic Vestment, Air Walk, Overland Flight, Telekinesis, and Disintegrate. 
Now, looking at build options:
* For a combat build you'll want Panoply Savant (Warrior), ideally with a combat bard archetype such as Arcane Duelist (strength-based melee with medium armour and a heavy shield) or Arrowsong Minstrel (longbow with buckler panoply implement – counts as full BAB for feats). For a “selfish” Dex build the dervish dancer and archaeologist work with fencing or slashing grace, which allow bucklers. The archaeologist is also thematic. You won't have a lot of extra focus after the panoply, so choose your other implements based on spells and maybe one or two utility focus powers. Conjuration and Illusion are good choices. Purge Corruption and Shadow Beast are worth considering. Divination's resonant power will probably not see much use due to the high cost, and its spell list is only meh, but Mind Eye is great. Enchantment, Necromancy, and Evocation generally rely too much on DCs.
* A caster has more options depending on how they prioritize stats. Transmutation and Abjuration are still excellent choices for the spell lists alone. Divination gets a lot more attractive when you have the focus to invest in it. Evocation and Necromancy also look a lot better if you can get your Int high enough for competitive DCs. Silksworn is a strong choice here, though unlike some gestalts losing armour hurts. I'd recommend it particularly for manipulator builds. Focus on Cha for mental focus, high DCs on superior bard enchantment spells, and social skills. Enjoy the sneaky bonuses from silksworn eloquence and silksworn deception. Use implements for buffing and utility. Alternatively, Chronicler of Worlds bard makes you entirely Int-based - compared to Silksworn you keep armour, get a bit more out of occultist spells, and have the option to choose an occultist archetype, but have less implement versatility. Note an interesting synergy between the Dirge Bard's ability to influence undead with mind-affecting effects, and the necromancy implement's resonant power applying a penalty to undead saving throws.


Bard // Oracle ****
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills+, full and 6-level casting, Cha
Great for support, healing, and debuffs. If you want a weapon, make it a reach build. Lore mystery is thematic and gives you access to crazy Cha synergy, though the Lunar and Nature mysteries also suffice for the latter. Voice of the Wild Bard//Nature Oracle makes a neat druidic sage. If you take the Heavens mystery make sure to have options for dealing with mindless opponents.


Bard // (Anti)Paladin ****
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills+, 6 and 4-level casting, physical Cha
A real do-everything build: combat, support, buffing, debuffing, and skills. The Dawnflower Dervish is both thematic and effective for a paladin of Sarenrae.


Bard // Psychic ***
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills+, full and 6-level casting, Int Cha
Enchantment, divination, and illusion – buffs and mind-affecting spells –  there's a lot of overlap on your spell lists. Since both classes are spontaneous you shouldn't run out of useful stuff, though I'd still prefer the versatility of a more divergent spell list. I recommend selecting any debuffs as a psychic since it'll be easier to achieve high DCs and you have access to Will of the Dead with those spells. Rapport is a thematic and useful discipline, though anything Cha-based will be fine. Plays like a support caster bard only more so. Chronicler of Worlds isn’t a game changer since disciplines will have you wanting a second mental stat regardless, but it’s worth looking at if you want to use bard spells with saving throws, want a Wis-based discipline, or expect to run into a lot of extraplanar challenges.


Bard // Ranger ***
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level and 4-level casting, physical Wis Cha
A little MAD, but still plenty competent in combat with good support and diverse utility. Archery and TWF (including shield bashing) are both valid styles with lots of static damage bonuses – consider Dervish Dancer archetype for the latter. Alternatively, Dwarven Scholar gives you a bit more stat synergy, and war chant could be interesting when combined with combat styles.


Bard // Rogue **
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 8 skills+, 6-level casting, physical Cha
The “selfish” archetypes add enough combat ability to keep the rogue competent, and invisibility helps with sneak attack, but this is still a sub-par combination with too much overlap in its basic abilities. Try bard//slayer.


Bard // Shaman **** (Dwarven Scholar)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills+, full and 6-level casting, Wis Cha
Until I get a chance to make a more in-depth dive into the Shaman, I defer to the excellent analysis from William and Idle Senescence, below. This is a nice package of buffs and debuffs with strong skill utility. You can either get a little more mileage out of your bardic charisma with the lore spirit or double-down on wisdom with the Dwarven Scholar archetype.
William says: An extremely versatile combination of casting, buffing, debuffing, high skill checks and potentially healing. Makes for a very potent support character that does not need much physical stat investment due to having access to hexes as a repeatable main method of attack, limiting the MAD aspect mostly to just Wis and Cha. The basic shaman hexes include Evil Eye, Chant (=Witch Cackle) and misfortune, to name a few, potentially aided by the Witch’s slumber hex for a potent offensive arsenal. This gestalt class combination also has innate access to multiple unique party-wide buffs such as Inspire Courage, Good Hope and Evil Eye (-2 or -4 on an  enemy’s AC, among other options, even if an enemy  succeeds their save) to give a party truly insane bonuses to their hit (up to an effective +10 attack rolls!) and damage capacity. It also has haste access on the bard and potentially shaman too.


The lore shaman spirit, a thematic choice, allows you to add a (cha-mod) number of spells from the Sorcerer and Wizard spell list to your shaman spell list (requires a bit of int investment), and (part-) human races can pick cleric spells to as a favored class bonus to add to their shaman list as well. As the shaman has some druid spells innate in their list, such as barkskin, you can easily cherry pick a ‘perfect’ selection of spells from each major ninth level casters to complement your hexes and bard spells. A life spirit shaman can also channel and has access to both breath of life and the heal spell at the same speed as a cleric. A Life-spirit shaman who picks up the lore spirit as a wandering spirit at level 6 (or the other way around) can do all the above in one build.  

The main reasons I wouldn’t rate it purple is that it does take a few levels to truly get rolling,  but it is definitely deep blue in my opinion. It is perhaps the best buffer and caster combination.

Bard // Slayer ***/*
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, physical Int Cha
Bonus feats are handy; weapon finesse, TWF and archery are all handy for the bard depending on your archetype choice (Dawnflower Dervish is great for TWF). Greater Invisibility plus Sneak Attack at level 10 is all kinds of lovely, especially for archers. You've also got a great array of skill bonuses depending on bard archetype and slayer talents – and Studied Combat is actually really nice for social interactions. Avoid talents with DCs and use buff spells to minimize your need for the mental stats.


Bard // Sorcerer ****
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills+, full and 6-level casting, Cha (physical)
Fine durability, charisma synergy, expanded spell options, lots of skills, and swift action abilities from the bard to complement a primarily casting focus. You can load up on charisma, or if your physical stats are decent take one or two combat feats (weapon finesse and arcane strike are good choices)  and step into melee in a pinch. Reach build works if you can get proficiency in the Elven Branched Spear. For a bloodline consider something that works well with your bard spells (such as Fey for the boost to compulsions), or aim for Great Fortitude as a bloodline feat.

Bard // Warpriest ** (Dervish ***)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, physical Wis Cha
You need TWF, one of the Dervish archetypes, and high stats to get good enough in combat for this to be worth it – otherwise Bard//Cleric or Bard//Paladin is better. Also a bit heavy on swift actions once bardic performance becomes one, though you can still perform as a move. Dwarven Scholar is a mixed bag: being able to ignore Cha removes some of your stat stress, but missing out not just on Dervish bonuses but inspire courage makes it hard for your to-hit to catch up.


Bard // Witch ***/* (Seducer or Chronicler of Worlds ***/*)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills+, full and 6-level casting, Int Cha
Strongly consider either Seducer witch or Chronicler of Worlds to remove MAD, which makes this at least a solid blue if not a purple. As a bonus, both can increase the thematic synergy of the class combo.


Bard // Wizard ***/* (Chronicler of Worlds ****/*)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills+, full and 6-level casting, Int Cha
Makes a pretty good loremaster with a solid array of spells and excellent skills. The weak Fort hurts but could be mitigated through choice of familiar. Chronicler of Worlds bard is thematic, and by removing MAD makes this at least a solid blue if not a purple. Alternatively consider Arcanist//Bard for more synergy with Charisma.
