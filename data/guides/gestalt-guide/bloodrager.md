# Bloodrager Combinations

Bloodrager


Like the barbarian, the bloodrager is a strong martial class, tough and capable of massive damage in melee. It also works best with hybrid classes that enhance combat options while also providing some utility, but with the Mad Magic feat allowing free casting while bloodraging, charisma casters work particularly well, including full casters. As a bonus, when you hit level 11 Mad Magic increases the DCs of all your spells.


Note that for the bloodrager, Mad Magic does not allow you to use spell-like abilities. And like the barbarian, psychic bloodragers still have to check with their GM about whether rage is considered a “hostile emotion effect” that would interfere with psychic components. The Id rager does explicitly prevent rage from interfering with your emotion spells (which may or may not imply anything about the base rage); otherwise it’s not a great archetype.


Loss of barbarian archetypes negatively affects some combinations, and it becomes MAD when combined with classes using Int or Wis.


Bloodrager // Brawler ***/*
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, 4-level casting, physical Cha
A solid combination for adding more combat tricks to your bloodrager. Check out Stalwart and Improved Stalwart – Combat Expertise is great for the Brawler since you can fill in maneuver feats with Martial Flexibility. Consider Blood Conduit to add a bit more hurt to your maneuvers. Still a little shy on utility, but spellcasting gives you a few extra options.

If you don’t mind losing healing potential, Elder Mythos Cultist has better stat synergy and the Aberrant Bloodline is both thematic and supports a reach style.

Bloodrager // Gunslinger * (Urban Bloodrager **)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, 4-level casting, Dex Wis Cha
You need the Dex rage for this to work at all, and even then it's a little lacklustre since many of the bloodline powers and feats are geared more towards melee combat. Switch hitting stretches your resources either with feats or with stats, and you're low on both mundane and magical utility. You can do some fun stuff with Arcane bloodline if you have Snap Shot, though. Iron Will as a bloodline feat is a bonus.


Bloodrager // Hunter ***/*
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 6 skills, 6- and 4-level casting, physical Wis Cha
The Barbarian//Hunter gets more use out of Greater Ferocious Mount, and the extra stat requirements are not fantastic. 


Bloodrager // Inquisitor ***/*
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 6 skills+, 6- and 4-level casting, physical Wis Cha
Stats are stretched a little thin here, but if you can make it work this is very nice. Inquisitor's combat buffs help you pile on the hurt, and you still have a good range of utility potential including scouting, tracking, social and knowledge skills. Your Intimidate can be absolutely devastating. Primalist // Sacred Huntmaster gives you access to a mount with Amplified Rage, though you'll have fewer rage powers to share than the Barbarian.


Bloodrager // Investigator **/**
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills+, 6- and 4-level casting, physical Int Cha
If you can use Studied Combat while Bloodraging this is a strong albeit a little MAD combination with highly damaging melee abilities and a good selection of utility. If you can't, this is not exciting.


Bloodrager // Magus ***
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 4 skills, 6- and 4-level casting, physical Int Cha
Mad Magic makes this better than Barbarian//Magus, but the requirement for Int and Cha is a bit steep. To make better use of the strength bonus, recommend grabbing a one-handed weapon that you can also two-hand for extra damage when not using Spell Combat. Alternatively, take Urban Bloodrager for a Dex rage.


Bloodrager//Kineticist */****
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, pseudo-casting, physical Cha
Like the barbarian, the bloodrager can't use spell-like abilities when raging. If you can convince your GM to let Mad Magic apply to your blasts this goes all the way to purple, thanks to the Con boost while raging.


Bloodrager // Monk ****
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves+/Fort and Ref+, 4 skills, 4-level casting, physical Wis Cha
Very good melee and self-buffing, potential for debuffing, light on party support and utility. No alignment restriction and less value for rage cycling mean you have more archetype options than just the martial artist. Unchained is notable for adding 1.5 Str when flurrying two-handed, and for giving you good mobility without having to trade bloodline powers for Beast Totem, though your Will save drops. Add Scaled Fist for Cha synergy. Sohei pairs well with Bloodrider. Alternatively, you could also take Master of Many Styles, wear armour, and suppress your monk fast movement. Kata Master is notable for giving you some Cha-based swashbuckler tricks in exchange for nothing significant. On the Bloodrager side, Blood Conduit is a good archetype due to maneuver synergy, especially if you keep fast movement on the monk end. Consider spelleater or untouchable if you can't afford 14 Cha. Skip Bloody-Knuckle Rowdy.


Bloodrager // Medium ****
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 4 skills, two 4-level casting, physical and Cha
A solid and thematic combination. Champion grants nice, reliable offensive power; the Guardian's defensive features are more useful for a spelleater or steelblood bloodrager since the DR will not overlap and you can deal with heavy armour spell failure. The medium spell list and minor class features add a bit of magical utility to the bloodrager's combat-focused list.


Bloodrager // Mesmerist ***/*
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, 6- and 4-level casting, physical and Cha
Like the barbarian, this gets an extra boost if Hypnotizing Stare (which requires “focus”) is compatible with bloodrage (which doesn't allow “patience and concentration”). If so, you get exceptional self-buffing and debuffing action economy with your magic tantrums, forcing opponents to wither before you. Charisma synergy and easier access to Mad Magic puts this a touch above Barbarian//Mesmerist.


Bloodrager // Oracle *****
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and 4-level casting, physical Cha
Pairing great combat abilities with a full casting list rich in self-buffs and which complements your bloodrager casting stat? Excellent choice. Once greater bloodrage kicks in rage cycling comes with a cost, but Lame Curse is still a good idea, especially for a Primalist with per/rage abilities. Bloodrager casting complements the Oracle list nicely with goodies like Shield, and you get more out of a Cha-focused build (Lore/Lunar/Nature) than the barbarian. Once again, Lunar is great for natural weapons.


Bloodrager // Occultist ***
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 4 skills, 6-level and 4-level casting, physical Int Cha
You get rage, self-buffs, and utility. On the other hand, you need two mental stats.
Recommend transmutation for offence (and fly), illusion for miss chance defences, divination for utility, conjuration for healing and teleportation, and evocation for a backup ranged blast. Check if your GM will let you use focus powers during a rage. As supernatural abilities they don't require concentration mechanically, but some stricter GMs might decide that expending “mental focus” requires concentration thematically. Mad Magic does not, RAW, help with this. This would make the evocation school and certain focus powers such as danger sense or side step much less useful.


Bloodrager // (Anti ***)Paladin ****
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will+, 4 skills, casting
No alignment restrictions means you can go for paladin and the great durability that implies. Primalist gives you access to Lesser Celestial Totem to boost your healing, and the fatigue mercy is great to have if you need to rage cycle. You're low on out of combat utility but at least the bloodrager brings a few extra skill points to the table. Antipaladin is less fantastic but still good. Consider Steelblood for heavy armour.


Bloodrager // Psychic ***
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and 4-level casting, physical Int Cha
Aside from MAD, a decent warrior-mage. The psychic gives you access to a greater range of buffs than bloodrager alone, and adds a lot of more subtle mental magic, though you'll want to avoid anything with DCs. The Pain discipline is notable for its limited Lay on Hands (which allows fatigue removal and pairs well with the Lesser Celestial Totem for significant healing) and is a good thematic fit.


Bloodrager // Ranger ***
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 6 skills, two 4-level casting, physical Wis Cha
Strong melee character or switch-hitter with decent utility. Rage + Str-based TWF is a good combo. Barbarian//Ranger is marginally better thanks to not needing the second mental stat.


Bloodrager // Rogue ***
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 8 skills, 4-level casting, physical Cha
The bloodrager shores up the rogue's weaknessess – poor accuracy and fragility – while the rogue gives the bloodrager more out of combat ability, a ref save, and sneak attack for a nice damage boost. If you're going primalist with the beast totem, consider scout rogue archetype to sneak attack on a pounce.

Bloodrager // Sorcerer ***
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and 4-level casting, physical Cha
Has some issues with arcane spell failure and action economy, but works pretty well if you can keep your casting mostly to utility spells and long-duration buffs, or go for a reach build. At higher levels you can throw a few quickened spells into the mix. Mad Magic is a no-brainer. Bloodlines take some thought as they need to match. Abyssal is notable – the first level claws are redundant but strength of the abyss is great on a bloodrager and you can use sorcerer spells like mirror image or displacement to cover for the Abyssal bloodrager's terrible AC. Aberrant is great for reach builds - in theory the reach increases stack, though Unusual Anatomy doesn’t. Overlapping bloodlines can trade out these abilities using primalist bloodrager or crossblooded (sorcerer is usually a better deal for crossblooded). The feat lists are almost identical so it's worth expanding them with crossblooded or blood conduit.

Bloodrager // Summoner ***
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 4 skills, 6- and 4-level casting, physical Cha
Strong melee with a side serving of charisma-based casting, though you need to stick with light armour. If you go synthesist you can add goodies like pounce without giving up on your bloodline powers, but have to skip the armour entirely. Ferocious Mount (via Primalist) is a nice buff, though unlike Barbarian//Summoner you probably don't have enough rage powers for Greater Ferocious Mount to be worth it.


Bloodrager // Swashbuckler * (Urban Bloodrager ***)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, 4-level casting, Dex Con Cha
If you don't get Dex rage, there's no point, but with your stats pointing in the right direction this can be pretty effective. You're nice and mobile (and eligible for pounce) and have some good defense options between decent AC, parry, and bloodline abilities - though unfortunately no DR or Con bonus. Switch hitting is possible, but out of combat utility is not great. 


Bloodrager // Warpriest ***/*
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 4 skills, 6-and 4-level casting, physical Wis Cha
Packs a heck of a wallop. You have fewer options for ranged combat than the Barbarian//Warpriest, but Greater Bloodrage (and possibly Arcane Bloodrage) plus easier access to Mad Magic for Fervour gives you better action economy for self-buffing. Consider Steelblood to cast bloodrager spells in heavy armour – in which case also consider adamantine full plate, when you can afford it, to replace your DR. Alternatively, Spelleater is handy if you don't want to invest in the Cha to actually cast your bloodrager spells.

Bloodrager // Witch *** (Scarred Witch Doctor ****)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and 4-level casting, physical Int Cha
As a warrior-mage, this isn’t bad. Most hexes are useable in bloodrage, even without Mad Magic. Consider a “bad touch” style with witch spells like Chill Touch and Frostbite on your list. Aberrant bloodline is thematic and allows for a reach build; it also offers Improved Initiative and Lightning Reflexes as bloodline feats. Stats are a pain.
