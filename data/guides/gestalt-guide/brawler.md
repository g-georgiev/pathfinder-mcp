# Brawler Combinations

Brawler


Brawlers get a large number of attacks with unarmed strikes, close, or monk weapons and can have good AC with light armour and a shield, losing no attack potential for the latter thanks to the way their Flurry works. Brawlers are also among the most flexible combatants in the game, thanks to – what else? - Martial Flexibility. This ability lets you pick up circumstantially useful feats as needed. They benefit from accuracy bonuses, damage bonuses, a good will save, out of combat utility, and any abilities that help them full attack more often due to dependence on Flurry.


If you don't want to have to keep track of all those combat feats, Mutagenic Mauler and Snakebite Striker gives you worthwhile passive damage boosts instead, plus a few minor abilities. Snakebite is especially good when your other class gives you a pet to flank with. Shield Champion is a really neat build for switch-hitting; the Brawler is otherwise weak at ranged combat, though you can do OK with shuriken or wushu darts in a pinch, and the Winding Path Renegade (Wind) gives you an extra bit of range.


Compared to the similar Monk, the Brawler's main advantages for gestalt are (1) Martial Flexibility (2) not needing Wis (3) ability to use armour and shields. It's also very good at pulling out combat maneuvers very effectively when needed, compared to the maneuver archetypes for monk (maneuver master and tetori) which are severely hampered when their speciality maneuvers become impractical. The Monk, depending on the build, can get better maneuverability, better defenses against non-weapon attacks, better ranged damage, and of course mystical powers. Monks tend to be better than Brawlers for characters that are overall Wis-based or that want combat to be a secondary role to casting.


Brawler // Cavalier (Snakebite Striker, Daring Champion)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, no casting, physical (Cha optional)
The usual mounted lance charge is really hard to pull off with the weapons the Brawler uses. You're better off using your mount as a flanking partner, especially with Snakebite Striker; your teamwork feats are handy with this. Daring Champion has better combat style synergy if you like Martial Flexibility; just snag a light piercing close or monk weapon and a buckler. Since Flurry doesn't actually use two weapons, it does as written work with Precise Strike. Accuracy is a little low, but my goodness the damage. 


Brawler // Cleric
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, physical Wis
Solid choice for a combat cleric. OK for bad punching, though Monk//Cleric gives you more out of the high Wis you need for debuffing and supports a greater range of builds.


Brawler // Druid (Goliath, Nature Fang)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, physical Wis
You can't use Feral Combat Training with the Brawler's Flurry, so that's one major point against this build. You can technically unarmed strike as a bear, but this is much more effective fighting in humanoid shape, with Goliath or Nature Fang. The druid's buffs are very useful to the Brawler, and the Goliath's size increases work especially well with the Brawler's big weapon dice. Lemeres suggests pairing Goliath Druid with Outslug Style for "I am punching you from the next town over." See also: Monk//Druid.


Brawler // Fighter (Viking/Shield Champion)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, no casting, physical
Too much overlap to be really good – do you really need that many combat feats? [Note: following written pre-Advanced Amour Training, see Nathan Brown’s comment.] You'll also want to take a fighter archetype that gets rid of armour training since the brawler prefers light armour – the unarmed fighter, brawler, free hand fighter, and two weapon warrior are worth a look. Viking and shielded fighter work well with shield champion (brawler), and viking has the additional advantage that you can trade excess combat feats for rage powers. Lack of a strong will save or out of combat abilities is a major downside.
Nathan Brown says: Armed Bravery is too good to give up.  If you take the close weapon group for compatibility with Brawler's Flurry you are always wielding Unarmed Strike so Armed Bravery applies any time you're conscious. Archetypes that keep both weapon training and bravery are rare.  In fact, with Border Defender requiring medium armor for its features I can only find Mutation Warrior or Vanilla fighters as options.  Advanced Armor Training has some options that make the Vanilla fighter advantageous, specifically Armor Specialization.  Even capping out at +3 for light armor that's still worthwhile.  And you can still use the other armor training upgrades for Adaptable Training if you don't like any of the other options.


Brawler // Gunslinger
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, no casting, physical Wis
You're working with two incompatible combat styles and no other bonuses of note.


Brawler // Hunter
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 6 skills, 6-level casting, physical Wis
Remember how I said Snakebite Striker works well with a pet for flanking? Well, Hunters get a pet AND teamwork feats for even better flanking. Base class and other archetypes also work well as a Feral Hunter.


Brawler // Inquisitor
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, physical Wis
Powerful and versatile in and out of combat. Brawler's Flurry with a single bane weapon is great, judgment or other buffs improve accuracy, and you get a good selection of bonus feats for fun stuff. Remember to consider teamwork feats when using martial flexibility! You also have a good selection of utility spells and skills. Consider Snakebite Striker//Sacred Huntmaster or Wild Child//Sacred Slayer for sneak attacking with a flanking pet.


Brawler // Investigator
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, physical Int
Remember how Sherlock Holmes liked himself some fisticuffs? It’s really handy for a detective to be able to walk around fully armed without a weapon, and to restrain suspects without killing them. It's also very effective in this combination. Studied Combat, Studied Strike, and the Weapon Training talent are great accuracy and damage boosters – Brawler's Flurry will hit and hit hard. Combat Expertise, which opens you up to take maneuver feats, allows you to trade excess accuracy for defense. Quick Study is a good 5th level Investigator talent; you are not heavy on swift actions. You're not hurting for utility, either, with extracts, lots of skill points, every skill except Fly and Survival on your combined class list, and miscellaneous talents.


Brawler // Kineticist
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, pseudo-casting, physical
Kinetic blasts don't generally work with the Brawler's style, but you can use Kinetic Fist and elemental overflow for a modest offensive bonus with UAS (mostly useful after level 5, when infusion specialization lets you use Kinetic Fist without burn). Having a ranged blasting option is also handy, and depending on element you can add significant defense, control, or utility options. Elemental Ascetic reduces the burn for Kinetic Fist, but takes away most of your ranged options and swaps your elemental defense for a monk's unarmoured AC. I'd use it only if you can afford a good Wis and only for a kinetic element that has a weak elemental defense and ranged options that don't rely on attack rolls: air, fire, and void.


Brawler // Magus
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, casting
Brawler's Flurry doesn't work with Spell Combat, and weapons in the close weapon group aren't the best choices for the Magus.  If you want Martial Flexibility, take Martial Master Fighter//Magus.


Brawler//Medium
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, partial casting, physical Cha
The champion's main powers don't work with flurry, but the static damage bonus and spirit surge are good and the ability to give up your extra flurry attacks for a semi-pounce is useful if you don't want to take Pummeling Style.


Brawler // Mesmerist
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, 6-level casting, physical Cha
Lots of physical and mental control options. Consider Brawler//Bard since buffing is a bit easier on the stats than debuffing.


Brawler // Monk
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves/Fort and Ref, 4 skills, no casting, physical Wis
There's a lot of overlap here, but you can reduce it by trading out flurry. Master of Many Styles and Maneuver Master are at the top of my list; Brawler//MoMS works especially well with Outslug Style and optionally Ascetic Style. Alternatively if you like Unchained Monk's mobility, Strangler gives you very effective grappling abilities with very little lost. Note that due to odd writing you'll have to actually take Improved Grapple, likely as your 1st level monk bonus feat. Overall, the combo gives you lots of options in melee, but drastically drops in usefulness at range or out of combat.


Brawler // Occultist
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, 6-level casting, physical Int
Transmutation implement is a must for accuracy & damage boosts. On top of the juicy stat bonuses, Legacy Weapon is great with Flurry (note: Bane). Enlarge Person is fantastic for increasing damage dice, and you can take it as a focus power or spell. After that, you can improve defence and utility to taste with abjuration, divination, and illusion. Conjuration is also worth considering at levels 6 or 10 for Side Step, or earlier if your group needs a secondary healer. It's also got the most painless resonant power to give up with Haunt Channeller – followed by Illusion. 


Brawler // Oracle
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, physical Cha
Well-balanced character for combat and support. Cha-to-AC works well here as a strength-based fighter wearing light armour, and you can also get an animal companion flank buddy from Lunar or Nature, which is extra handy if you have sneak attack from the Snakebite Striker archetype. Other notable options include demoralizing (Intimidate) and Revelation Strike. Alternatively, Dance of Blades from Metal Mystery works nicely with Outslug Style when using a metal close weapon, like the emei piercer or tri bladed katar.


Brawler // (Anti)Paladin
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves+, 4 skills, 4-level casting, physical Cha
Great melee combatant with potential for support and control. Flurry works really nicely with Smite's big bonuses. Shield Champion//Paladin is the gestalt Captain America build, and the ranged flexibility is nice. Champion of Irori (Enlightened Paladin) is also worth a look, particularly if you want to use unarmed strikes and your campaign isn't expected to feature a lot of evil enemies. The antipaladin's debuffs are less useful than the paladin's support, healing, and immunities, but it's still a pretty punishing match.


Brawler // Psychic
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, physical Int and Wis or Cha
Phrenic Strike is a very useful amplification because it lets you deliver a touch spell with an  UAS in the same round as you cast it, instead of needing to carry a charge. 


Brawler // Ranger
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 6 skills, 4-level casting, physical Wis
TWF fighting style is less useful since Brawler's Flurry duplicates the main line and Double Slice; You can still pick up Quick Draw, Two-Weapon Defence, and Two-Weapon Rend, but you're better off taking archery style for thrown weapons. Weapon and Shield is an excellent style for a Shield Champion, though note that if you take Shield Master as a combat style feat at level 6 it's redundant with the Brawler's feat at level 11. Retraining is a good option if available since you get early access without giving up a feat.


Brawler // Rogue
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 8 skills, no casting, physical
Flurry and Sneak Attack gives you high damage potential, but poor accuracy is an issue; this makes Mutagenic Mauler particularly attractive. Also consider Wild Child for the flanking partner, and ninja for supernatural tricks. Consider pairing Brawler with Archaelogist Bard, Vivisectionist Alchemist, or Investigator for a more versatile character with better self-buffing potential.

Brawler // Slayer
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 6 skills, no casting, physical Int
High damage potential with flurry + sneak attack, plus an accuracy booster! Combat style feats are best spent on throwing – or just skip them and grab more utility options. Alternatively, Weapon and Shield works well for a shield champion, especially if you can retrain Shield Master when it's duplicated at level 11.


Brawler // Sorcerer
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, physical Cha
Multi-touch spells are pretty nasty when delivered using a flurry of unarmed strikes, the brawler can easily handle melee combat unarmoured, and some bloodlines like Abyssal, Infernal (Pit-Touched), or Orc are really nice for a melee character. Buff spells like Shield or Mirror Image are also great.


Brawler // Spiritualist
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, 6-level casting, physical Wis


Brawler // Summoner
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, 6-level casting, physical Cha
Adding a bit of magical versatility to a solid combatant is never a bad call. Both classes favour light armour, so that's a plus. Snakebite Striker likes the flanking partner.


Brawler // Swashbuckler
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, no casting, Dex Cha
Not a lot of synergy, but you can at least use both styles together if you take a piercing weapon in the close weapon group, like the fighting fan or emei piercer – and if your GM agrees that since brawler's flurry only actually needs one weapon to attack it doesn't prevent you from using precise strike.


Brawler // Warpriest (Arsenal Chaplain)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, 6-level casting, physical Wis
Solid self-buffing ability, good action economy, and some feats to play with. Arsenal Chaplain is a great pick since the Brawler continues to advance your weapon damage dice. Most Brawlers don't get much out of Sacred Fist, but it works well with Strangler.


Brawler // Witch (White Haired Witch, Scarred Witch Doctor)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, physical Int
The feat Hex Strike provides interesting synergy with unarmed combat.


Brawler // Wizard
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, physical Int
Not a lot of synergy, but extremely versatile.
