# Cavalier Combinations

Cavalier


The cavalier is particularly poorly suited to gestalt with arcane casters.


Cavalier // Cleric (Elder Mythos Cultist)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full casting, physical Wis


Cavalier // Druid
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 4 skills, casting
Reasonably well-rounded combination. Use druid for utility and longer-duration pre-buffs like Barkskin and Greater Magic Fang, then cavalier about in combat. Consider Urban Druid or Nature Fang if you want to play a proper mounted cavalier, since wild shape isn't as useful in that case. Consider taking a domain as a druid instead of duplicating the animal companion – but keep in mind that means you can't Share Spells with druid spells. Alternatively if you'd like a backup firearm and can justify the flavour, Musketeer drops the cavalier mount, but none of the mounted combat abilities.


Cavalier // Fighter
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort, 4 skills, casting


Cavalier // Gunslinger
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, casting


Cavalier // Hunter (Musketeer) (Huntmaster)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 6 skills, 6-level casting, physical Wis
Musketeer gives up the duplicate mount for a decent ranged backup, but leaves the charging abilities alone and is even compatible with Gendarme. The Huntmaster is fun and thematic if you don't mind a lot of pets – use the Hunter's companion for your mount and then take a couple dogs or birds as hunting beasts.


Cavalier // Inquisitor
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 6 skills+, casting, physical Wis


Cavalier // Investigator
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, physical Int
It takes a round to set up, but Challenge + Studied Combat gives you massive damage bonuses (up to 2.5x level with Daring Champion's precise strike) as well as an accuracy boost. Combine them with a lance charge and Sudden Strike and things just die.
Daring Champion Cavalier with Order of the Dragon, there's a Gestalt option for an Investigator. Play Human, grab Fencing Grace at level 1, and put a single Feat into Extra Panache when you hit 5th, and you're good to go. +2 and 1/2 my level to damage and +3/4 my level on to-hit? Why yes, thank you, that sounds lovely. All good Saves and awesome skills as well? Why thank you! It's a fun, thematic, build. You even get Tactician on an Int character.

Cavalier // Monk (Sohei, Daring Champion)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves/Fort and Will, 4 skills, no casting, physical Wis
Normally the monk doesn't synergize well with the cavalier. However the Sohei's light armour and mount-enhancing abilities (sans mount) make this a good pair. At 6th level, take weapon training (spears) and not only do you get attack and damage bonuses with the lance but you get to flurry with it when not charging. Mounted Skirmisher lets you lance flurry when moving, though unfortunately not while charging. I'd say Order of the Sword is a good choice, coupled with Beast Rider. You can full attack from the back of your mount at level 1, and eventually your mount can pounce. Try to avoid charisma-dependent order abilities to reduce MAD. Biggest downside is lack of utility thanks to mediocre skills and no casting. Daring Champion is also not a bad choice thanks to adding massive static damage bonuses to flurry. It works with Sohei for a switch-hitting thrown weapon build. (Unchained, Weapon Adept?) Order of the Eastern Star gives you a welcome boost to attacks, DR, and saves when you have Combat Reflexes (and probably Crane Style) going. Alternatively, you could get Pummeling Style and laugh at any and all DR. If you're really determined, you could pick up those Chakra feats.

Cavalier // Sorcerer (Daring Champion)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 4 skills, casting
The cavalier is usually reliant on heavy armour and lance combat doesn't lend itself well to the sorcerer's strengths. The Daring Champion is a step up because it supports light or no armour and has a charisma-based panache ability – though finesse is a mixed blessing since it means you can't take advantage of the usual strength-based melee bloodlines like Orc or Abyssal.


Cavalier//Spiritualist
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 4 skills, casting


Cavalier // Summoner
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 4 skills, casting
The eidolon can serve as an extra-powerful mount (in which case strongly consider Daring Champion or Musketeer on the cavalier side to get rid of the duplicate) or you can keep the cavalier's mount and play around with the eidolon a little more. Note that Order of the Sword adds your mount's Str to your damage, which can be a big deal if you have a well-muscled eidolon mount.


Cavalier // Swashbuckler
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, casting
Take Daring Champion and gestalt it with something else, like bard, ninja, or paladin.
the swashbuckler/samurai is an interesting concept but you end up with only 4 skill points (which isn't a dealbreaker but is kind of low for gestalt), a poor will save (which you should always try to avoid), and no casting ability... you might be better off going swashbuckler/kensai or swordsaint/arcane duelist- both could give you a lot of the same feel but with all good saves and 6 level casting (though if you want a Dex build the swordsaint/arcane duelist would either need a 1 level dip in swashbuckler or a dueling sword and weapon finesse/slashing grace).
