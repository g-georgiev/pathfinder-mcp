# Cleric Combinations

Cleric


This powerful but somewhat bland caster can be gestalted most effectively by playing to the main types of cleric build. It mostly suffers from lack of skill points. If your other class has only 2 skill points, I recommend either the Roaming Exorcist or Herald Caller (but not Cloistered Cleric) archetypes for 4 skill points, or Cardinal for 6. The Cardinal works best with martial classes that restore the BAB and armour proficiencies (in which case you're trading a domain and spontaneous cures for 4 skill points and some class skills).


Combat: combine cleric with a full-BAB class, ideally one that doesn't use Int or Cha. Use cleric buffs to improve your own combat ability; check out the Strength or Growth domains. You can also cover healing out of combat, and have options to buff your allies or summon things. Herald Caller is handy for the latter – your combat half should provide the armour proficiencies you need anyway. Avoid enemy-targeting spells since you probably won't have a high enough Wis to deal with saves and SR. Tends to be a well-balanced gestalt.


Casting: combine cleric with another casting class, ideally one that also uses Wisdom, for increased versatility and possibly increased durability. This build is usually the best positioned for debuffing and controlling undead in addition to having support and summoning options.


Reach: use your standard actions to cast and a reach weapon to make attacks of opportunity. While this build requires heavy investment in feats and stats (you need better Dex than most combat clerics) it pays off in excellent action economy. This build can work when paired with either martial or casting classes, but it's especially important that the other class not use Int and Cha, and bonus feats or reach-enhancing abilities are extra welcome. I also like this build when combining clerics with classes that, like clerics, have both melee-focused and casting abilities, like Inquisitor or Druid. Using full-BAB AoO instead of iterative attacks can make up for having only an average BAB progression.


It's also notable that while clerics are MAD enough that they often end up with a low or middling Cha, they do have some useful Cha-dependent features that you should look into if your second class uses Cha. Channeling can be used not only to restore HP but grant buffs (eg through variant channeling) or remove conditions (Merciful Healer archetype), and with the Quick Channel feat you can Channel as a move action, which is very nice action economy for a caster cleric. The Evangelist archetype gives you a limited bardic performance – it's a generally worthwhile archetype that gets better with a higher Cha for more rounds/day. Controlling Undead (Undead Lord archetype recommended but optional) is also much improved by a higher Cha, since the Command Undead feat grants a Cha-based save to your prospective minions.


Alternatively, if you want to really go Cha-focused, consider the Elder Mythos Cultist. This archetype switches your casting stat to Cha and lets you use Cha for Will saves instead of Wis, but comes with limitations: vulnerability to insanity, restricted deity and domain choices and CN/CE alignment. You also trade Channel Energy for a more universally damaging Channel the Void effect, and lose spontaneous casting. Overall the restrictions on domains and loss of spontaneous casting and Channel make this a sub-par healer and ineffective undead controller. However, it can still excel at debuffing (including “bad touch”), summoning, and melee or ranged combat.


The Sacred Attendant gives you a small AC bonus if your other class doesn't wear armour.


Cleric // Druid
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, two full casting, physical Wis Cha
This nature priest gets a very nice range of spells with casting stat synergy.  You should be able to take domains from both classes (though remember you still only prepare each class's domain spells in their own domain slots). Good candidate for a reach build, though it also works as a focused caster. Supernaturalist Druid // Herald Caller has good summoning synergy. If you want a primarily combat build, look at Cleric//Ranger, or at least try combining Inquisitor or Warpriest with Druid, or Hunter with Cleric, for easier buffing. A charisma-focused build is also possible with Feyspeaker//Elder Mythos Cultist. It’s not a particularly good or thematic combination, but you get extra skills without giving up either BAB or medium armour.


Cleric // Fighter
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 2 skills, full casting, physical Wis Cha
Effective in a variety of combat styles. The bonus feats and lack of additional stat requirements are also useful for a reach build; plus, armour training lets you wear heavy armour while still benefiting from an above-average dex. Recommend an archetype with more skills: Lore Warden, Roaming Exorcist, Herald Caller, or Cardinal.


Cleric // Gunslinger
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting,  Dex Wis Cha
Definitely a combat cleric, but you should also be able to afford the Wis for good save DCs when you want them, and possibly even a solid Cha for channeling.


Cleric // Hunter
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills, full and 6-level casting, physical Wis Cha
Slower progression of Druid offensive spells and medium BAB means this works best as primarily support/healer character. However, Animal Focus and teamwork feats do give you a leg up on weapons combat, whether with a reach build (highly recommended), as a secondary role, or even in a more combat-focused build (preferably melee to make the most of your teamwork feats). Good utility, especially for scouting.


Cleric // Inquisitor
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 6 skills+, full and 6-level casting, physical Wis Cha
Priest and more priest. You can rely on your cleric's spontaneous casting of cure spells, use your inquisitor list for staples (like Divine Favour) or unique spells (Blistering Invective), and then fill out your cleric list with whatever else you want, including situational spells (Negate Poison, Banishment). While medium BAB is disappointing for gestalt, judgment and other buffs make weapons combat a viable option. Alternatively Relic Hunter or Monster Tactician improve magical versatility. The mix of casting and combat overall plays similarly to a non-gestalt cleric but with expanded options – including strong skills.


Cleric // Investigator
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills+, full and 6-level casting, physical Wis Int Cha
I prefer the Cleric//Inquisitor but if you've got good stats to work with, this has some things to recommend it: a strong Ref save, a more potent (if less versatile) primary combat buff, and the ability to pass out infusions of Shield or polymorph buffs like delicious candy.


Cleric // Kineticist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, Con Dex Wis
Kineticist has better offensive options, so I'd recommend focusing on those and using Cleric mostly for extra support. Divine Favour is more useful when making multiple attacks via Kinetic Blade or Flurry of Blasts. You might consider Elemental Ascetic for a bad punch build – and only for a bad punch build, because otherwise it's not worth it. Note however you don't get Wis to AC until level 2, which is dangerous. As always, Ecclesitheurge is a good archetype for gestalt clerics who don't wear armour. 


Cleric // Magus
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and 6-level casting, physical Int Wis
This is another MAD combination for the cleric, and the basics are pretty poor. You can land some pretty punishing spellstrikes with Broad Study, though – try rolling a crit for Harm.


Cleric // Medium (Elder Mythos Cultist or Spirit Channeler)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and partial casting, Wis Cha physical?
The hierophant doesn't add much since you already have the ability to pick new spells from the cleric list day-to-day and the ability to channel energy. You need champion spirit, cleric buffs, and a good stat spread to be competitive in combat. Elder Mythos Cultist gives you stat synergy - which could make for a serviceable reach build. Voice of the Void is thematic as a pair for Cultist on the Medium side. You could also try (Rivethun) Spirit Channeler to switch your Medium stat to Wis, but that also makes significant changes to how your spirits work. Overall I recommend Oracle//Medium instead for better stat synergy and better use of the hierophant – or pick Cleric//Inquisitor or Ranger for a more synergistic way to add utility.


Cleric // Mesmerist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills, full and 6-level casting, Wis Cha
A real cult leader (archetype optional), nice and durable with a variety of support, debuff, and utility options. 


Cleric // Monk
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, physical Wis
Works wonderfully for many flavours of cleric. You can make an excellent SAD support or bad touch/punching build with Sensei (and optionally Ki Mystic). Sohei with Weapon Training (polearms) makes a good reach build, though it takes a few levels to pull together; the initiative bonus is handy for a caster, but skip armour. For a more combat-focused build with secondary support, go Zen Archer or Unchained. Note that Unchained can also make a pretty strong and very mobile bad touch build, though it's more MAD unless you pick up Guided Hand for your unarmed strike. Crusader's Flurry is another feat to keep in mind to open up weapon choices, but note that the Sensei doesn't qualify and the feat may not qualify a weapon for Ascetic Style (monk weapon quality =/= monk weapon group – check with your GM). Panther Style is also worth considering if you're going to do a lot of running around the battlefield. Ecclesitheurge is a good choice on the cleric end since you won't wear armour anyway.


Cleric // Occultist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and 6-level casting, Wis Int Cha physical
In order from best to worst builds:
* Caster. Keep Wis and Int high, and maybe an above-average Cha for a bit of channelling. It's not a powerhouse of a gestalt caster but fairly versatile. Conjuration implement is a great tool for a summoning cleric (see Herald Caller), and also adds teleportation. Evocation gives you a bit more blasting and boosts spells like searing light or holy smite as well as any domain-based blasts you get your hands on. Transmutation and Divination are just plain good. Enchantment and Illusion have some nice stuff if you can keep Int-based DCs high. Abjuration is skippable. Necromancy has a handful of spells you won't get from the cleric and lets you dabble in minions, but overall it's also skippable unless you want the Mage's Panoply or an:
* Undead Master. For this one, prioritize Cha followed by Wis and Int. Necromancy implements are a no-brainer, though you'll want to cast animate dead as a cleric and use your occultist spells known elsewhere. Necromantic Servant and Soulbound Doll give you early-game minions and scouts. Necroccultist is a solid archetype here as it boosts your Necromancy DCs – useful since your Int won't be as high as you'd like – and gives you access to more necromancy spells from the wizard list. Alternatively, if you're hurting for stats or want more versatility and you can deal with losing armour, take Silksworn. This lets you rely on Cha for Mental Focus and only keep Int high enough to cast spells. Since this hurts occultist DCs you'll want to pick up spells like spectral hand, death ward, or sands of time with necromancy, and take support/utility schools like transmutation, divination, abjuration, illusion (mirror image, displacement) and conjuration (mage armor, teleportation). If you're hungry for stats but are OK with fewer focus powers and want your armour, substitute Relic Hunter Inquisitor for occultist. 
* Battle cleric. Stat requirements and not getting inherent full BAB are both major downsides here, but it's possible. I'd recommend Panoply Savant (Warrior) for the extra focus since your Int will be middling – you'll need all the help you can get keeping your Panoply implements topped up. For later implements I recommend Illusion and Conjuration since spells like Mirror Image and Dimension Door will be useful even without much focus – though Side Step and Shadow Beast may be worth a point or two. Also consider crusader cleric for the bonus feats – in particular note that Shield Focus qualifies you for Shield Brace at level 5 if you want a polearm with your Panoply. If your GM allows Relic Hunter to take Panoplies, take that instead.


Cleric // Oracle  (Elder Mythos Cultist)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, two full casting, Wis Cha (physical?)
Different casting stats, and same spell list twice, so no big improvement on versatility, though it is handy to be able to use oracle for your most frequently-cast spells and save cleric for more circumstantial things. With your high Cha, take advantage of channeling or Evangelist (you'll get armour and spontaneous casting back anyway).


Cleric // (Anti)Paladin  (Elder Mythos Cultist//Antipaladin)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will+, 2 skills, full and 4-level casting, physical Wis Cha
Stats will probably be stretched too thin for a reach build, but makes a fine combat build with versatile support spells and abilities. Take a skill points archetype for cleric (Roaming Exorcist, Herald Caller, or Cardinal), and note that the latter two don't lose armour or BAB after gestalting. Consider also Inquisitor//Paladin, which trades casting versatility for better skills, personal buffs, and slightly lower Wis requirements.

Cleric // Psychic
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 2 skills+, two full casting, Int Wis Cha
Good variety of healing, buffing, debuffing, and divination spells with both prepared and spontaneous options. Faith discipline is a little redundant, though still useful overall. Self-perfection is also worth considering if you're interested in Ecclesitheurge cleric for casting versatility. If your stats are generous or you take Elder Mythos Cultist you could even consider a Cha-based discipline. Reach build would be nice if you can manage the stats and feats.


Cleric // Ranger
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, full and 4-level casting, physical Wis Cha
Very well balanced across combat, skills, and spells. A reach build with the two-handed weapon style makes good use of this versatility, though a more combat focused build will also do fine and Inner Sea Combat has some neat deity-specific combat styles for the ranger. Thematically good for a deity of travel or wilderness, or for a hunter of undead or outsiders.


Cleric // Rogue
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 8 skills, full casting, physical Wis Cha
Not much weapon skill for gestalt – accuracy is particularly poor – and the rogue doesn't add much to a casting build. Try Cleric//Slayer.


Cleric // Skald
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 4 skills, full and 6-level casting, physical Wis Cha


Cleric // Slayer
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, full casting, physical Wis Int Cha
Another well balanced and versatile combination, leaning a bit more to melee combat thanks to studied target and sneak attack. Try to avoid class features using Int or Cha.


Cleric // Sorcerer (Empyreal or Elder Mythos Cultist)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 2 skills, two full casting, Wis Cha
Wide variety of spells and meets basic durability standards, though without armour you should be behaving more like a sorcerer than a cleric in terms of exposing yourself to danger. Empyreal and Elder Mythos Cultist are significant upgrades thanks to consolidating your casting stats. Ecclesitheurge is a good archetype for an unarmoured cleric, adding casting versatility. However, it's incompatible with Evangelist, the Cultist, and the skill-granting archetypes.


Cleric//Spiritualist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and 6-level casting, Wis Cha


Cleric // Summoner (Elder Mythos Cultist)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 2 skills, full and 6-level casting, Wis Cha
Versatile support spells, some debuffs & control, plus meat shield. Note that Herald Caller's Divine Heralds ability doesn't improve your summoner summoning, but the Augment Summons feat works just fine. Alternatively, Elder Mythos Cultist is extremely thematic and improves stat synergy. See also Oracle//Summoner for better stat synergy and skills, or Feyspeaker Druid//Summoner for better everything.


Cleric // Swashbuckler  (Elder Mythos Cultist)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, Dex Wis Cha
Pretty well-rounded combat/support character. Since your stat needs are minimal, you should be able to afford a higher starting Wis than most combat clerics, and you should be able to line up your key combat feats by level 5, both of which give you room to shift to a more casting-focused build at higher levels. Consider Evangelist since proficiency loss is irrelevant and performance synergizes well with your high Cha. See also Oracle//Swashbuckler.


Cleric // Warpriest 
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 2 skills, full and 6-level casting, physical Wis
Overlapping BAB, saves, spell lists. You can make a decent combat cleric, but it's not particularly powerful and definitely not versatile. Try Cleric//Inquisitor, or gestalt either Cleric or Warpriest with something full BAB.


Cleric // Witch
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 2 skills, two full casting, Wis Int


Cleric // Wizard 
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 2 skills+, two full casting, Int Wis
Two great spell lists, and more durable than a wizard. Strongly recommend Ecclesitheurge for added casting versatility at the cost of one die of channel energy.
