# Druid Combinations

Druid


A very versatile class. Spells include a good selection of control spells, buffs (especially long-duration defensive buffs like Barkskin and Resist Energy, good for your action economy), and utility. Wild shape offers mobility, scouting, pointy teeth, and sometimes reach. Natural Spell lets you cast spells from any class while Wild Shaping. Gestalting usually consists of doubling down on a “combat druid” build by picking a complementary full-BAB class, or on a “caster druid” build by selecting a full caster. However some hybrids also complement the class well, and many combinations work well as reach casters. The Feyspeaker archetype notably swaps your casting stat to Charisma, which makes a very good match with Cha-based casters.


There are a few ways to use Wild Shape, each of which relates differently to gestalt. The “tiger” is the easiest combat build and involves taking a form with lots of natural attacks and ideally pounce. It works well with most melee classes, but particularly those with large per-attack damage bonuses. The “T-rex” involves taking a big form with one powerful attack. It's usually less effective than the tiger but can work well with Vital Strike or other specific combos. The “Songbird of Doom” involves taking the form of something tiny and dextrous, getting Dex to hit and damage (usually Weapon Finesse + Agile Amulet of Mighty Fists) and attacking with large increases to accuracy and to AC and good damage. Works nicely with classes that encourage dex-based combat or improve reach, but sometimes considered “cheesy.” The “turtle” involves turning into a defensive form (usually not actually a turtle; small fliers and air elementals work well) and casting. Can overlap with the “Songbird” if you want to put in the investment to have a viable non-casting strategy.


If you want to stay humanoid in combat, you should choose an archetype that limits or eliminates Wild Shape in exchange for other powers. The standout options are Goliath Druid, Nature Fang, Skinshaper, and Supernaturalist. Goliath Druid is best for strength-focused combat builds, especially two-handers. Nature Fang and Skinshaper are more versatile and have better utility options. Supernaturalist is a neat option if your other class grants natural weapons that can’t be used with wild shape, or improves summoning. Note that the Supernaturalist’s summoning improvement does enhance celestial/fiendish animals, but not true outsiders. The Sunrider is a niche archetype that grants modest bonuses to allied mounts – it could be interesting if the entire party chooses to play mounted gestalts of one flavour or another.


The Animal Shaman archetypes with their standard-action summoning can work well if your second class enhances summoning. Lion and Saurian Shamans are generally the strongest.


Druid // Fighter
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full casting, physical Wis
Martial Versatility (Feral Combat Training) is a good deal since it lets you use unarmed strike feats to boost all of your natural attacks. Notable fighter archetypes include the Unarmed Fighter (which works well with the FCT trick) and Lore Warden (for +2 skill points and maneuver skills). Nature Fang also works fine as a druid archetype if you want to feel more like a fighter with nature magic than an extra-fighty druid – in which case, consider a reach build.


Druid // Gunslinger (Nature Fang, Skinshaper)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, Dex Wis
Decidedly weird flavour-wise, and despite promising basics you'll have a bit of trouble combining your gun with Wild Shape. Nature Fang and Skinshaper are significant improvements on that front, granting more synergistic combat bonuses on top of a good range of magical backup and utility. I prefer Nature Fang since it gets studied target in the low levels before Gunslinger pulls together, and slayer talents provide a flexible selection of feats (archery or crossbow style are useful for guns; finesse rogue talent gives you a melee backup) and utility.


Druid // Hunter
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills, full and 6-level casting, physical Wis
A fairly seamless gestalt that doesn't add many options, but does let you do more at once via more spell slots and your pet. Without full BAB or significant buffs, works best as a casting or reach build. The latter works well for supporting your pet through teamwork feats – the former should consider Divine Hunter. Make sure to use archetypes to trade out overlapping features (nature training, wild empathy, woodland stride). Snapping Flank? Consider Druid//Inqusitor (Sacred Huntmaster) since you'll expand your skill and spell options more.


Druid // Inquisitor
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 6 skills+, full and 6-level casting, physical Wis
Versatile set of skills and spells. A little behind on martial skills, but you can get there through vigorous buffing. Reach builds and tiger wild shape also help by giving you multiple attacks at full BAB instead of relying on your weaker iterative sequence. In a small party, try Sacred Huntmaster for a stronger pet, and use your nature bond for a domain. Alternatively, Supernaturalist // Monster Tactician gives you a pack of enhanced summons. Either way, check out Snapping Flank; either wild shape or totem transformation should qualify you for the bite.


Druid // Investigator
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills+, full and 6-level “casting,” physical Wis Int
Great variety of buffs (including most major healing), control, and utility. A few issues: it's a bit MAD, needs quite a lot of buffing to function well in combat, can't use extracts in Wild Shape, and doesn't really come together until level 4 when Wild Shape and Studied Combat both come online. Different builds address some of these issues. Goliath Druid, Nature Fang, and Skinshaper all fix the extract use issue. Nature Fang gives the build an early-level combat boost, but at higher levels the extra actions for buffing are clumsier than long-duration shapechanging. Dex builds help reduce MAD but take even longer to come online thanks to the feat requirements to get Dex to damage. Personally I like Skinshaper because the “Master of Disguise” thing is super thematic for an investigator, but follow your own tastes. Urushil is also thematic. Should I be encouraging combat at all?


Druid // Kineticist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, physical Wis
Moderate attack potential in melee and at range and lots of magical options for support, control, and debuffing. You can use various appendages to direct blasts, so they should be usable in wild shape, and kinetic fist is reasonably useful with natural weapons (I'd take kinetic blade for early levels and then swap it out at level 5). Being able to turn into an elemental is lots of fun and thematic, but note that the size bonuses to stats don't stack with elemental overflow. You can still get decent bonuses if you assign the overflow bonuses to different stats from your wild shape – for example turning into a medium air elemental  with +4 Dex at level 8 and assigning a +2 overflow bonus to Con gives you +2 Dex or Con compared to either alone. And since wild shape works when you don't have burn and provides natural armour, scouting utility, and other special qualities it's still a good ability to have. However if you want something simpler, consider taking Nature Fang or Skinshaper and sticking with kinetic blade, or taking Elemental Ally for a thematic pet (more useful in a small party).


I made a Druid/Geokineticist. Once they can change into elementals they can do some interesting things with their powers (there's something about a burrowing air elemental or a flying water elemental that amuses me). 


Druid // Magus
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and 6-level casting, physical Int Wis
Natural attacks aren't the best choices for the magus, and the combo is both MAD and low on BAB. There is potentially some synergy with Green Scourge’s improved Shillelagh and the Staff Magus, though Shillelagh’s inability to enhance a magic quarterstaff is a pain. You’ll want to enhance your staff with Shillelagh right before combat if possible, and in the first round with spell combat otherwise. Arcane Pool gives you a better bonus but probably needs to be applied after Shillelagh or else it will make your staff magic, and thus a non-valid target. Consider stacking Green Scourge with Skinshaper for this build for humanoid shapeshifting bonuses. Alternative weapon-based builds with Skinshaper or Nature Fang?


Druid // Medium
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and partial casting, Wis Cha physical?
The champion's damage bonuses are quite nice for natural weapons. The hierophant's nature code taboo is a freebie for you since you already need to follow it. Consider the kami medium for thematics, though it may be difficult to access the champion.


Druid // Mesmerist (Feyspeaker)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills, full and 6-level casting, Wis Cha
Control the entire battlefield: your allies, your enemies, and the terrain itself. Feyspeaker is thematic, gives you a bit more Cha synergy (possibly allowing a melee backup option if your stats are good), and adds some extra enchantment & illusion spells to your arsenal.


Druid // Monk (Unchained)
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, physical Wis
Really strong defenses with Wisdom synergy. Unchained Monk significantly improves your combat ability and just costs you a few monk immunities (including poison immunity, which you probably get as a druid anyway). Magic Fang can enhance your Unarmed Strike. Shillelagh and quarterstaff flurry is great for lower levels, though the inability to enhance a magic staff means it doesn’t retain value well at higher levels - Green Scourge mitigates this by giving you a better-scaling bonus, but the short duration is inconvenient. Recommended builds:
* For a wild shape build, take Feral Combat Training with a natural weapon that typically does a lot of damage or has rider effects on it (generally bite). You can now use flurry and UAS feats with that bite. Note: tigers get 2d6 damage on a bite, plus pounce, so you can cross the map and then flurry for 2d6+Str from level 6. The monk's AC bonus also means that you have a solid AC without expensive Wild Armour – and note that some interpretations of the Wild property rule it doesn't encumber you, letting you wear wild armour on top of your monk's bonus. Note also that the Amulet of Mighty Fists improves your Unarmed Strike as well as your natural attacks in the event you're caught without Wild Shape. 
* For grappling specifically, try Tetori. Kraken Caller is also thematic, but mechanically a bit of a draw. Kraken Style is both thematic and mechanically useful.
* For melee without Wild Shape, Goliath is your best bet. On top of the excellent strength bonus, your increased size does wonders for UAS damage dice and combat maneuvers. 
* For ranged, Zen Archer + Nature Fang or Skinshaper.
* For a support/caster build, take Sensei instead of Unchained. The druid half is flexible, but note that you do not get Wis to hit with natural attacks.
* Cha build option: Feyspeaker//Unchained Scaled Fist. Flavour's a bit odd but the mechanics line up well. Combat capability is a little lower than the above options thanks to weaker wild shape and the opportunity cost of not taking other archetypes, but it's not much of a drop and in some games the extra social utility will be worth it.


Druid // Oracle (Feyspeaker)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 6/4 skills, two full casting, Cha Wis (physical)?
Normally not a bad combination for a caster, with two diverse spell lists and an array of other features for mobility, defense, and utility. Capable of entering combat as a secondary role by making use of a reach or natural weapons build (Lunar is good for the latter). Feyspeaker is a massive upgrade with casting stat synergy and some extra skills and spells at the modest cost of two levels of wild shape.


Druid // (Anti)Paladin (Feyspeaker) (** to *****, see below)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will+, 6/4 skills, full and 4-level casting, physical Cha Wis
Using normal alignment restrictions, this is only possible if you take Grey Paladin, and it's not really worth that (**). Even if you take it with a normal paladin (***), the MAD is a pain. Feyspeaker archetype turns this around. Charisma-based casting, extra skills, all the paladin's melee power plus a solid full casting progression and the ability to wear armour. Fantastic (*****) if you can snag it without Grey Paladin, and very good (****) even if you do need to give up Divine Grace and paladin immunities.


Druid // Psychic
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, two full casting, Int Wis
With the druid covering the physical and the psychic concentrating on the mental, you have a very diverse set of magical effects to choose from. Neat and unusual option for a spy between shapeshifting, mindreading, and enchantment. Recommend Self-perfection discipline since it gives you Wis to AC when unarmoured/ in wild shape, and the other abilities aren't bad. Pure Body is especially handy if you have a druid archetype that trades in Venom Immunity.


Druid // Ranger (Goliath, Skinshaper)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, full casting, physical Wis
This looks obvious for a nature warrior, but you end up with more overlap than synergy. Natural weapon style feats are disappointing, most of your ranger spells are also druid spells, and unless you juggle archetypes you duplicate woodland stride. Works better as a weapons build or if you can make a confident favoured enemy choice, but usually I would recommend Druid//Slayer instead.


Druid // Rogue (Green Scourge)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 8 skills, full casting, physical Wis
With wild shape and rogue skills you make a great scout, and you should take the Scout archetype because sneak attacking when you pounce is your main combat perk for this combo compared to Druid//Slayer. Flame Blade is a nice way to deliver sneak attacks (and debilitating injuries) against touch AC, and Green Scourge gives you the ability to cast it spontaneously, add throwing and returning, and even change the energy type to electricity or cold. Weapon Finesse applies, though not Dex to damage. The duration is short, and energy immunity and SR will be problematic in the long run, so I’d still recommend carrying a mundane weapon. Bonus: Green Scourge’s scentless ability is an upgrade on trackless step for someone who expects to do a lot of sneaking around.

Druid // Slayer
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, full casting, physical Wis Int
Excellent scout, assassin, or mauler. One of the more versatile gestalts, with lots of utility and full casting but still outstanding in combat with full BAB plus self-buffs (both spells and studied target). TWF is handy for the Goliath druid, and archery works for a Skinshaper – otherwise you'll only want one or two natural weapons compatible feats. 


Druid // Sorcerer (Empyreal or Feyspeaker)
Basics: Poor/Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 6/4 skills, two full casting, Wis Cha
Most bloodlines and archetypes make this a moderately good combination: a caster with a good variety of spells and good durability but two casting stats. Wild Shape is useful defensively and for utility, but you probably won't have the stats for combat effectiveness. Sylvan bloodline is thematic and lets you pick up a domain without losing your companion. Orc is a neat option for a combat/blasting build – druids have their share of damaging spells – though the natural armour and size increase don't stack with Wild Shape, so that might be best if you're planning on taking Nature Fang. Supernaturalist Druid with the Abyssal Bloodline makes an interesting natural weapons/summoning build, though stats are tight. Empyreal turns you into an entirely Wis-based caster and is your best bet for getting the stats for a combat capable character - if your GM lets you crossblood it with Orc or Abyssal all the better. Alternatively, Feyspeaker makes you Cha-based for a really excellent casting build.


Druid//Spiritualist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and partial casting, Wis (physical?)


Druid // Summoner (Feyspeaker)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 6/4 skills, full and 6-level casting, Wis Cha (physical?)
Broad spellcasting options, mostly support and control, and durable enough. Feyspeaker hugely recommended due to better stat synergy, skills, and the fact that the loss of BAB and armour is irrelevant after factoring in the gestalt. Feyspeaker also works well with Synthesist, since you'll be using the synth suit instead of wild shape.


Druid // Swashbuckler ** (Nature Fang, Skinshaper, Feyspeaker***)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6/4 skills, full casting, Dex Wis Cha
Precise Strike doesn't work with natural attacks, so this one needs a weapons combat build to pay off. With a Dex-based combat style that generally means Nature Fang or Skinshaper, though if your stats aren't great consider Feyspeaker instead to consolidate casting and panache under your Cha score. While Feyspeaker doesn't get a combat buff like Nature Fang or Skinshaper, you do get extra skills, spells, and keep some wild shape for scouting/utility purposes. Consider also Nature Oracle//Swashbuckler.


Druid // Warpriest
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and 6-level casting, physical Wis
Very buff-dependent to reach combat strength, but does have good action economy and a nice variety of spells (including all necessary healing magic). Size increases give good value with sacred weapon damage dice. Avoid Nature Fang as it takes too many actions to buff. Wild Shape vs Goliath Druid?


Druid // Witch (Feyspeaker//Seducer)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, two full casting, Wis Int
Druid//Wizard provides a more diverse casting list.


Druid // Wizard
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, two full casting, Wis Int
“The Merlin” - a character who derives great magical power from their deep understanding of the natural world and its laws. Excellent versatile spell list and reasonably good defenses. Use Wild Shape defensively or for scouting. Invest in Dex and Weapon Finesse and you could even use a weapon halfway competently.
