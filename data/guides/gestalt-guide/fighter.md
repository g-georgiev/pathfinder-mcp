# Fighter Combinations

Fighter


The fighter has the potential to excel in a number of styles of combat. Unfortunately, it also has several major weaknesses: it lacks out-of-combat utility, resistance to magic, and the ability to improve performance in the toughest fights. The best fighter gestalts patch these weaknesses while complementing one or more fighter combat styles. The most straightforward choices are part-casters and divine full casters which provide improved saves, skills, and buffs. These casters also benefit from the solid combat base and generous bonus feats of the fighter. Combining fighter with other martial classes tends to leave you with an over-specialized character, but sometimes you can stack potent combat abilities while still mitigating the downsides.


Fighter typically gestalts less well with full arcane casters due to arcane spell failure issues and conflicting action economy. This can be mitigated in a few ways. A ranged build minimizes the danger associated with little or no armour, and fighters make effective archers. The Arcane Armour training feat (available at level 3) lets you cast in a Mithral Shirt with no spell failure at the cost of a swift action, while Arcane Armor Mastery (level 7) lets you cast in a Mithral Breastplate (or if you’re using it from 3.5, a Celestial Plate). The Child of Acavna and Amaznen gives improved armour resistance but losing weapon training is probably not worth it. A reach build improves action economy as you can use your standard action to cast spells while making attacks of opportunity. And of course you might as well make use of Arcane Strike.


Advanced Weapon Training abilities of interest:
* Armoured Confidence - a nice intimidate bonus for builds that give you fun things to do with intimidate.
* Armed Bravery and Fighter’s Reflexes - handy for covering weak saves
* Trained initiative - handy for caster fighters or sneak attacking fighters
* Warrior Spirit - a versatile weapon buff, particularly useful for fighter//martial combinations.


Note: guide has not been systematically updated to account for Advanced Armour Mastery.


For archetypes:
* If your second class encourages the use of light or no armour, look at Dawnflower Dervish, Dragonheir Scion, Mutation Warrior, Skirmisher and Lore Warden. Skirmisher also brings you up to 4 skills/level, while Lore Warden adds a bonus 2 skill ranks to be spent on any Int-based skills. That said, if you decide to keep armour mastery, consider the advanced armour mastery abilities Master Armourer and Armoured Master with the Sprightly Armour feat.
* Eldritch Guardian gives you a potentially combat-capable familiar at low cost, useful for flanking buddies or teamwork feats. 
* Drill sergeant gives you tactician at the cost of bravery and advanced weapon training options.
* Roughrider gives a number of bonuses for fighters planning on doing mounted combat with a pet from another class. Losing advanced armor and weapon training hurts, but note your attack and damage bonuses from Mounted Mettle also apply to your mount. 
* Trench fighter gives you dex to damage with firearms, so it’s nice to pair with other firearm-focused archetypes that don’t give that bonus. 
* For a theme game, the Blackjack fighter is an interesting archetype for everyone to have, as it gives you access to a neat tactics ability and you can still customize your combat style using weapon training and some of your bonus feats. 
* Other archetypes generally focus on improving a specific fighting style such as archery or spear & shield (phalanx fighter) - refer to fighter guides for comments on the effectiveness of these archetypes for the intended purposes. 


Fighter // Gunslinger
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, no casting, Dex Wis
Bonus feats help a ranged combat style come together more quickly and give you room in your build for a few extras. Really low on utility and self-buffing. Trench Fighter


Fighter // Hunter
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 6 skills, 6-level casting, physical Wis
At the very least, the pet is a solid flanking partner or mount. If you want your pet to offer its own offense, Roughrider together with Hunter buffs should do the job.


Fighter // Inquisitor
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, physical Wis
Good combat base with enough feats to do interesting things, ability to self-buff, and good utility. 


Fighter // Investigator
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level “casting”, physical Int
Lots of in combat and out of combat skill with no real weak points and quite a lot of staying power – extracts and inspiration are limited use but most of your abilities aren't. Studied Combat and Weapon Training provide big static attack and damage bonuses – try Combat Expertise to turn some of that into defense. Don't forget the Mutagen discovery as an Investigator talent.


Fighter // Kineticist
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, pseudo-casting, Dex Con
The kinetic fighting style doesn't have much use for bonus combat feats, and weapon training doesn't work with blasts. The lack of Will save and low skills are also weak points, though Kineticist does at least potentially add some utility and you should be able to fit Iron Will into your build. There’s a couple of ways to build this so it’s not terrible, but for something easier to work with, consider Fighter//Druid or Kineticist//Paladin or Slayer.
* Use your fighter abilities to focus on a melee weapon, and use blasts as a ranged/AoE option. The damage bonus from Energize Weapon is small, but it’s free after level 5 and does let you add substance infusion debuffs to your weapon. My preference would be to go this route with Aerokinesis, since Wings of Air gives you great mobility and electric blast lets you tag people with Magnetic Infusion at level 5. Consider adding the Mutation Warrior fighter archetype for a Con/Dex mutagen.
* Armor Master Fighter and Kinetic Knight is an interesting combination for a tanky, melee-locked elemental knight. If you go this route I’d suggest an energy blast focused build as your accuracy will likely lag behind other gestalts, and Armour Master’s Armoured Defense makes the Geokineticist’s defense less interesting. 
* Weapon Master Fighter gets to choose a specific weapon instead of a weapon group, which means you can get a weapon training-like bonus for blasts. Unfortunately you replace Bravery with the useless Weapon Guard ability, Mirror Move probably won’t come up much (how often will you be attacked with a blast?) and my reading says Unstoppable Strike doesn’t work with a blast either since it’s a special standard action. That said, Critical Specialist should boost the DCs of any blast debuffs on a crit. This is probably your best route with a Geokineticist, though it would also work with other elements.

Fighter//Medium
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 4 skills, partial casting, physical Cha
The champion and guardian give you a good boost to the fighter's primary combat role, and the others add secondary roles. But since the fighter is fairly lacklustre, this combination is particularly dependent on your ability to predict and access the spirit you need – Champion and Guardian don't add much aside from their combat boosts.


Fighter // Mesmerist
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, 6-level casting, physical Cha
A more subtle way to add magic to your fighter. Aim to use spells out of combat, with your in-combat magic focused on your debuffing stare and on using tricks to control the battlefield. 


Fighter // Monk
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves/Fort and Ref, 4 skills, no casting
* Unarmed fighter archetype has some nice benefits, though losing advanced weapon training means this isn’t a no-brainer.
* fighter//monk; kung-fu master. A Fighter/Zen Archer monk would be the unrivaled king of ranged combat, with a lot of scouting skills and high mobility.


Fighter // Occultist
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 4 skills, 6-level casting, physical Int
Good combat base, lots of buffing and utility, debuffing or control options if you can get your Int high enough for good DCs, no problems with armour or shields. Consider Haunt Collector.


Fighter // Oracle
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full casting, physical Cha
Strong in combat, self-buffing and support through the Oracle, and plenty of feats for a reach build if you desire. I recommend a mystery that has good non-combat options, such as Ancestor, Lunar, Stone, or Wind.


Fighter // (Anti)Paladin
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will+, 2 skills, casting
Viking gets Rage Powers for Lesser Celestial Totem


Fighter // Psychic
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 2 skills+, full casting, physical Int and Wis or Cha
Thanks to a lack of somatic components, this makes a fairly effective warrior mage which can handle a range of combat styles (in contrast to the much more limited Magus). Action economy is your biggest issue. This can be addressed by some combination of a reach weapon style in order to sneak in extra attacks on casting turns, making use of the psychic's handful of swift/immediate action spells, or pre-buffing. Utility is also a strong consideration for spells and discipline since the Fighter largely lacks it. Lore Warden's abilities get a boost from the Knowledge bonus of – surprise – the Rebirth discipline.


Fighter // Ranger
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 6 skills, 4-level casting, physical Wis
Fighter already has a good number of feats, so the reason to take Ranger would be some combination of (1) you know you'll be fighting a lot of a particular enemy type (2) you want to skip specific prerequisites, for example to make a Str-based TWF character or (3) you have a lowish point buy. Otherwise, you're better off with a Hunter or Inquisitor. 


Fighter // Rogue
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 8 skills, casting
The fighter provides the accuracy and durability the rogue lacks, and the rogue brings out of combat utility. Unfortunately the combination still has issues with buffing and with magic resistance. TWF build is probably your best bet – the Two-Weapon Fighter archetype has some options that make you less reliant on full attacks, and you have enough feats to cover the basic line as well as Two-Weapon Feint if you can't rely on flanking. Alternatively, Lore Warden brings you up to 10 skill ranks per level and adds minor combat bonuses including Combat Expertise - it’s a good choice if you want to use combat maneuvers. Consider Sneaking Precision, which gives you a fairly reliable debuff (or extra damage if you take Sneaking Critical). Remember you qualify for Critical Mastery, so if you want you can add two conditions any time you crit, and a third when you sneak attack twice in a turn.

Fighter // Sorcerer (Psychic Bloodline) ** to ****
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 2 skills, full casting, physical Cha
Strongly recommend psychic bloodline, since this gives you a great spell list (better than the Psychic) that you can use while in armour, and Bravery helps against fear effects that would mess with your emotion components. Otherwise, not one of the better fighting//arcane combos, even with a lightly armoured fighter archetype. If you’re keen, look at your melee bloodlines and archetypes that trade away armour training for something with better value to you. Since you have few swift actions, Arcane Strike is a good feat. Aside from armour, skills are the big pain point, though at least you get utility from spells.

Fighter // Warpriest
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 2 skills, 6-level casting, physical Wis
Your utility is pretty poor, but the warpriest adds support and much needed self-buffing ability to the fighter, with good action economy. Consider shield bashing – you certainly have the feats for it.


Fighter // Witch (Scarred Witch Doctor)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 2 skills, full casting, physical Int
Tactician is an interesting archetype, as it gives you an extra 2 skills/level and gives you an initiative bonus, the cavalier’s tactician, and two minor intelligence-based support abilities, though delaying weapon training isn’t great.


Fighter // Wizard
Basics:  Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will, 2 skills, full casting, physical Int
Tactician is an interesting archetype, as it gives you an extra 2 skills/level and gives you an initiative bonus, the cavalier’s tactician, and two minor intelligence-based support abilities, though delaying weapon training isn’t great. Look for schools that have martially oriented powers, such as Conjuration (Teleportion) Wizard - adding Dimensional Agility gives a lot of mobility to a reach build.
