# Gunslinger Combinations

Gunslinger


The gunslinger is, unsurprisingly, very focused on its gun. It works best with classes that can improve its ranged combat through martial or magical abilities; good Will saves, skills, and a backup option such as switch-hitting or magic are a plus. It also works passably for a switch-hitter when paired with a melee focus, or as a ranged backup for a caster, but this is usually not ideal because the gunslinger's intense focus doesn't provide much to someone who wants ranged combat to be a secondary option. Keep in mind that the gun does need 3-5 feats to be moderately functional, on top of the gunslinger's class features. The only archetype with notable gestalt implications is Mysterious Stranger, which works best when paired with a Cha-based full caster who won't  be using their gun every round, but will have lots of panache and a nice damage bonus for Focused Aim.


Gunslinger // Hunter
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 6 skills, 6-level casting, Dex Wis
Strong offence with some buffing and utility. Could use stronger buffs and a good will save - consider Sacred Huntmaster Inquisitor.


Gunslinger // Inquisitor
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, Dex Wis
Bane is highly effective when paired with a large number of ranged attacks, and the Destruction judgment adds more damage (you don't need the to-hit from Justice). Between Cunning Initiative and Gunslinger's Initiative you're a super fast draw. Plus good utility.


Gunslinger // Investigator
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, Dex Wis Int
Ranged Study is practically required as a 5th or 7h level feat so you can use Studied Combat/Strike with your gun, which is unfortunate since the gunslinger already needs so many feats. However, once you get your feats in a line you do have excellent ranged damage, the potential to enter melee, good buffs and great utility. 


Gunslinger // Kineticist
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, pseudo-casting, Con Dex Wis
Guns and blasts don't play together without a conductive gun, and even then you can only use energy blasts and won't be able to use gather energy. The kinetic blade melee option, defenses, and utility are still useful, but not enough to make this exciting – especially since the best energy blast for a conductive gun (blue flame, pure pyrokineticist) comes with the worst defense and utility abilities. 


Gunslinger // Magus (Eldritch Archer)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, 6-level casting, physical Wis Int
Base magus is melee focused, so it doesn't get much out of the gun that it wouldn't get from any other full-BAB class. Eldritch Archer, despite the name, works just fine with guns. The combo is a bit MAD but has good defenses, high damage potential, and moderate versatility depending on spell selection.


Gunslinger // Medium
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, partial casting, Dex Wis Cha


Gunslinger // Mesmerist
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, 6-level casting, Dex Cha Wis
Good damage, debuffing, control, and utility. You can try Mysterious Stranger if your stats are stingy.


Gunslinger // Monk
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves/Fort and Ref, 4 skills, no casting, Dex Wis
Great switch-hitter with just Dex and Wis. You'll have lots of grit and ki and your monk and gunslinger AC bonuses stack. Grab weapon finesse and an agile amulet of mighty fists for your unarmed strikes, or if that's too much investment take Sensei for Wis to hit with unarmed strikes. The former build gives you more freedom to take fun archetypes, but the latter adds support and a few skill bonuses to helps out with your otherwise mediocre utility. Far Strike and Zen Archer archetypes give you extra ranged combat feats for more specialization, but most of their abilities are limited to thrown weapons and bows respectively so overall it's a bad tradeoff for versatility. Unchained is lacklustre here – you already have full BAB and don't need mobility as much, but lack a good Will save. Sohei's weapon choices don't match up well. Kata Master is an interesting option since your panache and grit pools would merge, giving you a flexible set of tricks.


Gunslinger // Occultist
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, 6-level casting, Dex Int Wis
Get yourself a +2 dex item and magic gun with the transmutation implement. Then get yourself something fun. Battle Host with a panoply bond gun is tempting because you can ignore the consequence of a single misfire – but a second one will still blow up the weapon in your face and then you're screwed, so think carefully.


Gunslinger // Oracle
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, Dex Cha Wis
Gunslinger//Cleric has better stat synergy, but this is still a serviceable combination of weapon skill, self-buffs, and support magic. If you want to rely more heavily on casting, Mysterious Stranger puts the focus on your Cha.


Gunslinger // (Anti)Paladin
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves+, 4 skills, 4-level casting, Dex Wis Cha
Fantastic damage potential and survivability, decent support options. Divine Hunter gives you useful ranged abilities. 


Gunslinger // Psychic
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, Dex Int Wis
Not a whole lot of synergy between the two classes, but fairly versatile with the capacity for ranged damage, buffs, debuffs, and utility casting. It's your choice whether you want to prioritize casting with a gun as a backup option or prioritize your gun with casting for utility, pre-buffing, and the occasional swift or immediate effect.


Gunslinger // Ranger
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 6 skills, 4-level casting, Dex Wis
Less versatile: good utility, but in combat you do guns. Maybe a bit of melee if you pick up Weapon Finesse and a Dex to damage feat. Archery and crossbow style both have useful feats for you, depending on whether you want Point Blank Master or not. Consider using your animal companion as a mount.


Gunslinger // Rogue
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 8 skills, no casting, Dex Wis
Sneak attack is hard at range, and you lack self-buffing. Try Gunslinger//Investigator.


Gunslinger // Skald
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, 6-level casting, Dex Wis Cha


Gunslinger // Slayer
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 6 skills, no casting, Dex Wis Int
Studied Target is a nice reliable buff for your gun, and archery and crossbow style both have useful feats for you, depending on whether you want Point Blank Master or not. Sneak attack is a little harder to pull off, but nice if you get the jump on your foe or if you want to pull a melee weapon as backup (the finesse rogue talent helps). Stygian Slayer's invisibility helps, too, and you'll want light armour anyway.


Gunslinger // Sorcerer (Mysterious Stranger)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, Dex Cha Wis
If you're planning on significant in-combat casting (and why not?) Mysterious Stranger helps you streamline your stats and gives you a nice low-level damage bonus when your casting is lacking and you haven't lined up your feats. See also: Gunslinger//Wizard (spellslinger)


Gunslinger//Spiritualist
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, casting


Gunslinger // Summoner
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, 6-level casting, Dex Wis Cha
Gun, meat shield, a little magical support. Skip Synth.


Gunslinger // Swashbuckler
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, no casting, Dex Wis Cha
Seems obvious for a sword-and-pistol character, but the gun class and sword class don't provide buffs that help each others' weapons. Daring Champion Cavalier (with Gunslinger) and Savage Technologist Barbarian (with Swashbuckler) both provide buffs to help the sword and the pistol more evenly. Other suggestions?


Gunslinger // Warpriest
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, 6-level casting, Dex Wis
Extra feats from both classes help you cover the ranged basics, plus you have good support and self-buffing. Sacred weapon enhancement is a pretty good deal for a gun once you get rapid shot. The warpriest's heavy armour proficiency is a waste. I personally prefer Gunslinger//Inquisitor for a bit more utility but this is a very strong option.


Gunslinger // Witch (Scarred Witch Doctor)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, casting


Gunslinger // Wizard
Basics:  Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, Dex Int Wis
You can't shoot spells and bullets at the same time, and your features don't do a lot to reinforce each other. Still, at absolute minimum you're a wizard with good durability, and there will probably be times when it will be more efficient to use your gun than your spells. Spellslinger deserves some special mention here. You give up a lot – arcane bond, school specialization, cantrips, and an extra two opposition schools for a total of four – so this is not necessarily the best choice. However, you do get the ability to significantly enhance the DCs or ranged touch spells, rays, and cones, and to sacrifice spell slots to enhance your gun. Consider also Gunslinger//Eldritch Archer Magus, possibly with a one-level dip in Spellslinger.
