# Hunter Combinations

Hunter


Hunter is a real pet class. It doesn't just have a pet, it's designed around the pet, with most of its features improving the pet in some way. That means that in a small party the pet can easily fill the melee role while you focus on casting or ranged damage, and even in a high-power game it's possible to build and buff the pet to contribute meaningfully if you use teamwork feats wisely.


The melee build is perhaps the most potent, but also the least versatile, as both the hunter and the companion fill the same role. This makes it less good for a small party, but very nice in a high power game. Flanking feats such as Outflank and Precise Strike add significant boosts to both the hunter and companion, especially to builds with a large number of attacks. Pack Flanking allows you to “flank” when adjacent to your companion, which allows for mounted builds. Since you'll generally want to pair Hunter with a martial class, it's likely you'll end up missing a good Will save; the Hunter's above-average Wisdom helps but doesn't eliminate this weakness.


Ranged builds are slightly more versatile than melee ones, but the focus of both pet and master is still on damage. I'd only suggest this build if you really want an archer, but your group needs a frontliner. In this situation, make sure you prioritize your pet's defences since it'll take a lot of hits. You'll be looking to build like a ranged hunter, but select a second class with a good set of archery features.


Caster builds are highly versatile. The Hunter provides a competent frontliner in the pet, a baseline of martial competence if you don't want to cast, and a starter set of spells. The second class should provide the primary casting – ideally Wis-based and with the ability to wear armour. Teamwork feats will be significantly less useful to both you and your companion; consider trading them for a domain and the celestial/fiendish template using the Divine Hunter archetype.


The pet-free option, Feral Hunter, is quite similar to a Druid but with better skills, partial casting, a Ref save instead of a Will save, and an animal focus that's slightly more versatile than a free stat belt. The value of this trade depends on the other half of the gestalt as well as the level. The Feral Hunter is best at low levels where its skills and early-access Ranger spells pull a lot of weight, while the Druid becomes more powerful at high levels with more potent casting (especially 7th-9th level spells) and advanced elemental forms.


Generally the Hunter is designed for wilderness settings. If you're playing in an urban game, try the Roof Runner or Courtly Hunter archetypes, or even Sacred Huntmaster Inquisitor.


Hunter // Inquisitor
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, physical Wis
This is a good small-party option, with a wide range of spells and skills, a strong pet for the melee role, and the potential to buff yourself for combat. Preacher, Cloaked Wolf, or Divine Hunter swap out surplus teamwork feats.  However, your buffing will have trouble keeping up in a high-power gestalt situation. Consider Druid//Sacred Huntmaster for an equally strong pet but with stronger casting.

Hunter // Monk
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves/Fort and Ref, 6 skills, 6-level casting, physical Wis
Good secondary roles in scouting & support casting. A couple of good ways to build your primary role:
   * Unchained Monk gives you the best overall combat skills but you want a very high Wis since you lose a good will save in exchange for Full BAB. Works fairly well as a pet-free version with Feral Hunter: see Druid//Monk for Wild Shape strategy. You can take Monastic Mount as a ki power, though mounted combat wastes much of the UnMonk's innate maneuverability. Instead consider:
   * Sohei, to take mounted combat feats as bonus feats and can flurry with a lance (use Mounted Skirmisher!) It also tolerates a modest Wis since it has a good base Will save and can wear light armour, so feel free to start with a Wis 12. You'll probably still want to ditch the armour as your class bonus improves and magic armour bonuses become reliable. Unmounted, this is a good reach build with a polearm of your choice – Paired Opportunists lets you give your pet extra AoO, too. Feral Hunter doesn't mesh well with weapons. You can also flurry with a bow as a late game second option, but if you want to specialize in archery take:
   * Zen Archer. After level 3 able to grant flanking bonuses to its hunter companion while still attacking with a bow (don't forget Gravity Bow). Bad match with Feral Hunter since you can't use a bow when wild shaping and the animal focus benefits aren't great for the Zen Archer. You can easily get Wis high enough to take advantage of control spells on the druid list, though for a casting focus consider:
   * Sensei for a support/control build. Focus on Wis, send your companion into melee, and use Wis to hit and maneuver mastery to trip, disarm, etc when not casting. Plays a bit like a maneuver bard but nature focused, more SAD, and with a big nasty pet attached. Surprisingly not bad with Feral Hunter since inspire courage works well with your improved summoning and you can snag extra maneuvers (or scouting utility) from wild shape. Animal Focus (tiger) gives you the best summons and a good stat benefit. However, Druid//Sensei is a better pet-free option.

Hunter // Rogue
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Ref, 8 skills, 6-level casting, physical Wis
Despite only getting medium BAB and HD, you can pull off a very effective combat build by flanking with your companion. Outflank helps with accuracy. Hunter 540 provides an example progression (note use of Pack Flanking to expand flanking options to include companions that are adjacent or sharing space). Consider Hunter//Slayer if you want to hedge your bets a bit and be slightly less reliant on having your pet in position.

Hunter // Sorcerer (Empyreal)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills, full and 6-level casting, Wis (physical, Cha)
Empyreal is great. If you focus on support or no-save spells you can make a reach build, or pump Wis for superior casting. Either way, versatile spell list with good durability and a furry buddy watching your back in melee. Works OK with Feral Hunter.
