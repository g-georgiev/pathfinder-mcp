# Inquisitor Combinations

Inquisitor


The Inquisitor's big strengths are self-buffing and utility (particularly social, investigative, and tracking). It works very well with most martial classes. Their Bane ability is particularly effective for archers and other combat styles involving making a large number of attacks with a single weapon.
Inquisitor can be gestalted effectively with wis-based full casters. Int-based full casters can also work with the Living Grimoire archetype, though this reduces your overall weapons combat ability and pushes you into using a light mace (albeit one with scaling damage dice). 


Most archetypes are minor thematic variations, but some offer significant benefits for certain types of builds. Sacred Huntmaster and Monster Tactician are good choices for casting builds in a small party. Preacher is good if you can't find useful teamwork feats. Teamwork feats tend to be melee-slanted, but ranged characters should look at Escape Route, Friendly Fire Maneuvers, Lookout, Swap Places, Target of Opportunity, and Volley Fire. Alternatively, if you’re building an archer and feel like being able to really ruin a spellcaster’s day, take Spellbreaker combined with the Spellkiller Inquisition, which adds a +6 bonus to concentration checks to cast within your threatened area and an extra +2 if you hit them with a ranged attack. Sanctified Slayer and Relic Hunter are good if you anticipate a lot of combats in an adventuring day. Sanctified Slayer adds a straightforward swift-action combat boost. 


Relic Hunter is a bit more complicated, offering a powerful but limited selection of utility and/or martial powers at the cost of reducing the scope of your spell list. It's a great archetype for a casting build, which get more benefit from implement powers than the typical inquisitor buffs, and can cover some of the casting basics with their other spell list. Martial builds have more to lose, but should also consider the Relic Hunter if the overall build needs more utility and already has a lot of swift actions. See Occultist for notes on implement focus powers, though keep in mind that with fewer focus powers you should be making choices based more on resonant powers and spell list, which affects choices as follows:
   * Similar to the occultist:
   * Abjuration - a smattering of core defensive spells. Resonant and base powers are pretty good.
   * Divination - an array of moderately useful utility spells. Resonant and base powers are great, focus powers are worthwhile – top choice for the Relic Hunter.
   * Better for the Relic Hunter:
   * Enchantment is more versatile, including buffs like Wrath, Heroism, and Prayer in addition to mind control – this makes it a good choice for a support or combat build. Powers  skippable.
   * Evocation gets smite spells, light spells, Blistering Invective, Divine Favor, and Divine Power. Since focus powers add energy rays (base power), blasts, and walls, the inquisitor again ends up with more versatility from this school. Only thing you're really missing is force effects.
   * Less good for the Relic Hunter:
   * Transmutation's spell list is lacklustre. The resonant and base powers are good enough that martials still need this school, but casters can wait until level 4 or 7.
   * Conjuration basically just has healing, and the Side Step power.
   * Illusion gets Invisibility, the “Disguise” line, and that's about it. 
   * Necromancy is pretty thin with only Inflict and a few not-fantastic debuffs.


*Update - Archetype: Sworn of the Eldest makes the Inquisitor Cha-based, slightly adjusts the skill set, and swaps the solo tactics and teamwork feats for domain spells. This archetype hasn’t been incorporated into the ratings consistently, but generally it will be a very good choice for gestalting with Cha-based casters or archers (since these are less likely to miss the teamwork feats). It’s probably also worth it for other characters that are fairly Cha-invested, and a fine option for other characters that won’t miss teamwork feats. 


Inquisitor // Investigator
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves+, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, physical Int Wis
Relies too much on buffing for effectiveness, and stat synergy is poor. Living Grimoire is a half-step better, with only one mental stat required. In an intrigue-based game, it might go to green thanks to good utility (stern gaze, detect alignment and discern lies work very well with investigator) and decent defences. But the combat end is still below average for gestalt, with the sacred weapon/tome and solo tactics leaving you only slightly ahead of a single-class investigator.


Inquisitor//Kineticist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level and pseudo-casting, Con Dex Wis
Bane doesn't work on blasts, since you need to “wield” the affected weapon. Sanctified Slayer's studied target  and the Destruction and Smiting judgments should work; Smiting is particularly nice as a way of dealing with DR/alignment. Consider archetypes that give up bane and not much else, such as the Trackless Operative or Secret Seeker. Relic Hunter is another option. One way or another, you'll get decent but not fantastic buffs and some extra utility on top of the kinteicist's competent damage and potentially strong defense/utility/mobility. 


Inquisitor // Magus
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, physical Int Wis
MAD, swift action heavy, and a lot of overlap on the basics. Living Grimoire reduces MAD, but the “light mace” is a terrible weapon for a magus.


Inquisitor//Medium
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 6 skills+, two partial casting, physical Wis Cha


Inquisitor // Mesmerist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, physical Wis Cha
Too unfocused, MAD.


Inquisitor // Monk
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves+, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, physical Wis
The really nice thing about this – aside from Wisdom synergy – is how effective Bane is when you're making a bunch of attacks with a single weapon (i.e. flurry). Unchained gives you great melee power with good mobility and excellent special durability between improved evasion, stalwart, and purity of body (additional defensive bonus feats or ki powers optional). Zen Archer is your ranged option, slightly less durable but massively Wis-focused with Wis to hit, AC, initiative, monster identification, and possibly social skills; consider Preacher to replace the teamwork feats, which tend to be better for melee. Either way, great skills and a few casting tricks to supplement your primary combat role. Other archetypes are mostly fine but not as impressive. Biggest issue is how many swift actions you use. Consider Relic Hunter for passive buffs; you can actually keep Bane as a standard action if you take the Transmutation implements. 


Inquisitor // Occultist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, physical Int Wis
With the inquisitor's toolkit your best bet is a martial build, taking Trappings of the Warrior with either Haunt Collector for the Champion spirit, or Panoply Savant if you're short on Focus (you probably will be) and don't mind delaying your utility implements. Living Grimoire helps if your stats are middling, though the tradeoffs aren't trivial. If you want an inquisitor with implements you're better off with the Relic Hunter Inquisitor paired with a full Wis-based caster or martial.


Inquisitor // Oracle
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 6 skills+, full and 6-level casting, physical Wis Cha
Unfocused in terms of your primary combat strategy, no stat synergy. If you do go this route I’d generally recommend a reach build with combat mystery; studied combat being a move action is less limiting if you’re not full attacking. Lore isn’t as useful for you as the Alchemist//Oracle since it doesn’t have as big an effect on your offense, but it could make a thematic build for a combat-light game.


Inquisitor // (Anti)Paladin (Sworn of the Eldest)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will+, 6 skills+, 6-level and 4-level casting, physical Wis Cha
Massively durable between Lay on Hands, Stalwart, and Divine Grace. Potentially brutal offense if you can get all your buffs active (Relic Hunter makes this somewhat easier). Not enough feats to be really versatile in combat, but good out of combat versatility. Stats are demanding, but you can minimize need for Dex by suiting up in full plate or alternatively go for a dex-based build. With Lay on Hands and a good AC you don't need all that much Con. The Sworn of the Eldest Inquisitor archetype lets you run Inquisitor abilities (including Cunning Initiative) off Cha if you’re OK with the Gathlain requirement - even having to trade solo tactics for domain spells, this archetype nudges a good combo into a very solid blue rating. On the paladin side, taking Tortured Crusader and going Wis-based is potentially thematic, but the trade-offs are steep, so depending on your stat array the combo is about on par with accepting the MAD.


Inquisitor // Psychic (Relic Hunter)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will,  6 skills+, full and 6-level casting, Int Wis
Strong in detective and intrigue plotlines, with a lot of mundane and magical abilities for investigation and manipulation. Also a pretty good selection of support spells from both lists, and debuffs mostly via Psychic. The inquisitor's personal combat buffs can be used to make you a passable backup combatant, but I recommend using Relic Hunter to swap them out for more versatile implement powers (which can include combat abilities if you wish). Faith is a solid discipline, though anything Wis-based is fine. You could even try a Cha discipline with Living Grimoire, though as this doesn't actually reduce MAD, I don't recommend it. Consider also Investigator//Psychic.


Inquisitor // Ranger
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves+, 6 skills+, 6- and 4-level casting, physical Wis
Thematic hunter, very versatile, capable of a lot of self-buffing. All good saves plus Evasion and Stalwart means you'll shrug off lots of pain. Also has plenty of skills and utility spells. You can use the combat style for switch hitting or to invest in a style that's usually too feat-intensive for the Inquisitor, like Weapon and Shield – though keep in mind Bane and TWF aren't a terribly strong combination since you can only enhance one of your weapons.


Inquisitor // Rogue
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves+, 8 skills+, 6-level casting, physical Wis
Not enough offense. Try Inquisitor//Slayer. 


Inquisitor //  Skald
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills+, casting


Inquisitor //  Slayer
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves+, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, physical Wis Int
Another thematic hunter with lots of self-buffs and utility. Sneak Attack works nicely with the flanking-based teamwork feats, and Sacred Huntmaster can give you a very strong flanking buddy. Avoid spells and talents with DCs.


Inquisitor // Sorcerer (Relic Hunter or Monster Tactician, Empyreal)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 6 skills+, full and 6-level casting, Wis (Cha)
Too MAD to make good use of the Inquisitor's combat buffs, and there are better options for adding utility to the sorcerer. Relic Hunter and Monster Tactician replace the combat buffs with more useful abilities. Even better, Empyreal bloodline gives you excellent Wis synergy as a casting stat and bonus to Initiative and, with the right inquisition, social skills. Stick to casting with one of the aforementioned Inquisitor archetypes, or if your stats are good enough try a reach build.


Inquisitor//Spiritualist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, physical Wis


Inquisitor // Summoner
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, physical Wis Cha
Inquisitor wants to enter combat, summoner isn't so sure. The MAD doesn't help. Teamwork feats are easier to use with an eidolon around, and the eidolon can also benefit if it takes those feats.


Inquisitor // Swashbuckler
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, Dex Wis Cha
Decent melee base with self-buffing and utility. Not very versatile in combat. Takes a lot of swift actions - consider Relic Hunter for more long term buffs. Alternatively, Paladin//Swashbuckler for better stat synergy.


Inquisitor // Warpriest
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, physical Wis
Definite issues here. You don't want to focus on casting (or you'd play Inquisitor//Cleric) but you've got only average BAB/HD. You need a lot of buffing to make up that gap, but most of your buffs are swift actions (Judgment, Bane, Fervor, Sacred Weapon). You can make it work, but it's tricky and will lag behind builds pairing either Inquisitor or Warpriest with a suitable full BAB class. Consider Relic Hunter Inquisitor to replace Judgment and Bane with passive benefits.


Inquisitor // Witch (Living Grimoire)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 6 skills+, full and 6-level casting, Int Wis


Inquisitor // Wizard (Relic Hunter, Monster Tactician, Living Grimoire)
Basics:  Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 6 skills+, full and 6-level casting, Int Wis
No casting stat synergy. Inquisitor can't carry this one in combat but doesn't provide much help for a casting build without the Relic Hunter (but see Occultist//Wizard) or Monster Tactician, which is useful in a small party for throwing more bodies on the field before opening up with Wizard casting. Living Grimoire is also an option – it doesn't add to your casting like the previous two, but SAD is nice and you're at least getting most of the Inquisitor utility. Strongly consider Investigator//Wizard.
