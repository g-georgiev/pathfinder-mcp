# Investigator Combinations

Investigator


A canny and versatile class, the investigator has a lot of things that frontliners want – extra saves, lots of skills, magical utility, and two juicy combat self-buffs. Studied Combat can be activated as a move action (swift with Quick Study), while Mutagen is available starting at level 3 as a long-duration prebuff. Int-based casters also pair fairly well with the Investigator, but usually the Alchemist is a better match thanks to its strong Fort save and access to the Cognatagen (which the investigator, oddly, doesn't get). 


Special note to Jinyiwei for keying investigator abilities off of wisdom (thanks to Shade L0rd for catching this archetype). The archetype also swaps your alchemy for inquisitor spells, swaps trapfinding and trap sense for skill/save bonuses against deception, and gives you the Judgment ability of an inquisitor three levels lower in exchange for Swift Alchemy.


Investigator//Kineticist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level and pseudo-casting, Con Dex Int
Studied Combat and Studied Strike work with the kinetic blade and encourage melee style – if you want ranged blasting, go Alchemist//Kineticist. This is a good combination for the Elemental Annihilator – Investigator provides plenty of utility, devastating infusion compensates for the fact that you're not actually Full BAB, and the static damage bonuses from both classes make TWF very effective. Quick Study makes sure that studied combat doesn't get in the way of gather power (or full attacks) and the Con mutagen is also a big boost.


Investigator // Magus
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, Int & physical
Studied Combat is really handy for the Magus since it gives you a damage and accuracy booster that doesn't drain your arcane pool or spell slots – and is more reliable than the Sneak Attack you could get from the vivisectionist alchemist. You end up with greater than full BAB. Mutagen is useful for you as long as you watch out for the Int penalty on the Str boosting version – consider a rapier-based Dex build. Good stat synergy, especially with the Empiricist archetype which reduces your need for Wis and Cha. The biggest downside is that you can't use your extracts with spell combat.


Investigator//Medium
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills+, casting, physical Int Cha
The archmage and hierophant are disappointing since the seance boon (and crafting feats) don't work with your extracts, and you probably can't afford both an Int and Cha good enough for serious casting. Champion is your best bet, and it's not a bad one – though you'll probably occasionally want other spirits if you anticipate an unusual day.


Investigator//Mesmerist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, physical (?) Int Cha


Investigator // Monk (Jinyiwei *** // Unchained ****)
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, physical Int Wis
MAD and swift action heavy. Jinyiwei makes this green by reducing the MAD. Unchained gets quite nice thanks to the better melee fundamentals and can make good use of Investigator buffs and the studied combat bonus (and, with Jinyiwei, Judgment). Compared to the Monk//Rogue you have less peak damage and need to manage your actions more carefully, but all good saves and more support versatility with extracts or spells. Zen Archer could work with Jinyiwei, though you need an extra feat for ranged studied combat. Sohei is not bad if you really don't want to invest in Wis. 


Investigator // Occultist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, Int and physical
For a resourceful and analytical character who is always after the truth. Between Studied Combat and Transmutation implements, you're able to buff yourself into combat competence  and still have tons of non-combat tricks up your sleeve. Divination implements are a useful and thematic choice. You'll want Trappings of the Warrior eventually, but it can probably wait until level 6 or even 10.


Investigator // Oracle
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills+, full and partial casting, Cha Int (physical?)
Decent enough for support and utility, with the potential to pick up a weapon in a pinch with Studied Combat, but synergy is mediocre.


Investigator // (Anti)Paladin
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves +, 6 skills+, 6 and 4-level casting, physical Cha Int
Powerful and durable in melee, lots of utility, and some support and debuff potential. The biggest challenge here is managing your swift actions, since both the Investigator and Paladin rely on them for buffing. The second biggest challenge is stats. If you're having trouble covering the stats, consider Archaeologist Bard//Paladin. 


Investigator // Ranger ***/* (Jinyiwei ****)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills+, 6 and 4-level casting, physical Int Wis
Strong in melee, and can work well at ranged with archery combat style and the Ranged Study feat. Tons of utility. Stat-intensive unless you go with Jinyiwei, which would make this solidly blue.


Investigator//Psychic
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills+, full and 6-level casting, Int and Wis or Cha
Excellent mastermind with mundane and magical investigation and manipulation abilities, lots of support magic, and some debuffing. Studied Combat is a good enough buff to make you a competent melee combatant in a pinch as long as you put a little effort into physical stats – I recommend Weapon Finesse. Consider Studied Defense if you don't expect to be on the offensive much. Psychic Detective is redundant and not recommended despite letting you use psychic Phrenic Amplifications on Investigator effects.


Investigator // Rogue
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 8 skills+, 6-level casting, physical & Int
Studied Combat adds important accuracy and Studied Strike can be used with Sneak Attack, so this is actually quite good despite underwhelming basics. Your first few rogue talents should probably be the combat feats (finesse, weapon focus, and combat trick). Take a rogue archetype to replace the duplicate Trap Sense – lots of them do, though they aren't much good. Or, if you have good enough stats for a charisma bonus, take Ninja instead and get access to more tricks.
Nick says: I'm playing this combo in a campaign. Do Knife Master archetype for TWF builds; the replacements for Trapfinding and Trap Sense are only okay, but the Sneak Attack supplement is great. A rapier build should be doable, too, especially if you dip Swashbuckler (Inspired Blade) in lieu of an Investigator level; I can't speak for Rogues generally, but rapiers are the perfect weapon for Investigator. Also generally recommend Unchained over vanilla Rogue.


Investigator // Skald
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, Int Cha & physical


Investigator // Slayer
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, physical & Int
Though it feels a bit weird, you can combine all your key combat boosts – Studied Target, Studied Combat, Sneak Attack, and Studied Strike – though Quarry overlaps. This gives you massive static bonuses on attacks with, at higher levels, relatively little setup. TWF is extremely effective under these circumstances. Consider Two-Weapon Feint, which makes it easier to get in sneak attacks – you can qualify as early as level 3. The Combat Expertise prerequisite is also useful for converting some of your massive accuracy bonus to a defensive bonus. You can apply debuffs using both your Sneak Attack and Studied Strike, with saves based on Int for both, and have tons of skill and utility options.


Investigator // Sorcerer (Sage)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills+, full and partial casting, Cha Int
Focus on buffs with Investigator and you don't need much Int.


Investigator//Spiritualist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills+, casting


Investigator // Summoner (Synth)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, Int Cha physical?
Increased versatility in your spell list, but not much synergy. Synthesist can be competent in melee. Bard//Summoner is significantly better.


Investigator // Swashbuckler (Inspired Blade)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Ref and Will, 6 skills+, 6-level casting, Dex Int Cha
Dex mutagen is a big boon for buckling swashes. Studied Strike and Precise strike are both precision damage, which makes you a bit overspecialized, but Studied Combat at least is an insight bonus. Inspired Blade gives you a bit more kick for your Int, which is enough to take the combo up a step. Consider Empiricist if you want to focus even more on Int, though since you're already motivated to have a good Dex and Cha it won't make a big difference.


Investigator // Warpriest **
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills+, two 6-level casting, Int Wis & physical
MAD, and needs a lot of swift actions to buff you up from average BAB. Consider Arsenal Chaplain or Mantis Zealot to cut down on swift actions on the warpriest side. Alternatively, using Studied Target as a move action works fairly well with Vital Strike. Jinyiwei makes you less MAD, but doesn’t solve the other problems - and trading extracts for inquisitor spells is a small versatility hit, so I’m still rating that archetype as yellow.


Investigator // Witch *** 
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills+, full and 6-level casting, Int
Mindchemist//Witch is a better gestalt, but that doesn't mean that this one's bad – you get a lot of skills, some useful extracts, and a lot of int synergy (especially with the Empiricist). You'll also be able to stand up in melee if you have to. Studied Defense is worth a look since you won't find the to-hit bonus as useful as characters with a more martial focus – though note that Studied Combat and Studied Strike both work just fine with the White-Haired Witch's attacks.


Investigator // Wizard ***
Basics:  Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6+skills, full and 6-level casting, Int
As above, it's usually better to take Mindchemist over Investigator, but if you really want skills in addition to your arcane goodness, this will do fine. The Divination school is thematic.
