# Kineticist Combinations

Kineticist


Broadly, you have three options.


First, focus on blasts. Your second class should be a martial class that can improve your blasts. This means general bonuses to weapon damage like Studied Combat – not effects that improve manufactured weapons or spells. Gestalting with partial casters can work well for ranged energy blasters who don't care as much about accuracy or HP. Elemental Annihilator and Kinetic Knight are good archetypes for blast-happy builds, with the latter serving as a heavy armour melee-only option. 


Second, elementally enhance attacks with Kinetic Fist or Energize Weapon. This adds a little more than half a point of damage per kineticist level to your attacks – mediocre, and it costs burn until level 5, but after that it's an unlimited bonus. You can also debuff with substance infusions if you can handle the burn. Some of the defence talents are excellent, and ranged and area blasts and utility talents add some versatility. For this type of build the other gestalt half needs to handle the martial style. Note that Energize Weapon is more limited than Kinetic Fist – it works on only a single weapon (no TWF) and only with energy blasts (no terrakinetic weapons). Air is a good elemental choice for Energize Weapon builds, bringing electric blasts, the magnetic infusion (free with energize weapon at level 11) and wings of air. Note also that holding manufactured weapons prevents you from gathering power.


Third, combine with a full caster, with kineticist providing improved durability and extra combat options. Single element builds are safer as casting gestalts since the casting half provides more strategic versatility.


See also Rylar's guide to gestalting the kineticist.


Kineticist // Medium
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 4 skills, partial casting, Con Dex Cha
This is most effective as a kinetic blade user, making use of the champion's abilities enhancing full attacks. Note however that it's not quite clear whether blasts count as “spells” for purposes of the damage bonuses that the spirits grant, meaning that the damage bonus may or may not apply to blade attacks.


Kineticist//Mesmerist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills, 6-level and pseudo-casting, Dex Con Cha


Kineticist // Monk (Unchained)
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves/Fort and Ref, 4 skills, pseudo-casting, physical Wis
Ultimate bender it's not. Since monk doesn't give you anything to enhance blasts, you're forced into strategy two: Kinetic Fist or Energize Weapon. This means you really need the Unchained Monk's full BAB and HD. Hydrokineticist's defence is particularly useful when you don't wear armour. For other options mixing fists and elemental magic, check out Monk//Druid, Monk//Empyreal Sorcerer, Kineticist//Brawler, and Kineticist (Elemental Ascetic)//Cleric (Ecclesitheurge).


Kineticist // Occultist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 4 skills, 6-level and pseudo-casting, Con Dex Int
Trappings of the Warrior doesn't work with blasts, so I suggest using energy blasts for better accuracy. There aren't a whole lot of synergistic options aside from Transmutation's resonant power and maybe a Haunted (Champion) implement – Legacy Weapon doesn't work on blasts (though you can use it on friends' weapons). That means you're looking for defensive options and versatility. Try to coordinate spell, power, and talent selections. For example, an aerokineticist probably wants celerity instead of the quickness focus power – in contrast, conjuration implements do a better job than kinetic healer. Evocation is generally inferior to and doesn't synergize with your blasts, but it does provide alternative energy options and force effect. This makes it useful as a fallback option, especially for single-element builds.


Kineticist // Oracle
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves,  4 skills, full and pseudo-casting, Con Dex Cha
Not a lot of synergy, but decent versatility. Divine Power and similar buffs are helpful with blasts. With a relatively low HD and BAB, consider staying at range for safety and/or using energy blasts for better accuracy. Thematically tempting to overspecialize, for example with a Pyrokineticist//Flame Oracle. Try to instead pick complementary elements & mysteries, such as Fire & Solar, or Void & Dark Tapestry. Note that the Dance of the Blades and Wood Bond powers only work when wielding an appropriate weapon, so they don't help with metal or wood blasts.


Kineticist // (Anti)Paladin (Geokineticist)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves +, 4 skills, 4 level casting, Con Dex Cha
Versatile combo with some synergistic ability. Blasts provide melee and ranged combat options with relatively little feat investment, and infusions allow debuffing. Smite makes your blasts more lethal and also allows them to bypass DR (a major weakness for physical blasts). Though Lay on Hands doesn't remove burn it does make it less dangerous and also provides the option to act as a secondary healer. Depending on element, you can also pick up added defenses or utility. Geo is my favourite pick because of the excellent defensive talent and the synergy between smite and physical blasts, but you could still get a lot of mileage out of the other elements. 


Kineticist // Psychic
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves,  4 skills, full and pseudo-casting, Dex Con Int and Wis or Cha


Kineticist // Ranger
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 6 skills, 4-level and pseudo-casting, Dex Con Wis
Combat styles are not all that useful (unless you're an Elemental Annihilator) but all kineticists can at least use some of the ranged feats, and favoured enemy applies, so combat abilities synergise reasonably well. Mounted Combat is also an option if you want to ride your animal companion. Good assortment of utility and defensive abilities depending on build. 


Kineticist // Rogue (Telekineticist)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Ref, 8 skills, pseudo-casting
Sneak attack does work with blasts and being able to hit touch AC helps with accuracy, though your defenses aren't great when you take burn into account. You do get access to some pretty nice utility – telekineticist deserves special mention for its ability to use sleight of hand and disable device at range and its at-will invisibility starting at level 6. You also get the ability to sense invisible things in turn. Aerokinesis is a good second choice for flight and an electric blast.


Comment: I'm right now playing a Telekineticist/Rogue (Phantom Thief). He's a LOT of fun. 


Kineticist // Skald
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, casting


Kineticist // Slayer
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 6 skills, pseudo-casting, Con Dex Int
Strong combat presence with good utility. Blue assuming you can use studied target on blasts – sneak attack definitely works but is not as reliable a bonus. Between the two bonuses, melee is a more effective strategy, but you're pretty effective at switch hitting as with just Precise Shot from the archery style. Telekineticist notably grants invisibility and some long-range thievery skills, but any element is a good choice. 


Kineticist // Sorcerer
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, Con Cha Dex
While different in format, the abilities from each class can do very similar things (such as blasting, flight, and invisibility), so make careful selections to avoid overlap. Spell damage bonuses from your bloodlines don't affect your blasts, so consider using the sorcerer magic to do things other than blasting. Overwhelming Soul makes you  more SAD, but also limits your ability to use the more powerful kineticist abilities so it's a bit of a wash.


Kineticist//Spiritualist (Ectoplasmatist)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 4 skills, casting


Kineticist // Summoner
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 4 skills, 6-level and pseudo-casting, Con Dex Cha
Good number of options here, but not much synergy – features from your two classes just don't overlap.


Kineticist // Swashbuckler
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 4 skills, pseudo-casting, Dex Con Cha
As far as I can tell, your kinetic blade is considered a light or one-handed piercing weapon as long as you pick a blast type that does piercing damage – so start with Earth, Aether, or Wood, with composite blasts from Earth, Void (Gravity), Air, Fire (with wood only), or Water (cold, with wood only). If I'm right about this, you get a strong melee focus with ranged capability. Depending on build you can also get moderate to excellent defense, mobility, battlefield control, and utility. If you can't use Swash stuff with Kinetic Blade, don't bother.


Kineticist // Warpriest
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 4 skills, 6-level and pseudo-casting, Con Dex Wis
Sacred Weapon won't help your blasts, and you shouldn't need the bonus feats, so Kineticist//Cleric or //Paladin is better. If you really want to go this route, consider Mantis Zealot to replace Sacred Weapon with Sneak Attack. Sacred Fist is OK with Kinetic Fist – still not an ideal style, but probably better than Elemental Ascetic (note: don't combine the two).


Kineticist // Witch (Old Scarred Witch Doctor)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, Int Con Dex
Durable enough for a caster/blaster, with lots of debuffing and some support and control options. Between simple blasts and hexes you can basically go all day, and still have the potential to throw down high level spells or accept burn when you want to pick it up a notch. If you're using the pre-errata SWD this goes up to blue/purple thanks to fantastic Con synergy.


Kineticist // Wizard
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, Int Con Dex
The kineticist provides a boost in durability, extra potential for spontaneity, and more low-level staying power on top of the versatility of the wizard. While most casting features don't boost blasts, you can get double mileage out of Spell Penetration and Elemental Focus.
