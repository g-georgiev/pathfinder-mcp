# Magus Combinations

Magus


The action economy advantage of spell combat is really strong in gestalt, and makes the Magus one of the best classes to combine with spellcasters, especially Int-based ones. Broad Study lets you use your gestalt spells with your magus abilities at level 6. The magus also does fairly well with full-BAB classes that complement its one-handed combat style. Psychic classes can be problematic because their thought components make spells difficult to cast defensively, which the magus does frequently. The magus tends to be a bit more MAD so avoid classes that require Wis or Cha. Also keep in mind that the magus is very swift action heavy. Eldritch Scion would appear to make a good fit for Charisma-based classes, but the delay in Spell Combat and the requirement to refresh your Mystic Focus every two rounds to use magus abilities is a very steep price, so it’s hard to recommend.


Kensai is a strong archetype when paired with most non-armour wearing classes, thanks to its unarmoured defense. Spire Defender is worth looking at for gestalts that get armour proficiency from their non-magus side, since you get Combat Expertise, Dodge, and a minor skill bonus in exchange for just Spell Recall. Eldritch Archer supports a ranged combat style, and Myrmidarch works both in melee and at range and gains access to Weapon Training, at the cost of some magical power.

The champion's best abilities don't work with spell combat, and the archmage's flexibility doesn't mean as much for a prepared caster – plus you only have average BAB, and are MAD. Don't bother.


Magus // Mesmerist **
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills, two 6-level casting, physical Int Cha
Tricks and Stare are handy, but your stats are spread too thin and your BAB is low. Eldritch Scion helps by making all of your casting Cha-based, but the limitations on spell combat are generally considered a poor trade. 


Magus // Monk */*
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves, 4 skills, 6-level casting, physical Int Wis
MAD, unarmed strikes aren't the best weapons for the magus, and Spell Combat and Flurry are not compatible since they are both pseudo-TWF. By working carefully to mitigate these issues you could make something halfway functional (moving the combo from red to yellow). General strategies: 
   * Use Ascetic Style (temple sword) to improve your crit range. Costs 2-4 feats depending on how far up the chain you go, and a swift action to activate.
   * Use multi-touch spells, which work better than one-off spells if you plan to alternate between spell combat and flurry, and conserve your relatively limited spell slots. 
Specific archetype combinations:
   * Eldritch Scion//Unchained Scaled Fist reduces MAD by swapping both classes to Cha-based, and gives you full BAB and HD. Eldritch Scion’s low-level restrictions on spell combat are less onerous when you can Flurry instead.
   * Eldritch Archer//Zen Archer. The main synergy here is simply specializing in the same weapon, but both archetypes are pretty good at that, so this mostly works. Note that while you can’t use Spell Combat or Manyshot with Flurry, you can take Manyshot as a monk bonus feat and use it with Spell Combat. If you do this, skip Rapid Shot - the attack penalty isn’t worth it.
   * Master of Many Styles // Kensai (temple sword), AKA “Sword Style Master.” The bonus Weapon Focus feat qualifies you for Ascetic Style at level 1, and you can combine it with other styles. Take Combat Style Master at level 5 to eliminate the pesky swift action cost, and at level 8 your always on +3 to attack mitigates the loss of full BAB. Canny Defense gives you extra unarmoured AC. Better in a game with a generous stat allotment and not too many combats per day - between diminished spellcasting and the lack of Flurry getting in the way of Spell Combat, you’ll run out of spells fast.

Magus // Occultist ***
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, two 6-level casting, physical & Int
This gish//gish is not as reliable and durable as a good gish//martial, nor does it have the raw world-bending power of a gish//full arcane. You've also got two significant conflicts. First, Trappings of the Warrior requires a shield, which interferes with spell combat unless you take Skirnir, which doesn't come together until it actually gets spell combat at level 8 and thus is un-advisable unless you start at high levels. Second, spell combat doesn't work with focus powers so it doesn't improve the occultist's action economy as much as one would like. However, you've got a versatile set of abilities and some methods for keeping your swordmagery respectable.  Offensive tips:
   * Transmutation implements are a must. Legacy weapon stacks with the arcane pool enhancement, allowing you to get a +1 bane (whatever) weapon right out the gate. 
   * Haunt Collector archetype is strongly recommended. Champion spirit gives you a passive weapon damage bonus and a short-term boost to attack rolls based on focus invested. Archmage also adds to spell damage with a short-term Int still boost for utility. Haunt implements that have poor resonant powers but good spell lists and ideally some good focus powers. Necromancy implements are a great choice; see also conjuration, enchantment, and illusion. 
   * Evocation implement adds a modest extra bonus to evocation spells. The focus power Radiance also works well with a typical crit-fishing magus. 
   * Your to-hit is low, so take Weapon Focus and consider Arcane Accuracy and Accurate Strike Arcana.
Use occultist spontaneous casting for staples like Shield or things that aren't on the magus list, which include by school:
   * Abjuration – A couple utility spells like Alarm, Arcane Lock, and Nondetection, plus Resist and Protection from Energy, Magic Circle Against [], Break Enchantment, Dimensional Anchor, Freedom of Movement, Globe of Invulnerability, Spell Immunity, Life Bubble, and Antimagic Field.
   * Conjuration – The biggest thing here is Cure access, but as a bonus you get Mage Armour, Minor Creation, Major Creation, and Create Demiplane.
   * Divination – Everything worthwhile except Detect/Read Magic, Arcane Sight and True Seeing.
   * Enchantment – Basically everything; magi don't do enchantment.
   * Evocation –  A handful of spells: Frost Fall, Call Lightning, Deeper Darkness,  Invisibility Purge, Tiny Hut, Resilient Sphere,  Sending, Call Lightning Storm, Blade Barrier, Contingency.
   * Illusion – Disguise Self, Magic Aura, Disguise Other, Misdirection, Invisibility Sphere, Greater Invisibility, Illusory Wall, Permanent Image
   * Necromancy – Most spells, including False Life, the Inflict line, and Harm.
   * Transmutation – Mostly a range of utility spells such as Ant Haul, Pass Without Trace, Air Step, Darkvision, Knock, Make Whole, Rope Trick, Warp Wood, Wood Shape, Shrink Item, Air Walk, Echolocation, Control Winds, and Fabricate. Also a handful of combat spells: Lead Blades, Versatile Weapon (early-access) Magic Vestment, Rusting Grasp, Transmute Rock to Mud.


Magus // Oracle **/*
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and 6-level casting, physical Int Cha
MAD is a pain, BAB is a pain, defences aren't great. Decent variety of spells, and action economy is good. Broad Study helps you get the most out of nasty touch spells. Consider the Wind mystery for the Vortex Spells revelation, which will stagger your target on a spellstrike crit. Also, flight. Add warsighted if you don't like many of the other revelations. Lore is a good alternative for defraying MAD. Note that Winter’s Freezing Spells revelation does not work with Frostbite.


Magus // (Anti)Paladin
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves +, 2 skills, 6- and 4-level casting, physical Int Cha
MAD, and your best abilities are almost all competing for your swift actions. On the other hand, they are good abilities. Deep Marshal//Stonelord is an interesting archetype combination thematically and it gets rid of your MAD issue by reducing your need for Cha (lay on hands uses only) and Dex (you get medium armour immediately); biggest downside is needing to use dwarven weapons on a magus.


Magus // Psychic ***
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 2 skills+, full and 6-level casting, physical Int and Wis or Cha
I would like to be more excited about this, but the extra mental stat requirement compared to a Magus//Witch or Wizard and the existence of the Kensai for armour issues makes the Psychic a little less shiny. There's also the fact that the thought component makes psychic spells difficult to cast defensively as the magus often does (the Intense Focus amplification helps). Still, you get magus action economy with an extended array of spells, including a broad selection of divination and enchantment and some good staple buffs for spontaneous casting.  Decent choice if you want lots of casting with a non-Kensai archetype like Myrmidarch.


Magus // Ranger (Myrmidarch, Skirnir, Eldritch Archer)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, 6- and 4-level casting, physical Int Wis
Slayer works better with the typical magus einhander style. However, switch-hitting works with Myrmidarch, shield bashing with Skirnir, and archery is extremely effective with the Eldritch Archer. Divine Marksman is a good archetype for archers or switch hitters, and since it gets rid of Ranger spellcasting it removes your need for an above-average Wis.


Magus // Rogue **
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 8 skills, 6-level casting, physical Int
The biggest draw is the no-strings-attached Dex to damage. You can also use Dimensional Assault to flank with yourself, which makes landing your first Debilitating Injury (AC debuff) easier, and you’ll get buckets of skill ranks. Sylvan Trickster’s hexes make good use of high Int but can’t be used with Spell Combat or Spellstrike. Alternatively, consider Kensai//Eldritch Scoundrel for more magical versatility at the cost of some skill ranks and sneak attack progression. Still, expect accuracy and durability issues. If you can do without Rogue’s Finesse consider Magus//Slayer for better martial ability, or gestalt either Magus or Rogue with a full caster if that's your focus.

Magus // Slayer ****
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, 6-level casting, physical Int
Excellent set of combat and utility abilities. You can pick up extra combat feats like Weapon Finesse or Weapon Focus, take archery style feats (with the Eldritch Archer, or Myrmidarch for switch hitting) or grab shield bashing with Skirnir. Quarry lets you automatically confirm crits, which is great if you've got spell damage riding on it. The dimensional dervish feat chain lets you flank with yourself for an easier sneak attack. A little heavy on the swift actions, but not too badly.


Magus // Sorcerer *** (Kensai + Sage *****)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 2 skills, full and 6-level casting, physical Int Cha
Being able to spam basic combat spells is very nice. Stat Synergy and unarmoured AC are super important here, so Kensai and Sage bloodline make a big difference. If your GM will let you combine Wildblooded with other archetypes, consider taking Crossblooded with a combat bloodline, or adding Eldritch Scrapper for flexible combat feats.

Magus // Summoner ** (Synthesist ***)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 2 skills, two 6-level casting, physical Int Cha
Not enough personal combat ability to hold its own, not enough casting to rely on that. Synthesist is a bit more workable because it makes it easier to get the physical stats you need and adds personal combat abilities. Note that because the Synth can't wear armour, Kensai is a solid choice here.


Magus // Swashbuckler *** (Inspired Blade ****)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, 6-level casting, Dex Int Cha
The swashbuckler's fighting style synergises well with the magus (Precise Strike works with spell combat) and the bonus feats are very welcome. Both classes use a lot of swift actions so look for archetypes and feats granting passive abilities. Inspired Blade's Int to panache is the reason you're not taking Daring Champion Cavalier. Unfortunately Fencing Grace no longer works with Spell Combat, but the rapier is still a decent weapon, and you can get it with Agile. Since you automatically qualify for Fencing Grace, it might even be worth taking it for Dex to damage when not using spell combat before you can afford Agile. Dervish Dance does work with Spell Combat, and the Whirling Dervish archetype gives it to you for free, but this limits your panache (especially if you consider Whirling Dervish’s Merciful restriction on gaining panache from dropping foes), and since the archetype doesn’t add much on top of the Dervish Dance feat there’s a strong argument for building this concept with Daring Champion over the sadly slightly underpowered Swashbuckler. Kensai is roughly an even trade-off for this class combo, as an unarmoured style works just fine for the swashbuckler but the diminished spellcasting hurts more when your second class doesn’t bring spells to the table.


Magus // Warpriest **
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 2 skills, htwo 6-level casting, physical Wis Int
MAD, uses too many swift actions, and too much overlap on the basics. That said, the action economy is excellent (Fervor + Spell Combat = two spells and a full attack), so this is worth considering if you have really good stats. Dervish Dance is very helpful in this respect.


Magus // Witch **** (Kensai, Hexcrafter, White Haired Witch)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 2 skills+, full and 6-level casting, physical Int
You’re an Int-based full caster so Kensai shouldn’t be a surprise recommendation here. The main alternative archetype on the Magus side would be Hexcrafter, which is nicely thematic and offsets White Haired Witch’s loss of hexes.


Magus // Wizard **** (Kensai *****)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 2 skills+, full and 6-level casting, physical Int
You're a wizard who gets an additional Int-based partial casting progression and can also function in combat with good action economy, so right off the bat this is pretty cool. Kensai is an obvious choice since you get Int to AC instead of the armour that would interfere with your wizard spells. You also get Int to initiative, number of AoO, crit confirmation, and surprise round damage, plus some other minor bonuses. Diminished spellcasting and loss of spell knowledge and knowledge pool are less important when you have full wizard casting backing you up. While a Diviner specialization will skyrocket your initiative, consider also Evocation (Admixture) for making sure your Shocking Grasp always uses the right energy type, or Transmutation (Enhancement) if your stats and AC still need buffing. Accuracy may be a  little low. You can boost it with Weapon Focus (which Kensai gets for free), Accurate Strike arcana, and prioritizing weapon enhancement bonus over properties. The Dimensional Dervish feat is also a nice pick for this combo as you have more spell slots for Dimension Door.
