# Medium Combinations

Medium


The medium is best as a “secondary” class for gestalt, which is to say that you should use your other class to define your usual tactics and then use spirits to either supplement your primary role or increase versatility.  This works best with full-BAB classes, since the Champion and to a lesser extent the Guardian offer good combat abilities for general use and most martials will get a lot out of the ability to select increased casting or skill abilities for specific missions; the good Will save is an added bonus. If the proficiencies granted by the Champion and Guardian are irrelevant, you don't have to worry about losing proficiency with key gear when you pick a different spirit and can channel a weaker spirit at levels 1-5 for extra spirit surge.  However, casters can also benefit from the seance bonuses of the archmage or hierophant and from the choice between expanded spell options, various skill or defence options, and (for some) the champion's ability to enter combat.


Consider Spirit Dancer for more flexibility in switching between spirits. Also note that while the medium's psychic spells aren't subject to armour spell failure, the ones they get from the archmage spirit are arcane and can be spoiled by your armour.


Medium//Mesmerist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills, two partial casting, physical Cha




Medium // Monk (Unchained Scaled Fist)
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves, 4 skills, partial casting, physical Cha Wis
Unchained Scaled Fist gives you two key things: full BAB and stat synergy. The champion is underwhelming since sudden attack doesn't work with flurry of blows and flying kick gives you mobility without fleet charge. The spirit bonus is still welcome in combat and you can channel a weaker spirit to get extra surge, but consider a Guardian or Marshal instead at higher levels. A nice perk of this build is that unlike most martial//mediums you can use the archmage without worrying about armour.


Medium//Occultist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, two partial casting, physical Int Cha
Quite a lot of day-to-day flexibility between your spirits and allocating your mental focus, but it's going to take a lot of work to fill any role competently, and the stat requirements are hefty. 


Medium // Oracle
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Will, 4 skills, full and partial casting, Cha (and physical?)
While the single good save hurts, the Oracle definitely benefits from the increased spell flexibility of the archmage and hierophant and can benefit from both seance bonuses. A modest investment in longspear reach tactics gives you a good non-spell option and lets you fill in for a frontliner with the Champion or Guardian spirit. The arcane staples on the medium's base list (haste, heroism) are also appreciated no matter the spirit. Bonus: thematic synergy between channeling spirits and the oracle's mysterious power.


Medium // (Anti)Paladin
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will+, 4 skills, two 4-level casting, physical and Cha
Armour spell failure will be a bit of a problem for the archmage, but otherwise the . The hierophant's paladin code taboo is a freebie for you since you already need to follow it.


Medium // Psychic
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Will, 4 skills, full and partial casting, Int Cha
This combination is more fragile than I like, but the Medium does add day-to-day versatility to the spontaneous Psychic. Heirophant and Marshall are most complementary since they add support capability with the ability to wear armour, but the others could still be useful in some situations. Aside from spells borrowed from spirits, the medium's list is largely a subset of the Psychic's – though that still lets you pick up a few more spells known, and you can use them with phrenic amplifications.


Medium // Ranger
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, partial casting, physical Wis Cha
TWF and archery work really well with the Champion's damage bonus. Don't invest too many resources in casting, though with a decent stat allotment you'll be able to take advantage of the magical flexibility of the Archmage and Hierophant; remember that some archery feats benefit rays.

Medium // Skald
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 4 skills, casting, physical and Cha


Medium // Slayer
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, partial casting, physical Cha Int?
TWF and archery work really well with the Champion's damage bonus.


Medium // Sorcerer
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Will, 4 skills, full and partial casting, Cha (and physical?) 
The archmage seance bonus is pretty useful for a blaster sorcerer, and the ability to select different daily spells greatly increases your flexibility. You can also use hierophant to get access to completely new spells powered by the same casting stat, feats, items, etc. You're a bit fragile, though, since you can't use the medium's armour proficiency.


Medium//Spiritualist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, casting


Medium // Summoner
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Will, 4 skills, two partial casting, Cha
Unless you're a synthesist, build for casting. The summoner doesn't otherwise get enough combat abilities to make a credible threat in gestalt, even with champion. If you want to give the champion's seance bonus to allies, use marshal. If you plan on using summoned monsters and not just the eidolon, strongly consider spirit dancer so you can grant summoned creatures your seance bonus. 


Medium // Swashbuckler
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Ref and Will, 4 skills, partial casting, Dex Cha
This is a good choice if you want to emphasize the medium thematically and to use a variety of different spirits. The swashbuckler supplies reliable combat competence without requiring much investment in feats or stats. This means that while all martials will be effective when channeling the champion, you'll also have the charisma and casting feats to back up the archmage or hierophant (and a decent AC if you doff armour to avoid impeding the archmage's casting).


Medium // Warpriest
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, partial casting, physical Wis Cha
Works OK for TWF and archery between the champion's static damage bonus and the warpriest's sacred weapons and bonus feats. However accuracy is low and you'll rely heavily on the champion spirit for your combat contribution, meaning switching out spirits is risky business. Using paladin or battle oracle instead of warpriest is recommended.


Medium// Witch
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Will, 4 skills, full and partial casting, Int Cha


Medium // Wizard
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Will, 4 skills, full and partial casting, Int Cha
I highly recommend Medium//Sorcerer instead; the archmage's spell flexibility isn't nearly as useful for the wizard as the sorcerer, and you need an extra stat.
