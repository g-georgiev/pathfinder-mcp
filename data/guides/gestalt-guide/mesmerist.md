# Mesmerist Combinations

Mesmerist


Similar to the bard but more debuff-focused, the Mesmerist works well with charisma-based warriors and casters. Warriors benefit from increased utility, better saves, and some the swift and free action debuffs and control effects associated with the Mesmerist's Stare and Trick abilities, and from the ability to use traditionally arcane defenses like Displacement with armour. Note that with Painful Stare putting a lot of hurt behind your first hit in a round, this works very well with builds that don't normally full attack (large vital strikers, chargers, reach builds). Casters benefit in particular from the Stare's ability to weaken Will saves or SR, and have an easier time maintaining a high enough Charisma to get the most out of the Mesmerist's spells, which often require saving throws. Because of this trend towards spells with saves, Mesmerist is particularly sensitive to MAD gestalts.

Mesmerist // Monk (Unchained Scaled Fist)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills, 6-level casting, physical Cha Wis
Not enough casting for a caster focus. Unchained Scaled Fist gives you an OK martial base and Cha synergy. Consider Bard//Monk or Mesmerist//Bloody Knuckle Bloodrager. Building this one without Scaled Fist leaves you uncomfortably MAD, though if you have really good stats you might try a reach build with Sohei.


Mesmerist // Occultist (Silksworn)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills, two 6-level casting, Int Cha  physical
Strong utility, but the MAD is a problem for your offence. You could use Trappings of the Warrior and Haunt Collector to work up a passable martial presence, but Bard//Occultist works better for that. In an intrigue-based game, you'll get good mileage out of Silksworn as it gives you more focus to work with, more magical versatility, and some sneaky/social benefits. The mesmerist is pretty focused on enchantment and illusion with some divination, so abjuration, conjuration, transmutation, evocation, and necromancy implements add the most versatility – though divination is still worth taking for the resonant and focus powers, and consider illusion around level 6-10 for shadow beast.


Mesmerist // Oracle (Heavens)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills, full and 6-level casting, Cha
Very SAD combination with strong support and debuffing abilities, some battlefield control, and good skills with a particularly social bent. Heavens mystery gets a special mention for the will save penalty from Hypnotic Stare and the fact that Mental Potency stacks with Awesome Display for some outrageously effective pattern spells. Of course, there's nothing wrong with using Lore, Nature, or Lunar to turn you into a Charisma powerhouse. 


Mesmerist // (Anti)Paladin
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves +, 6 skills, 6- and 4-level casting, physical Cha
This combination has a strong physical presence coupled with good action economy for support, debuffing, and battlefield control, as well as a solid socially-leaning skill list. The flavour lends itself a bit more easily to the sinister Anti-Paladin, but most of the Mesmerist's abilities are still appropriate for Paladins, depending on how your GM interprets “honourable” behavior. For example, Meek Facade is a great trick for attracting attacks to a paladin, Gift of Will is a completely unobjectionable way to support an ally, and the Mesmerist's Stare works equally well when making the unjust quail in their shoes as when bending the weak to the will of the strong. Immunity to fear is handy for a psychic caster, especially if you've used the otherwise redundant Towering Ego to compensate for trading away Divine Grace with an archetype.


Mesmerist // Psychic
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills, full and 6-level casting, Int Cha
Want to play with peoples' minds? This is for you. Tons of enchantment and illusion, some divination; range of buffs and debuffs with some battlefield control. Rewards careful planning between setting up Mesmerist Tricks and your ability to apply Phrenic Amplifications to Mesmerist spells. Having two casting stats isn't quite ideal, but with various save-penalizing and DC-boosting effects you should be able to get stuff to stick. While this is definitely good, it's also worth noting Mesmerist//Sorcerer for increased spell diversity.


Mesmerist // Ranger
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, 6- and 4-level casting, physical Wis Cha
A bit stat heavy, but enough tricks to make it worth it.

Mesmerist // Slayer
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, 6-level casting, physical Cha Int
The slayer appreciates the Mesmerist's sneakiness. In particular, the Invisibility spell and feinting abilities help you get Sneak Attack. Consider combining a two-weapon style with two-weapon feint (Consummate Liar means you only need Dex 15 to qualify) and the Vexing Daredevil archetype – remember that you can still cast psychic spells while holding two weapons. Unless you have unusually good stats, I would avoid Slayer talents with DCs in order to focus on Cha for Mesmerist spells.


Mesmerist // Sorcerer
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills, full and 6-level casting, Cha
Excellent range of SAD casting with few vulnerabilities (watch that Fort save) and a good handful of other tricks between the Mesmerist and your bloodline. Bloodlines that involve manipulating others, such as Fey, Infernal, and Serpentine, are both thematic and synergistic with the Mesmerist's focus, but it's also perfectly fine to diversify a bit more with the Sorcerer. Psychic Inception, Umbral Mesmerist, and Undead Bloodline are all options for opening undead up to mind-affecting spells from either class.

Mesmerist // Summoner
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 6 skills, two 6-level casting, Cha
The minion build. Decent array of support and control spells and effects with stat synergy, good skills, light armour, and you're fielding an eidolon (or other summons).


Mesmerist // Swashbuckler
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Ref and Will, 6 skills, 6-level casting, Dex Cha
Mental trickery gives the swashbuckler more ability to control the battlefield and hamper enemies; spells give you utility, pre-buffing, and a backup plan if your sword isn't working. Minimal stat requirements keep Mesmerist DCs high (and give you lots of panache) without sacrificing ability to hold up in melee. Vexing Daredevil is thematic, but feinting actually doesn't do all that much for you – I would still prefer to keep the Bold Stare improvements.


Mesmerist // Warpriest
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills, two 6-level casting, physical Wis Cha
Too MAD, not enough combat competence to justify Warpriest. You want Mesmerist//Cleric or Oracle, or maybe Medium//Warpriest.
