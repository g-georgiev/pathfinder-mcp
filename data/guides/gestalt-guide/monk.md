# Monk Combinations

Monk


On their own, most monk variants don't have the offense necessary to stand up beside other classes. However, they do have a lot of defensive or mobility options and other neat tricks that can be very valuable when paired with a more active class, whether martial or caster – and the variants that do have strong offense still work well for characters that want a little extra punch. Unarmed strikes optional. If going without armour hurts, look at the Sohei archetype or archetypes that get rid of flurry and/or fast movement (such as Sensei or Master of Many Styles) which lose less when wearing armour. Remember to use Qinggong to trade out any abilities you won't use (slow fall, high jump) for handy spell-like abilities. There's something for everyone!


First off, characters that use Charisma will want either Scaled Fist (dragon themed charisma based monk), or the Nornkith (fey themed charisma based monk). Only the Scaled Fist is compatible with Unchained Monk, which gives it a significant advantage in gestalting with part-casters (which will badly want the full BAB).


Characters that want ranged combat as a primary strategy or a complement to Wis-based casting will love Zen Archer. It comes with great offense and good mobility. You lose out on evasion and poison/disease immunity, but keep unarmoured AC, saves, and some defensive bonus feats. Less good for Int or Cha casters, but still worth looking at.


Sohei is your pick for mounted combat, reach, or switch hitting (with thrown weapons or bow and temple sword) thanks to its ability to flurry with unusual weapons. Weapon Training is a fairly powerful passive boost, especially with gloves of dueling. It also gives you access to advanced weapon training and weapon mastery feats. For a thrown build, for example, you can take Focus Weapon and Trained Throw for damage increase and Ricochet Toss to retrieve your weapon. MAD combos that can't afford a good Wis will appreciate light armour, at least at low levels. 


Other melee builds generally want Unchained Monk for superior mobility and strong offense, especially if the second class doesn't already have full BAB. Full-BAB classes have more options. Flowing Monk, Maneuver Master, and Tetori give you better battlefield control through combat maneuvers. Master of Many Styles makes it really easy to use combat styles including Ascetic Style, and unlike most archetypes you keep all your special defenses – it's also relatively easy to pair with other archetypes. Hungry Ghost Monk gives you vampiric powers in exchange for monk resistances, and is especially good with crit-fishing builds.


Wis Casters should consider Sensei for a support/control build with good use of combat maneuvers, at the expense of mobility. It is compatible with Ki Mystic for extra support at the expense of special defenses. Master of Many Styles is good for defense and mobility with Crane, Snake, Monkey, and Panther Styles. Elven Battle Style is also a good choice for Int-based casters if you don't mind the two prerequisites. Note that having a monk weapon is not significant without flurry – the exception is if you want Ascetic Style.


Note: while monks are generally required to be lawful, you can get around this requirement with the Enlightened Warrior trait (be adopted by aasimar if necessary) or the Martial Artist of Menhir Guardian archetypes. This is useful for gestalting with barbarian or divine casters following Chaotic deities.


Monk // Occultist (Zen Archer, or Unchained Scaled Fist // Silksworn)
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves, 4 skills, 6-level casting, physical Int Wis/Cha
Normally the MAD makes this one difficult, really only decent with the offensively strong Unchained monk or maybe the Zen Archer, since shield-free monks can't use Trappings of the Warrior. However, there is a superior option, and it involves emulating dragons both in martial arts style and taste for bling. The Silksworn giving up armour isn't a problem since you have Cha to AC and Focus, and giving up panoplies isn't a big deal because they're not great choices for the monk. The only downside is that your casting is still Int-based – I recommend getting just enough Int to cast spells and avoiding DCs. Transmutation, Abjuration, Conjuration, Divination, and Illusion are top choices, though you can get a bit of mileage out of Evocation (see: energy ray base power, floating disk, daylight, wall of force) and Necromancy (necromantic servant, soulbound puppet, false life, sands of time, death ward). Note that you can use Legacy Weapon to enhance a monk's unarmed strike.


Monk // Oracle (Unchained Scaled Fist)
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, physical Cha Wis
Ability synergy is key here, and once you're taking Scaled Fist you might as well go unchained because there aren't any other compatible archetypes you'd be interested in. This gives you full BAB, mobility, good defences, and of course full casting. You can build as a caster and use the monk for mobility, defence, and a backup role – the mobility angle is especially nice for healers. Or you could do monk-style combat with oracle providing extra buffing and support. Note that the Monk's Cha to AC doesn't stack with the oracle revelations that allow you to use Cha instead of Dex to AC – but all those revelations that let you summon armour just got really useful.


Monk // (Anti)Paladin (Scaled Fist)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves +, 4 skills, 4-level casting, physical Cha Wis
Pros: Great saves, lots of immunities and special defenses, full BAB with ability to combine Flurry & Smite (with Crusader's Flurry if you prefer your deity's weapon to the monk options), potentially very mobile. Cons: swift action heavy, minimal utility, conflict between heavy armour paladin and no armour monk. Keep in mind:
   * Unchained Scaled Fist for top mobility and Cha synergy. Check out the Ki Guardian power to let you roll saves for allies using your probably-excellent-Grace-enhanced save bonuses.
   * Sohei for mounted: less MAD (Wis 12 is fine) and has Weapon Training and monastic mount to buff a Divine Bond mount. You lose poison immunity (and disease immunity if you swap it out on the paladin side) but your saves are high enough to cope.
   * Zen Archer eliminates the feat barrier to the very effective archer paladin. With Wis to hit and AC, and Weapon Specialization/Smite/Ki Arrows for damage you don't need as much in the way of physical stats or any armor. Priorities: Wis=Cha>Str=Dex>Con>Int. Might be worth Tortured Crusader to make this all Wis-based (see Paladin notes). You lose evasion and poison immunity, but do you care?
   * Kata Master is a neat option if you've got great stats and want to melee. You get a Cha-based pool of panache and some swashbuckler tricks, and give up nothing much. 
   * While it seems logical, I do not recommend Enlightened Paladin. Personal Trial is generally weaker than smite (unless you fight a lot of non-evil opponents) and you have an overlapping UAS damage progression and ki pool. If you're a fan of the archetype consider Enlightened Paladin // Unarmed Fighter.

Weirdo says: As mentioned under the general Paladin comments, I don't generally recommend Tortured Crusader because it's a bad trade in terms of paladin abilities. Zen Archer // Tortured Crusader might be worth it (note added above), but otherwise I’d stick to either minimizing your need for Wis (Sohei) or giving the monk more use for Cha (Scaled Fist, Kata Master).


Monk // Psychic
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, physical Int Wis/Cha
Phrenic strike is a very useful amplification because it lets you deliver a touch spell with an  UAS in the same round as you cast it, instead of needing to carry a charge. For disciplines, self-perfection is a definite winner despite the redundant AC bonus - thematic and gives you a great set of bonus spells with useful abilities for a more martial character. Combine with mutation mind for someone who seeks perfection through pushing their body to the absolute limit, getting strength bonuses and other handy abilities in the process. Overall, I'd recommend Unchained, Zen Archer, reach Sohei, or Sensei, in order of most combat-focused to most casting-focused, with Mutation Mind being useful to the Unchained or Sohei builds. Unchained Scaled Fist is an option if you want a Cha discipline, but it's not a big difference in power.


Monk // Ranger
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves/Fort and Ref, 6 skills, 4-level casting, physical Wis
Good combat base, lots of bonus feats, damage bonus on lots of attacks, good utility, good mobility with the right build. For an unmounted melee character that's Unchained (despite the low Will – Wis will compensate). Mounted on your animal companion, consider Sohei. And then there's Zen Archer. All the Ranger's archery feats are also on the Zen Archer's list, but mostly this means that you'll end up taking Dodge, Mobility, or Combat Reflexes in addition to the archery staples. You can also opt for a more mystic build such as Sensei with the Ranger's full BAB backing you up.


Monk // Rogue (Unchained)
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves/Fort and Ref, 8 skills, no casting, physical Wis
Fully Unchained on both sides, you get a mobile skirmisher with excellent damage potential when combining flurry with sneak attack. Also strong defences, good utility, and some debuffing between monk powers like stunning fist and sneak attack riders. Scout is a good archetype since at level 8 any flying kick is a sneak attack. Also strongly consider the Dimensional Savant feat line, since flanking with yourself lets you make sure you've got sneak attack as long as you have ki. It's a lot of feats, but you're not using them on TWF. Try to pick up Iron Will since your will save will be a bit behind. Ninja also works. You get one shared ki pool instead of two separate ki pools, which makes you more flexible but also likely short on ki. Much less effective without Unchained Monk due to poor accuracy. If you want a different type of Monk, go Monk//Slayer.  For a wild debuffing build with a bit of multiclassing, check out Kazumetsa Raijin’s suggestions here and here.


Monk // Skald
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves, 4 skills, casting


Monk // Slayer
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves/Fort and Ref, 6 skills, no casting, physical Wis Int
Swift action heavy. The big draw over Monk//Rogue is that this doesn't need UnMonk to be effective. A thrown weapons build with Sohei gets neat weapon training tricks (like Trained Throw or easy access to Ricochet Toss) and benefits from thrown weapon style feats. Or try a Master of Many Styles or Maneuver Master, using TWF instead of the flurry you give up.


Monk // Sorcerer (Empyreal or Scaled Fist)
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, physical Wis or Cha
Lots of defences, mobility, full casting, all good. Strong melee potential between the monk base and sorc buffs. Frostbite and Chill Touch are nasty when delivered with a Flurry of Unarmed Strikes. And you've got two options for stat synergy: Scaled Fist Monk for Cha or Empyreal bloodline for Wis.  If you don't want to invest in physical stats you can go Wis and take Zen Archer for ranged damage or Sensei for extra support and combat maneuvers. On the other hand, the bloodline flexibility with Scaled Fist gives the option of taking a more combat-focused bloodline like Orc or Abyssal – or the Eldritch Scrapper archetype which cannot strictly be combined with Empyreal due to both replacing the 9th level bloodline power. (Though a GM with a more lenient interpretation of the wild bloodlines may allow them with such archetypes).


Monk//Spiritualist
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves,  4 skills, casting
Punch people while your stand phantom punches people. Get an Anger or Dedication Focus Phantom, put points in performance dance or acting so you pose with skill, say ORA ORA ORA, and name your stand phantom after a song. Bonus points if the first and last names of your character start with Jo, Gio or a similar sound, no matter their race. Double jeopardy points if you choose your touch spells known inside a theme as your stand’s phantom’s power.


Monk // Summoner (Unchained Scaled Fist, Synthesist)
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves, 4 skills, 6-level casting, physical Cha Wis
Normally not a lot of synergy. The unchained scaled fist gives you a full-BAB partial caster with stat synergy, which is pretty good. Add Synthesist and it gets a bit nuts. Your unarmoured AC will be great. You can also make a really strong teleporting build with Dimensional Assault plus Pounce by level 9 (using the synthesist's Maker's Jump ability). Note that many offensive evolutions are linked to your natural attacks; while it's perfectly effective to use your evolutions for utility, defence, and ability score increases, you may also want to consider taking Feral Combat Training in order to incorporate a single beefed-up natural attack into your Flurry.


Monk // Swashbuckler (Scaled Fist)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves/Fort and Ref, 4 skills, no casting, Dex Wis Cha
As written, Precise Strike works with Flurry, which gives you excellent damage potential. Unfortunately, there aren't many light or one-handed piercing monk weapons, with cestus and bich'hwa being your best options. You can add the unarmed strike to that if you take Snake Style, or daggers if you take both Flying Blade and Far Strike Monk and make a thrown weapon specialist. Slashing Grace does not work with Flurry, so if you want dex to damage you'll need an Agile weapon. You are also hungry for swift actions and have mediocre utility, which makes this combo merely pretty good. Scaled Fist is a moderate improvement for the Cha synergy, but not enough to make the combo great.


Monk // Warpriest (Sohei, Unchained//Arsenal Chaplain)
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves, 4 skills, 6-level casting, physical Wis
Tempting – you have stat synergy, a bunch of combat-focused abilities, and some spellcasting. The enhancement bonus from sacred weapon supplements an expensive AoMF. The Airless Touch blessing of the Void domain works very well with Chokehold or Kraken Throttle – force the air from someone's lungs and then choke them so they are immediately in danger of unconsciousness. However, it has some problems. First, you don't actually have full BAB without Unchained. Second, you're very heavy on swift actions. Third, sacred armor is useless to you if you can't wear armour (it doesn't have language allowing it to affect clothing as if it were armour with a +0 AC bonus). Finally, sacred weapon overlaps with monk's improved unarmed strike damage, which is wasteful. Archetypes make a big difference.
   * Arsenal Chaplain gives you Weapon Training for a great passive bonus and gets rid of Sacred Armor. If you're using UAS, Ascetic Style, or a large weapon, the cap on Sacred Weapon damage won't bother you. 
   * Unchained Monk gives you actual full BAB and d10 HD, with Warpriest patching the poor base Will save. Works well with Arsenal Chaplain.
   * Far Shot Monk gives you a bunch of thrown weapon feats and the ability to flurry with them, which go marvelously with Arsenal Champlain's weapon training (and Trained Throw, and Ricochet Toss). You can even get your sacred weapon damage back with Focused Weapon.
   * Sohei allows you to wear light armour, thus benefiting from sacred armour, and gives you Weapon Training (flurrying with trained weapons). If you take Weapon Focus (UAS) you can make up for the Sohei's stunted UAS damage progression. Divine Commander optionally gives you a mount to use with the monastic mount ability. Good if you're not taking Arsenal Chaplain.
   * Mantis Zealot replaces sacred weapon with sneak attack (less swift action competition), and sacred armor with a thematic uncanny dodge ability (unfortunately still requiring a swift action to activate, which is especially limiting with uncanny dodge, but something is better than nothing). Generally not as good as Arsenal Chaplain, but worth considering.
   * I do not recommend Sacred Fist, which just adds more overlap. It may be worth taking if you want a monk archetype that gives up Flurry: Sensei (lots of Wis, some support), Master of Many Styles (9 bonus style feats, up to 5 styles at once), or Maneuver Master (Flurry of Blows and Flurry of Maneuvers stack). However, note that Sacred Fist, unlike Monk, does not grant a BAB increase while flurrying, which means accuracy is a problem.


Monk // Witch (White Haired Witch, Old SWD, Seducer//Unchained Scaled Fist)
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, physical Int Wis
Witches have a lot of close range or touch effects; the monk makes these easier to deliver with strong defense and mobility. Hex Strike is a good feat. White Haired Witch in particular enjoys some fun maneuver abilities that benefit from monk maneuver training and bonus feats. Tetori is notable for really beefing up your hair's grappling abilities. Note that Feral Combat Training allows you to use your hair as a flurry attack.  As usual, the old Con-based Scarred Witch Doctor makes an excellent melee monster – or combine Seducer with Scaled Fist (Unchained) to switch all your class features to Cha.


Monk // Wizard
Basics:  Med/Full BAB, d8/d10, all saves, 4 skills, full casting, physical Int Wis
This one is about adding durability, mobility, and a physical combat option to the wizard. It fills that function respectably, though I would recommend Kensai//Wizard instead, or even Esoteric Magus//Wizard.
