# Occultist Combinations

Occultist


The occultist is an extremely versatile class, but individual occultists are limited by implement school selection and amount of mental focus. Because Focus is such an important resource, an occultist normally wants more intelligence than spellcasting alone requires, so watch your MAD! Your other class should guide your role and thus implement selection:


If your other class is martial, you want Transmutation for offence, and usually at least one of Abjuration or Illusion for defence (mirror image). Divination's a tough choice – the powers are great, but to get the most out of them you're diverting focus from Transmutation. Definitely take it if you expect to alternate between combat and non-combat days. Otherwise it depends on how much Focus you have and whether you have racial Darkvision. Conjuration's got some good utility, and Side Step. Evocation is fine as a backup option, and the force spells are useful, but it's not high priority. Necromancy is probably a pass - but since the Necromantic Servant shares your BAB and HP it can make a handy extra body in a small party or if you need a disposable flank partner. Enchantment is skippable. Armour doesn't interfere with occultist spells, so feel free to suit up if you get heavy proficiency. Haunt Collector archetype with the Champion is a nice damage buff and is best applied to the conjuration or necromancy schools.


Casters are looking to fill in any holes in their spell lists and for synergistic casting benefits. The latter come from necromancy (undead creation), conjuration (minor duration boost), and evocation (damage boost plus shape mastery). Haunt Collector with the archmage or hierophant spirits can also be a nice boost if you cast a lot of damaging or healing spells. The Mage's Paraphernalia gives you metamagic abilities and expanded access to divination, evocation, and necromancy spells.


Partial casters work a bit better with the Occultist than most other part-hybrids because you can build the occultist with a bit more martial or caster focus than most hybrids. A martial occultist//part caster will want to take the Trappings of the Warrior panoply to get full BAB with one weapon. This can limit your fighting style options depending on how your GM interprets the use of a shield. It also cuts down on your options for utility schools compared to the martial//occultist (see above), but usually the non-occultist part caster has a good amount of utility. A casting occultist//part caster often wants Silksworn as this increases your amount of focus and number of implement schools.


In terms of archetypes, Silksworn is a great pair with unarmoured classes, since you get bonus implement schools and spells per day in exchange for losing the ability to cast in armour. It also gets to add both Intelligence and Charisma to its Mental Focus, which is helpful when gestalting with charisma-based classes (though note you still cast using Intelligence). Similarly, if you gestalt Occultist with something that needs a lot of Wis, you can take Reliquarian for Wis-based focus and keep your Int modest – though in this case consider taking Relic Hunter Inquisitor instead. The Relic Hunter gets at least as many implement schools as the Reliquarian and is entirely Wis-based, though it does sacrifice focus powers and by strict RAW can't take Panoplies (but ask your GM as this may be an oversight). Panoply Savant is also worth a look.




Occultist // Oracle
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and 6-level casting, Int Cha physical?
MAD makes a combat build difficult. Casting works better, especially if you stick to support, utility, and if it's a small party gestalt, minions. Themes:
   * Blasting. Flame and Winter are the best mysteries for this, though Solar also works. Evocation implements and a haunted implement with the archmage spirit add damage. Since metamagic works nicely with blasts, consider Mage's Paraphernalia for Metamagic Secret. Seeker archetype's level 15 ability is also a nice cheap metamagic option since you'll be using your mystery spells a lot.
   * Healing. Life oracles are great healers and the occultist can take that even further – or make a slightly better healer out of an oracle whose main role is elsewhere. The saint's regalia panoply improves Cure spells, and an implement haunted with the hierophant improves all abilities that restore HP (though not fast healing – technically Life Link is not fast healing, but check with your GM). Purge Corruption is a handy early-access condition removal.
   * Undead Master. Prioritize Cha, take Necromancy implements and either the Juju or Bones mystery. Necromantic Servant and Soulbound Doll give you early-game minions and scouts. Necroccultist boosts your Necromancy DCs and gives you access to more necromancy spells from the wizard list. Alternatively, if you're hurting for stats or want more versatility and you can deal with losing armour, take Silksworn. With a lower Int you'll want to pick up spells like spectral hand, death ward, or sands of time with necromancy, and take support/utility schools like transmutation, divination, abjuration, illusion (mirror image, displacement) and conjuration (mage armor, teleportation). Armour of Bones is also a nice revelation for this combo.
   * Battle: Trappings of the Warrior, probably with Panoply Savant for extra focus since your Int will be low. For later implements try Illusion and Conjuration since spells like Mirror Image and Dimension Door will be useful even without much focus – save just a few points for Side Step and Shadow Beast.
   * Utility/Versatility: Silksworn//Lore Oracle. Lots of mental focus, and Cha to AC from Sidestep Secret improves your unarmoured AC. Mental Acuity revelation improves your Int. Has versatile magic from extra implements, plus the arcane archivist and spontaneous symbology revelations. Focused Trance and Think On It give you excellent Knowledge checks, though Lore Keeper is probably skippable since you'll want good Int. Plenty of potential on face skills. 


Occultist // (Anti)Paladin
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will+, 4 skills, 6- and 4-level casting, physical Int Cha
Quite MAD, and Silksworn makes you give up armour. You can still get buffs and utility out of the Occultist, but the Bard//Paladin is more efficient at that and gets better saves to boot.


Occultist // Psychic
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and partial casting, Int and Wis or Cha
Armoured and shielded – or using a two-handed reach weapon - this combination is unusually front-line capable for something with so many magical tools at its disposal. Tricks from both classes synergize well, since phrenic amplifications work on occultist spells and the conjuration and evocation implement powers work on spells cast as a psychic.
   * Build for a secondary melee role. Take Panoply Savant (Warrior), with Abjuration and Transmutation as your starting implements and Trappings of the Warrior at level 2. You now have effectively full BAB, a small assortment of combat buffs, and medium armour. At 6th level and higher, Divination and Illusion are good picks for utility.
   * Casting-focused build. Take Silksworn, along with a Cha-based psychic discipline. Without armour you need to stay out of melee, and your phrenic amplifications no longer work on arcane occultist spells – but that also means your occultist spells don't get shut off by emotion effects, and you get a wider range of magical tricks to play with. This is your best bet if you're short on stats.
   * Intermediate. Keep your armour, take Transmutation as one of your starting schools, and get enough strength to wear the armour and hit something at low levels when spells are limited.  Don't invest in combat going forwards. In the long run, Transmutation will still be useful for defence and support, and armour's nice to have if you end up next to something nasty. Divination can also be used to boost your attack at low levels and provide utility going forwards.


Occultist // Ranger
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, 6- and 4-level casting, physical Int Wis
Pretty well-rounded for combat and utility, and you can cast psychic spells while TWF – though archery is also an excellent choice. Consider Occultist//Slayer if the need for a Wis score is crimping your style, though having access to ranger spells does give you a bit of flexibility with how you get spells such as lead blades or resist energy.


Occultist // Rogue (Unchained Eldritch Scoundrel // Silksworn)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 8 skills, 6-level casting, physical Int
You could make a halfway decent attempt at combat with Trappings of the Warrior, in which case you may want to detour into Illusion at 2 for invisibility and mirror image, and take Divination at 10 for assorted utility and buffs. But that just leaves you with an inferior Occultist//Slayer, so why bother? If you're looking to do something interesting (and halfway effective) with this combo, take Unchained Eldritch Scoundrel with Silksworn and make a sneaky caster build. This gives you a versatile base set of wizard spells, albeit at 6-level progression, to be supplemented with the occultist's best powers. Rays and other weapon-based spells get a bit of extra damage and flexible debuffing with sneak attack + debilitating injury). Ability priorities are Int > Dex > Cha: Int for magic; Dex for initiative, AC, ray accuracy, and weapons combat; and Cha for extra focus and skills. If you've got spare feats, accomplished sneak attacker can make up for some of the lost sneak damage. While you lack the overall power of Rogue//Wizard or Sorcerer, there's a few advantages to this build. First, you're a bit more physically resilient with a good Fort save and access to danger sight and the abjuration school powers. Second, the transmutation implement and divination’s sudden insight make you better at physical backstabbing if necessary as a backup option. Third, some focus powers (eg Mind's Eye and Unseen) are better than comparable full casting options. Finally, being able to grab healing spells from Conjuration may be useful depending on party composition. School priorities:
   * Divination is almost required. You can't sneak attack what you can't see, and Third Eye prevents opponents from hiding in darkness or invisibility – those are your tricks! The perception bonus and Mind Eye are also big boosts to your scouting ability. Sudden insight can help a key attack land (applying debilitating injury to soften them up for the follow-up) or boost an important skill check, and danger sight keeps you safe.
   * Illusions are great for defence and trickery. Unseen helps you get sneak attacks, though as it's expensive to maintain while attacking you'll want another method, such as ninja vanishing trick, darkness, or:
   * Necromantic Servant can act as a flanking partner.
   * Evocation's energy ray is a handy sneak attack delivery device.
   * Transmutation's Dex boost and legacy weapon are strongly recommended for physical backstabbing.
   * Conjuration, as mentioned, gives you access to healing as well as teleportation. Servitor can also create flanking partners, albeit less durable ones than Necromantic Servant.
   * Abjuration and Enchantment are fairly straightforward. The focus powers are fine, but you can grab key spells through Eldritch Scoundrel if you don't want to bother with these schools.


Occultist // Skald
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 4 skills, two 6-level casting, physical Int Cha
Lots of issues. MAD, overly spread out between combat and casting, and Raging Song prevents Occultist casting. Either bard or barbarian works better.


Occultist // Slayer
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, 6-level casting, physical Int
Excellent combat ability between the slayer's base abilities and the occultist's buffing, with lots of utility options on both sides. No somatic components, so you can cast while TWF. Invisibility from illusion implements helps you get sneak attack.


Occultist // Sorcerer (Sage or Silksworn)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and 6-level casting, Cha Int
Sage bloodline lets you go SAD Int. Then take Mage's Paraphernalia, using the Metamagic Knowledge power to gain metamagic feats which you can then use for either class's spells without increasing casting time via the Metamagic Adept bloodline ability – or use the Metamagic Master  ability to use metamagic without increasing spell level (again, as written with either class's spells). The required implement schools (divination, necromancy, evocation) are also pretty good for this combo, with evocation adding another blasting damage bonus (and you can haunt the necromancy implement with the archmage for yet another damage bonus, making this a great blasting build). Of course, most of this works with different bloodlines if you can support two casting stats. Silksworn also adds stat synergy – it cuts off metamagic shenanigans with Mage's Paraphernalia but gives you a lot more flexibility with implement schools.

Occultist // Summoner
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, two 6-level casting, Int Cha physical?
Works well in a small party as a versatile mage with a strong melee companion – otherwise underpotent compared to Bard, Sorcerer, or Bloodrager//Summoner. Toss-up between Silksworn and Mage's Paraphernalia. You can make a combat build with Synthesist, though you have to use a manufactured weapon and mithral buckler to make use of Trappings of the Warrior, which is less than ideal. Summoners are pretty focused as casters, so most of the implement schools add variety:
   * New things – take for:
   * Divination – Scouting and scrying. Summoners get Detect/Read Magic, See Invisibility, Tongues, and True Seeing. 
   * Enchantment – Mind control, especially humanoids. Summoners get charm/hold/dominate monster, Heroism, and a handful of incidentals.
   * Evocation – Blasts, force effects, light. Summoners get only nine evocation spells, including Wind Wall, Wall of Fire, and Wall of Ice.
   * Illusion – Deception. Summoners do get Blur, Invisibility, and Displacement, so if you're just looking for defensive illusions you can skip this school.
   * Necromancy – Single-target debuffs, undead minions, deception... summoners just get seven necromancy spells as of writing.
   * Synergy:
   * Conjuration – The resonant power applies to tons of summoner spells, and getting access to healing is nice. There are also a handful of other spells the summoner doesn't get, such as Create Food and Water, Sepia Snake Sigil, Secret Chest, and Getaway. Servitor is weaker than the summoner's SLA but you can use it while your eidolon is active so it's not a waste.
   * Some overlap:
   * Abjuration – Summoners get most of these but are notably missing Freedom of Movement and Antimagic Field. Overall pretty skippable – though being able to get a resistance bonus while your eidolon also wears a cloak is nice.
   * Transmutation – Summoners get some good buffs but the occultist adds other useful transmutations including: Magic Weapon, Pass Without Trace, Air Step, Darkvision, Make Whole, Magic Vestment, Shrink Item, Stone Shape, Air Walk, Echolocation, Control Winds, Fabricate, Telekinesis, Transmute Mud/Rock, Animate Objects, Disintegrate, Transformation. Better if your eidolon uses a manufactured weapon, which gives you a target for Magic Weapon and Legacy Weapon.


Occultist // Swashbuckler (Inspired Blade)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 4 skills, 6-level casting, Dex Int Cha
Decent martial base, lots of buffs. Dex focus and free feats make it easier to devote resources to casting. Inspired Blade takes it up a notch since you won't need to rely on Cha for panache. Silksworn works OK if you want both Int and Cha to focus and panache. In this case you want to prioritize defence at level 1 with at least two of Abjuration, Conjuration (mage armour), and Illusion, plus Transmutation for offence.


Occultist // Vigilante
Basics: Med/Full BAB, d8, all saves, 6 skills, 6-level casting, physical Int Cha
Avenger gets you access to strong combat talents plus full BAB immediately and consistently, freeing you up to shuffle mental focus around for utility purposes. Casting focus.


Occultist // Warpriest
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, two 6-level casting, physical Int Wis
MAD and with mediocre basics. If you have great stats and take Trappings of the Warrior you can make something halfway decent with it, but really this is trying to do too much. If your GM lets the Relic Hunter take panoplies, use that instead for better stat synergy.


Occultist // Witch
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and partial casting, Int
The Mage's Paraphernalia is a great choice for this gestalt since it gives you access to a broad range of wizard spells. It's also got two good schools for you – Divination has great focus powers, and Evocation gives you some handy force spells that the witch doesn't get. Necromancy is less exciting since the witches get access to the best spells, but they're still pretty good spells and Necromantic Servant ain't bad. Outside the panoply, Illusion's got a lot of non-witch spells and Transmutation and Abjuration each have a few biggies (e.g. Haste, Magic Circle, Freedom of Movement). Conjuration and Enchantment are skippable as most of the good spells are also on the witch list.


Occultist // Wizard
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and partial casting, Int
Wizard is obviously the magical heavy lifter, but occultist brings improved durability, a couple of synergistic spell boosts, and a few powers that are better than wizard spells – plus the option to take a secondary healing role with conjuration. For boosting spells you've got the resonant abilities on conjuration (stacks with the conjuration school's summoner's charm), evocation, and necromancy (if you're animating undead – in which case you also want the necromancy school or undead subschool), plus a haunted implement with the archmage spirit. You can also take Mage's Paraphernalia for Metamagic Secret – the base power may occasionally be useful if you really need a wizard spell but don't have a wizard slot. Best focus powers belong to Divination, with Mind Eye and Third topping the list followed by Danger Sense. See also necromancy (Necromantic Servant), Conjuration (Side Step), Illusion (Shadow Beast and Unseen), Transmutation (Mind over Gravity, Size Alteration, and Sudden Speed). Enchantment is the most skippable, followed by evocation if you're not planning on doing a lot of blasting. Silksworn is fine, though the versatility isn't as big a deal with wizard on the other side of the gestalt and the opportunity cost on the Haunt Collector and Panpolies is considerable.
