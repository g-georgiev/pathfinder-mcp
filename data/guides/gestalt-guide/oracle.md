# Oracle Combinations

Oracle


As a spontaneous caster with a limited list of spells known, the oracle has a higher level of specialization than most divine casters. Mysteries reinforce this specialization – and thanks to mystery spells, also allow you to specialize in roles not typical of the cleric, such as blasting. As a result, it's fairly easy to find synergies between your oracle's specialty and your second class. However, it's especially important to make sure that your gestalt maintains some versatility. A Fighter//Oracle should make sure their spell selection includes group buffs or summons in addition to personal combat buffs, and a Red Dragon Sorcerer // Flame Oracle should have options other than fire damage. The Spirit Guide archetype can improve versatility, as it gives you a small selection of abilities which you can change from day to day. 


The oracle is also notable for its ability to use Cha instead of Dex for purposes of AC and either CMD or Ref saves, through the Lore (Sidestep Secret/Reflex), Nature (Nature's Whispers/CMD), and Lunar (Prophetic Armour/CMD) Mysteries. These abilities are most useful when your second class is also Charisma-focused (such as paladin) and/or prefers light or no armour (such as sorcerer or swashbuckler), since your Cha modifier is limited by your armour's max dexterity modifier.


Note also that the Oracle, unlike most divine casters, does not provide a good Fort save, so if your second class also has a poor Fort save you will want other ways to boost that defense.


Like the cleric, the Oracle can be built primarily for combat, casting, or as a reach build. 


Combat: You almost always want your other class to have full BAB and no requirement for Int or Wis. Battle Mystery is the obvious choice, though if you want access to a wider variety of revelations consider Ancestor, Dark Tapestry (Many Forms & Wings of Darkness), Metal, Stone, and Flame (Cinder Dance). Wood is a good choice for archers or reach weapon users, and Lunar provides the best combat revelations for a low-Dex build. Consider Warsighted if your chosen mystery has few revelations you are interested in, though note that this will delay your first revelation until level 3 – problematic if it's important to your concept (such as Prophetic Armour) - and you’ll need Extra Revelation for any additional revelations after that.


Reach oracles gestalt similarly to the combat oracle, except they don't necessarily need the second class to have full BAB – just be sure to buff and look out for accuracy-increasing revelations like Wood Bond, Dance of the Blades (Metal), or Spirit of the Warrior (Ancestor). They also have little use for the Dex-replacement revelations since you still need a good Dex for extra AoO. 


Casting: Most mysteries will give you useful stuff, but particularly note Bones and Juju for necromancers (plus other offensive options), Flame or Winter for blasting, Apocalypse for blasting and controlling, and Heavens for ridiculous debuffing. Also keep in mind that the Waves and Winter mysteries’ Freezing Spells revelation provides an excellent debuff that is extra useful when combined with a class that provides save-based cold spells. Lore’s Mental Acuity gives you up to a +5 inherent bonus to Int, which can be very useful for gestalting with Int-based casters like the wizard, though see below, and the mystery is otherwise lacklustre; if you’re really needing to minimize MAD you can also take Sidestep Secret, but as a caster you probably still want Dex for initiative. Casting oracles are particularly likely to run into the low Fort save issue. 


Extended on Lore Mystery: When planning a Lore Oracle / Int caster, keep in mind that the +5 is equal to the increase you’d normally get by putting all of your level-based increases into Int, which more or less gives you level-based advancements to two casting stats at the same rate as a SAD caster. However, you still have to worry about splitting your initial stat allotment and your stat headband budget between the two stats, which means you’re not on the same footing as a SAD caster even after devoting your mystery to this. If your starting stats are generous and versatility is at a premium (for example in a small party) this may still be worth it. Indeed, if you lean into Oracle as a support caster you could potentially use some or all of your level increases and Mental Acuity to increase your Int above normal limits, which is great for DC-focused mages - just make sure your initial stats and expected wealth support enough charisma increases to cast your higher level oracle spells! One last caution: Mental Acuity isn’t available until level 7, so make sure you’ve planned to have acceptable casting stats through level 6.


General: The Time and Wind mysteries are fairly useful all-around with mobility and defensive options, and the Ancestor and Lore mysteries (and the Spirit Guide archetype) are notable for providing all Knowledge skills as class skills. The Life mystery gives you good action economy for healing and is the easiest way for an oracle to handle condition removal.


Oracle // (Anti)Paladin ****
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will+, 4 skills, full and 4-level casting, physical Cha
Do you love charisma? This is your gestalt. Charisma to spells, saves with Divine Grace and attacks with Smite, and AC with the Lore, Lunar, or Nature mystery – Lore also lets you add Charisma to Knowledge checks with the lorekeeper revelation. Alternatively, take the Life Mystery to make a fantastic tank/healer - the peak version of the popular “oradin” multiclass.


Oracle // Psychic *** (Lore ****)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Will, 4 skills, two full casting, Int Cha
I prefer Cleric//Psychic for the better saves and the versatility of prepared divine spellcasting. However, this still has two full casting classes with diverse spell lists, a d8 hit die, and armour. The main advantage over Oracle//Cleric is the Lore Mystery: a nice, thematic Int boost which mitigates MAD, especially if you use the oracle for support casting. For disciplines, Rebirth gives you a useful measure of flexibility in day to day spell selection. Alternatively, Rapport's ability to add Cha to your save a few times a day patches a major weakness and the status effect is useful for a healer.


Oracle // Ranger
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, full and 4-level casting, physical Cha Wis
Well-rounded combo for combat with skills and support.


Oracle // Rogue (Ninja)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 8 skills, full casting, Dex Cha
Strong skills and full divine casting isn’t bad, but defenses aren’t ideal and offense is… tricky. Oracle buffs help, but you’re very reliant for sneak attack for damage and accuracy (since landing a Debilitating Injury - Bewildered is your main accuracy buff). Average BAB isn’t great for TWF progression, either. If you can get sneak attack consistently, then this is green, though again watch your defenses! Otherwise yellow. Strategies for getting sneak attack with this combo:
   * Flanking pet with the Nature or Lunar mystery.
   * Gaze of Flames, either through the Flame mystery or using Spirit Guide channelling the Flame Spirit: lets you see through smoke and fog, which gives you total concealment if you lay down Obscuring Mist and attack from 10ft away using thrown weapons or reach. 
   * Invisibility and then Deeper Darkness spells with Shadow mystery; at 11th level the Pierce the Shadows Revelation gives you the ability to see through your Deeper Darkness spell. Added bonus: lots of sneaky revelations.
   * Invisibility with the Wind mystery: gives you 1 round/level of Greater Invis starting at level 9. Very limited use, but spending a round will at least let you slap your first Debilitating Injury on a target.
Ninja has nice charisma synergy and Vanishing Trick, but also lacks the accuracy boost from Debilitating Injury. Consider also Oracle//Slayer for more reliable combat abilities and a good Fort save, or Oracle//Archaeologist Bard if you want to prioritize casting.


Oracle // Skald
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 4 skills, full and 6-level casting, physical Cha


Oracle // Slayer
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, full casting, physical Cha Int
Well-rounded combo for combat with skills and support. I like the Lunar mystery for sneakiness, Cha to AC, and a flanking buddy.


Oracle // Sorcerer
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Will, 4 skills, two full casting, Cha
Great casting and an assortment of other magical goodies. You want to diversify your spell selection, but also take advantage of abilities like the Orc bloodline's damage boost or the Waves mystery's Freezing spells ability to boost your magic across class boundaries. Saves are a serious weak point – consider Great Fortitude at least, which you can get as a bonus feat from bloodlines such as Draconic, Orc, Abyssal, or Stormborn. Losing armour makes revelations that function like armour more valuable. Benefits especially well from charisma to AC revelations.


Oracle//Spiritualist (Fractured Mind)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and 6-level casting, Cha Wis
Fractured Mind has stat synergy.


Oracle // Summoner
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Will, 4 skills, full and 6-level casting, Cha
Excellent stat synergy and two strong lists for support and battlefield control, with some debuffing potential depending on mystery choice. Can pick up a reach weapon, or enter melee personally as a synthesist, though it's not particularly strong at either. Poor saves make this green instead of blue.


Oracle // Swashbuckler
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Ref and Will, 4 skills, full casting, Dex Cha
Solid option for a melee & support character with access to a lot of buffs to augment the swashbuckler's basic combat abilities. Watch your Fort save.


Oracle // Warpriest
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and 6-level casting, physical Cha Wis
MAD, mediocre basics, second cleric spell list doesn’t add any versatility. Try Oracle//Paladin.


Oracle // Witch ** (Seducer ***)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Will, 4 skills, two full casting, Int Cha
Lack of casting stat synergy is a pain, the oracle and witch tend to have similar access to similar healing and debuffing abilities so versatility isn’t great, and your saves hurt. Lore mystery can get your Hex DCs unusually high but remember that you’ll still need to plan for how to prioritize your stats. Seducer keys witch effects on charisma, leaving you with two fairly potent spellcasting chassis, making this solidly green, though I think the weak saves and less-than-ideal spell list versatility does prevent this from being blue even with stat synergy. Strongly consider charisma to AC revelations or armor revelations.


Oracle // Wizard
Basics:  Med BAB, d8, Will, 4 skills, two full casting, Int Cha
Better spellcasting versatility than Oracle//Witch, but still no casting stat synergy and painful saves. Again, with Lore you can get an edge on your Int-based DCs, which makes combining debuff/control wizard spells with support oracle casting a reasonable strategy. Consider also Oracle//Arcanist.
