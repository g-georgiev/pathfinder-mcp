# Paladin Combinations

Paladin


The paladin's big weakness is out of combat utility - two skill ranks per level, limited class skills, and limited magic mostly focused on combat or healing. It also doesn’t give you a lot to work with in combat style - no bonus feats of any kind pushes the Paladin towards a simple two-hand fighting style, though it can certainly benefit from more complicated styles if the other half supports. Its biggest strength is durability, with excellent saving throws and lay on hands. As a rule, paladin gestalts excellently with Charisma-based casters, whether full or partial. 


However, the Paladin’s high requirement for Cha is limiting when gestalting with Int and Wis based casters (including part casters). Two archetype options can help here. For dwarves, Stonelord trades away most Cha-based features (aside from lay on hands uses), and is generally a solid archetype, though keep in mind that the natural armor bonus and DR/adamantine may overlap with some other class features and note that losing Divine Grace means getting a good Ref save from your second class matters a bit more. Tortured Crusader archetype makes you Wis-based and increases your skill ranks, but also costs you Divine Grace and several support features for not a lot in return, meaning you should think carefully before choosing a Tortured Champion over gestalting with Fighter, Ranger, or Cavalier. 


Paladin // Psychic
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will+, 2 skills, full and 4-level casting, physical Int Cha
A little MAD, but tons of defences and being immune to fear makes it just a little less likely that someone will mess with your emotion components. Since the armour is your main advantage over a Paladin//Sorcerer and you can't really afford the Int for offensive spells, you should probably make martial combat your primary role. Psychic magic adds more diverse support options and some utility for coordinating allies or rooting out wrongdoing – Rapport is a nice choice of discipline.


Paladin // Ranger
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves+, 6 skills, two 4-level casting, physical Cha Wis
Is your campaign going to focus on fighting some specific type of evil enemy, such as demons? Congrats, you killed them. All of them. Even without your offensive buffs, you're still super durable and have decent utility, and combat styles help you do more interesting things with your weapons. Note TWF and archery are great styles for a paladin that can actually afford the feats.


Paladin // Rogue
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves+, 8 skills, casting
Surprisingly strong, with the paladin covering the rogue's combat weaknesses and the rogue providing out of combat utility – plus a good Ref save and Evasion, which with Divine Grace means you laugh at Fireball. If you can Sneak Attack and Smite at the same time you can do massive damage – though check with your GM since some might consider Sneak Attack dishonourable. Consider Ninja to get a few more supernatural abilities powered by Charisma.

Paladin // Slayer
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves+, 6 skills, 4-level casting, physical Cha Int
Durability, bonus feats, and utility. Swift action activation makes using Smite and Studied Target at the same time a bit of a pain, though it's definitely nice to have a combat buff that'll work with any enemy type. Consider using your Divine Bond for a flanking “mount.”


Paladin // Sorcerer
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will+, 2 skills, full and 4-level casting, physical Cha
Such sweet charisma synergy that it compensates for the usual combat caster problems. 
   * It is easiest for Paladin // Sorc to focus on casting and tap Paladin for the defenses, healing, and the ability to swing a sword as a backup option – consider Sacred Servant or Hospitalier for this route. 
   * A reach build is also quite effective, though feats are tight.
   * It's possible to make casting the secondary option if you fill up on utility and pre-combat buffs. The nice thing here is that you'll still have a high enough casting stat to get lots of bonus spells and decent DCs. Since your AC will be low for melee, even with Mage Armour, archery is a good option – consider Divine Hunter for the feat help. Alternatively, Iroran/Enlightened Paladin gives you unarmoured AC, and unarmed strikes can be used to deliver multi-touch spells like Frostbite or Chill Touch to good effect. Consider also switching from Sorcerer to Dragon Disciple for more combat on that side of the gestalt. Eldritch scrapper is a good archetype.

Paladin // Summoner

Thematic combination – a holy warrior with a particularly strong connection with the heavens and the ability to summon angelic allies. Also has good stat synergy, ability to punch face, and lots of options for support. Aura of Justice is nasty with summoned critters.

(Anti) Paladin // Warpriest
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Will+, 2 skills, 6- and 4-level casting, physical Cha Wis
Very MAD, but pretty effective if you have the stats to make it work. A bit heavy on the swift actions, but with full BAB and extra combat feats you shouldn't need more than one or two buffs at a time. Channel energy is basically redundant and Sacred Weapon has a similar function to the paladin's Weapon Bond, so you can play with archetypes that give up these features, like Arsenal Chaplain, Chosen One, Sacred Servant, or Sacred Shield. Stonelord Paladin is an excellent choice if you want to focus on Wis – you trade away everything that uses Cha except for Lay on Hands, and get some decent abilities in return. Note that you do lose Channel Positive and Divine Bond, so if those things are important to you make sure you keep them on the Warpriest end. Lack of skills is a major weakness – Cult Leader is a good patch if your GM doesn't think it's too dishonourable and if you don't mind losing bonus feats.
