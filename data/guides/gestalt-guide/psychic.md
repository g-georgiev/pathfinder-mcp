# Psychic Combinations

Psychic


The psychic is unusual as an int-based spontaneous caster with access to many key arcane buffs and debuffs such as Enlarge Person or Sleep, but without suffering arcane spell failure. This makes them a great pair for full divine casters since you get diversified casting without losing armour. In this case, it's important to pick a psychic discipline that matches your divine class's casting stat. Similarly, Psychics are often better matches for armour-wearing martials than full arcane casters are, though the phrenic pool and discipline place greater than usual stat requirements. Such combinations work best when using spells for utility or pre-buffing – and don't overlook swift action spells like Burst of Speed or Thought Shield. Mutation Mind is potentially a useful archetype for a more combat-oriented Psychic gestalt, though as it grants an Enhancement bonus to Strength it doesn't stack with a magic belt and may be better suited to someone who wants melee to be a backup plan. Unsurprisingly Psychics also generally work well with partial casters, as long as stat requirements are complementary. Phrenic Amplifications will work with psychic spells from another class, which gives you a lot of extra flexibility in how you use them, though it does reinforce your spellcasting vulnerability to emotion effects.


Psychic // Ranger
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, full and 4-level casting, physical Int Wis
Fairly well-rounded. Note that without somatic components you don't need a free hand to cast, so TWF if you so desire. MAD enough that you'll probably need to avoid effects with DCs.


Psychic // Rogue
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 8 skills, full casting, physical Int and Wis or Cha
The rogue doesn't help your Psychic casting, but the Psychic doesn't have enough buffing capacity to push the rogue into martial competence. The sneaky stuff is fun, but you'll do better pairing Psychic with Investigator (for more diverse “casting”) or Slayer (for more martial skill).


Psychic // Skald
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 4 skills, full and 6-level casting, physical (?) Int Cha


Psychic // Slayer
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, full casting, physical Int and Wis or Cha
Well-rounded gestalt with lots of capacity for sneakiness. Make sure to snag Invisibility and Greater Invisibility. To reduce stat requirements you'll either need to avoid effects with DCs or else let your phrenic pool and discipline abilities suffer. Rebirth is a good  discipline – or self-perfection if you're willing to sacrifice first level powers for better bonus spells.  Note that without somatic components you don't need a free hand to cast, so TWF if you so desire. You'll need to be a bit more careful with your swift actions than most Psychic//martials.


Psychic // Sorcerer
Basics: Poor BAB, d6, Will, 2 skills, two full casting, Int Cha
Durability issues, two spontaneous casting classes without a shared casting stat (but WITH a very similar spell list), limited synergy between other features...


Psychic//Spiritualist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, full and 6-level casting, Int and Wis or Cha
Relatively versatile spells for a twice-psychic gestalt since the spiritualist is more cleric-like. Remember you can use Phrenic Amplifications on Spiritualist spells. Fractured Mind works well with Cha-based disciplines.


Psychic // Summoner
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Will, 4 skills, full and 6-level casting, Int Cha
Between the poor saves and significant overlap on spell lists, this is a little underwhelming. Consider Hunter//Psychic for more durability and versatility if you're not picky about the nature of your pet, or Sorcerer//Summoner for more stat synergy and a broader spell list. Still not terrible as long as you pick a Cha-based discipline.


Psychic//Swashbuckler (Inspired Blade)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Ref and Will, 4 skills, full casting, Dex Int Cha
Dex focus and a charisma-based discipline (suggestion: Rapport or Rebirth) keep your stat requirements manageable, especially if you use Inspired Blade to take advantage of the Psychic's intelligence. A little swift-action starved.


Psychic//Warpriest
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 2 skills, full and 6-level casting, physical Int Wis
Warpriest doesn't blend well with full casters, but if you want a warpriest with typically-arcane spells this is the way to do it since you don't have to give up armour and can use your Wis for phrenic pool. You'll have to use Psychic spells for prebuffing or utility, because the swift-action effects that work well for a Fighter//Psychic compete with all the Warpriest's swift actions.


Psychic//Witch
Basics: Poor BAB, d6, Will, 2 skills+, two full casting, Int and Wis or Cha
Fragile. But, does get both spontaneous and prepared casting based on the same stat, and the use of different components for the different class's spells does make it less likely your casting will be shut down. Avoid the Abomination discipline – while thematic, it prevents you from using witch spells.


Psychic//Wizard
Basics: Poor BAB, d6, Will, 2 skills+, two full casting, Int and Wis or Cha
As above, getting two very divergent types of casting that are still Int-based goes a long way to compensating for the fragility of this combination. Pick class-specific and staple spells with Psychic and then diversify with wizard. Any discipline other than Abomination is fine, though Faith deserves a special mention for granting access to healing and other typically divine spells.
