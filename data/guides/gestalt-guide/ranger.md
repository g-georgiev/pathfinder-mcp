# Ranger Combinations

Ranger


Rangers are well-balanced martials, with good combat abilities, lots of skills, and two good saves. They make synergistic and versatile characters when gestalted with full divine casters, and also work well with most partial casters and some psychic classes. Full arcane casters tend to find their focus split more between spells and weapons, but can still use some ranged combat style feats (such as point blank shot and precise shot) with rays, and spells requiring attack rolls do benefit from favoured enemy. Some martial characters also work well with rangers, depending on fighting style and whether the other class needs utility and a good reflex save.


The most useful Combat Styles for most characters are Two-Weapon Fighting, Weapon and Shield, or Archery. Most combat styles have something to offer, but some feat-light styles such as Two-Hand Fighting or Natural Weapons are less useful than taking Archery to switch-hit. Archers can also get extra tricks with Divine Marksman (reliable damage bonus on non-favoured enemies) or for gestalts with a decent Cha Hooded Champion (panache with bows).


If your enemy types are unreliable (and you don't want to rely on Instant Enemy), you can take the Wild Hunter archetype to trade favoured enemy for animal focus, or wild stalker for rage (this also replaces your combat feats and hunter's bond). Similarly, favoured terrain can be replaced with more reliable but minor abilities using Infiltrator or Shapeshifter (though the latter only works for natural weapons builds). Guide replaces your favoured enemy but also relies more heavily on favoured terrain. Alternatively, check out Slayer. If you're worried your animal companion won't keep up with a high-powered gestalt game, try Divine Tracker.


Rangers have a modest need for Wis to cast their spells, but this is usually not a problem. If your point buy is tight, consider a spell-less archetype such as Divine Marksman, Trapper, or Skirmisher. Note Skirmisher does use Wisdom for uses/day, but it may be preferable to not having enough Wis to cast spells.


Ranger // Rogue
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 8 skills, 4-level casting, physical Wis
Sneak Attack, strength-based two weapon fighting, full BAB, and an animal flanking buddy. Plus pretty good utility, despite minimal casting.


Ranger // Skald
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, 6- and 4-level casting, physical Cha Wis


Ranger // Slayer
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 6 skills, 4-level casting, physical Wis Int
Overlap in skills and talents (including combat style access) makes this one less than exciting, though your main combat bonuses are compatible. Try Ranger//Rogue, Ranger//Inquisitor, or Investigator//Slayer.


Ranger // Sorcerer
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, full and 4-level casting, physical Cha Wis
The basics look really good but you suffer from the usual action economy problem for combat casters and you need two positive mental stats on top of whatever you're using for combat – which is probably all of them thanks to going unarmoured.


Ranger // Spiritualist (Ectoplasmatist)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, 6- and 4-level casting, physical Wis
Ectoplasmatist gives you two free magical reach shortswords for TWF. 


Ranger // Summoner
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, 6- and 4-level casting, physical Cha Wis
Saves, utility, and support casting all good. Biggest offensive advantage is archery, or TWF if you get your favoured enemy bonus reliably. OK with other combat styles, but Slayer is better for TWF if you don't get your favoured enemy, Cavalier and Barbarian are better for mounted melee builds, and Paladin and Bloodrager are better for most other builds.


Ranger // Swashbuckler (Flying Blade)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 6 skills, 4-level casting, Dex Cha Wis
The ranger and swashbuckler's melee styles aren't very synergistic, thanks to TWF being incompatible with Precise Strike and with Dex-to-damage generally; deceptive, underhanded, and menacing are basically your only worthwhile melee options. Thrown weapons make better use of the bonus feats. Favoured enemy and utility still make this a decent combination, but consider Fighter or something with more divine casting instead of Ranger.


Ranger // Warpriest
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, 6- and 4-level casting, physical Wis
Lots of bonus feats so you can do some really interesting stuff in combat, and a good assortment of skills and support spells. Rangers don't use many swift actions so the warpriest's buffs aren't hard to use. TWF is a great option thanks to Sacred Weapon damage and the ability to ignore Dex requirements, though other styles are also fine.
