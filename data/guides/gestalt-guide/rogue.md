# Rogue Combinations

Rogue


Animal companions provide a reliable means to flank and thus Sneak Attack. Note the Pack Flanking feat, which allows you to flank with an animal companion that is adjacent or sharing your space (including a mount), thus making it vastly easier to flank. To use it, you will either need a feature that lets you share teamwork feats with your companion, or else a Horsemaster’s Saddle.


Rogue // Skald
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 8 skills, casting


Rogue // Slayer
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 8 skills, no casting, physical Int
You don't get both sneak attacks and there aren't enough good rogue talents to be worth getting them from two halves of the gestalt. Try Rogue//Ranger or Investigator//Slayer.


Rogue // Sorcerer (Ninja)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 8 skills, full casting, Dex Cha
Moderate durability and lots of skills, and you can use sneak attack on spells that require an attack roll. Touch spells mean high accuracy, though you're rolling fewer attacks than most rogues. Some bloodlines have great stealth and infiltration abilities. Ninja’s Charisma-based ki pool gives you an additional set of tricks, though unless your GM lets you combine it with the Unchained Rogue you’ll miss Rogue’s Finesse and Debilitating Injury.


Rogue//Spiritualist (Ectoplasmatist)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 8 skills, 6-level casting, physical Wis/Cha
Ectoplasmatist gives you two free magical reach swordswords for TWF.


Rogue // Summoner
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 8 skills, 6-level casting, physical Cha
The best thing about this combination is that the Eidolon makes flanking easy. Your accuracy is still poor, and you are still fragile. Try Slayer//Summoner.


Rogue // Swashbuckler
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Ref, 8 skills, no casting, Dex Cha
I really want to like this because it's so thematic, but having only a strong Ref save is a big weakness in gestalt, you have no casting, and your combat styles aren't even all that synergistic – the rogue wants to use TWF to get as much sneak attack damage in as possible, but Precise Strike doesn't support TWF. Ninja is a little better thanks to Cha-based ki, but really you should look at Archaeologist Bard//Swash or Investigator//Inspired Blade.


Rogue // Warpriest
Basics: Med BAB, d8, all saves, 8 skills, 6-level casting, physical Wis
OK for TWF, but needs more basic combat competence. Try Slayer//Warpriest.


Rogue // Witch 
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 8 skills, full casting, Int (physical?)
Can't hold up in gestalt combat, the rogue doesn't help your casting, and you can get skills elsewhere. Consider Slayer (if you do want to fight), Alchemist, or Investigator instead of Rogue.


Rogue // Wizard
Basics:  Med BAB, d8, Ref and Will, 8 skills, full casting, Int (physical?)
Arcane trickster is a great concept, but this can't hold up in gestalt combat, the rogue doesn't help your casting, and you can get skills elsewhere. Consider Slayer (if you do want to fight), Alchemist, or Investigator instead of Rogue.
