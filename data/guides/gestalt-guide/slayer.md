# Slayer Combinations

Slayer


Slayers are well-balanced martials, with good combat abilities, lots of skills, and two good saves. They make synergistic and versatile characters when gestalted with various forms of partial or full caster, which add magical options and round out the saving throws. If the second class uses Int, that's a bonus, since slayer talents with DCs are based on intelligence. Psychic casters, especially Int-based, are a great fit, especially if you want to TWF without losing your spellcasting ability. Divine casters and partial arcane casters also generally work well. Slayers also work relatively well as fighter-mages since some ranged combat style feats (such as point blank shot and precise shot) work with rays, and spells requiring attack rolls benefit from studied target and sneak attack. 


Access to combat styles means slayers are a great choice for Two-Weapon Fighting, Weapon and Shield, Archery, or switch-hitting (taking archery combat style). However if you want to use a different fighting style you can always spend your talents on other things, including the Weapon Focus or Weapon Finesse feats. 


Slayer // Sorcerer
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, full casting, physical Cha Int
Well-rounded, and gets some melee abilities from the right bloodline or Eldritch Scrapper. Recommend finesse or reach build since without armour you could use a little defensive help.


Slayer//Spiritualist (Ectoplasmatist)
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, 6-level casting, physical Wis or Cha
Ectoplasmatist gives you two free magical reach swords, which is just plain fantastic. 


Slayer // Summoner
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, 6-level casting, physical Cha Int
Competent in combat, good support and utility. Eidolon or other summons can help flank, Invisibility and Greater Invisibility can also get you sneak attacks. Synthesist's abilities can be very useful, especially pounce if using TWF.


Slayer // Swashbuckler
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Fort and Ref, 6 skills, no casting, Dex Cha Int
Not much point to TWF, since it doesn't work with Precise Strike or most forms of Dex to damage, and a finesse build doesn't benefit as much from skipping the TWF feat prereq's anyway. Works OK as a switch-hitter or thrown weapons build, though Sneak Attack is hard to get with ranged attacks. Utility is pretty good, but you can get that – and better combat boosts – from Bard//Swash.


Slayer // Warpriest
Basics: Full BAB, d10, all saves, 6 skills, 6-level casting, physical Wis Int
Fantastic for TWF, very good for switch hitting, OK for other styles. Heavy on swift actions, but you can use Studied Target as a move action if you need.
