# Sorcerer Combinations

Sorcerer


Bloodline choice is especially important. Combat focused bloodlines like Abyssal, Orc, or Infernal (Pit-Touched) work really well with classes that provide basic combat ability, and combat-focused bloodline feats can help you avoid too many tradeoffs between combat and casting feats. Alternatively, bloodline feats often include Great Fortitude or Lightning Reflexes, which you can use to compensate for poor saves. The Arcana also applies to spells from a gestalt class, which is great if you're gestalting with something that has a more specialized spell list. For example, a Fey Sorcerer//Bard can get a lot of mileage out of the increase in compulsion DC with bard spells, while picking up a more versatile repertoire from the sorcerer list. The Psychic bloodline is also notable since it lets you cast spells in armour. 


Sorcerer//Spiritualist
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 4 skills, casting


Sorcerer // Summoner
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Will, 2+ skills, full and 6-level casting, Cha
Serious enough weaknesses to cap this one at green, despite excellent casting synergy. Note the Celestial bloodline works well if you plan on using summoning spells, but it doesn't work with your Summoner SLAs. 


Sorcerer // Swashbuckler
Basics: Full BAB, d10, Ref and Will, 4 skills, casting
Can't fight and cast at the same time, but otherwise good synergy. In addition to offense, the swashbuckler adds mobility and non-armour defenses such as parry and nimble, which are valuable even if you focus on casting. Though it's a bit awkward with the swashbuckler, you can use reach tactics either by pre-buffing with Long Arm, or playing a Mysterious Avenger with Improved Whip Mastery (granting a 10ft threatened area).


Sorcerer // Warpriest (Empyreal//Sacred Fist)
Basics: Med BAB, d8, Fort and Will, 2 skills, full and 6-level casting, physical Wis Cha
Mechanically, Sorcerer//Paladin is superior in most ways. However, the combination of the Empyreal bloodline and the Sacred Fist archetype gives you some of the same key defensive benefits of the Monk gestalt – like adding your casting stat to AC and greatly improved resistance to Fortitude assaults – while also getting 6 levels of cleric casting. For a character who wants to cast primarily and punch only occasionally, that's a good deal. Empyreal//Unchained Monk is still better if you want to punch more than you cast. Guided Hand may be useful in the long run, but since you can't take it until level 7 you need to plan for the low levels.


Sorcerer // Witch 
Basics: Poor BAB, d6, Will, 2 skills+, two full casting, Int Cha
Fragile fragile fragile. The Sage bloodline makes this SAD but you've still got terrible HD and saves.


Sorcerer // Wizard
Basics:  Poor BAB, d6, Will, 2 skills+, two full casting, Int Cha
I know many people like this one, but I really don't think that getting access to the same spell list twice – even a very good spell list – is worth having the worst HD and saves in gestalt. Sage Bloodline makes it more SAD but doesn't help with the core problem.
