# 1st Level

1st Level


Hermean Potential / Ill Omen: Hermean potential and ill omen are two sides of the same coin, allowing allies to reroll a certain number of d20s, taking the higher result (hermean potential) or forcing enemies to reroll a certain number of d20s, taking the lower result (ill omen). In either case, these spells are easily your best-in-class for 1st level, and good candidates for a ring of wizardry (if your GM will allow a psychic caster to have such an item) or a quicken metamagic rod. Quickened ill omen + hypnotic stare + a save-or-suck spell can be a brutal combo if you have a round or two to set it up.


Anticipate Peril: A great, cheap spell for improving your initiative bonus. I’m always a fan of anticipate peril.
Charm Person: A classic for mesmerists, doubly so because of hypnotic stare’s Will save penalty. Feel free to swap out for charm monster at higher levels—the save DC will remain too low as you grow.
Color Spray: A real workhorse at lower levels, color spray drops off a bit in power as you level, a fact that mental potency can’t really do much about. Enjoy it at low levels, though, because the combination of blinded and unconscious is vicious, especially for those focusing on illusion spell save DCs early. A real encounter-ender.
Expeditious Retreat: Always a good self-buff for any level.
Faerie Fire: A classic (and relatively class-exclusive!) spell for eliminating concealment and invisibility, you will never find yourself sorry that you have faerie fire ready at a moment’s notice. Plus, no save!
Grease: An all-purpose battlefield control spell that scales pretty well as enemies get bigger and bigger (and their Acrobatics scores get worse and worse). 
Heightened Awareness: Heightened awareness and anticipate peril can stack to give you some enormous boosts to initiative when you really need them.
Obscuring Mist: Every low-level team needs someone with obscuring mist: it’s a great getaway and battlefield control spell, and if you later get access to ashen path, your entire team can abuse fog and mist tactics.
Ray of Enfeeblement: Pretty hefty penalties, even on a save for half. There are two tactics here: attack the strongest enemies (full martials) who will likely succeed on the save, or attack the weakest enemies (full casters, rogues) who will likely fail on the save. Both have their uses for blunting attack and damage rolls (martials) or for making encumbrance or paralysis a real, immediate problem (casters).
Silent Image: A classic in any illusionist’s repertoire, silent image is notable because enemies only receive a save to disbelieve the illusion if they physically interact with it. Be honest: if your character sees a wall in front of them, do they automatically suspect that it might be an illusion and attack it or toss a rock at it? Probably not. Where silent image is concerned, in many cases illusion might as well be reality.
Touch of Gracelessness: Between ray of enfeeblement and touch of gracelessness, mesmerists have the tools to penalize enemies’ physical ability scores quite effectively at 1st level. Fortitude saves for half (rather than Fortitude negates) will ensure that these spells stay at least somewhat relevant throughout the game.
Vanish: Limited-duration invisibility is exactly what the doctor is always ordering. You’d think she’d order something else once in a while, but nope, always limited-duration invisibility.


Auditory Hallucination: A better save DC than ghost sound, and you can confine the “sound” to the minds of specific enemies, rather than allowing anyone in range to hear (and potentially disbelieve) the sound. Good for intrigue purposes.
Bane: Bane has a lot of weaknesses, notably its Will save to negate and its mind-affecting/fear descriptors, but it’s a great AoE debuff at low levels.
Burst of Adrenaline: This spell would encompass Fortitude and Reflex saves, attack rolls, initiative rolls, etc., and the fatigued condition is quite mild. A decent choice for high-level play.
Comprehend Languages: A classic buff that helps smooth over tense negotiations with other creatures.
Deja Vu: Can be crippling to enemies at low levels if you catch them doing an inopportune action.
Delusional Pride: Not half-bad as a low-level combat debuff, actually, and it stacks with all your other debuffing modes. At higher levels, of course, you have much better options.
Disguise Self: Sure, it’s good enough for what it needs to do. Mesmerists are likely to be sneaky snakes who want to fool people like this.
False Face: Good for retaining a stable of identities that you can swap out to enact your various machinations. I know you’ve got at least a few. Don’t lie.
Hideous Laughter: An early-game save-or-suck spell that benefits from hypnotic stare.
Illusion of Calm: A nice spell for denying enemies attacks of opportunity for common spellcasting activities.
Innocence: A nice bard/skald exclusive that the mesmerist gets access to! Lies told only to convince others of your innocence are a little narrow, but this is a big enough skill bonus that I’m willing to overlook that.
Mental Block: The Will save at the end of each round prevents mental block from attaining blue status, but it’s still a brutal debuff for just about anyone. Heightened mental block can still be a big threat against martials at later levels, too.
Murderous Command: Burns enemies’ actions, fouls up enemies’ positioning, potentially deals damage. I can think of worse 1st-level spells.
Paranoia: Can be a pretty fun spell to tag a bulked-up martial enemy with, watching as they use reach weapons or natural reach to attack their own allies. The debuff also works well with flanking.
Psychic Reading: A good information-gathering spell.
Remove Fear: Extra useful for psychic casters like the mesmerist. Even outside that, remove fear helps your team immensely in some fights.
Shadow Trap: Shadow trap either entangles enemies and keeps them in place or it forces them to burn multiple rounds of actions trying to escape. I’m fine with either outcome.
Telempathic Projection: Charm person is better for direct charm offensives, but telempathic projection can boost skill checks where charm person can’t. It has its niche, to be sure.
Unnatural Lust: The short duration, save bonuses, and Will save to negate ultimately limit what unnatural lust can do, but it’s a great way to get enemies to burn their actions if they fail their saves.
Unprepared Combatant: Sluggishness is one of the better bold stare improvements, and combined with hypnotic stare and unprepared combatant, you can hit enemies with a -3 to Will saves, a -7 to Reflex saves, and a -4 to initiative without much trouble. Best used before a fight starts.
Unseen Servant: A timeless spell, unseen servant, and actually pretty good for triggering traps, serving you tea, etc.
Ventriloquism: Unlike silent image, which must be interacted with in order to be disbelieved, ventriloquism grants an immediate save to anyone who hears you throwing your voice. Enemies likely won’t fall for your puppet stand-up routine, but you never know.


Adoration: A +2 morale bonus to Diplomacy checks isn’t even really worth a 1st-level spell, but it’s functional, at least.
Animate Rope: I like that there’s no save, but you have much better ways to debuff people than animate rope.
Aphasia: Aphasia’s best use is as an anticaster tool, but even with your hypnotic stare’s Will save penalty, casters won’t have much trouble passing their saves when they get the opportunity for a new save each round.
Assumed Likeness: Okay for social encounters in the early game, but the bonuses and Will save don’t scale well.
Beguiling Gift: Interesting for intrigue applications. Offering enemies objects imbued with nodes of blasting is an oldie, but a goodie.
Blurred Movement: Decent for avoiding some attacks of opportunity, but otherwise not very useful.
Bungle: Kind of like an inverse true strike. Unfortunately, the Will save to negate and HD cap make ill omen a better choice in nearly every situation.
Call Animal: Good for nipping low-level animal encounters in the bud, but not much more than that.
Compulsive Liar: Again, very on-brand for mesmerists, but the intrigue applications are probably limited.
Coward’s Cowl: Eh. I think there are easier, more reliable means of getting a +1 to some checks.
Crime of Opportunity: A fun, fun spell for mesmerists to frame a rival with. Could make a good distraction, too.
Demand Offering: Not terrible, but also not great.
Detect Undead: You’ll know whether you’re in a game that would benefit from detect undead.
Fastidiousness: Even thought fastidiousness has basically zero combat uses, it’s one of those quality of life spells that you’d genuinely want if you were a real-life adventurer.
Fool’s Gold (AA): Fool’s gold + node of blasting is a classic spell combo: offer someone a bribe, then watch as they get debuffed through the floor and hit with a bunch of psychic damage. It’s pretty limited otherwise.
Fumblestep: Tengu-only. It might work a few times at low levels, but won’t scale well into the endgame.
Fumbletongue: Between the mind-affecting tag, the short duration, and the Will save to negate, fumbletongue isn’t the world’s most effective anticaster spell. For 1st level, though, it’s not terrible.
Implant Urge: Implant urge is fine for intrigue applications.
Interrogation: I guess it’s a good way to get your hands on some answers when you need them most. It’s evil, though.
Jitterbugs: The debuff is strictly okay.
Linked Legacy: I feel like this is something that GMs mostly allow players to do for free, but if not…?
Polypurpose Panacea: Fun, but not mechanically powerful.
Ray of Sickening: There are plenty of more powerful debuffs available.
Recharge Innate Magic: If you have some spell-like abilities, sure.
Shadowfade: Essentially 1st-level invisibility with some strings attached. Not terrible, but you may as well wait for invisibility.
Sleep: Sleep can end encounters in the first few levels, but its scaling is abysmal, even with mental potency. Lose it at your first opportunity if you do take it.
Sow Thought: Permanent duration on a 1st-level spell is interesting, and while I don’t think you’d be able to use this spell effectively against monsters (the save DC won’t scale well enough) it can remain useful against commoners and other low-level pawns that you want to manipulate and incept at will.


Detect Charm: Sure, why not? People under the influence of compulsion effects can be trouble. Stick with a scroll, though.
Detect Secret Doors: A single scroll or wand with a few charges will let you just wander through a floor of a dungeon, pinging for secret doors. That’s enough.
Diagnose Disease: Ideal as a scroll or wand with a few charges.
Healing Thief: Mesmerists get this spell at a considerable discount, relative to other classes, but even so it’s best as a scroll spell for stealing fast healing or regeneration.
Invigorate: A scroll is all you’d need to keep an exhausted ally functional for one combat.
Magic Aura: A scroll or wand, if it’s anything.
Mindlink: Easily done as a scroll.
Obscure Object: Again, you’d only need a scroll or wand, even if these effects were ever of interest to you.
Remove Sickness: The target cap never scales like remove fear, which makes it a little more appropriate for scrolls and wands.
Share Language: The very definition of a wand/scroll spell.
Speechreader’s Sight: A good scroll if you’re in an intrigue-heavy game.
Thought Echo: As speechreader’s sight—a good scroll for an intrigue game.
Undetectable Alignment: With basically no effects that scale with CL, undetectable alignment is about as good a wand spell as you could hope for. Perfectly functional at CL 1.
Vocal Alteration: Fine as a scroll. You definitely don’t need it as a spell known.


Blend: Elves aren’t terribly good mesmerists, and blend isn’t a terribly good spell.
Burst of Insight: Although burst of insight mimics burst of adrenaline, the dazed condition here is head and shoulders more crippling to you than the fatigued condition in burst of adrenaline. Can’t recommend.
Cause Fear: Cause fear is not eligible for mental potency, which makes it all the worse. Mind-affecting, fear, spell resistance, Will negates, HD cap. This is about as bad as a debuff can get.
Charge Object: I’m having real trouble seeing the point.
Command: Language-dependent, mind-affecting, Will negates, spell resistance, limited action options. Nah.
Compel Hostility: Mesmerists weren’t built to tank hits.
Confusion, Lesser: One target, for one round? Nope.
Decrepit Disguise: What’s the point of disguising an item as a crappy version of itself?
Discern Next of Kin: Why would you…?
Disguise Weapon: Disguising a weapon as a different kind of weapon—now there’s the world’s best idea.
Doom: You have way, way better debuffing options.
Echo: Again, I fail to see the point.
Egorian Diplomacy: If you’re going to allow an enemy a Will save to negate, use charm person instead.
Fool’s Gold (VC): Uhhh...for cheating shopkeeps out of their wares, I guess?
Forbid Action: The ability lasts only one round, which isn’t nearly enough to be worthwhile.
Forced Quiet: Very niche bonuses with a short duration.
Hide Bruises: Truly a poor spell. Can’t think why you’d want this.
Hobble: Saves every round, and you can’t even block 5-foot steps. Blergh.
Hypnotism: I know, I know, you’ve got mental potency. Hypnotism is still an exceptionally bad spell. Use sleep if you want a low-level save-or-suck.
Instant Portrait: In case you need to paint...a picture of someone?
Intensify Psyche: Small bonuses to some skill checks, but some pretty nasty penalties to saves.
Lock Gaze: For PCs without gaze attacks, this seems unneeded.
Magic Mouth: Pretty useless.
Memorize Page: When would you ever need this?
Memory Lapse: An exceptionally short range of wiped memories doesn’t bode well for this spell.
Mirror Mantis: Seems like a lot of hassle to make someone shaken.
Obscure Poison: Don’t use poisons.
Opportunistic Loyalty: Very circumstantial, and a Will save negates, to boot.
Quintessence: What’s the use here?
Reveal Secrets: Is there something here that you couldn’t learn through discern lies or similar questioning methods?
Songbird: Meant to be a bard spell, not a mesmerist spell.
Theft Ward: Not an adventuring spell for fairly obvious reasons.
Wizened Appearance: Highly unnecessary.
