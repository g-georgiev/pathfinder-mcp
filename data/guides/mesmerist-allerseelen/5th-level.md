# 5th Level

5th Level


Mind Fog: A -10 penalty to Will saves is almost impossibly high. Any enemy who fails their Will save on this effect will become silly putty in your hands.


Befuddled Combatant: Save-or-suck effects keyed off of fear auras, on-hit save riders, etc. are some of the biggest threats in high-level Pathfinder. Befuddled combatant can not only shave off a modest amount of damage from an enemy, it can also cripple their save DCs. Add in the Sapped Magic bold stare improvement, and you could easily hit someone with a -7 penalty to all their spell or spell-like ability save DCs in one round. How’s that for protecting the team, eh?
Feeblemind: The spell of choice at 5th level for destroying arcane casters, namely wizards, sorcerers, arcanists, bards, magi, skalds, summoners, and witches. If you suspect that a target you’re facing has class levels in any of those classes, hypnotic stare + feeblemind will put them in the grave.
Mydriatic Spontaneity, Mass: As good as mydriatic spontaneity, which is to say very good. Nauseated for rounds/level is good enough, even not counting the potential blindness.
Persistent Image: All of the good stuff from major image, but with no concentration needed. Good stuff.
Possession: Combat-effective possession, at last. I’m sad that mesmerists don’t get access to greater possession, as occultists do, but them’s the breaks.
Primal Regression: Huge debuffs, especially against spellcasters, and you can follow it up with dominate person/monster to take control of the biggest, dumbest martial enemy on the field, turning them against their allies.
Prying Eyes: An excellent intelligence-gathering spell. You’ll never regret having prying eyes.
Scripted Hallucination: As persistent image.
Shadow Evocation: Still a classic spell for illusionists. Getting access to any evocation spell you want is certainly not something that most mesmerists can do.
Synesthesia, Mass: Another great AoE illusion debuff. Enemies are certainly less effective while this is active.
Unwilling Shield: The expensive material component is worth noting, but otherwise unwilling shield is a great way to punish creatures for daring to attack you. The luck bonuses to AC and saves can also be easily supplemented with the Fate’s Favored trait or Chaldira’s Deific Obedience boons for a +2 to everything.


Castigate, Mass: How many tags can we fit into one spell? Let’s find out with mass castigate. It won’t work all the time, but even one or two enemies cowering for even one or two rounds can make a huge difference. Add in a slayer teammate with Dastardly Finish and Merciless Butchery, and you can just coup de grace enemies before they get a chance to recover.
Cloak of Dreams: The one-round casting time is unfortunate, but it’s a risk-free way to put enemies to sleep as they close with you. And since they’re helpless while asleep, the same combo goes for cloak of dreams as for mass castigate: coup de grace them while they’re helpless.
Dahak’s Release: I’m kind of surprised that dahak’s release doesn’t have the evil tag, considering who Dahak is in the Golarion pantheon. Anyway, this is a fabulous power if you happen to roll 26-75 on the percentile die, but 1-25 and 76-100 will most likely be you or your teammates, especially because you have to be close enough to touch an enemy in order to cast this spell.
Grand Destiny: A nice, long duration helps grand destiny along, although its common bonus type is unfortunate. Hermean potential at 1st level has less control over when the bonuses occur, but is a much cheaper alternative when you need it.
Inflict Pain, Mass: Inflict pain is an awesome 2nd-level debuff spell, but it starts to lose steam here as more enemies become immune to mind-affecting or pain effects. You’ll be looking increasingly for debuffs to saves, too, and mass inflict pain doesn’t debuff those.
Mage’s Private Sanctum: This is probably better left to a full caster, but you’ve got the means if you want them.
Mislead: It’s interesting, to be sure, but see invisibility or true seeing will put you out in the cold awfully quickly. Watch out for those at this level.
Nex’s Secret Workshop: Better than communal nondetection, because it will actually protect everyone for up to 20 hours/day.
Passwall: Great for progressing laterally through dungeons and other structures.
Phantasmal Web: Better than phantasmal putrefaction, but still less effective than mass mydriatic spontaneity.
Psychic Asylum: Such a cool spell. It’s your mind palace! Mesmerists aren’t prepared spellcasters, so that portion of the spell won’t apply, but everything else is top-shelf stuff.
Shadow Walk: It’s fast travel, but for mesmerists. As good as you would expect, although combat-focused spells may take priority. You can buy scrolls if it’s an issue.
Suggestion, Mass: Plenty of uses here, especially against hordes of low-level commoners.


Cloak of Shadows: Evil-aligned, and doesn’t do anything that you couldn’t get with displacement and the improved shadow armor enchantment. I’m inclined to think it’s not worth it.
Crime Wave: Hilarious and fun, but mechanically fairly irrelevant.
Deceptive Redundancy: Eh. Psyching out casters is generally a fool’s errand.
Dream Reality: Kind of like a proactive modify memory. It’s still niche, but potentially useful.
Foe to Friend: A hugely expensive resource, given the effect. I’d say you’re better off with calamitous flailing.
Foster Hatred: More niche attitude manipulation.
Mirage Arcana: Better than hallucinatory terrain, but still pretty limited.
Phantasmal Putrefaction: Mass mydriatic spontaneity is going to be much more effective in most circumstances than phantasmal putrefaction. Two saves each round means that enemies will very rarely be affected by it for long unless you can land it on people as they sit inside a mind fog or something.
Shadow Endurance: If you’re afraid your team won’t be able to help you up from unconsciousness regularly, then take shadow endurance. Otherwise, you’re simply denying your team the opportunity to help you.
Subjective Reality: I think I prefer shadowform to subjective reality, but it can help you to tank one creature while you focus on others.
Vengeful Outrage: At minutes/level, targets won’t have much time to find their objects of vengeance, much less engage them in combat. Dunno. I can’t see it working all that often.
Waves of Fatigue: The lack of a save is the only thing saving waves of fatigue from a red rating. The debuff is otherwise mild, and you can’t stack the conditions for exhaustion.


Dream Council: Will work just fine as a scroll.
Dream Scan: As above.
Joyful Rapture: Giving an ally a scroll of joyful rapture for when you’re placed under a non-harmless emotion effect is a good idea. Even though the target section of the spell calls out enemies, it doesn’t appear from the spell description that they’re actually affected.
Mage’s Decree: A scroll would easily suffice here.
Mind Swap: Maybe a scroll? Don’t think this would be good as a spell known.
Object Possession: Still leaves your body a bit too vulnerable to be a spell known.
Psychic Surgery: A very, very good scroll to have on hand. You never know when this could save an ally’s life.
Secret Chest: Fine as a scroll. Or, you know, use a bag of holding.
Seeds of Influence, Greater: Still best as a scroll.
Seeming: For group infiltrations of enemy organizations.


Appearance of Life, Greater: There’s no reason for mesmerists to use this spell, since they have no means of acquiring undead servitors.
Charnel House: A wand of grease would have been ten times cheaper, and just as effective.
Compelling Rant: You can stop reading the spell text as soon as you see “1d4 Wisdom drain.” The rest of the spell isn’t any better, but dealing Wisdom drain to yourself is a non-starter.
False Vision: Expensive material component, non-24-hour duration, and requires concentration to feed an enemy information that they would actually believe. Meh.
Inveigle Monster: Charm monster is better for your purposes.
Major Phantom Object: Again, the point of the creation spells is that they actually create real items. Without that, what’s left?
Nightmare: Only for dreamstalkers.
Phobia: The save DC doesn’t scale for some reason, which makes it more or less worthless in the long run.
Pox of Rumors: What an ineffectual way to smack-talk someone.
Repress Memory: I fail to see how this spell is better than common anti-divination tools.
Symbol of Pain: Ugh. Don’t use the symbol spells. They’re meant for NPCs.
Symbol of Sleep: As above.
Village Veil: So...protecting a little hamlet from roving marauders? At 13th level, you should be able to hunt them down, no sweat.
