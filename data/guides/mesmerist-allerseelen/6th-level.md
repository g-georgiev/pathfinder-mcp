# 6th Level

6th Level


Song of Discord, Greater: Everyone who fails their save turns on their allies and begins ruthlessly attacking them for rounds/level, employing their deadliest spells, attacks, and abilities. Plus you give them a Strength bonus, plus even a successful save gets them to attack for one round!


Euphoric Tranquility: Assuming you can touch someone, pass your spell resistance check, and negate mind-affecting or emotion immunities (e.g., with Psychic Inception), euphoric tranquility is a no-save equivalent of charm person. Great for peeling minions away from hard fights or getting a boss to waste actions while you summon creatures, create battlefield control, or perform other combat actions that don’t directly threaten the target.
Geas: Geas (pronounced “gesh” in Irish dialect or “case” in Scottish dialect, by the way) is a great spell that can compel most living creatures with whom you share a language to obey your orders for weeks, months, or even years at a time. It’s not combat-functional, but if you ever want to stop a creature from betraying you, force it to sabotage its master’s plans, send it on a potentially suicidal mission, etc., geas will do the trick. It’s bloody difficult to remove via magic, too, so enemies can’t even rely on that.
Getaway: A great spell for teleporting the party out of danger, especially because mesmerists don’t normally get access to teleport or greater teleport.
Heretic’s Tongue: An absolutely brutal debuff that will destroy pretty much any spellcaster (but especially divine spellcasters) pending a failed Will save. It’s technically meant only for worshippers of Geryon, though, so...grain of salt.
Irresistible Dance: Fun flavor, and there’s nothing an enemy can do to prevent themselves from dancing for at least one round. Want that great wyrm red dragon to do the Charleston? He’s gonna do the Charleston.
Mantle of Doubt: A pretty great way to disincentivize enemies to attack you, or to punish them if they do. Shaken, save penalties, no flanking or attacks of opportunity, staggered condition, Wisdom and Charisma damage...lots to like here.
Overwhelming Presence: Great debuffs on multiple enemies. Heck, if you can make enough enemies helpless, your teammates can simply walk around coup de gracing people.
Scrying, Greater: Long-term magical espionage is now possible, although most BBEGs will probably take precautions against scrying.
True Seeing: Material component be damned—everyone needs access to true seeing. It simply cuts through too many common high-level spellcaster tricks to ignore.
Uncanny Reminder: It’s a summon spell and a debuff, rolled into one. What’s interesting here is that the creature you summon (a clockwork angel) is CR 15, while summon monster IX caps out around CR 13. The casting time is slow, and it can only be used on living creatures, but it has no mind-affecting tag, which is somewhat unusual for the mesmerist spell list.
Waves of Ecstasy: Stunned, staggered, multiple targets, good effect even on a successful save...I’m a fan of waves of ecstasy. In my mind, greater song of discord is even more devastating, but waves of ecstasy isn’t bad, either.
Waves of Exhaustion: Weaker status effects than waves of ecstasy, but you get a bigger AoE, no save, and no mind-affecting tag in exchange. We’ll call that even stevens.


Charm Monster, Mass: The HD limits are unfortunate, so you’re probably better off using a normal charm monster (heightened to 6th level, if need be) against high-HD targets. Against hordes of weaker enemies, though, this can be a great way to pick up a few disposable meat shields.
Hold Person, Mass: Facing crowds of humanoid enemies would be somewhat unusual at 16th level, but who knows what kind of campaign you’re in? The resource is expensive, given what the spell does, but then again, that’s why it’s not rated blue.
Impenetrable Veil: A fantastic spell that’s held back from a blue rating only by its expensive material component. I don’t think a full caster would want this more than mind blank, but a mesmerist just might.
Insanity: Insanity is fine, but in PC terms, there’s very little difference between an enemy being confused forever and an enemy being confused for rounds/level. I’d prefer a heightened confusion spell in nearly all ways.
Permanent Image: Lower-level spell slots will probably be just fine, even for illusion-specialized mesmerists, but permanent image isn’t an inherently bad spell.
Power Word Blind: The main benefit of the power word spell suite is that they don’t require a failed save to work; the main downside of the power word spell suite is that they only affect enemies who are below a certain HP threshold. Blinded is a strong status condition, and the HP thresholds here are higher than for power word stun or power word kill, but it’s still not what I would call a top-shelf mesmerist spell for this level.
Programmed Image: Great for luring enemies into traps and other effects that you want to proceed without concentration input or advanced timing planning from you.
Triggered Hallucination: Basically the enemy-targeted version of programmed image.


Create Mindscape, Greater: Still somewhat niche. It certainly doesn’t do you any good in combat unless you’re going to focus 100% on instigating psychic duels.
Demand: Will you ever need to use suggestion from this kind of extreme range? Seems like a pretty qualified “maybe.”
Demanding Message, Mass: For large-scale intrigue applications.
Hostile Juxtaposition, Greater: You’re probably going to have to use this spell with the “single target larger than you” option, rather than multiple creatures. It’s fine, but one teleport in exchange for a 6th-level spell seems costly.
Invisibility, Mass: A very expensive spell for something that should be easily duplicated by other spells or items at this level.
Project Image: I can’t think of any fantastic uses for project image, but I’m sure it’s got some. Doesn’t seem broadly powerful enough to merit a spell known in most cases.


Dream Travel: This is as close to class-exclusive as the mesmerist gets, but it’s incredibly niche, especially if you haven’t been focusing on doing anything with dreamscapes once you arrive.
False Vision, Greater: Far better than false vision, but still way too niche to merit a strong recommendation.
Knock, Mass: A scroll would certainly suffice.
Life of Crime: Life of crime is a lot of fun, but it’s not at all functional in combat. 
Phantasmal Revenge: Even if you wanted to cast this spell (which is unlikely) you’d do just fine with a scroll.
Veil: High-powered disguise self, basically. There’s no reason you’d need this as anything but a scroll.


Eyebite: You’re at least 16th level by the time you get 6th-level spells, which means that eyebite will only ever give you the sickened condition as a standard action, with a Fortitude save to negate. That’s not a good deal, especially as a 6th-level spell. The emotion and pain descriptors further limit your target selection.
Invisibility Bubble, Mass: For when the whole team needs to be invisible...underwater?
Night Terrors: Way too slow for PCs to use on enemies. It’s not a bad NPC spell, though.
Symbol of Distraction: Don’t use the symbol spells. Expensive and mostly ineffective.
Symbol of Persuasion: As symbol of distraction.
What Grows Within: An NPC spell that no PC will ever find themselves using.
