# Aromaphile

Aromaphile


The Basics


        A ghoran-only archetype that focuses on player-based AoE debuffing instead of single-target ranged debuffing with their hypnotic stare. Probably slightly better at debuffing than the base mesmerist, but far less resilient (due to the loss of towering ego) and powerful (due to the loss of painful stare).


        Versatility: +0
        Power: -1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Hypnotic Aroma (Su): Replaces hypnotic stare, painful stare, and towering ego. Oof. I’m not against having every creature within 10 feet be affected by hypnotic stare/bold stare, but to lose painful stare and towering ego is crushing. Plus, creatures who are immune to poison (many of them), don’t breathe (some of them), or choose to hold their breath (anyone who wants to) can just ignore the penalties. Not good.


        (3) Debilitating Aroma (Su): Replaces bold stare. This is probably a net positive for bold stare, since it can be used to lock enemies down for as long as they remain within your aromasphere. Confusing Stare + Blinding Stare for 1 minute? Please and thank you.


        (3) Aromatic Manipulation (Su): Alters touch treatment and replaces mental potency. Aromatic manipulation turns touch treatment into both a buffing and a debuffing tool, and I don’t mind giving up mental potency for that upgrade. Enemies get saves every round to overcome their status conditions (stunned in particular is a brutal debuff) so you may not get many rounds—but against enemies with poor Will saves, you’re probably pretty safe.


Is It Worth It?


        If you want to build a melee range debuffer, I guess it might be. At least ghorans are a good mesmerist race. I still think the loss of towering ego and painful stare is pretty crippling.
