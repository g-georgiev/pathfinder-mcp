# Core Combat Feats

Core Combat Feats


Universal


Deific Obedience: Celestial, Fiendish, Deific, Fey, and Monitor Obediences can be extraordinarily powerful if you get the right combo, and the evangelist prestige class isn’t a bad investment at all for the mesmerist class. And hey, wouldn’t ya know it, I already wrote a 400+ page guide to the Obedience feats! The hyperlink there will take you to the best deities to consider when playing a bard, which is a pretty good proxy for the mesmerist class—high Charisma modifiers, ⅔ spellcasters with ¾ BAB progression, good face skills, enchantment and illusion specialists, etc.
Divine Fighting Technique: Desna’s Shooting Star is probably the strongest DFT for mesmerists, although Sarenrae’s Mercy deserves an honorable mention because of how perfectly suited it is for demoralization builds that use Enforcer.
Flickering Step: Many of the conduit feats are pretty great, and Flickering Step is no exception. What, you can’t use a free dimension door a couple times a day?
Improved Initiative: More than many classes, mesmerists benefit from being able to go first, because it allows them to lay down a brutal AoE debuff before enemies get the chance to react. You’d be well-advised to fit this in somewhere.
Nature Soul / Animal Ally / Boon Companion: The classic three-feat trade for a fully scaling animal companion. You’ve got a good Charisma score for Handle Animal, so perhaps a decent investment.
Step Up / Following Step / Step Up and Strike: I’m not saying you’ll have room to take these feats, only that they’re excellent feats for any non-reach melee combatant who wants to hunt ranged and spellcasting enemies more effectively.
Wanderer’s Fortune: Wanderer’s Fortune effectively eliminates any fear you might have had about being grappled or swallowed whole. Mesmerists have freedom of movement on their spell list, but a bit more never hurts—this is one of the best spells in the game we’re talking about here.


Additional Traits: There are always more traits worth taking out there.
Armor Proficiency, Medium: Mesmerists are in desperate need of better armor options, so this feat is always a choice you can make. In general, though, brief multiclassing is a more highly optimized path to Strength builds and heavier armor proficiencies.
Big Game Hunter: A classic—if boring—passive bonus to hit and damage against larger creatures that isn’t really capable of steering you wrong after about 10th level.
Ghost Slayer: If you don’t want to spring for a ghost touch weapon to deal painful stare damage to incorporeals, Ghost Slayer will do the same thing, although the constant swift action input is unfortunate. I’d probably still stick with ghost touch most of the time, but it’s good to know the option’s here.
Great Fortitude: Static bonuses to saving throws aren’t the sexiest feats to take, but they are always solid choices if you’re casting around for something to do with a feat. As a Fortitude-weak class, mesmerists need all the help they can get.
Improved Critical: Mesmerists will deal most of their damage through painful stare, and precision damage doesn’t multiply on a crit. Some builds will still be interested in Improved Critical, but it’s not nearly as strong in this class as it is in others. The exception might be a Butterfly’s Sting build, which would definitely want Improved Critical or a keen weapon.
Lunge: Especially for reach builds, Lunge can be an excellent feat, allowing you to take oodles of attacks of opportunity against enemies that move toward you.
Noble Scion (Scion of War): Using your Charisma modifier instead of your Dexterity modifier for initiative checks is cool beans, especially if you’re building a spellcasting mesmerist.
Toughness: I mean, yeah, more HP never hurt.
Weapon Versatility: Probably the simplest, cheapest way to get around DR/damage type. That’s not usually penetrable with enhancement bonuses, so the feat is well worth considering.


Blind-Fight: Mesmerists can’t deal their painful stare damage to creatures with concealment (no precision damage to concealed creatures, bro) but even once they get Shadow Strike, they might still need Blind-Fight to help them out. Blind-Fight is easily replaced by select weapon enchantments, though—veering, truthful, heartseeker, etc.
Combat Expertise: I frankly would have preferred that your consummate liar class feature just granted you Combat Expertise as a bonus feat instead of trying to massage it around feinting. In fact, I would prefer this feat didn’t exist at all. Just use Elephant in the Room on this one. Butterfly’s Sting builds will be so much happier.
Critical Focus: Probably useful for mesmerists only as an adjunct to Butterfly’s Sting builds.
Weapon Focus: So many feats depend on Weapon Focus, it’s not even funny. Take it if you need prerequisites out of the way, but otherwise leave it alone. You have enough buffs.




Melee (Strength)


Furious Focus: Power Attack becomes a lot more palatable when you take no penalties on your first attack.
Power Attack: A little less necessary for ¾-BAB classes than for full-BAB classes, Power Attack is still a great feat to have for Strength-based mesmerists. In lieu of self-buffs to offset the attack roll penalties, mesmerists are mostly going to have to use spells to debuff enemies’ AC.




Melee (Dexterity)


Butterfly’s Sting: In out-and-out combat, mesmerists most resemble rogues, and in many cases Dexterity builds will have taken a swashbuckler dip to grab rapier or cutlass proficiency, Weapon Finesse, opportune parry and riposte, etc. Trouble is, crits do next to nothing for classes that deal mostly precision damage! But what if you could give those crits to someone else? Butterfly’s Sting is that feat. Sure, it’s only for Desna worshipers, and sure, the Combat Expertise tax totally bites, but man, is the payoff worth it.
Weapon Finesse: The original melee Dexterity feat par excellence, Weapon Finesse is absolutely required if you plan on being a lithe, lightly armored fighter. A number of simple weapons will work with Weapon Finesse, as will the sword cane or the whip if you’re more of a fancypants.


Combat Reflexes: There’s nothing specifically barring mesmerists from triggering painful stare on attacks of opportunity, although whether you’ll have the reach to take attacks of opportunity consistently is doubtful. Still, potentially worth a buy.
Piranha Strike: Power Attack for light weapon builds. Note that not all the weapons on your list (even those that seem like they intuitively should work with this feat) work with this feat: sword canes, for example, are one-handed weapons that can’t be used with Piranha Strike.
Weapon Focus / [Weapon] Grace: Slashing Grace, Fencing Grace, Starry Grace, etc. are all there for one purpose: to add your Dexterity modifier to damage on your weapon attack rolls. Usually I’d give these feats a blue rating for melee Dexterity builds, easy, but frankly speaking, the damage that a Dexterity-based mesmerist deals should be coming from painful stare + Intense Pain, not from their weapon damage die or a high Dexterity modifier. Mesmerists tend to have a lot of build priorities, and I’d rather you have Compounded Pain, Intense Pain, Blinding Stare, Confusing Stare, and all three instances of Manifold Stare before considering Dexterity-to-damage feats. If you’re fighting to take Weapon Focus and the Grace feats at low levels, you’re not taking your 3rd-level Manifold Stare, Spell Focus, Combat Casting, or any of the other bajillion high-priority feats you need. There’s an opportunity cost to all of this.
Weapon Focus / Whip Mastery / Improved Whip Mastery: Pretty much de rigeur for mesmerist whip builds. Mesmerists won’t need the Greater version for the most part, since combat maneuvers are often a trap for full-BAB classes, much less ¾-BAB classes with no bonus feats.


Accomplished Sneak Attacker: Only enigma mesmerists will have sneak attack dice, but they’ll want this feat, easy.
Agile Maneuvers: I wouldn’t recommend combat maneuvers to many people, least of all mesmerists, but if you’re going to play that way on a Dexterity build, you need Agile Maneuvers.


Amateur Swashbuckler: A 1-level swashbuckler dip is a much better idea.
