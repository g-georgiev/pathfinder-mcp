# Cult Master

Cult Master


The Basics


        A mesmerist who is followed by a slavish cult of followers, each ready to die at a moment’s notice in the service of their master’s cause.


        Versatility: -1
        Power: -1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Insidious Personality (Ex): Replaces consummate liar. Diplomacy is generally a better skill than Bluff for out-of-combat social encounters, but Bluff is great for in-combat feinting. I’ll call it even stevens.


        (1) Fanatical Stare (Su): Replaces painful stare. Losing painful stare is, well, painful, as it comprises a big chunk of your debuffing and damage potential in the long term. What you get in return, however, is a supercharged boost to one person, probably your biggest, dumbest, beefiest fighter—barbarians, cavaliers, paladins, that kind of class. With  attack, damage, and Will save bonuses this big, you’re going to turn one member of your team into an absolute wrecking ball, which may compensate you for the loss of painful stare. Watch out for the competence bonus overlap with a bard’s inspire courage.


        (1) Cult Tricks (Su): Alters mesmerist tricks. I don’t particularly like either mesmerist trick, but they’re not compulsory, so I don’t need to ding this ability.


        (3) False Healing (Su): Replaces 3rd- and 6th-level touch treatments. The fact that you can only use this once per day on a given creature is what really sinks it for me. Doesn’t measure up to touch treatment.


        (7) Faithful Followers (Ex): Replaces 7th-level bold stare. Even assuming your GM is willing to let you have Leadership, you could have just taken this as a feat. Why waste a bold stare—an irreplaceable resource—on what could just been a feat purchase?


        (10) Fanatical Devotion (Ex): Replaces 10th- and 14th-level touch treatments. Well, okay, you can eliminate the penalties from Leadership, or you could simply accept the fact that you’re playing an evil character and get Vile Leadership instead, which rewards you for killing your own minions to keep them in line.


        (12) Masterful Cult Tricks (Su): Alters masterful tricks. Okay, both of these are pretty good; I’d rate either green. Still nice that they’re optional.


        (20) Reborn (Su): Replaces rule minds. Capstone replacements are mostly noteworthy because they prevent you from getting alternate capstones like Perfect Body, Flawless Mind. Reborn isn’t bad, as capstone replacements go, but it’s not the best, either.


Is It Worth It?


        I’m inclined to think not. Fanatical stare is the only part that really interests me, and I’m not convinced that it’s better than painful stare. Everything else could be accomplished with a wand of cure light wounds and the Vile Leadership feat, so it doesn’t seem like giving up touch treatment and one of your bold stares is worth it.
