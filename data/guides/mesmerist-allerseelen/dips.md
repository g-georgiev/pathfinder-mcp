# Dips

Dips


        For new players, “dipping” describes the process of taking some small number of class levels in a different class than the class the player intends to end up in eventually. One-level dips are the easiest, and most players agree that after about three levels you’re not really “dipping” anymore, but going for a truer multiclass. Dipping usually accomplishes a few things—the more things, the better the case for the dip.


   * Gaining class features that aren’t level-dependent. The paladin’s divine grace class feature, for example, simply adds your Charisma modifier to all your saving throws. You don’t need any further levels in paladin to enable the combo, which makes this particular dip attractive for Charisma-based classes.
   * Smoothing away feat prerequisites. Mesmerists are notoriously low on feats, which makes more involved combat builds difficult to achieve. Some dips throw a number of bonus feats at you in the first few levels, which helps you become combat-effective faster.
   * Weapon or armor proficiencies. This is a big one for mesmerists, who by default have access only to light armor and an underwhelming array of weaponry beyond simple weapons. If you want to build a ranged mesmerist, a Strength-based melee mesmerist, or heck, even a good crit-fishing Dexterity-based melee mesmerist, chances are good that you’ll need to dip at least one level.
   * BAB or saves. Mesmerists have ¾ BAB progression and weak Fortitude saves, plus what amounts to a mandate never to fail a Will save against an emotion or fear effect, lest they lose both their spellcasting and their towering ego save bonus. Classes that can shore up one or both of those weaknesses will be highly favored.
