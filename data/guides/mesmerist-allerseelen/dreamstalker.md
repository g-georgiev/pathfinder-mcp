# Dreamstalker

Dreamstalker


The Basics


        A mesmerist who infiltrates the dreams of other creatures—or forces them to fall asleep when they least want to—and then torments them while they lie at rest.


        Versatility: +1
        Power: -1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Dreams of Pain: Alters painful stare. Changing painful stare so that it deals nonlethal instead of lethal damage is...okay. Some enemies are immune to nonlethal damage, so that’s not ideal, but otherwise it’s a valuable skill to have for taking enemies alive and questioning them. There’s unfortunately no synergy here with the Enforcer feat, as Enforcer specifies that you need to deal damage with a melee weapon.


        (1) Somnomancer: Alters mesmerist tricks and replaces 1st-level mesmerist trick. What I love about somnomancer is that it doesn’t lock you into anything—it just guides you gently by the hand over to a huge pile of options and says, “Feel free to take or leave any of that.” The one compulsory part of the ability—the witch’s Slumber hex—is characteristically excellent, and you can then move on to take as many or as few of the other hexes and spells as you’d like. Especially since you later surrender manifold tricks, the easiest and best way to play a dreamstalker is to simply replace most or all of your mesmerist tricks with hexes or bonus spells.


        (2) Sleepless (Ex): Replaces towering ego. Oof. Now that’s a straight downgrade. Towering ego is one of your most valuable class features, and sleepless doesn’t even begin to match up. Fatigue and exhaustion? Immunity to sleep? Nah.


        (3) Touch of Night (Su): Alters touch treatment. In my experience, nausea and fear conditions are more common than exhaustion or sleep conditions, although picking up the ability to remove the staggered condition is quite nice. I’d still call this a net loss.


        (5) Dreamwalker (Su): Replaces manifold tricks. Huh. Interesting. Dreamwalker basically incentivizes you to attack enemies in binary mindscapes, dreamscapes, etc., rather than facing them head-on. Anyone who’s played Book 3 of Ironfang Invasion knows that there’s a particularly nasty pair of assassins that can seriously mess you up with dreamscapes and nightmare, and dreamwalker basically ensures that you rule the roost in the realm of the Sandman. 


        (11) Traumatic Spell: Replaces glib lie. Dreamstalkers don’t do quite as much interaction as other mesmerists, so this is fine by me. Really just another way to keep your enemies terrified, sleepless, and exhausted while you hunt them down in your binary mindscapes.


Is It Worth It?


        The loss of towering ego is definitely the biggest blow to the archetype—if not for that, I likely would have rated it green instead of yellow-green. As it is, it’s not terrible; I can certainly think of occultism-heavy campaigns (Strange Aeons, e.g.) in which dreamstalker would be an amazing archetype.
