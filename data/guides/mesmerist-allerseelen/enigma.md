# Enigma

Enigma


The Basics


        Someone call Edward Elgar, ‘cause we’ve got some enigma variations! Much like the cipher investigator archetype, the enigma mesmerist tricks enemies into completely forgetting that they exist. 


        Versatility: -1
        Power: -1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Solipsism (Su): Alters hypnotic stare. As cool as I think it is that you can force enemies to forget that you’re even there—and eventually work your way up to permanent greater invisibility against one enemy at a time—there are some caveats that bear mentioning. First, constant see invisibility or true seeing are not uncommon among high-level enemies, and your hypnotic stare is still a mind-affecting effect that will require Psychic Inception to work. Second, because your hypnotic stare penalty will never advance beyond -1, you’re incentivized to select bold stares like Disquiet, Nightmare, Restriction, and Unaided that don’t rely at all on a high hypnotic stare penalty. Third, although you will be significantly stronger when you’re under the effects of solipsism (especially after 8th level) your team will be significantly weaker, as no one but you benefits from the effects of your invisibility. I’m inclined to think that this is a bit of a downgrade, but YMMV.


        (1) Veiled Steps: Replaces consummate liar. Between solipsism’s invisibility effect, veiled steps’ bonuses to Stealth, and enigmatic stare’s penalty to Perception, you should be among the sneakiest classes in the game, at least when maneuvering past single enemies. I don’t think this is better or worse than consummate liar—just a different way to play.


        (1) Enigmatic Stare (Su): Replaces painful stare. Bold stare and painful stare are the mesmerist’s two most important class features, and although solipsism is a decent replacement for hypnotic stare, enigmatic stare is emphatically not a good replacement for painful stare. Reminder: painful stare can help you deal 9d6+10 damage on every attack in a round, make enemies almost permanently confused or blinded, and more. Enigmatic stare simply debuffs Perception. Never, never, never.


        (3) Transfer Affliction (Su): Alters touch treatment. The only issue with transfer affliction is that, until 14th level, you need to be actively suffering from a status condition before it’ll do...well, anything. And we generally want to avoid suffering from status conditions. Plus, you can’t even rid yourself of the condition unless the enemy fails their save, which is another potential point of failure. This class feature might sneak up to green at 14th level, when you can replicate the effects of bestow curse (bestow curse be powerful) but until then I’d call it a straightforward downgrade.


        (5) Sneak Attack (Ex): Replaces manifold tricks. Full sneak attack progression (or maybe slayer sneak attack progression) in exchange for painful stare might have been a swap I was willing to call even-stevens, but 4d6 sneak attack damage over 20 levels in exchange for manifold tricks is definitely not. Now, thanks to solipsism, you should be able to proc sneak attack a lot more regularly than a rogue, but you’d need to take a bunch of instances of Accomplished Sneak Attacker in order to boost your damage to normal rogue or even slayer levels. Why go to all this trouble when painful stare could give you equivalent damage and all the debuffing you could want?


        (11) Detection Void (Su): Replaces glib lie. Sure, fine. I’ll call this even.


        (20) Absentia (Su): Replaces rule minds. Without a save, this is actually quite powerful. You can just hang out in the bedroom of the BBEG, watching them eat cheerios in their boxers, and they’ll never even know you’re there.


Is It Worth It?


        I think the best argument for an enigma mesmerist is as an unchained rogue 3 or 4/enigma mesmerist X, where you hop into finesse training, sneak attack, and Accomplished Sneak Attacker early, then essentially treat yourself as a rogue with some spellcasting and the ability to go invisible more consistently than a normal rogue. Grab Bleeding Attack as your rogue talent and the Flensing Strike feat later on for extra fun. As a single-class archetype that you take from 1-20, though, I can’t say I’d recommend enigma very highly—you give up way too much of your core mesmerist functionality for (mostly) inferior options.
