# Face Skills

Face Skills


Betrayer: A fun way to enter into a combat with a “new friend,” although the prereqs are unfortunate.


Axiomatic Discourse: A fun little feat for helping you not alienate people when you fail your Diplomacy checks. Not a priority, but nice to have.


Deceitful: Deceitful is unfortunately a fairly common prerequisite feat, but unless you’re building for those feats, you can skip it. Skill Focus (Bluff) would be more universally useful, since Disguise doesn’t see a lot of play.




Feints


Improved Feint / Greater Feint: Two indispensable upgrades to your feinting abilities, if you’re building a feinting build. Don’t forget that your consummate liar class feature allows you to ignore the Combat Expertise prereq, and that if you’re interested in building a top-shelf feinting build, the vexing daredevil archetype is probably what you’ll want to go with.


Disengaging Feint / Disengaging Flourish: They’re okay feats, but vexing daredevils will generally be able to use the Outmaneuver dazzling feint to achieve a similar effect without surrendering their standard action for the round.


Blistering Feint: Ifrit make decent mesmerists, and the ability to eke out a little more damage on a feint build is welcome. You have so, so many other priorities, though.
Mesmerizing Feint / Greater: I suppose I can understand thematically why feints don’t work well against mindless creatures—I guess they’re just too dumb to react to sucker punches like humanoids?—but even so, two feats just to be able to feint the full range of targets in the Pathfinder bestiaries seems like an unnecessary burden to place on a combat style that’s already not, ya know, ludicrously strong. Not like we’re building a TWF fighter here. Fortunately, only vexing daredevils will really get into hardcore feinting, and they get these feats for free.
