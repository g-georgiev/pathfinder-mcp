# Fey Trickster

Fey Trickster


The Basics


        A mesmerist that gains the hunter spell list and some fey-themed class features.


        Versatility: +1
        Power: -1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Class Skills: Alters class skills. I’d say you come slightly ahead here. Appraise and Profession are useless, generally speaking, and Knowledge (Dungeoneering) is probably the least-used of the monster identification Knowledge skills. Knowledge (Nature) and Survival are pretty good, even if Handle Animal isn’t.


        (1) Nature’s Lure: Alters spellcasting. Hunters actually have one of the best spell lists in the game, although you wouldn’t necessarily think of that class when someone asks you who the best ⅔ casters are. Why? Well, they get every druid spell up to 6th level—and the druid spell list is incredibly strong—as well as every spell on the ranger spell list at an earlier level than the ranger gets! The druid spell list is full of powerful debuffs and battlefield control spells, and the ranger spell list has mostly self-buffs like gravity bow, lead blades, etc. that can instantly turn you into a better attacker. This isn’t necessarily better or worse than the base mesmerist spellcasting, but it is quite different.


        (2) Feytouched (Ex): Replaces towering ego. Oh, jeez. If you want to sit in the towering ego throne, you’d best bring a powerful butt. Feytouched did not bring a powerful butt. Nature’s lure and woodland stride are widely regarded as some of the weakest class features in the game, while towering ego is exceptionally strong. I’m not okay with this swap.


        (3) Fey Veil (Sp, Su): Replaces touch treatment. Most of the fey veils that you receive grant bonuses to skill checks or social interactions. Although these aren’t worthless per se, they are far less workable in combat than touch treatment. I’d call it a downgrade.


        (20) One with the Fey: Replaces rule minds. Most of these abilities are, I would say, more powerful than rule minds. Here are my general ratings:


   * Nymph: For my money, the nymph has the strongest defensive power: adding your Charisma modifier to all your saves and as a deflection bonus to your AC. Especially since this archetype gives up towering ego, if you can reach this point, your saves and AC become nigh-unstoppable. Heck, deflection bonuses even apply against incorporeal creatures!
   * Erlking: Constant haste is a perpetual goal of alchemists with Eternal Potion, and it’s just as good here. The best all-around offensive and defensive capstone you can get.
   * Rusalka: Grabbing four primary natural attacks that can grapple people using both your Strength and Charisma modifiers ain’t half-bad. At the very least, this lets you proc almost all of your painful stare abilities every round.
   * Quickling: The equivalent of the Wind Stance feat, plus evasion and uncanny dodge. It’s not a terrible defensive package, but it certainly doesn’t beat adding your Charisma modifier to all your saves and AC.


Is It Worth It?


        Unless you’re playing at a high enough level to access your 20th-level capstone, the primary reason a person would want to play a fey trickster is to get their hands on the ranger and druid spell lists. I’m hesitant to give it my full-throated endorsement because of the loss of towering ego, but it’s definitely a different style of mesmerist than what we’re used to so far.
