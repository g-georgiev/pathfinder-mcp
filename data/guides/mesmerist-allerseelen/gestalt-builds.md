# Gestalt Builds

Gestalt Builds


Gestalt builds are builds that mash up two classes completely, taking the highest of any overlapping class features; they’re typically used when GMs either want a very high-power game or when there are fewer than four players and the GM needs to balance PC power with enemy power. It’s not usually that difficult to build a good gestalt character, since you’re getting so much power for free, but we can go over some of the things we’d look for in a gestalt pairing:


   * Full BAB or full casting. Gestalt builds are often most powerful when they’re combining two very dissimilar classes: fighter and wizard, sorcerer and vigilante, etc. Dissimilar class pairings enable each class to play to its strengths: the fighter contributes full BAB and a strong Fortitude save, while the wizard contributes full casting and a strong Will save. You can pair mesmerists with other ¾-BAB classes, of course, but you’ll be getting neither higher-caliber spells nor better BAB.
   * Strong Fortitude save progression. Fortitude save effects can be pretty dangerous, so it stands to reason that you’d want a strong Fortitude save.
   * Synergy with Charisma. This just makes good sense: you want to spend attribute points in as few places as possible, hence grabbing a gestalt class that also benefits from Charisma is ideal.
