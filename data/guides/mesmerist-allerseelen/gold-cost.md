# Gold Cost

Gold Cost


Shadow / Slick (CL 5/4, 3,750 gp): As a primarily Dexterity-based class with only ¾ BAB progression, mesmerists need to be able to move about quietly and escape from grapples, pins, swallows, etc. with ease. Shadow and slick armor grant big, big bonuses to your Stealth and Escape Artist scores, especially once you transition into the improved and greater versions of the enchantments. Every mesmerist should have at least the base enchantments at some point. If you’re just concerned with resisting grapples, amorphous armor is also a good alternative.


Deceiving (CL 7, 5,000 gp): Very, very mesmerist. How about if no one ever knew which side you were playing for?
Determination (CL 10, 30,000 gp): You’d hate to need it, and you’d hate to pay for it, but 5d8+10 healing when you get hit with a massive crit might well save your life.
Malevolent (CL 7, 5,000 gp): Bluff for feinting is covered by malevolent armor, and with a Shatter Defenses build, you can easily set yourself up to trigger the attack bonuses on the regular.




+1 Bonus


Defiant (CL 8): The armor equivalent of a bane weapon, and similarly good in campaigns that feature one enemy type to the exclusion of all others.


Spell Storing (CL 12): Can be a good investment if you have a good debuff spell in mind that you want to be able to tag melee aggressors with.
