# Hate-Monger

Hate-Monger


The Basics


        Real meanies who get a favored enemy and boatloads of bonus spells known in exchange for most of their mesmerist trick capacities.


        Versatility: +2
        Power: +1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Alignment: You don’t have to be evil, but you can’t be good. Alright, I can take that.


        (1) Mental Manipulator (Ex): Replaces consummate liar and alters mesmerist tricks. Wow. Wow wow wow. Mental manipulator is an amazing class feature for a few reasons that aren’t immediately obvious unless you’re doing a close reading of the mesmerist spell list. First, this is fundamentally similar to the dreamstalker’s somnomancer ability, but where the dreamstalker gets mostly niche mindscape- and dream-related spells, hate-mongers get handed some of the mesmerist’s most powerful debuffs—for free. This is a big deal! Unlike other ⅔ casting classes, mesmerists have no favored class bonus that allows them to gain new spells known: what you pick, you’re stuck with. Getting this many powerful options added is amazing. Second, mental manipulator gives you free CL and save DC boosts for the spells you pick up from this list, albeit only against the hate-monger’s favored enemy (in the latter case). Anyone notice an unusual addition to the mesmerist spell list here? That’s right! It’s instant enemy, the spell that allows you to change which enemy type or subtype your favored enemy applies to as a swift action. That’s your third big reason for loving mental manipulator: you’ll have more spell slots to use this than a ranger ever would. An amazing class feature.         (3) Insidious Hatred (Su): Alters touch treatment. I could do without insidious hatred, if I’m honest. The few intrigue applications available here don’t compensate for the loss of your swift-action healing.


        (5) Xenophobe (Ex): Replaces mental potency and manifold tricks. Since you’re losing manifold tricks anyway, it behooves hate-mongers to simply give up as many of their tricks as they can—the spells known will be more valuable. Anyway, getting a favored enemy is absolutely aces for the class, especially since you have instant enemy on your bonus spell list from mental manipulator. Once you turn an enemy into a favored enemy, you can get your huge bonuses from the ability itself, as well as the save DC bonus from mental manipulator. A wonderful addition to the class.


        (7) Out for Blood (Ex): Replaces 7th-level bold stare. Bold stares are incredibly valuable resources, so you shouldn’t think about giving one up lightly. Out for blood essentially gives you the Bleeding Stare feat for free, except it doesn’t count against your limit of one stare feat per painful stare proc. Since you get more bleed damage if you attack an enemy yourself, you’re incentivized to make Intense Pain your go-to painful stare rider, at least until you can take Compounded Pain at 13th level. As far as how to structure your bold stare progression, make Psychic Inception your 3rd-level bold stare, as usual, and choose either Disorientation or Sapped Magic at 11th level.


        (11) Insidious Emotions (Su): Replaces glib lie. Not the best ability in the world, but then again, neither was glib lie. All the more reason to go with an enchantment build for hate-mongers.


Is It Worth It?


        Heck yeah! Mesmerist tricks are the biggest loss in the archetype, but the power that you gain from your mental manipulator spells and favored enemy abilities easily override the loss. Overall, a more offensively focused, less support-focused mesmerist.
