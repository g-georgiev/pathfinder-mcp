# Illusion

Illusion


Effortless Trickery: Gnomes get many of the best illusion feats, and Effortless Trickery is a great example. Being able to maintain concentration on a figment as a swift action opens up your action economy in a really nice way, although you do still need to worry about competition from hypnotic stare.


Spell Focus / Greater Spell Focus (Illusion): Illusion is the last school of magic that I would consider getting Spell Focus for, and even though the number of illusion spells that mesmerists have is rivaled only by the number of enchantment spells they have, I still consider illusion a bit of a weaker option. For starters, illusions are rarely capable of turning the tide of battle on their own—they create distractions, waste time or resources, or sow confusion among enemy ranks. Illusion does have some enemy-targeted debuffs, but those are rarer in this magic school than in necromancy or enchantment. Finally, many of your best figment spells offer no saves unless interacted with, making their spell save DCs somewhat less important than in other magic schools.
Threatening Illusion: Another gnome-exclusive feat that lets you flank using your illusions. It does cost you a spell level, though, so I’ve rated it less highly than Effortless Trickery.


Believable Veils: Talk about a bait-and-switch on the short description, eh? Believable Veils is a 1/day +2 to an figment or glamer spell save DC, which is strictly inferior to Mahathallah’s Deific Obedience boon. Sorry, Sivanah. Others just do illusions better than you do.




Necromancy


Spell Focus / Greater Spell Focus (Necromancy): Necromancy is easily the mesmerist’s second-best debuff school, containing spells such as waves of exhaustion, eyebite, possession, enervation, bestow curse, deafness/blindness, and howling agony. I still think I’d prefer enchantment in most cases, but necromancy is also not a poor choice.
