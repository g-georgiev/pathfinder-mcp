# Knacks

Knacks


Detect Magic: Absolutely every spellcaster needs access to detect magic, which is why it’s my pick for your 0-level purple spell. Go and find some traps and treasure.


Dancing Lights: Movable light is good, even if you do need to be concentrating in order to use it. Light is the other illumination cantrip that’s worth your time, so consider getting both in the long term.
Ghost Sound: Since enemies get a Will save to disbelieve your ghostly noisemaking, this cantrip will be of limited use as you increase in level, but it’s incredibly potent in the early game for distracting enemies, creating diversions, etc.
Light: Yup, every adventuring party needs light. Get either dancing lights or light at 1st level.
Mage Hand: A good spell for interacting with things that you’d rather not be standing right next to. The weight/force limit isn’t ideal, of course; if you want to pull mage hand-esque shenanigans, but with a higher weight cap, play an aether kineticist.
Message: Always good for covert communication between teammates.
Prestidigitation: Change the color of poisoned food, clean your filthy body, make portions of a wall or floor dirty or clean to mark danger or give yourself directions in a maze, amuse children...prestidigitation can do a lot if you’re creative, so have fun.


Open/Close: Good for opening doors and chests that you suspect may be trapped.


Detect Poison: Probably the best argument is for detecting poisonous creatures, but that’s pretty niche.
Detect Psychic Significance: Not impossible that this could come into play, just a little unlikely.
Read Magic: Someone else on the team will probably be responsible for reading magic, but it’s not an inherently bad spell.
Touch of Fatigue: Okay at low levels, but doesn’t scale at all due to the low save DC.
Unwitting Ally: Can be useful at low levels for preventing flanks, but the Will save obviates it in the long term.


Bleed: If an enemy is unconscious, they’re not a threat, and someone can always coup de grace them at their leisure.
Daze: Even with mental potency, daze is a bad spell. That Will save will be totally ineffective after a few levels.
Flare: The dazzled condition is weak, and enemies will almost never fail their Fortitude save.
Haunted Fey Aspect: So...it’s going to prevent 1 point of damage...once? That’s incredibly unworthy of a cantrip slot.
Lullaby: Very on-brand for mesmerists, especially since you do get some sleep abilities, but few enemies will fail their Will saves in the long run. If you want to debuff someone’s saves, use hypnotic/bold stare.
