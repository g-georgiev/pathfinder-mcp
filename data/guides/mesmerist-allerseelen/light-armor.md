# Light Armor

Light Armor


Celestial Armor (CL 5, 22,400 gp): If you’re wearing light armor and attacking using Dexterity, celestial armor is the one to angle for in the endgame: huge maximum Dexterity score, minimal armor check penalty, and even a 1/day fly whenever you need it. The AC simply can’t be beaten by other light armors.


Boneless Leather (CL 3, 12,160 gp): Better Escape Artist checks, better CMD for grapple prevention, and even some DR/damage type! What a package.
Calamitous Mail (CL 3, 3,750 gp): A +1 luck bonus to all saves is nothing to sneeze at, especially if you happen to have the Fate’s Favored trait. That trait isn’t particularly good for mesmerists, who don’t have many luck bonuses on their spell list, but heck, I’d be more than happy to take it for this armor alone.
Red Stalker Armor (CL 3, 3,035 gp): Probably the first unique armor that a Dexterity-based melee mesmerist would be interested in, and the competence bonus to Bluff checks made to feint remains relevant throughout your career.


Veiled Armor (CL 3, 4,490 gp): Disguise self and vanish are certainly on brand for the mesmerist, although you’ll have to decide whether those 1/day spell-like abilities are worth the cash.
