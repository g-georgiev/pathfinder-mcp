# Material Manipulator

Material Manipulator


The Basics


        A mesmerist that can become either a transmutation master or an illusion master.


        Versatility: +2
        Power: -1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Manipulator Spells: Replaces consummate liar, manifold tricks, and 2nd-level mesmerist trick. Okay, let’s start with the obvious: receiving access to this many spells off of the wizard and sorcerer spell lists is excellent. The spell list is powerful, and there’s no end to what you can do with these spells—illusion specialists in particular would do well to consider material manipulator as their archetype. There are some points in the negative column, however. Unlike the dreamstalker or hate-monger archetypes, which allow you to trade away tricks for spells known, the material manipulator doesn’t get any bonus spells known; your manipulator spells compete, rather than complement, your base mesmerist spells. You also lose a good portion of your trick capabilities, which reduces the versatility you can bring to the team. If I were going to rewrite this archetype, I’d surrender manifold tricks and consummate liar, then permit you to trade away one trick for two spells known off of the manipulator spell list, same as other archetypes. 


        (3) Revision (Su): Replaces touch treatment and rule minds. I get what they’re going for here, and it’s fine. Revision turns you into a master of disguise, and you can even do some debuffing with it at 10th level once you get access to the age categories. The polymorph effects are probably too little, too late—elemental body I and beast shape II (standard on polymorph) are already quite weak when wizards and sorcerers get them at 9th/10th level, and you don’t get them until wizards and sorcerers have already moved on to 7th-level spells. It’s interesting that clothing and equipment don’t merge with the targets; you could potentially use that to turn someone into, say, an earth elemental, but keep their armor and weapons intact. Anyway, I prefer touch treatment, but YMMV.


Is It Worth It?


        Material manipulator works as a straight power for versatility swap—you get a crack at a bunch of new spells, but you also lose most of your team buffing in mesmerist tricks and touch treatment. Could be a great deal worse, and illusionists and transmutationists will doubtless need to consider this archetype as a contender no matter what.
