# MES 201: Races

MES 201: Races




With our discussion of the mesmerist’s roles and class chassis out of the way, you’ve hopefully made a determination about whether the class is likely to suit the needs you have in your campaign or not. If not, thanks for reading, bon voyage, and have fun out there! If so, it’s time to actually build a mesmerist PC. The first place that many people start when building a PC is their race/ancestry—an important choice, as race/ancestry determines your attribute bonuses, your racial traits, and any favored class bonuses that the class might have. There are a punishing number of racial options available in Pathfinder, so my custom is to review the core races that make up much of Golarion society, then highlight a few less common races that have unexpected perks for the class. It’s not actually that difficult to figure out whether a race is good for the class you’re investigating, though, so I’ll show you what I’m typically looking for:


   * Bonuses to Charisma and Strength/Dexterity. Charisma is one of the mesmerist’s most important attributes, determining everything from your spell save DCs, to the number of tricks you can use per day, to your towering ego bonus to Will saves, to your facility with face skills like Diplomacy and Bluff. Strength and Dexterity, meanwhile, are Pathfinder’s main attack stats, depending on whether you’re crafting a Strength-based melee build or a Dexterity-based Weapon Finesse or ranged build. Dexterity / Charisma pairings are shockingly common among the Pathfinder races, so mesmerists are nicely set up for a wide selection here.
   * Bonus feats. Mesmerists are chronically feat-starved, and even though only a few races offer bonus feats (humans and half-elves are the big ones among the core races) you’ll still want to seriously consider your feat priorities before selecting a race. Humans start to look mighty enticing when you realize how long it takes to progress two more levels to that next feat.
   * Favored class bonuses. Favored class bonuses are bonuses that Pathfinder races can take in lieu of bonus skill ranks or HP at every level. As later additions to the Pathfinder canon, the occult classes never got as many favored class bonuses as other classes, but they still do reasonably well for themselves. There are some common themes among the favored class bonuses: first, bonus tricks per day. Gnomes, halflings, and kitsune all get this favored class bonus; it’s best suited for defensive, support-based mesmerist builds that want to bulk up their allies, and pairs very nicely with the vexing trickster archetype. Second, bonuses to Acrobatics and Bluff checks. This is the elf favored class bonus, and it’s excellent for feinting builds and the vexing daredevil archetype. If you’re looking to make one of those, chances are good that half-elf with an elven curve blade is going to be your best bet because of this favored class bonus. Third, painful stare damage bonuses. Although both dwarves and half-orcs technically get this favored class bonus, the half-orc is the better mesmerist race by a country mile. You can’t really maximize mesmerist damage without this favored class bonus, so get used to seeing some half-orc builds. Fourth, towering ego bonuses. Humans get this favored class bonus, but it’s limited to a +2 total. You should still take it, but it stinks that you can’t scale it for a full 20 levels. Finally, bonuses to CL for enchantment spells for the purposes of spell duration. This one is huge for half-elves, and helps cement their place as one of the best mesmerist classes in the game—certainly the best for enchantment builds. An effective +50% to the duration of all your enchantment spells makes your rounds/level spells much more effective in the low levels, and can boost your days/level spells (charm monster, e.g.) to monstrous durations at later levels.
   * Vision. Mesmerists have both light and dancing lights on their spell list, but having better vision never hurt anyone.
   * Skill bonuses. These bonuses have to be evaluated in light of how useful the skill is. Bonuses to Perception, like the elf and half-elf’s Keen Senses? Pure gold. Bonuses to Appraise or Handle Animal? Poop on a stick.
