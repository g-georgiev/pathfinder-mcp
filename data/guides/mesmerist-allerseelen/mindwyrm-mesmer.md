# Mindwyrm Mesmer

Mindwyrm Mesmer


The Basics


        A mesmerist with breath weapons and some dragon-inspired abilities.


        Versatility: -1
        Power: -2


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Innate Coercion: Replaces consummate liar. I gave the gaslighter’s Bluff-to-Intimidate class feature a green rating, and that would seem fair here, too, if not for the fact that you don’t get any maneuver feats to compensate. Honestly, Intimidate DCs aren’t that hard to hit, so you shouldn’t need a lot of help unless you’re going for a Signature Skill (Intimidate) build or something.


        (1) Phantasmagorical Breath (Su): Replaces painful stare. Ha! No. Painful stare is one of the mesmerist’s most important abilities, not only for damage output but also for debuffing—feats like Blinding Stare or Confusing Stare do a lot to make enemies suffer when they tangle with you. Not only is the damage here weak and keyed to one type of energy damage that you can’t change, a Will save negates all damage, it’s mind-affecting, and you can’t use it more than a few times per day. No, I know which class feature I’d take every time, and it ain’t phantasmagorical breath.


        (1) Draconic Trick (Su): Threatening Mien could be a useful tool for forcing enemies to focus on one ally, making it a pseudo-tanking tool, and since it doesn’t replace anything, I’m fine with rating it green.


        (12) Masterful Draconic Trick (Su): Eesh. Mind-affecting, fear, Will save negates. I get that it makes the attack miss, too, and that can be worthwhile when there are dire consequences other than damage associated with an attack roll, but this is still pretty weak.


Is It Worth It?


        Losing painful stare is, well, too painful, and phantasmagorical breath does a horrible job of replacing it. Heck, losing access to all those stare feats alone is an awful blow that the archetype just can’t recover from.
