# Projectionist

Projectionist


The Basics


        A mesmerist that fills inanimate objects with their consciousness in order to...well, I don’t know what. The uses that this archetype would have are limited, to say the least.


        Versatility: -1
        Power: -1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Spells: Alters spellcasting. Spells with the word “possession” in them are generally quite good, although I would have preferred the spells that allow you to possess a creature, rather than an object. At any rate, this is strictly inferior to normal spellcasting, where you get to choose which spells known you want. Especially because mesmerists don’t have any favored class bonuses that allow them to choose new spells known, this hit to versatility is difficult to stomach.


        (4) Implant Consciousness (Su): Replaces 4th- and 8th-level mesmerist tricks. Psssh. Unless you’re building a mesmerist to be the proprietor of a haunted house business, implant consciousness doesn’t have many uses. More of an NPC ability than anything else.


        (5) Hidden Presence: Replaces mental potency. Mental potency is among the mesmerist’s worst class features, so sure, trade it out for a bonus feat. This is still quite niche, so I’m not going to give it a rating, positive or negative.


Is It Worth It?


        No, definitely not. Projectionist doesn’t give away anything vital from the base mesmerist chassis, but it gives you so little for what you do give up that you’re left wondering, “Why would I bother?”
