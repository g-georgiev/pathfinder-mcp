# Spirit Walker

Spirit Walker


The Basics


        A mesmerist who specializes in controlling the undead.


        Versatility: +1
        Power: +1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Spells: Alters spellcasting. Nothing but upside here: you get the option to learn a few new spells (command undead is probably the best on the list) but are not forced to take them.


        (1) Undead Inception (Su): Replaces consummate liar and mental potency. Undead are probably the most common type of enemies that you’ll face who are immune to many of your spells and abilities, so it’s not intrinsically a bad deal to be able to affect them normally with mind-affecting effects, especially in undead-heavy campaigns like Carrion Crown or Tyrant’s Grasp. Now, oozes, plants, vermin, and constructs will still be immune unless you take Psychic Inception, but those enemies are perhaps rare enough that you don’t need to grab the bold stare. Anyway, I’d call this a great trade for two of your weaker class features.


        (3) Continued Animation (Su): Replaces 3rd- and 14th-level touch treatments. Continued animation can only animate humanoid targets, which makes it somewhat niche, but your “undead” do have some advantages over normal undead: they can cast spells, full-attack, use class abilities, etc., and they can use them as often as you like until the duration expires or they get re-killed. I like it!


        (6) Command Undead (Su): Replaces 6th- and 10th-level touch treatments. I mean, I’ll never say no to free undead minions. Assuming that you attempt to command mostly unintelligent undead (which should be your go-to strategy) you essentially have a pool of disposable meatshields at your beck and call permanently. Make sure you look into some means of creating undead (e.g., animate dead) so that you have the full complement of tools available to you.


        (20) Master of the Dead (Su): Replaces rule minds. It’s rule minds, but for undead creatures. Seems about equal.


Is It Worth It?


        Assuming you’re okay losing touch treatment, I think spirit walker is a fantastic archetype. Undead minions raise the power bar for just about any class you’d care to name, and the ability to use your mind-affecting spells/hypnotic stare on undead with no penalties is a huge upgrade, even over Psychic Inception.
