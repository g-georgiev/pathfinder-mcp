# The Abilities

The Abilities


        (1) Insidious Influence (Su): Replaces consummate liar and 3rd-level bold stare. Insidious influence is a net loss, on the whole. I don’t mind trading away consummate liar, although it seems unlikely that PCs would want to cast save-based spells on their supposed allies—that’s more of an NPC thing. Losing your 3rd-level bold stare is a big deal, though, because that locks you out of Psychic Inception until 7th level at the earliest, and out of Disorientation or Sapped Magic until at least 11th level. This also compromises your ability to trade away bold stares for Manifold Stare at later levels, too, which cuts into your feat progression.


        (2) Power Behind the Throne (Su): Replaces towering ego and mental potency. As I’ve said before, when replacing towering ego you need to make sure that you’re bringing an exceptionally good substitute, and power behind the throne is not that substitute, at least not for PCs. If you’re really concerned about other creatures noticing your casting, I’d say that the Conceal Spell and Improved Conceal Spell feats can do as good a job as power behind the throne without sacrificing one of your most powerful class features.


Is It Worth It?


        For an NPC, maybe. I could see PCs trying to defeat an evil ruler, only to discover that its their advisor who’s really the big threat. I feel like Jafar from Aladdin made this a common way to think about viziers, though, so your players might figure out the ruse quickly. At any rate, this isn’t a good archetype for PCs. You trade away too much for very little benefit.
