Strategy


        The Daredevil build is about as archetypal as you can get: an Errol Flynn-meets-Hannibal Lecter fighter who uses its innate Dexterity, skill with a blade, and uncanny ability to get inside an enemy’s head to dismantle foes piece by piece. There are a few things happening here, so let’s dive in. First, the build has a 1-level swashbuckler dip. It’s an easy way to pick up opportune parry and riposte, panache, Weapon Finesse, and bonuses to your Fortitude save, Reflex save, and BAB, all with minimal penalties down the line. The vexing daredevil mesmerist archetype is going to synergize quite naturally with this chassis, granting us a bunch of bonus feats focused on feinting (Improved Feint, Greater Feint, Greater Mesmerizing Feint). Combined with the half-elf’s Skill Focus: Bluff (or Acrobatics, if you prefer) and the elf favored class bonus granting us bonuses to our Acrobatics and Bluff checks, there should basically be no enemy you can’t outmaneuver and no enemy that you can’t feint, including those typically immune to mind-affecting effects.
        Feinting doesn’t typically allow PCs to take more than one attack (Improved Feint allows you to feint as a move action, but you’re still left with only a standard to attack), so the cornerstone of this build is the Surprise Strike dazzling feint that you’ll receive at 8th level. Sloppy Defense is going to be your go-to for targets with exceptionally high AC, but for most garden-variety targets, you should start with a feint (move action), then follow it up with a Surprise Strike (two attacks at BAB/BAB-5). At later levels you’ll pick up Improved Critical, Critical Focus, the Critical Strike dazzling feint, and the Vision of Blood masterful so that you can regularly stun-lock enemies with no save. I really, really wanted to work Butterfly’s Sting into this build, but it’s that stupid Combat Expertise prerequisite that spoils everything. If your GM is okay with waiving Combat Expertise (or is playing with feat tax system like Elephant in the Room) then you should absolutely pick up Butterfly’s Sting instead of Combat Reflexes at 7th level—the ability to hand out crits to your teammates is way too good to pass up, especially since none of your bonus damage from painful stare multiplies on a crit.
        Now, without any bold stare improvements (notably Psychic Inception, but also Sapped Magic, Sluggishness, etc.) or significant investment in casting feats, this build is going to be pretty terrible at targeting enemies with spells, which is why I didn’t bother to pump Charisma all that high. Instead, you should focus on solid, no-save team buffs or battlefield control spells: displacement, see invisibility, obscuring mist, pyrotechnics, freedom of movement, etc. I’d go so far as to argue that this build really shouldn’t be thought of as a mesmerist, but rather as a swashbuckler with some unusual tricks and spellcasting up its sleeves. I’m not super fond of the feint playstyle myself, but with so many feinting tools given to the class, I felt that I had to devote at least one build to it, and vexing daredevil is unquestionably the right archetype for the job.
