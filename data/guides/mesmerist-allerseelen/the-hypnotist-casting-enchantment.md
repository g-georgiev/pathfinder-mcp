Strategy


        Sometimes I get incredibly tired of how feat-starved the mesmerist class is, and this build is one in which I feel the crunch is particularly restrictive. The cool thing to do in a kitsune enchanter build would be to take as many of the Magical Tail feats as you could, but we can’t do both that and get multiple instances of Manifold Stare, Compounded Pain, Blinding Stare, Confusing Stare, etc., so we choose the Magical Tail route just for a little variety…but that comes with serious tradeoffs. Anyway, we have the barest bones of a combat build here, with rapier proficiency, panache, opportune parry and riposte, Weapon Finesse, and eventually favored enemy to work with. Everything else, however, is dedicated to enchantment spells: the kitsune magic racial trait, Spell Focus and Greater Spell Focus feats, and mental manipulator class features will increase our enchantment spell save DCs by a total of +4, and the Favored Enemy Spellcasting feat can increase our save DCs by a further +2. Since we have instant enemy on the hate-monger spell list, we can grab those bonuses pretty easily even though the archetype only ever gets one favored enemy.
        Beyond just enchantment spells, we’ve obviously leaned quite hard into Magical Tail in this build, with the eventual goal of working up to dominate person, confusion, etc. Although the mesmerist class is not nearly as well-suited for this goal as an oracle with the wrecking mysticism curse, it’s serviceable—and a gestalt kitsune mesmerist + oracle with wrecking mysticism would be an absolute monster of an enchanter, if you ever got the opportunity to play one. If you’re not interested in Magical Tail, there are plenty of other build priorities you could substitute into this build: Combat Casting, Weapon Focus + Fencing Grace, Confusing Stare + Blinding Stare + Manifold Stare + Compounded Pain, or anything else you could want will work instead. I just got a bit tired of writing those builds, you know? Thought I’d try something new.
