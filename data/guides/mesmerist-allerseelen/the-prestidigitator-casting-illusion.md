Strategy


        Taking the Deific Obedience with Mahathallah is getting to be a bit of a trope with me, but I don’t care: where else can you get a +2 to all your illusion spell save DCs in addition to a +2 bonus on saves against mind-affecting effects at 3rd level? Nowhere. It’s ridiculously good. You’d be a fool not to take it. So, let’s detail our bonuses, shall we? The Illusion Obsession trait gives us +1 CL on figment spells, which will be the bulk of what you use in this build—great for bypassing spell resistance and getting better durations on your illusions. Mahathallah’s second obedience boon, persistent illusions, will let your illusions keep going even after you stop concentrating on them, and the gnome-exclusive Effortless Trickery feat lets you sustain concentration on illusions as a swift action, allowing you to juggle anywhere between three and six illusions at once. Varisian Tattoo (Illusion) would also be a good feat for this build for an additional +1 CL. Mahathallah’s basic obedience benefit grants a +2 bonus to the save DCs of any illusion spells you cast, and in conjunction with the faerie dragon magic racial trait (+1), Spell Focus (+1), and Greater Spell Focus (+1), you’re looking at a +5 bonus to your save DCs by 11th level. Paired with hypnotic stare imposing its usual -3 penalty to Will saves and a mesmerizing tattoo (-1), and the effective DC of a 3rd-level illusion spell like major image should be somewhere in the high 20s by 12th level (10 + 3 spell level + 7 Charisma + 1 racial + 4 feats + 3 hypnotic stare + 1 mesmerizing tattoo = DC 29). The median Will save at CR 12 is +10, giving the average enemy a 90% chance of failing their Will save. Slap on Persistent Spell at higher levels, and you’ve got an even nastier combo on your hands.
        Beyond using figment spells to torment enemies, the rest of the build is quite simple. You should obviously focus on getting your hands on as many debuff spells from the illusion school as possible, including color spray, shadow trap, mad hallucination, oneiric horror, paranoia, phantasmal affliction, haunting mists, twilight haze, silence, greater oneiric horror, synesthesia, impossible angles, mass synesthesia, mantle of doubt, and of course any spell in the shadow subschool (shadow enchantment, shadow conjuration, shadow evocation, etc.). You’ve got mesmerist tricks and touch treatment to occupy your actions when you’re not casting spells, and you should plan on triggering Confusing Stare as often as possible in order to keep the hardiest enemy on the battlefield stun-locked. The fact that Confusing Stare allows you to pick which of two d100 rolls you’d like enemies to use is downright brutal, as it greatly increases the chances you’ll be able to make enemies attack their allies, attack themselves, or just stand there, babbling inanely. Since you have essentially no combat abilities in this build, you’re better off triggering Confusing Stare when allies hit the target of your hypnotic stare—you shouldn’t be doing too much attacking with this build.
