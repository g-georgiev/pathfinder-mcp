Strategy


        The Red Death was one of my two takes (the other being the Sacred Voice) on a pure damage build for the mesmerist. Although this build is probably superior in terms of spellcasting and raw power against the hate-monger archetype’s favored enemy, it’s a little less well-rounded than the Sacred Voice build. It’ll excel in campaigns where you’re fighting one type of enemy to the exclusion of others—undead for Tyrant’s Grasp and Carrion Crown, humanoids (goblinoids) for Ironfang Invasion or humanoids (giants) for Giantslayer, etc. We’re going to start things off with a one-level fighter dip to shore up our Fortitude save, grant us martial weapon and heavy armor proficiency, and set us up with a bonus combat feat. Power Attack + Furious Focus is a pretty nice combo for low-level play, but you could feel free to swap out Furious Focus for Spell Focus (Enchantment) if you felt so inclined, since the vast majority of bonus spells you’ll get from your archetype will be enchantment spells. With the advent of xenophobe at 5th level and instant enemy at 8th level, you’ll have all the necessary tools to act like a ranger in most circumstances, although 3rd-level spells are a pretty high-level resource to be burning just to port over your favored enemy bonuses. Still, against bosses and other nuts that are tough to crack, there’s no substitute for suddenly flexing into the huge bonuses that xenophobe + instant enemy can provide. These bonuses, combined with your greatsword, two-handed Strength bonus, Power Attack + Furious Focus, Intense Pain, Manifold Stare, out for blood, and the half-orc favored class bonus should ensure that you’re dealing extremely high damage for a mesmerist, and Psychic Inception + Blinding Stare + Confusing Stare + Compounded Pain is an easy combo in later levels for some low-cost debuffing.
        Of course, the other big draw of the hate-monger archetype (even more than favored enemy, in my eyes) is to trade away your mesmerist tricks for bonus spells. Since you give up manifold tricks and the ability to implant more than one trick at a time, you’re not really going to be missing out by trading away your trick variety, so my recommendation would be just to pick your favorite blue- or purple-rated mesmerist trick at 2nd level (Mesmeric Mirror and Astounding Avoidance work well, since they’re pretty class-agnostic) and then trade away the rest of your tricks for the spells outlined above. You could easily trade away Furious Focus, Power Attack, and Great Fortitude for Spell Focus (Enchantment), Spell Penetration, Varisian Tattoo, etc. if you wanted to build a better spellcaster; that’d be a pretty strong road to walk, thanks to your bonuses to caster level and spell save DCs from xenophobe and all the many bonus spells known you pick up. The combat flow is pretty simple: you find the biggest, baddest enemy, slap them with hypnotic stare and whatever debuffs you feel like, then get in their face and mash with your huge damage numbers. If you really wanted to be mean, you could lean into this build’s chaotic neutral or even chaotic evil nature, worship Shax, take the Fiendish Obedience + Damned Soldier feats, and grab either 5d6+10 sneak attack damage or the ability to heal yourself with every ongoing bleed effect around you. But no, that would be almost comically cheesy.
