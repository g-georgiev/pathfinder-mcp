Strategy


        The Sacred Voice was my second take on a pure damage build for mesmerists, this time leveraging the superior defensive power of the paladin class to create a fairly capable magehunter. Since I mentioned defenses, let’s start there by discussing that divine grace is boss on a mesmerist. Between divine grace, the base saves of the paladin and mesmerist classes, and Sacred Tattoo + Fate’s Favored granting a +2 luck bonus to all your saves, you should be rocking bonuses of about +8/+8/+11 by 4th level. To add to this package, all of your mesmerist tricks are focused on defense, including Astounding Avoidance, Mesmeric Mirror, Gift of Will, Mask Misery, and Slip Bonds, and the Sapped Magic bold stare improvement gained at 9th level will reduce casters’ spell save DCs by 3, further hindering their ability to pose you any threat. You can feel free to focus on defensive spellcasting (displacement, mirror image, animus mine, greater invisibility, see invisibility, echolocation, freedom of movement, etc.) if you like, or else branch out into more offensive debuffs. There’s not huge support for a spellcasting role in the build, but Fortitude effects will generally dispatch enemy casters pretty handily.
        As far as how to kill those spellcasters while you’re weathering their arcane assaults, that’s where the Step Up feat line and the vox archetype come in. Step Up + Following Step + Step Up and Strike is a time-honored magehunting feat line that’s great for getting enemies to stay put. Once opponents realize that you can maneuver around them (Following Step) and take an attack of opportunity (Step Up and Strike) every time they try to five-foot step away, chances are very good that they’re just going to plant themselves, allowing you to full-attack at will. The second half of the equation is to use a readied standard action with wounding words + painful stare + Intense Pain to interrupt spellcasting. By my math, at 15th level a single attack with wounding words will deal 2d6 (greatsword) + 10 (two-handed Strength bonus, assuming 24 Strength) + 6d6 + 7 (painful stare + Intense Pain) + 1d6 + 13 (wounding words), even before factoring items or buffs. The average damage for such an attack is around 62 damage, which makes the concentration check needed to continue casting a spell 10 + Damage Dealt + Spell Level, or 72 + Spell Level. If they step away, you take an attack of opportunity; if they draw and try to use a magic item, you use your attack of opportunity to sunder that item; if they try to cast a spell, you release your readied action, likely dealing massive damage and forcing them to abandon casting. Rinse and repeat until death.
