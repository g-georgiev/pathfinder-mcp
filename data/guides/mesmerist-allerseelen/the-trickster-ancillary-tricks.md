Strategy


        This is the wrong build to play if you’re intending to deal a lot of damage. All clear on that point? Great. This build is the right build if you want to be a sneaky pseudo-caster who’s always got a nasty surprise waiting for enemies...or a nice surprise waiting for allies. The vexing trickster archetype—the core of the build—is the premiere archetype for players who want to do the most with mesmerist tricks, and while there are some difficult sacrifices to be made (towering ego in exchange for Combat Expertise? Really?) the skill bonuses, bonus trick feats, and ability to implant multiple tricks modified with these feats at a single time are all aces for this kind of playstyle. To start with, you should really consider this build as a caster, not a fighter, so I’d recommend your attribute increases go to Charisma, rather than Dexterity, and that you not focus on getting Intense Pain. Instead, just stick with your normal painful stare + Blinding Stare + Confusing Stare + Compounded Pain to lay down intense debuffs on enemies as your allies hit them, toss out buffs or debuffs whenever you think they’re warranted, and otherwise content yourself with being mostly a support caster—a “pocket witch,” if you will.
        The main attractions of the build, of course, are the tricks. I’ve given the build all of the oldies-but-goodies, for the most part, with an emphasis on defensive options. Split Trick is a fantastic first trick feat to have, because it can help you conserve your tricks per day and benefits from your manifold hijinks class feature better than options like Reflexive Trick would. Got two casters on your team who would both be in trouble if they got grappled? Give ‘em both Slip Bonds and make the decision about which one had it once one of ‘em does get grappled! Reflexive Trick at 10th level doesn’t work phenomenally well with manifold hijinks, since it only targets you, but Bouncing Trick is mediocre, you don’t qualify for Spell Trick or Contingent Trick yet, and Swap Trick is more or less redundant once you get Contingent Trick. So Reflexive Trick it is! By this point, you’ll have mirror image, displacement, vanish, Mesmeric Mirror, Astounding Avoidance, Slip Bonds, DR 3/cold iron, and a host of other goodies, so you won’t have to worry much about standing in melee range. Flank with allies, aid another with a benevolent sword cane, eliminate status conditions with touch treatment, give teammates buffs with Combat Advice, swap out Combat Advice for Flagbearer + banner of the ancient kings and buff, or ditch Combat Advice for Feign Curse and make sure enemies never hit your allies again. Anyway, you’ll eventually reach Contingent Trick, which essentially doubles each implanted trick: implant both Cursed Sanction and Free In Body, and they only take up one slot. Pretty nice deal, yeh?
