# Umbral Mesmerist

Umbral Mesmerist


The Basics


        A mesmerist who specializes in using shadow conjuration-esque abilities to emulate summon monster. It’s quite atrocious.


        Versatility: -1
        Power: -2


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Diminished Spellcasting: Alters spellcasting. Well, that’s transparently bad. Shouldn’t need to explain this one much.


        (1) Shadow Summoning (Sp): Replaces 1st-, 4th-, 8th-, 12th-, 16th-, and 20th-level mesmerist tricks. Ugh. It’s like someone at Paizo looked at the summoner’s abilities and said, “How can we make everything here worse?” So, let’s start with the pros: you can use summon monster as a standard action, and the summons last for minutes/level, just like the summoner class feature. Now the cons: your summons are illusions, which means that enemies with true seeing, blindsight, etc. will instantly recognize that they don’t actually exist. Your summons also have half the HP and AC of normal summons, and you scale up to only summon monster VI by 20th level, rather than summon monster IX. (Celestial dire tigers are probably the strongest summons in summon monster VI, and they’ll have an AC of 14 with about 65 HP at 20th level. Womp womp.) Finally, if enemies pass their Will saves to disbelieve, your summons are only 50% real, and will deal 50% damage, have a 50% failure chance on their special abilities, etc. This is a very bad class feature.


        (2) Umbral Solipsism (Su): Replaces towering ego. Okay, ignoring for now the fact that you’re losing one of your best class features, what does umbral solipsism really do? Sure, enemies will be more likely to believe that your illusions are real, but your summons still have low AC and low HP, they still cap out at summon monster VI, and they still can’t effectively damage enemies that get a lucky save and disbelieve their existence. And you traded away towering ego for this ability, you fool.


        (5) Ephemeral Stare (Su): Replaces mental potency. Fair is fair: this is a good archetype ability. Mental potency stinks; ephemeral stare does not. But man alive, is it too little, too late with this archetype.


Is It Worth It?


        Nope. Nope nope nope. There are certain archetypes that do monster summoning well (see, e.g., the inquisitor’s monster tactician archetype); this is not one of those archetypes. Umbral mesmerist is a real kick in the fruitstand to anyone who was hoping for a decent illusion specialist archetype.
