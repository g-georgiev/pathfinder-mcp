# Vexing Daredevil

Vexing Daredevil


The Basics


        Kind of a swashbuckler-meets-mesmerist archetype. It’s fun, it’s flavorful.


        Versatility: -1
        Power: +1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Class Skills: Alters class skills. ‘Twere naught but upside here, sirrah.


        (1) Martial Weapon Proficiency (Ex): Replaces 1st-level mesmerist trick. There are a couple ways you could have gotten this apart from surrendering a mesmerist trick: the Heirloom Weapon equipment trait, a one-level dip in a d10 class, etc. Seems a little unnecessary.


        (3) Bonus Feats: Replaces touch treatment. Basically gives you everything you need for a feint build, other than Mesmerizing Feint and perhaps Disengaging Feint/Disengaging Flourish. And a free stare feat at 14th! That’s fun. You have to be okay losing touch treatment, but I’d call this a worthy replacement: no better, no worse.


        (3) Dazzling Feint (Su): Replaces bold stare. Feint builds typically feint as a move action, which means that you only get one attack per round. This might reduce your damage output from painful stare, especially if you’re picking up multiple instances of Manifold Stare, and you have to weigh whether denying an opponent their Dexterity bonus to AC is worth the extra damage you could potentially do by full-attacking. Additionally, you can only activate one dazzling feint ability at a time, whereas all of your bold stare improvements apply to hypnotic stares. Keep all this in mind. Anyway, here are my ratings:


   * Sloppy Defense: Probably better than Surprise Strike when you need to hit a target with sky-high AC, but might be overkill on weaker targets.
   * Surprise Strike: Ah, there you go. Surprise Strike should be your go-to dazzling feint against most targets, since it essentially gives you two of your iterative attacks and a feint. One instance of Manifold Stare is obviously required here in order to get your painful stare damage on both hits.
   * Blinding Strike: Seems like lots can go wrong here: maybe you miss the enemy’s AC, or you hit AC but miss on concealment, or you hit on concealment but they pass their Fortitude save, or they fail their Fortitude save but have blindsight, tremorsense, lifesense, etc. It’s pretty good, but it could be better.
   * Piercing Strike: Practically speaking, this will translate to a bonus of between 1 and 9 damage. Good, but by no means mandatory.
   * Critical Strike: Could be somewhat useful, I suppose, if you selected rapier, cutlass, kukri, etc. as your proficient weapon and then went on to get keen or Improved Critical. Still, most of a mesmerist’s damage comes from painful stare, and that doesn’t multiply on crits. I think you’re likely to have the same problem as rogues: the classes most able to get crits are the least likely to benefit from them.
   * Outmaneuver: Fine for skirting around large enemies, although a +4 bonus isn’t much when you compare average Acrobatics scores to average CMD scores. Good luck...
   * Combat Maneuver: Remember that enemies are not flat-footed when you feint them, only denied their Dexterity bonus to AC; enemies who have lost their Dexterity bonus to AC can still make attacks of opportunity. Anyway, this is a bad dazzling feint ability. Your Strength is likely to be low, you have ¾ BAB, and you clearly haven’t invested in any feats like Improved [Maneuver] if you’re using this dazzling feint, which means that your CMB will be rock-bottom. Give it up, kiddo.


        (11) Shimmering Body (Sp): Replaces glib lie. Sure, that’s fine. If you’re five-foot stepping regularly, constant blur will help reduce the amount of damage you take. Good stuff.


Is It Worth It?


        Vexing daredevil surrenders much of the mesmerist’s buffing capacity (via touch treatment) and debuffing capacity (via bold stare), but gives you the ability to feint like a boss. If that’s what you’re looking for, then vexing daredevil will deliver, but you should know that you can build a feinting mesmerist just fine without this archetype.
