# Vexing Trickster

Vexing Trickster


The Basics


        A mesmerist who focuses heavily on their implanted tricks.


        Versatility: +2
        Power: -1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Consummate Trickster (Ex): Replaces consummate liar. For my money, I like consummate trickster better than consummate liar. Your Bluff bonus will be nearly as high by the end of the game, but you also an additional +6 bonus to Sleight of Hand, Disguise, and Stealth, all of which make you better at infiltration and general sneakiness than consummate liar would alone. It makes a feint build harder to put together, of course, but mesmerists don’t truly need feint to be good in combat, so long as they’ve retained hypnotic stare, bold stare, and painful stare.


        (2) Trickster’s Ego (Ex): Replaces towering ego. Oh, for cripe’s sake, you can’t trade away consummate liar and then re-gift you the same class feature in exchange for towering ego! Nevertheless, that’s basically what’s happening here. Combat Expertise is almost never a good feat choice, and vexing trickster has no particular synergy with the combat maneuver builds that this would enable. Talk to your GM about whether they’ll let you simply drop this archetype feature—it makes no sense given the rest of the archetype, and unnecessarily punishes the class. Tell ‘em Allerseelen sent you.


        (3) Bonus Feat (Ex): Replaces 3rd- and 10th-level touch treatments. I like being given trick feats for free—you wouldn’t normally have many spare feats to take them, but they’re quite good. Reflexive Trick should almost certainly be your 3rd-level feat, and Spell Trick should almost certainly be your 10th-level feat. In conjunction, they can probably protect you from most of the effects that you would have had to use touch treatment on anyway, provided you know your tricks well enough.


        (6) Manifold Hijinks (Su): Replaces 6th- and 14th-level touch treatments. Split Trick and Spell Trick are probably the best modified tricks you have available, and once again, they can help ensure that you always have the perfect trump card to frustrate your GM’s nefarious designs. This is kind of like playing a prepared caster, except your “spells” are tricks.


Is It Worth It?


        I like vexing trickster, for what it’s worth. Mesmerist tricks can be extraordinarily powerful if used wisely and given a little feat support, but you have to decide whether you want that kind of prepared, proactive playstyle or a more reactive, healer-ish playstyle. Both have their advantages and disadvantages.
