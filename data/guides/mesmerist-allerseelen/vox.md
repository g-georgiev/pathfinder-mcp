# Vox

Vox


The Basics


        A mesmerist who focuses on sonic spells, sonic damage, and the power of their voice.


        Versatility: +1
        Power: +1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Spells: Alters spellcasting and replaces towering ego, 10th-level mesmerist trick, and touch treatment (break enchantment). The spells that get added to your spell list are pretty good, all in all, but the addition of verbal components in addition to the psychic casting components you already have to provide is gnarly. Plus you lose towering ego and a mesmerist trick. I think you come out just a little weaker here, but your opinion may differ. My ratings for the spells are below.


   * 1st
   * Ear-piercing scream
   * 2nd
   * Cacophonous call
   * Piercing shriek
   * Disrupt silence
   * Distracting cacophony
   * Sonic scream
   * 4th
   * Shout
   * Wall of sound
   * Sonic thrust
   * 5th
   * Echolocation
   * Cacophonous call, mass
   * Resonating word
   * 6th
   * Shout, greater
   * Sonic form
   * Magnifying chime


        (1) Compelling Voice (Su): Alters hypnotic stare. Yeah, I’m fine with this. The form factor of your hypnotic stare doesn’t matter that much, so long as you can keep your painful stare and bold stare improvements. Compelling voice satisfies those constraints, so it’s a-okay in my book. No rating here.


        (3) Wounding Words (Su): Replaces touch treatment (minor and moderate). Mmmm, that’s quite nice. You’re obviously losing out on some defense and team support from touch treatment, but in exchange you get a nice boost to damage that stacks with painful stare.


        (10) Subsonic Strike (Su): Replaces touch treatment (major). And now some nice AoE sonic damage to go with the single-target damage you got in wounding words. Great! The damage isn’t terribly high, but it’s a good option when you have a lot of weaker enemies on the field and are out of full-attack range.


Is It Worth It?


        Sure is! The vox archetype dials up the offense and dials down the defense, so pairing it with a defense-heavy multiclass (paladin 2 comes to mind) would be ideal; it’s even fine that you trade away towering ego if you’re going for a paladin 2 dip, since divine grace and towering ego don’t stack.
