# Weapon Enchantments

Weapon Enchantments


Gold Cost


Dueling (UI, CL 5, 14,000 gp): Initiative bonuses? Feint bonuses? Sign us mesmerists up! Dueling weapons are pretty expensive, but the initiative bonus alone is well worth the cost.




+1 Bonus


Agile (CL 7): You’re essentially paying a +1 bonus for two feats: Weapon Focus and whatever [Weapon] Grace feat you’re using to get Dexterity to damage. That’s actually a pretty good tradeoff, considering how strapped for feats mesmerists are.
Bane (CL 8): In certain campaigns, a bane weapon is practically required. Who’s going to play Ironfang Invasion without taking bane (humanoid [goblinoid])?
Cruel (CL 5): Mesmerists have plenty of opportunities to demoralize foes, especially with some kind of Enforcer build; a cruel weapon simply amplifies the debuffing that you get from that demoralization process.
Deceptive (CL 9): More feint bonuses: just what the doctor ordered. The immediate action feint is particularly welcome if you’re building a crit-fishing mesmerist.
Ghost Touch (CL 9): Mesmerists are incredibly reliant on painful stare to deal satisfactory damage, but guess what incorporeal creatures are immune to? That’s right, precision damage from non-ghost touch weapons. I wouldn’t try to slap ghost touch on your main weapon—get a +1 ghost touch dagger or something similar and use it as a backup when you need it.
Keen (CL 10): A classic. Even though mesmerists might not deal the most damage on their crits, they can take advantage of some crit-fishing tactics: the Vision of Blood masterful trick is great in conjunction with crit-fishing, and Butterfly’s Sting is always possible if you want to pass those crits off to allies.
Seeking (CL 12): A great way to eliminate miss chances as a ranged mesmerist.
Shadowshooting (CL 8): The primary benefit of a shadowshooting weapon is that it doesn’t need to be reloaded, which might permit you to use firearms or crossbows without having to take Rapid Reload, Crossbow Mastery, or gunslinger levels. Enemies who pass their Will saves to disbelieve your ammunition will take minimum damage, of course, but that’s not a problem for mesmerists, who rely far more on damage from painful stare than they do on weapon damage dice.
Veering (CL 5): A seeking veering ranged weapon is, like, 90% of the way to Improved Precise Shot, and that’s a dang good benefit that most mesmerists wouldn’t be able to pick up for a very long time. I highly recommend that combo.


Answering (CL 7): A great enchantment for mesmerists taking a swashbuckler dip.
Courageous (CL 3): Mesmerists and other psychic spellcasters hate fear effects; courageous wouldn’t be a bad idea.
Distracting (CL 5): Increasing the DC of concentration checks with no save is devastating to casters. Magehunting mesmerists, take note.
Limning (CL 5): You get faerie fire on your spell list, but having it on a weapon ain’t a bad idea, either.
Merciful (CL 5): Enforcer + a merciful weapon is a classic combo for debuffing with Intimidate. Assuming your GM is okay with the cognitive dissonance of having a cruel merciful weapon, you can do even more debuffing with that setup.
Sapping (CL 5): Mesmerists are in fact proficient with saps, so maybe? 
Skewering (CL 5): Another possibility for crit-fishing, swash-dipping mesmerists.
