# OCC 201: Races

OCC 201: Races




Apart from archetype and implement schools, an Occultist’s race is the only other element that truly determines their suitedness for the various roles discussed in the previous section. The choice of race or ancestry in Pathfinder is important for three reasons:


      * Race determines starting attribute bonuses. At the very least, any race that’s going to be in serious contention for an Occultist build needs to boost Intelligence or Strength/Dexterity—preferably both. Any race that penalizes Intelligence should go right to the bottom of the barrel; it’s simply impossible to build a good Occultist when you’re struggling against that headwind. Many other classes have archetypes that change the class’ key mental attribute to something different than it initially was (think the Living Grimoire Inquisitor, Mysterious Stranger Gunslinger, Tortured Crusader Paladin, etc.) and while the Occultist headfakes toward this mechanic with the Reliquarian and Silksworn archetypes (which grant some additional bonuses for Wisdom and Charisma, respectively) neither archetype alters Intelligence as your spellcasting and focus power attribute. Brièvement: give me Intelligence boosts or give me death.
      * Race determines favored class bonuses. Every race can select HP or skill ranks as their favored class bonus, but going down this road often ignores the powerful favored class bonuses available to certain races. Occultists need mental focus more than anything else in life, so it should come as no surprise that elves, half-elves, and halflings rule the roost here, with a special emphasis on elves and half-elves due to their beneficial stat distributions. Humans, ratfolk, vanara, and half-orcs can get new focus powers; although it’s not the mechanically optimal choice, it’s better than nothing. Gnomes get a special pass for casting builds, especially Silksworn, because Distortion is an incredible resonant power when paired with their favored class bonus. Everything else is garbage, as far as we’re concerned. Stick with skill ranks or HP.
      * One way to determine how good a favored class bonus is is to compare it to similar feats. Races that grant +½ mental focus per level grant you, in essence, a free Extra Mental Focus feat every 4 levels. Races that grant +⅙ of a focus power per level grant you a free Extra Focus Power feat every 6 levels. By taking skill ranks or HP, you’re essentially spending 20 levels’ worth of investment on the Toughness or Skilled feats, which isn’t that great. The +½ mental focus option beats everything else because it’s what Occultists need most anyway, but also because it grants you the equivalent of 5 feats over 20 levels, compared with 3 feats over 20 levels for the focus power option and 1 feat over 20 levels for HP or skill ranks. See how that works?
      * Race determines racial abilities. Racial abilities can range from highly mediocre (small skill bonuses or spell-like abilities) to game-changing (humans’ bonus feats or samsarans’ bonus spells known). We’ll get into it down below.


        As has been usual in my last few class guides, I’m not going to go over every race, as that would be minimally fruitful. Simply ask yourselves the questions, “Am I getting bonuses to Strength, Dexterity, or Intelligence? Is the favored class bonus going to grant me more mental focus or focus powers? Are the racial abilities ones I can live with? Does this fit my backstory?” If the answers to those four questions are yes, congratulations: you’ve got a winner.
