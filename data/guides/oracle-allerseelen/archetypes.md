# Archetypes — Oracular Archetypes

## A Word About Archetypes

Oracle archetypes are odd birds. Without many core class features to trade away, most alter your Curse somewhat, swap out a few skills or spells here and there, or grant access to a new Revelation or two. Truly revolutionary archetypes—I’m thinking things like Psychic Searcher or Spirit Guide—are scarce. That’s not entirely a bad thing, honestly, given that Mysteries function for Oracles in much the same way that archetypes function for other classes. My favorite use is to swap out mediocre spell lists for better ones, but you needn’t stretch your archetype muscles more than that unless you really want to.

## The Wheat

### Ancient Lorekeeper (Advanced Race Guide)

What's the niche?

Ancient Lorekeepers are Elf- or Half-Elf-only Oracles who tap into their arcane heritage to poach arcane spells from the Wizard/Sorcerer list.

What you’re getting

Class Skills: Undeniably useful skills, just far too few of them. Most Mysteries give you four class skills, while Ancient Lorekeeper gives you only two.

Elven Arcana: And just like that, you got one of the best Oracle features of them all: stealing spells from the Wizard list. That list is incredibly, incredibly versatile, and Ancient Lorekeepers are free to choose not predefined spells, but whatever spells they want. Go ahead, pick the cream of the crop at every level; the really good ones don’t go out of style, even when they’re gained three levels later than a Wizard or Sorcerer would have them.

Is it worth it?

If your Mystery’s spell list is lackluster but has good Revelations, strongly consider this archetype. Elven Arcana is incredible, and the class skills are decent enough. Unless you’ve got a specific concept that works better, go Half-Elf rather than Elf—Half-Elves get access to the amazing Human FCB in order to compensate for the lost Oracle spells, and otherwise have better stat distributions than Elves for an Oracle build. (Thanks, /u/CoeusFreeze, for the catch about Half-Elves qualifying for this archetype!)

### Black-Blooded Oracle (Inner Sea Magic)

What's the niche?

Acquire Black Blood of Orv, disregard cold: Black-Blooded Oracles don’t trade anything away that breaks the bank, but neither to they gain much.

What you’re getting

Curse of Black Blood: So long as your team is briefed on the fact that they’ll need to use a wand of inflict light wounds on you if you fall unconscious, rather than a wand of cure light wounds, this Curse shouldn’t be much of a curse to you at all. You wouldn’t be using DEX-based skills like Sleight of Hand and Disable Device anyway, most likely, so that part comes off as fluff. And eventual immunity to cold damage is quite nice! It’s a straightforward and unobtrusive Curse.

Optional Revelations: Hm. Not much here. Black Blood Spray stinks, while the Darkvision Revelation is easily bypassed racially or through spells and Revelations. Dark Resilience is the only ability here potentially worth taking, as many of the listed effects will target your weak Fortitude save. Not a terrible number of uses per day, either!

Is it worth it?

Like I said up there, Black-Blooded Oracles’ biggest strength is that there’s nothing overtly wrong with the archetype. It neither contributes nor detracts. It is a perfect null. Take it if no other Curse appeals, I guess?

### Cyclopean Seer (Inner Sea Monster Codex)

What's the niche?

Cyclopean Seers are Divination/Recon specialists who port over some of the best abilities of their ancient giantkind ancestors.

What you’re getting

Class Skills: Lore and Ancestor Oracles might not care much about getting all Knowledge skills; for everyone else, however, that’s a bunch of skills getting an extra three points.

Oracle’s Curse: Tongues and Powerless Prophecy are the best on this menu for PCs: Tongues if you want something that won’t get in the way too badly, and Powerless Prophecy if you want something a little more aggressive that also grants better boons.

Bonus Spells: Assuming, and I mean really assuming that you want to become a Divination specialist, there’s hardly a better way to do it than how the Cyclopean Seer does it. Go ahead and cherrypick your favorite Divination spells off the Cleric list, and if you don’t see one that you like, look at other lists. The spell lists of the Occult classes, Psychic in particular, have some incredibly interesting Divination spells, including offensive spells like the mind thrust suite and crazy self-buffs like glimpse the akashic. Just set the spell filter on aonprd.com and go to, John Woo.

(1) Assume Fate: There may well be moments when a BBEG nukes their own henchmen, in which case feel free to steal your save bonus from one of them; in all other circumstances, however, I can’t think your team would be thrilled about you decreasing their survival chances by snatching away a save bonus. Maybe, maybe, if you happen to know for a fact that their save bonus is incredible where yours is not—stealing a Reflex save from a Rogue with Improved Evasion, for example.

(7) Brutal Trance: By the time you get Brutal Trance, the probabilities and caster level math work out to about two questions per trance. Not bad, but not entirely good, especially considering you’ll inevitably hurt yourself or others during the trance.

Optional Revelations: All three of these abilities are excellent, so it’s hard to pick one to start with. Doomsaying is best used to get an opponent to fail a crucial save against a crippling debuff—don’t bother with the skill or attack versions. Flash of Insight is, well, Flash of Insight. If you’ve ever fought a Cyclops, you know how OP it is to be able to automatically threaten or confirm a critical hit, blow through SR, pass a terrifying save, etc. Lastly, Prescience is like Mobility and Combat Casting had a baby. Both good, both worthwhile.

Is it worth it?

Absolutely! Cyclopean Seer already makes it extraordinarily easy to spy on your enemies, but it’s the addition of spells from other casting lists that really makes the archetype for me. I wish you were forced to take the optional Revelations, and given the option to take the forced Revelations, but if wishes were fishes, we’d all cast nets. Make some space in your build for the Extra Revelation feat, as you’ll probably need it to supplement your Revelation list.

### Divine Numerologist (Disciple’s Doctrine)

What's the niche?

Oracles that use the Path of Numbers to calculate the surest way through the chaos of the cosmos.

What you’re getting

Bonus Spells: They’re pretty good, with numerological evocation standing out, ironically, as the only stinker on the list. Prying eyes is a fabulous recon ability, as is greater arcane sight. Moment of prescience and foresight round out your high-level self-buffs.

(1) Calculate the Odds: Great bonus, and (Circumstance) stacks with everything. But for only one d20 roll? And only 1/day until 7th level? A Cleric with the Luck Domain can do the same thing many times per day, and for every d20 roll within one round.

(7) Program the Divine Algorithm: /u/OneWishes points out that there are more uses here than meets the eye. You can use it on confusion rolls to ensure that you do nothing but babble, ensure a decent saving throw, or skew your chances on a d20 roll with the God-Meddled Curse. At 15th level, the ability takes everything to the limit, allowing you to choose to maximize your rolls. That’s free Maximize Spell with no increase in casting time or spell level, or the best result on a Primal Magic event, or a guaranteed weapon crit.

Is it worth it?

The spell list comes on a bit late, as does Program the Divine Algorithm, but if you’re willing to wait, Divine Numerologist is willing to deliver. Probably not a great archetype for low-level games.

### Dual-Cursed Oracle (Ultimate Magic)

What's the niche?

Dual-Cursed Oracles strain under the heavy burden of their Curse, but receive even greater gifts to compensate.

What you’re getting

Oracle’s Curse: So that looks pretty bad on its face, right? Two Curses, and only one of them goes anywhere you’d want it to go. There are plenty of Curse combos to make this alright, however: Deaf and Wolf-Scarred Face are the classic set-up, giving you permanent Silent Spell to nullify the spell failure chance from being deafened. Plagued / Wasting can give you immunity to the sickened condition, which happens to be the main drawback to Covetous / Hunger / Legalistic.

Bonus Spells: Could you pick a better list for a dedicated Debuffing specialist? Ill omen is brutal and scales well with level thanks to its lack of a save, and the pamphlet “Debuffing and You” distributed by Oracle University is literally just a stock photo of bestow curse, copied and pasted 50 times. Oracle’s burden will obviously vary in brutality depending on your selected Curses.

Class Skills: Ach, that hurts. The base Oracle’s skill list is nothing special, and most Mysteries rely on the influx of class skills to make them viable contributing outside of combat. So...no Skills role for Dual-Cursed Oracles, it would seem.

Revelations: Two bonus Revelations. That’s amazing for Mysteries like Shadow that simply have too many good Revelations to discriminate.

(1) Misfortune: No save and unlimited uses. Jesus. You can use this to protect your team, or just tank enemy saves with it and use debuffs to cripple them.

(5) Fortune: Plenty good, yet limited by uses per day.

Is it worth it?

It really, really is. Not every Mystery has the Revelation list to make Dual-Cursed Oracles’ drawbacks worthwhile, but those that do instantly ascend into the upper echelons of Support and Debuffing effectiveness. Mysteries like Time can become unholy forces of nature with Dual-Cursed, jinxing enemies into a jelly before they ever get the chance to return fire.

### Elementalist Oracle (Ultimate Wilderness)

What's the niche?

Oracles that eschew connection to a single element in favor of all them.

What you’re getting

Bonus Spells: It’s a good list, full of spells that you’d likely take anyway if you had access to them.

(1) Elemental Linguist: Blergh, no, not with tongues on your spell list. You could do much better things with a Revelation.

(11) Elemental Form: Excellent! I love energy immunities and novel movement modalities. Pity it comes so late.

Is it worth it?

The turd that is Elemental Linguist is the only thing holding me back from a full-throated recommendation. Elementalist Oracle is, on the whole, a decent way of replacing a mediocre spell list while paying little in return.

### Enlightened Philosopher (Ultimate Magic)

What's the niche?

An archetype that puts Skills- and Utility-based oomph behind otherwise unskilled Mysteries.

What you’re getting

Class Skills: Gaining all Knowledge skills is an improvement over 90% of the class skills offered by other Mysteries or archetypes. Luckily you’ve got Mental Acuity coming up to help you take advantage!

Bonus Spells: Thematic cohesion, where art thou? Good Support, Recon, and Utility spells here, albeit without much explanation of how water walk relates to discern lies or wind walk relates to astral projection.

(7) Mental Acuity: Passive INT boosts ratchet up your Knowledge and skill game. Lore Oracles always take this, anyway.

Is it worth it?

Yeah! It’s maybe the best archetype out there for players who want to engage with skills as an Oracle, but don’t want to jump in feet-first to Lore or Ancestor. Lore Oracles can still act as excellent Enlightened Philosophers, however, as it overhauls their shitty spell list.

### Hermit (Legacy of the First World)

What's the niche?

Seriously misanthropic Oracles who just don’t like people in their personal space, dammit. Whoa! Whoa! Who’s around me right now!? Who’s around me!?

What you’re getting

Mystery: The complete list of Mysteries that satisfy this stricture is: Apocalypse, Heavens, Juju, Life, Lunar, Nature, Outer Rifts, Solar, Spellscar, Stone, Succor, Volcano, Winter, and Wood. A lot of good Mysteries in there.

Oracle’s Curse: Reclusive is ideal for self-buffers and magehunters. Add that to your teleportation and debuff-centered spell list, and you’re looking at someone that casters should be very, very afraid of.

Bonus Spells: Ohhh, that’s good. Blindness/deafness and crushing despair are both top-shelf debuffs; wall of force is one of the preeminent battlefield control spells in the entire game, and maze simply sends people away with no save, pending an incredibly difficult INT check to return.

(1) Recluse’s Stride: Battlefield positioning becomes a thing of the past when you can extricate yourself from nearly any situation without provoking. And dimension door on top of grace? You’re making me faint, Paizo.

(7) Fade from Memory: A little harder for Melee Oracles to pull off, but eminently doable for Ranged Oracles. I think I’m going to do a Wood Mystery / Hermit Archetype / Hinterlander PrC in my builds below, so check there for how this might work.

Is it worth it?

Good gravy, that’s crazy. Reclusive won’t be everyone’s cup of tea, but the whole package coheres into a scarily self-sufficient and dangerous magehunter. I’d say that Heavens, Lunar, Nature, Outer Rifts, Stone, Succor, and Wood can benefit most from the archetype.

### Keleshite Prophet (Inner Sea Intrigue)

What's the niche?

Divination and Skills specialists who can parlay interpretive dance into spellcasting.

What you’re getting

Class Skills: Yeah yeah yeah. All good, even Perform (Dance).

Bonus Spells: Put on your Divination hat, kids, we’re going to cast prying eyes today. Yeah, Keleshite Prophet seems to stack naturally with the Intrigue, Streets, or Occult Mysteries.

(1) Divining Dance: Funnily enough, I think the best effect on the list is the one you can get from 0-9, but maybe that’s just me. Not sure why you’re given the bonuses to casting time—it’s not like the difference between 1 and 10 minutes is huge when you’re not in combat.

Is it worth it?

Hmmm. Keleshite Prophet makes it above the Chaff threshold, but only barely. I mean, how many Divination-specialized archetypes can one class reasonably sustain? Cyclopean Seer, Keleshite Prophet, Psychic Searcher, Seer...there are better options out there.

### Ocean’s Echo (Blood of the Sea)

What's the niche?

Merfolk Bard-Oracles (Bardacles? Barnacles? There’s got to be an aquatic pun in there somewhere.).

What you’re getting

Class Skills: Okay, good start. Face skills are par for the bardic course.

Bonus Spells: Sonic-based blasts and some soft control/debuffing dominate the list. And that’s not a bad thing!

(1, 3, 15) Inspiring Song: What needs to be said about Bardic Performance? Bards are fantastic team buffers, and the addition of full casting doesn’t hurt. Make sure you pick up Extra Revelation a few times to cover your losses.

Is it worth it?

If you’re playing in the Cerulean Seas campaign setting, the latter books of Ruins of Azlant, etc., Ocean’s Echoes will outperform other characters. How are you going to make a Merfolk work in most games, though? Constant slipstream or sky swim?

### Pei Zin Practitioner (Healer’s Handbook)

What's the niche?

Oracles that heal, heal, heal, using a combination of Paladin abilities and mystical herbalism.

What you’re getting

Master Herbalist: You’ll need as many ranks in Profession (Herbalism) as you can get once Master Healing Technique comes online, so yeah, pump ranks into it early and often.

(1) Healer’s Way: Lay on Hands as a Revelation. Haha, gross. This is how Life Oracles with Life Link can heal their entire team without having to resort to Paladin levels: let your HP flow to them as wounds are sustained, then use your swift actions to Lay Hands on yourself.

(7) Master Healing Technique: A CHA score of 20 should be doable by 7th level. That leaves you with 7 Ranks + 3 Class Skill + 5 CHA + 3 Master Herbalist = +18 Profession (Herbalism) and an auto-success on the minor conditions. Humans can take the alternate Heart of the Fields racial trait to boost this even more, or if you’re playing a long-lived race, Breadth of Experience. Even Skill Focus can do good work! Won’t be long before you hit that DC 35 check almost all the time.

Is it worth it?

Pei Zin Practitioners make the perfect counterpart to Life and Succor Oracles that are going for team buffing and healing. Life Link builds in particular will be salivating.

### Possessed Oracle (Ultimate Magic)

What's the niche?

Creepy, creepy Oracles who can do creepy, creepy things.

What you’re getting

Oracle’s Curse: Tongues is unobtrusive, with few benefits; Haunted is more difficult, but also more powerful.

Bonus Spells: With the bonus spells from Haunted especially, Possessed Oracles can confuse, trick, and otherwise trip up enemies. Sleepwalk is the only spell on the list that won’t get used regularly.

(1) Two Minds: It’s essentially Iron Will and Improved Iron Will, packaged together. You don’t necessarily need it, but I’ll take it. Anticaster builds can do especially good things with an untyped bonus like this.

Is it worth it?

The spell list is unusual and thematically disconnected, yet strong overall. Has a very strong Witch feel to it.

### Psychic Searcher (Advanced Class Guide)

What's the niche?

The other side of Enlightened Philospher’s Skills coin: Oracles who gain access to Divination magic and Investigator class features.

What you’re getting

Bonus Spells: As with many Oracle archetypes, your entire gimmick is Divination magic. You’ve really got to want a role as an information gatherer and remote spy to make it work.

(2) Inspiration: Inspiration is fantastic for builds that want to use skills effectively, and you get free Inspiration uses on many of the best Face and Knowledge skills available. That’s a great package on its own; in addition, however, you should qualify to take other Investigator Talents that modify Inspiration, because you receive Inspiration “as the Investigator class ability.”

(3) Psychic Talent: The Talents you can choose from are Amazing Inspiration, Eidetic Recollection, Empathy, Inspired Alertness, Item Lore, Perceptive Tracking, and Hard To Fool, although it’s unclear whether you need to have an Oracle level equal to the listed Investigator level in order to select the Talent. (Thanks, /u/HadACookie!) If that’s the case, your choices at 3rd level are...poor. Perceptive Tracking is about it, unless you want to take Hard to Fool. That would be a hard pill to swallow, however, when Empathy so completely outclasses it! If you are allowed Talents at any level, Eidetic Recollection is absolutely your best bet here, allowing you to take 10 or 20 on Knowledge checks a full 8 levels before your Investigator cousins. Coupled with Revelations like Lore Keeper and your aforementioned Inspiration class feature, you’re poised to become an incredible Knowledge monkey. Talk to your GM about whether Inspiration and Psychic Talents qualify you to take other Talents that modify Inspiration: if they do, I can recommend a few others.

Amazing / Tenacious Inspiration: You can take one or the other, but they work best in tandem.

Atheist Inspiration: Awesome anticaster tool.

Device Talent: Improves your ability to activate wands and scrolls, a cornerstone of the spontaneous casting repertoire.

Inspirational Expertise: Quick way to turn your successful Knowledge checks into team buffing, and with an uncommon (Insight) bonus.

Underworld Inspiration: Inspired Intellect and Expanded Inspiration are great for Investigators; they have considerable overlap, however, with Psychic Searchers’ free Inspiration skills. Underworld Inspiration helps you get sneaky-scary in addition to all that.

Is it worth it?

Enlightened Philosopher gives you more information by letting you scout, sniff out lies, etc., but Psychic Searcher puts a bunch of Divination magic and skill bonuses in your hands and says, “Here, you figure out how to use this.” Mysteries that grant access to all Knowledge skills (Ancestor, e.g.)  can take advantage of Psychic Searcher’s benefits, but will find themselves missing all the powerful spells that come with their Mystery. Better instead to select a Mystery like Lore, which grants you many of the same spells while adding an undeniably potent layer of skill mastery.

### Reincarnated Oracle (Advanced Race Guide)

What's the niche?

Samsaran-only Oracles who remember some stuff from their past lives.

What you’re getting

Oracle’s Curse: Just like Possessed Oracles. Haunted is tougher but more powerful; Tongues is weak but easy to live with.

Bonus Spells: I like the 8th and 9th-level spells the best, which is a pity. See alignment and detect thoughts are reasonable enough if you’re going for a Recon/Divination role. Contact other plane is, as always, awful.

(1/3) Location Memories: You’ve already got low-light vision. Scent is good in conditions where enemies are invisible or otherwise shrouded in darkness; I suppose it could also help you play detective. Perception and Survival bonuses are slim, but of a rare typing.

(1/3/7) Spirit Memories: Sickened and staggered is a big debuff. The ability text says both “Once per day” and “You can use this ability a number of times per day equal to 3 + your Charisma modifier,” so your guess is as good as mine as to which applies.

Is it worth it?

Samsarans don’t immediately leap to mind as Oracles because they don’t receive a bonus to CHA, but their Mystic Past Life alternate racial trait is more than enough to put them on the list. Assuming Spirit Memories functions 3 + CHA times per day, it’s got some good Recon/Debuffing potential.

### Seeker (Pathfinder Society Field Guide)

What's the niche?

Part Rogues, part metamagic adepts? Hard archetype to classify.

What you’re getting

Tinkering: I’m fine with this, actually. Being a CHA-based class, Oracles might never be as good as Rogues at spotting and disabling traps, but they can still become passable at it. All the better if your Mystery’s bonus skills stink.

(3) Seeker Lore: This ability could use some serious clarification by Paizo. The flavor of the ability is clearly meant to apply only to the bonus spells that an Oracle receives, yet the text granting you the bonuses—thanks to poorly placed commas—appears to apply to all spells. The former interpretation is perhaps behind the power curve of most Revelations, whereas the latter interpretation is drastically ahead of the power curve. Up to your table to decide, and likely best used by Mysteries whose bonus spells are heavy on debuffs, blasts, and other enemy-targeted effects.

(15) Seeker Magic: Metamagic benefits blasters and debuffers most, so hey, look at that! Good design. Now all your spells can be Piercing, Focused, Bouncing, or Extended.

Is it worth it?

Assuming your Mystery grants you enemy-targeted bonus spells and you intend to use them quite heavily (Winter is the classic example) Seeker can be great at what it does, amping up your casting ability without trading much away.

### Seer (Ultimate Magic)

What's the niche?

...another Divination magic specialist?

What you’re getting

Bonus Spells: I hope you’re ready to do nothing but Divination, because that’s the train you’re on now. I mean, Seers do even more Divination than the Occult or Lore Mysteries, and that’s saying a lot.

Mandatory Revelations

(1) Natural Divination: This Revelation gets a big ol’ pass in Nature and Juju, and it gets the same here. Flexible and powerful.

(3) Gift of Prophecy: I’ve given Automatic Writing a green rating elsewhere, so I’ll do the same here. Some of these spells are normally quite expensive to cast, so getting them without material component costs is a steal.

Is it worth it?

Cyclopean Seer, Psychic Searcher, Seer...all the Divination-based archetypes need to remember to speak with their GM before the game begins to see how far they’re willing to play along with Divination magic. I think it would also be common courtesy at my table to alert the GM about which targets you’re going to scry on, which questions you’ll ask during commune, etc., ahead of time so that they can prep appropriate answers.

### Shigenjo (Advanced Race Guide)

What's the niche?

A Tengu-only archetype that gives off very Monk-ish vibes, complete with a ki pool.

What you’re getting

Bonus Spells: The early spells focus on self-buffing, which I’m fine with; Shigenjo will likely play with a Melee role in mind anyway. Later spells segue into a possession effect (not sure why that’s included, but magic jar is plenty of fun) and a relatively weak Sonic blast. Good, all in all.

(7) Ki Pool: I wouldn’t bother with the Ki Strikes—you’ll be way behind the curve, and no Mystery but Ascetic would even need help bypassing DR with magic weapon and align weapon on your list. Out of all your other options for expending ki, Ki Magic is the best, especially if you intend to deliver touch-range debuffs and such. The other options will be circumstantial.

(15) Quivering Palm: Fortitude is a bad save to target this late in the game, but a little less bad for you than it would be for a Monk, as your DC is likely to be higher innately and you get some support from debuffing spells. I’d highly recommend hitting your target with a spell from the curse line—bestow, mathematical, major, greater bestow, etc.—beforehand in order to soften up the Fortitude save.

Is it worth it?

I tend to think of Tengu as Clerics, Monks, Inquisitors, Druids, etc., but rarely Oracles. Shigenjo can get the job done, generally speaking, but I’m not entirely convinced you wouldn’t be better off choosing a stronger Oracle race and simply selecting the Ascetic Mystery to achieve the Monk flavor that the archetype leans toward.

### Spirit Guide (Advanced Class Guide)

What's the niche?

Oracles that gain access to a different Shaman Spirit every day. Yeah, it’s amazing.

What you’re getting

Class Skills: All Knowledge skills is already excellent, even with no other context.

(3) Bonded Spirit: How amazing is this ability!? Oracle Mysteries are incredibly deep, but not broad. Adding the breadth that comes with the Wandering Spirit class feature naturally brings a whole new layer of power to the Oracle chassis. Getting your Spirit’s bonus spells is probably the most important facet of Bonded Spirit, because it allows you to change roles on a dime. If you’re going to need blasting, you grab Flame; healing, Life; skills, Lore. The good news is that the Spirits sync up almost 1:1 with Mysteries, so if you know your Oracles, you know your Shaman. Don’t worry about the lost Revelations—you can always make those up with the Extra Revelation feat if you truly need to.

Is it worth it?

Ohhh, good gracious yes. It’s hard to oversell how worth it Spirit Guide is. Oracles sometimes feel locked into their roles, as spontaneous casters who get static abilities; once you combine the flexibility of Wandering Spirit with the power of Mysteries, however, you have an incredibly strong package that can both fulfill a primary role and layer on a secondary role as the day’s adventuring demands. If your Mystery only grants a few worthwhile Revelations, there’s absolutely no reason not to go Spirit Guide.

### Warsighted (Advanced Class Guide)

What's the niche?

Half-Brawler Oracles.

What you’re getting

(1) Martial Flexibility: Hot take: Martial Flexibility isn’t very good on an Oracle. I mean, yeah, it’s still good, but Oracles can’t use it nearly as well as Brawlers can. Why not? For starters, full BAB vs. ¾ BAB progressions. No Brawler’s Cunning to ignore INT requirements. No Fighter and Monk levels. No Improved Unarmed Strike and scaling unarmed damage. No Maneuver Training. No Brawler’s Flurry. No bonus combat feats. The Brawler works as a class because it plows down feat taxes and build obstacles wholesale, allowing you to progress far enough down many combat trees with your static feats that you can afford to mix and match anything you need at a moment’s notice. Warsighted Oracles will be too busy fretting about having to pick up a bunch of prerequisites to get to the good stuff.

Is it worth it?

Lacking the ability to get too fancy with your feat selection, you’re going to have to choose simple options: Blind-Fight, Combat Reflexes, Mobility, Cleave, the like. As I said, Warsighted isn’t as OP as it looks at first blush, especially when you consider that you’re giving up a whopping four Revelations for Martial Flexibility.

## The Chaff

### Community Guardian (Advanced Race Guide)

What's the niche?

Community Guardians are Halfling-only Oracles who focus on team buffing, at least ostensibly. He protecc; he attacc; but most importantly, he got your bacc.

What you’re getting

Class Skills: Perception’s the only really good one here, with Knowledge (Local) behind. Surival is mediocre, and Linguistics is downright bad.

Bonus Spells: Nah, nah, nah. These are spells for an NPC or a scroll. You’d want none of them as spells known.

Revelations: Getting locked out of your 1st- and 3rd-level Revelations is kind of a big deal; they gave you something excellent in return, right? Well, not really. Spirit of Community is far too anemic a buff, and to skills, no less. That might be okay when you’re out of combat and trying to disable a trap, say, but won’t do any good in combat. And it’s a competence bonus, on top of all that! No shortage of competence bonuses in PF. I quite like Renewing Radiance, actually, but you get it only 1/day, which is terrible.

Is it worth it?

Definitely not. Halflings make good Oracles, but PCs should let NPC Halflings have this one. The buffs and spells are simply too watered down to compete with Mysteries like Life or Succor.

### Inerrant Voice (Heroes of the High Court)

What's the niche?

Oracles that have a side gig as bodyguards to nobility.

What you’re getting

Bonus Spells: Why all the Divination spells if the archetype is meant to be focused on Abjuration magic? I get it, sniff out threats before they become threats, but it still feels like these slots could have gone to more useful spells.

(3) Vigilant Protector: Buffing should really be done before a fight, at least as often as you can manage. I’m not saying it’ll never come up, only that it won’t come up often if you’re doing your job well.

Is it worth it?

I’m almost positive Inerrant Voice was meant to be an NPC archetype.

### Planar Oracle (Ultimate Magic)

What's the niche?

They’re Oracles who like the Planes. It’s right in the name, man.

What you’re getting

Bonus Spells: If you’re going to live full-time in the Abyss, sure, pick these up. They have the feel of something that an NPC planar guide would have, though; they’re certainly not spells you’d use every day.

(3) Planar Resistance: Good stuff, but ultimately not enough to overcome the bad spell list.

Is it worth it?

Leave it for NPCs. Planar exploration might comprise a significant portion of high-level campaigns; for most of us, however, just making it past Book 1 of an AP is a fucking Herculean feat.

### Purifier (Advanced Race Guide)

What's the niche?

Aasimar-only Oracles that take the fight to Evil Outsiders. It’s got a very Holy Vindicator-ish feel to it.

What you’re getting

Diminished Spellcasting: Let’s start with the bad news first, shall we? You don’t get as many spells per day as normal Oracles. That’s mostly okay, though, because Purifiers are practically screaming to become Melee specialists who only burn spell slots to become combat monsters. You’ll want to have a decent CHA score so that all your targeted dispel effects can land, though.

Bonus Spells: A lot of anti-possession, anti-summoning, and anti-Evil Outsider effects, as predicted. Awesome for campaigns like Wrath of the Righteous or Hell’s Rebels.

Mandatory Revelations

(3) See Sin: It’s only a DC 25 to notice an Enchantment effect, so it’s not like you’ll need that many ranks. Unnecessary.

(7) Celestial Armor: Personally, I like Armor Training. It’s not as flashy as Weapon Training, but it does good work. See whether you qualify for Advanced Armor Training at 11th level, because those are even cooler.

(11) Sin Eater: Might as well choose a Curse that makes you immune to the sickened condition, so that you can avoid the negative effects of a failed check. It’s decent! These are bad effects. You’d hate for anyone close to you to suffer them.

(5) Sacred Scourge: Your Channels are only good against Evil Outsiders, so burn ‘em if you’ve got ‘em.

(9) Holy Terror: As Sacred Scourge.

(13) Celestial Master: Isn’t compelling Good Outsiders to work for you kinda...not good? Seems like Good-aligned creatures, especially creatures so strongly Good-aligned, would want to help you anyway because they’re, ya know, Good-aligned. Maybe I’m missing the point.

Is it worth it?

I want to like it more, because there’s no doubt that Aasimar make fantastic Oracles. Can you imagine an Angel-blooded Aasimar with the Ancestor or Battle Mystery going ham on some poor Devil’s ass? Ultimately, though, the archetype is way too overinvested in taking down Evil Outsiders. If you’re in a Hell’s Rebels or Wrath of the Righteous campaign, it’s absolutely worth a look, especially if you’re creating a high-level character; beyond that, though, look elsewhere.

### River Soul (Ultimate Wilderness)

What's the niche?

Oracles who are bound to the fate of a particular river.

What you’re getting

Class Skills: Good enough for my purposes. Even Swim finds a home, finally.

Bonus Spells: Okay soft control and even a blast or two. Waves already has a strong spell list overall, but this one isn’t terrible, strictly speaking.

River-Bound Curse: This is the most non-Curse Curse I’ve ever seen. All you have to do is carry a container of water? Just be prepared for your GM to try to steal it at least once.

Mandatory Revelations

(1) River Flow: Underwater combat is something that no Pathfinder PC really spends a lot of time preparing for, unless you’re Dracius Oppiturnia. (GCP Naish, represent!) It’s so specific, so limited in duration, and so weak that I can’t give it a good score. You should be using a longspear anyway! That’ll work fine underwater.

(11) River Form: By 11th level, you should be plane shifting and teleporting, not playing Lazy River with the kids.

Is it worth it?

Bad mandatory Revelations suck up slots that would normally be taken by much better ones, as in the Waves Mystery. Ignore this archetype if you can.

### Stargazer (Ultimate Magic)

What's the niche?

Kind of a Heavens-lite Oracle?

What you’re getting

Class Skills: Perception is the best one, obviously. Knowledge (Nature) and Survival are okay.

Bonus Spells: Faerie fire and glitterdust are strong starters, but the class list goes off the rails thereafter.

Mandatory Revelations

(1) Guiding Star: So...Survival? Heal? Sense Motive? Perception is always useful, I suppose. The free metamagic is nice indeed, but has finicky requirements and doesn’t scale with level.

(7) Star Chart: Star Chart gets you commune without the expensive material component several levels early. That’s something, I guess.

Is it worth it?

Kinda meh. Guiding Star and Star Chart aren’t the most useful Revelations, and the bonus spell list pretty much sucks after 4th level. Just doesn’t wow me.

### Tree Soul (Ultimate Wilderness)

What's the niche?

Oracles who are just pining for adventure. Who can’t leaf well enough alone. Who root for the forest. I’ll see myself out.

What you’re getting

Class Skills: Mediocre skillset.

Weapon and Armor Proficiency: Same weapon and armor prohibitions as Druids. Man, that stinks.

Bonus Spells: Not a bad list, all in all, yet overly reliant on being in a forest or facing enemies with manufactured wooden weapons.

Tree-Bound Curse: Much like River Soul, I’m not sure it’s actually a curse. Just pick a tree, any tree, in the remotest area you can, and go about your business.

Mandatory Revelations

(1) Transform Wood: Am I missing the point of this Revelation? You’d want to wear darkwood armor ASAP as a Tree Soul Oracle, and the only other use is to convert other people’s armor...and they’re likely to wear metal.

(11) Living Steel Dolls: Animate objects is good; what exactly are you getting that you couldn’t get simply by animating steel objects, though?

Is it worth it?

Wood is a lackluster Mystery, and Tree Soul doesn’t do anything to improve that reputation. Get ready for a hot bowl of who-cares.
