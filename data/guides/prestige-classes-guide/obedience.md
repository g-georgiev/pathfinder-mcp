# Obedience Prestige Classes

Obedience Classes
The “Obedience” feats were first introduced as “Celestial Obedience” for those who worship the Empyreal Lords. Each Lord has a different “obedience” taking 1 hour to perform, and in exchange the player receives a special bonus and access to powerful “Boons” at 12, 16, and 20 Hit Dice. However, the Mystery Cultist but certain prestige classes speed up this progression, starting after 7th level and granting these boons at 10th, 13th, and 16th levels if you take the PrC as soon as possible. The concept was a success and was expanded upon with “Deific Obedience” for the main pantheon of gods. 


There are now feats for Demons, Fiends, Fey, and Monitors each with their own special prestige classes. Deific Obedience works for any deity, so any worshipers can become the main trio of Obedience PrCs. Martial-focused Sentinels, Spellcasting-themed Exalted, and the All-Rounder Evangelist, which can be taken at a minimum of 5th level, and granting Boons at 8th, 11th, and 14th level. PrCs belonging to smaller groups of gods require their specific Obedience feat and can be taken at a minimum of 7th level, granting Boons at 10th, 13th, and 16th level. The quality and effectiveness of each of these feats depends largely on the odiousness of the required Obedience and the quality of the Diety’s Boons. 


For example, Asmodeus requires you to have a ruby-bladed knife, and use it to inscribe symmetrical cuts into the flesh of another creature, drain some of their blood into a bowl of bone made from the skull of a sentient humanoid, use it to draw a large pentagram on the ground, kneel within the pentagram, and pray to Asmodeus. This requires you to either bring or find another creature and to perform this crazy ritual every. frickin’. day. In exchange, you get +4 on saves against fire, and his boons vary widely in quality. Caeden Cailean, by contrast, requires you to compose a little song, and sing it while taking sips of alcohol. In exchange, you get +4 on saves vs poison and on average his boons are pretty good.


Each of these PrCs that require an Obedience feat will be marked with “Obedience” in the title. They will be assessed based on the static abilities granted, as the quality of each God’s boons will heavily impact the effectiveness of the PrC. For a full breakdown of these, I heavily recommend that you check out Walter’s Guide to Deific Obediences.
