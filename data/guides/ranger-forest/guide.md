Rating System
All Guides use the Color Rating System and honestly? It’s a good Idea. 
* Game Breaking (6/5): This feature or option is so powerful, so useful, so unapologetically ridiculous that it very well make snap the game in half. DM’s reading this guide are welcome to ban these options, or are warned to somehow work around them. Players? Use these powers carefully.
* Take Me Now (5/5): Class defining. Taking them changes the game, or adds so much power it's insane not to.
* Fantastic (4/5): Incredibly solid, Not class breaking, but something that can add a fair bit of power and versatility to the character.
* Okay/Fine (3/5): Pretty good. They'll be helpful and have some use, but aren't likely to make things incredibly amazing. 
* Maybe Don’t (2/5): Unhelpful. It probably doesn’t...do much for you to pick these. Either they make your life slightly harder, or the bonuses just aren't very relevant to what you do.
* Actively Bad (1/5): God why. These options are the least helpful. They either are pointless, replaceable or completely outdated. These are abilities that will likely never be used and make you ask why they are there in the first place.

Okay, so about this guide…
So this is usually the part of the guide where I give you an overview of the class, why I’m doing a review of it and why I think it deserves more love than it currently is receiving, and while I am...sorta going to do that I want to be honest with those who love this class and wanted more love for it.

The Ranger is not good. I don’t mean it doesn’t have it’s niches, or it’s specific builds, but unlike a lot of other “not good” classes, like the rogue, or the fighter, it hasn’t really received anything to massively improve it’s viability (i.e. Unchained Rogue or Advanced Weapon/Armor Training for the Fighter.) 


In fact, the opposite has happened. Rather than improving the Ranger class, I think Paizo basically pimped out most of its best aspects to other classes that are just...more focused and perform generally better. The Ranger tries to be a lot of things, a beast master, a damage dealer, a spell caster, a tracker, and while this isn’t a particularly new thing for Paizo, the Ranger is particularly kneecapped as it is not given powerful enough features to support all of these contrasting ideas.


So...what are we doing here, and what is this guide about? Well I don’t want to leave you players high and dry. I think there are things WITHIN the ranger that are interesting, flavor and mechanical, and I can understand the draw, but I want to help players who may be drawn to the Ranger in concept, find something more appropriate in practice.


In other words, this is not a guide to the Pathfinder 1e Ranger. This is a guide on how to be a Ranger Better than the Ranger.
The Chassis: What are we working with?
So before we list out our other options, we’ve got to talk about what attracts people to the Ranger in the first place, aka, why play one at all? For all of the shit that it gets, the class does have some features worth looking at, and some neat little tricks worth keeping track of, so let’s take a look! I’m not going to list everything.
The Wheat
* (5/5) Full BAB: Best in the game, yay for smackage and max arrows.
* (4/5) 6 + Int Skill Mod: WOWZA thats a lot of skills!!! Second only to the Rogue. Hell yeah...but you aren’t an int based class, so it’s a little less great.
* (4/5) d10 Hit Die: Classic for people in the fray, good shit.
* (3/5) Favored Enemy: Yo this is your thing, like YOUR thing. Now a lot of people have something similar but this one is yours. +2 to attack and damage every 5 level’s is pretty nice...it’s a shame it only applies to a specific type...sometimes weirdly specific.
   * You can work around this with spells like Instant Enemy and Hunter’s Howl but...we’ll get to spells later.
   * This becomes much more useful if you are fighting one type of enemy over and over, and worst if you are dealing with a sort of monster of the week situation.
* (5/5) Ranger Combat Styles: Free feats with no prereqs! Actually amazing! No cap!
* (4/5) Hunter’s Bond: You get an animal companion...from a limited list...and an...inherent level penalty...but you can get an animal companion...ORRR you can give half of your favored enemy against one guy to your whole team as a move action which is also really good.
* (3/5) Ranger Spells: Anyone who has read my Druid Guide knows that I think spells are amazing and for the right classes, are without a doubt the most powerful class feature in the game. The Ranger List even has some stand out spells, like Bow Spirit or Find Quarry.  So why am I rating this so low? Well a couple reasons.
   * You don’t get many spells in the first place.
   * The spells you DO get have a reduced caster level.
   * Unlike the Paladin (whose spells are mostly form the cleric list and contain a fair number of buffs), most of your spells come from the druid list, means a lot of the best ones are AOE or control or summoning, things that the ranger isn’t really built to be good at or have high DC’s for.
   * Some of the BEST and most notable spells...basically just fix features that should have been better in the first place (i.e. Instant enemy and terrain bond, which are like MUST CASTS every day)
* (4/5) Evasion: Okay so rangers for some reason, at like level 9 get evasion...I dunno why. I mean this is good but why do you get it so late, the rogue got this at like level 2.
The Chaff
* (2/5) Ranger Traps: So some of you might not know that the ranger has an archetype that trades out it’s spells for neat traps. These traps aren’t...great but they are unique enough to the ranger that I’m willing to talk about them because there isn’t another trapping class? Also you can pick up a Trap using the Learn Ranger Trap feat.
* (1/5) Track: Half to survival to track...never seen this used once.
* (2/5) Wild Empathy: Not diplomacy for animals. IT’s meh on the druid and it’s meh for you.
* (1/5) Endurance: Probably not something you are going to see a lot of use out of in most games.
* (2/5) Favored Terrain: Okay so this is your second major RANGER feature, the one is unique to the ranger and it is...not good. See it’s a scaling bonus to initiative and skill checks, which would be welcome and quite nice IF it weren’t for the fact that those benefits only applied on specific terrains. Thing is, DND stories often involve people moving in and out of various different scenarios...so unless you campaign is going to be in one area, you basically are at your DM’s mercy as to whether or not you can use this and there isn’t really a way to change it.
* (1/5) Woodland Stride: More flavor than actually helpful, if it worked on magical plants we’d have something else to say.
* (1/5) Swift Tracker: You are faster at tracking, a thing that is not commonly done with survival and by now a wizard is probably better at than you.

How To Live The Dream
So we’ve taken a look at the ranger and what it can bring to the table! And it’s not nothing! It’s got some tricks! That said, most players aren’t trying to play every role I listed, and pathfinder rewards specialization. So based off what we know, are there alternatives to the ranger that can help us live our ranger fantasies, and perhaps, a little easier!
Hunter: A Boy And His 9-Foot Tall Death Beast
Roles: Nature’s Warden, Beastmaster


The Pathfinder Hunter is a Hybrid class combining the talents of the Ranger (ayeee) and the druid, capturing the essence of both: A defender of nature. The Hunter is a powerful class that rather than go out in multiple directions, focuses down on the character’s connection to nature through a number of super rad buffs known as Animal Focus, which can give you stat boosts for a few minutes, skill boosts, and sometimes even new abilities! On top of that, you get 1-6 spellcasting (score) and can choose ANY spell from either the druid ORRRR RANGER LIST. Yes that’s right. We keep all of our spells! 


But the TRUE star of this class, is of course the animal companion. Fully leveled and without limitation, unlike the rangers, your animal companion can also take advantage of your animal focus...and in fact, can do so indefinitely. With planar focus on top of that, you can even give your companion some elemental attacks or neat tricks. BUT THAT'S NOT ALL. On top of this, you can teach your animal companion skirmisher tricks, which the ranger has to give up ALL of it’s spells to get access to, and you get it for free. ON TOP OF THAT, your animal companion shares all of your teamwork feats...which you get once every three levels, WITH THE MOST RECENT ONE BEING A FLOATING FEAT THAT YOU CAN CHANGE AS A STANDARD ACTION???? Jesus christ. And should, somehow, your insanely jacked beast die, any summon nature’s ally spells you cast in it’s absence last a minute per level instead of rounds, just so you don’t feel too lonely. Which you get for free, btw. YOU CAN ALSO SEE THROUGH YOUR COMPANION AT LEVEL 4 FOR SCOUTING??? WHAT CAN’T IT DO???


TLDR Improvements:
* 0-6 Spontaneous Spellcasting (from ranger and druid lists)
* Obscenely powerful and versatile animal companion
* Access to Skirmisher Tricks
* Free teamwork feats that your animal companion shares automatically.
* Animal Focus, versatile mini buff that can be activated whenever
TLDR What it keeps or equivalents:
* 6 + Int Skill Mod
* Wild Empathy
* Woodland Stride
* Track/Swift Tracker


Slayer: How To Kill Everything With Anything.
Roles: Hawkeye’s Cooler Older Brother, Drizzt, The Forgotten Edge, Monster Hunter


Brother to the Hunter, the Slayer is a hybrid between the ranger (our boi) and the rogue, basically focusing on everything that the Hunter didn’t. A master of tracking and eliminating targets, the Slayer is an absolute powerhouse on the battlefield. Rather than relying on spells or an animal companion, the Slayer brings to bear the full skills of a master in the arts of death, ala buff or the Winchester brothers. How do they accomplish this?


Full bab, right of the gate matching. Ranger combat styles? HA! We have that, rogue talents AND extra slayer talents. Favored Enemy? Anyway could be an enemy. No, we get Studied Target. You get half as much bonus, but being able to pull it out at any time, against any enemy is golden! You even get sneak attack ON TOP OF EVERYTHING ELSE opening you up a wealth of feats and features (remember, basically all sneak attack’s stack). Honestly, with all of these different bonuses and options, the slayer blows a combat oriented ranger out of the water with ease...and most other classes too while you are at it!


TLDR Improvements:
* Sneak Attack
* Slayer Talents (Also Rogue and Ninja Talents)
* Studied Target (Favored Enemy)
TLDR What it keeps or equivalents:
* Full BAB
* Ranger Combat Styles
* 6 + Int Skill Mod
* d10 Hit Die
* Track/Swift Tracker

Sacred Huntsmaster Inquisitor: Holy Hunter Batman!!!
Roles: Monster Hunter, Beastmaster, Nature’s Warden, Drizzt, The Forgotten Edge
Guide: The Inquisitor's Symposium


Armed with skill and cunning, the inquisitors are sent out to find the enemies of the faith and subdue them...with extreme prejudice. On their own, they are a pretty neat class, known for their sort of ridiculous archetypes, one of which being the infamous Sacred Huntsmaster. What makes it so infamous? Well it actually takes a lot of the Hunter’s gimmick for its own! Hunter animal companion? Check? Access to skirmisher tricks? Yep. Teamwork feats? Galore. Yeah even the floating one. Empathic link? Yeah but it’s delayed. Animal focus? Same as the last one. Even 0-6 casting!

So where do the two options differ? For one, it’s spells come from the inquisitor spell list, rather than being freely chosen from the ranger or druid spell list. While the inquisitor and ranger share some spells, it’s not the same as being able to pick any of them, but it also gives them access to more self buffs than a hunter may gain access to, rather than animal buffs for your companion. 


Secondly, they get access to Domains, which can be extraordinarily powerful, such as the Madness Domain, which can make your foes take a -7 to everything that matters and make them expert basket weavers, or Liberation Domain, that will save you and your party more than once. Don’t like this or want less mystical power? That’s fine. Trade it out for an Inquisition or Divine Fighting Technique. Even if you don’t get the bonus spells or slots of a normal domain, this is a wonderful option to have at the ready.


What else? A few other little tidbits. Bonuses on knowledge checks for identifying the weaknesses of creatures (knowing things continues to be useful). Bonus to Intimidate and Sense Motive. Oh WISDOM ADDED TO INITIATIVE. Also they have their own little version of favored enemy, called BANE. While it’s bonus to attack is tiny, it boasts a bonus 2d6 to damage, and can help you bypass DR via enhancement bonuses. Favored enemy won’t be able to keep up with that on average until like level 15! Honestly? This class is a monster hunting MACHINE and should anyone dare question the ways of the forest...well...god help them.


TLDR Improvements:
* 0-6 Spontaneous Spellcasting (inquisitor list)
* Obscenely powerful and versatile animal companion
* Access to Skirmisher Tricks
* Free teamwork feats that your animal companion shares automatically.
* Cleric Domain, Inquisition or Divine Fighting Style
* Animal Focus, versatile mini buff that can be activated whenever
* +Wisdom To Initiative, and some other skills.
TLDR What it keeps or equivalents:
* Bane (Favored Enemy)
* 6 + Int Skill Mod
* Track
Unchained Snare Setter Rogue: Watch Where You Step.
Roles: Home Alone: Lost In The Woods, Drizzt, The Forgotten Edge, Monster Hunter
Guide: One Thousand Years of Pain


So I’m making this guide for people who want to do ranger things. Traps, historically, are weird, but if you want to do traps (outside of the craft infinitely resetting trap cheese) then this class is basically THE way to do it, outside of the ranger. You have to be a kobold, and a rogue but trust me, those things go together much better than one would expect. Great for Rainbow Siege players who like planning things out before going in for the kill. 


So what do we get? Well we get traps, and our traps do more damage. So what else do we get out of it? Well...actually a fair bit! We only lose some of our sneak attack progression and while we don’t get spells or a neato animal companion, we DO get:

*Inhales*

Sneak attack, rogue talents (and by extension ninja tricks), a bazillion class skills, 8+int skills per level, evasion at level 2, Debilitating injury, uncanny dodge, skill unlocks (like for survival that basically give you trackless step and endurance), and free dex to attack and damage for one weapon of your choice. *Exhales.*


So you can hunt and kill with the best of them, probably track better than the best of them and have all da traps. Go in.


TLDR Improvements:
* Ouchier Ranger Traps
* 8+Int Skills
* Delayed Sneak Attack
* Rogue Talents
* Evasion (but at level 2)
TLDR What it keeps or equivalents:
* Skill Unlock: Survival (Endurance/Trackless Step)

Samsaran Nature’s Fang Druid 
Roles: The Pathfinder Ranger, Monster Hunter, Beastmaster, Nature’s Warden, Hawkeye’s Cooler Older Brother, Drizzt, The Forgotten Edge, Home Alone: Lost In The Woods
Guides: Becoming A Force Of Nature


Okay, so you’ve read through this guide, looked at all of the options, but none of them really hit ALL of the points you were hoping for. After all, every class has its niche, so that’s only logical. These options are great, sure but they can’t capture the same...sort of style that the ranger does. Something about how all of its individual pieces fit together. And to those who think that way, I get it, and honestly, appreciate the feedback. BUT, before you go, from one ranger lover to another, give me one more chance to woo you. Just one...because I honestly believe this option has everything you need and more.

I present to you, the Nature’s Fang Druid. SO. What makes this option so good? What makes me so confident that this class blow the normal ranger out of the water in just about any category? Well let’s go down the list


The nature’s fang druid is basically a slayer druid mix. What does that mean? All of the neat tricks from the slayer, with most of the neat tricks from the hunter. Slayer talents? Check. That also means it has Ranger Combat Styles, off the bat. Also, if you pick up some Shifting Jerkin, guess what? You can change your combat styles in your downtime, since they are slayer talents! Along with Studied target (which helps make up for that BAB drop). You also get a baby sneak attack. Animal companion? You are the OG, fully leveled, and ready to go! Now it’s not as tricked out as the Hunter’s but luckily you are a druid. Which means you get access to druid spells. All 0-9 of them. That’s right. You can do all of this and you are a FULLCASTER. The difference between a full 0-9 caster and a quarter caster is worlds away, and the dizzying array of abilities and powers you can get from spells ALONE make it one of the most powerful options on this list, if not THE most powerful.


But we can go farther. How? Choose the Samsaran race. With the Samsaran race, not only do you get a bonus to some of your favorite stats (Int and Wis) but you also get access to mystic past life, allowing you to pluck ANY ranger spells that didn’t already show up on the druid list, and possibly, a few from other powerful divine classes as well and you get them MUCH faster than the ranger would. 


Also for you trappers out there? With this build, take the Learn Ranger Trap feat. If you want to get into infinitely resetting magic trap cheese, congrats, you actually have the spells to be able to do it and actually craft insane traps!!!! The only things it is really missing are the BAB and some HP, but toughness and FCB should deal with the health and you have more than enough spells to make up for the BAB.

I Wanna Be a Ranger
...Okay. Well...okay. In which case I’ll just...give you a push in the right direction...
* Ranger Combat Styles: Choose the one for the fighting style you intend to use. Best for builds that need help with feats, like Archery, Shield-Bashing or Two Weapon Fighting.
* Favored Enemy: The one you’ll fight the most
* Favored Terrain: The one you’ll be in the most
* Animal Companion: Ask This Guy
* Feats: 
   * General: 
      * Fey Spell Versatility: Adds a lot of possible versatility to your list.
      * Dreamed Secrets: More spell versatility
      * Boon Companion: To fix up your animal companion.
      * Beast Speaker: Neat animal companion
      * Favored Defense: Add half favored enemy to AC
   * Combat:
      * STR Melee: Power Attack
      * DEX Melee: Weapon Finesse, Slashing Grace/Fencing Grace/Dervish Dancer, Piranha Strike
      * Ranged: Point Blank Shot, Percise Shot, Manyshot, Clustered Shot, Rapid Shot, Deadly Aim
* Archetypes (thanks UnArcaneElection on Paizo):
   * Divine Marksman: Really good at archery
   * Hooded Champion: Swash-ranger
   * Guide: Be Aragon For Real
   * Poison Darter: Blowgun fun time
   * Bow Nomad: having a hard time choosing between Archery and Two-Weapon Fighting? Why not do Two-Weapon Archery? 
   * Divine Tracker -- getting proficiency with your deity's favored weapon is only useful in a few cases, but you also get Warpriest Blessings.
   * Falconer -- get a Bird Companion at 1st level, which becomes full progression at 4th level, and at 6th level starts to be able to Stagger enemies when successfully dive-bombing them (as well as doing serious damage on a confirmed Critical).
   * Flamewarden -- trading out Hunter's Bond for Flaming Weapon a few rounds per day without even an Enhancement Bonus isn't all that hot, but trading out the late Evasion for being able to cast Breath of Life once per day is not too shabby, and later on, being able to remove some nasty conditions from yourself when you get hit by Fire damage is pretty good, and note that unlike many Pathfinder damage-taking-dependent abilities, you don't actually have to be hurt by the Fire damage, and you also get Druid Fire spells, so you could get yourself some Fire Resistance or Immunity and then bomb yourself with Druid Fire spells to cure yourself. You eventually get a Phoenix's death (so be careful at levels 18 and 19), but at 20th level you also get Phoenix's resurrection once per week.
   * Freebooter -- be a pirate, and trade in Favored Enemy for Freebooter's Bane, which is much more flexible and buffs your whole party.
   * Nirmathi Irregular -- trade out flexibility in Favored Enemy and Favored Terrain (you only get the 1st one of each) in exchange for more spell slots and the ability to prepare 1 Druid spell per day. This is a real specialist, but might be good for a campaign like Ironfang Invasion or Kingmaker.
   * Sable Company Marine -- if you want ride a Hippogriff in the service of Korvosa, this is for you.
   * Toxophilite -- never mind Hawkeye, be Lars Andersen, and note that you can even go into a StarCraft 2 Point Defense Drone mode (also demonstrated in the video).
* Spells:
   * 1st Level: Leadblades/Gravity Bow, Long Strider, Resist Energy, Hunter’s Howl
   * 2nd Level: Allfood, Barkskin, Protection From Energy, Spike Growth
   * 3rd Level: Instant Enemy, Magic Fang Greater, Strong Jaw, Thorny Entanglement, Burrow
   * 4th Level: Animal Growth, Bow Spirit, Bloodsworn Retribution, Terrain Bond
* Items:
   * Big Six (Magic Weapon, Magic Armor, Cloak of Resistance, Ring of Protection, Amulet of Natural Armor, Wondrous Item of Stats)
   * Boots of Friendly Terrain: Just get favored terrain boots for wherever else you are going to be.
   * Bracers of the Falcon: Makes you a better archer
   * Irongrip Gauntlet: Use it to grab an oversized bow.
   * Horn of the Huntsman: If you didn’t choose an animal companion, once per day, give your team a full favored enemy bonus for a minute.
