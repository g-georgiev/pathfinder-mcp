# Archetypes

Archetypes
There aren’t many and I STILL will not be reviewing all of them, just the ones that I think are particularly rad. The shaman surprisingly has REALLY powerful archetypes and I don’t know how to handle that so uh…here is the review.

There are basically 3 types of shamans. The base shaman that has a spirit and a wandering spirit. The speaker for the past shaman which has no wandering spirits but really good abilities and the unsworn shaman that has no base spirit but a bazillion hexes. Those are the three playstyles…have fun with them.


All Archetypes Review: Shaman
 (4.5/5) Overseer [Spirit of Domination]
	What you gain
	What you lose
	* Controlling Magic: Oh hey you replace your main spirit magic with…actually pretty good mind affecting spells. 
* Spirit Surge: Your familiar can deliver your mind spells as touch attacks and the CL Goes up by 2 for some reason.


	* Spirit Magic Spells: Well yeah…it replaces them but…ya know…if they were bad this is an upgrade so…yeah??? That's the point?


	Overview: Replace a bad spirits spell list with better spells. I consider this really good for any of the elemental spirits besides flame, or even the lore spirit, who has REALLY cool abilities but their spell list is mostly meh. Like seriously you lose basically nothing and you get a cool spell list out of it….Woo?
	

 (5.5/5) ✰Serendipity Shaman✰ [Fortune’s Favorite]
	What you gain
	What you lose
	* Luck Magic: Replace your normal spell list with the spell list from the luck domain…which includes protection from energy, freedom of movement, and miracle so…not a bad pick up at all?????
* Luck Hexes: Literally just get more hexes to choose from that are all really good and also one of them gives you back your spirit magic spells if you liked them omg


	* Limited Calling: RAW you can only take this amazing totally busted archetype if you have a racial trait that has luck in the name…or you take the defiant luck feat which anyone who counts as human can take…you know…the ones with the really cool FCB.


	Overview: This is the best shaman archetype. It may be your favorite but you lose NOTHING and get great spells, new hex options and overall just feels amazing. This is a straight upgrade to the base shaman, and can make almost any spirit at least somewhat useful. I am not overhyping this…it’s that good. Stacks with Speaker for the Past
	





 (4.5/5) Speaker For The Past [History’s Strongest]
	What you gain
	What you lose
	* Mysteries of the Past: So this ends up giving you a few class skills…and a total of 18 spells added to your spell list which are pretty fucking good. This does NOT replace the spirit magic from your chosen spirit…just free extra spells which we, as a shaman, love.
* Revelations of the Past: You get ORACLE revelations…from TIME or ANCESTORS which are pretty good! And you can pick 6 of them! That's REALLY GOOD!!! Some revelations give you haste and shit. 


	* Familiar: YOWCH. Okay so as much as this hurts me, losing your familiar sucks…but its also losing a vulnerability. If your familiar gets capped, you can’t cast, and I can’t say that losing that fear is…bad but you do lose some versatility…but we can just take wasp familiar if we want a familiar back.
* Wandering Spirit: Okay this one ACTUALLY hurts. One of the shamans strongest points is its flexibility and being locked into a spirit makes your choices matter that much more. It means you can’t really AFFORD to choose a less than ideal one and you can’t pull off some wild tricks, like using arcane enlightenment.
* Wandering Hex: So this is not ideal…but its also not the worst? We can still abuse spirit talker and ritual hex Still not…a great feeling.


	Overview: So this one is definitely a hard one to rate because it gives you…lots of spells and fucking oracle revalations (which i argue you should count as having the revelations class feature), and you no longer have to worry about your furry friend getting nuked by a fireball and being unable to be a team player. On that note…it does make your shaman much more specialized, which isn’t impossible to deal with but in a way, completely changes the nature of the class. So my review is that it is an amazing archetype for people who want to play a not-shaman or just don’t want to have to make choices every day. 

Stacks with serendipity shaman for some reason.
	





 (4/5) Unsworn Shaman [I Heard You Liked Hexes…]
	What you gain
	What you lose
	* Minor Spirit: Aka, Hex on Hex on Hex. Basically every single day you can get a extra hex the shaman…or witch hexes. Every day. And then you can get two..then three…then more! You only get them every 4 levels, but they are swappable!
* Wandering Spirit: Get wandering spirit a level earlier and later on get two!
* Spirit Animal: You can pick between your wandering spirits for your spirit animal bonus.


	* Hexes: So according to this FAQ you keep the hex class feature but you can ONLY get hexes via the minor spirit class feature. This means you end up with less than half of the hexes you’d normally have!!! That BLOWS!!!
* Spirit: So you just don't get a main spirit progression…which basically means all of your spirit abilities are delayed which kinda sucks…but your wandering spirits still get your spirit magic, and you have access to all of the hexes you need so…its not actually that big of a loss.
* Wandering Hex: You get a better version of this so whatever.


	Overview: So if the Speaker of the Past is the least shaman shaman then the unsworn shaman is the MOST SHAMAN. Fuck making any solid choices, here is a bazillion hexes, go to down and switch it up every single day. Losing your permanent hexes hurts because you lose a lot of your reliability as your entire kit is basically made to be changed each day but you do have unparalleled versatility. 

Still I can’t say that I’d prefer this to the base shaman. Still since you keep the hex class feature, you can still take extra hex!!!! Woo!!!! Use it to fill in your hex hole.
