# Blood Hexes

Blood Hexes
So blood hexes are a specific type of feat that both the shaman and witch can take in place of a normal hex. Blood hex feats work only against targets you have dealt hit point damage to recently, with the exact time frame and any requirements for how you deal the damage outlined in each feat. The saving throw DC of a blood hex is always equal to 10 + 1/2 your character level + your Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma modifier (whichever is highest).   


A shaman or witch can use blood hexes an unlimited number of times per day, but still cannot target the same creature with the same blood hex more than once every 24 hours. All blood hexes with a duration of 1 round can be extended normally using the cackle hex or the chant hex.


Since you have to deal damage to use them, best used on fighty shamans, the rare few of you that there are,


* (5/5) Abeyance: Bab someone with a metal weapon and then seal all of their SLA’s. This can absolutely trivalize some encounters, and is one of the few blood hexes that are actually worth trying to set up for.
* (1/5) (TH-3/5) Blunt Blade: Kinda meh, but if you happen to find yourself fighting an army of ninjas, this is a great way to ruin their day.
* (1/5) Bull's Eye: TOoooo many pre-reqs not worth it, hard pass.
* (1/5) Cataract: Would have been cool but its too specific.
* (2/5) Consumption: While this IS annoying you probably aren’t going to time out the enemies resources…so its not that helpful.
* (3/5) Falter: Movement halved and no 5 foot step. It’ll need to be cackled to keep it going but its not a bad debuff at all!
* (4/5) Hinder (Blood Hex): Reflex save or no full attacks for you, which can absolutely DESTROY most enemies in melee. Not being able to full attack basically makes full bab useless, and multiple natural attacks less helpful, turning tigers into kittens. Keep chant and making it impossible for them to do what they want to do.
* (1/5) Uncertainty (Blood Hex): MAke someone bad at social skill checks. Pass!
