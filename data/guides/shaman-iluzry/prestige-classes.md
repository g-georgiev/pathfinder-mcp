# Prestige Classes

Prestige Classes
So in a surprising turn of events, the shaman mutliclasses very poorly…but prestige classes exceedingly well! Most of your hexes don’t have limits and after level 8, you can usually feel free to jump into whatever you want knowing that you probably have the upgraded version of whatever hexes that you dO have, your greater spirit manifestation, and both wandering spirit and hex, with your spirit magic scaling with your spellcasting, not your shaman levels so…in a lot of classes you stand to gain a lot! Lets find out just how much.

Just remember, After Level 8 as a shaman, you can go into whatever you want. You do not want to prestige before then
* (5/5) Stargazer
   * Gives you everything you could ask for and more! 
      * You get hexes (which can be cribbed from the heavens list too)
      * It scales your familiar, 
      * Gives you MORE good spells and a DOMAIN so you get those juicy domain slots annnd oracle revelations without giving you the mystery so you aren’t locked.
   * If that wasn’t enough its full progression and gives you EVEN MORE WILD ABILITIES. Seriously, if you can’t think of what you want to do with your shaman, just take this after level 8 and enjoy being the most helpful teammate ever.
* (5/5) Evangelist 
   * Lose one level to progress everything you had before along with the amazing boons of the evangelist. It’s very very good! You also get more skillpoints which we love. 
   * All you lose is 1 level of spellcasting, which blows but like…we can live with that.
   * You wanna know how to break this? Allerseelen has you covered On Bended Knee: A Guide to Pathfinder's Obedience Feats. Trust me, its worth the read.
* (4/5) Brewkeeper (Take Prestigious Spellcaster)
   * If you wanna rock the mad doctor vibe, a brewkeeper is a great choice for you! You get a little bit of free metamagic, some fun potion debuffs, and you can hand out your spells as draughts so that your other teammates can use them as they need or hand it to your familiar to pass out!
* (4.5/5) Pathfinder Savant (Take Prestigious Spellcaster)
   * AYYYE adding spells from other peoples list to yours! This gets you a total of 9 neato nifty spells that you can enjoy, though their levels might be messed with a bit. Still for a shaman, esoteric magic is a godsend as we cherry-pick the best spells from everywhere!
   * On top of that you get a bunch of cool item abilities, and the ability to cast greater dispel magic spontaneously! THAT'S RAD.
* (5/5) Argent Dramaturge
   * So the anti outsider performances are cute and all, and they will come in handy sometimes but we are here for the BARDIC MASTERPIECES which we can pick up every other level. We LOVE bardic masterpieces here, as they can often be hilariously powerful, and godly team abilities, and unlike the bard, you don’t really have anything that competes with them.
   * Best options Include: Blazing Rondo (Haste++), Battlesong of the People's Revolt (Tactician??), Music Beyond The Spheres (Limited Wish for ability damage…that you can totally heal off), Symphony of the Elysian Heart (Freedom of Movement Mass), or Waning Bolero (Slow++)
* (5/5) Loremaster (One Level Dip)
   * 5/5 for a one level dip? Loremaster must give you something really powerful! Nope. All it gives us is access to the Secret’s class feature, and most secrets are incredibly lame..
   * BUTTTTTTT you need secrets to pick up Secret of Magical Discipline which allow you to cast ANY SPELL from ANY CLASS at that classes spell level once a day. Not at it +2 or +1. Same level. This is dumb
   * Take the feat to get another use of ultimate anyspell.
