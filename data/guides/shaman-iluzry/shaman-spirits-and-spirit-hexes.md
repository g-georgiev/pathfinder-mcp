# Shaman Spirits and Spirit Hexes

Shaman Spirits and Spirit Hexes
Okay this is our MAIN class choice! Each spirit determines what spells we can cast spontaneously with spirit magic, what boon our spirit animals get, a number of neato abilities, and most importantly, what HEXES we have access to. That’s a lot! 


That said it is almost important to remember that we get access to Wandering Spirit! Once per day, we can get access to another spirits' spells, and manifestations, and temporary access to the hexes that said spirit has! This means, much like hexes from before, we have to rate spirits as a Normal Rating (?/5) or a Wandering Spirit Rating (WS-?/5).


Now when reviewing hexes, we have to think about how they rate as permanent hexes versus how they rate as temporary hexes of any kind (wandering hex, spirit talker, ritual hex, etc). As such each hex will have a Normal Rating (?/5) and a Temporary Hex Rating (TH-?/5). If you dont see a temporary hex rating, that just means its about as a good as a temp hex as a permanent one. Note that for the base hexes, you technically can’t wandering hex them, but they still get a temp rating for the other options.


Example: (4/5) (TH - 5/5) Cackle (Su)


 (3.5/5) Ancestors (WS-4/5)
* Overview: Spirit that provides a lot of support options, has a pretty damn good spell list and some okay abilities. Overall, it definitely feels better as a wandering spirit, but if you pick it as a main spirit it probably won’t lead you wrong.
* Spirit Magic Spells: unseen servant (2nd), spiritual weapon (4th), heroism (6th), spiritual ally (8th), telekinesis (10th), greater heroism (12th), ethereal jaunt (14th), vision (16th), astral projection (18th)
* Spirit Animal Boon: Little buddy can speak and understand languages up to your charisma modifier! Got ourselves a little polyglot for a familiar. This also means if your familiar has hands, but normally couldn’t speak…this gives it speech! Woo! Otherwise its nothing huge.
* Spirit Abilities:
   * (1st) Ancestors Council: So you are basically spending your standard action to give your teammate a +2 to all attack rolls, saving throws, and skill checks they make before the beginning of your next turn...which is a really nice buff if you just want to give someone an extra bit of boost.
   * (8th) Ancestral Weapon: So I know that I said I don’t want you using weapons…and I don’t! But nothing in this says you have to choose the SAME weapon, only that you summon a weapon from your history, so this is a neat way of just getting temporary omniproficient on the occasion you DO need to wack someone.
   * (16th) Ancestral Guardian: It’s literally just planar ally..which means its as good as your DM lets it be because you are not in control of what you call with planar ally. Sad face!
   * (20th) Ancestor Spirit Manifestation: You get blindsense 60, a bonus to will saves equal to charisma, and free astral projection which is basically a free life. Not bad buddy.
* Spirit Hexes:
   * (3/5) Ancestral Blessing (Su): Baby bonus to attack and damage! Does not stack with inspire courage…and also doesn’t have ways to buff it very much. Still, its a force multiplier! 
   * (3/5) (TH-4/5) Ghost Blade (Su): Touch a creature to grant it ghost touch abilities! I doubt you’ll be fighting ghosts all the time, so this makes a much better temporary hex than a normal one.
   * (2/5) Intercessor (Su): So speak with dead is neat as an idea but you probably don’t have that many corpses you NEED to interrogate. Still, if you happen to have a murder happy party, might get some use out of it. A little bit worst because you DO get it as a spell.
   * (3.5/5) Might of the Fallen (Su): Restoration, the hex!!! Now they can only be targeted once, but we can probably hex vulnerability that. This comes up LESS often than normal healing but is still a nice trick to have up your sleeves when you don’t have time for NORMAL restoration.
   * (4/5) Wisdom of the Ages (Su): AYYYEEE if we dumped int now we don’t care! Wisdom to all knowledges is actually a pretty dope boon.






(2/5) Battle (WS-3/5)
* Overview: The battle spirit is one that vaguely wants you to do melee combat but doesn’t really give you any new proficiencies or a lot of strong and consistent tools to pull that off. Really, you can occasionally use it as a wandering spirit if you want to have a few buffs, but in most cases, I think you can probably just manage with divine favor and a good racial weapon.

* Spirit Animal Boon: +2 bonus to natural armor for your little buddy. This isn’t the worst boon but it also isn’t the best. In general we don’t want them to be anywhere near the fight!
* Spirit Abilities:
   * (1st) Battle Spirit (Su): Okay so this is either okay good hope, or REALLY REALLY bad heroism depending on how its ruled. Technically this ability doesn’t tell you how you MAINTAIN it even though it clearly is limited by rounds per day. So does it last one round and you have to restart it? Does it last multiple and you can decide when it ends? Unclear.
   * (8th) Enemy Bane (Su): Oh hey swift action bane weapon. We absolutely adore this, no questions asked. Free damage, more accuracy and it lasts a whole minute. Woo!
   * (16th) Paragon of Battle (Su): So by this point if you WANTED to cast enlarge person, you could definitely cast quickened enlarge to get this effect but with a better buff to go along with it than deadly juggernaut. Ya know, like divine power, which is only a level higher. That said it IS supernaturally and unable to be dispelled so…take that for what you will/
   * (20th) Battle Spirit Manifestation: You get pounce, your crits ignore DR, you are a little harder to crit and a little harder to kill. Not bad, but at level 20 we could probably be doing better.
* Spirit Hexes:
   * (3/5) Battle Master (Ex): Two okay feats an extra AoO. Not bad if you are trying to be in the fight! 
   * (3.5/5) (TH - 4/5) Battle Ward (Su): Deflection bonuses are fairly common and don’t stack but this gives you a way to hand it out to basically your entire team for free and it lasts all day. Definitely worth the pickup if you don’t have something better. Best if you can swap it out later!
   * (2/5) (TH-1/5) Curse of Suffering (Su): Errr is no save but its also bleed damage…and I guess making it harder to heal. Still it’d be better to get a hurt move.
   * (1/5) Eyes of Battle (Su): Err there are better uses for our swift action really. Let’s pass.
   * (4/5) Hampering Hex (Su): Finally something solidly useful! A -4 to ac is a serious hit and actually 100% worth using if you can make it stick! Stack it with Evil Eye to continue giving someone the worst day ever. Goes down a point because it cant be extended with CHANT!
