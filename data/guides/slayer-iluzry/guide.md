Rating System
All Guides use the Color Rating System and honestly? It’s a good Idea. 
* Game Breaking (6/5): This feature or option is so powerful, so useful, so unapologetically ridiculous that it very well make snap the game in half. DM’s reading this guide are welcome to ban these options, or are warned to somehow work around them. Players? Use these powers carefully.
* Take Me Now (5/5): Class defining. Taking them changes the game, or adds so much power it's insane not to.
* Fantastic (4/5): Incredibly solid, Not class breaking, but something that can add a fair bit of power and versatility to the character.
* Fine, I Guess (3/5): Pretty good. They'll be helpful and have some use, but aren't likely to make things incredibly amazing. 
* Maybe Don’t (2/5): Unhelpful. It probably doesn’t...do much for you to pick these. Either they make your life slightly harder, or the bonuses just aren't very relevant to what you do.
* Actively Bad (1/5): God why. These options are the least helpful. They either are pointless, replaceable or completely outdated. These are abilities that will likely never be used and make you ask why they are there in the first place.
* Unrated: This is rare but some options are unratable. Likely a class feature that is powered on DM fiat, in which case...ask the DM, something that is campaign dependent or something that literally does nothing. 




Class Overview
Way too many people write this class off for being “straight foward” or “just a better ranger.” Every class has its quirks, and are often worth exploring why this specific niche was filled, and what fantasy it can play. I want to show you what the slayer can bring to your table.

The slayer is a class with DOZENS of possible build paths, from TWF, to Shield Bashing, to Skill Monkey. What’s more, it can handle multiple roles at once! If you wanna kill, this class has got you covered, in more ways than you would expect.
The Chassis
* (4/5) Hit Die - d10: Pretty god damn solid. This is the fighter hit dice and honestly will start the trend of “fighting with the fighter”. Anyway, it’ll super keep you alive but it isn’t beating out your barbarian.
* (5/5) BAB - FULL: It’s the best you can get! You can slay at full capacity with any weapon that you desire.
* (5/5) Skills - 6 + INT: So to my knowledge, the slayer has the highest amount of skills amongst all FULL bab classes (tied with the ranger), which gives you access to great in combat and out of combat options, while also expanding your kit! We love this, especially since one of the major concerns with A LOT of martials is that they just dont tend to have a lot of skills.
   * Class Skills Worth Paying attention to:
      * Acrobatics (Dex): AoO’s suck, and being able to ninja your way to your opponent is rad.
      * Bluff (Cha): Lying is always helpful to murder, and if you want to attempt to feint for sneak attacking, you’ll need this.
      * Heal (Wis): Hey, if you put effort into this, its actually some serious healing, and like…its fair.
      * Intimidate (Cha): You can make life harder on your enemies occasionally, and if you plan to take the Shatter Defenses route to get sneak attack, you’ll need this.
      * Know. Dungeoneering (Int): Knowing things is useful.
      * Perception (Wis): Most rolled skill in the game, also its nice to be able to see people that you want to kill.
      * Sense Motive (Wis): Knowing when people are lying is very helpful, and if you build into it, you can get bonuses on initiative and basically read people's minds occasionally so…neat!
      * Stealth (Dex): Being able to get out of sight and then murder people is kinda what you are all about, and gets your your sneak attack. Also, its much easier to win if people don’t see you coming.
   * Non Class Skills Worth Paying Attention To:
      * Know. Arcana/Nature/Planes/Religion (Int): Knowing things is useful.
      * Sleight of Hand (Dex): Sometimes, you’ll want to be subtle with what you are doing. This is good for that.
      * Use Magic Device [UMD] (Cha): Being able to use items that you shouldn’t is incredibly helpful, and gives you more options. Definitely worth picking up.
* (4/5) Saves - Good Fort and Ref: So you have two good saves, buuuut not to the saves we REALLY want as a martial, which would be Fort and Will. That said, this is still helpful. It’s not the worst by a long shot.
* (4/5) Weapon And Armor Proficiencies: So this gives you basically everything you will ever need, and is great BUT is lacking distinctly in heavy armor, which we do love for AC. Still, that besides, you’ll rarely be wanting, and can kill with anything from a shotgun, to a longsword. Let’s go!
Class Features
* (5/5) Studied Target (Ex): A.k.a, Fuck you in particular. So this ability is…massively useful. Even if the bonuses seem small, you get an untyped bonus to both your attack and damage against a target, can activate it as a move action at the beginning and eventually as a swift action, AND it lasts until you turn it off OR until its dead. Since martials aren’t particularly known for aoe’s, this really shouldn’t be a problem if you know how to pick your fights.

As if that wasn’t enough, these bonuses ALSO apply to ALL OF YOUR DC’S 

This, somehow, boosts your out of combat versatility as well by giving you a bonus to Bluff, Knowledge, Perception, Sense Motive, and Survival rolls. Why is this important? Well there is no indication you are using the ability, and it can be activated at any time with no usage limit…which means that if you are ever rolling a knowledge on a person you are in the room with or sense motive, or a perception that’s a free bonus. 

This is a killer ability, and is what makes Slayers so fucking deadly. Abuse it.
* (1/5) Track: okay so you just get a bonus to a skill…that almost never gets used…for something you almost never use it for. Uh…just..just let the wizard scry okay?
* (5/5) Slayer Talents: HEYYYYY OUR FUN TALENT BOX. Every two levels, we get one of these little ditties! It’s relatively small BUT gets larger once you know that you can pick up things from your two parent classes: Ranger Combat Styles (which can get you a bunch of feats early) and Rogue Talents (Which come from a specific list). Both of these are super useful, and yes, I will be going over every single one. Remember, that your studied target boosts these DC’s.
* (4/5) Sneak Attack: Hey look, it’s FREE DAMAGE in exchange for SMART GAMEPLAY. Wow. Love this. Now, you get less than the rogue proper, only going up to 6d6 damage at level 18, but like all sneak attack damage, it stacks with other forms of sneak attack damage, and comes with a LOT of content to help make you even more of a threat.

That said, you will want to build around this in some way. Either by getting amazing at flanking and getting a familiar/companion, feinting or breaking defenses, you wanna get this as often as possible!
* (3/5) Stalker: Add Disguise, Intimidate, and Stealth to skills buffed by studied combat. Both intimidate and stealth enjoy the free bonuses frankly, so while its not huge, you’ll probably get some use out of it.
* (5/5) Advanced Talents: Wow slayer talents, but potentially better! This also gets us Advanced Rogue/Ninja Talents and the silly talent of Assassinate. Woo new options!
* (2/5) Swift Tracker: Never seen this situation come up even once. If it is coming up…well then ya know its sorta helpful then.
* (3/5) Slayers Advance: 1/day, rush a guy with double move speed as a move action. This doesn’t get many uses, but you’ll love it when someone is trying to kite you and you remind them that you kill people for a living.
* (4/5) Quarry: As a standard action, you choose someone to super die. You auto track them (which is whatever) and get an additional +2 insight bonus to attack anddddd all crits are autoconfirmed, which can be very fun if you are doing a TWF sneak attack build. If you kill your quarry, you can select a new one an hour after it is confirmed dead.
* (3/5) Improved Quarry: Easier to track, bonus goes up to +4, and if it dies you can choose a new one in 10 minutes. Neat.
* Capstones: As always, this is your ultimate ability and super mega helpful. We love this.
   * (4/5) Mastery Slayer (Ex): So the bad news is that this is a fort save and a standard action. The good news is that this is an extraordinary ability, and we boost our save DC’s using studied target. When this works, you can immediately remove powerful opponents from the field no diff, as this SOMEHOW does not count as a death effect, and works on basically anything. When it doesn’t, well, you’ve wasted your turn. Welcome to the final art of Omnicide.
   * (1/5) Against The Odds (Ex): You can study two more targets…yay…I guess…remember how we can do this as a swift action to like 5 people now? I think that's enough in most cases.
   * (2/5) The Right Spot (Ex): Get sneak attack without having to flank or feint once per round. Now it isn’t like we don’t like sneak attacks, but by this level, you should have a way of doing it reliably. Good for when you are in an unfortunate situation and need that little extra kick, but little else.
   * (5/5) With This Sword (Ex): You gain a Noble Phantasm minor artifact!!! This is not only powerful, but also really really cool. Love that. Even cooler because you probably have a super holy avenger/unholy avenger by now.
   * (4/5) Won’t Stay Dead (Ex): Once per week, when you should lose, you don’t. 
   * (5/5) Perfect Body, Flawless Mind (Ex): Get 8 points that can be added to any ability score in any way. 5 to one, 3 to another. This is always helpful and people love free stats.
   * (5/5) The Boss (Ex): Get super leadership! Luckily everything else is so busted at this level, so this is fine.



Roles and Ability Scores
Now, it’s sort of hard to talk about what ability scores you need without talking about what roles the Slayer can take. So first, let’s cover the roles that they can take, and we’ll choose abilities based on that. Try to have a 16 in your main stat and a 14 in your secondary stat. All of the following reviews are for the Base Class, without any archetypes, multiclasses, or Prestige Classes which would augment their ability. 

Also gonna go a bit easier on the names this time, just for my own sanity.
(5/5) Archery/Firearm (Ranged) [Dex >>> Strength > Con/Wis/Int > Cha]
Haha, yeah you knew this was coming. With access to bonus feats, studied target, and ranger combat styles, you can be a godly ranged combatant. Moreover, remember, while its difficult, you CAN sneak attack from range, which is just more free damage, and you’ll get one proc per hit, which ranged builds are known to have a lot of! Their extra feats also mean they can mess around with firearms, if you are feeling winchester enough.
(5/5) Two Weapon Fighting (Melee/Ranged) [Dex/Str > Con/Wis > Int Cha]
So remember how we love getting attacks? Like LOVE it? Welcome to two weapon fighting, something that rogues have been doing for years to get the most out of sneak attack, but we can do better because we get free bonuses to attack to offset our penalties AND full bab to be more accurate in general. Not to mention, again, Ranger Combat Styles for early feats. You wanna be buffy? This is how you buffy.
(5/5) Combat Maneuver Demon (Melee) [Str/Dex > Con/Int > Wis/Cha]
So there are actually some fun talents and archetypes around various maneuvers, but the long and short of it is that you can give yourself more bonuses to make it harder to resist, and get extra feats to be able to keep up. Specifically, I think slayers will really enjoy building into Dirty Trick Builds, which are annoying as fuck, but super helpful team wise.
(5/5) Two Handed (Melee) [Str > Con/Dex > Int/Wis > Cha]
If you don’t want to get complicated about it? Handle it the old fashion Guts way, and just pick up a claymore or a great sword and then just cut into evil. Everything said above with apply to you, your full bab lets you take advantage of power attack more often, and nothing says you can't sneak attack with a great sword so like…pop off my slayer.
(4/5) Skill Monkey (Support) [Int > Cha > Str/Dex/Wis/Con]
So when you think slayer, you don’t really imagine a skill monkey but…they can actually have a fair number of them. You see, most of your abilities are stat agnostic, and the few you do get seems to bend between Intelligence and Charisma. Moreover, studied target gives you even more bonuses to various skills that are pretty useful so yeah… you aren’t bad at this! Don’t be afraid to be batman!
(4/5) Party Face [Cha > Int/Wis > Str/Dex/Con]
So if we go out from like all skills, and specifically into face, again, not what you’d think of when you see slayers but again…apparently they are good at this! You get bonuses to bluff, intimidate, sense motive, and you probably wouldn’t be too bad at diplomacy if you tried, and you have the skills to spend. 
(3/5) Tank [Con >>> Str/Dex/Cha > Int/Wis]
So…you have a d10 hitdie and you CAN do this, but tanking in pathfinder is difficult, especially if you don’t have any damage mitigation abilities or way to draw specific agro other than being scary. Again, doable with the right feats, but being a person without heavy armor makes this a little bit more difficult.
Summary Arrays
In general, you want Strength or Dex for attack, Con to survive, and can probably live without  charisma. Some of your DC’s are based on your intelligence, so if you are leaning into that, put that up there with your con, and if you are doing charisma based stuff the opposite.

Races
So here’s the thing about races. Besides ya know, flavor for your campaign and your character… they don’t actually… matter. They are balls of stats and abilities, which is good! If you want to play a human fighter, but you want the elf stat block, really there isn’t much reason to deny you because again...they are just stat balls. If your dm doesn’t let you split your flavor and your stats, that’s fine! Either way, I think its more important to rate races on what they give you, rather than what they are. I won’t go into a lot of detail on any one race, but I’ll give you the gist of what is worth looking at.


The Slayer as a class is one that is fairly open stat wise, given the range of things it can do. In general, you will get a lot of benefit from putting energy into strength, but if you want to do dex, you can! If you want to do ranged you can! Really that makes rating races based off of their stats a bit difficult, so instead, i’ll try rating them on what they can bring to ya know…a slayer.

These guys are monsters

      * Munavri (+4 Dex, +2 CON/INT/WIS/CHA, -2 STR): Munavri is a dumb race that gets a bonus to all stats, except strength, get’s us free spell resistance that isn’t bad, telepathy, and once per day can use spell trigger items for classes she shouldn’t be able to so...yeah no great stuff.

      * Drider (+2 STR/DEX/WIS, +4 CON): Great spell resistance, you start large which means more reach and damage, you are incredibly hard to trip, can climb up walls, get fast movement for free, and some extra AC as the cherry on top. Amazingly amazing.

They are absolutely LOADED with goodies for us to abuse, like what even is this??? Also, since you count as a gnome, you can take their FCB and get extra slayer talents. Woo!!!!

Trade-out baits: Defensive Training (unless you are in a campaign against a lot of Giants), Greed, Hatred (unless you are in a campaign against Goblinoids or Orcs), and possibly Weapon Familiarity (although it does give some nice options).” - UnArcaneElection

Slayer Talents
So the slayer gets one of these every two levels and these will be your main form of customizing your specific slayer besides its archetype and fighting style choice. Because of this, I will be reviewing every talent, with special subsections for Rogue Talent and Ranger Combat Styles, because both of those are a massive barrel of monkeys.


Base Talents
         * (2/5) Aligned Sneak Attack*: Okay so reducing DR is good…but reducing it only for yourself is kinda meh, even if it is alignment DR. Moreover, without some er…shenanigans, you can only remove up to 6 DR from an opponent…which uh….ain’t much.
         * (4/5) Sever Alignment: HAHAHAHA Now this is more like it! Have them lose access to all alignment based DR, Regen and SPELLS with alignment descriptors for 1 round per 2d6 sneak attack die. Yes it’s a fort save…but we can pump those with studied target, and there are a LOT of creatures with alignment subtypes in pathfinder.
         * (4/5) Blood Reader: Okay so like…in game this will not help you kill anyone faster…but it’s always nice to be able to metagame and see who you are going up against, as this lets you see the EXACT NUMBER of hp they have left. So not mandatory, but you will love it if you take it.
         * (3/5) Deadly Range: So you can only take this at level 4 but it increases the range at which you can ranged sneak attack (which is usually 30ft). Rogue Talents (*)
First, let's get the combat trick ruling out of the way. Basically, unlike normal rogue talents that rogues can take, there is no line that says something along the lines of “A slayer cannot select an individual rogue talent more than once” only that each time you take this talent, you gain a new one.

Now because of this, there is some contention on whether or not new means “unique and different” or “just another one that you wouldn’t have had before”. I for one am willing to lean towards a more lenient ruling and say you can take any talent you want as many times as you want, because they didn’t say you couldn’t and this is a specific case. For example, in advanced talents, there is a line that specifically states that you can only choose each advanced rogue talent/ninja trick once, whereas that line is absent from the base rogue talent rules.

Most detractors of this argument I’ve seen have two opinions. 1.) That rogues are beholden to these rules, and thus you should be and 2.) That this may allow a slayer become “more powerful” than the fighter by choosing a combat trick 10 times.

In response I think that is fair to say that a slayer is different from a rogue and is clearly designed to be a much more active combatant than the rogue is, and being slightly stronger than the fighter isn’t game breaking. Hell, the fighter still gets advanced weapon training, which is amazing.

TDLR; I will be rating rogue talents as if you can take any rogue talent as many times as you want, but their effects don’t stack. If you DON’T agree with this at your table, that's okay, just treat rogue talents overall as 1 level lower and you can still have fun.

Prestige Classes
So I hear a lot of chatter online that when it comes to prestige classes, you shouldn’t even consider them, as they are far and wide inferior to just progressing your class. While I agree that a vast majority of prestige classes are not designed with power in mind, I would say the same thing about the vast majority of options in pathfinder in general. As such, I think its important to note when prestige classing could be good. 

In particular, for the slayer, prestige classes can let you either focus into an ability, or diversify your skill sets, if not both. The slayer is AMAZING at dealing damage, especially in melee, and excels when given the chance to do so, but from there, lacks a lot of variety outside of its skills and feats. As such, prestige classes can give the slayer really interesting abilities that can expand its capabilities, or enhance ones that are already available to improve the experience.

               * Sword of Subtlety: +4 bonus to attack and damage when we sneak attack. We should be doing this a lot.
               * Menacing (Weapon Enhancement): Hey you are going to be flanking a lot. This lets you get even higher bonuses for doing that. Free bonuses are nice. Take it.
               * Dueling (Weapon Enhancement): Hey gives you a bonus to initiative and feint checks…yeah take that shit.
               * Keen (Weapon Enhancement): Increase the Critical window by 1! Remember, increasing the window does so on both sides. If you plan on doing a lot of hits, ya know like TWF or building around crits, this is kinda essential. 
               * Magic Armor
               * Armor of the Shadow Lord: Dope intelligent armor that somehow makes you better and sneak attack and speaks with you telepathically. NICE!
               * Cloak of Resistance
               * Stat-boosting item (headbands for mental stats, belts for physical)
               * Ring of Protection
               * Amulet of Natural Armor
               * Bag of Holding / Handy Haversack / Portable Hole
               * (5/5) Shifting Jerkin: FASTEST PURCHASE OF YOUR LIFE. Having the ability to swap out your talents can be SUPER RAD and basically open you up to a lot of versatility on a day to day basis.
               * (5/5) Lenses of Predator’s Gaze: Temporarily boost your studied target bonus as if you were 5 levels higher!!! YOOOOOOO THIS IS DOPE TAKE THIS SHIT ASAP!!!! 
               * Snipers Goggles: Are you planning on doing sneak attack from range? Well congrats, these are mandatory! Yep, you heard what I said…MANDATORY!!! 
               * Bloodstained Gloves: Extra damage for bathing in the blood of your enemies. FUN STUFF!!!!
               * Slayer’s Robe: Sneaky cloak of resistance that also allows you to turn into a sneaky cloud. Very sneak. Much hide.
               * Ring of Invisibility: Lets you be invisible…buff said.
               * Burglar’s Buckler: Buckler full of thieves tools that lets you also get bonuses to dirty tricks..ya know those things you like being good at. Niceu
