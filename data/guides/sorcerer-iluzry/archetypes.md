# Archetypes

Archetypes 
So again, not going to list out every archetype, that’s dumb but I am going to list the ones that I think are super rad, interesting, or just worth talking about, good bad or otherwise. If your favorite archetype didn’t show up, tell me why it’s awesome in the discussion thread and I might just add it. Wouldn’t be the first time. 


So sorcerer archetypes are...weird. So because you basically only have your bloodline and spells, if they are going to take it, it’s going to be from your bloodline...which would make sense but that’s not really how most other archetypes work with similar abilities? Like rarely does a cleric archetype remove one of your cleric domain powers, rather it'll just..remove domains, Because of that, rating what you lose is going to be weird...because it depends on your bloodline.

This gets weirder with the Wildblooded…. “Archetypes”. They basically function like subdomains for bloodlines...but since they count as archetypes, any archetypes that also alter the abilities given don’t work. So lets say we take a wild blood that gives you a shitty 1st level abiltiy and really cool 3rd level ability, and also want to take an archetype that would replace that first level ability with something actually cool.  You can’t do that...even though again, you can do that for arcane schools and cleric domains. So...it’s weird. As a DM I treat these more like subdomains, and you can swap your normal bloodline for a wildblooded one and still replace those abilities as normal as your new “base”. 

Note: I will not be rating the wildblooded archetypes down here. Just didn’t really make sense to have them separate from the other bloodlines so if you were looking for your particular mutant blood, look back up here.


Ratings for All Sorcerer Archetypes


(?/5)* Crossblooded [Dual Wield Destiny]
You get two bloodlines instead of one! Did you find yourself at the crossroads, unable to decide? Were you the child of two sovereign lines? Did you reach out into the void only to have more than one voice reply? Are you the sick combination of a horrendous experiment? Who knows! But you’ve got twice the juice now.
	What you gain
	What you lose
	                                 * You get both bloodline’s Bloodline arcana, and Skills
                                 * You can choose between each bloodline’s Spells (and can learn lower level spells that were not chosen later on), Bonus Feats and Bloodline Powers
                                 * The ability to use the bloodline arcana from BOTH of your bloodlines is MASSIVE and the entire reason why this archetype is a dip classic for other spellcasting classes...they just can't do it like you do. There isn’t really any other way to get access to a bloodline arcana so getting two at once is amazing. Not to mention the versatility of getting to choose which powers or spells you want. Honestly this could be pretty powerful in the right hands...I wonder how they balance it.
	                                 * What do we Lose: One spell known at each level and a -2 to will saves.
                                 * OW FUCK OUCH. As a sorcerer you are more or less STARVED for spells known, which is why the Human FCB is coveted so highly and we don't really have a reason to have a super high Will save either...
	Is it Worth it?: So honestly hard to tell. Spells known are our lifeblood, so to lose one every level is incredibly draining. It means that any level that you’d usually unlock a new spell level (4, 6, etc) you get 0 spells known. That’s right. You are effectively delayed a level. These just happen to be even levels, so you can’t even take a feat to compensate. Moreover, the best say to get new spells known, prestige classes, well...they undercut what you DO get because they don't progress bloodlines. 


There are basically three times when this is worth it.
                                 1. Dipping For Bloodline Arcana: This is the reason most people do it. 1 level, in and out.
                                 2. Very Focused Builds: If you have a specific goal for what you want your sorcerer to do that can take a few spell level hits? Then you can make it work..not suggested, but you can make it work.
                                 3. Words of Power: Since you can combine lower words into higher slots, you don't lose nearly as much versatility, while getting more options.
                                 4. You have a thematic combo: You are here for the fluff or some story crunch thing.

Don’t get me wrong, this can be...magnificently powerful but also, really really really painful if you aren’t perfectly on point. There are too many combinations to be had, so...I just won’t rate it for now. You know what you get, you know what you lose, you all keep it going from there.
	

(1/5) Mongrel Mage [Power Platter]
You choose your bloodline daily! Are you attuned to magic as a whole and have various foci? Are you a creature of the maelstrom, born of chaos that manifests in your power? Do you get your power from constellations, and every night you choose your new benefactor amongst the stars? Well any way, you can now reach out into the weave and take fate into your own hands!
	What you gain
	What you lose
	                                 * Basically everyday you get to pick a different bloodline 
                                 * BUT you can only have it active 3+Level times per day AND when you DO activate it it only lasts CHA mod Rounds.
	                                 * Basically any one stable bloodline.
                                 * Also your bloodline spells are staggered like a lot.
                                 * Also by RAW (since it says you can activate your 7th level powers...which we do not have..instead of your 9th level powers) you cannot use your 9th or 15th level powers until level 20.
                                 * So this becomes an issue because if you run out of your mongrel reservoir you just...dont have a bloodline anymore and you only get one bloodline per day anyway.
	Is it Worth it?: Noooo. It’s not. IT’s really not. Look getting a different bloodline everyday sound super cool, but you don't get your last three powers (until level 20), which are usually really good, your spells are delayed, and what you DO get only lasts rounds equal to your charisma modifier and eventually just using your powers becomes expensive...and when you are out you are just out. So is getting an objectively way worst version of any bloodline for a few seconds each day….I...we can do better. Please.
	

(6.5/5) Razmiran Priest [Deceive The Divine]
You are a false priest, one who has learned how to falsify the power of the divine or perhaps channel it. You may be a miracle worker acting with abilities beyond your understanding or a Charlatan mage, serving the “Living God Razmir” and his wicked schemes. No matter how you came about it, you have found the power of the gods nestled between your fingertips. Who shall be worthy of your salvation?
	What you gain
	What you lose
	                                 * Know. Religion and Perform: Knowing things is always useful and you can perform.
                                 * Get half your class level to UMD checks: Fuccck yes. We were already great at these and now we are even better! I mean this is pretty good in general. It’d be even better if we had a way to use it..
                                 * False Focus: This is objectively better than what it’s replacing, and is actually useful. This will save you A LOT of gp in the longer run. Hell yes.
                                 * Add Aid and Remove Disease to spells known: This is meh but like hey spells are spells.
                                 * Cast from any divine spell trigger or spell completion item: You heard me. Roll UMD, a thing we are already good at and get a bonus to. Remember, skill checks do not fail on nat 1’s. IF you succeed, you can use a spellslot of 1 higher than the actual spell instead of using the charge on the item...FOR ANY DIVINE SPELL. We have spell slots for days. We do not have lots of spells known. This changes EVERYTHING.
	                                 * Fly and Appraisal:  Honestly fly checks aren’t that hard and appraisal was never your bag. 
                                 * Eschew Materials: This was basically useless anyway, so throwing it in the trash doesn’t matter!
                                 * Replaced 2nd and 3rd Level Spells known from your bloodline: Depends entirely on what your bloodline is. Ranges from “good” to “awful”
                                 * 9th Level Bloodline Power: Does this suck? Yes! Does any bloodline power come even CLOSE to what you get in exchange? HElllllllllllllllllllllllllllllllllllllllllllllllllllllllllllllllllllll no.
	Is it Worth it?: Yes, yes a billion times yes. This basically allows you to turn scrolls into your spells known for divine spells...all divine spells. Inquisitor, Ranger, Cleric, Warpriest, Druid, Reliquarian Occultist, Paladin, Shaman, Onmyoji spiritualist, Filidh Bard. All of them. NOW technically you are only using your spell slots instead of the charges...buttt with a few levels of Pathfinder Savant you can have scrolls use your caster level, and some Cypher Mage can make it so that you can draw scrolls as a free action each turn and occasionally use your save DC (if it doesn't already). Congrats on being the Omnitheurge and probably the only Tier 0 spellcaster in the game.
	



(4/5) Seeker [Bloodline Mastery]
You are the seeker! The magic of your bloodline fascinates you, and so by miracle or happenstance you have founds means by which to enhance it. Could it be that your ties to your magic are particularly strong? Maybe you learned the family secrets about how to tame your power? Perhaps the entities that gave you these gifts are pleased with you, and want to show their favor? Nevertheless, you seek greater mastery of your bloodline spells, until they are honed to a point.
	What you gain
	What you lose
	                                 * Tinkering (Ex): Uhh basically trapfinding. You probably aren’t going to get much use out of this unless you are going arcane trickster.
                                 * Seeker Lore (Ex): +4 to concentration checks AND +4 to caster level checks against SR and also knowledge arcana and spellcraft checks related to bloodline spells. The bonus to SR checks is a MASSIVE boon, and constant, so if we have good bloodline spells, we can really get some use out of this. 
                                 * Seeker Magic (Su): When you apply metamagic to bloodline spells, level adjustment goes down by 1...which is amazing! It doesn’t stack with anything else, but it works for ALL bloodline spells and ALL metamagic.
	                                 * Eschew Materials: Eh? It hurts a little for some shenans with alchemical items but you can just take false focus.
                                 * 3rd Level Bloodline Power: Depends entirely on the bloodline. Third level powers are usually either amazing or garbage.
                                 * 15th Level Bloodline Power: Depends entirely on the bloodline, but 15th level powers are far more likely to be useful. 
	Is it Worth it?: Honestly yeah, I think it could be. It replaces your 3rd and 15th level power, and I’ve found a lot of bloodlines get their strength or iconic nature from their 9th level powers….but honestly you don’t lose TOO much. Honestly the fact that you get +4 to SR checks with your bloodline spells is a really nice boon to get so early on! Now this would be even better if you had a way of choosing all of your bloodline spells….did I mention this stacks with Wishcrafter?
	



(3.5/5) Tattooed Sorcerer [Ink Arcana]
The tattooed sorcerer! Magic isn’t simply within you, you wear it across your body in intricate and colorful shapes and images. Were you cursed with this power, having your skin scarred or marked by a greater power? Are you someone from the bard’s college who wants to show that body art is valid? Is this part of an ancient ritual to pass down the magic of your family via crest runes? Either way, now you have sick tricks from your ink job. Let’s make it work!
	What you gain
	What you lose
	                                 * Familiar Tattoo: Familiars are amazing and can do so much. And you can keep yours around safely in sick body art? Hell yeah.
                                 * Mage’s Tattoo: Wouldn’t take it myself, but hey +1 caster level is free caster level.
                                 * Bloodline Tattoos: Mainly flavor but like hey again you look dope and get +1 CL.
                                 * Create Spell Tattoo: Take a spell you know and turn it into a cool ink scroll.
                                 * Enhanced Spell Tattoo: Pick one spell you know at this level that is affected by Mage’s Tattoo and you can now cast it as an SLA once per day. Since you can only change it when you gain bloodline powers, pick something staple so you can trade out the spell known.
	                                 * 1st Level Power: Most of these are bad and don’t scale well anyway. Getting a Familiar is almost always a better choice.
                                 * Eschew Materials: Almost anything is better than this feat so like woo.
                                 * 7th Level Bloodline Feat: This one is a toss up for me. Feats are nice, so if you have a good list this is bad but if you don't this is fine.
                                 * 9th Level Bloodline Power: This is less fun, as these tend to be pretty good. Still only losing one big thing is pretty nice.
	Is it Worth it?: First of all, if you are going to do this, get Inscribe Magical Tattoo. It’s a good feat and is on brand. Anyway, the tattooed sorc is basically an upgrade to the base sorc if your bloodline has a crappy 9th level ability. You don’t lose spells known, bloodline spells, or really anything of note besides that, and in exchange you get some really nice options. Definitely a simple and solid choice for anyone who enjoys magic ink.

Also, if you are willing to take 3 feats (Favored Prestige + Prestigious Spellcaster x2) then consider Tattooed Mystic that can give you standard action summons and the ability to negate or subdue magical effects.
	

(4/5) Wishcrafter (Ifrit Only) [*Rubs Magic Lamp*]
Oh child of the lamp, how did you come upon such mighty power? Are you an Efreeti, now reincarnated as penance for your wicked wishes? Are you a child of flame, inexplicably drawn to the desire of those around you? Perhaps you are favored by an Elemental Lord or have consumed some of their essence and thus now are wracked by their power? Did a shooting star fuck your mom? No matter how you slice it, it’s time to get to wishin!
	What you gain
	What you lose
	                                 * Wishbound Arcana: Mainly thematic but also the crutch mechanic of the archetype. Someone else wishes as a verbal component and gives your spells +1 CL. Only works once per person...remember that.
                                 * Expanded Wishcraft: Pros, You get to choose your bloodline spell list. This is massively amazing. Cons, it only works with wishbound arcana...but hey wishes are limited per person. Get some summons to make wishes and you are golden.
                                 * Heart’s Desire: Force someone to make a wish. It’s a will save and is kinda whatever BUT basically sets up the next ability.
                                 * Twisted Wish: You can fuck with wishes giving people a -4 ON THEIR SAVES so long as they made it. Relies basically on Heart’s Desire but MAN when it works, this hurts.
                                 * Perfect Wishcraft: Cast any spell you want once per day on your list. Hell fucking yes. Still you get it so late, by this point you should be breaking reality anyway.
	                                 * Your Bloodline Arcana: Complete toss up. Either this is awful and the worst or amazing and way better. 
                                 * All Bloodline Spells: So you basically get to pick them instead of getting presets which is always objectively better.
                                 * All Bloodline Feats: So again, it depends on the list, but giving someone a -4 to a save as a will save swift action is pretty nice.
	Is it Worth it?: Actually probably. So a fun note is while this replaces your bloodline arcana and spells, It DOES NOT replace a single one of your powers, which means this is great for bloodlines with amazing powers, but meh spells and arcana, like the Astral or Scorpion Bloodlines


You are basically trading your arcana and feats to pick your bloodline list and get like +1 CL. Definitely worth considering if you don’t have a strong concept in mind for a basic sorcerer. It also stacks with the Seeker Sorcerer archetype, which makes you the undisputed master of your Bloodline Spells.
