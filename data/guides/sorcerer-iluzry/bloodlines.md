# Bloodlines and Heritages

Bloodlines and Heritages
So the sorcerer has...A LOT of bloodlines. People before me have reviewed them and honestly I could just add a few lines to their guides and you’d be good. I could have...and usually would have...but i think for this guide to be truly complete and also up to date, I have to give my own opinions. Which sucks, as we all know that I am quite lazy.

So if you have ready this guide, appreciate the information, but think I rate things like a madwoman, feel free to use any of the previous guides included below. Otherwise, enjoy going down this shit hole with me.


Note: I will be rating the Wildblooded Archetypes here, as my predecessors have. It makes no sense for them to be in archetypes anyway. If you want to see how I rate the rest of the archetypes, well go here. Also remember that Wildblooded Bloodlines still count as the base bloodline for pre-reqs, feats and abilities unless specifically called out.
Note 2: So any bloodline spell that I mark in black means that you probably want it in an item or from Versatile Spontaneity/Paragon Surge/Mnemonic Vestments, but not as a permanent spell known.
Note 3: Rating capstones as if you were a base sorc and had waited until level 20. Things may change if you get it early using a robe of arcane heritage.

(3/5) Aberrant [Warped]
   * Skill: Know. Dungeoneering
   * Bloodline Spells: enlarge person (3rd), see invisibility (5th), tongues (7th), black tentacles (9th), feeblemind (11th), veil (13th), plane shift (15th), mind blank (17th), shapechange (19th)
   * Bloodline Feats: Combat Casting, Improved Disarm, Improved Grapple, Improved Initiative, Improved Unarmed Strike, Iron Will, Silent Spell, Skill Focus (Knowledge [dungeoneering]).
   * Bloodline Arcana 
   * [Aberrant]: Increase Polymorph spell duration by 50%. More duration means longer buffs
   * [Warped]: Get random effect from the table when polymorphing, all of which are bad.
   * Bloodline Powers:
   * (1st) Bloodline Power
   * [Aberrant] Acid Ray: Terrible garbage damage, switch for Blood Havoc or Bloodline Familiar Immediately, or take the Aberrant Tumor feat and have both.
   * [Warped] Warp Touch: Dazed is frankly, nothing to fuck with. This is basically a save or lose ability at level 1, because if dazed, they can’t take any actions for one round, which doesn’t sound like a lot, but is more than enough for your party to rip them to shreds, or just get in some serious hits.
   * (3rd) Long Limbs: Your reach increases specifically for Melee touch attacks. This is okay because it keeps people far away, but again, and I’m going to keep hammering this in, you don’t want to be involved with melee ANYTHING. That said, you probably just want to take Spectral Hand. Blood Intensity it is.
   * (9th) Unusual Anatomy: Ah free fortification. This can actually be pretty nice as denying crits can save a character and this is always on. Gets even better at 13
   * (15th) Alien Resistance: Having spell resistance is great, and if you get to this level, it should be pretty decent. Most casters are used to preparing for spell resistance...most monsters are not. 
   * (20th) Aberrant Form: No crits, blindsight, and DR 5/-. I can’t help but feel at this level you deserve something better. It’s not bad but...again it’s level 20. We can do better.
   * Overview: Okay bloodline around being a monster, and definitely a fine option to pick up. Nothing about it is oppressive...just fine. If you plan to be using polymorph buffs a lot, I’d consider it for the duration boost arcana, but definitely get that first ability shifted out.
   * Eldritch Heritage: Wouldn’t necessarily suggest it. I think Eldritch heritage is best for getting something unique, relatively early. This does not do that, besides long limbs, which isn’t enough on it’s own. 
   * Exotic Heritage: Great for Melee Magi or Bad Touch Clerics because Daze is strong and Long Limbs + the Long Arm Spell can be shocking grasps from shocking distances or inflicting wounds without a care in the world.
(2/5) Abyssal [Brutal] 
   * Skill: Know. Planes
   * Bloodline Spells: cause fear (3rd), bull’s strength (5th), rage (7th), stoneskin (9th), dismissal (11th), transformation (13th), greater teleport (15th), unholy aura* (17th), summon monster IX (19th).
   * Bloodline Feats: Augment Summoning, Cleave, Empower Spell, Great Fortitude, Improved Bull Rush, Improved Sunder, Power Attack*, Skill Focus (Knowledge [planes]).
   * Bloodline Arcana: 
   * [Abyssal]: Summons get DR/good equal to half your sorc level. While it doesn’t stack with other DR, you aren’t going to be fighting good people often, and the free reduction will make monsters tankier than before. We like that
   * [Brutal]: If a spell does damage, one target takes...2 more damage...just two...this is TRASSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSSHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH
   * Bloodline Powers:
   * (1st) Claws: You grow weak claws that you only have temporarily and again, aren’t supposed to be in melee. Even if you build for it, you are better off just getting a sword...or a particularly well crafted butter knife. Trade for Blood Havoc or Abyssal Bloodline Familiar.
   * (3rd) Demon Resistances (Ex): You get 5 electricity resist and +2 to poison which upgrades to 10 resist and +4 at level 9. These are super meh really. You can do better. Replace with blood intensity if you like blasting.
   * (9th) Bloodline Power
   * [Abyssal] Strength of the Abyss (Ex)*: Okay...so WE don’t really care about strength...but a scaling inherent bonus to strength is REALLY REALLY good. So like...this is bad for most Sorcs because strength doesn’t really help us do what we do best? For VMC’s or otherwise who like strength, this is wonderful. Trade for Blood Piercing, it’ll be more useful to you most days.
   * [Brutal] Wings of the Abyss (Su): Supernatural flight is fucking fantastic. Minutes per level isn’t bad, and it’ll always be helpful and basically saves us a spell known. Yay~
   * (15th) Added Summonings: When you summon a creature with the demon subtype or the fiendish template, you get an additional monster. Considering the fact that more monsters means more actions, and summon monster is a great spell, this kicks ass. Shame it comes so late though...
   * (20th) Demonic Might: Okay if you make it this far you do get something nice for it. Immunity to electricity and poison, okay resistance to acid, cold and fire, AND telepathy. We’ll make a proper demon lord of you yet.
   * Overview: If I’m totally honest, I would not suggest this bloodline for sorcerers. Sure you can just trade out most of the powers, but the spells aren’t great, and even if you could trade out the spells...at that point you aren’t really here for the bloodline now are you? It’s not...the worst. It’s just that everything that is good comes on fairly late.
   * Eldritch Heritage: So you are solely here for Strength of the Abyss, which again, is a massive boost in strength...BUT to get it, it costs you 3 feats...and if you are here for that, you likely don’t care a lot about everything else. So for like cheese strength builds maybe, but for most people, skip.
   * Exotic Heritage: Nope, the one thing it gets is a fly speed and was cool as that is there are waaaaaaay too many ways to fly. Almost as if the whole game was designed around you getting that ability.

(3/5) Accursed
   * Skill: Perception
   * Bloodline Spells: ray of enfeeblement (3rd), touch of idiocy (5th), ray of exhaustion (7th), bestow curse (9th), feeblemind (11th), eyebite (13th), insanity (15th), dimensional lock (17th), energy drain (19th).
   * Bloodline Feats: Alertness, Blind-Fight, Combat Casting, Deceitful, Defensive Combat Training, Endurance, Great Fortitude, Mounted Combat
   * Bloodline Arcana: You uh...count as a hag for the sake of a coven...which unless you are trying to cheese your way into...is basically useless like 90% of the time. Into the garbage it goes

That said...it does have it’s uses sometimes...
   * Bloodline Powers:
      * (1st) Horrific Visage (Su): Target makes a will save or is shaken...this would be neat iiiff it stacked with anything, worsened conditions or affected more than one person. Stick with intimidate and replace this with Blood Havoc
      * (3rd) Wretched Endurance (Ex): Get bonus to saves against charm...cold, fear, fire and sleep effects. I...I don’t know why specifically these but hey bonus is a bonus, and you’ll encounter at least some of these.
      * (9th) Dread Gaze (Su): Once per day you can stagger one person for half your level in rounds. At this level you have access to the spell Slow...which basically does the same thing, affects more creatures, has double the duration and it’s DC is 1 lower (10+3+Cha vs 10+4+Cha). It’s not that this ability is bad, it’s just mainly overshadowed, especially since you can only use it once per day.
      * (15th) Dream Walking (Sp): Once per day, jump into the dream realm. The Nightmare ability is only so-so in usefulness, but ethereal jaunt is a nice trick for bypassing security and you get a frankly insane duration. Use this to raid the enemies castle from another dimension, spy on people while they are completely unaware, or simply plan out silly pranks. Great Recon Tool.
      * (20th) Fearsome Survival (Su): DR 10/cold iron and SR 6+level...there are races that give you better options at level 1, and at this level, this is stopping exactly 0 people.
      * Overview: Great debuff spells, sort of lackluster abilities and feats. I think that makes it mid because again, the spells are really good and you can just replace most of the meh abilities with blood mutations.
      * Eldritch Heritage: Don’t waste the feats.
(3/5)* Aquatic [Seaborn] 
      * Skill: Swim
      * Bloodline Spells: hydraulic push* (3rd), slipstream* (5th), aqueous orb* (7th), geyser* (9th), control water (11th), beast shape IV (13th), summon monster VII (15th), seamantle* (17th), world wave* (19th).
      * Bloodline Feats: Athletic, Brew Potion, Defensive Combat Training, Dodge, Mobility, Silent Spell, Skill Focus (Swim), Toughness.
      * Bloodline Arcana:
      * [Aquatic]: Water spells are cast at +1 CL and swimming, aquatic or water monsters get +1 to attack and damage. It’s not much but it’ll never be a bad thing?
      * [Seaborn]: +1 CL when you are in enough water to float in. I mean...there are ways to use this but its just +1 CL.
      * Bloodline Powers:
      * (1st) Bloodline Power
      * [Aquatic] Dehydrating Touch (Su): Deal 1d6+1 nonlethal damage. This is bad. Replace with Blood Havoc for objectively more damage omg.
      * [Seaborn] Water Blast (Su): Reflex save or be knocked prone. This isn’t a game changer but it’ll help the Melee boys in the front.
      * (3rd) Aquatic Adaptation (Ex)*: Uh you get a swim speed, a tiny bit of natural armor and when you are in water you get blindsense...if you are in a ya know...underwater campaign this is godly. Otherwise this is...No? I guess???? Definitely worth replacing for Blood Intensity.
      * (9th) Aquatic Telepathy (Su):* Okay you get 100ft telepathy with water, aquatic or creatures with swim speeds and can cast suggestion on them, which isn’t a bad spell. Also at 15th level you can call and request services as demand or GREATER PLANAR ALLY. Okay so be aquaman. Again, if you were in a water based campaign, I would give this a 5/5 easy but since we ARENT and not everyone has swim speeds or water typing I have to rate this as meh. Take Blood Piercing
      * (15th) Raise the Deep: Control water, but specifically for raising the tide at this level you should be able to create…*does some background math* 675,000 square feet of water, which is about….672,975,000kg of water out of fucking nowhere. Drown the BBEG’s throne room, or just sort of turn the capital into a swimming pool. Sure it doesn’t last long at all, but it’s really funny and one of the only powers that works better OUTSIDE of the water.
      * (20th) Deep One: Blinsense 60ft, DR 10/piercing, Resist cold 20 and Freedom of movement whenever you are on land. On top of that, Evasion and blindsight 120 feet when you are underwater. THIS is a fucking capstone. 
      * Overview:* Are you underwater or going to be near, around or themed around water? Then this is fucking excellent. Makes for great pirate NPC’s or crewmembers on famous vessels. Are you not doing water stuff literally all of the time? It is now trash. Luckily you can trade out your situational amazing abilities for pretty common amazing ones via blood mutations so...if you do that I think it’s mid to good*.
      * Eldritch Heritage: Are you in a water campaign? Yes makes this purple, No Makes this Red. Alternatively if you are someone who just enjoys having a legion of water elementals, this might be okay, but that’s thematic and can’t really be rated.
      * Exotic Heritage: See Above


(5/5) Arcane [Sage] 
      * Skill: Know. (pick one)
      * Bloodline Spells: identify (3rd), invisibility (5th), dispel magic (7th), dimension door (9th), overland flight (11th), true seeing (13th), greater teleport (15th), power word stun (17th), wish (19th).
      * Bloodline Feats: Combat Casting, Improved Counterspell, Improved Initiative, Iron Will, Scribe Scroll, Skill Focus (Knowledge [arcana]), Spell Focus, Still Spell.
      * Bloodline Arcana:
      * [Arcane]: Whenever you use a metamagic feat that raises a spell’s level, the DC goes up by one. Doesn’t feel like it matters, but DC’s are all about nickel and diming. Every little bit counts.
      * [Sage]: You now use Intelligence as your main stat for everything. Spells. Bloodline Powers. Everything. THIS IS A MAJOR boon as having a high intelligence means you’ll have more skills, be able to pull off knowledge and spellcraft checks easier (btw you get a +2 to those) and can qualify for some feats much more easily now. ALL HAIL INT!!!!
      * Bloodline Powers:
      * (1st) Bloodline Power:
      * [Arcane] Arcane Bond: HELLLLLLL YEAH starting strong with a familiar or bonded object off the bat. Amulet is probably the go to for bonded items, to get an Amulet of Spell Mastery that can be used with versatile Spontaneity. If not? Familiars are fucking fantastic, and add more actions. WIN-WIN!
      * [Sage] Arcane Bolt: Shoot a piddly 1d4 force bolt at an enemy that counts as a spell for SR. I hate this...BUT WE CAN TRADE IT FOR A BLOODLINE FAMILAR THAT’S RIGHT BABY WE GET A FAMILIAR ANYWAY FUCK YOUUUUUUUUUUUUU HAHAHAHA.
      * (3rd) Metamagic Adept (Ex): Apply metamagic without increasing the casting time a few times per day. Honestly? Not amazing but pretty nice in general, especially in a pinch.
      * (9th) New Arcana (Ex): CONGRATS ON JUST GETTING THREE MORE SPELLS BECAUSE WHY NOT. Seriously getting extra spells known is nice. Not game breaking but always nice. About even with Blood Piercing in the long run i think.
      * (15th) School Power: The DC of one school of magic goes up by 2 and stacks with spell focus. Not bad, and gets better if you are using one school a lot (illusions spells anyone?)
      * (20th) Arcane Apotheosis (Ex): Metamagic no longer raises the casting time of spells, meaning you just have a lot more flexibility, and you can burn spell slots to charge magic items, which is HUGE for using staves. Burning lower level slots to cast higher level spells is RIDICS. Definitely a solid capstone. This jumps up to cheesy if your DM allows you to craft staves with absurd metamagic.
      * Overview: Look this is the OG standout bloodline for a reason. Even if it isn’t the most powerful, all of its abilities are solid and make you a better sorcerer. THIS is the bar by which every single other bloodline is measured, not to mention that the SAGE wildblooded archetype makes you an even more capable caster than you were before.
      * Eldritch Heritage: Hell yeah! Two feats in and you get a nearly fully leveled familiar or a bonded item for an Amulet of Spell Mastery. Three, and if you are a spontaneous caster, you save yourself a lot of time or Free spells known. Definitely a bit of diminishing returns but like...in this case that’s kinda a good thing? 
      * Also great for Paragon Surge Cheese for half elves (who get skill focus anyway, spend 1 feat to get a familiar and then just paragon surge for free spells known at level 11 or better metamagic.)
      * Exotic Heritage: So it drops down a lot because arcane bolt sucks, so you get a bad start and it gets okay from there. Just take the eldritch version.




(5/5) Astral
      * Skill: Know. Planes
      * Bloodline Spells: True strike (3rd), false life (5th), sands of time (7th), wandering star motes (9th), permanency (11th), contingency (13th), ethereal jaunt (15th), create demiplane (17th), time stop (19th).
      * Bloodline Feats: Combat Reflexes, Dodge, Forge Ring, Improved Initiative, Lightning Reflexes, Quicken Spell, Skill Focus (Knowledge [planes]), Weapon Finesse
      * Bloodline Arcana: When you cast a spell, and then cast a lower level spell next turn, that lower level spell can get +1 dc. Again, doesn’t seem like much, but every little bit counts
      * Bloodline Powers:
      * (1st) Astral Warp (Sp): Create a tiny distortion field that does 1d4+1 baby damage and they take a -1 to saves and ac...while in this 5ft square. NOPE. Replace with Blood Havoc.
      * (3rd) Peerless Speed (Su): UHHH once per day you can use Quickened Spell without altering the level!!!  This starts out meh but quickly becomes an incredibly useful ability even if it’s only once per day.
      * This gets bumped up to absolutely insane if your DM rules that since it it augments spells AS IF they were effected by quicken spell (and not directly by applying that metamagic) that you can effect spells with a longer casting time than 1 full round action.
      * (9th) Astral Voyager (Sp): Once per day, cast lesser astral projection on you and your friends for basically a free extra life for that day so long as you are on the material plane and can hide your bodies somewhere smart. This then evolves to plane shift (friendlies only) and then evolves into Astral Projection but for real. Yes, yes, always yes, come the fuck on yes.
      * (15th) Arrest the Flow (Su): You move out of time and once per day, as an immediate action, and can remove one condition (that isn’t death) instantly until the end of your next turn. Basically a one turn get out of jail free card..which at this level is enough.
      * (20th) Timeless Soul: You don’t get weirdly aged by timeless planes, Astral projection is now 100% free, and quickened spell now only boosts a spell +3 levels, which matters because now we can quicken 6th level spells. 
      * Overview: You are the time mage. I know it says Astral but that is incorrect, you are the Chronomancer. You can warp time and space, quick cast, throw yourself and your team into an alternate timeline (for safety), throw yourself out of time, and then become a Time Lord...and its fucking sick. Arcane may have met its match yet. Really good Wishcrafter Bloodline actually….. Also, you will almost definitely be picking up Sorcerous Bloodstrike, because you have way too many amazing abilities with one use.
      * Eldritch Heritage: Okay so the base ability is trash but if you can stay long enough for Improved Eldritch heritage??? Getting an extra life + plane shift is amazing, as is (an albeit delayed) free quickened spell each day.
