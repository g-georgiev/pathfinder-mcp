Rating System
All Guides use the Color Rating System and honestly? It’s a good Idea. 
* Game Breaking (6/5): This feature or option is so powerful, so useful, so unapologetically ridiculous that it very well may snap the game in half. DM’s reading this guide, know that these options are disportionately powerful, and are warned to somehow work around them. Players? Use these powers carefully.
* Take Me Now (5/5): Class defining. Taking them changes the game, or adds so much power it's insane not to.
* Fantastic (4/5): Incredibly solid, Not class breaking, but something that can add a fair bit of power and versatility to the character.
* Okay/Fine (3/5): Pretty good. They'll be helpful and have some use, but aren't likely to make things incredibly amazing. 
* Maybe Don’t (2/5): Unhelpful. It probably doesn’t...do much for you to pick these. They either provide bonuses that are too small to matter, or too inconsistent to be worthwhile or too inconvenient to use properly.
* Actively Bad (1/5): God why. These options are the least helpful. They either are pointless, replaceable or completely outdated. They are options that either don’t consider what makes your class better, or are completely irrelevant to your class's design.
* Unrated: This is rare but some options are unratable. Likely a class feature that is powered on DM fiat, in which case...ask the DM, something that is campaign dependent or just something that can’t be rated by the same standard as everything else. 
Class Overview
So let me talk to you all about the sorcerer. Introduced in 3.5 with the new spontaneous variant of the vancian spellcasting system. It had 1 class feature besides spell, which was summon familiar, which, it just so happened it’s older sibling, the wizard, could also do...and had more class features….and got its spells earlier. This overall led people to see it as a simply inferior wizard...which to be fair, it was at the time…

Unfortunately, being a derivative of 3.5, a lot of those sentiments carried over into Pathfinder, even as the game has changed. There is still the idea that the sorcerer is just “a worse wizard whose dad fucked a dragon” which is pretty far from the truth. Bloodlines, new feats, new class interactions and options have helped elevate the sorcerer beyond just a pale imitation into its own niche, a caster who does not learn to simply control magic, but embodies it. 

As the Sorcerer you are a being whose body, mind and/or soul has been imbued with power unfathomable. Either by being suffused with energy, chosen by a being beyond understanding, wielding a sacred artifact, experimentation, or simply inheriting the power of your mystic forebears, magic cascades from your every breath and awaits your commands as heir apparent. 


In play, if the Wizard is the spell specialist, choosing a few things every day to be the undisputed master of, you are trying to be the spell generalist. While the wizard is able to have a versatile powerset that they can adjust to their day to day (giving them a reason to take more situational abilities) your goal should be to be reliably useful everyday, no matter what. The wizard may not have haste prepared everyday, but if you have haste, you ALWAYS have haste. If you build your spell list correctly, you should have an answer for almost any problem that comes to haunt you and your teammates on hand, all the time. Add on the auxiliary powers of your bloodline that can give you access to new spells, metamagic boost your stats, change your scores, and otherwise give you access to power that wizards dream of? 

It’s about time we show the world that we are more than what we’re made of.

The Chassis
* (1/5) Hit Die - D6: Thhhhe worst HD in pathfinder. You and the wizard share this tiny itsy bitsy baby die and will always be...pretty damn fragile. Probably invest in some constitution unless you want to be flattened.
* (2/5) BAB - ½ (Half): WORST progression, absolutely trash. The reason it’s NOT a 1 is because you...well you don’t have any reason to have a good BAB and you have touch abilities that will make this...workable. Don’t get me wrong, it sucks, but at least it’s not something you’ll really be relying on.
* (1/5) Skills - 2+Int: Okay. You have the least skills per level...AND you have no incentive to boost your intelligence. Nada. None. Moreover, you don’t get that many skills in the first place so...ouch yeah you aren’t a skillmonkey. Leave that to...to anyone else.
   * Class Skills Worth Paying attention to:
      * Bluff (Cha): These are social skills and It depends on how your dm feels about social skills and how they can be used.
      * Intimidate (Cha): Intimidate’s a social skill! Why does it get a different rating? Well my dear friend, that is because for some reason, they gave it a combat application! Shaken gives someone a debuff to saves too, making it easier to curse them for all eternity.
      * Know. Arcana (Int): Knowing things continues to be useful! 
      * Spellcraft (Int): Identifying spells and crafting is tight and therefore, so is this skill. It’s also used for a dizzying number of feats, abilities, and items, so you will be WELL rewarded for having a lot of ranks.
      * Use Magic Device (Cha): Holy shit!!! Okay this skill can be used to emulate basically whatever you need to cast from a scroll, wand, staff, or other spell trigger item. More spells is more spells, and you are a charisma MAIN.
   * Non Class Skills Worth Paying Attention To:
      * Perception (Wis): Most rolled skill in the game, you will use it, no matter the class, and not having it will get you in trouble at least once. Take it, by any means that you can.
* (3/5) Saves - Good Will: So having a good will save is like...nice don’t get me wrong...but you only get one save, and again, you don’t have much, if any reason to put points into wisdom. ON TOP of that, some of the most deadly abilities are FORT saves...which you aren’t great at resisting so...yeah not...not amazing.
* (1/5) Weapon and Armor Proficiencies: Only simple weapons, no armor and if you even wear armor of any kind, you get arcane spell failure chance. God. Damnit. They want you to be made of fucking bubbles and glass. At least you can use a long spear I guess?
Class Features
* (5/5) Spellcasting and Cantrips [0-9, Spontaneous, Arcane]: This is your gold. This IS your class feature, your number 1, and everything else is cherries on top. You cast spells. Everything you do, everything you are, all of your best abilities and traits revolve around this singular ability that you have...and yet it is so unreasonably powerful that somehow, that is okay. Let’s break it down shall we:
   * You are a Fullcaster: This means you get progression from 0 (cantrips) all the way up to 9th level spells. Even though your spell progression is a bit delayed, it’s still the full range, meaning that you will have LOTS AND LOTS of options on how to deal with problems. Magic is sick and you have the most of it.
   * You are a Spontaneous Caster: So for those of you not in the know, there are two main kinds of spellcasters, prepared, and spontaneous. What does that mean? Let’s take a look a CASTING RULES 
      * No Prepapred Slots: Rather than prepare spells each morning, you’ve got magic in your fucking bones. That’s right, no one can steal your spellbook, or burn your staff. You are magic incarnate and known how to cast innately. You can cast any spell that you know so long as you have the juice (spell slots) to do so.
      * Limited Spells Known: Bad news? Because your magic comes from within, so do your spells...which means you only have a limited number of them that you know and it’s fairly difficult to get new ones. Since we only get a few spells per level, the spells we do pick have to follow certain rules.
      * Swapping Out Old Spells: At 4th level, and at every even-numbered sorcerer level after that (6th, 8th, and so on), a sorcerer can choose to learn one new spell in place of one she already knows. 
      * Metamagic Casting Time: Anytime you use metamagic, since it’s being applied on the spot, it takes a little longer to cast the spell. A standard action becomes a full round action, and anything longer just adds another full round action at the end. 
         * Because of this, we should only use metamagic on standard action spells (which is most of them) or spells that we cast before combat that last a long time...which just so happens to be how we want to do buffs anyway! Woo!
   * You are an Arcane Caster: It almost feels more appropriate to say THE arcane caster. You and the wizard share arguably the strongest and most versatile spell-list in the game. Everyone wants to cast like you, and whole archetypes, feats and abilities are judged SOLELY by the fact that they give themselves access to YOUR spell list. The one you get to choose from at your leisure. The only downside is that you are not able to wear armor while doing so...in exchange for godlike power. We’ll live.
* (?/5) Bloodlines: THIS is what makes you a sorcerer. Note, that while it is called a bloodline, again, your power does NOT have to come from your blood. Radioactive? Cosmic Rays? Planar Travel? Family Curse? Tool of destruction? Your magic is your magic. Bloodlines vary in power and usefulness but all come with the same sort of things:
   * Bloodline Skill: You get a skill for free!
   * Bloodline Spells: Extra spells known! You get these every other level after 3rd, but remember, sorcerer spells must be versatile, varied, valuable. If the spell isn’t useful in multiple situations, is too similar to your other spells, or isn’t something that gives a lot of value, it’s really just wasted space...but free spells is nice.
   * Bloodline Feats: You get BONUS FEATS. NICE!!! They are rarely very useful or what you need but NICE
   * Bloodline Arcana: The secret magic that comes from your origin. These range from being pointless to class defining, and can oftentimes be the core of a sorcerer’s power.
   * Bloodline Power: Each bloodline gains 5 neato abilities at levels 1/3/9/15/20 with the final one basically being your capstone.
      * Some sorcerers can replace their first level power with a Bloodline Familiar, in exchange for getting their bloodline spells a level late. Considering that a lot of first level powers are awful, and familiars are almost always great, this isn’t a bad trade to make at all in most cases.
   * Extra - Bloodline Mutations: Any sorcerer can switch one of their powers for a bloodline mutation, all of which basically make you better at blasting, but are good. Alternatively, you can swap a bloodline feat for a bloodline mutation, so long as when you select it, you are a high enough level to have swapped it out for a power. Can’t be used on powers that have been altered or replaced.
      * Blood Havoc (1st Level): +1 damage per die on any damaging spell whose school you have spell focus for. Considering the most potent spells often fall under Evocation, this isn’t too hard.
      * Blood Intensity (3rd Level): Once per day (and again every 4 levels after 3rd) you add a charisma modifier number of extra damage die to the spell (so 9thd level burning hands with Cha 18 goes from 5d4 to 9d4) as Intensify Spell (the metamagic). This is strictly better than intensify spell, because it doesn’t raise the spell level, or the casting time. If you want to put out the hurt? Great option. Remember, you will still need the caster level to pull this off.        
      * Blood Piercing (9th Level): Once per day (plus every 5 caster levels you have beyond 3rd level), reduce your opponents energy resistance or SPELL RESISTANCE by your charisma mod. That is MASSIVE. Spell resistance will be the BANE of your existence as a late game spell caster, and this can cut it down by A LOT when you need your spells to count. Incredibly worth it. (Also remember, since you get it at 9th level, you get two uses of it.) Only works for damaging spells...still great.
* (2/5) Eschew Materials: Oh okay it’s like...nice. You can also just have a component pouch. So like, I wouldn’t ever tell you to take it as a feat, but like, getting it for free isn’t like a problem.
   * Now...this technically covers anything up to 1gp so you CAN use it with some alchemical reagents. This doesn’t make it much better but...gotta give u/Theaitetos their props. We get it for free, might as well enjoy.
* Capstone: So usually your capstone is just...whatever you get from your bloodline so you don’t really get one BUTTTT you can always get a level 20 capstone via some alternates and 
   * (4/5) Deep Magics (Su): You get one spell known at every level! This is a great boon to be sure, as spells known is what we love and our lifeblood.
   * (4/5) Unique Bloodline (Su): Doubled up bloodline! You get the bloodline arcana (score!), the 1st, 3rd, 9th bloodline power and all of them are level scaled!!! This can be really really fun, and talented. 
   * (5/5) With This Sword (Ex): You gain a Noble Phantasm minor artifact!!! This is not only powerful, but also really really cool. Love that.
   * (4/5) Won’t Stay Dead (Ex): Once per week, when you should lose, you don’t. 
   * (5/5) Perfect Body, Flawless Mind (Ex): Get 8 points that can be added to any ability score in any way. 5 to one, 3 to another. SUPER MAXED OUT CHARISMA FUCK YEAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAH.
   * (5/5) The Boss (Ex): Get super leadership! Luckily everything else is so busted at this level, so this is fine.

Roles and Ability Scores
….So usually I just have my copy paste template here but I’m going to level with you. You are a sorcerer. You use charisma. You need charisma. You fucking live, breathe and ooze charisma. You have two class features, and both of them scale almost entirely on your charisma. This isn’t really a question of what your primary stat is (which is charisma) but more what you are doing with your physical stats. So I’m going to do this section a bit different than usual and just...give you each score.


* Strength (STR): Are you going into melee? If so, first of all, WHY? I assume you are just dipping sorcerer, but for anyone who is here seriously...uh well you probably shouldn’t but if you are going to this is likely important. Otherwise? Trash. We smite gods, not lift weights. Doesn’t matter if you aren’t in melee.
* Dexterity (DEX): Always good to have. This is our AC, Initiative, and sometimes even our attacks depending on what spells we are using. This should likely be one of the first physical stats you bump up, because it’s just so gosh darn helpful. Probably okay at 16-18
* Constitution (CON): HEY remember how we have GARBAGE health and GARBAGE fort saves? This fixes both of those. So yes, dex is super important, but this...keeps you alive? So you choose what is most important to you. Don’t want this below a 14, the higher the better.
* Intelligence (INT): So intelligence gets you more skills, which you desperately need, and helps with knowledge or spellcraft, which are both useful. Can’t say this is ever like required to put points into but, if you can it’s nice. You’ll need at least a 13 to pick up Versatile Spontaneity, which you definitely super want, but not much else.  Not much need for above a 13-14.
* Wisdom (WIS): So like this boosts your will save...which is your only good save...and perception...which is a skill so most of its scaling just comes from your level...so uh...I mean besides that not much reason for it I’m afraid. Just don’t let it go below 8-10. Try not to have a negative modifier.
* Charisma (CHA): THIS IS YOUR GOD. YOU ARE THE SMILE THAT LIGHTS THE SKY AND YOUR WILL CREATES THE WORLD. Seriously though, this is entirely something you need to constantly keep up. You always boost this stat first. This WILL be your highest stat. Don’t go below 19. You want this in the ceiling. More charisma is always good.




For those of you who wanted to know what role the sorcerer could fill or for those of you who think the sorcerer is only good at blasting, let me be clear. YOU SHOULD BE A JACK OF ALL TRADES. You aim is to build up a spell repertoire of abilities that can handle just about any problem you run into, and look cool as hell doing it. So what can our spells do? Buff / Debuff / Battlefield Control / Blast / Utility. And you are going to do ALL of them. If anyone tells you that the sorcerer is only good for blasting, take over their body and curse their entire family line.




Races
So here’s the thing about races. Besides ya know, flavor for your campaign and your character… they don’t actually… matter. They are balls of stats and abilities, which is good! If you want to play a human fighter, but you want the elf stat block, really there isn’t much reason to deny you because again...they are just stat balls. If your dm doesn’t let you split your flavor and your stats, that’s fine! Either way, I think its more important to rate races on what they give you, rather than what they are. I won’t go into a lot of detail on any one race, but I’ll give you the gist of what is worth looking at.
(6/5) Extreme Stat Bonuses and/or Multiple Powerful Abilities
* Half-Elf [Kindred](+2 CHA/ANY): So how did a normal race with pretty okay racial abilities get into “game breaking?”. Well two simple reason: 
   * Human Favored Class Bonus: As a half elf, you count as both a human and an elf for all purposes. This includes favored class bonus options. I don’t often talk about favored class bonuses, but the human’s is oppressive in this case: “Add one spell known from the sorcerer spell list. This spell must be at least one level below the highest spell level the sorcerer can cast”. Yep, you read that right. Every level, get an extra spell known. This is insanely powerful, and thus any class that gets access to this and gets a bonus to charisma? Automatically 5/5. 
   * Paragon Surge: This 3rd level half elf only spell gets you a +2 to Int and dex for it’s duration AND let’s you take one feat that you have the prerequisites for...meaning we can take Expanded Arcana. That’s right, in exchange for a third level spell slot and a standard action, we can just DECIDE to know any spell on our ENTIRE list. Now, according to some FAQ’s you do have to keep whatever your choice is for that feat, each day...UNLESS you take Emergency Attunement which is honestly great anyway, and allows you to change the nature of your spell as a standard action. Stack this with the Mutated Flesh from Shapechanger???? AND WE ARE FUCKING UNSTOPPABLE!!! UN. FUCKING. STOPPABLE. 

If your dm bans paragon surge, this goes down to a 5/5 race again.
   * Munavri (+4 Dex, +2 CON/INT/WIS/CHA, -2 STR): Munavri is a dumb race that gets a bonus to all stats, except the one we don’t care about, get’s us free spell resistance that isn’t bad, and once per day can use spell trigger items for classes she shouldn’t be able to so...yeah no great stuff.
   * Drow Noble (+4 DEX, +2 INT/WIS/CHA, -2 CON): So how does the Drow Noble keep up with have any spell whenever, and all of the stats? Well...basically by having a little bit of both. 
   * Abilities Scores: You get a penalty to CON which sucks but in exchange get a huge boost to Dex, and a boost to all of your mental stats.
   * Spell Resistance: 11+ Character Level is A LOT and there are going to be a fair amount of spells that just fizzle out on you if your DM isn’t careful.
   * So Many Spell Like Abilities: I’m going to list them below but just know that you get so many, your caster level is your character level, and they are all based off of your charisma which is going to be super high anyway. This gets wilder if for some reason you DM allows you to change some of these spells. I wouldn’t.
   * Constant: Detect Magic (0th)
   * At-Will: Dancing Lights (0th), Deeper Darkness (3rd), faerie fire (1st), featherfall (1st), Levitate (2nd)
   * 1/day: Divine Favor (1st), Dispel Magic (3rd), Suggestion (2nd)
   * Drow Favored Class Bonus: Oh? What could it be?  Add one spell known from the sorcerer spell list. This spell must have the curse, evil, or pain descriptor, and be at least one level below the highest spell level the sorcerer can cast. It’s the human one but evil! Woo! Sure you can only choose douche spells but evil and curse are pretty wide still.
(5/5) Bonus to Main Stat, Bonus to Secondary, and Powerful Race ability
   * Human (+2 ANY): You have the best favored class bonus, skilled to help make up for your ABYSMAL skill points, and a bonus feat to make you even more broken. Great option!
   * Deep-One Hybrid (+6 STR, +8 CON, +2 WIS, -4 DEX) [Post Final Change]: So you don’t get a human FCB or a Bonus to charisma. What gives? Well you get a WHOOPING +8 to your con score, completely eliminating your Fort and Health problem entirely, meaning that you can throw more points into charisma without having to worry about how they spread out. Sure your Dex suffers, but now you can basically dump all the stats that don’t matter, still be fine, and max out what you like.
   * Half-Orc (+2 ANY) [Sacred Tattoo / Skilled]: With sacred tattoo and the fate’s favored trait, you can enjoy +2 to all saves, and again, skilled is amazing. Add on human FCB? We are off to the races.
   * Gemsoul Oread (+2 STR/CHA, -WIS) [Mostly Human / Oread Gem Magic]: So you get a bonus to strength which isn’t ideal, but human FCB, charisma bonus, you don’t lose anything from our go to stats AND gem magic is a cool little buff to your normal spells. Luckily most stone spells are different enough and generally helpful enough that this isn’t a total waste.
   * Stormsoul Sylph (+2 DEX/CHA, -WIS) [Mostly Human]: Human FCB, Bonus to charisma, and also you can get wings of air for a supernatural flight speed! Still can’t be dispelled! Nice!
   * Gillman (+2 CON/CHA, -2 WIS) [Throwback]: Okay so I don’t like this race, i think it’s boring and sort of lame BUTTTTTTt it has the human FCB, bonuses to what we care about, which is unfortunately JUST BARELY enough to be 5/5...just barely. Second lowest on the ladder though.
   * Variant Aasimar [Scion Of Humanity]: Human FCB, great stats, free skills, and a cool SLA (which if allowed, can be traded out for more charisma on the variant table. Option 90 for aasimars)
   * Agathion-Blooded (+2 Con/Cha)
   * Azata-Blooded (+2 Dex/Cha)
   * Variant Tiefling [Pass For Human]: See Aasimar’s, BUT option 9 for the extra charisma. Also, if you stay on brand and choose the Abyssal or infernal bloodlines. You are treated as having another +2 to charisma for ALL sorcerer class features. HELL YES. And if you don’t want to have a demon curse? Trade it out for a tail that can hold rods and items for you.
   * Div-Spawn (+2 DEX/CHA, -2 INT)
   * Kyton-Spawn (+2 CON/CHA, -2 WIS)
   * Rakshasa-Spawn (+2 DEX/CHA, -2 WIS)
   * Kitsune (+2 Dex/Cha, -2 Str ) [Human Guise]: Perfect stat spread, the ability to shapeshift, gain more skills via Skilled or Gain a fox shape for infiltration, or even Realistic Likeness for near perfect mimicry. That said this race is infamous amongst sorcerers for its own FCB, the ability to get +¼ DC to ALLL enchantment spells...which can make you QUITE the mind flayer, if I do say so myself. Being able to choose what you invest in make it a great option. Also, let’s be real...Magical Tail is SUPER cool.

(4/5) Races: Bonus to Main Stat, Bonus to Secondary or Powerful Race Ability.
   * [Base Sorc] Anything with a Charisma Bonus and Dex/Con Bonus (with Penalty to Str or Wis)
   * [Sage] Anything with a Intelligence Bonus and Dex/Con Bonus (with Penalty to Str or Cha)
   * [Empyreal] Anything with a Wisdom Bonus and Dex/Con Bonus (with Penalty to Str or Cha)
(3/5) Races: Bonus to Main Stat
   * Has a bonus to casting stat and like doesn’t have a penalty to con but bonuses to other things we don’t really care about
(2/5) Races: No bonus to Main Stat
   * Has no bonus to any stats we care about but also doesn’t have a penalty either
(1/5) Races: Penalty to Main Stat
   * Has a penalty to casting stat and or constitution and no bonus to the other.

Archetypes 
So again, not going to list out every archetype, that’s dumb but I am going to list the ones that I think are super rad, interesting, or just worth talking about, good bad or otherwise. If your favorite archetype didn’t show up, tell me why it’s awesome in the discussion thread and I might just add it. Wouldn’t be the first time. 


So sorcerer archetypes are...weird. So because you basically only have your bloodline and spells, if they are going to take it, it’s going to be from your bloodline...which would make sense but that’s not really how most other archetypes work with similar abilities? Like rarely does a cleric archetype remove one of your cleric domain powers, rather it'll just..remove domains, Because of that, rating what you lose is going to be weird...because it depends on your bloodline.

This gets weirder with the Wildblooded…. “Archetypes”. They basically function like subdomains for bloodlines...but since they count as archetypes, any archetypes that also alter the abilities given don’t work. So lets say we take a wild blood that gives you a shitty 1st level abiltiy and really cool 3rd level ability, and also want to take an archetype that would replace that first level ability with something actually cool.  You can’t do that...even though again, you can do that for arcane schools and cleric domains. So...it’s weird. As a DM I treat these more like subdomains, and you can swap your normal bloodline for a wildblooded one and still replace those abilities as normal as your new “base”. 

Note: I will not be rating the wildblooded archetypes down here. Just didn’t really make sense to have them separate from the other bloodlines so if you were looking for your particular mutant blood, look back up here.


Ratings for All Sorcerer Archetypes


(?/5)* Crossblooded [Dual Wield Destiny]
You get two bloodlines instead of one! Did you find yourself at the crossroads, unable to decide? Were you the child of two sovereign lines? Did you reach out into the void only to have more than one voice reply? Are you the sick combination of a horrendous experiment? Who knows! But you’ve got twice the juice now.
	What you gain
	What you lose
	                                 * You get both bloodline’s Bloodline arcana, and Skills
                                 * You can choose between each bloodline’s Spells (and can learn lower level spells that were not chosen later on), Bonus Feats and Bloodline Powers
                                 * The ability to use the bloodline arcana from BOTH of your bloodlines is MASSIVE and the entire reason why this archetype is a dip classic for other spellcasting classes...they just can't do it like you do. There isn’t really any other way to get access to a bloodline arcana so getting two at once is amazing. Not to mention the versatility of getting to choose which powers or spells you want. Honestly this could be pretty powerful in the right hands...I wonder how they balance it.
	                                 * What do we Lose: One spell known at each level and a -2 to will saves.
                                 * OW FUCK OUCH. As a sorcerer you are more or less STARVED for spells known, which is why the Human FCB is coveted so highly and we don't really have a reason to have a super high Will save either...
	Is it Worth it?: So honestly hard to tell. Spells known are our lifeblood, so to lose one every level is incredibly draining. It means that any level that you’d usually unlock a new spell level (4, 6, etc) you get 0 spells known. That’s right. You are effectively delayed a level. These just happen to be even levels, so you can’t even take a feat to compensate. Moreover, the best say to get new spells known, prestige classes, well...they undercut what you DO get because they don't progress bloodlines. 


There are basically three times when this is worth it.
                                 1. Dipping For Bloodline Arcana: This is the reason most people do it. 1 level, in and out.
                                 2. Very Focused Builds: If you have a specific goal for what you want your sorcerer to do that can take a few spell level hits? Then you can make it work..not suggested, but you can make it work.
                                 3. Words of Power: Since you can combine lower words into higher slots, you don't lose nearly as much versatility, while getting more options.
                                 4. You have a thematic combo: You are here for the fluff or some story crunch thing.

Don’t get me wrong, this can be...magnificently powerful but also, really really really painful if you aren’t perfectly on point. There are too many combinations to be had, so...I just won’t rate it for now. You know what you get, you know what you lose, you all keep it going from there.

Variant Multiclassing (VMC)
Now THIS is a completely different story. You see, the sorcerer is mostly self sufficient, and with exception of a few extraordinarily powerful feats, you don’t actually need much to be a monster. If you have a good race, and pick your spells well, you should be your own kind of animal, so we can afford to trade out some feats, so long as what we get is worth it. Just know that if you go down this path, you have to have a VERY specific build in mind.


Note: Remember, that a VMC is a secondary class, meaning that while you gain their class features you do NOT count as having levels in that class for anything but what is described in the VMC.

Feats
So like all sections, I will not be going over EVERY feat in pathfinder and really not even every feat the sorcerer gets access to. I will primary focus on feats that I think either give you unique abilities, new and powerful options, or are common enough in sorcerer/arcane/spontaneous builds to be worth talking about.
General Feats
                                 * (5/5) Versatile Spontaneity: Congrats, you are now a prepared caster. Prepare one that is on your list from a written source, be it a scroll, or a spellbook. If you do it from a scroll, the scroll is expended. If you do it from a spellbook it is not. It uses a spell slot one higher than its actual level and it takes 10 minutes per spell level to prepare...AND ITS STILL FUCKING AMAZING. Getting to have a spellbook of spells that you can search through when your (hopefully) well crafted list doesn’t help is huge. On it’s own this is great, but when combined with things like Paragon Surge, and the Mnemonic Vestments, you start to get a sorcerer that can have ANYTHING they need WHENEVER they need it.
                                 * Preconstructed Spellbooks that you can totally just buy and are worth every copper.
                                 * (5/5) Planned Spontaneity: You can now multi prep spells into single spell slots, thus raising your versatility even further! And the not highest spell thing doesn’t really matter because they are already going to be a spell slot up.
                                 * (5/5) Magical Epiphany: Once per day, prep one spell slot as a standard action. Nice! With planned spontaneity, enjoy walking into a fight, pulling out a spell book and double prepping whatever you need instantly.
                                 * (5/5) Emergency Attunement: Honestly, I didn’t know this feat existed until I made this guide but holy moley. This makes otherwise situational spells really adaptable, as you just need to make a baby spellcraft check and can just...switch it. This makes you even more versatile, and we love that. Spell immunity? Swap that. Paragon Surge Spells? Swap That. Resist energy? SWAP THAT. Amazing feat that makes our spells better at the cost of duration...which is pretty easy to get a lot of. 
                                 * (5/5) Experimental Spellcaster: Get access to a little bit of wordcasting. You get all Target words, the boost metaword and one effect word of any level you can cast. While this system has not gotten the love it should have, there are some DEFINITE stand out amazing words that can change the game. Moreover, there are lesser spells that can be “boosted” into greater spells, meaning you get two spells known for the price of one!
                                 * (5/5) Aberrant Tumor: Aberrant bloodline gets a tumor familiar! These are...gross but they can basically hide in your body. Definitely worth picking up as again, familiars are great.
                                 * (4/5)* False Focus: OKAY THIS IS IT. This is what eschew materials SHOULD be. Burn 100gp on a symbol and never have to pay for any spell that costs less than that again. Now this is only good if your DM ya know...cares about this sort of thing for anything short of a diamond. Also, again...Alchemical Reagents are dope.
                                 * (4/5) Irrisen Icemage: For some reason, a Sorcerer only feat. All of your non-ice spells are weaker and your ice spells are stronger. Three times per day, turn non ice spells into ice spells. This can be really good if you are trying to make a winter blaster, and doesnt increase the level of the spell like the Elemental Metamagic would, in exchange for only letting you do cold.
                                 * (6/5) Leadership: Our first 6/5 feat. Why? Because for a feat at level 7, you get ANOTHER CHARACTER SHEET. This is far and away the strongest feat in all of pathfinder...and it barely has any prerequisites. If your DM allows this, it’s objectively the best option.
                                 * (3/5) Chill Amplification: Targets hit with cold damage can’t make 5-foot steps and have their speed reduced by 5. This may not sound like much, but this can keep larger fighters from getting in your face, or keep people from from getting out of your fighters face.
                                 * (4/5) Expanded Arcana: Gives you one spell known of highest level or two of less than highest level. I for one would always say go for the second option, just a better bang for your buck. That said, MORE SPELLS KNOWN IS ALWAYS GREAT. This is an amazing feat if you are feeling cramped or just want to round yourself out a little bit and don’t have anything else to take. It’s probably BEST if you...I dunno...had a way of picking it up temporarily multiple times and changing which spells you get...but that's not like...possible...haha...
                                 * (5/5)* Augment Calling: Okay this is only for calling builds but getting an extra 2 HD can be HUGE, giving you access to creatures that would be otherwise impossible to bind, like Balors or Pit Fiends. Obviously skip if you aren’t into planar binding like that, but if you are, even a little, this is a MUST HAVE.
                                 * (3-5/5)* Dead Inside: Whatever for most sorcerers but INCREDIBLY important for psychic sorcerers who will lose the ability to cast if they are under emotional effects. If you have psychic casting this is amazing and will save you from the DM’s Wrath
                                 * (4/5) Gifted Mesmerist: Get one of your charm/compulsion spells as an SLA, trade it out later. Can definitely help add a bit of versatility, and as you level up, you can keep switching out the SLA for higher and higher level spells, which is nice.
                                 * (3/5) Heavenly Radiance: Aasimar Only. Burn a use of Daylight SLA to use a better spell like ability. I’d say wait until you can at least get searing light. Nice way for a Sorcerer to get access to a few cleric abilities without ya know...getting access to all of them.
                                 * (3/5) Ordered Mind: Higher DC for the skill check but now you can now dispel using any spell of the same school so long as its the same level or higher. Since you are going to have spells from EVERY school more or less, this should make it easier since you can cast whatever whenever. That said….counterspelling still ain’t great.
                                 * (4/5) Improved Initiative: Going first is amazing! It’s a pretty solid bonus.
                                 * (4/5) Noble Scion (Scion of War): Remember, we want to get Charisma to as many things as we can. Initiative is going first, and going first is winning first. If you are able to get this and AC switched over to charisma, you really don’t need dex for much of anything.
                                 * (3/5) Toughness: Look it’s not complex, but it helps. You don’t have a lot of health and so having more, even just one per level, is nice.
                                 * (2/5) Exotic Heritage or (3/5) Skill Focus / Eldritch Heritage / Improved Eldritch Heritage / Greater Eldritch Heritage: So this lets us snap powers from other bloodlines. But don’t we already have a bloodline? Well yeah but no reason you can’t have two. Besides, there are some bloodlines, especially wildblooded, that get some really cool first level powers. Like Sylvan for example, which just gives you an animal companion. Tha said, I can't really rate these because they depend on their bloodline.
                                 * (5/5) Magic Trick: So you should definitely not think of magic trick as one feat but rather a collection of feats that all augment different spells, giving you more spell options. Each spell has its own set of tricks, and so long as you meet the pre-reqs, they are all open to you, and can COMPLETELY change how you interact with said spells. While I have to rate each trick list independently, as a sorcerer, simply taking a feat that makes one of our spells known is much more valuable and viable is incredibly useful. I’ll list my favorites below, as per usual:
                                 * Fireball: You know it, you love it, its fireball. So why is this purple? Well because if you use the cluster bomb trick, you half the radius in exchange for removing the dice cap...without raising the level of the spell. That’s insanely powerful and that’s just one trick. If you combine it with concentrated fire, each individual fireball goes from being 2d6 to 3d6, which is basically the same as empower, but instead it basically becomes a single target attack...which ya know, is still really ouch. If you get a Rod of Widen Metamagic, this can get even more nutty. Sculpt flames also makes it so that you never have to worry about hitting your friends again. So...yeah just make sure to put points into spellcraft (which you should be doing anyway) and get Selective Spell.
                                 * Floating Disk: Beyond looking super cool and being integral for playing as this guy, being able to have a pseudo fly just for having ranks in fly is really good, as a lot of low level problems can be solved by flying. Ask your dm if this will let you fly up walls or across ceilings, for extra radness. OH and don’t forget...this lasts HOURS per level.
                                 * Obscuring Mist: Great battlefield control spell gets more options. If you have the extend feat (which isnt a bad idea at all) you can make mist cling to people giving your allies total concealment. Combine this with Mist Screen and you can basically blind the entire enemy situation without much effort. 
                                 * Shield: Spend an immediate action to turn your shield spell into a tiny wall of force, allowing you to block spells, or attacks from one angle. This CAN be a life saver, but otherwise is just okay.
                                 * (3/5) Sorcerous Bloodstrike: Once per day, kill a dude, get one of your tricks back. Since it’s only once per day I can only rate it so highly.
                                 * (3/5) Spell Focus / (3/5) Greater Spell Focus: Okay I’m going to let you all in on a little secret...I don’t think spell focus is a good feat…..ON its own. On it’s own it raises the DC’s for spells of a specific school by 1, which is effectively the same as weapon focus, BUT we also have to contend with spell resistance. On it’s own, I don’t think spell focus is worth you time. Where it shines however, is in combination with other feats and abilities. Blood Havoc needs spell focus. If you already have another way of pumping DC’s, like a kitsune enchantress? Spell focus. So I’d rarely suggest taking it on it’s own but at times, this is your point blank shot, or weapon focus...you just gotta to get to the good stuff.
                                 * (1/5) Deceitful / (4/5) Cunning Caster: Just like knowing things is useful, having your enemies not know things is also very useful. That saiiiiid deceitful is a trash feat, so I can’t recommend the tax lightly. Only do this if you have a whole build around it.
                                 * (4/5) Spell Penetration / (4/5) Greater Spell Penetration: So these are basically late game feat taxes depending on how you play, each offering a +2 bonus on all spell resistance checks. Which again...suck and we hate them.
                                 * (4/5) Knowledgeable Spellcaster: Okay so this one is hilariously powerful because in exchange for a knowledge roll, you can get +3 to overcoming SR and then when you have 10 ranks, its +5. That beasts out and stacks with spell penetration and all you have to do is be decent at knowing things. 1000% worth it.
Combat Feats
                                 * (3/5) Arcane Strike: As a swift action, do a bit more damage. It’s again, one of the few scaling bonuses to damage, so we like it for that, but its for weapons...and if you are using a weapon I am concerned. If you aren’t using a weapon please don’t.
                                 *  (5/5) Spell Cartridge / (4/5) Infused Spell Cartridge: Did you want spell bullets? This is how you get spell bullets. Spell cartige is AMAZING because it takes out the need to reload, which is what holds a lot of firearms back from being amazing. Infused basically gives you a ranged spellstrike-lite, which we might actually be able to use, but it involves a lot of feats.
                                 * (2/5) Raging Blood / (4/5) Blooded Arcane Strike: You no longer have to use a swift action to activate arcane strike, but will likely need to take the Mad Magic feat in order to ya know, use your main class feature.


Item Creation Feats
                                 * (5/5) Craft Wondrous Item: If you are going to get a feat, and you don’t know which to pick? Pick this one. This has one of the widest array of items, they are always helpful, AND you can ignore a lot of the pre-reqs so long as your skill checks are high enough.
                                 * (6/5) Craft Fleshcrafting Poison: Stealing monster abilities is busted, and if you get away with this, then be grateful or very afraid of your GM. It is part of my uh recycle mage build
                                 * (4/5) Craft Arms and Armor: So on its own it’s really good...for your team. Don’t get me wrong, there is armor you can wear and this will come in handy for that but in general, this will be a lot more useful for your party than for yourself.
                                 * (5-6/5) Craft Construct: So these can actually be SILLY powerful if you know how to use them. Constructs have some of the wildest cheese, from the Tiny God Homunculi to the Painter Wizard, this can get REALLY busted REALLY fast if your DM isn't paying attention.
                                 * Also never forget: “Once the crafting process is complete, the resulting construct is ready to receive orders. A construct recognizes its creator intuitively and obeys all commands issued to it by that individual.”
                                 * (5/5) Inscribe Magical Tattoo: Basically allows you to make a second set of magical items but at a much higher cost. This is important because it lets you have two items in one slot...also come on, sexy super magic tattoo’s? That’s sorceror as fuck.
                                 * (3/5) Scribe Scroll: Lets you make scrolls of your spell list, and honestly? You have so many ways of abusing scrolls, you might as well pick it up. That said, without some cheese you generally won’t be as good as the wizard at this one.
                                 * (6/5) Creating Magical Traps*: So technically this isn’t a magic item creation feat, and doesn’t really require a feat but these are basically in the same ballpark so I just wanted to let you know, magical traps are busted, expensive but busted, especially with automatic resets. You can basically have contingency whatever, and place multiple spells on one trap and so long as you can cast it, it’s gucci so just...yeah no this is dumb.
Metamagic Feats
                                 * (6/5) Sacred Geometry: Free metamagic. FREE METAMAGIC. Combined with the right metamagic feats, this ability is downright godly, the qualifications are dirt easy and you can make every spell unstoppable. Why don’t most people take it? Well all of that horrible math of course! If only someone had made an online easily accessible and useful calculator...hint...hint!
                                 * (5/5) Spell Perfection: More free metamagic! Very late game, but its incredibly useful, and all that it asks from you is that you take more metamagic! Can do!!! (Also remember that it doubles ANY other feat based numerical bonuses. Caster level, DC, anything.) This is the capstone for any blaster
                                 * (2-6/5) Ascendant Spell: Okay so basically, ascendant spell lets you cast a spell as its MYTHIC version which is...completely ridiculous. All mythic spells are just straight up better versions of the base spell, and getting access to them in non mythic games is great...or it would be if it didnt bring you up +5 spell levels. Damn. If only we had a way, that was relatively easy, to be able to use metamagic without raising the actual level of the spell! Then well this would be amazing wouldn’t it? Probably busted.
                                 * (5/5) Cherry Blossom Spell: If a target takes damage from a spell (even if they succeed their save) They take 2 ABILITY DAMAGE to either all their mental or physical scores. So long as your spell has a save, they don't get a fort save to negate it. Even for +3 spell levels, this is probably worth it. Imagine if we didn’t have to do that?
                                 * (4/5) Extend Spell: Doubles your duration. Short sweet, gets the job done, great for long term buffs or other effects. Definitely worth picking up.
                                 * (5/5) Quickened Spell: You are probably going to get a rod of this, because its sort of expensive BUT action economy is the name of the game. Being able to cast two spells in one round is ridiculously powerful, don’t forget it.
                                 * (4/5) Dazing Spell: HAHAHA IF THEY TAKE DAMAGE FROM THE SPELL THEY ARE DAZED. DAZED. Dazed creatures can take no actions, completely removing potentially dozens of enemies from the board. Dazing fireball is classic for a reason, and honestly this is one of the best debuff tools in the game. Fucking terrifying.
                                 * (4/5) Rime Spell: Ice spells entangle. Good cute debuff and you have plenty of ice spells.
                                 * (3/5) Heighten Spell: Usually, whatever, just makes a spell count as a higher level than it is. Gives you some extra DC’s. Not great...unless you are getting into Angel Summoner Cheese, in which case, it’s necessary.
                                 * (5/5) Studied Metamagic: Make a knowledge check. If you pass, you completely ignore the targets energy resistances or DR from its race...which is what most monsters have. Better for the wizard I think, but still great on you. BLASTERS, THIS IS FOR YOU.
                                 * (4/5) Empowered Spell: All variable numerical effects...ALL OF THEM are increased by 1.5. All of them. Thats damage dice, possibly creatures affected for some spells. It just says it all. Go off I guess. Blasters, you are also taking this.
                                 * (4/5) Maximized Spell: All variable numeric effects are maximized. Healing is maxed, damage is maxed yadda yadda.
                                 * (4/5) Intensified Spell: Raises the number of possible damage dice by 5. An important note is that it doesn’t increase your caster level, so if you dont have enough CL to use all of those dice it’s a waste BUT its a great way of getting more dice, and making weaker spells continuously frightening. There is a reason this is the magus staple.
                                 * (5/5) Persistent Spell: Effectively roll twice and take the lowest for all saves involved with the spell. For a lot of later game spells, it’s save or be absolutely destroyed, so this can be an INCREDIBLY useful power to have. Incredibly. Very very worth picking up mid to late game.
                                 * (4.5/5) Piercing Spell: Hey do you know that SR is the worst? Like the absolute worst? This makes your enemies SR count as being 5 lower and only raises your spell level by 1. This can and will save your ass at least once so it’s a good pick up...possibly better as a rod, but a good pickup nonetheless.
                                 * (4/5) Reach Spell: There are a SURPRISING number of incredibly powerful touch spells, and while ya know, most of them you can deal with via spectral hand, once you get to level 10 or so you’ll definitely want to be able to cast some of your 5th or 6th level spells at a a range.

                                 * Spell and Meditation Books: Okay so if you are doing prepared casting shenanigans, eventually you might look into spell books, most notably, preconstructed spellbooks. If you don’t, well here it goes. Technically spell books are for prepared casters proper, and most of these spell books come with neat things called “preparation rituals” that give you a 1/day boon that you can use if you prepare 3 spells from said book. 

That said, any spellbook can be transcribed as a meditation book for spontaneous casters instead, and instead of preparing 3 spells to get access to it, you have to expend a spell slot equal to the highest level spell in said meditation book. Not all of these preparation rituals are amazing, nor are the spellbooks but I figured I might as well tell you about the ones that are worthwhile. Also note, you can benefit from ANY NUMBER of preparation rituals, so long as you pay the cost for each one.
