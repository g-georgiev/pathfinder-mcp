# Multiclassing, VMC, and Prestige

Multiclassing
In general don’t. No seriously, your spellcasting progression is delayed already, so delaying it further really just handicaps you. Which means whatever you are multiclassing in had better give you mana from the heavens and also do your taxes...and considering that the one thing you do is spells, and you generally won’t be moving from that point, it’s REALLY hard to convince me this is worth it. 


There are two times when you are allowed to multiclass.
                                 1. When you need a pre-req for a VERY good prestige class that you can’t get yourself. These i will list with whatever prestige class they go with, but in general, ar
                                 2. If multiclassing gives you a MASSIVE boost in power, survivability, or versatility. 


Best Multiclass Options
                                 * (3/5) Scaled-Fist Unchained Monk (1 Level)
                                 * You are here solely for Charisma to AC. Is that worth losing a spell level? Probably not but it’s one of the few things that’s even worth considering.
                                 * (4/5) Dual-Cursed Oracle (1 Level)
                                 * You are here for a fun curse and some good revelations. Take misfortune, and if you have the free feat, get like prophetic armor (Lunar Mystery) or sidestep secret (Lore Mystery) to get charisma to AC and Reflex saves instead of Dexterity. They can also get a BUNCH of other super amazing things depending on what you are building for, these are just the easy ones.
                                 * Consider using some early entry shenans to get into Mythic Theurge if you decide to do this, so that at least you’ll get a good amount of spells out of it.
                                 * (4/5) Spellslinger Wizard (1 Level)
                                 * You are here solely for Arcane Gun. All of the limitations apply to wizard spells, which frankly, you don’t really give a shit about, since you are a sorcerer. Same reasons wizards don’t give a shit about crossblooded problems. Arcane gun makes it so that you can add your weapons enhancement bonus to the DC of cone, ray, ranged touch or line spells...which is a lot more than you think. Being able to add potential +5 to the dc of a spell is insane.
                                 * Really really good for blast lovers (especially Word’s of Power players).
                                 * Depending on how you interpret mage bullets, being able to sacrifice a spell slot to make your weapon 
Variant Multiclassing (VMC)
Now THIS is a completely different story. You see, the sorcerer is mostly self sufficient, and with exception of a few extraordinarily powerful feats, you don’t actually need much to be a monster. If you have a good race, and pick your spells well, you should be your own kind of animal, so we can afford to trade out some feats, so long as what we get is worth it. Just know that if you go down this path, you have to have a VERY specific build in mind.


Note: Remember, that a VMC is a secondary class, meaning that while you gain their class features you do NOT count as having levels in that class for anything but what is described in the VMC.

Best VMC Options
                                 * (3/5) VMC Bard
                                 * (3rd) Bardic Knowledge {Full Level}: Free half level to knowledge checks and can make them untrained. Nice. Considering how many skills we have, this will actually be pretty useful. It’s not the biggest bonus, but it’s enough to matter
                                 * (7th) Bardic Inspiration {Level-4}: Our bread and butter! Inspire courage and inspire competence are good gets, but you only get CHA+Level rounds of either and you count as four levels lower...so take Lingering Performance and get a Banner of Ancient Kings (maybe) and keep it moving! I’d also suggest looking into a Poet’s Cloak for more silliness.
                                 * (11th) Versatile Performance: Again, free skills, and we are GOOD at performance! Charisma classes BABYYYY
                                 * (15th) Lore Master {Level 5}: You can take 10 on any knowledge checks you have ranks in (woo) and once per day can get a nat 20 on a knowledge check as a standard action. Again, free skill boons are great. But it’s a bit late ya know?
                                 * (19th) Additional Performances {Level-4}: You get dirge of Doom and Inspire greatness. Meh, I’m fighting god ATM.
                                 * Overview: So the VMC bard basically gives you non spellcasting options to help your party in the form of knowledge skills and some bardic inspiration. Considering that combat usually isn’t that long, as long as you have lingering performance you should be able to hold on easy. That said, later on, you aren’t really getting anything amazing and it doesn’t help you be a better sorcerer.
                                 * (4/5) VMC Cavalier
                                 * Special: At first level you choose your order and you have to follow it’s edicts. 
                                 * (3rd) Challenge {Level-2}: You can issue a challenge! Now we don’t particularly care about the damage here. We don’t wanna be in a melee. No what we care about is the ORDER bonus. Specifically, the Order of the Blossom or Order of the Staff bonus, which gives challenged creature’s a penalty to saves against your spells! This is fucking RUTHLESS and you can take Chain Challenge to get more kick out of, so long as you are bringing down enemies. I’d say that Order of the blossom is the go to here, but if you hate the fey, order of the staff is passable. Alternatively, go Order of the Dragon or Order of the Reins for that sweet sweet teamwork.
                                 * (7th) 2nd Level Order Ability {Full Level}: Get the second level ability of your order. IF you are a blossom, this is sneak attack. Take accomplished sneak attacker and go arcane trickster. Dragon? Aid another bonuses. Reins? Make all the animals do a rad trick (great for summoners). 
                                 * (11th) Tactician {Full Level}: There are actually teamwork feats that are useful for casters, it’s just no one ever wants to use teamwork feats! At this level you get 3 uses per day and it comes with a bonus feat which is actually pretty nice. If you are willing, burn the feat tax for Bonded Mind and get a familiar somehow (it’s way too easy to get a familiar for you, you have no excuse). Then pick up Share Spells teamwork feat, so now, you can cast personal spells onto your allies...which opens up A LOT of really powerful tricks with polymorph spells.
                                 * (15th) 8th Level Order Ability {Full Level}: Blossoms get fun SLA’s, Rein’s Get free action mass control for animal minions (go dire tigers!) and Dragon’s give their teammates a shitty bonus.
                                 * (19th) Greater Tactician: Actually can be worth it!!! Get Another feat and it’s a swift action activation now.
                                 * Overview: So hilariously enough, this VMC can actually make you a little bit of a monster if you know how to use it. I’d choose either Blossom’s for huge save debuffs or Rein’s for some fun minonmancy! Teamwork feats can be based around whatever your squad’s composition is and best of all, the majority of your features have no level penalty, which is amazing.
                                 * (3/5) VMC Cleric
                                 * Special: So at level 1 you choose a deity. You gain aura (Sacred Summons Get), bonus languages (score) and can’t cast spells opposite of your alignment (Boo). You also get the spontaneous casting cure ability...even though you can’t use it. BUT you do have the feature...just in case anyone asks or if you multiclass into a prepared caster that loses it idk #feyspeakerdruidmythictheurge. 
                                 * (3rd) Domain {Full Level}: You choose a domain. You get the first domain power at full level. This can be MASSIVE. From freedom of movement to incredible debuffs to game changing re-rolls, this can be straight gold. If so inclined you should also be able to get Inquistions or Divine Fighting Techniques. Don’t forget that you can also choose from subdomains and animal/terrain domains (so long as your deity grants the appropriate domain in the first place.)
                                 * (7th) Channel {Level-6}: You get incredibly shit channel energy for charisma + 1 uses...that said it IS channel energy which means you get access to things like bless equipment and also, since you follow a deity, variant channel energy, which can be very potent buff wise. Also, look into Channel Foci, which aren’t broken, but are kinda interesting ya know?
                                 * (11th) Improved Channel {Level-6 ⇨ Level-4}: Your channel is less shitty.
                                 * (15th) Improved Domain {Full Level}: You get the SECOND domain ability, which, assuming you cherry picked, should also be awesome.
                                 * (19th) Greater Channel {Level-4 ⇨ Level-2}: Now your channeling only MINORLY sucks.
                                 * Overview: So the real draw here is the fact that you get two domain powers fully leveled and based on your character level. That’s powerful enough to make up for your weirdly weak channeling abilities.
                                 * (5/5*) VMC Oracle
                                 * Special: Choose an Oracle Curse and Mystery. You count as an oracle of half for character level for the sake of your curse. 
                                 * Curse: I say take Blackened Hands, Haunted, Elemental Imbalance, Lycantropy, Pranked, Shadowbound or Wrecking Mysticism if you are kitsune.
                                 * Mystery: Depends on your revelation rulings but if you go with mine, I suggest the Shadow (No seriously Dark Secret’s is dumb and 100% works), Lunar, Nature, Juju, Time, Ancestor, Heavens, Wind, Winter, or Dragon Mysteries
                                 * Note - New Spells Known: So big question is, can we get spells known from the curse or from this VMC at all? The answer is YES but only if those spells known happen to be on our spell list...which is lucky for us,because most of them are! This, of course, is amazing.
                                 * (3rd) Revelation: You get a revelation from the mystery you chose at level 1 as an oracle of your character level - 6. This CANNN be amazing...depending on the ruling. So uh let me do some confirmations
                                 * Q1: “Are you limited to choosing ONLY the revelations list in the VMC description?” You are technically allowed to choose any mystery so this is...weird. I think this is a case of the rules just not getting updated. 
                                 * My ruling: You can choose any revelation from the mystery you choose.
                                 * Q2: “Is your oracle level equal to your character level-6 for selecting only for choosing your revelations or for all purposes” I think it’s for all purposes but of course, if your dm rules that you get full revelation level for the actual EFFECT of the revelation, just not for choosing it, this obviously bumps it up a point. 
                                 * My ruling: You are character level-6 for your oracle revelation including it’s effect.
                                 * Q3: “Can you make use of a Ring of Revelations?” So a ring of revelations requires that you have the appropriate mystery, which we do, and that you meet the level requirement for the revelation. Since we do have oracle levels, specifically for the sake of the revelation class feature, I think we can. If your DM rules that you can’t because you are specifically barred from the Extra revelation feat, obviously goes down one, and vice reversa.
                                 * My ruling: You can use a ring of revalation’s but are still character level -6 since you use it as if it were “a normal class feature”
                                 * So depending on how you answered the above questions this can either be pretty meh to fucking incredible. I think with my rulings it’s pretty damn good.
                                 * (7th) Orison SLA: This is lame and will always be lame.
                                 * (11th) Curse Focus: Get +5 to your level for your curse, jumping from 5 to 10. Did I hear MORE free spells known for us? Yes? Wow and it’s our birthday too. YAY!!!!
                                 * (15th) Improved Revelation: See third level revelations for why I can’t rate this. This has the ADDED confusion of not listing ANY character level. Ugh. If your DM rules that this means this one can be at full character level, this is fucking stellar, if you used my previous rulings and are still character -6 but can choose whatever this is really dope
                                 * (19th) Greater Revelation: Same as improved Revelation.
                                 * Overview: Okay so REALLY HARD TO RATE uh...if you go with the most restrictive ruling, it’s okay, if you go with my ruling it’s rad as hell and if you go with the most advantageous ruling it’s fucking oppressive.
                                 * (5/5) VMC Wizard
                                 * Special: Your wizard level is your character level for the arcane school of your choice. Crisp, clear wording. Yay. Remember you can pick focused schools and elemental schools.
                                 * (3rd) Familiar {Full Level}: You get a fully level familiar. This is great, and really needs no notes. Enjoy!
                                 * (7th) 1st-Level School Powers {Full Level}: These are fucking wicked! For some reason wizards just happen to be pretty front loaded. I’d suggest Divination, Divination (Foresight), Conjuration (Teleportation), Evocation (Admixture) or Void Schools
                                 * (11th) A Cantrip: Always meh, especially meh for you because you can already do this.
                                 * (15th) Arcane Discovery {Full Level}: Holy shit. Wizard discoveries are no joke joke. The fact that you get it so late ACTUALLY does you a favor.
                                 * True Name let’s you fucking call an outsider with 18 hit dice as a standard action. 
                                 * Time Stutter gives you TIME STOP twice per day, even if it’s just for one round. 
                                 * Multimorph makes polymorph spells all basically shapechange.
                                 * Infectious Charms can be used to turn dominate person into dominate compound as it has NO usage limit. 
                                 * Beyond Morality can have you neutralize high level divine spells and abilities. 
                                 * Observant Illusion lets you see through your illusions, which can be whatever you want for INSANE sneak potential. 
                                 * (19th) Greater School Power {Full Level}: You get a 8th-level power. Most of these range from meh to okay good but you get it so late that it’s like whatever.
                                 * Overview: AYEEEE you get some serious power. This drops off at 11th level but KICKS BACK INTO HIGH GEAR at 15th. Definitely gives you real tangible power and is very worth picking up. You aren’t a wizard and you shouldn’t try to be one one….but stealing their thing every now and then is okay :)
                                 * (4/5)* VMC Witch
                                 * (1st) Patron: You get a witch patron and never can get extra hex. Having a witch patron gets a whooping 9 extra spells known, one for each level!!! BUT REMEMBER we can only cast these spells if we have high enough spell slots and if these spells ALREADY APPEAR ON THE SORCERER/WIZARD list. We are not doing this to get cross class spells, we are doing this to get more spells we can use.
                                 * Note if your DM rules that you do not get any spells known from your patron, which would be weird because thats all having a patron basically does, this becomes much worst.
                                 * (3rd) Familiar {Full Level}: You get a fully level familiar. This is great, and really needs no notes. Enjoy!
                                 * (7th) Hex {1st Level}: So this is aight. There are hexes you can get that don’t need to scale with level, like fortune or protective luck hex, and since you have the hex feature, you sHOULD be able to get Ritual Hex.
                                 * (11th) Cantrip: This will always be traaaaasssssh
                                 * (15th) Improved Hex: Your level 1 hex becomes a level 8 hex and you get a new level 1 hex. A good time to mention that we can retrain hexes.
                                 * (19th) Major Hex: So major hexes can be really good no joke but again, at this level, im dropping meteors. Luckily with ritual hex, we might be able to steal a grand hex.
                                 * Overview: Sooo this gets us A LOT of spells known potentially, a familiar, and some sorta shitty hexes. Think about it as a second bloodline that stacks on top of your first one. If you can figure out how to make ritual hex good, I think this can put in some actual work, but really even if you can’t, it’s pretty damn solid.

Prestige Classes
So okay remember what I said in multiclassing? Well that doesn’t necessarily apply here. If you don’t really care about your later bloodline abilities or spells, you might actually have a lot to gain from prestige classing, as again, the sorcerer does not get a lot of class features. You have spells, and your bloodline...that’s it...and a prestige class can keep the spells going.

THAT SAID. You CANNOT afford to lose a spell level. Not one. So if you take a prestige class that loses you one level? You are taking Prestigious Spellcaster for EVERY. LEVEL. MISSED. EVERY TIME.
                                 * Full Spell Progression
                                 * (5/5) Stargazer [Entry Level 6]
                                 * We get a Hex, a Familiar, A bunch of wicked good spells known like FLY and PLANAR BINDING (which you can be amazing at btw), oracle revelations, and a bunch of neat little abilities...with full spellcasting progression. Yeah this prestige class is cracked. We love it.
                                 * You can honestly put like 3-4 levels in this and get a bunch of cool stuff like its so good that you can just dip and be a god.
                                 * (4/5) Mystic Theurge [Early Entry Level 4]
                                 * Take this if you only care about your bloodline arcana and maybe your first level ability and just want a butload of spells. My advice? Since you are a spontaneous caster, take a prepared divine caster as your backup.
                                 * Early Entry Cheese: Djezet spell component or Equipment Trick Sunrod
                                 * Best class options: Empyreal (Celestial) Sorcerer / Cleric or Druid OR Feyspeaker Druid or Elder Mythos Cleric / Sorcerer.
                                 * Either way, in exchange for one spell level (which still hurts) you get access to a total of 6 levels of divine casting which...woo can be powerful. 
                                 * Fun build if you do this and then go stargazer! A lot of their abilities stack with cleric levels after all.
                                 * (5/5) Veiled Illusionist [Entry Level 6]
                                 * Do you wanna be the sickest illusionist? Well then yay! This lets you be sick as fuck
                                 * Human veil gets you access to a total of 10 new illusion spells known (which can be very versatile) whenever you just pop on the veil.
                                 * Note: depending on how you read it, you may get to hot swap? It seems like activating the veil gives you the ability to add these spells to your list, so going 10 level in basically lets you be an illusionist god and bumps this up to cheesy nonsense. Which we love.
                                 * Elf veil lets you reroll against spell resistance, cyclops gets you true seeing and naga lets you put an illusion IN your illusion which is just terrifying.
                                 * (3/5) Loremaster [Entry Level 7] (One Level Dip)
                                 * We are here EXCLUSIVELY for Secret of Magical Discipline. That said it requires...fuck….7 divination spells known. 7. Jesus. You might want to pick this up later in your career...and this rating is assuming that you did.
                                 * (5/5) Argent Dramaturge [Entry Level 6]
                                 * Okay so this lets you have some cool bardic performances. No extra spells known but that’s okay, you still get access to some neat performances and Bardic Masterpieces which are sick. A lot of late game enemies are outsiders, so this actually works well.
                                 * Best Masterpieces: Music Beyond The Spheres and Pageant of The Peacock
                                 * Honestly? Sense it doesn’t require a lot of feats, I’d definitely do this with VMC bard. It gets you a lot more rounds for your bardic performance and more performances to use. All in all you end up being a bardic fullcaster which is sick as hell. Maybe with maestro bloodline, just for flavor?
                                 * (3/5) Bloatmage [Entry Level 6]
                                 * So we are a sorcerer...we really aren’t wanting for like spell slots, but if you want to cast all day all the time, using blood points aint bad.
                                 * So like the captsone is good...but okay I honestly think it’s better on the wizard. NOW DON'T KILL ME YET. We are the sorc and already get a bloodline. To take this prestige class, we take 10 levels to get to this BUT those are 10 levels you could be taking in YOUR bloodline...with bloodline spells and such. You can only use the bloodline powers from bloatmage...so like...yeah... 
                                 * (5/5) Old Diabolist [Entry Level 5]
                                 * You get an Imp COMPANION (not familiar ;D ) that scales with your caster level. That’s right...CASTER LEVEL. Meaning you have a basically always fully leveled familiar light that lets you also have a familiar. Hell yes.
                                 * Also you get free Augment summoning, can make your spells do HELLFIRE damage which is fire and unholy, get hell fire ray twice a day but are damned
                                 * Honestly? Great PRC if you are okay with being damned. Super easy to get into, lots of little boons, almost all of which are charisma based. Solid!
                                 *  I say go in 6 levels, and then just do something else so that you can be revived. Maybe souldrinker, if we wanna just be the worst, lmao.
                                 * (3/5) Thought Thief Arcane Trickster [Entry Level 5]
                                 * So this is specifically for the Psychic and Esoteric Draconic Sorcerer, but honestly I think this is just better arcane trickster. At 3rd level you get a neat short duration domination (that is a supernatural ability and not mind effecting or technically compulsion) as long as they have an int score and can be sneak attacked. This is pretty freaking dope and i think a fair bit better than impromptu sneak attack even if it only gets two uses per day.
                                 * Also people have to pass a possibly INSANE check to notice your mind control spells. Good shit.
                                 * So what should you take? Well since you are only going to want to lose one level, I say Mask of The Whispering God Ninja or Unchained Rogue or ya know… any of these classes and take Accomplished Sneak Attacker.
                                 * Loses Levels (take Favored Prestige Class and Prestigious Spellcaster) 
                                 * (5/5) Agent of the Grave [Entry Level 9]
                                 * Undead fortitude means that we don’t have to care about CON for health and turns you into a fucking TANK (because your charisma should be through the roof). 
                                 * Also makes you an undead monster! Woo, get HD for undead, discrete and some necromanacy spells known...based on...intelligence? Egh…
                                 * If your DM is nice to you, and you are playing a tiefling, consider asking if you can choose your ability that replaces your normal SLA for the 001 roll on the d% table. Summoning a 1HD skeleton isn’t very useful most days BUT it does count as being able to cast animate dead, which can get you into this prestige class 3 levels earlier.
                                 * (5/5) Pathfinder Savant [Entry Level 5]
                                 * Snag rad spells from all of your favorite classes, get dispel magic, and greater dispel magic for free, and use your caster level for scrolls. Also make your UMD even more ridiculous than it already was. Definitely a great option for upping your arsenal.
                                 * (4/5) Sphere Singer [Entry Level 6]
                                 * Another Bard VMC favorite that gives you neat performances, tho this one also costs spell levels...in exchange for pretty damn good performances tbh.Overland flight as a performance is wild and find the path can sort of change ENTIRE adventures. You can also do a little inception. 
                                 * Honestly stay till level 7-8 and then dip, it’s not worth burning 3 feats just to get our spell levels back.
                                 * (3/5) Feysworn [Entry Level 5]
                                 * Fey Obedience thing! Idk about the other ones but i thought this one was neat! Getting DR is cool and so are the fey boons. Try not to stay beyond level 5.
                                 * Read this guide: On Bended Knee: A Guide to Pathfinder's Obedience Feats
                                 * (4/5) Proctor [Entry Level 7]
                                 * Get a Familiar, Boons, neat abilities like spirit sense or swift action freedom of movement and some extra monsters for your summon list. WOO.
                                 * Read this guide: On Bended Knee: A Guide to Pathfinder's Obedience Feats
                                 * (5/5*) Blackfire Adept [Entry Level 6] (3-4 Levels)
                                 * Specifically if you are going for an insane planar binding build and you need to get as high of an HD as possible. Stack blackfire pact with Augmented Calling for another 4HD which can be a vastly more powerful creature. Otherwise Skip
                                 * (2/5) Dragon Disciple [Entry Level 5]
                                 * I think this is a trap option, mainly because most of the benefits of joining this Prestige class (which there are many benefits) can either be replicated by spells or aren’t particularly appealing for us as Sorcerers. Besides the D12 hit die, which is tasty. In exchange, to keep up our spells, remember one of our two main class features, we have to trade 4 of your 10 feats...which is a lot. 
                                 * Again, I’m not saying this is useless or has no benefit, just that I think if you wanna be a dragon, we have other ways to do it, and give you more for it. That said? If you are a Bloodrager, or Kobold Divine caster with Scaled Disciple? Pop the fuck off, this is money.
                                 * (5/5) Souldrinker [Entry level 5 or 7]

                                 * Best for item crafters (totally can make a recycler build around Harvest Parts feat line and this to have a person who wastes nothing).
                                 * IF you are good or don't really have anything to do with the soul gems, skip
                                 * Also if you are using the new version….read this guide: On Bended Knee: A Guide to Pathfinder's Obedience Feats
