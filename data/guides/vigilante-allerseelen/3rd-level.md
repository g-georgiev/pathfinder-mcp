# 3rd Level

3rd Level


Celebrity Discount (Ex): A 10% discount on all equipment below a certain purchase limit is certainly enticing—you’ll probably start off with potions, scrolls, alchemical items, etc., and progress to low-cost wondrous items at later stages of renown. A solid social talent.


Loyal Aid (Ex): Loyal aid has its uses, but your mundane helpers won’t do anything for you that you couldn’t do for yourself, especially if you have a solid social identity going for you or had the many guises talent.
Triumphant Return (Ex): Keyword? “In any settlement where the vigilante previously gained renown.” If you’re traveling from one location to another, triumphant return won’t do a lick of good—it’s useful only if you plan to travel back and forth in between settlements, switching your area of renown every time.




5th Level


Feign Innocence (Ex): Feign innocence pairs nicely with notorious fool; the combo allows you to stroll into places that you don’t belong, get caught, and then reel off incredibly believable lies about how you got lost on the way to the restroom. It’s a lot of fun to get constant spell effects, too, especially ones that are extraordinary abilities that can’t be dispelled or suppressed.


Celebrity Perks (Ex): New life goals: establish an area of incredible renown and then settle in to receive exquisite food and lodging for free, forever. All joking aside, though, celebrity perks deals with stuff that most GMs handwave anyway, and the money that you save is basically pocket change by the time you can acquire the relevant renown talents. If you want to actually save some chunks of change, celebrity discount is a better purchase.




7th Level


Great Renown (Ex): By the time you get to great renown, you’ve actually started to get build toward some decent-sized towns. I only wish there were a way to establish renown faster than with the instant recognition talent at 13th level! One week is a hideously long time to spend on these talents.




9th Level


Subjective Truth (Ex): Meh. I think I’d rather just grab feign innocence and consciously lie about things. Guarding against truth detection magic is kind of a fringe use case.




11th Level


Incredible Renown (Ex): The capstone to the renown social talent line doesn’t disappoint. Finally, your name can live in infamy throughout seedy metropoli in the Inner Sea region! Korvosa, Westcrown, Riddleport—every criminal is going to fear and loathe you.




13th Level


Instant Recognition (Ex): Ugh, finally. GMs, I really think instant recognition could have been a social talent at a much earlier level—the bonuses that PCs get are still gated at 1st/7th/11th levels, and all that instant recognition does is ease everyone’s frustration at not being able to get the bonuses they paid for without literally spending a week working on an in-game PR campaign.




Social Grace Line


1st Level


Social Grace (Ex): Good skill bonuses that can easily overlap with ancestral enlightenment or skill familiarity. You can decide which skills are most needed in your group.




5th Level


In Vogue (Ex): There’s no need to use Profession or Craft to earn money when one combat encounter will probably have a higher payout. Just ignore these talents that deal with crafting and wage-earning.




Songbird Line


1st Level


Beast Friend (Su): Okay, sure. Lots of campaigns have some animals in them, and it’s nice to be able to help them feel comfortable around you. Animals have a much lower chance than humanoids of retaliating once they realize they’ve been mind-controlled, too.
Beast Speech (Su): As beast friend.
Songbird (Su): As beast friend.
