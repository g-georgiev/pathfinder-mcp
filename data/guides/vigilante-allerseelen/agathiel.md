# Agathiel

Agathiel


The Basics


        Vigilantes who transform themselves wholly or partly into animals as their vigilante identities. An interesting choice for natural attack builds, but it sacrifices a bit too much power for my tastes.


        Versatility: -1
        Power: -1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Immortal Commitment (Su): Although it makes complete sense that agathiels must be aligned with the standard neutral good of Nirvana and other agathions, it does stink that the archetype has more or less the same alignment restrictions as a cleric. Keep a scroll of atonement handy in case you get mind-controlled, unwillingly outfitted with a helm of opposite alignment, etc.


        (1) Bestial Identity (Su): Alters dual identity and replaces 4th-, 8th-, 12th-, and 16th-level vigilante talents. Ayayay. What a mish-mash of mechanics, eh? I’m going to drop some links, first to the beast shape suite of spells, and second to the polymorph subschool rules. Those will be necessary as we go on. Okay. Let’s dive in, blow by blow. The first stop along Bestial Identity Avenue is just being able to imitate an animal. You’re not an animal, although your seamless guise bonuses (+20) combined with the -10 penalty associated with this ability make it relatively easy for you to pass as an animal—a total +10 to your Disguise check. While in this shape, you won’t have any natural attacks or special abilities, so you’re pretty weak to start off, although you can still wield weapons at this point.
        By the time 4th level rolls around, you have access to a permanent version of beast shape I, with the exception that you get no ability adjustments and can pick only one of the special abilities mentioned by beast shape I. The former is less of a big deal than the latter; polymorph spells are powerful precisely because you can often pick up multiple special abilities, and the restrictions to this and later iterations of bestial identity do not inspire confidence. The upgrades at 8th, 12th, and 16th levels hand you more abilities from beast shape (pounce, poison, rend, and tremorsense/blindsense are the best available) but don’t change your form at all—you’re not going to be turning into a Warcat of Rull or other Large- or Huge-sized creature, as with beast shape II/III/IV, but rather just tacking on abilities to your base Small- or Medium-sized form. The final wrinkle with bestial identity is that your equipment does not meld with your body, as stated in the polymorph rules text! This is a net positive for the archetype, as it allows you to wear armor normally without having to worry about tanking your AC values into the basement. Overall, though, I think that bestial identity is a swing and a miss for agathiels. Vigilante talents are so powerful that you need an extremely compelling reason to give them up, and a few natural attacks and animal special abilities don’t truly compensate you for their loss. There aren’t a huge number of valuable forms in beast shape I anyway.


        (2) Agathion Blessing (Su): Replaces 2nd-level vigilante talents. Aspect of the Beast is a decent enough feat, I suppose, but the opportunity cost is again incredibly high. Claws of the beast is the only truly worthwhile option; don’t even bother with the others.


Is It Worth It?


        On balance, probably not unless you have a very specific character concept in mind. Beast shape I simply doesn’t pack a powerful enough punch to keep your combat efficacy on par with the base vigilante, and the special abilities you get are rationed out too stingily to compensate for the loss of most of your vigilante talents.
