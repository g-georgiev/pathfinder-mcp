# Anaphexia Thought-Killer

Anaphexia Thought-Killer


The Basics


        Norgorber-flavored vigilantes who cut out their own tongues in exchange for the ability to read minds and conceal their thoughts from others. Not a great archetype, as cool as it sounds.


        Versatility: -2
        Power: -2


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Tongue Sacrifice (Su): Tongue sacrifice has to be one of the most metal openings to any archetype I’ve ever read, but man, is it not worth it. Your social identity has to be a Pharasmin priest or priestess, and you cannot speak or cast spells with verbal components at all until you cast regenerate…which you only receive a whopping 1/day. What if you need your social identity to talk, and you’ve already regrown your one tongue for the day? And you deal damage to yourself, too. Ayayay. Probably the only upshot to tongue sacrifice is that it appears (RAW) to pave the way for a standard action transition from your social to vigilante identity, but that’s not much of a comfort when the quick change social talent can do much the same thing with a lot less hassle and hemorrhage.


        (2) Monastic Communication (Ex): Replaces 2nd-level vigilante talent. Monastic communication begins a long tradition in this archetype of mislabeling social and vigilante talents—someone at Paizo clearly got confused and didn’t read the class in depth before writing it. Monastic communication actually replaces a vigilante talent, and no, there’s no way in Phlegethon’s infernal foundries that getting bonuses to Bluff to pass messages through pantomime is an adequate replacement for a vigilante talent. This is as bad a trade as they come.


        (6) Silent to Magic (Su): Replaces 6th-level vigilante talent. Ugh. No, no, no! Bad archetype. Bad. Nondetection is already a little shrimpier than I’d like, but it earns its bones with an hours/level duration and fairly wide coverage over elementary divination magic. Make it only apply to mind-reading effects, and only apply to your social identity until 10th level, and it’s way, way undertuned. And for a vigilante talent? No. No, no, a thousand times no.


        (7) Thought-Scent (Su): Replaces 7th-level social talent. Credit where it’s due: thought-scent is a wicked cool ability, thematically speaking. Lack of credit where it’s also due: smelling whether someone knows a fact that you already know is incredibly niche, and certainly doesn’t portend well for 7th level, when any sane vigilante would be picking up quick change. I’ll grant it a yellow for pure rule of cool, but that’s it.


        (15) False Reading (Su): Replaces 15th-level social talent. Most opposed divination effects feature a save, and low-level magic like detect thoughts or detect anxieties should be a cinch for the Will-strong vigilante to plow through. Frankly, most Norgorberite PCs in this archetype would probably use false reading to guard against their allies more than their enemies, and that’s not usually a good sign for an ability. Just move on from this archetype already.


Is It Worth It?


        If it weren’t for the brute archetype, anaphexia thought-killer might easily take the cake for worst vigilante archetype of all time. Self-mutilation, a cumbersome and bloody transition into your vigilante identity, near-useless bonuses to pass hidden messages or conceal your thoughts from divination magic…there’s just so little to redeem the archetype. I want to love it, conceptually, but the mechanics are abysmal.
