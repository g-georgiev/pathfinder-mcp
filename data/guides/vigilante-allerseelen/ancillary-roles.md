# Ancillary Roles

Ancillary Roles


Ancillary roles are the roles that Pathfinder characters might expect to play outside of their primary combat or casting roles, including scouting/reconnaissance, anticaster functions, skill monkeying or Knowledge jockeying, non-magical healing, etc. Only in rare instances would you want to make these roles the main focus of a build, but some amount of support for them helps to create a well-rounded character that can contribute off the battlefield as well as on. 




Face


        “The face” is a generic term for the party member who handles most of the team’s social interaction. Whether that’s a Diplomacy check to sway an unfriendly NPC, a Bluff check to explain why the party was somewhere they weren’t supposed to be, or an Intimidate check to subtly intimate that terrible things might happen if an NPC was ever to double-cross you, the face role is ideally suited for vigilantes. You’re a Charisma-heavy class; you’ve got tons of social talents that amplify your social abilities, such as feign innocence, renown, skill familiarity, social grace, and subjective truth; and you’ve even got plenty of incentives to invest in combat builds that use those skills, such as feint builds (Bluff) and Shatter Defenses or Dazzling Display builds (Intimidate). Only you can know whether your party needs face skills, and if so, which ones, but suffice it to say that vigilantes can stand with the best of them—their skill familiarity social talent in particular lets them achieve shockingly consistent results even in heated conversations or interrogations, which is a boon when you have a good skill bonus but a low roll would still result in a failure.
