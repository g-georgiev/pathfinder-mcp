# Avenger Specialization

Avenger Specialization


Offense


(12) Mad Rush (Ex): Well, sure, Paizo, if you’re going to offer me freaking pounce, of course I’m going to rate that purple. The AC penalty is problematic, but can be offset with shield of blades, close the gap, and other AC-boosting effects. Not to mention that a full-attack pasted onto the end of a charge action is much more likely to outright kill your target than a single attack is, making mad rush somewhat safer to use than it appears at first blush.


(2) Fist of the Avenger (Ex): When you combine fist of the avenger, take ‘em alive, steel soldier, and Dragon Style (facilitated by a two-level dip in scaled fist unchained monk for flurry of blows), you can get some truly frightening bonuses to unarmed strikes on an avenger vigilante. I’m going to be making a sample build with all of the above, so look for that down in the builds section.


(2) Signature Weapon (Ex): Weapon Focus is an unfortunate feat tax in many builds, so you might truly want signature weapon to smooth that tax away. Weapon Specialization is fine on its own, but deserves a special shout-out for ranged builds, who need it to unlock Point-Blank Master, and Dazzling Display builds, who often won’t want to have to purchase Weapon Focus à la carte. “Nice, but not essential” is the motto here.
(2) Sweeping Strike (Ex): Cool, Cleave and Great Cleave for free, plus the ability to initiate a Great Cleave even if you miss your first attack. If you’ve got access to size increases or other abilities that will allow you to reach more foes, sweeping strike sets you up nicely for melee carnage, but it can also be easily ignored if you don’t prefer Cleave tactics.


(2) Combat Skill (Ex): The only, and I mean only reason to ever take combat skill is if you need a fighter-exclusive feat. Otherwise, you’re making a 1:1 talent-to-feat trade, and that trade is almost never worth it.
(2) Sucker Punch (Ex): Nearly all melee vigilantes have to at least consider whether they want to create a nonlethal damage build, given how powerful the take ‘em alive vigilante talent is, and given how effective the Enforcer feat is at demoralizing foes. Sucker punch is fine for these builds, but faces the usual obstacle that enemies must be completely unaware of the vigilante in order for it to function. This honestly feels like more of a stalker ability, so I’m frankly surprised that avengers are the intended audience.


(2) Weapon Familiarity (Ex): Weapon familiarity is strictly inferior to signature weapon unless you desperately need access to proficiency for an exotic weapon. Even then, you may be better off with a one- or two-level dip into a class that grants you proficiency, rather than wasting a vigilante talent on it.




Defense


(2) Heavy Training (Ex): There are Dexterity-based avenger vigilantes, but many full-BAB builds go with Strength as their main combat attribute because of the superior damage such builds typically offer. Heavy training pairs nicely with these Strength builds, allowing you to safely dump Dexterity while maintaining a good AC value. The fact that armor skin also applies to heavy armor with heavy training is only icing on a very nice cake.
(2) Unkillable (Ex): There are a bunch of bonuses packed into this talent, all of them good. Diehard is a polarizing feat, but leads to some wonderful prereqs; what’s more, the 12th-level benefit ensures that you can stay alive, well, forever, so long as it was HP damage that “killed” you, and so long as your team is quick enough to use healing magic on you.


(2) Steel Soldier (Ex): Alright, let’s unpack this talent. You modify a set of armor that you’re proficient with, paying both a time cost and a financial cost to do so. Once modified, the armor (1) can be concealed on your person with Sleight of Hand, and (2) ports over your armor’s enhancement bonus to the enhancement bonus of any gauntlets you use to attack. The first benefit is mediocre, since it’s often easier for heavy armor users to conceal the fact that they’re wearing armor (using the glamered armor enchantment, etc.) rather than take it off and put it on again. The second benefit is astoundingly good for unarmed strike/gauntlet builds, and will save you tens of thousands of gold on amulets of mighty fists or enchanted gauntlets/handwraps. You’ll definitely want to purchase steel soldier if you’re building toward unarmed strikes.




Mobility


(2) Nothing Can Stop Me (Ex): As hilarious as it is to Kool-Aid Man your way through walls, doors, etc., nothing can stop me is in no way a priority for most combat builds, which means that it will get set aside much of the time in favor of more relevant vigilante talents.
