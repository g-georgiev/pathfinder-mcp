# Avenging Beast

Avenging Beast


The Basics


        Vigilantes who cast spells as the hunter class and can wild shape (albeit somewhat poorly) into animals.


        Versatility: +1
        Power: -2


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Class Skills: Almost nothing but upshot here! Knowledge (Nature) and Spellcraft are both very useful skills (Spellcraft more so than Nature) and Knowledge (Engineering), Perform, and Sleight of Hand are almost never used. Certain builds might regret the loss of Disable Device, but those certain builds would also probably have a means of reacquiring Disable Device as a class skill through traits or something.


        (1) Skill Ranks: Boo, hiss. Well, avenging beasts aren’t going to be skill monkeys, that’s for sure. Plenty of classes survive with 4 + Intelligence skill ranks, though, and you can, too. Necessity, mothers, inventions—you know the saying.


        (1) Animal Mask (Ex): Alters dual identity. There are a lot of opportunities to get screwed in this archetype if your animal mask gets stolen, sundered, etc., but with the only class feature reliant on your vigilante identity being the undertuned version of wild shape you get at 5th level, there’s not much lost if you have to play an avenging beast in your social identity most of the time. Granted, that’s the iconic feature of this archetype, but it’s not essential to the playstyle.


        (1) Spellcasting: Replaces 4th-, 8th-, 10th-, 14th-, and 16th-level vigilante talents. I’m on record as believing that most Pathfinder players seriously underestimate the hunter spell list. First, you gain all the spells up to 6th level on the druid spell list, which is absolutely crammed full of valuable battlefield control spells. Perhaps more interestingly for a class that’s meant to be primarily combat-oriented, however, the hunter spell list grants you all the spells on the ranger spell list—with far more spell slots and access at far earlier levels than rangers themselves get! Allfood, bow spirit, hunter’s howl, protective spirit—there are lots of amazing ranger-exclusive spells, and hunters get ‘em, too. You’ll obviously have to pick your vigilante talents with care, but that’s not impossible to do, and the sacrifice is worth it in this instance.


        (1) Patron Spells: Replaces vigilante specialization. With so many of your vigilante talents gone, I couldn’t have gotten too excited about a specialization anyway, but patron spells still feels a little lackluster for my taste. In exchange for full BAB or hidden strike dice, avenging beasts get their patron’s spells added to their spell lists. You don’t automatically learn them as spells known, so choose wisely!


        (5) Wild Shape (Su): Replaces startling appearance, frightening appearance, and stunning appearance. I’m mostly okay with surrendering the appearance abilities, but I do wish that this version of wild shape weren’t so tragically limited. You can only ever assume the form of the animal represented by your mask, and when you do, it only acts as beast shape I. You’ll never get larger combat forms, better attribute bonuses, more exotic abilities, or wider versatility when wild shaped, taking much of the wind out of the ability’s sails before it even leaves harbor.


Is It Worth It?


        I’m not quite convinced, no. Gaining access to the hunter spell list is a great pickup, true, but wild shape, patron spells, and your notorious animal mask are all fairly steep downgrades from the base vigilante class features they replace. My advice would be just to play a hunter if you want this kind of playstyle.
