# Bellflower Harvester

Bellflower Harvester


The Basics


        Typically halfling vigilantes who focus heavily on the aid another action and teamwork tactics. Interesting as a support choice for a mostly combat-oriented class, and it segues directly into the bellflower tiller prestige class, if you’re interested in going that direction.


        Versatility: +0
        Power: +1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Rebellious Identity (Ex): Alters dual identity. I mean, this makes total sense for the archetype conceptually. Bellflower harvesters are, to all appearances, slaves, so the social identity matches that.


        (1) Bellflower Crop (Ex): Bellflower crop is absolutely necessary for the playstyle that this archetype encourages, but it has three major drawbacks: first, the action cost; second, the 30-foot range; and third, losing allies from your crop if they ever move outside of that range. GMs, please consider a little tactical homebrew on one of these three points. A swift action cost makes it bearable, as does a range that scales with vigilante level or the ability to “reinstate” allies into the crop if they/you move back in range. As it stands, though, it’s very hard to keep bellflower crop when combats can be mobile, far-ranging, and demanding on the action economy.


        (1) Crop Vigilance (Ex): Replaces vigilante specialization. Hoo boy! There’s a lot to go over with this one. Crop vigilance turns the bellflower harvester into a support-oriented melee class that employs the aid another action to great effect. The class feature slowly scales your bonuses up, so you need traits, feats, wondrous items, etc. that add bonuses directly to the top (rather than modifying the progression itself), give you a more streamlined action economy for using the aid another action, improve the duration of aid another bonuses, or increase the range of aiding another. Of note, feats and class features that improve the flat numerical bonuses of aid another don’t qualify under crop vigilance, but the rules text says nothing about other types of improvements (e.g., improvements to the action economy or duration of aid another), or improvements to the numerical bonuses that come from non-class feature, non-feat sources. There’s a summary below, and then a longform discussion after that.


      * Improving the Bonuses of Aid Another
      * Item: Benevolent weapons and armor
      * Item: Gloves of arcane striking
      * Feat: Got Your Back
      * Feat: Vanguard Style
      * Improving the Action Economy of Aid Another
      * Feat: Combat Reflexes + Bodyguard
      * Feat: Swift Aid
      * Feat: Team Up
      * Improving the Duration of Aid Another
      * Feat: Harrying Partners
      * Improving the Range of Aid Another
      * Feat: Magic Trick (Mage Hand) → Ranged Aid
      * Feat: Equipment Trick (Ladder) → Guarding Ladder
      * Feat: Cooperative Swarming
      * Wondrous Items
      * Benevolent Weapon and/or Armor: An absolute no-brainer for bellflower harvesters, benevolent weapons and benevolent armor add your enhancement bonus to whatever your granted aid another bonus is. The archetype caps out at +6 at 20th level, but if you have +5 benevolent leather armor or a +5 benevolent dagger, you can squeeze a +11 bonus out. So squeeze, pal.
      * Ring of Tactical Precision: An easy +1 to all aid another bonuses, along with some more teamwork feat shenanigans. And, uh…Profession (Soldier) bonuses? Sure. Sounds good.
      * Gloves of Arcane Striking: Ohoho, how I wish this combo worked. Bellflower harvester is tragically incompatible with other vigilante archetypes (cabalist, magical child, warlock) that grant an arcane caster level, but this would be a phenomenal pickup for a gestalt build. Again, all you’re doing is adding your arcane strike bonus right onto the top of what you’ve already got, so there are no conflicts between the bellflower harvester’s aid another bonus progression and this item.
      * Vigilante Talents
      * Team Player: Team player truly is all bellflower harvesters need from their vigilante talents. Gaining Swift Aid without the Combat Expertise requirement is peaches, of course, but the real power of team player is that you can aid every adjacent ally with one standard action. If you have three melee allies at your 11:00, 12:00, and 1:00, a single standard action will grant them all bonuses to hit. Add in Harrying Partners, and you’ll grant those bonuses for the entire round; add in Bodyguard, and you can use attacks of opportunity instead of any further actions for aiding AC. Truly, an incredible vigilante talent for this archetype.
      * Feats
      * Harrying Partners: Oh, good grief, what an amazing feat for this class. There are so many useful teamwork feats that involve the aid another action, but Harrying Partners is undoubtedly the best. The issue with aid another, beyond the relatively low bonuses and high action costs, is that your aid only persists for one triggering event. Aiding only improves one attack roll, not all of them on a full-attack; aiding only improves AC against one attack, rather than many. Harrying Partners is the solution. Now, whenever you aid another, the bonuses last until your next turn begins. All of your ally’s iterative attacks are buffed! One use of Bodyguard defends against an entire round’s worth of attacks! It’s hard to overstate what a big deal this is for the archetype. (Just remember that aid another is enemy-specific, so if you buff an ally’s attack rolls, the bonuses will only apply to the enemy you aided against. Ditto for AC.) You could ask your team to pick up Harrying Partners, but teamwork feats are usually tough sells, so using your tend the garden ability or a wand of shared training might be a better bet.
      * Combat Reflexes + Bodyguard: Combat Reflexes and Bodyguard are fairly effortless ways to ensure that your team rarely has to worry about AC. Grab a high Dexterity build, plant yourself (no pun intended) as close to as many allies as possible, and then just burn your attacks of opportunity from Combat Reflexes to blast those allies’ AC values into the lower stratosphere with benevolent armor, crop vigilance, and Harrying Partners.
      * Magic Trick (Mage Hand): We’re here in Magic Trick for the Ranged Aid option, which lets you use mage hand (probably from a hand of the mage) to aid another at a range of 30 feet. This is a huge gain! Aid another is typically restricted to what you can reach in melee range, which necessitates either a ratfolk + Cooperative Swarming build (so that you can share the space of your ally and wield a normal melee weapon) or a reach weapon build (so that you can stand behind an ally and poke at their legs). Now you can stand back with the spellcasters and provide Assistance From A Distance!™
      * Swift Aid: Swift Aid is an excellent feat, but it has to be considered in the context of the team player vigilante talent, which grants the feat for free and provides additional functionality for Swift Aid on top of what it normally does. With the team player talent, you’ll seldom find yourself actually using your swift actions to aid allies—team player’s standard action use case will buff the attack rolls of all of your adjacent melee teammates, and Combat Reflexes + Bodyguard is a much more effective means of buffing AC than Swift Aid. It’s not a bad idea to have it, since natural 1s do happen on aid another rolls, but most of what you need is provided by team player, not Swift Aid.
      * Equipment Trick (Ladder): Guarding Ladder is the trick we’re after here, which permits even Small-sized vigilantes to aid another from up to 10 feet away. I prefer Magic Trick (Mage Hand), but if you’re intent on not having a magical means of aiding another, this would be a good one.
      * Got Your Back: I would normally love Got Your Back, as it’s a quick way to turn Bodyguard into an anti-rogue/anti-slayer/anti-feint powerhouse. Unfortunately, it’s a teamwork feat, and tend the garden never scales you up past one teamwork feat—a teamwork feat that must go to Harrying Partners—shared with the team. The Combat Expertise requirement is also a bit onerous unless you’re willing to part with a vigilante talent for combat expertise. If you’re playing a gestalt build that grants shared training, tactician, etc., by all means, pick it up with all haste.
      * In Harm’s Way: In Harm’s Way is a bit more niche than the Combat Reflexes + Bodyguard wombo combo, since it requires you to take damage if your ally gets hit. Paired with a traditional non-magical healing build, however—that’s Healer’s Hands + Incredible Healer, for those following along at home—you can take hits from allies, then heal yourself for the damage. Think of it like a nonmagical oradin build!
      * Vanguard Style: I like Vanguard Style, but crop vigilance doesn’t scale the bonus to Reflex saves. The bonus will therefore remain small throughout your career.
      * Cooperative Swarming: This is a ratfolk-exclusive feat that would normally bump up your aid another bonuses, but the rules text of crop vigilance negates that.
      * Exceptional Aid: Modifies the bonus scaling.
      * Open Up: In addition to all the usual problems with bellflower harvesters sharing more than one teamwork feat, far too few enemies will ever carry physical shields for Open Up to be effective.
      * Team Up: Team Up is a good feat to have if you want to use aid another multiple times per round. Bellflower harvesters already have the team player vigilante talent, Harrying Partners, Combat Reflexes + Bodyguard, and more to enhance their efficiency, though. Skip the Combat Expertise tax. Skip Team Up.
      * Virtuous Creed (Protection): Modifies the bonus scaling.
      * Traits
      * Battlefield Disciple (Basic: Combat): Battlefield disciple grants a straight +1 to any aid another attempt used on an attack roll, and has no clause about adjacent allies. Great.
      * Azlanti Inheritor (Race: Gillman): Azlanti inheritor is a great trait, but you have to be a gillman to take it, and it only works for adjacent allies—Magic Trick (Mage Hand) won’t work.
      * Kin Guardian (Basic: Combat): A flat +2 to AC with aid another, but the catch is that you need to be buffing a member of your family. I’ve played family games of Pathfinder pretty successfully, but it’s not everyone’s deal.
      * Helpful (Basic: Combat) and Helpful (Race: Halfling): These are classic traits for halflings, but they modify the bonus scaling, and that’s a no-go.
      * Protective Faith (Religion: Torag): Modifies the bonus scaling.


        (1) Obsequious (Ex): Replaces 1st-level social talent. Obsequious could be a good social talent! Bluff is generally a much more useful skill than Disguise, so using Bluff in place of it when you need to shank someone in your social identity will help you get away scot-free more often. Seamless guise bonuses don’t apply when you’re making your vigilante identity conspicuous like that; if it comes down to a straight competition between Bluff with a bonus and Disguise with no bonus, I know which one I’m taking to the ball.


        (2) Tend the Garden (Ex): Replaces 2nd-level vigilante talent. I’m conflicted about tend the garden. On the one hand, it’s entirely necessary for the playstyle, since so many of the best aid another feats are teamwork feats; on the other hand, tend the garden costs a standard action, has a pretty abysmal duration, and never scales the action economy or number of teamwork feats you can share. I offer only two suggestions to lenient GMs: first, for the love of all that’s holy, make the duration 1 round per 2 levels. It’s ridiculous that a 20th-level vigilante could only share one teamwork feat for 24 seconds. Increasing the duration certainly doesn’t bust the power curve, it just allows the archetype to spend standard actions on other things such as—and this is just a spitball here—the aid another action. You know, as it was designed to do. Second, allow bellflower harvesters to sacrifice vigilante talents for improvements to the action economy (standard → move → swift → free) or for more teamwork feats shared (1 → 2 → 3 → 4). This puts the ability in line with the cavalier’s tactician ability, but the continued sacrifice of vigilante talents would ensure that the power level isn’t overtuned. With those two changes, I wouldn’t have any problem improving my rating of tend the garden.


Is It Worth It?


        Bellflower harvester has some serious limitations, notably the preponderance of standard action abilities, the tragically short range of bellflower crop, and the short duration of tend the garden. Is it better than other aid another builds? No, probably not. But at 17th level, team player, Swift Aid, a +5 benevolent weapon, and a ring of tactical precision would allow you to grant a +12 bonus to attack rolls for all adjacent allies against one enemy. Bodyguard, meanwhile, could grant that same +12 AC bonus to any ally as an attack of opportunity. Assuming that you take Harrying Partners and are regularly sharing it with allies (either through tend the crop, a wand of shared training, or some other means) then all of these bonuses will last an entire round. If given a little love by sympathetic game masters, I don’t see any reason it couldn’t be a great archetype, but you may be more frustrated by its potential than fulfilled by its implementation if you play it strictly as Paizo wrote it.
        Of note, the mechanics of the bellflower harvest archetype and the aid another action favor large, melee-oriented adventuring parties. Bellflower crop has no target cap; neither does tend the garden or the team player vigilante talent. The more melee allies you can cram in next to you, the more you’ll be able to pass out the monstrously large aid another bonuses that the bellflower harvester is capable of. If you have eidolons, animal companions, summoned creatures, cohorts, etc., start looking at this archetype much more seriously.
