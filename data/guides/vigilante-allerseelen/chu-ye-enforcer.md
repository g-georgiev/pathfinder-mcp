# Chu Ye Enforcer

Chu Ye Enforcer


The Basics


        Predominantly evil vigilantes from the Tien nation of Chu Ye who draw mechanical and thematic inspiration from oni. Works best for dedicated natural attack/unarmed strike builds.


        Versatility: +1
        Power: +1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Yokai Heart (Su): What an interesting ability! I mean, alignment restrictions usually get an eye roll from me, but if you’re imitating oni (who are pretty bad customers, let’s be honest) then you probably deserve to be non-good. The more interesting thing is that you get low-light vision and 60 feet of darkvision while in your vigilante identity, which is a huge upgrade for races like humans and halflings. The clause about counting as a native outsider or a humanoid, whichever is worse for you, is likely to be more nuisance than true problem—you’d be locked out of buffs like enlarge person or reduced person that affect only humanoids, and susceptible to effects like dominate person or charm person even though you still count as a native outsider. It’s worth tracking potential interactions with team buffs or enemy debuffs, but I’m inclined to think you won’t notice it more than about a dozen times in a six-book Paizo AP.


        (1) Oni Mask (Ex): Alters dual identity. Another polarizing ability. Here’s the positive take: scaling bonuses to Intimidate are great, especially for an already Charisma-heavy archetype that gets to retain its vigilante specialization. The ability makes a strong case for a stalker specialization so that you can take advantage of the twisting fear vigilante talent, Shatter Defenses + hidden strike, and all the other Intimidate-based goodies available to rogue-like classes. The negative, on the other hand, is that binding your class features to an external object that can be destroyed is (as every wizard’s spellbook knows) an invitation for savage GM tricks that destroy your combat effectiveness. There are a few possibilities here, all of them bad:


      * Your oni mask gets sundered. Probably the least likely scenario, both because monsters don’t often sport Improved/Greater Sunder and also because the mask has a decent hardness rating and a good amount of hit points. At higher levels, though, a sustained assault by a sunder-specialized creature will destroy your mask in probably only one or two hits, and without it, you’re down a vigilante identity and a bunch of gold, in addition to having been doxed in front of your team. It’s likely that greater make whole is the only remedy in this case, since I would assume the caster level of the mask scales with your vigilante level. Make friends with a prepared caster who has access to that spell.
      * Your oni mask gets stolen. A sneaky, steal-the-mask-while-you-sleep approach is possible, and certain enemies might do just that. A more front-door approach would involve using the steal combat maneuver to snatch the mask right off your face—and I’m afraid it wouldn’t be that difficult. At best, a mask would count as fastened for the purposes of the steal rules, meaning it would be your enemy’s CMB check against your CMD + 5. At worst, it would count as loosely attached, which is CMB vs. CMD. You may want to explicitly stipulate that you’ve built your mask into some kind of helmet, etc., so that you can advance the argument that it’s closely worn, making it impossible to steal.
      * Your oni mask gets lost. Lost? How did you lose it!? Okay, you may deserve this one.


        (1) Steel Dictate (Ex): Replaces 1st-level social talent. In general, swapping social talents for combat abilities is a great idea—vigilantes frankly have more social talents than they truly need, and never enough combat efficacy. This is a carbon copy of the fist of the avenger vigilante talent, and it’s a phenomenal pickup for the archetype. If you become an avenger, you can always take fist of the avenger anyway, for a combined +10 to damage (yes, please); if you become a stalker, you can simply enjoy more static damage bonuses. Either way, it reduces the need for a scaled fist monk dip (although you may still wish to take one anyway for Charisma to AC, flurry of blows, and some bonus feats).


        (4) Deceitful Form (Sp): Replaces 4th-, 14th-, and 18th-level vigilante talents. Oh, this is a fun one. You might think that losing three vigilante talents isn’t great—and it’s not—but there’s more here than meets the eye, much like with oni themselves. First, at-will alter self is a great ability, both for deception/infiltration as well as for combat. Since most Chu Ye enforcers will go for a hybrid unarmed strike/natural attack build, you’re going to want to turn into a lizardfolk berserker, which grants a bite and two claws right away. Giant form I should prioritize troll forms: troll monarchs get a 19-20/x2 crit range on their claw attacks; troll berserkers get a 40-foot movement speed; troll furies get fire resistance 20, which helps keep that regeneration up and running; rock trolls lose their regeneration to sonic damage, rather than fire damage. All troll forms give you three natural attacks, rend, and regeneration, which is more than worth the price of admission. Giant form II opens things up a bit more: sun giants and moon giants have great energy resistances; ocean giants dominate aquatic combat; and mountain trolls remain the undisputed kings of offense. No matter what, I highly recommend you use my friend Zack’s resource Project Artemis, which has the best polymorph form search engine I’ve ever seen. I used it to write this section, in fact!
        Beyond the immediate applications of functionally unlimited polymorph spells, deceitful form also gives you the shapechanger subtype. This boon allows you to skip the malleable flesh vigilante talent, which is somewhat subpar, and go straight to seamless shapechanger and morphic weaponry. Seamless shapechanger adds your seamless guise bonuses to any Disguise checks you make to appear as the creature you’re impersonating with alter self or giant shape, which is huge for infiltration. It starts out as a +20 bonus, +10 from the polymorph effect itself, and then another potential +10 from favored class bonuses like the human’s, half-elf’s, or kitsune’s. Add a Charisma focus and a few ranks, and literally no one will know that you’re not actually a fire giant, undine, moss troll, etc. Morphic weaponry, unfortunately, grants you natural attacks only up to a maximum of four at 15th level, and they replace other natural attacks, rather than add to them. All of your polymorph forms already have three natural attacks; with your helmet gore, that’s four. Anyway, it’s quite feasible to have about eight attacks in this build (four iteratives made as kicks with flurry of blows + Improved Unarmed Strike + bite + two claws + deadly horns) so consider getting Multiattack to cut down on your secondary natural attack penalties.


        (6) Deadly Horns (Ex): Replaces 6th-level vigilante talent. Okay, this is a bad trade. Consider that morphic weaponry grants four primary natural attacks, and you’ll start to see why. I’m all for more natural attacks, especially when they stack with your existing tools, but still…


        (12) Third Eye (Su): Replaces 12th-level vigilante talent. I love a lot of things about this ability: that you can use it as a swift action, that it has a massive range, and that it targets touch AC. What I don’t love is that the damage is quite anemic for this level, is subject to energy resistance, and replaces a much-needed vigilante talent. Good for tagging runners, pausing regeneration or fast healing, squeezing out a little more DPR in critical rounds, etc., but overall, fire resistance is too prevalent for it to work as well as Paizo seems to want it to work. If I were to rewrite the ability, I would increase the damage (1d6 per 2 levels or even 1d6 per level) and dramatically lower its uses per day.


Is It Worth It?


        I wish that I could give up third eye and potentially deadly horns, but the overall chassis is very solid, especially at high levels. Although a Strength-based Chu Ye enforcer is a perfectly legitimate build, I think I’m going to pop a Dexterity-based stalker version of this archetype into the builds section, so head there if you’d like to see this playstyle in action more.
