# Cleave

Cleave


Cleave Through / Goblin Cleaver / Orc Hewer / Giant Killer: Why are some of the best Cleave feats dwarf-only? Beats me. Dwarves don’t make fantastic vigilantes, with their penalties to Charisma, but they can be incredibly hardy, and these feats make them the unquestioned kings and queens of cleaving in Pathfinder.


Cleaving Finish / Improved Cleaving Finish: Cleaving Finish lets you whirl through a crowd of minions like they’re nothing, but requires you to be dealing consistently high damage against enemies with consistently low hit points. Good if your GM likes to add more trash mobs to encounters to heighten the difficulty.
Surprise Follow-Through / Improved Surprise Follow-Through: Denying Dexterity to AC is never a bad build goal, especially when you can get it as part of an action you would already be taking. Avenger vigilantes don’t get hidden strike dice, however, which incentivizes them to take Surprise Follow-Through less than stalkers.


Cleaving Sweep: Somewhat nonsensically requires you to be using a greataxe, and forces you to give up your iterative attacks for a chance to knock everyone down. Still, you get to make every trip maneuver at your highest BAB, so it’s a great feat for trip builds that plan on growing to Large size.


Cleave / Great Cleave: Better just to take the sweeping strike vigilante talent to get these bad bois.
