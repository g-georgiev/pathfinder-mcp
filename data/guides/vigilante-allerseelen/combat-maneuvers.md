# Combat Maneuvers

Combat Maneuvers


Chokehold: An excellent feat for grapplers who need to stop an enemy from crying out, casting spells with verbal components, etc. Unfortunately, it doesn’t work on all enemy types, so make sure you have some Knowledge checks under your belt before you try to choke someone out.
Demonic Style / Momentum / Slaughter: Demonic Style is a lovely adjunct for any avengers using the mad rush vigilante talent—an extra +1 to attack and +2 to damage never goes out of fashion. The following feats, Demonic Momentum and Demonic Slaughter, are probably unnecessary unless you’re building heavily for a bull rush build.
Dirty Fighting: If you need to flex into combat maneuvers for some reason, Dirty Fighting is the way to do it. Not only does it give you a (generally) better bonus than Combat Expertise, it also satisfies the prereqs for Combat Expertise, Improved Unarmed Strike, Intelligence 13, and Dexterity 13, at least for the improved maneuver feats or anything that lists them as prerequisites. That’s a lot of bang for your feat buck.
Dirty Trick Master: Dirty trick is among the more effective combat maneuvers, and Dirty Trick Master opens you up to some nasty debuffing combos. Shout-out to the nauseated option, which essentially prevents enemies from removing the status condition (you need a standard action to do so, but nauseated enemies are limited to a single move action per round).
Equipment Trick (Net Tricks): Excellent bonuses for grapple and dirty trick builds.
Improved [Maneuver] / Greater [Maneuver] / Quick [Maneuver]: If you’re building for combat maneuvers, you should basically assume that you need the Improved, Greater, and Quick maneuver feats. No one gets away without them.
Rapid Grappler: 100% useful for grappling builds. Streamline that action economy as much as you can, my droogs.
Superior Dirty Trick: Dirty Trick Master and Superior Dirty Trick are both essential to a high-quality dirty trick build. My advice would be the blinded condition, but YMMV.
Throat Slicer: Assuming you can get good enough at grappling, Throat Slicer will put a quick end to most opponents: you initiate a grapple (standard action) and pin (move action with Greater Grapple) in Round 1, then coup de grace (standard action with Throat Slicer) at the top of Round 2. Fast and lethal.


Bushwhack: Since vigilantes spend so much time attacking from Stealth, Bushwhack is a great adjunct to grapple builds. Grapple someone from invisibility, pin as a free action, and then do whatever you want after that.
Disengaging Feint / Flourish: Fun mobility feats for feint builds. I’m not a huge fan of feint, but the cunning feint vigilante talent does at least make it a viable option for the class.
Equipment Trick (Tanglefoot Bag: Captivating Embrace): Literally gluing yourself to an opponent you want to grapple is hilarious for grapple builds, and confers some pretty strong bonuses.
Felling Smash: Felling Smash is compatible with Vital Strike, making it a nice option for trip builds. Hey, why not take a swift action trip opportunity?


Deadly Grappler: Unfortunately for this feat, most of the damage from avenger vigilante unarmed strike builds comes from static damage bonuses—fist of the avenger grants a static bonus, steel soldier grants a static bonus, take ‘em alive grants a static bonus—that don’t increase your base damage die beyond 1d3, 1d4, and other small numbers. As such, it’s more useful for monks and brawlers, who get the chance to progress their damage to bigger dice, and therefore stand to gain more from increases in effective size.
Unfair Grip: Minor bonuses for grapple builds. Every last bit helps when you’re trying to make combat maneuvers usable.


Combat Expertise: Even for avengers, combat maneuver builds are probably sub-optimal—but if you’re determined to make one work, you’ll need Combat Expertise, guaranteed. The combat expertise vigilante talent is probably worth it in this instance, as the ability to ignore the Intelligence 13 prerequisite for any feat is too tasty to pass up. If you’re playing with Elephant in the Room feat tax rules, Combat Expertise is simply an option given to every character, depending on their BAB, and won’t need to be selected at all.
