# Combat Roles

Combat Roles


        “Combat roles” refers to something that every class needs to figure out: how am I going to deal damage in combat? Full casters can often get away without any reliable means of dealing damage in combat, provided that they can offer enough buffing, debuffing, summoning, battlefield control, etc. to support the team anyway, but ¾-BAB strikers or ⅔ casters must often juggle twin mandates: deal effective damage, and offer spellcasting or skill functionality outside of combat. Much of a class’ suitedness to certain combat roles is decided by their weapon and armor proficiencies, as well as—in the case of ¾-BAB classes—their ability to debuff or self-buff in order to soften enemies up a little for their invariably lower attack rolls. In the next sections, we’ll spend some time examining the vigilante’s facility with each role, and talk about opportunity costs you might face for specializing in one thing over another. With respect to attributes, you can expect to build vigilantes very similarly: STR > CON, CHA > DEX, WIS > INT for Strength-based melee vigilantes wearing medium or heavy armor, and DEX > CON, CHA > STR, WIS > INT for Dexterity-based melee vigilantes or ranged vigilantes wearing light armor. Owing to the vicissitudes of the lethal grace vigilante talent, it does behoove melee vigilantes who use Dexterity as their attack stat not to dump Strength, so keep that in mind as you’re building.
