# Core Combat Feats

Core Combat Feats


Universal


Nature Soul / Animal Ally / Boon Companion: The classic three-feat trade for a fully scaling animal companion. You’ve got a good Charisma score for Handle Animal, so perhaps a decent investment.
Champion of [Alignment]: Your GM may not be planning to play with the shifts and affirmations subsystem from Pathfinder Unchained, but if they’re open to the idea, many of these feats give you some great weapon/spell damage bonuses in addition to powerful spell-like abilities. Worth investigating.
Divine Fighting Technique: A great, flavorful, versatile feat for vigilantes. Cayden Cailean, Gorum, Iomedae, Lamashtu, Sarenrae, Torag, and Urgathoa are generally thought to have the strongest styles, but I need to give a special shout-out to Norgorber’s Silent Shiv for stalker vigilantes. The main issue with stalker vigilantes is that once an enemy becomes aware of your presence, you lose a decent chunk of your combat efficacy—everything from your downgraded d8 → d4 hidden strike damage dice, to your ability to apply hidden strike rider talents, to the efficacy of startling, frightening, and stunning appearance is affected. In defending this feat, then, I’m just going to quote from Norgorber’s rule text: “At the start of each round, choose one opponent that you can see and attempt a Stealth check as a swift action. If the result of your check exceeds the target’s CMD, treat the target as if it were completely unaware of your presence for the purpose of the initial benefit of this divine fighting technique. If you beat the target’s CMD by 5 or more, you also treat it as being completely unaware of your presence for the purposes of all class features and feats you have.” In other words, if you can succeed at a high enough Stealth check, none of your combat efficacy goes away. Deal your full d8 hidden strike damage all the time! Proc startling appearance continuously! Use the foe collision and leave an opening vigilante talents every round! This is truly the most powerful combo in the game that I know of for stalker vigilantes, and grows to ridiculous levels of effectiveness when you have feats like Skill Focus (Stealth) or Dampen Presence, magical items like armor with the greater shadow enchantment, and vigilante talents like sure-footed, owl’s sight, hide in plain sight, and skill familiarity pumping your Stealth score through the roof.
Flickering Step: You’re telling me you couldn’t use a free dimension door a few times per day?
Improved Initiative: Never, ever a wasted feat.
Martial Focus: Martial Focus is a noteworthy feat because it gives you access to weapon mastery feats—normally feats that are exclusive to the fighter class—for a relatively small investment. Cut from the Air, Smash from the Air, and Spellcut are classics for ensuring that martial classes need never fear a spellcaster or archer ever again, but Burrowing Shot, Devastating Assault, and Weapon Style Mastery can also be good in the right builds.
Noble Scion (Scion of War): Swapping Dexterity for Charisma on initiative checks is a great idea for Strength-based vigilantes who wear heavy armor and like the smashy-smashy. It’s likely to add up to at least Improved Initiative over time, if not more.
Wanderer’s Fortune: Wanderer’s Fortune effectively eliminates any fear you might have had about being grappled or swallowed whole.


Additional Traits: Heck yeah! Get you some additional traits.
Barroom Brawler: Barroom Brawler is a limited version of the brawler class’ martial flexibility feature, and although you may not necessarily have the build space to purchase it, it’s definitely a powerful versatility option for vigilantes of all stripes.
Big Game Hunter: It’s not the most exciting feat on the block, but it does make you better at attacking the Large and larger enemies you’ll find in high-level Pathfinder play.
Canny Tumble: Although the prereqs are kinda nutty, stalker vigilantes who want to consistently proc hidden strike damage may wish to investigate Canny Tumble. It’s way easier to get working if you’re using the Elephant in the Room feat tax rules, which combine Dodge and Mobility into one feat.
Combat Reflexes / Combat Patrol: A pretty easy feat combo to flex into, thanks to the vigilante’s reflexes talent, and excellent for avenger vigilantes using reach weapons to set up massive “oh no no zones” where any movement of any kind triggers an attack of opportunity. Pair with the vital punishment vigilante talent for extra chips and guac.
Critical Focus: A classic feat for both avengers and stalkers, although avengers will tend to have both more attacks and less reliance on non-multiplying precision damage.
Vital Strike / Improved / Greater / Devastating Strike / Improved Devastating Strike: Vigilantes can get their hands on Vital Strike pretty easily, thanks to the vital punishment vigilante talent. Not only does Devastating Strike allow you to apply the bonus damage to a Vital Strike as normal for the feat, vital punishment + Devastating Strike would allow you to apply the bonus damage to an attack of opportunity 1/round!
Great Fortitude: Vigilantes are, somewhat bafflingly, a Fortitude-weak class. Can’t walk around failing saves against poisons and nausea, ya hear?
Ghostslayer: Everybody hates incorporeal fights, right? Well, Ghostslayer is a ghost touch weapon, but as a feat. Sure can come in handy, especially for stalkers, who need to deal hidden strike damage like a diabetic needs a new pancreas.
Improved Critical: Avengers will be most likely to get a lot of use out of Improved Critical, as they’re going to be attacking more often and dealing higher static damage than stalkers. Stalkers, who deal a good chunk of their damage through non-multiplying hidden strike dice, won’t get nearly as much out of crits.
Lunge: Especially for reach builds, Lunge can be an excellent feat, allowing you to tag enemies farther and farther outside of your normal threatened range. Remember that your reach increase ends when your turn ends, so you can’t take attacks of opportunity while lunging
Power Attack: As with Deadly Aim and Piranha Strike, Strength-based avengers will want Power Attack; stalkers will most likely not.
Toughness: More HP. Not thrilling, but serviceable.
Weapon Versatility: Probably the simplest, cheapest way to get around DR/damage type. That’s not usually penetrable with enhancement bonuses, so the feat is well worth considering.


Anatomical Savant: Of some use to high-level stalker vigilantes, who may need to deal with precision damage immunities regularly. Not perfect, but better than bringing nothing to the fight.
Arcane Strike / Riving Strike: Certain vigilante archetypes (cabalist, magical child, warlock) turn the vigilante into an arcane caster. Arcane Strike and Riving Strike are good feats for keeping damage and spell save DCs up in such builds.
Armor Proficiency, Heavy: I suppose you could be a heavily armored stalker; avengers will mostly just take the heavy training vigilante talent and reap the benefits there instead of taking this feat.
Armored Athlete: Fine, I suppose, if you’re not taking the armor skin vigilante talent.
Butterfly’s Sting: Desna worship and Combat Expertise requirements notwithstanding, Butterfly’s Sting is a wonderful feat, especially for stalker vigilantes, who will deal most of their damage through hidden strike. Instead of unsuccessfully trying to multiply that precision damage, you can instead pass the crit off to allies!
Deceitful Incompetence: Quite good for reach builds, which will be interested in hitting with every attack of opportunity they possibly can.
Dodge / Mobility: Although Dodge and Mobility lead into a ton of feat combos, the main ones that you’d consider are Spring Attack, Crane Style, Osyluth Guile, and perhaps Canny Tumble. If you’re just here for Spring Attack, skip it and grab the fantastic stride vigilante talent. If you’re here for other purposes, you have my blessing to proceed.
Equipment Trick (Thunderstone: Startling Noise): AoE hidden strike damage is great for stalker vigilantes, although you’ll have to put up with the low save DCs on thunderstones.
Weapon Focus: So many feats depend on Weapon Focus, it’s not even funny. Take it if you need prerequisites out of the way, but otherwise leave it alone.


Blind-Fight: While Blind-Fight is a good feat, vigilantes (especially stalker vigilantes) are better off just taking the strike the unseen vigilante talent to acquire Blind-Fight, Improved Blind-Fight, Greater Blind-Fight, and the equivalent of Improved Shadow Strike.
Careful Sneak: The armor skin vigilante talent does a much better job of enabling stealth builds, if that’s what you’re looking for.
Diehard: Even if you wanted Diehard, only avenger vigilantes have any business using it, and the unkillable vigilante talent does a much, much better job of things than the feat does. Now I have a machine gun. Ho ho ho.
Shadow Strike: Stalker vigilantes can already deal hidden strike damage to targets with concealment! If you want to deal precision damage to targets with displacement or other forms of total concealment, the strike the unseen vigilante talent does a much better job.
Spring Attack / Improved / Greater: Again, replicated better by the fantastic stride vigilante talent.
