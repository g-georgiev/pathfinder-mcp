# Darklantern

Darklantern


The Basics


        Elven vigilantes who transform into drow in order to infiltrate drow societies in the Darklands. Comes with a meddlesome Will save mechanic that deals Wisdom damage each time they try to resume their social identity.


        Versatility: -1
        Power: -2


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Elven Ancestry: Elves aren’t any great shakes as vigilantes, so you’ll want to go half-elf for the overlap with the human favored class bonus and floating stat bonus.


        (1) Dark Identity (Su): Alters dual identity. Dark identity turns your vigilante side into a drow, and while there are decent compensations for the form (+2 Dexterity and Charisma is nice, as is darkvision to augment the half-elf’s low-light vision) the disadvantages outweigh the advantages. First, there’s that nasty Will save mechanic to resume your social identity—reminiscent of the brute archetype—with Wisdom damage attached to every failure. Until you can reliably hit your Will save DC to transform back on everything but a natural 1, it’s best not to increase the time you spend in your dark identity beyond 1 hour—ability damage is hard to deal with at low levels, and every point of Wisdom damage reduces your ability to hit the DC the next time you try to break away from your drow form. Second is a thematic objection: absent everybody’s favorite drow Drizzt, anybody on Golarion who knows even a little bit about drow will despise and probably attack you in your vigilante identity. Is that something you want to deal with?


        (1) Drow Magic (Sp): Replaces 1st-level social identity. This is essentially one 0th-, 1st-, and 2nd-level spell per day, and while faerie fire is undoubtedly a great spell, dancing lights and darkness are considerably less useful as 1/day abilities. Plus, drow magic competes with owl’s sight, companion to the lonely, social grace, renown, and other good 1st-level social talents. My instinct is that this ain’t worth it.


        (4) Drow Paragon (Su): Replaces 4th-level vigilante talent. Unfortunately, SR 6 + level is about as weak as it gets. Against a caster of an even level, you have only about a 25% chance to avoid a spell—and that’s not even counting Spell Penetration or similar effects. Now, granted, a lot of monsters with class levels tend to have caster levels much lower than a PC’s level, but spell resistance will still prevent your allies’ spells from affecting you unless you take a standard action to lower your spell resistance. That makes in-combat buffing pretty difficult. I don’t know. I’m not enthused.


Is It Worth It?


        Between the lore complications of assuming the form of a drow, the hardships of transforming from your vigilante identity to your social identity, and the relative weakness of drow magic and drow paragon, I can’t recommend it. It might work for certain Darklands-themed campaigns, but otherwise, no.
