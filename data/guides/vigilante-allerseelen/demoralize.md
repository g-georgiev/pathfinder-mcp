# Demoralize

Demoralize


Cornugon Smash: Melee vigilantes who deal lethal damage (and are therefore ineligible for Enforcer) will do better picking up Cornugon Smash, which lets you demoralize as a free action whenever you hit someone with Power Attack. Avengers in particular can be very frightening with this playstyle.
Dazzling Display / Disheartening Display: Vigilantes are a Charisma-based striker class with no spellcasting, so they’ll always be looking out for ways to use UMD or Intimidate to contribute spellcasting or debuffing to the team where they wouldn’t normally be able to. Dazzling Display is a wonderful AoE debuffing tool; stalker vigilantes especially should strongly consider it for its easy synergy with the twisting fear vigilante talent.
Enforcer  / Bludgeoner: Enforcer is perhaps the single best method of single-target demoralization in Pathfinder, at least against enemies that are susceptible to nonlethal damage. You may need Bludgeoner in some builds to convert warhammers, heavy flails, etc. to nonlethal damage.
Hurtful: A classic feat for wailing on demoralized enemies. More attacks taken as swift actions are always good things!
Shatter Defenses: Hit a shaken enemy with an attack, make them flat-footed until your next turn. Apart from being a great way to continuously proc hidden strike damage and improve your chances to hit, Shatter Defenses is also one of only a handful of abilities in the game that imposes the flat-footed condition; Sap Master builds are the main builds for which the flat-footed vs. Dexterity to AC distinction matters. Note also the excellent synergy between Shatter Defenses and the malevolent armor enchantment.
Signature Skill (Intimidate): It’s relatively easy to make enemies shaken, and you can sometimes ratchet that up to frightened if you use Disheartening Display, but what about more severe fear conditions like panicked or cowering? If you want those, there’s no feat purchase more important than Signature Skill (Intimidate). It takes a while to get going, but by 10th or 15th level, you should be able to make enemies at least frightened—possibly panicked—regularly. Great for peeling enemies away from hard fights.


Dreadful Carnage: While not quite as “demoralize on demand” as Dazzling Display, Dreadful Carnage fits better into a two-handed weapon build and doesn’t require nearly the action cost as Dazzling Display. An interesting choice to consider.
Equipment Trick (Cape: Dazzling Trail): An extra 1d4 rounds on any demoralize effect ain’t bad at all.
Golden League Tattoos: Take 10 on Intimidate checks. Pretty nice, especially if you’re not an ifrit with the Fiery Glare trait.
Gory Finish: Gory Finish doesn’t permit full-attack actions, but it does permit Vital Strikes, so you can use it on weakened enemies when you have to move up to them or know they’re on their last legs.
Shadows of Fear: Free sneak attack damage as long as you’re targeting a shaken creature. Yes, stalker vigilantes will be more than happy to have that, as it guarantees hidden strike damage at least once per round against enemies that aren’t immune to fear effects.


Frightening Ambush: Stalker vigilantes in particular love to attack enemies from Stealth, but many will have better modes of demoralization—Enforcer, Dazzling Display, etc. If you’d like at least some facility with Intimidate, Frightening Ambush isn’t the worst feat in the world.
Intimidating Prowess: Many avengers will likely be Strength-based, making Intimidating Prowess an easy (if not particularly exciting) boost to Intimidate checks.
Motivating Display: Morale bonuses are pretty common, but still, free bonuses for using Dazzling Display, which you would have been doing anyway.
