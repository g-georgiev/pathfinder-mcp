# Falconine (Allerseelen’s Rewrite)

Falconine (Allerseelen’s Rewrite)


        (2) Aquiline Instinct (Ex): At 2nd level, a falconine wildsoul has begun to emulate the grace and perceptual acuity of raptors. He is constantly under the effects of glide while in his vigilante identity; treat this as an extraordinary effect. Additionally, he gains a racial bonus on Perception checks equal to 1/2 his vigilante level (minimum +1) and a +2 racial bonus on initiative checks in any surprise round in which he can act.


        Perfect fall on its own is a poor excuse for an archetype ability, so I modified the original soft landing class feature and combined it with the cleric’s Feather domain ability eye of the hawk—the Perception bonuses are better, both in terms of breadth and bonus type, and a little initiative bonus doesn’t matter so much. Glide is mostly a quality of life upgrade, and mimics the tengu racial trait in terms of power.


        (6) Take to the Air (Ex): At 6th level, a falconine wildsoul can harness the flight inherent in his blood. While in his vigilante identity, he has feathery wings that grant him a fly speed of 40 feet (with good maneuverability).


        You’re turning into a literal bird. It shouldn’t take until 12th level to be able to fly. Enough said.


        (12) Aerial Supremacy (Ex): At 12th level, a falconine wildsoul has honed his skill in the air to an incredible degree. His fly speed increases to 60 feet (with perfect maneuverability) and he gains the Hover and Powerful Wings feats, ignoring their prerequisites.


        Again, a literal bird should be at least as good at flying as a creature using fly. Hover and Powerful Wings are fun little abilities for kicking up clouds of dust, staying in place without a Fly check, and qualifying for feats like Buffeting Wings or Snatch and Drop. This is another good ability, but nothing that full casters haven’t been doing for a bunch of levels by now.


        (18) Deadly Dive (Ex): At 18th level, a falconine wildsoul has learned how to mimic the swooping and diving of birds of prey. As a full-round action while he is flying at least 30 feet above the ground, a falconine wildsoul can dive onto a creature by moving up to twice his fly speed and making a full-attack action. The movement provokes attacks of opportunity as normal, and the attack provokes an attack of opportunity from the attack’s target. On the first successful attack of the full-attack action, the falconine wildsoul deals the attack’s damage plus 2d6 points of damage for every 10 feet he dove (to a maximum of 30d6 damage if he dove 150 feet or more). Even if each attack misses, the target still takes the extra damage for the distance of the dive, but it receives a Reflex save to halve the damage (DC = 10 + 1/2 the falconine wildsoul’s vigilante level + his Strength or Dexterity modifier, whichever is higher). A falconine vigilante can dive in this way a number of times per day equal to his Strength or Dexterity modifier, whichever is higher.


        Falconine wildsouls now get to make a full-attack action at the end of their dive, and the height cap is increased to 150 feet to accommodate the improved fly speed from aerial supremacy and haste. All these changes do is help make one round of ascension followed by one round of a dive more comparable to two rounds of full-attack actions. Depending on attack and damage bonuses, two rounds of full-attack actions may still come out ahead, so I don’t think this is too strong.
