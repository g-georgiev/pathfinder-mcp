(2) Soft Landing (Ex): Yeah…perfect fall is a pretty bad vigilante talent, and it doesn’t make any sense to me that a bird would need a wall within arm’s reach to fall like this. I’ve rewritten the ability below to be closer to the tengu’s glide racial trait.


(6) Eagle Eye (Ex): Competence bonuses are widely replicated (see, e.g., the eyes of the eagle wondrous item) and, moreover, eagle eye only applies to visual Perception checks. I prefer the version presented by the cleric Feather subdomain, which grants a racial bonus to all Perception checks. Again, rewritten below.


(12) Take to the Air (Ex): Finally, at 12th level, the bird takes to the skies. Why did it take this long to get a 40-foot fly speed when casters have been zooming around with a 60-foot fly speed for 7 levels?


(18) Deadly Dive (Ex): Credit where it’s due: deadly dive is a fun and interesting ability. Assuming you have the vertical space to use it (not a guarantee!) you’ll typically use a withdraw action to pull 80 or so feet up in the air, hitting that DC 20 Fly check to do your vertical move, then dive on your next turn for an additional 16d6 damage on your attack. Whether this is worth it is a separate question: you’re essentially sacrificing two rounds of full-attack actions for as much damage as your dive allows, and the math simply doesn’t work out in favor of that option, especially for full-BAB avengers, and especially especially because enemies get to take attacks of opportunity against you as you move and attack. Probably only useful when you’re up against high DR or an enemy has such high AC that you’re unlikely to hit with any iterative attacks.
