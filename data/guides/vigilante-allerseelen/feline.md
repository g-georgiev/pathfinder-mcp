# Feline

Feline


        (2) Feline Attributes (Ex): Low-light vision, scent, and Perception bonuses are all well and good, but probably the premiere ability here is the fulfillment of prerequisites for Aspect of the Beast. You’ll probably want to take the claws option—I don’t think the others are particularly worthwhile.


        (6) Feline Grace (Ex): I’ve rated the stalker sense vigilante talent a green, and that’s what I’ll do here. Feline wildsouls have huge incentives to be stalkers anyway, so you’re just taking a talent you probably would have taken anyway. Uncanny dodge and improved uncanny dodge are good class features to have.


        (12) Feline Pounce (Ex): This is why you’d choose to play a feline wildsoul, and more specifically why you’d choose to play one on a stalker chassis. Typically, rogues and rogue-like classes have a lot of trouble setting up damage: you can feint, Shatter Defenses, blind enemies with dirty tricks, Gang Up, have greater invisibility cast on you, etc., but ultimately your damage per round still depends on being within 5 feet of an enemy at the start of your turn so you can step and full-attack. Mad rush solves that. Sure, you take a big penalty to AC, but if you’ve got greater invisibility, haste, a weapon, and secondary claw attacks or morphic weaponry + Multiattack, then charge an enemy for a full-attack action, you might conceivably pull off five to seven, each dealing full hidden strike damage, before a creature has a chance to respond. Few will be able to withstand that kind of onslaught, especially if you open with it against a flat-footed or unaware enemy. Avenger feline wildsouls could simply purchase mad rush normally, so there’s no real incentive here.


        (18) Nine Lives (Su): If you’re being brought below 0 HP more than eight times per day by the time you take a character to 18th level, I frankly don’t know what to do for you. Git gud, I guess. Defensive roll is a neat rogue talent, but by its nature, it’s not going to work every time. Even with a +12 base Reflex save, a +5 cloak of resistance, +1 from haste, and a +7 Dexterity bonus, you could soak only 45 points of damage on a natural 20, and only 26 points on a natural 1. CR 18-20 enemies tend to hit on the harder end of that spectrum, for sure, and will often blow right past the outer limits of “Is this save even possible?”
