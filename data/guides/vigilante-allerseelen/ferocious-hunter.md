# Ferocious Hunter

Ferocious Hunter


The Basics


        Exclusively half-orc vigilantes who gain extra proficiency with racial orc weapons. Quite strong, actually, without surrendering much.


        Versatility: +0
        Power: +1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Hidden Heritage (Ex): Alters dual identity. There’s something fun about appearing to be two different ancestries when other people see you, and it heightens the sense of “Bruce Wayne was Batman the whole time!?” It’s a mild mechanical bonus, though.


        (2) Symbol of Pride: Replaces 2nd-level vigilante talent. Sure! Signature weapon is a pretty good vigilante talent, and Strength-based ferocious hunters—you’ll almost have to be Strength-based, by the way, since none of the weapons listed work with Weapon Finesse—can always benefit from more attack and damage bonuses. If you play a stalker, it’s fun to be able to poach an avenger vigilante talent.


        (3) Spirit of Ferocity (Ex): Replaces unshakable. Orc ferocity is always a risky gamble, since enemies tend to keep attacking you for as long as you’re standing…and if you’re still standing at -15 HP, you could easily die. It’s better than unshakable, though, so we’ll give it a green. Just play dead if you need to.


        (8) Symbol of Mastery: Replaces 8th-level vigilante talent. So, maybe-not-so-hot take: I think ferocious hunters have plenty of good reasons to be stalkers, rather than avengers. Let’s think: who loves symbol of pride most? Not avengers, who can already select it normally, but stalkers! Who loves symbol of mastery most? Not avengers, who already have full BAB for all purposes, but stalkers, who would normally have to wait quite a while for Improved Critical, Critical Focus, and all the BAB-gated critical feats. Plus, Sneaking Critical and Critical Precision give some fun tools to stalkers that wouldn’t normally apply to precision damage. Definitely choose the falchion if you mean to lean into crit fishing; that x2/15-20 crit range is monstrous. If you don’t want to take maximum advantage of crit fishing, the orc hornbow and greataxe are probably your best bets for ranged and melee combat, respectively.
        Oh! And one last synergy here that I love: Lead By Example, which is a half-orc only feat that grants every ally within 30 feet a +2 morale bonus to attack rolls for 1 round whenever you confirm a critical hit. Think of it like mass heroism every time you do what this archetype incentivizes you to do anyway.


Is It Worth It?


        For crit-fishing half-orc stalker vigilantes, sure, it earns its keep. Grab Critical Focus, Critical Precision, Sneaking Critical, Staggering Critical, and Bleeding Critical, and you’ll be pretty much set for life against any enemy that’s not outright immune to crits.
