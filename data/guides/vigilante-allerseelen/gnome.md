# Gnome

Gnome


        Gnomes do excellent work for spellcasting vigilantes, but they’re generally a B-tier race for other types of vigilantes. Halflings beat them easily in the Small size category, and any of the humans/half-humans available among the core races are better Medium-sized picks. They’re still good—you won’t shake your fist at the heavens and rue the day you chose to play a gnome—but you are leaving a bit of optimization on the table.


Pros
	Cons
	Pretty good stat distributions. You’d never build a Strength-based gnomish Pathfinder PC anyway, so gnomes do fairly well for themselves as infiltrators and Finesse fighters. Not having a bonus to your main attack stat isn’t ideal, though, especially given the huge bevy of races that grant bonuses to Dexterity and Charisma. If you’re playing a spellcasting vigilante, though, Constitution + Charisma is ideal.
	Lame favored class bonus. Maybe I’m just too smooth-brained to think about how this favored class bonus would benefit you, but I don’t see any bonuses here that couldn’t be replicated with the feign innocence and subjective truth social talents.
	Spellcasting boons. Gnomes can snag a number of racial increases to their spell save DCs, which can be huge for archetypes like the cabalist that focus on one school of magic. Most vigilantes aren’t casters, though.
	

	Small size. You’d probably want to build a gnome with the stalker specialization, in which case very little of your damage would be coming from a Medium-sized weapon damage die. Just enjoy your improved AC, attack bonuses, and Stealth bonuses!
	

	Low-light vision.
