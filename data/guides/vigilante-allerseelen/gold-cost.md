# Gold Cost

Gold Cost


Glamered (CL 10, 2,700 gp): As with glamered weaponry, glamered armor appears to be entirely mundane on casual (or even fairly motivated) inspection. Especially with the many guises social talent line, where you could conceivably be walking around as a common menial much of the time, you don’t want to forego the protection of your armor. Mandatory for any infiltration specialist.
Shadow / Slick (CL 5/4, 3,750 gp): Shadow and slick armor provide cheap (and, in the case of the improved and greater versions) numerically huge competence bonuses to Stealth and Escape Artist, two key skills for stalker vigilantes in particular. If you’re Dexterity-based, you need these, 100%.


Deceiving (CL 7, 5,000 gp): Yet another excellent infiltration enchantment. Want to be a chaotic evil character who always detects as lawful good? With deceiving armor, you can.


Hosteling (CL 9, 7,500 gp): Mounted furies can’t take their equine amigos everywhere; hosteling armor gives you free room and board while you squeeze through doorways and tight passages.
Malevolent (CL 7, 5,000 gp): Staggeringly effective for Shatter Defenses builds, which regularly make enemies flat-footed. Worth considering also for stalker vigilantes focusing on ambush tactics—those big attack bonuses are tasty.




+1 Bonus


Bolstering (CL 5): Damage an enemy, gain a +2 competence bonus to all saves against their abilities for a round. An easy way to keep your defenses in tip-top shape.
Defiant (CL 8): The armor equivalent of a bane weapon, and similarly good in campaigns that feature one enemy type to the exclusion of all others.
