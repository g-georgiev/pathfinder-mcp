# Gunmaster

Gunmaster


The Basics


        Vigilantes who draw inspiration from the gunslinger class. It’s as straightforward as it sounds, but loses most of what makes gunslingers, well, good.


        Versatility: -1
        Power: -1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Weapon and Armor Proficiencies: Alters weapon and armor proficiencies. Gummasters will be focused on Dexterity by default, so medium armor is no huge loss. And since the archetype is built around mastering, ya know, guns, I suppose it’s also no huge loss to give up martial weapons.


        (1) Gunmaster: Replaces vigilante specialization. Every gunslinger needs the ability to craft their own firearms and ammunition, since these items are so rare in Golarion canon. The second part of the ability is, unfortunately, quite a bit worse than what you would get from a 5-level dip into gunslinger. Firearms really don’t need any extra attack bonuses, since you’ll almost always be trying to hit touch AC; what you’re left with is a vastly inferior damage bonus than the one gunslingers get from gun training (gun training grants the full Dexterity bonus to damage immediately; gunmaster grants a +1 damage bonus every 4 levels). I don’t personally believe this is an upgrade from either stalker or avenger, but your mileage may vary.


        (2) Gunmaster Talents: Alters vigilante talents. The issue with gunmaster talents is that gunmasters don’t get a grit pool, and without a grit pool, all of your most important deeds (questionably deadeye, but certainly quick clear and lightning reload) are based off of maximum daily uses. That’s bad. Gunslingers get to use their deeds as often as they have grit, which they replenish throughout the day with kills, critical hits, etc. Gunmasters end up in the unenviable position of needing these to use deeds just as much as gunslingers do, but being far less able to use them than gunslingers are.


      * (2) Deadeye (Ex): Deadeye is always a good pick for gunslingers. Targeting touch AC is kinda the whole ballgame where firearms are concerned, making the deed particularly useful for picking off a target from long(er) range with a final iterative attack when you might have chosen to target regular AC for iteratives at higher BABs. The daily use cap sucks.
      * (4) Gunmaster Initiative (Ex): Gunmaster initiative is identical to gunslinger initiative, which is to say that it’s pretty good, allowing you a better chance of pulling off a full-attack action against enemies that ambush you at close range in the first round of combat. Since it doesn’t cost any grit, it has no daily use cap.
      * (20) Death’s Shot (Ex): A 20th-level save-or-die effect that only procs when you crit with a firearm? Nah. I mean, with a base x4 crit modifier, a firearm should still hurt like hell when you manage to confirm, and plenty of enemies at this level will be immune to death effects, Fortitude saves, critical hits, or all three.
      * (12) Lightning Reload (Ex): I always thought lightning reload was a bit of a bizarre deed for gunslingers. All firearm reload times go down in steps from full-round → standard → move → free, and both the Rapid Reload feat and alchemical cartridges reduce the loading time by one step. (Hint: there’s a reason they’re considered mandatory investments for gunslingers.) So for any one-handed firearm, you’d have been full-attacking with free actions by, what, 5th level at the very latest? That leaves only two uses for lightning reload: first, to avoid attacks of opportunity when reloading—issue is, enemies within threatened range will still ping you for ranged attacks, regardless of reloads. Second, to allow two-handed firearm users to reload as a free action. But oh look, where gunslingers can do this all day (as long as they have grit in their pool) gunmasters can do it only enough for one, maybe two combats per day. Horrid.
      * (2) Quick Clear (Ex): Once per day? Once per day? Look how they massacred my boy! Quick clear is among the most important deeds for gunslingers to have, because all it takes is an unlucky roll while your firearm is misfired for something to explode. This deed was supposed to be the fast, cheap answer to that problem. Instead, you can’t even clear as a standard action consistently, and you can’t clear as a move action at all. Ugh.


        (4) Nimble (Ex): Replaces unshakable. Sure, a few points of free AC. It’s not numerically dramatic, but it’s sure as shootin’ better than unshakable.


Is It Worth It?


        Gunmaster doesn’t force you to pick up gunmaster talents, so really all the archetype changes is your vigilante specialization and weapon/armor proficiencies. You’d be hard-pressed to make firearms work with just those core abilities, though, and when you do move into gunmaster talents, everywhere you turn you’ll be confronted by the fact that the gunslinger class stands head and shoulders above this archetype. Since the vigilante doesn’t have any Wisdom-based archetypes and the gunslinger doesn’t have any Charisma-based archetypes (apart from mysterious stranger, which is terrible as a dip since you don’t get gun training until 9th level) I can’t think of a great way to fuse the two classes apart from a traditional 5-level gunslinger dip that mostly ignores Charisma at point buy to prioritize a larger grit pool. That’s a clunky solution, though, and frankly I think you’re better off letting the two parent classes stay separate. They both still love you very much, and you’ll see each of them on alternating weekends.
