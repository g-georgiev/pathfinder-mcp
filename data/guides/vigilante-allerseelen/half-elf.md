# Half-Elf

Half-Elf


        Half-elves don’t distinguish themselves from humans as much as they do in some other classes, thanks to a favored class bonus that is the same between both races. They’re still strong choices for vigilantes, with adaptability, a floating stat bonus, and access to two separate favored class bonuses.


Pros
	Cons
	Floating stat bonus. Floating stat bonuses are the name of the game for half-elves, half-orcs, and humans, and they’re flexible enough to let you build any kind of vigilante you want. 95% of the time, you’ll go with Strength or Dexterity.
	None. Half-elves are almost always a top-tier race. The vigilante is no exception to this rule.
	Adaptability. Adaptability (and the racial traits that replace it) is a fantastic racial feature. Skill Focus is a flexible feat that can help with Stealth, Bluff, Intimidate, Disguise, or many of the ancillary roles that vigilantes often occupy. Moreover, ancestral arms, weapon familiarity, and dual-minded are all excellent replacements for adaptability, if you’re interested in replacing it.
	

	Decent favored class bonuses. Half-elf vigilantes don’t get the usual trifecta of favored class bonuses that they sometimes do in other classes. The elf favored class bonus is pretty bad unless you plan to spend your entire career in a forest, and the human and half-elf favored class bonuses are the same time—a bonus to seamless guise. I like that favored class bonus, as I’ve discussed previously, but it’s not as good as the halfling’s favored class bonus.
	

	Multitalented. Multiclassing is fairly common among vigilantes, especially to swashbuckler, scaled fist unchained monk, etc., and while you’re probably not going to spend huge amounts of time in those secondary classes, it helps to be scaling your favored class bonuses while you do so.
	

	Low-light vision, but no darkvision.
