# Hangman

Hangman


The Basics


        Vigilantes who lasso enemies with nooses in order to grapple them. The archetype is a not-so-subtle homage to Wonder Woman and her lasso of truth, and synergizes nicely with whip builds.


        Versatility: -1
        Power: +1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Weapon and Armor Proficiencies: Alters weapon and armor proficiencies. Nets and whips, but no shields. Got it.


        (1) Vigilante Specialization: Alters vigilante specialization. This is a little unfortunate, as ¾-BAB classes will always have a much harder time of making combat maneuvers (lookin’ at you, grapple) stick than full-BAB classes will. You’ll get to do some tasty hidden strike damage if you manage to maintain your grapples, though, so we’ll look for other sources of bonuses.


        (2) Hangman’s Noose (Ex): Replaces 2nd-level vigilante talent. There are a lot of goodies baked into hangman’s noose, not all of them obvious. First, of course, you effectively gain Improved Grapple for free without needing Combat Expertise. Amazing for a feat-starved class! Second, a length of rope can essentially count as three things when wielded by a hangman: a rope, a net, or a whip. Any of these three types of items are therefore interchangeable as main weapons. Some of my favorite rope items are ropes of climbing, gum roap, iron rope, and bloodvine rope. (Iron rope is particularly funny if you hogtie someone, then issue the command word to turn the rope into iron. How are they getting out? I have no idea.) You can also weave nets and whips into nooses, but good unique nets and whips are harder to come by; I honestly can’t recommend the majority. Worthwhile enchantments for grapple-heavy net or whip builds are likewise slim pickings. Still, having some extra variety here in terms of which items you can reasonably loot from enemies and use is lovely. Third, counting a rope as a net and whip allows you to tick multiple boxes of the Equipment Trick feat all at once! Grappling Net is particularly useful for hangmen, grants a large +4 bonus on grapple checks, and is functional right out of the package. Hogtie and Slip Away are probably the standouts from Equipment Trick (Rope). Anyway, a fun vigilante talent that’s undoubtedly highly thematic.


        (3) Bound to Truth (Ex, Sp): Replaces unshakable. Ahhh, here’s where the Wonder Woman parallels start arising. I quite like this class feature—it’s relatively easy to take creatures alive when you’re grappling them, knocking them unconscious with Chokehold, etc., and you may well want to interrogate them later.


        (4) Twisted Rope (Su): Replaces 4th-level vigilante talent. Twisted rope feels more like a talent tax to prevent enemies from simply sundering your main grappling tool. The break DC scales quite well (DC 33 at 20th level compares favorably with the average CR 20 enemy’s Strength modifier of +12) but the noose’s hardness and HP will still leave it vulnerable to concerted sunder attempts.


        (5) Chokehold: Replaces startling appearance. Chokehold imparts a large enough penalty to your grapple check that you wouldn’t want to use it on anything but the softest targets. Spellcasters tend to be pretty soft targets, though, and they definitely care if you deny them access to their verbal components. Bushwhack is a necessary complement to this playstyle so that you can lasso an enemy from stealth, then immediately move to pin them (denying somatic and material components) or choke them (denying verbal components).


        (11) Tighten the Noose (Ex): Replaces frightening appearance. Tighten the noose is one of the better class features that the hangman has. An endgame action flow for a hangman might look something like the following: 


      * Surprise Round
      * Standard action: initiate grapple (the ambuscading grapple rogue talent is great here for a little extra oomph at the start of combat)
      * Free action: activate Bushwhack to move into a pin or Chokehold
      * Round 1
      * Standard action: maintain pin
      * Move action: deal damage with grapple and Greater Grapple, dealing reduced hidden strike damage because enemy is denied Dexterity to AC when pinned
      * Swift action: deal increased hidden strike damage through tighten the noose).


        You can see that there’s not a ton of room for damage in that lineup, although with an enemy pinned and choking, there’s also a good argument to be made that your contribution to the team is not damage but control of a single dangerous enemy while the rest of your allies handle business elsewhere. It’s lovely to get to deal your full hidden strike damage at least once per round.


        (17) Suffocation (Ex): Replaces stunning appearance. I like suffocation, but I’m not going to pretend that it’s much more efficient than simply dealing hidden strike damage every round. You have to establish a grapple, then spend three rounds choking an enemy to death—and that’s assuming that they fail every Fortitude save, which they might well not if you’re invested in Dexterity rather than Strength. Besides, constructs and undead don’t breathe, so they’re outright immune to the ability. It’s fun, but it has pretty obvious flaws.


Is It Worth It?


        Potentially? It’s certainly an interesting way to play a whip build. Stalkers (the baseline vigilante specialization that all hangmen have to take) will deal more damage once they have an enemy grappled, but their CMB scores are always going to be somewhat anemic compared with their full-BAB avenger brethren. Your mileage for a grappling whip build may vary.
