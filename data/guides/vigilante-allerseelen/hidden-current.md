# Hidden Current

Hidden Current


The Basics


        Amphibious vigilantes who need to transition often between terrestrial and aquatic terrains. The only Paizo AP to which this archetype is even somewhat suited is Ruins of Azlant, so keep that in mind.


        Versatility: -1
        Power: -1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Guise of the Land Walker (Ex): Replaces 1st-level social talent. Don’t get me wrong, it’s awesome to pick up many guises several levels before you’d normally be able to, but restricting you to a non-aquatic humanoid is…cumbersome, to say the least. I could only see this working in a game like Ruins of Azlant, and even then not all of the time.


        (2) Stealthy Swimmer (Ex): Replaces 2nd-level vigilante talent. You know you’re looking at a severely undertuned class feature when even in the type of campaign it’s built for, it’s still weak. Such is the unfortunate case of stealthy swimmer. Bad even in aquatic campaigns, especially compared with the usual power level of vigilante talents.


        (11) Sea’s Return (Sp): Replaces frightening appearance. I’ll never say no to free dimension door, but the uses per day and land-sea targeting limitations don’t make it quite as versatile as the spell. Still pretty good!


Is It Worth It?


        Not on the whole, no. Even in campaigns centered around aquatic environments, sea’s return (which you don’t get until 11th level!) is the only class feature that earns its keep in comparison with the base vigilante. Plus, taking this archetype locks you out of other, better ones. Let it go.
