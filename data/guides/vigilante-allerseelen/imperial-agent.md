# Imperial Agent

Imperial Agent


The Basics


        Intrigue-heavy vigilantes who focus on extortion, blackmail, and false flag operations. Although it may not wow you at first blush, there are actually a huge number of interesting things you can do with it between manipulative and false flag—definitely an archetype that’s best in the hands of a devious, conniving player.


        Versatility: +2
        Power: +0


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Slander (Ex): Replaces 1st-level social talent. While I don’t think that slander is more effective than other common 1st-level social talents like renown, social grace, or owl’s sight, it does open up some interesting possibilities for urban campaigns, where you might be able to spend some downtime subtly turning the populace against a public figure. The DC is fairly beatable, too: with skill familiarity in Bluff, 10 ranks in Bluff, a +5 untyped bonus from manipulative, a +3 class skill bonus, and a positive Charisma modifier, you can easily beat the DC 20 + opposed Charisma modifier check for a public figure to which the populace is indifferent. Add even a little bit of investment (say, Skill Focus: Bluff or a wondrous item like the ring of eloquence or circlet of persuasion) and you can hit the DC 30 + Charisma modifier check for targets about whom the populace feels helpful. It’s worth investigating.


        (3) Manipulative (Ex): Replaces unshakable. Ahhh, who cares that manipulative doesn’t affect demoralization or feinting? The Intimidate and Bluff bonuses are great, particularly as replacements for unshakable. I’m fine with this.


        (5) False Flag (Ex): Replaces 5th-level social talent. Good grief, the possibilities! Disguise yourself as a member of law enforcement and extort/threaten the common folk or criminals, backed up by your bonuses from manipulative. Disguise yourself as a criminal and commit a particularly flagrant crime to bring the fuzz down on the real criminal organization. Disguise yourself as an ally from a powerful organization, assure another organization of “your” support when they commit some especially audacious act, then turn them in preemptively. Disguise yourself as a member of a noble house of a particular nation to lend credibility to a diplomatic endeavor. There are so many opportunities to use this ability to make a plan go right, and even more opportunities to use this ability to make a plan go dramatically (and deliberately!) wrong. I love it. Heck, it even acts as many guises, which I purchase at 5th level on nearly every vigilante I create anyway!


Is It Worth It?


        I think so! It’s the rare archetype that really improves your versatility without also making you much more powerful, but I think imperial agent manages it nicely. False flag will give you a vast array of ways to employ cunning rather than force in your adventuring, and manipulative gives you a bit more social power to back it up. Plus, imperial agent doesn’t give up many class features, allowing you to stack it with plenty of other archetypes, if that’s your jam.
