# Introductions

Introductions


Ho, Pathfinders!


        When Paizo introduced the vigilante class in Ultimate Intrigue, a lot of players thought they were dreaming: here was a class that could seemingly be turned into anything: if you wanted a rogue, it could out-rogue the rogue with pools of precision damage dice, evasion, infiltration abilities, and all the skills you could shake a stick at; if you wanted a fighter, it could out-fighter the fighter with full BAB, better saves, more out-of-combat utility, and more potential bonus feats even than that superlatively feat-laden class. The vigilante eschewed dogmatic progressions of class features—some of which are stinkers in every class, let’s be honest—in favor of a la carte packages of feats or abilities, including many that conferred mechanical benefits not found in any other ability, feat, or item in the game. It seemed that whatever kind of build you wanted to play, the vigilante offered an option for it that gave you the essential abilities for playing it, all while giving players the opportunity to roleplay as their favorite defenders of justice. The class experienced such a vogue that a joke arose among the community: “How many builds does it take to create a competent Pathfinder PC?” “Just one, but there’s a vigilante archetype that does it better.” The best jokes have an element of truth, it would seem.
        As a person who’s always gravitated toward skillful classes that can accomplish more with guile and finesse than magical prowess or brute strength, the vigilante is an absolute playground of a class—one that I’ve enjoyed exploring time and again in one-shots, Pathfinder Society games, and other campaigns. So regardless of where your vigilante is going to fall on the alignment spectrum, let’s strap on those dark masks, pick a symbol that will strike fear in the hearts of evil-doers (or goody two-shoes—I don’t judge) everywhere, and take a deep dive into the dark side of heroism. Along the way, we’ll learn how to conceal our identities for fun and profit, infiltrate enemy organizations like it’s going out of style, and hopefully drag some baddies into the shadows, never to be seen again. Take your vengeance. Be your vengeance. There’s no better fit for it in Pathfinder.
