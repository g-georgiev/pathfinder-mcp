# Masked Maiden

Masked Maiden


The Basics


        Traumatized former Gray Maidens whose suits of armor are as much psychological prisons as they are physical protections. The archetype comes with some pretty stiff penalties (no morale bonuses or benefits of bardic performances while in the vigilante identity, e.g.) so make sure you know what you’re doing here.


        Versatility: -2
        Power: +1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Weapon and Armor Proficiency: Alters weapon and armor proficiencies. Gray Maiden plate armor for free? Sure! Saves you a vigilante talent spent on heavy training.


        (1) Imperfect Control (Ex): Alters dual identity. Imperfect control + scars of the past kind of work together to create a strange, strange vigilante experience. Imperfect control forces involuntary changes (pending failed Will saves) between your social and vigilante identities, so you won’t be able to go the Iron Man route and simply conduct your business fully in your social identity. On the other hand, scars of the past eliminates seamless guise, which is the core mechanical barrier separating your social and vigilante identities. So you have all the drawbacks of people knowing that you’re a former Gray Maiden who still can’t shake the compulsions while also having all the drawbacks of having to switch between identities, often involuntarily and often at the worst possible moment. It’s rough, I’ll be honest. Probably your best bet is to remain as a vigilante for the entire day; you’ve lost a majority of your social talents, so it’s not like you’ll be missing much power there. Staying as a vigilante ensures that you don’t get forced into that identity against your will.


        (1) Scars of the Past (Ex): Replaces seamless guise. As above. Losing seamless guise means that people will associate your social and vigilante identities.


        (3) Armor Training (Ex): Replaces 3rd-, 7th-, 11th-, 15th-, and 19th-level social talents. If we were just trading out social talents for armor training, I’d say no way, but fully scaling armor training opens the way for advanced armor training, and some of these options are amazing. Armor specialization is an easy pick, since you’ll only ever wear Gray Maiden plate; armored juggernaut is a simple source of DR/—; adaptable training and critical deflection have their places, but probably not as your first picks. At any rate, this is a straight power for versatility trade.


Is It Worth It?


        Honestly, the masked maiden doesn’t really play all that much like a vigilante. You’ve lost most of your social talents and seamless guise, and dual identity has been replaced by the strictly poorer imperfect control—so what’s left? You’re an avenger, so you still have full BAB, and you still have all your vigilante talents. It might honestly be best to think of this one as a fighter archetype that grants a few nifty tricks from vigilante. I’d recommend you just stay in your vigilante identity all day; you won’t have the Disguise bonuses to navigate the switch between identities well, and a single failed Will save will lock you into that identity all day anyway.
