# Melee (Improvised)

Melee (Improvised)


Catch Off-Guard: Improvised weapon builds need the ability to use improvised weapons without penalties and Catch Off-Guard is the main feat you’ll use to achieve that goal.
Shikigami Style / Mimicry / Manipulation: Shikigami Style was introduced relatively late in 1e’s run, and it really changes the game for improvised weapon builds. The ultimate goal is to wield a CL 20 wondrous item in order to benefit both from three effective size increases (1d6 → 1d10 → 2d8 → 3d8 for two-handed weapons, or slightly less for one-handed weapons) and from a +5 enhancement bonus. Sovereign glue is the classic example, as a CL 20 wondrous item that only costs 2400 gp, but you can also use a traveler’s any-tool to get a +2 sledge weapon as soon as 5th level. It’s a ridiculous and cheesy feat line, but c’mon, who wouldn’t want to wield a bottle of glue with as much lethality as a holy avenger? Only idiots, my friends. Only idiots. Of note, Shikigami Style builds make some of the best Vital Strike builds in the game, because you multiply your already-boosted base damage dice when calculating Vital Strike damage.


Chairbreaker: Shikigami Style is a much more potent avenue for vigilantes to pursue, and doesn’t require you to destroy weapons in order to deal good damage.
