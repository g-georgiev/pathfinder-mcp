# Melee (Strength)

Melee (Strength)


      * Paladin 2 or Paladin 3 / Antipaladin 2 or Antipaladin 3: Paladin and antipaladin have quite a lot to offer the aspiring vigilante: heavy armor proficiency, full BAB, a good Fortitude save progression, and a 1/day smite evil or good. Divine grace or unholy resilience are the true keys to the kingdom, adding your Charisma modifier to all saves. Vigilantes, especially Strength-based vigilantes, tend not to have the greatest saves: Wisdom usually can’t be boosted beyond 10 or so at point buy, Dexterity remains low due to heavy armor, and it’s difficult to pump Constitution hard enough to compensate for a weak Fortitude save progression. Two levels of paladin will get you +3 Fortitude and Will from the dip alone, plus an additional 6 or 7 points to every save over the course of your career. Antipaladin 3 also gets a shout-out for aura of cowardice, which makes enemies much more susceptible to effects like twisting fear and frightening appearance. Do note that alignment restrictions don’t often play nicely with vigilantes, though: you’ve got to stay lawful good or chaotic evil in both identities, social and vigilante, to retain your dipped abilities.
      * Fighter 1: A one-level fighter dip snags you a bonus feat, a +2 Fortitude bonus, and heavy armor proficiency. There are worse dips, and there are better dips.
      * Iroran Paladin 2 / Scaled Fist UnMonk 1 or 2: Unarmed vigilante builds are quite common, and there are few dips for Charisma-based unarmed builds as worthwhile as scaled fist unchained monk 1. A single level gets you +1 BAB, +2 Fortitude and Reflex, flurry of blows, Improved Unarmed Strike, Stunning Fist, a bonus feat that should probably be Dragon Style, and, crucially, Charisma to AC. A second level gets you another bonus feat and evasion. Iroran paladin, while not an exceptional dip, can fill some of the same roles as scaled fist unmonk while also giving you divine grace.


Melee (Dexterity)


      * None. Unlike other classes, which might consider a swashbuckler or unchained rogue dip to kickstart a career in Dexterity-based melee combat, the teisatsu vigilante already kinda out-rogues the rogue, if you take my meaning. The lethal grace vigilante talent is all you should need to make a Dexterity fighter work in this class.


Ranged (Dexterity)


      * Musketeer Swashbuckler 1: “Call an ambulance…but not for me.” Yes, a vigilante with a gun is about as iconic as it can get, but beware, Punisher, because Paizo built basically zero support for ranged vigilantes into the system. You’re on your own with this one.
      * Divine Hunter Paladin 2 / Arrow Champion Swashbuckler 1 / Bolt Ace Gunslinger 1 or 5: Probably some of the better options for bow-wielding vigilantes.
