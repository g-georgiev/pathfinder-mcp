# Melee (Unarmed)

Melee (Unarmed)


Cerberus Style / Snare / Crush: Cerberus Style is a strong unarmed strike style in general, and doubly so for the vigilante class, which gets so many bonuses on unarmed strike builds. Avengers can easily get everything they need for a Cerberus Style build with the fist of the avenger, vital punishment, and sweeping strike vigilante talents. This will eventually allow them to hit three opponents per round with Greater Vital Strike, preventing each from taking 5-foot steps or using teleportation magic.
Dragon Style / Roar / Ferocity: Dragon Style is a classic fighting style for unarmed builds, packaging great bonuses to damage with some other nifty buffs. This style feat line will probably be most applicable to vigilantes who are taking a scaled fist unchained monk dip.
Possessed Hand: A +1 insight bonus to attack and damage rolls, plus a few skill rolls. Pretty great.
Snake Style / Sidewind / Fang: Assuming you’re willing to invest in Sense Motive, Snake Style can be an amazing style feat line, giving you the chance to take lots of attacks of opportunity and negate enemy hits with little effort. Pairs amazingly well with the animal patron (snake) vigilante talent to get huge bonuses to hit with your attacks of opportunity, as well as the skill familiarity social talent to ensure that you can take 10 on Sense Motive checks in combat.


Boar Style / Ferocity / Shred: Boar Style is a nice unarmed strike style feat line that does a lot of what vigilantes want to do: deal some bleed damage, demoralize as a free action, get around DR/damage type, and more.
Chaos Reigns: An extra slam attack for the price of one feat! Not bad at all.
Crane Style / Wing / Riposte: Defensive fighting builds are somewhat niche, but a halfling avenger vigilante with Crane Style, Crane Wing, Crane Riposte, Osyluth Guile, Cautious Fighter, Blundering Defense, and Uncanny Defense can get ridiculous bonuses to AC, CMD, Reflex saves, and more while taking only a minor penalty to attack rolls.
Outslug Style / Weave / Sprint: A very solid style feat line for unarmed or gauntlet builds, Outslug Style gives you offense, defense, reach, and mobility, all packaged into one.


Adder Strike: Unarmed strike builds are eminently doable for many vigilantes, and with the poisoner vigilante talent, you can both synthesize your own poison and qualify for Adder Strike. It’s great for applying poisons to enemies without sucking up too many of your actions.
Belier’s Bite: A little extra bleed damage never hurts, but you might do better with the Boar Style feat line.
Shielded Gauntlet Style / Attack / Master: Gauntlet builds are surprisingly good for avenger vigilantes, between steel soldier, take ‘em alive, and fist of the avenger, and the Shielded Gauntlet Style feat line—especially the latter two feats—can really, really pump up your damage and AC. Shielded Gauntlet Attack, for example, can take your gauntlet damage from 1d3 at 20th level to 2d6 at 20th level. Definitely no slouch!


Improved Unarmed Strike: Nearly no one should take Improved Unarmed Strike with one of their normal feats. Either get it through the fist of the avenger vigilante talent, or grab a monk/brawler dip to get it. (That said, IUS is obviously essential to unarmed strike builds, and can’t be ignored at all.)
