# Mounted Fury

Mounted Fury


The Basics


        As the name implies, mounted furies are vigilantes who ride animals into battle, gaining all the usual bonuses that mounted builds tend to have when charging.


        Versatility: -1
        Power: +1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Class Skills: Since your mount is treated as a druid’s animal companion, you’ll want Handle Animal in order to teach it new tricks, push it to stick it out in the face of unnatural threats, etc. Just a refresher on the Handle Animal rules.


        (1) Thorough Change: Alters dual identity. Thorough change isn’t necessarily good or bad—it just means that you’ll have to go with the Iron Man approach as a mounted fury. People will know that you work in a stable and that you’re the Lone Ranger, and that you don’t ever apologize or explain yourself for beating up bad guys. The alternative is staying in your vigilante identity more or less permanently, which locks you out of a lot of great social talents (e.g., social grace).


        (1) Mount (Ex): Replaces vigilante specialization. Mount is an amalgam of many different class features. You’ve got a druid-scaling animal companion; you take no armor check penalty on Ride checks, like the cavalier; you share teamwork feats (kind of) like a hunter; and your mount gets to use startling appearance, like…you, I guess? No one else gets to do that. Plus, you get to select avenger talents even though you’re not an avenger, and that ain’t bad, neither!
        Mechanically, it’s hard for me to know what to make of the teamwork feats that you can take in place of vigilante talents. Most teamwork feats exploit either adjacency or flanking, and therein lies the problem: you won’t want to use the mount as its own autonomous, flanking combatant, simply because so much of the archetype’s power—both defensive and offensive—comes from being mounted and using Spirited Charge, Wheeling Charge, mad rush, furious charge, and mighty charge to deal damage with a lance. And yet, if you stay on your mount—in defiance of God and men—you only count as a single entity for the purposes of teamwork feats. What, then, is the point of having teamwork feats when you still need to convince your human allies to purchase them in order to use them? I could see some amazing gestalt build ideas here with inquisitor, hunter, etc., but it won’t be very effective with a single-class vigilante.
        A final word about vigilante talents: if they give you static damage bonuses, You Want That.™ A lance gives you double damage (including static bonuses) on a mounted charge; the Spirited Charge feat gives you triple damage. Under these circumstances, vigilante talents like take ‘em alive and even signature weapon become much, much more valuable. Sure, Weapon Specialization is only a +2 to damage, but triple that, and you’re looking at a different picture. Of note, the mad rush avenger talent that unlocks at 12th level (14th is the earliest you can take it) doesn’t triple every lance attack with Spirited Charge, only the first. Legitimately disappointing, but there you have it. At any rate, any mounted fury build will be very dependent on charging, so consider anything that will let you charge over difficult terrain (Nimble Moves, Acrobatic Steps, air walk on your mount, etc.), around corners or other obstacles (Wheeling Charge), and through allies (also Wheeling Charge).


        (6) Furious Charge (Ex): Replaces 6th-level vigilante talent. A always, B be, C charging. Always be charging. Always be charging. Furious charge is already quite good for these purposes; when you pair it with mad rush, however, it gets way, way better. What’s mad rush’s primary weakness? The big AC penalty you take. And what does furious charge eliminate? The AC penalty from charging—any AC penalty, including mad rush’s. And what’s mad rush’s primary strength? Pasting a full-attack action onto the end of a charge. What does furious charge improve? The attack bonus you get while charging—an attack bonus that can be applied to any attack made after charging, including three lance attacks and four natural attacks from morphic weaponry. So, yeah. She good, fam.


        (12) Mighty Charge (Ex): Replaces 12th-level vigilante talent. I really, really wish Paizo could have moved mighty charge to either 10th or 14th level, because as it stands, it’s conflicting with mad rush at 12th level. And you know what? That really chaps my chops. If you can get past the staggering opportunity cost of the talent, it’s not bad: you get Improved Critical more or less for free, and I’ve already covered how devastating a Spirited Charge lance crit can be. The combat maneuver part isn’t likely to work with a ¾-BAB class that hasn’t specialized in it, especially not when both enemies and CMD scores are getting larger and larger. Since it doesn’t provoke, though, there’s not much risk to trying, so long as you don’t select a combat maneuver that you could fail by 10 or more (trip or disarm) and incur a boo-boo.


        (20) Vengeance Strike (Ex): Alters vengeance strike. Okay? I mean, you’ll want to be mounted most of the time anyway. Just stay mounted.


Is It Worth It?


        Assuming that you’re fine toting around a horse with hosteling armor and having far fewer social talent uses than most vigilantes, the mounted fury can eventually deal competitive damage with Spirited Charge and mad rush. More than a base avenger? That’s debatable. It’s entirely possible to play a base avenger vigilante, pick up Nature Soul + Animal Ally + Boon Companion, put a horsemaster’s saddle on it to share teamwork feats, and play as a mounted vigilante without needing to touch this archetype at all.
