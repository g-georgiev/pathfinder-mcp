# Mutated Defender

Mutated Defender


The Basics


        Obviously a nod to the X-Men, mutated defenders gain mutant powers in their vigilante identities that they can hide while in their social identities.

        Power: -1

The Abilities


        (1) Mutant Specialization: Replaces vigilante specialization. Full BAB is good, although I’m less enthused about the lack of stalker or vigilante talents on tap. As for mutant deformities, oh boy, it’s time for some good, old-fashioned Pathfinder cheese! Wherever there’s a loophole, we’re gonna find it. Deformities are rated by how easy they are to bypass, as well as how detrimental they are before they’re bypassed.


      * Lame: Super easy! Barely an inconvenience! You love to see it. Reducing your base speed by 10 feet isn’t much of a hit at all, especially if you’re playing a ranged vigilante. Boots of striding and springing (5,500 gp) will solve your problem permanently, or else you can use a wand of longstrider if you’re even more strapped for cash. A couple thousand gold to solve your deformity is way better than the five or six feats it would take to solve blindness.
      * Light Blindness: Another deformity that’s super easy to solve with the 2nd-level spell protective penumbra. Have your resident prepared caster prep it for the day, use a lesser extend metamagic rod, and you’re off to the races. Heck, your GM might even let you have the cantrip version, penumbra, which doesn’t cost any spell resources at all.
      * Misshapen: I’m knocking this one down a rung only because it would require a dip into scaled fist unchained monk to easily bypass it. Still, scaled fist unchained monk is a great dip for vigilantes anyway: pump Dexterity and Charisma, grab the lethal grace vigilante talent and a wand of mage armor, and ignore manufactured armor entirely. Your GM might also consider something like flowing fortress armor, which is literally just water turned into armor. How could that not reasonably fit any body type?
      * Deaf: While not quite as crippling as blindness, deafness is still pretty bad. You don’t have any verbal spellcasting components to worry about—not an issue—but communication with teammates, social interaction, and auditory Perception checks will all become impossible. Still too challenging.
      * Poor Ability: Intelligence would probably be your best choice—or Strength, perhaps—but even then, penalties to your ability scores are gnarly. I’d pass.
      * Useless Arm: I don’t think this is the roughest deformity in the world, but it’s a far cry from easy to work around. You’d have to play a one-handed Dexterity build for optimal mechanics, and even then, you wouldn’t be able to draw and drink potions, use wands, etc.
      * Blind: Blindness is really, really hard to cope with in Pathfinder. Oh, sure, you could worship Vildeis, take the Blind Zeal trait, dip master of many styles monk to pick up Blinded Blade Style and Improved/Greater Blind Fight, and eventually arrive at 30-foot blindsight, but is it really worth five feats and a trait to overcome? Cool concept—it just requires more squeeze than I’m willing to give it for the juice.
      * Fragile: Vigilantes have poor Fortitude saves, so you might end up staggered a surprisingly high percentage of the time. Let’s not make failed saves worse than they already are, mkay?
      * Fractured Mind: As fragile, but with even worse consequences.
      * Spasms: As fragile and fractured mind. Failed saves are bad enough.


        (2) Mutant Talents: Alters vigilante talents. Given that you entered here, abandoned all hope of vigilante or stalker talents, and took a mutant deformity to boot, we’d better hope it was worth it, huh? Well, I hate to spoil the ending with that color rating, but let’s take a look at the individual talents:


      * (2) Mutant Evolution: Eidolon evolutions are typically great when applied to PCs, but the issue here is that many of the best evolutions are denied to you—whether explicitly by the ability text or implicitly because you don’t meet the eidolon’s form requirements (lookin’ at you, pounce). You can turn yourself into a Big Ball O’ Jaws ‘N’ Claws, as is the time-honored summoner tradition, but honestly, vigilantes can already do that with significantly less investment via morphic weaponry. Reach and resistance are the only non-natural attack evolutions I’d want to take out of those available to you, and that doesn’t make a great argument for the archetype.
      * (9) Mutant Blast (Sp): Man, this is…as bad as they come. If you got a limited version of kineticist’s simple blast or some variant of the warlock vigilante’s mystic bolts, I might consider it, but the maximum scaling at 20th level for elemental ray is 1d6+10. Does that seem worthwhile to you, especially when resistances and immunities are a thing and you get so few uses per day? It sure doesn’t to me.
      * (4) Mutated Lobe (Sp): I don’t like to say no, so I’ll just say…nah, bro. Detect thoughts isn’t great anyway, as every enemy will eventually be able to crush the Will save DC for a 2nd-level spell. Any serious enemy will have abjuration magic in place to prevent this kind of mental intrusion, too. So what’s the point?


Is It Worth It?


        No. Some mutant deformities are easily worked around, but why would you bother when the mutant talents being offered range from mediocre to downright bad?
