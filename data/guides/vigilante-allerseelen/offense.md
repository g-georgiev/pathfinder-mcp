# Offense

Offense


(2) Twisting Fear (Ex): Haaahahaha, Paizo really outdid themselves on this one. Twisting fear turns any fear effect you have (Cornugon Smash, Enforcer, Dazzling Display, etc.) into a sustainable nonlethal damage source. For stalkers, Cornugon Smash might not be the preferred form factor, but Enforcer and Dazzling Display are aces for single-target and AoE demoralization, respectively. If you’re going for AoE fear, you’ll probably want Disheartening Display so that you can deal the damage in two successive rounds, and any twisting fear build will want Signature Skill (Intimidate) so that you can crush Intimidate DCs and start enemies at frightened or even panicked. Endless fun!


(4) Up Close and Personal (Ex): Let’s get one thing clear off the bat: unless you’ve heavily invested in Acrobatics, stalker vigilantes are not going to want to use this talent on huge targets with monstrous CMD scores. Where up close and personal really finds its stride is in tackling pint-sized enemies, spellcasters, and other squishy targets, none of whom will be able to effectively prevent you from shanking them as you travel through their square. Especially if an enemy is not currently flanked or denied their Dexterity bonus to AC, using this talent will often beat the damage from a full-attack action based on hidden strike’s contribution alone. Pairs exceptionally well with the mighty ambush hidden strike talent.


(6) Sniper (Ex): Required for ranged stalker builds. If you’re using thrown weapons with a small range increment, I’d skip sniper—you shouldn’t be attacking from more than two range increments away with shuriken, for example.




Defense


(2) Evasive (Ex): Evasion and improved evasion, as the rogue class feature. Easy purchase, between your strong Reflex save progression and the high likelihood that stalker vigilantes will choose Dexterity as their main combat attribute.


(2) Stalker Sense (Ex): Uncanny dodge and improved uncanny dodge are a little less potent than evasion and improved evasion, but still perhaps worth your investment if you like to live in melee range.
