# Psychometrist

Psychometrist


The Basics


        Psychometrists draw heavy mechanical and thematic inspiration from the occultist class, gaining focus powers and implements.


        Versatility: +1
        Power: +1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Class Skills: Alters class skills. Knowledge (Arcana) is probably better in most games than Knowledge (Dungeoneering), and History and Engineering are about equally pointless. Still, mostly just an even-stevens swap.


        (1) Occult Awareness: Replaces 1st-level social talent. Occult skill unlocks are fun, but I wouldn’t say that they’re better than permanent low-light vision (owl sight) or rerolls on failed Will saves (companion to the lonely), both of which are available instead at 1st level. Meh.


        (2) Implements and Focus Powers: Replaces 2nd-, 6th-, 12th-, and 18th-level vigilante talents. Gosh, if only there were someone who knew about occultists…wait, I do! Occultists are one of my favorite classes (I love all the occult classes, in fact, and have written guides on three of them already) so it bears examining how the psychometrist differs from that class:


      * First—and worst—the psychometrist doesn’t gain any spells or resonant powers from their implements. This is a big, bad deal. I wasn’t necessarily expecting spells (the occultist’s spellcasting is pretty limited anyway) but a huge chunk of the occultist’s power comes from being able to beef up their attributes with a transmutation implement, their saves with an abjuration implement, their animate dead HD caps with a necromancy implement, etc.—and that’s without delving into the greatness of panoplies. Take away the amazing passive buffs, and focus powers on their lonesome suddenly don’t look so appealing.
      * Second, your mental focus assignment is far more finicky than the base occultist. Base occultists assign one pool of mental focus to an implement school, and are then free to budget that mental focus to any focus power from the implement school however they see fit. Expecting to need mind eye from the divination implement school a bunch, but wound up blowing your whole budget on sudden insight? Not a problem. For psychometrists, however, it is a big problem, because they assign mental focus to specific focus powers. If you assigned 5 points of mental focus to mind eye, there’s no overlap—you use it on mind eye or you lose it entirely. 
      * Third, the psychometrist’s relationship with focus powers is a bit bizarre. Base occultists simply get focus powers at every odd level that they can select from among any of their implement schools; psychometrists get only the base focus power of their chosen school, plus one additional focus power from each previous school. This creates a strange endgame power surge, and incentivizes you to always select implement schools with the most potent focus powers first, so that you’ll get more bites at the apple as the levels roll by. Evocation and enchantment are essentially non-starters in the race, and Point #4 below eliminates conjuration. Necromancy starts strong but has little to offer beyond soulbound puppet and necromantic servant. Divination is hurt badly by the loss of resonant powers, but dang, mind eye still delivers, and sudden insight and danger sight are perennial favorites. Illusion is kinda in the same boat—you really want distortion as a resonant power, but unseen, mirage, masquerade, and shadow beast all have their niche. That leaves abjuration and transmutation as the old standbys! You know ‘em. You love ‘em. Aegis, energy shield, and unraveling are the standouts from abjuration; quickness, mind over gravity, and telekinetic mastery are the best from transmutation. Legacy weapon, as always, rocks the house. Check out my occultist guide for recommendations on which enchantments to snag with both aegis and legacy weapon.
      * Fourth, you can’t take a conjuration implement at all. I have no idea why this might be. Servitor, flesh mend, psychic fog, and side step are all great focus powers, but they’re not wildly overpowered, relative to other implement schools. What gives, Paizo?


        Now, even with all that red and yellow in mind, gaining some of an occultist’s implements is still exceptionally powerful. Divination, illusion, abjuration, and transmutation will give you haste, fly, telekinesis, invisibility, dispel magic, legacy weapon, aegis, mind eye, etc., all several times per day. If you wanted to play a pseudo-caster without surrendering too many of your vigilante talents, there are far worse ways to do it.


        (2) Mental Focus (Su): Another subpar knockoff of the occultist. Psychometerists get only ½ level + Intelligence modifier, whereas occultists get their full level + Intelligence modifier. Granted, psychometrists don’t need mental focus to activate resonant powers, but we’re still talking about a full ten points of mental focus missed over the course of your career. That’s a hefty chunk of change! This class feature, by the by, more or less forces you to place all your mental attribute investment in Intelligence, rather than Charisma, in order to maximize your implement save DCs and mental focus pool. It’s the smart thing to do—grab some traits like Pragmatic Activator, Student of Philosophy, Bruising Intellect, etc. if you feel like you’re really hurting for Charisma-based skills.


(2) Object Reading (Su): Replaces unshakable. Unshakable is hot garbage, to use a technical term, so emerging the victor in a head-to-head class feature contest is laughably easy. Nevertheless, object reading is a tremendously useful class even when no comparison is drawn. Mysterious objects of unknown provenance are common in Pathfinder, whether weapons, armor, mementos or keepsakes, etc., and are often one of the only clues left as to a creature’s origin and backstory after the bloodthirsty PCs have summarily slaughtered them. Now you can learn that information yourself by communing with the object directly! It’s a great feature on the occultist, where I try never to give it away if I can help it.


(20) Psychometric Strike (Su): Alters vengeance strike. I suppose psychometric strike is strictly better than vengeance strike—it’s nothing but upside, even if you’re unlikely to run into enemies who have yoinked your belongings that often. Very few people play at 20th level, anyway.


Is It Worth It?


        It depends on how you weigh the main tradeoff psychometrist is asking you to make: four vigilante talents in exchange for 10 focus powers and about 17 points of mental focus by 20th level. I certainly wish the psychometrist’s version of implements and mental focus weren’t so neutered, but I’m guardedly optimistic that the versatility and strength of focus powers would ultimately outweigh the lost vigilante talents. Just be smart about which focus powers you take, when you take them, and how you apportion your mental focus. It matters even more for psychometrists than it does for occultists.
