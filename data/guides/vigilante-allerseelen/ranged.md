# Ranged

Ranged


Clustered Shots: DR penalizes lots of low-damage shots quite harshly, which makes Clustered Shots vital for most ranged builds, especially those that use Rapid Shot. You’ll want it.
Manyshot: Probably of more interest to avengers than stalkers, especially since stalkers don’t get to double-proc hidden strike damage on a manyshot. Regardless, blue for anyone who has the feats, the desire, and a bow.
Point-Blank Master: Only accessible to avenger vigilantes who took the signature weapon vigilante talent, Point-Blank Master is a gem for being able to continue shooting enemies who close the distance and get up into your face.
Point-Blank Shot / Precise Shot / Rapid Shot / Improved Precise Shot: The feats in this list are presented in roughly the order you’d take them in—if you’re using Elephant in the Room feat tax rules, feel free to skip Point-Blank Shot. Anyway, each feat here is absolutely essential to a ranged combat build, and ensures that you can hit hard and hit often.


Amateur Gunslinger: A better option for gun-toting vigilantes than the gunmaster archetype, and that’s saying something. Quick clear should be your deed of choice.
Deadly Aim: Avengers will be the only ones who can take full advantage of Deadly Aim’s bonuses to damage; stalkers will likely need to pass due to their slower BAB progression.
Hammer the Gap: A decent little damage bonus for ranged avengers.
Overwatch Style / Tactician / Vortex: Overwatch Style is fun for interrupting multiple casters, striking multiple enemies when they become visible, etc., and can yield some great results for Pathfinder players who really understand tactics.
Reckless Aim: Reckless Aim can actually prove to have a pretty big upside, assuming you’re judicious about when you use it. If you’re not currently threatened by an enemy, it’s essentially a free +2 to hit, and if you target a crowd of enemies, rolling a natural 1 will still allow you to hit someone. Just maybe don’t use it on targets your allies are threatening.
Snap Shot / Improved / Greater: Snap Shot and its older cousins allow you to threaten enemies with your bow and take ranged attacks of opportunity. It’s not hugely important for vigilantes, but always nice to have.


Expert Sniper / Master Sniper: Potentially of use to stalker vigilantes who want to remain hidden while continuing to attack enemies.


Rapid Reload / Crossbow Mastery: There’s really no reason for a vigilante to be using crossbows or guns, and yes, that includes the abysmal gunmaster archetype. Skip.
