# Splintersoul

Splintersoul


The Basics


        Vigilantes who have wildly divergent social and vigilante identities.


        Versatility: -1
        Power: +1


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Splintered Identity (Ex): Alters dual identity. Splintered identity seems uniquely tailored to multiclassed vigilantes who are dipping into classes with more stringent alignment restrictions. While that’s not necessarily a bad market to try to corner, I know many GMs who dispense with alignment restrictions altogether. Does it make sense that a monk has to be lawful, or that a barbarian has to be chaotic? Paizo itself produced three different versions of the champion class in 2e to accommodate different flavors of “warrior for righteousness.” If you’re just playing a single-class vigilante with no alignment restrictions, splintered identity is nothing but downside—truly. It’s not a huge downside, since many vigilantes will go to great lengths not to reveal their vigilante talents while in their social identity anyway, but still, less flexibility, not more.


        (3) Sudden Change (Ex): Replaces unshakable and alters 3rd- and 7th-level social talents. You mean I get two of the most purchased, most essential social talents for keeping identities separate several levels before other vigilantes? Heck yeah, I want that! This ameliorates many of the problems with splintered identity, since it doesn’t take nearly as long to transition from a social identity that can’t use vigilante talents without being ID’ed to a vigilante identity that can.


        (7) Surprising Change (Ex): Alters startling appearance, frightening appearance, and stunning appearance. Surprising change is the mechanical equivalent of “Call an ambulance—but not for me,” in that your transition from mild-mannered, dog-loving retiree John Wick into slaughter-happy, killed-ten-men-with-a-****ing-pencil Baba Yaga is so sudden that enemies get unnerved by it. It caters to a more social style of play, rather than a dungeon crawl style, since you wouldn’t just wander around in your social identity in the middle of an obviously dangerous locale. The penultimate line should probably read something like, “A splintersoul can use frightening appearance and stunning appearance against foes who are unaware of his presence as normal, but can only do so a number of times per day equal to his Charisma modifier.” I found the version presented on Nethys needlessly difficult to parse. Anyway, I’m going to rate this ability a yellow, but it’s more a sidegrade than a downgrade.


Is It Worth It?


        I’m as surprised as anyone to say that I actually kinda like this archetype, since I always had it in the back of my mind that it was hot garbage. Grabbing quick change and immediate change so many levels earlier than other vigilantes is a recipe for a hit, and neither splintered identity nor sudden change derail gameplay as much as you might fear. Of course, there’s always an opportunity cost to archetypes in that you can’t select another (potentially better) archetype that modifies the same class features, so take with a grain of salt.
