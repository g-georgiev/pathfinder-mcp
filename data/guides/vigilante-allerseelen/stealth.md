# Stealth

Stealth


Dampen Presence: Dampen Presence is the rare feat that actually beats its comparable vigilante talent, blind spot. Where blind spot grants monsters huge bonuses to notice you, Dampen Presence simply beats blindsense and blindsight, no questions asked. You’ll still have to have negate aroma, fly, and other tools to beat other senses, but Dampen Presence beats the two hardest senses there are.
Shadow’s Shroud: Essentially the hide in plain sight class feature, but for limited rounds per day. You’d probably want to use it to proc hidden strike damage in combat, rather than for mundane scouting—the duration isn’t long enough for the latter.


Expert Sniper / Master Sniper: Sniping is a bit of a niche market, but ranged stalkers will most certainly be interested in attacking enemies without those enemies becoming aware of where they are. The fact that Master Sniper eventually allows you to eke two attacks out of a single Stealth check is icing on the cake. You’ll probably want to grab the sniping weapon enchantment (the one from Ranged Tactics Toolbox) to maximize the playstyle.
Moonlight Stalker: Some decent bonuses here, especially for stalker vigilantes.


Eldritch Assault: Teisatsu stalkers who’ve taken the vanishing trick ninja trick might be able to get some mileage out of Eldritch Assault, although you have to wonder whether you’re not better off just picking up Critical Focus.
Hellcat Stealth: If it were me, I think I’d wait until 8th level and pick up the hide in plain sight stalker vigilante talent, but Hellcat Stealth will do in a pinch, too.




Support


Combat Advice: Spend a move action, give someone a +2 competence bonus to their next attack roll. It’s not fabulous, but it’s an all-day source of buffing.


Battle Cry: Morale bonuses are fairly common, but the buffs against fear effects are particularly tasty.
