# Teisatsu

Teisatsu


The Basics


        Teisatsu draw tons of inspiration from the ninja class. The versatility of having a ki pool and access to ninja tricks/ki powers, combined with the relatively few class features that you lose, should make you consider teisatsu as the “base archetype” for the stalker vigilante specialization unless you truly, truly need that 2nd-level vigilante talent.


        Versatility: +2
        Power: +2


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Weapon and Armor Proficiency: Alters weapon and armor proficiencies. Lots of great exotic weapons on tap here. The wakizashi and katana are easily the best offered, especially for crit-fishing.


        (1) Infiltrator (Ex): Alters vigilante specialization. Yes, let’s all just try to imagine an avenger ninja for a second, huh? This decision makes all too much sense. You’re a sneaky snake, so act like it!


        (2) Ki Pool (Su): Replaces 2nd-level vigilante talent. Lethal grace would normally occupy a stalker’s 2nd-level vigilante talent slot, but honestly, it’s not a big deal to just take Weapon Finesse at 1st level, take lethal grace at 4th level, and retrain the feat for free. On its own, a ki pool is already pretty good, most notably for the extra attack you can make at full BAB as a swift action. If you have an enemy dead to rights and lined up for a full-attack with hidden strike, there’s no reason you shouldn’t invest a point of ki to make the attack extra lethal. Where the ability truly shines, however, is in ki powers and ninja tricks, all of which give the teisatsu a huge amount of versatility and build flexibility. Get ready for the full scoop below the fold, and remember that the Extra Ki feat exists! You may want some more fuel in the tank for these abilities.


        (2) Teisatsu Talents: Wow. Just wow. Teisatsu is such an enormous power jump for stalker vigilantes, it’s not even funny. It’s like Paizo said, “Hey, I have this crazy idea knocking around my head. What if we gave an already strong class the best features from two other strong classes?” and everyone else in the room pulled out guitars and started doing sick metal riffs. I won’t bother to review every option available to teisatsu, but we do need to cover a few essentials. Keep in mind that because every ki power and ninja trick needs to be taken in place of a vigilante talent, we can only be satisfied with the absolute cream of the crop. Anything else will be trading down.


      * Ki Powers
      * (8) Freedom of Movement (Su): Freedom of movement slays, plain and simple. Solid fog? You’re gone. Black tentacles? Gone. Insane CMB checks to grapple you? Gone. There’s a reason every prepared caster totes this spell around at all times. There’s a reason you should have it, too.
      * (8) Abundant Step (Su): An ultimate get-out-of-jail-free card, dimension door for 2 ki points seems eminently reasonable. Great for infiltration, exfiltration, and general ninja hijinks.
      * (8) Insightful Wisdom (Su): This wouldn’t be my first choice of ki power, both because of its cost and the existence of higher priorities, but it’s a nice little buffing effect that can save an ally from death on failed saves.


      * Ninja Tricks
      * (1) Forgotten Trick (Ex): Consider forgotten trick the martial flexibility of the ninja class. For the price of 2 ki, you can select any ninja trick (not master tricks, unfortunately) and behave as though you knew it for multiple rounds. True, it’s costly, but the versatility you get in exchange is peerless.
      * (1) Redirect Force (Ex): I first came into contact with redirect force when writing my Spheres of Might guide, and honestly, it’s a hilarious ninja trick. True, you have to take damage to use it well, but if you do, there’s almost no enemy that can prevent themselves from being tripped, dirty tricked, bull rushed 25 feet over a cliff, etc. Great for combat maneuver builds—no enemy will ever see it coming. And best of all, it’s free to use!
      * (1) Shadow Clone (Su): Mirror image, one of the better defensive spells in the game, for 1 ki point. Need we say more?
      * (1) Vanishing Trick (Su): I truly wish the invisible blade master trick were available to teisatsu, but I suppose we’ll have to make do with vanishing trick. Look: stalkers need hidden strike, and while you can sometimes use dirty tricks to blind an enemy, Intimidate and Shatter Defenses to make them flat-footed, Gang Up to flank more effectively, etc., invisibility is a time-honored tactic that works against a solid tranche of Bestiary enemies, at least in early to mid levels. You Want This.™
      * (1) Flurry of Stars (Ex): Shuriken builds can be super fun for teisatsu. Why? Well, a majority of your damage isn’t going to come from your weapons anyway, so you can just dump a huge number of attacks into the meat grinder and proc hidden strike damage like crazy. With enough bonuses, you can nail an enemy with obscene damage before they even figure out what’s going on.
      * (1) Pressure Points* (Su): It’s reasonable to assume that teisatsu are going to be attacking enemies who are unaware of their presence quite often. Why not deal ability damage at the same time? There’s no save to do so, and the Heal checks enemies would need to pass in order to remove the damage are quite cumbersome in the middle of a fight. It’s a good way to soften enemies up for other allies, either on offense (Strength, usually) or defense (Dexterity, usually). And if you can get a big, lumbering enemy down to 0 Dexterity or a small, weak enemy down to 0 Strength, the effect is the same—they’re paralyzed.


Is It Worth It?


        Teisatsu is the pinnacle of a worthwhile archetype: like primalist bloodragers, you give up virtually nothing and gain oodles of powerful options from the unchained monk and ninja. Don’t worry too much about the lost 2nd-level vigilante talent—I assure you, the trade is worth your while.
