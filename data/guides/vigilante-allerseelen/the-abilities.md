# The Abilities

The Abilities


        (1) Class Skills: Alters class skills. Pretty much nothing but upside here. Knowledge (Religion) and Spellcraft are way more useful than any of the four replaced skills.


        (1) Skill Ranks Per Level: Alters skill ranks per level. Boo, hiss. I like my skill ranks. Don’t take my skill ranks from me.


        (1) Weapon Proficiencies: Alters weapon proficiencies. Vigilantes are already proficient with all simple and martial weapons, so this will only make a difference if your deity has an exotic weapon as their favored implement of murder. It’s nice that Irori worshipers get Improved Unarmed Strike for free, but you’d probably want a scaled fist unchained monk dip anyway.


        (1) Spellcasting: Replaces 4th-, 8th-, 10th-, 14th-, and 16th-level vigilante talents. I’m going to go out on a limb and say that this actually isn’t so great for the zealot archetype. I’ll be the first to sing the praises of the inquisitor class, but what I won’t do is lie to you and tell you that its spell list is superior to other options on the market. Its only competition among ⅔ divine casters are the warpriest spell list (which mimics the cleric spell list and is therefore quite comprehensive) and the hunter spell list (which poaches every spell from both the druid and ranger list, for a pretty unbeatable selection). You’ll find great recommendations for spells in the linked guide, but my advice would be to stick with self-buffs and leave the rest of it alone.


        (1) Aligned Spells, Alignment, and Aura: Fairly boilerplate cleric/inquisitor stuff. Don’t worry about it too much.


        (1) Inquisition (Ex): Replaces vigilante specialization. This isn’t a fair trade; hidden strike dice and full BAB outmatch inquisitions, hands down. I’ve outlined some good inquisitions here, but you’ll have to figure out which one works best for you. Check with your GM to see whether they’ll permit you a domain as a normal inquisitor would get, because that would open up your options considerably.


        (2) Zealot Talents: Alters vigilante talents.


      * (2) Zealot Smite (Su): If you pick no other zealot talent, pick zealot smite. It’s essentially a paladin’s smite evil class feature, minus the AC bonus and minus a few niche cases where zealot smite won’t penetrate DR that smite evil would (DR/damage type or DR/—, notably, which can’t be pierced by weapon enhancement bonuses). To compensate, zealot smite permits you to smite a wider variety of creatures—not just evil or good, but chaos and law, too! This incentivizes you to pick an alignment at the extremes (lawful evil, lawful good, chaotic good, chaotic evil) so that you can smite a wider spectrum of alignments. Excellent, at any rate.
      * (2) Harsh Judgment (Su): I’m not sure why Archives of Nethys lists this talent on the vigilante talents page, but not on the zealot archetype page. *Shrug.* At any rate, it’s a fantastic talent. Even without second/third judgment, and even without the full spectrum of judgments available to inquisitors, you should still be salivating at the thought of judgments of destruction and purity. Easily worth a purchase.
      * (12) Stalwart (Ex): Part of the reason stalwart works pretty well for inquisitors is that inquisitors have strong Fortitude and Will saves. Vigilantes have strong Will saves, but not strong Fortitude saves—and when the ability relies on a successful save to work, that might be a bigger deal than you think. Stalwart will always be limited by the number of Fortitude or Will half/partial effects in the game, too: there are only about 220 spells in the game that have those save configurations, out of over 3000 spells overall. So…nice to have, but not essential by any stretch.
      * (2) Stern Gaze (Ex): Vigilantes specialize in Intimidate quite often, and especially with Signature Skill: Intimidate, it’s certainly not unreasonable to think that you might want to pump that skill bonus as high as possible so you can escalate fear conditions directly to frightened, panicked, or cowering. Even here, though, a skill bonus is a mighty thin rationale for burning one of only three vigilante talents on.
      * (2) Channel Energy (Su): As a zealot, you’ll get three vigilante talents (2nd, 6th, 12th) before most Pathfinder campaigns end. Do you really want to spend one of those talents on channel energy (a dubious class feature even for the cleric) when it will take you until 7th level to go up to 2d6 healing? I sure wouldn’t.
      * (6) Discern Lies (Sp): Discern lies earns a yellow rating in my inquisitor guide when it’s given to you for free. When you have to burn one of your three vigilante talents on it? Nah.
      * (6) Empower Symbol (Su): The addition of a permanent consecrate/desecrate effect to your holy symbol is going to be useful only for necromancers, who can get much more out of animate dead in a desecrated area than they can elsewhere. Unfortunately, animate dead isn’t on the inquisitor spell list, so…that’s a bummer. You could make do with a staff of animate dead, I suppose, but that’s a lot of trouble to go to.
