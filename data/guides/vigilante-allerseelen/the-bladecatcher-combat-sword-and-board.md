Strategy


This chicanery of a build is founded on the unnaturally strong interaction between Shield Gauntlet Style and the steel soldier vigilante talent, and gets you all the melee blender-style combat you could want while also sporting pretty fantastic AC numbers and surprisingly economical attribute allocations. Let’s jump in!
For starters, you’ll notice that much of this build is a pretty boilerplate Two-Weapon Fighting sword-and-board build with heavy armor. But from 2nd to about 10th level, everything about this build takes a fun left turn, and that starts with Shield Gauntlet Style at 3rd level. What this feat does is allow you to treat a spiked gauntlet as though it were a buckler. Simple enough, right? Under normal circumstances, you wouldn’t be able to shield bash or do anything other than wield your gauntlet if you wanted to receive its shield bonus to AC, but shield of fury (which comes online at 2nd level) permits you to shield bash with a gauntlet if you really wanted to. (You won’t, at this level, as your main hand weapon will do far more damage.) Take ‘em alive at 4th level will start to rack up the all-important bonuses to attack and damage whenever you deal nonlethal damage, and Shielded Gauntlet Attack at 5th level will give you better damage dice than you would normally enjoy on your punchy-punchy attacks, but this build doesn’t truly start to emerge until 6th to 8th, when something very important happens each level.
At 6th, shield of fury finally grants you Two-Weapon Fighting, as long as one of the weapons you’re using is a shield. No problem! Shield Gauntlet Style turns our gauntlet into a buckler for those purposes. You’ll now be able to take three attacks in a full-attack action, two with your main hand weapon and one with your off-hand buckler. At 7th level, Shielded Gauntlet Master comes online. Although this would technically allow you to wield two manufactured weapons and a gauntlet, we’re mostly interested in the fact that your gauntlet’s weapon enhancement bonus is also treated as its shield enhancement bonus. But don’t enchant it yet! Don’t do anything until 8th level, when the steel soldier vigilante talent lays down the final puzzle piece: when wearing your modified armor, you treat your armor’s enhancement bonus as its weapon enhancement bonus. What this does is spread your armor enhancement costs over three domains: if you’re wearing +4 hellknight plate, you’ll get the +4 enhancement bonus to your armor from its base enchantments, but also a +4 enhancement bonus to your gauntlet’s weapon attack and damage rolls (steel soldier) that then gets transferred again to your gauntlet’s shield enhancement bonus (Shielded Gauntlet Master). In essence, every point of investment in your armor’s enhancement bonus grants +1 attack, +1 damage, and +2 AC. How’s that for economy?
But wait! Who says you need to wield any other weapon in your main hand at all? I mean, yeah, technically this is a sword and board build, but you’re already not wielding a board, so why not ditch the sword, too? The wording in Shielded Gauntlet Style is somewhat ambiguous, but my interpretation is that a main hand gauntlet is also acceptable, but only the gauntlet in your off-hand counts as a buckler. That’s fine! If you wield two spiked gauntlets, each will inherit the armor’s enhancement bonuses to attack and damage (steel soldier), each will inherit sacred weapon’s scaling damage dice (Shielded Gauntlet Attack), and one will inherit its weapon enhancement bonuses as shield enhancement bonuses (Shielded Gauntlet Master). Once fist of the avenger drops at 10th level, each attack will pick up a +5 damage bonus on every hit. Double Slice at 11th lets you use your full Strength modifier with your off-hand, Two-Weapon Rend at 13th grants another quasi-attack, and finally Greater Two-Weapon Fighting at 15th grants yet another attack. Assuming you’ve picked up +5 armor and have a Strength score of ~24, at 15th level you’ll deal an average of 228.5 damage on a full-attack charge (thanks, mad rush and close the gap!) if all your attacks hit. It’s worth being judicious with Power Attack in this build, because your attack bonuses are already lowered from Two-Weapon Fighting and your Power Attack damage bonuses are already lowered from not two-handing a weapon. Only turn on Power Attack when you’re beating down a minion—it’s better to take +31/+31/+26/+26/+21/+21 (plus Two-Weapon Rend) and hit with most of your iteratives for an average of 180.5 damage than it is to go for +27/+27/+22/+22/+17/+17 and miss with most of your iteratives.
