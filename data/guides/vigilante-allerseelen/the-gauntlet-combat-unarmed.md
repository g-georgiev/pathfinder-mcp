Strategy


        Did I build Batman? Yeah, I kinda built Batman. We can quibble about whether Batman is properly an avenger or a stalker—I think there’s a case to be made for either—another time, because right now it’s time to talk about mashing face as an unarmed vigilante. This build combines roughly four elements: (1) great unarmed strike damage, (2) heavy armor and good AC values, (3) a surprising amount of mobility, and (4) the basics of a demoralization build. Moving in order, Power Attack is obviously a must, and flurry of blows + fist of the avenger + take ‘em alive will give us a combined +5 to unarmed attack rolls and +10 to unarmed damage rolls with an extra attack as a cherry on top. Dragon Style and Dragon Ferocity are mostly present to multiply our static Strength modifier, although they have excellent benefits for saves and mobility, as well. Later in the build, the mad rush vigilante talent ensures that you basically never spend a turn not full-attacking. Moving on to defenses, heavy training is obviously a good place to start, but if you wanted to do a much more bare-bones build, say, to focus a bit more on offense, you could always ditch heavy training and armor skin in favor of a wand of mage armor plus the scaled fist’s Charisma bonus to AC. At any rate, you’ll pick up shield of blades at 11th level, which is another evergreen source of AC.
        Mobility: you have a lot of it, somehow! Armor skin is a great vigilante talent for ensuring that you can run, jump, sneak, etc. while still wearing your full plate, but the real star of the show is Dragon Style, which grants a host of benefits to improve your ability to charge at enemies. Once you reach mad rush at 13th level, you should always close with enemies by charging, and can even mitigate the AC penalty with close the gap and shield of blades. Finally, we’ve got the essentials for an Intimimancy build here, including Enforcer and Signature Skill: Intimidate. There’s nothing very special that you need to do to trigger this part of the build—simply attack using nonlethal damage (you’d be doing this to proc take ‘em alive anyway, Batman) and roll Intimidate checks whenever needed. I’m fond of cruel handwraps for this build, since they’ll allow you to both demoralize and sicken enemies when you punch them. The many renown social talents should keep your Intimidate scores relevant with fairly minimal investment, but you’ll likely want to have full ranks in the skill by 11th level, when Signature Skill: Intimidate opens up a whole new can of whoop-ass on enemies. 10 ranks is when you start becoming able to dole out the panicked condition, so this is your cue to start peeling enemies off of large fights by scaring the living daylights out of them. Remember what Ra’s al Ghul taught you: fear is a weapon.
