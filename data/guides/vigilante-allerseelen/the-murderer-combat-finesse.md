Strategy


Sometimes you just want to murder, you know? At least, that’s the thought I assume is running around most Norgorberites’ heads. Well, here’s the build you need to do just that. For most of their career, the Murderer will play as a very boilerplate, bog standard stalker vigilante with a rogue variant multiclass. You’ll use a short sword and lethal grace to pop out of invisibility or stealth, then do the traditional rogue flank ‘n’ shank routine, adding three secondary natural attacks at 6th level (morphic weaponry) to complement your existing iterative attacks. Hide in plain sight, skill familiarity, owl’s sight, Skill Focus: Stealth, and Dampen Presence all boost your Stealth expertise, and at 10th level it should become abundantly clear why you’ve been focusing on it so intensively: Norgorber’s Silent Shiv. Yes, our intrepid little halfling is a secret devotee of Thamir Gixx and Norgorber! While the base benefit of Norgorber’s divine fighting technique is fairly uninspiring, bumping up your damage die from 1d4 → 1d6 for short swords, the advanced benefit that unlocks at 10th level is decidedly not uninspiring. I’ll quote: 


At the start of each round, choose one opponent that you can see and attempt a Stealth check as a swift action. If the result of your check exceeds the target’s CMD, treat the target as if it were completely unaware of your presence for the purpose of the initial benefit of this divine fighting technique. If you beat the target’s CMD by 5 or more, you also treat it as being completely unaware of your presence for the purposes of all class features and feats you have.


Now, class, who can tell me what the significance of this passage is? Let’s not always see the same hands. Yes! Well done. Norgorber’s Silent Shiv is the only effect in the game (that I know of, at least, but at this point I know quite a bit about Pathfinder) that allows you to treat an enemy as completely unaware of your presence for the purposes of all class features and feats you have. At a bare minimum, Norgorber’s Silent Shiv ensures that you deal your full, non-reduced hidden strike damage (plus your sneak attack damage) every time you’re able to beat an enemy’s CMD + 5 with your Stealth check—a feat that should be a cinch, given that your Stealth score should be about +48 by 15th level (15 ranks + 3 class skill + 8 Dexterity + 4 Small size + 6 Skill Focus + 2 Secretive Survivor + 10 improved shadow armor; additional bonuses might come from Dimdweller or owl’s sight in conditions of dim light) against a median CMD of 42. Remember that skill familiarity allows you to take 10 on Stealth checks even when you’re hurried or threatened, sure-footed allows you to move at full speed while stealthing, and hide in plain sight allows you to use Stealth even while you’re being observed! Anyway, apart from massive hidden strike and sneak attack damage proc’ing on every iterative and natural attack, Norgorber’s Silent Shiv helps you sidestep the “only usable against enemies who are completely unaware of your presence” restriction of hidden strike vigilante talents; I chose leave an opening as my hidden strike talent in this build, since it can be used against one enemy repeatedly for a guaranteed attack of opportunity every round, but mighty ambush, throat jab, and foe collision are also great. Finally, Norgorber’s Silent Shiv clears the way for you to proc startling appearance, frightening appearance, and stunning appearance multiple times per round—even if an enemy is aware of you, your whisper-quiet shanking ensures that they still take the penalties. Frightening appearance and stunning appearance can unfortunately affect an individual enemy only once in 24 hours, but startling appearance can be made permanent with Silent Shiv, rendering enemies flat-footed forever and imparting a hefty -4 penalty to hit you.
Our overview of this build wouldn’t be complete without mentioning how insanely awesome the halfling vigilante favored class bonus is. Free social talents!? Please and thank you. Conveniently, many guises, everyman, and any guise become available at 5th, 11th, and 17th levels, just one level before you’ll receive a free social talent at 6th, 12th, and 18th levels, so my advice for most halfling builds would be to simply select those social talents at the listed levels. You’ll be pretty great at crushing skill checks (skill familiarity, social grace, ancestral enlightenment), switching between your social and vigilante identities (quick change, immediate change), and infiltrating enemy organizations (skill familiarity, hidden magic, songbird, many guises, everyman, any guise) without sacrificing a single feat from your limited progression. I’d personally be thrilled to play this build!
