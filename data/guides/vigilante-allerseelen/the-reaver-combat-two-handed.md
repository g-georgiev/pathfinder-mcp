Strategy


Awww, everyone wants to play one of these builds at some point in their career, right? Absolutely zero subtlety, just rage and damage. The Reaver is predicated on the idea that you should ABC: A, always, B, be, C, charging. Always be charging. Always be charging. At low levels, this will be the whole strategy: activate Demonic Style for some attack and damage bonuses, charge an enemy with Power Attack on, and watch the damage numbers roll. (Don’t forget to use your bite attack from Maw or Claw! It’s a primary natural attack, so it’ll hit at your full BAB.) Levels 5-9 see some more interesting elements of the build come online. I’ve built you for bull rushing in this build, both because of how thematically cool it is for a raging tiefling descended from demons to toss enemies about the battlefield with ease and because Demonic Style requires it at later levels. Against squishy targets with low CMD scores, you can feel free to use Demonic Momentum to slam enemies back with a bull rush at the end of charge, then follow them to take advantage of your big damage bonuses on the following turn; Demonic Slaughter mitigates the loss of damage from bull rushing by allowing you to use Great Cleave whenever you succeed at your CMB check. Against tough or physically imposing enemies, best to stick with a normal charge, as the bull rush attempt won’t succeed consistently even with heavy investment.
The real boom sauce, however, comes into effect at 12th level with the mad rush vigilante talent. Mad rush allows you to paste a full-attack action onto the end of a charge—and that’s where Quick Bull Rush enters the scene. In place of one of your highest-BAB attacks (don’t worry—you’ve got haste and Maw or Claw) you get to bull rush an enemy as part of your full-attack! If this gambit works, you trigger a Great Cleave via Demonic Slaughter, immediately followed by the rest of your full-attack action—and all of these attacks, the Great Cleave, the full-attack, everything, snag the bonuses from both Demonic Style and Demonic Momentum. (Demonic Style specifies “melee attacks made as part of a charge,” which mad rush fulfills.) With those crazy attack and damage bonuses locked in, you’re incentivized to prey upon hordes of weaker enemies, knocking them around like nine pins and cutting them down en masse. The rest of the build is just devoted to making you harder to kill or facilitating charges: heavy training + armor skin + shield of blades is a classic Strength-based vigilante combo for strong-yet-mobile AC values, and close the gap both mitigates the AC penalty from charging and ensures that you can reach larger enemies without being cut to shreds. Sure-footed would be another great adjunct to this playstyle for charging through difficult terrain, but I opted for signature weapon at 14th level instead—your mileage may vary. At any rate, I really like this build, at least conceptually—anything founded on combat maneuvers is going to be a little shaky sooner or later, but you can always fall back on full-attacking enemies with mad rush, even if your bull rushes never work out.
