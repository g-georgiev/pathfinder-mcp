# Universal

Universal


Combat Casting: Vigilante archetypes that trade away vigilante talents in exchange for spellcasting can expect to spend a significant amount of time casting spells while being threatened by enemies, so Combat Casting is a must-have feat for cabalists, warlocks, zealots, magical children, ferocious hunters, etc.


Feign Curse: Feign Curse is a fun debuffing feat that can best be thought of as ill omen, but with no spell resistance, and keyed off a skill check, rather than a spell save DC. Vigilantes tend to have good Bluff scores and Charisma modifiers, so you could do far worse than Feign Curse if you want to get enemies to fumble all over themselves.
Spell Focus / Greater: Several vigilante archetypes receive spellcasting capabilities, and if they want to target enemies with those spells, they’d be well-advised to take Spell Focus.
Spell Penetration / Greater: As with Spell Focus, beating caster level checks to overcome spell resistance is a necessity for offensive casters.


Divine Interference: Only zealots will qualify for Divine Interference, but holy crap, what a feat. Force an enemy to reroll any successful attack at a penalty for the cost of a spell slot. Monsters critting your allies too often? Not anymore, they’re not!
