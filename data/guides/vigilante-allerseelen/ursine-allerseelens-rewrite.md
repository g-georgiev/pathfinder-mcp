# Ursine (Allerseelen’s Rewrite)

Ursine (Allerseelen’s Rewrite)


(2) Apex Predator (Ex): At 2nd level, an ursine wildsoul gains two claw attacks and a bite attack while in his vigilante identity. These are primary natural attacks. The bite attack deals 1d6 damage if he is Medium or 1d4 damage if he is Small; the claw attacks deal 1d4 points of damage if he is Medium or 1d3 if he is Small. Additionally, he gains a natural armor bonus of +1. This natural armor bonus increases by 1 at 6th level and every four levels thereafter, to a maximum of +5 at 18th level.


I’m sorry, but the ursine wildsoul just needed more oomph. This frontloads many of the necessary combat bonuses in the archetype, but isn’t fundamentally dissimilar to something like a ratfolk getting Sharpclaw and Sharptooth by 3rd level for three natural attacks.


        (6) Big Game (Ex): At 6th level, an ursine wildsoul has become incredibly difficult to kill. He gains the Endurance, Diehard, and Toughness feats, ignoring their prerequisites. Additionally, he may ignore the BAB and Constitution requirements of any feat that lists Endurance or Diehard as a prerequisite, and counts as an orc with Ironhide for the purposes of qualifying for the Tenacious Survivor, Deathless Initiate, Deathless Master, and Deathless Zealot feats.


“Three feats!” I hear you say. “Isn’t that a bit overboard?” Well…no. These aren’t fantastic feats. Endurance is generally quite poor, and Diehard can be dangerous to use without a very clear idea of what you’re doing. Only Toughness is unambiguously good. This is mostly here for the flavor of being an unstoppable bear, and opens the way for some more animal ferocity-esque feats like Heroic Recovery, Fast Healer, Tenacious Survivor, Stalwart, Survivor, and the Deathless line. 


(12) Bear Form (Su): At 12th level, an ursine wildsoul has learned how to take a bear’s form. He can change into bear form as a standard action, at will, and the transformation lasts until he changes back. While transformed, he becomes either a grizzly bear or a polar bear, as beast shape II. An ursine wildsoul loses his ability to speak while in bear form and is limited to the sounds that a normal bear can make, though he can communicate normally with other bears, as speak with animal. At 16th level, this ability grants +8 Strength, -4 Dexterity, +2 Constitution, +6 natural armor, and the rake (2 claws) and rend (2 claws) abilities, dealing damage as appropriate for the wildsoul’s Strength modifier and size. At 20th level, this ability grants +12 Strength, -4 Dexterity, +6 Constitution, +8 natural armor, and DR 10/—.


If you want to incentivize bear form and can’t afford to grant magical beast shapes, it’s got to come with better attribute bonuses. Essentially, this rewrite packages Huge- and then Gargantuan-sized attribute bonuses into your existing Large-sized bear form, plus adds a little more meat onto your grapples (through rake) and full-attack routine (through rend). See, now there’s actually a reason to abandon your vigilante talents and run around mauling people, Revenant style. I also handed out a polar bear form, because they’re objectively way scarier than grizzly bears. “If it’s black, fight back; if it’s brown, lie down; if it’s white, say good night.”


        (18) Ursine Paragon (Ex): At 18th level, an ursine wildsoul has become one with the bear’s ecosystem. He gains a combined favored terrain bonus of +12, as the ranger class feature, that he may apportion as he sees fit between plains, cold, and forest terrains. No terrain may exceed a +8 bonus. (For example, he may choose a +8 bonus in cold terrains and a +2 in both forest and plains, a +6 bonus in forest, a +4 in cold, and a +2 in plains, or a +4 in all terrains.) Additionally, the ursine wildsoul gains the corresponding terrain mastery and terrain dominance benefits of the horizon walker prestige class in each terrain to which he apportions a bonus of at least +4.


For an 18th-level ability, ursine paragon is frankly still a bit tame. In very specific campaigns like Reign of Winter, of course, that cold terrain ability is going to slap, but then again, rangers always slap when terrain and enemy conditions are in their favor. The horizon walker bonuses are mostly fun fluff: cold resistance 20, +10 to base move speed, a +4 competence bonus on Stealth checks—none of this stuff is going to break the bank when full casters are laying down wish and mass heal every other round.
