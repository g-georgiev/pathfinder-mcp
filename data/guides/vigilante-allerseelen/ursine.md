# Ursine

Ursine


        (2) Deadly Claws (Ex): It’s hard to get excited about any of the ursine wildsoul’s three natural attacks when morphic weaponry, available for the cost of two vigilante talents with seamless shapechanger to boot, would grant four. Heck, these natural attacks don’t even do much damage! Bite attacks are usually 1d6, at least.


        (6) Bear Jaws (Ex): Yeah, ditto. These just needed to be stronger.


        (12) Thick Hide (Ex): A whole +1 to natural armor? Golly jee, mister, you shouldn’t have! Seriously, though, this is a pathetic talent when compared with practically any other wildsoul’s 12th-level talent. Felines get mad rush, falconines get a permanent fly speed, and even arachnids can start web swinging at this level. You get…+1 AC? Nah.


        (18) Bear Form (Ex): Let’s see…bear form will get you a +4 bonus to Strength, a -2 penalty to Dexterity, a +4 natural armor bonus, low-light vision, scent, two claws, a bite, and grab. But you lose the ability to speak, use manufactured weaponry, and do basically anything else. You do realize that by the time you get beast shape II, full casters will have been casting beast shape IV for, like, seven levels, don’t you? This is just a bad deal.
