# Utility

Utility


(8) Hide in Plain Sight (Ex): Look, stalker vigilantes are supposed to be sneaky snakes, and you can’t be a sneaky snake unless you can use Stealth whenever you need it. HiPS does that for you, and can be combined with the sure-footed vigilante talent to make you fast and silent.


(2) Blind Spot (Ex): Awww, blind spot, you could have been so good! The effect here is similar to the Dampen Presence feat, but where Dampen Presence just shuts down blindsense and blindsight, no questions asked, blind spot instead grants monsters scaling +20 bonuses to Perception to notice you for each sense they have. This is no bueno. Even if you’re invisible, it’s not going to take much for monsters (especially high-level monsters) to spot you in an instant. I debated a red rating here, but it does still have some uses.


(2) Rogue Talent (Ex): Look, I’m going to be honest: the vast majority of rogue talents are bad, plain and simple. Unchained rogue talents are better, and a benevolent GM might allow you to take those talents, but those are not the rules as written. Honestly, you’re better off going into the teisatsu archetype, which unlocks ninja tricks—a much better proposition than rogue talents.
