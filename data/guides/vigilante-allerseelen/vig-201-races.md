# VIG 201: Races

VIG 201: Races




Assuming that you’ve made it this far and know that you like the cut of the vigilante’s jib—what it’s good at, what it struggles with, and what role it can be expected to play in a party composition—it’s time to set about the nitty-gritty work of actually building a vigilante. Race (or ancestry, as Pathfinder 2e calls it) is one of the first decisions that you’ll make, and it’s undoubtedly an important one. Fortunately, we don’t need to review every one of Pathfinder’s enormous number of races, because a simple heuristic will help you arrive at a good racial choice 99% of the time. Some things that vigilantes are intensely interested in acquiring through racial abilities:


      * Bonuses to Strength or Dexterity principally, and to Charisma secondarily. Vigilantes are first and foremost a combat class, so they positively must have an attribute bonus that goes to their attack stat. If you’re considering a race that doesn’t grant such a bonus or (gods forbid) imposes a penalty to Strength or Dexterity, you’re far better off opting for a race that allows you to assign one +2 bonus to any attribute—races like humans, half-elves, and half-orcs will all suffice.
      * Bonus feats. Vigilante talents often duplicate or improve upon feats that are already available, but since vigilante talents are incredibly valuable, you’ll want to buy as many feats as you can with your normal feat progression unless there’s a compelling, unique bonus to the talent. (For example, cunning feint allows you to feint in place of your first attack in a full-attack action, which can’t be found anywhere in Pathfinder’s feinting feats. Since that’s a really good ability for feinting builds, they’ll typically want the talent, not the feats.) Humans are the classic choice for this category, as they grant one bonus feat at 1st level, when feat crunches tend to be felt most acutely.
      * Favored class bonuses. Favored class bonuses are bonuses that Pathfinder races can take in lieu of bonus skill ranks or HP at every level. As later additions to the Pathfinder canon, the vigilante never got as many favored class bonuses as other classes (and fewer still are useful!) but there are a few worth highlighting: first, the cream of the crop, the king of the castle, +⅙ of a new social talent. Only halflings receive this favored class bonus, and that fact combined with their excellent attribute bonuses puts them in easy contention for the best race to play ranged and finesse vigilantes. It’s impossible for me to overstate how powerful most vigilante talents and social talents are—and this is the only place in the game where you can get more of them. At a somewhat distant second, you have +½ on the Disguise bonus provided by seamless guise. Seamless guise already provides a +20 bonus to conceal the fact that your social and vigilante identities aren’t one and the same; barring a tangle with, say, a high-level inquisitor, you should almost never have to worry about that doomsday scenario unless you’ve skimped on your investment in Disguise. This favored class bonus gets considerably better if you have the seamless shapechanger talent, which allows you to add your seamless guise bonus to your Disguise checks whenever you’re polymorphed. Of course, it’s also notably worse if you’re not attempting to keep your identities separate, and would militate for skill ranks or HP, as you prefer.
      * Vision. Vigilantes have ways to get both low-light vision (the owl’s sight social talent) and low-light vision + darkvision (the shadow’s sight vigilante talent) but it’s better not to have to spend limited resources on them.
      * Skill bonuses. These bonuses have to be evaluated in light of how useful the skill is. Bonuses to Perception, like the elf and half-elf’s Keen Senses? Pure gold. Bonuses to Appraise or Handle Animal? Poop on a stick.
