# VIG 245: Social Talents

VIG 245: Social Talents


If vigilante talents are what make the vigilante feel like an à la carte rogue or fighter, social talents are what make them feel like à la carte bards, investigators, and mesmerists. The most mechanically powerful social talents tend to come online in a very well-ordered progression, so I almost always find myself taking something like the following:


      * 1st: Companion to the lonely / owl’s sight / social grace
      * 3rd: Dealer’s choice of 1st-level social talents
      * 5th: Many guises
      * 7th: Quick change
      * 9th: Skill familiarity
      * 11th: Everyman
      * 13th: Immediate change
      * 15th: Hidden magic / ancestral enlightenment / mockingbird
      * 17th: Any guise
      * 19th: Dealer’s choice


        Everyone’s mileage will vary, of course, but for my tastes that’s the best, most flexible package of social talents you can have at a given level. Races like halflings that have a +⅙ social talent favored class bonus will be in an even better position, since they can take some of the wildcard talents listed at 1st and 15th levels at 6th, 12th, and 18th levels instead. Remember that there are no ways—short of those favored class bonuses—to get more social talents. Choose wisely.




Universal


1st Level


Companion to the Lonely (Ex): How exactly one hour of sexytimes per day translates to a bunch of free rerolls on Will saves, we may never know, but regardless, Marvin Gaye, I’m ready to get it on! Vigilantes naturally have a strong Will save progression, but you can never have a high enough Will save. I would advise that you not use your rerolls on Charisma-based skill checks unless they’re the most important skill checks you’ve ever rolled. The utility of this social talent is obviously predicated on having a willing partner to…hem…woo. If you don’t, well, no one ever handed out bonuses for romancing your own nethers. Regardless, for the sake of all involved, figure out your group’s lines and veils before showing up with this social talent. Please and thank you.


Always Prepared (Ex): Always prepared really shines with creative players who are adventuring in one location consistently—think campaigns like Curse of the Crimson Throne with Korvosa. Probably the best use for the talent is to purchase a bunch of potions, scrolls, alchemical items, etc. that you might need on the fly. If your campaign is going to involve constantly moving around to different locales, you may want to skip it.
Morphic Mask (Su): In addition to being thematically cool, morphic mask increases your Disguise bonuses from seamless guise, and I’m all about that. Can’t have anyone guessing that Lance Hunt is Captain Amazing.
Owl’s Sight (Ex): Sure. Owl’s sight gives you some decent vision and skill bonuses, and you don’t have to spend any vigilante talents for ‘em.


Case the Joint (Ex): Case the joint is…interesting. The reliance on Knowledge (Engineering) is a bit bizarre; that skill generally isn’t worth your skill ranks unless you’re playing with the Background Skills rules. If you can become good at Knowledge (Engineering) and expect to spend a lot of time in urban environments where hanging around in your social identity wouldn’t be conspicuous, sure, you have my permission to take it. The bonuses are nice.
Guise of Unlife (Su): Unlike guise of life, guise of unlife at least opens up some interesting roleplay and interaction opportunities. Maybe you want to infiltrate a necromantic cult and appearing to be undead would give you an advantage, or you want to really spook someone by making them think that a powerful vampire is monitoring them. It’s not good enough to rate better than yellow, but I can think of one or two situations in which it could be useful.
Kalistocrat’s Acument (Ex): Potentially of use if you intend to adventure out in the boonies where purchase limits are low. Of course, you need to be fine with living life as a Prophet of Kalistrade, which may not fit most people’s backstories.
Notorious Fool (Ex): An easy way to “get let off with a warning” when attempting to break into places you don’t belong. If they catch you, just pretend you were asleep!
Seamless Shapechanger (Ex): Seamless shapechanger is only going to be useful to you if you also took the malleable flesh vigilante talent. If that’s the case, it becomes extraordinarily easy to impersonate just about anyone and anything, and all that well before the many guises social talents start to come online. It does take some investment, though.
Transformation Sequence (Su): Personally, I think the disadvantages to transformation sequence outweigh the advantages, but your mileage may vary. Most vigilantes can simply wait until the quick change social talent comes online at 7th level.
Well-Known Expert (Ex): Benefits from renown, but doesn’t require it. A fun, thematic social talent that encourages you to focus on aiding your teammates instead of hogging the spotlight. Adjunct Professor Maxine Vetro of the Glass Cannon Podcast’s Side Quest Side Sesh series does a particularly great job with this talent.


Beginner’s Luck (Ex): There are two fatal problems with beginner’s luck. First, you can’t fool many onlookers with your sudden use of a vigilante talent until you attain a high level—if you have even a small crowd present, someone is going to realize that you’re more than what you seem. Second, you can’t fool the same onlooker more than once per week. This doesn’t leave a lot of leeway if you need to get into a combat while in your social identity.
Bellflower Innuendo (Ex): Passing secret messages with Bluff is an extraordinarily niche use of the skill, and one that I’ve frankly never seen used. Remember that talents are precious, precious things—if you can’t envision yourself using it all the time, it’s dead weight.
Conflicted Identity (Ex): Alignment-based effects are very rare—hardly worth building around.
Double Time (Ex): Benefits from social grace, but doesn’t require it. There’s no reason any vigilante should be interested in mundane crafting or wage-earning. Safely skippable.
Entrepreneur (Ex): Benefits from social grace, but doesn’t require it. As with double time, there’s no reason why you should use any skill to earn money through mundane means unless you have literal months of downtime on your hands.
Gossip Collector (Ex): Benefits from renown, but doesn’t require it. I can think of very few if any situations in which you would need to gather information with the Diplomacy skill at top speed, and that tells me that it’s not worth a social talent.
Guise of Life (Su): I suppose dhampir PCs could take guise of life in order to pass as human, but that’s about the extent of the talent’s utility.
Intrigue Feats (Ex): I’m sorry, but none of the feats listed would rate more than about a yellow for vigilantes, especially when they’re up against a lot of other worthwhile social talents. Just forget it. Social talents are too valuable.




5th Level


Ancestral Enlightenment (Ex): An untyped +4 to any Knowledge skill you have ranks in is an easy buy for me. You may not be a Knowledge jockey, but this at least ensures that you can hold your own on monster identification checks, aid another actions, etc.
Hidden Magic (Ex): Magic aura and greater magic aura are fantastic spells for appearing to be less threatening. With the hidden magic talent, you could walk into a heavily guarded fortress carrying all kinds of magically enhanced gear and appear to be no more than a humble commoner toting around some high-quality but ultimately unremarkable items. If you have equipment enchantments like glamered, so much the better! You’ll look like you’re wearing nothing more than normal clothing and toting nothing more than a walking stick.
Mockingbird (Ex): Another highly customizable social talent for creative players. One of the issues with each of these spells for most players is that they’re all pretty low-level, which means the save DCs to disbelieve your warbling will never scale very well. You’re not most players, though: as social talents, your mockingbird save DCs will always scale with your vigilante level, making sound-based trickery much more viable as an adventuring tactic. It’s kind of like using Heighten Spell on ghost sound, ventriloquism, and vocal alteration.




9th Level


Skill Familiarity (Ex): A fabulous social talent that makes life easier in a lot of ways. Disguise and Intimidate have obvious synergy with the vigilante’s skillset; you might also consider Bluff, Acrobatics, Diplomacy, Perception, Stealth, and UMD as strong contenders, depending on your needs.
