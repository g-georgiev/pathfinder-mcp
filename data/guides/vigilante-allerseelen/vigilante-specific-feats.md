# Vigilante-Specific Feats

Vigilante-Specific Feats


Convincing Persona: I don’t know how many vigilantes will have build space for Convincing Persona, but it’s a good feat that prevents your social and vigilante identities from merging in the public eye. Maybe talk to your GM about whether they would permit this as one of the intrigue feats available in place of a social talent, because I’d consider picking it up through that route in certain builds.


Notorious Vigilante: It’s a mild action economy boost for Dazzling Display builds, but otherwise isn’t super necessary.
Sinister Reputation: The biggest benefit to Sinister Reputation is the ability to demoralize enemies as a swift action, but because that feature only works on creatures with fewer HD than you have, it’s always going to be limited.
Startling Getaway: Startling Getaway assumes that you’re beginning each combat by attacking an enemy that’s unaware of your presence. Stalker vigilantes will manage that more than most, but it’s still a bit niche.


Agent of Fear: Unfortunately, Agent of Fear doesn’t work twice on the same enemies in the same combat, per the rules text, which means you still haven’t solved the primary problem of frightening appearance and stunning appearance. Womp womp.
Masked Symbol: Blasé bonuses to skill checks. You’re better off taking Skill Focus if you’re truly trying to become good at Bluff, Intimidate, Diplomacy, etc.
