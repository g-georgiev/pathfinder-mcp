# Warlock

Warlock


The Basics


        Like the cabalist archetype, the warlock archetype is also indebted to the magus class. Where cabalists focus on blood and shadow magic, though, warlocks shoot beams of elemental energy at foes as their primary mode of attack.


        Versatility: +2
        Power: -2


        Stacks With: 


The Abilities


        (1) Class Skills: Alters class skills. I’m going to call this mostly a win. Knowledge (Arcana) and Spellcraft are both great skills, so you’ll just have to work out a way (the fey thoughts racial trait that many races have access to is one such) to grab Perception again.


        (1) Skill Ranks Per Level: Alters skill ranks per level. A straight downgrade, but then again, warlocks are Intelligence-based, so you’ll probably end up with more skill ranks overall than base vigilantes.


        (1) Armor Proficiencies: Alters armor proficiencies. Yeah, so there’s not a great way to build a Strength-based warlock. Weapon Finesse and ranged combat with mystic bolts make far more sense for the archetype, anyway.


        (1) Spellcasting: Replaces 4th-, 8th-, 10th-, 14th-, and 16th-level vigilante talents. Man, you really hate to see that many vigilante talents fly out the window, huh? With only three available to you before the end of most six-book Paizo APs, you’re going to have to be very, very discriminating about which talents you pick. The good news is, you cast spells like a wizard now—at least up to 6th level—and wizards have the best spell list in the game. I’ll refer you over to my Spell Codex resource if you want to spot some generally good picks on the list.


        (1) Mystic Bolts (Su): Replaces vigilante specialization. Mystic bolts gets a lot of grief from the community. It has plenty of weaknesses—susceptibility to energy immunities, no possibility of adding enhancement bonuses through weapon enchantments or greater magic weapon, no feat support, no Deadly Aim (doesn’t apply to touch attacks)—but it’s perhaps not quite as bad as it’s cracked up to be. Deliquescent gloves or demonsmith gloves could be combined via magic item rules or tattoos to give an extra 2d6 energy damage; a rogue or vivisectionist alchemist dip would give you sneak attack that could then be bulked up via Accomplished Sneak Attacker; sense vitals is on the wizard spell list for another source of precision damage; the arcane striker vigilante talent can squeeze some more damage out. It’s still a long haul, but targeting touch AC many times per round is workable unless you run up against a wall of energy resistances.
        GMs, if you’re looking for a quick and dirty way to modify mystic bolts so that it’s better, you might consider allowing players to select one simple blast wild talent from the kineticist class—no burn, no gather power, no elemental overflow—with Intelligence replacing Constitution as the damage modifier. Whenever players would be granted a new damage type (7th, 13th, and 19th levels) they can instead select a new simple blast and a single 1-burn form or substance infusion that they can apply to their blasts for free. You still get the “souped-up elemental blast cantrip” feel of mystic bolts, but it’s actually good: maximum damage at 20th level will be around 10d6+17, which will work better against DR and energy resistance than the nickel-and-dime damage that base mystic bolts deal. You can experiment with the empower metakinesis at high levels if you feel like the modified bolts are falling further behind in power than you’d like. I’ve got a guide on the kineticist class, Going Nova, that gives a thorough rundown of what all that jargon means, if you feel like implementing the changes.


        (2) Warlock Talents: Alters vigilante talents. Many golden oldies will be familiar from the cabalist list, so I won’t bother generating new text for those abilities. 


      * (2) Familiar (Ex): This won’t come as a surprise to anyone. Familiars grant good bonuses, the Alertness feat, the ability to deliver touch spells for their masters—and that’s all without touching the flexibility of familiar archetypes or the Improved Familiar feat. If you have any spare room in your build, you want this talent. My recommendation is to make either familiar or tattoo chamber your 6th-level vigilante talent.
      * (2) Tattoo Chamber (Su): Tattoo chamber is a great talent whose primary purpose is to allow you to use spell trigger items without actually holding them. What counts as a spell trigger item? Wands, for one, and staves, for another. It’s unclear whether metamagic rods count, because while they’re use-activated items, not spell trigger items, they don’t need to be either donned or activated with a command word, only held in the hand while casting a spell. My recommendation would be to store predominantly wands in your tattoo early on, transitioning to staves at later levels and adding in a quicken metamagic rod or a persistent metamagic rod as your budget (and GM!) allows. In all cases, prioritize spells that you’ll want at your fingertips in combat without having to devote spell slots to them.
      * (2) Arcane Striker (Su): I hate that this talent is so integral to warlocks, because it’s a tacit admission that mystic bolts can’t entirely hold their own in combat. More damage is obviously good, but truly, you should just modify mystic bolts using my kineticist suggestion or third-party content from Legendary Games. Remember that the gloves of arcane striking are a bonkers-good wondrous item for these builds!
      * (2) Elemental Armor (Su): Resist energy is so much more versatile and powerful that I can’t see much of a use case for elemental armor. Immunity at 16th level might be intriguing if you were in a game like Reign of Winter, but honestly, the campaign’s about done by that point, and you’d have to wait until 18th level to pick up the talent anyway, since spellcasting trades away your 16th-level vigilante talent.
      * (8) Social Simulacrum (Sp): I’ve seen simulacra used intelligently, but since they’re not under your control, there’s at least as good a chance of things going dramatically wrong when you use them as there is of things going dramatically right. Especially with your tight talent budget, I just don’t buy it.


        (3) Piercing Bolts (Ex): Replaces unshakable. Targeting touch AC is great, don’t get me wrong, but this feels like a class feature tax for something that should have been available from 1st level. If you go with my modified version of mystic bolts, you can just eliminate piercing bolts altogether, as the delineation between physical and energy blasts will determine whether it targets touch AC or not.


Is It Worth It?


        Even if spellcasting, tattoo chamber, and familiars create an incredibly versatile ⅔ caster, the cabalist can do all of those things without the power of manufactured weapons. With my changes to mystic bolts, I’d leave my versatility rating at +2 but change my power rating to +0, leading to a green rating overall. The archetype could be so cool, but it just needs a little help over the finish line. C’mon, GMs. You know you want to dance with the occult classes.
