# Weapon Enchantments

Weapon Enchantments


Gold Cost


Sniping / Improved / Greater (CL 5/10/15, 1,875/7,500/16,875 gp): Even though vigilantes aren’t set up at all for ranged builds, stalkers can still derive great benefit from sniping weapons. Remember that the 5-rank benefit of Signature Skill: Stealth and the Expert Sniper feat will each reduce the penalties for sniping by 10, as well. If you’re going to snipe, you can’t be taking any penalties on your Stealth checks.


Lesser Concealed / Concealed (CL 5/10, 3,000/7,500 gp): Whereas shrinking weapons remain identifiable as weapons when “concealed,” lesser concealed and concealed weapons actually turn into other things: lesser concealed weapons into things of approximately the same size as the original weapon, and concealed weapons into things much smaller than the original weapon. You’re free to pick among these three enchantments which you need/want, but I’d recommend at least one.
Glamered (CL 10, 4,000 gp): Glamered weapons only appear to be something different than what they are, but since true seeing is the only way of penetrating that illusion, you’re probably safe from most enemies. I still think I’d rather go lesser concealed than glamered. Do note that glamered is the only way to conceal a ranged weapon, though.
Shrinking (CL 5, 1,000 gp): Because of their talents for infiltration and ambush, vigilantes will often need to conceal weapons. Shrinking is an exceptionally cheap weapon that can turn a naginata into a dinner fork, a greatsword into a dagger, or a greataxe into a small camping hatchet. Should make it much easier to conceal via Sleight of Hand, even if it’s clearly identifiable as a weapon of some kind. Keep in mind that the hidden magic social talent will conceal the magical aura of a shrinking weapon, making it look exactly like a mundane piece of cutlery under even magical scrutiny.




+1 Bonus


Agile (CL 7): Don’t want to burn a vigilante talent on lethal grace? An agile weapon + Weapon Finesse is your best bet, since Weapon Focus + [Weapon] Grace is two feats that feat-strapped vigilantes don’t particularly want to take.
Bane (CL 8): In certain campaigns, a bane weapon is practically required. Who’s going to play Giantslayer without taking bane (humanoid [giant])?
Cruel (CL 5): Vigilantes, especially stalkers with twisting fear, can often make a lot of hay with demoralization tactics; a cruel weapon simply amplifies the debuffing that you get from that process.
Keen (CL 10): A classic. Stalkers may not value crits quite as much as avengers do, but they can always move into Butterfly’s Sting to pass them off to more martially minded colleagues, if that’s appealing.
Training (CL 3): How many places can you buy feats for gold? Getting a training weapon is one way. Useful if you need to squeeze just a little more out of your build to bring some giant, complicated feat chain to fruition.
Veering (CL 5): A seeking veering ranged weapon is, like, 90% of the way to Improved Precise Shot, and that’s a dang good benefit for ranged vigilantes. Highly recommended.


Distracting / Greater Distracting (CL 5 / 8): Vigilantes don’t have access to the full complement of fighter feats (Spellbreaker, predominantly) that would be required to really chew up spellcasters, but distracting is still a great investment with Disruptive to force difficult concentration checks on enemies.
Fortuitous (CL 8): Some vigilantes, especially those with vital punishment, vigilante’s reflexes, or the snake animal patron specialize in taking attacks of opportunity. The fortuitous enchantment will grant you a second attack of opportunity 1/round at a -5 penalty. Great stuff!
Merciful (CL 5): Enforcer + a merciful weapon is a classic combo for debuffing with Intimidate. Assuming your GM is okay with the cognitive dissonance of having a cruel merciful weapon, you can do even more debuffing with that setup.


Benevolent (CL 5): A special call-out for bellflower harvesters is necessary, since this is an incredible—mandatory, even—enchantment for them.
PSFG Dueling / Leveraging (CL 7 / 6): Hey! Do you want insane bonuses to your CMB for certain combat maneuvers? Yeah, I thought you might. If you’re bull rushing, dragging, disarming, tripping, repositioning, or dirty tricking, you need these enchantments.
Ghost Touch (CL 9): Stalker vigilantes are rogue-likes, and like all rogue-likes, they depend on precision damage to win combats. Any guesses what type of damage incorporeal creatures are immune to? That’s right, precision damage from non-ghost touch weapons. I wouldn’t try to slap ghost touch on your main weapon—get a +1 ghost touch dagger or something similar and use it as a backup when you need it.




+2 Bonus


Truthful (CL 11): A choice anticaster enchantment. Blur? Displacement? Mirror image? Nuh-uh.


Distracting, Greater (CL 8): As distracting. A great anticaster enchantment.
Phase Locking (CL 7): Prevents outsiders and spellcasters from teleporting away when they get tired of a fight. Stay here, dammit!


Silencing (CL 8): Probably a trap for most vigilantes, since silent dispatch is so demonstrably superior.
